# Mythic Greek — Cassirer / Snell Substrate Shelf

**Status:** Live substrate shelf. This is not a full Greek literature campaign
and not a promotion of Snell into a standing modern encounter.

## Why This Exists

The mythic Greek axis is now active because two shelves pressure it together:

- Cassirer's PSF II apparatus gives direct pressure for Homer, Aeschylus,
  Sophocles, Euripides, tragedy, and mythic-symbolic Greek evidence.
- Snell's *Die Entdeckung des Geistes* gives sustained chapter pressure for
  Homer, the Olympian gods, Hesiod, early lyric, Pindar, tragedy,
  Aristophanes, and Callimachus.

This shelf gives web chats and agents a direct, curlable/searchable Greek
surface for that axis without folding it into the philosophy-of-knowledge
Greek substrate.

## Contents

- `source/tei/` — TEI XML witnesses downloaded from PerseusDL and
  OpenGreekAndLatin/First1KGreek.
- `source/plain/` — generated plain-text search surfaces.
- `source/manifest.yaml` — source URLs, CTS URNs, local paths, and cluster
  labels.
- `source/checksums.sha256` — checksums for the generated shelf.

Coverage:

- Homeric epic and Homerica: `Iliad`, `Odyssey`, the 33 Perseus Homeric Hymns
  witnesses, and Homeric Epigrams.
- Hesiodic material: `Theogony`, `Works and Days`, `Shield`.
- Pindar: Olympian, Pythian, Nemean, and Isthmian odes.
- Tragedy: Aeschylus, Sophocles, and Euripides TEI witnesses available in
  PerseusDL.
- Comedy and aesthetic reflection: Aristophanes.
- Hellenistic afterlife: Callimachus where curlable TEI is available, including
  `Aetia`, `Iambi`, `Hecale`, epigrams, and the hymns.

## Gaps

- Early lyric fragments beyond Pindar are pressured by Snell but are not yet
  represented by a clean local TEI shelf.
- Greek mystery-religion materials are pressured by PSF II through modern
  scholarship, but the primary Greek surfaces are not yet represented here.

## Use Discipline

Search `source/plain/` first for discovery and cross-reading. Use the TEI XML
and the source URLs in `source/manifest.yaml` when exact textual structure,
citation, or attribution matters.

This shelf exists to support substrate work around Cassirer PSF II and Snell.
It should not become a general "all Greek myth" expansion unless repeated
material pressure earns that move.
