## Non-Ethica Material in the Vol. 2 Spinoza Chapter

The Vol. 2 Spinoza chapter is not pure Ethica. Two parallel source paths run alongside:

- **Korte Verhandeling via Sigwart 1870 German** -- the entire chapter section I (Parts 017-018) is anchored on Spinoza's earliest work, which Cassirer reads in Sigwart's German translation. This campaign's Van Vloten / Land vol. 1 contains the KV in Dutch, but verifying Sigwart-vs-Dutch is a different methodological move from Latin Ethica verification and may not warrant primary contact unless the Renaissance/Campanella connection becomes load-bearing.
- **Tractatus de intellectus emendatione (Latin)** -- Cassirer reads this directly in Latin. Parts 019-022 walk through the TIE. The TIE is in Gebhardt II and Van Vloten / Land vol. 1.

## Sister Campaigns

This campaign sits alongside two other Vol. 2-anchored Latin campaigns:

- `sources/latin/gassendi-disquisitio-metaphysica/` -- the original primary-source campaign, opened first against Cassirer's Gassendi chapter (Erk. Vol. 2 Parts 007-011). The Gassendi encounter format (Trigger / Witnesses / Local Context / Cassirer Passage / Working Translation / Surrounding Argument / Reply / Notes / Re-entry) is the template to follow for the first Ethica encounter.
- `sources/latin/campanella-metaphysica/` -- the Campanella primary-source campaign, opened against Cassirer's Spinoza chapter Part 018 (the Renaissance/Campanella historical perspective). The first encounter is on the `intrinsecatio` passage.

## Boundary

Open encounters only when specific Vol. 2 pressure (a footnote quotation, a chapter-review question, a Leibniz-opening test) calls for them. The Schuon limit applies: this campaign's witness set is broad enough to support many encounters, but expansion without absorption is not progress.

The natural first encounter is the *Ethica* I prop. 10 scholium (Erk. Vol. 2 Part 030 footnote 2): it is short, self-contained, structurally load-bearing for the chapter-close anthropomorphism claim, and present in all three witnesses for triangulation.
