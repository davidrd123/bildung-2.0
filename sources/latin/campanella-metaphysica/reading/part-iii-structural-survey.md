# Part III Structural Survey

Status: working survey from hOCR sparse scan plus verified ABBYY extractions of major book openings. This is an orientation map, not a complete index.

Method: searched the flat hOCR for `PARS TERTIA`, `LIBER`, `CAP.`, `ART.`, and topic terms, then verified the main Part III book openings by extracting and normalizing the corresponding ABBYY leaves. Leaf ranges below are therefore mixed evidence: book-opening leaves are verified by dedicated raw/normalized extracts; ending leaves are inferred from the next verified book opening or hOCR-heading sequence.

## Opening Finding

Part III is not only the "heterodox applied" remainder. It starts with cosmology and world-duration questions, then moves through angels and the mental world, archetypal ideas, the human soul, governance, religion/law/prophecy/magic, beatitude, and ages of ages. For the current long-run purpose, the strongest first sequential target is Liber XIV, `De Anima Humana`, because it directly opens soul, hidden self-knowledge, self-sense, animal comparison, and immortality without first requiring a detour through 70 leaves of cosmology.

## Zone Selection

The first sequential run should begin at Liber XIV, leaf 0791, rather than at the first leaf of Part III. Liber XI would answer "how Part III starts," but it spends roughly 78 leaves on world-eternity, cosmic origin, stars, celestial motions, and Copernican/Ptolemaic disputes before the explicitly soul-centered material arrives. Liber XIV is the shortest path into what made Part III attractive in the first place: soul, self-sense, animal comparison, and immortality.

Working rule for this run: treat `part-iii-NNN-*` files as a local page-order sequence inside Part III. This avoids making the older Part I trial `001-*` look like the predecessor of a non-contiguous global `002-*`.
