## Opening Finding

Cassirer's bridge is real but too smooth. He is right that Campanella gives a Renaissance precedent for cognition as ontological becoming: leaf 906 defines intuitive understanding as `intrinsecatio, per quam unum fit aliud`; leaf 402 says external understanding is suffering from exterior things and becoming them; leaf 85 says knowing is part of alienation.

But the opened Latin now shows four controls on Cassirer's Vol. 2 paragraph:

1. Intrinsecation begins in gustatory and bodily contact, not in a pure noetic formula. Leaves 23 and 28 show taste as intrinsic contact, but also show taste and pepper-contact altered by body, organ, and medium.
2. Intrinsecation is not a generic triumph of immediate truth. Leaf 438 says that even if the knower were intrinsecated, cognition would still be by `mensura sui`, not by `mensura entis, & veri`.
3. Not all knowing is dissolution into an object. Leaf 402 distinguishes exterior intellection, which is passive and alienating, from hidden essential self-intellection, which does not need to become because it already is.
4. The leaf-906 intrinsecation passage is not merely epistemological. It belongs to beatitude and is grounded in the gustatory analogy: the mouth/taste figure, not the eye/species figure, supplies the bodily model for intuitive becoming.

So Cassirer gets the formal bridge right: Campanella gives him a pre-Spinozistic language of knowing as becoming-one. What he compresses is the internal differentiation of that language: external passive becoming, essential self-intellection, finite `mensura sui`, and divine causative intellection are not the same register.

Leaf 444 now confirms Cassirer's second Vol. 2 Campanella footnote exactly. The love of God is essential because self-love is love of being, and God is being simply; participated divinity has `saporem` because each finite thing's own being is a participation in divine entity. This strengthens Cassirer's claim that finite self-affirmation points beyond itself to all-encompassing being, while keeping Campanella's argument grounded in hidden love, hidden self-sense, and participation rather than in a free-floating mystical sentiment.

## Reading Decision

Vol. 2 Part 018 should keep Cassirer's Campanella-Spinoza bridge, but with a sharper gloss:

- `innerliches Einswerden` is a defensible rendering of the leaf-906 definition, provided it is read as directional becoming, not symmetric union.
- The phrase should not be allowed to swallow Campanella's whole theory of cognition. Leaf 402 says exterior knowing is becoming the intelligible, but essential self-knowing is not becoming at all; it is the knower's hidden essence knowing itself.
- Leaf 438 prevents a triumphalist intrinsecation reading. Finite intrinsecation remains finite unless it reaches the divine register; otherwise it is still `mensura sui`.
- Leaf 444 confirms the essential-love and `saporem` footnote, but it also shows that this claim is a participation argument: self-love is not displaced by love of God; it becomes love of God insofar as finite being participates divine being.
- The Spinoza comparison works best if treated as a shared Renaissance pressure on cognition as ontological participation, not as a settled identity between Campanella's beatific intrinsecation and Spinoza's mature `scientia intuitiva`.
