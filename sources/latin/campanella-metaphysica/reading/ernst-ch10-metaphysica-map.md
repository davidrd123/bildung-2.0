# Ernst Chapter 10 As Secondary Map

Status: secondary guide for triage, not primary evidence.

Source: `../../../modern/ernst-campanella-book-and-body-of-nature/source/raw/019-the-new-encyclopedia-of-knowledge.txt`, section "The New Metaphysics" (Ernst, *Tommaso Campanella: The Book and the Body of Nature*, chapter 10).

## Use

Ernst's chapter gives a contemporary scholar's triage map of the 1638 *Metaphysica*. Use it to identify likely pressure points, not to decide in advance what the Latin means. The campaign should still earn its structure from transcription, translation, and local commentary.

## Four Ernst Zones

### A. Skeptical Opening

Ernst treats the fourteen opening `dubitationes` as the first major zone: the possibility of knowledge, sensory error, alienation, self-forgetting, and the Socratic use of learned ignorance.

Primary citations in Ernst:

- *Metaphysica* part I, p. 4a
- *Metaphysica* part I, pp. 20-21

- Page-order work should still decide whether to resume at the omitted leaf-23 right column or lower leaf 28.

### B. Self-Knowledge And Primality

Ernst treats part I, p. 73a/b as the strongest philosophical node for self-knowledge: hidden/innate knowledge, added/accidental knowledge, passion as occasion, and knowledge as a primality alongside power and will/love.

Primary citations in Ernst:

- *Metaphysica* part I, p. 73a
- *Metaphysica* part I, p. 73b

### C. Primalities And Harmony

Ernst treats the primalities doctrine and harmony as the main part II zone: power, wisdom, love; the nothingness-counterpart of impotence, ignorance, hate; and the great influences of necessity, fate, and harmony.

Primary citations in Ernst:

- *Metaphysica* part II, p. 169b
- *Metaphysica* part II, p. 217
- Adami, `Ad philosophos Germaniae`, *Opera Latina* I, pp. 17-18

Current campaign contact:

- Encounter 002 is in part II and gives a metaphysical ascent anchor, but the later great-influences/harmony pages Ernst cites are not yet opened.

- Adami's preface is a separate witness problem; it may deserve its own source scaffold rather than being folded into the *Metaphysica* parts stream.

### D. Abstract Substances, Soul, Cosmology

Ernst treats part III as a zone of angels/demons, laws, religion, prophecy, miracles, the human soul, immortality, and cosmology.

## Method Cautions

- Ernst is a guide, not authority for this campaign's structure.
- Her four-zone map is useful for triage, but should not become the official ontology of the *Metaphysica*.
- Paganini references named by Ernst are scholarship to consult after primary contact, not evidence to import before the Latin is opened.
- The Adami preface may be Campanella-near, but it is not the 1638 *Metaphysica* witness and should be handled as a separate witness if used.
