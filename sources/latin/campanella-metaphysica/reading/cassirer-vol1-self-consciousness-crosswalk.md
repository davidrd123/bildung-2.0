## Opening Finding

Cassirer's reading is materially stronger after the Campanella pilots, but the fit is not one-to-one. Leaf 85 confirms the machinery Cassirer needs: knowing as partial alienation, objects as occasions/specifications rather than causes of wisdom, hidden self-knowledge prior to added object-knowledge, and wisdom as a primality. Leaf 86/87 confirms that Campanella distinguishes inborn/infused knowing from introduced/acquired knowing and can make apparent foolishness into a sign of higher wisdom.

Leaf 402 now opens one of Cassirer's direct Part II self-consciousness loci: `Unaquaeque res intelligit se ipsam intellectione abdita per suam essentiam`. It confirms the leaf-85 analogue and sharpens the distinction Cassirer needs: external knowing is suffering and becoming the intelligible, while essential self-intellection is hidden, original, and not reflexive because the knower already is itself. The `cogito` precursor and the `esse animae... est cognitio sui` locus remain unopened.
