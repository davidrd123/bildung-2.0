# Campanella: Sapor as the Cognitive Form

Absorption note synthesizing evidence opened across leaves 23, 28, 438, 444, 449-450, 799, 803, 810-814, 860, and 906 on the role of *sapor* (taste, flavor) in Campanella's metaphysics. Related Liber XV pressure from leaves 821-824 is included where it affects the Bruno comparison and open participation questions. The material has been opened in multiple parts and one encounter; the synthesis itself does not appear in any of those files. Captured here so the doctrine is articulable as the Part III soul-book substrate is reviewed for occurrences.

## Thesis

For Campanella, the bodily figure for the cognition of God is gustatory, not visual. This is not metaphor — it is a structural commitment that does work the dominant Christian-Platonist mind-as-eye tradition (Augustine / Aquinas / Bonaventure) cannot do, because the eye-model preserves distance, and cognition of God for Campanella requires *becoming*.

## Evidence chain

**Leaf 23 (taste as foundational figure)**: *solus hic sensus intrinsecatur obiecto* — "this sense alone is intrinsicated into the object." Taste is the sense where cognition is literally becoming. The etymology *sapientia* < *sapere* < *sapor* makes wisdom-as-tasting doctrinal, not punning. But Campanella immediately denies that ordinary sensory taste delivers true cognition (variability by body, age, illness, animal). Taste names the *structure* of true cognition; the senses cannot perform it in ordinary use.

**Leaf 28 (intrinsication is bodily real but altered)**: *rei natura nobis intrinsecatur* — the thing's nature is intrinsicated into us — but altered by saliva, tongue, flesh, intervening air, accidents. Intrinsication happens; it does not preserve truth automatically.

**Leaf 438 (the metaphysical chain)**: *Affectio enim ex sapore nascitur, sapor ex posse* — affection from taste, taste from power. This locates *sapor* between the metaphysical primality of power and the moral phenomenon of affection. The same leaf adds the limit: even if a knower were intrinsicated into the species, his cognition would still be *non mensura entis et veri, sed mensura sui*. Finite intrinsication is real but bounded.

**Leaf 444 (the participation claim)**: *Divinitas participata talem saporem habet* — participated divinity has such a flavor. Finite being is a participation in divine being; that participation carries the *sapor* of the divine within it. Coupled with *Amor abditus, quo se cuncta amant* (hidden love by which all things love themselves) and *sensus abditus, quo sese cuncta sapiunt* (hidden sense by which all things sense/taste themselves). Each being's hidden self-relation is simultaneously love and taste. Also: *omne ens seipsum esse amando, magis amat Deum quam se* — in loving its own being, each being loves God more than itself, because finite being is participated divine being.

**Leaves 449-450 (the triad)**: every true being is essentiated by *Potentia, Sapientia, et Amor*. Three primalities — not three faculties but the metaphysical structure of being-as-such, with God having it infinitely and creatures finitely.

**Leaf 799 (Liber XIV soul-book confirmation)**: the Caput IV title confirms that the *sapere* family structures the immortality investigation itself: *In anima, ut in omni ente finito, dari partem passivam et partem activam; sed non Aristotelico modo, et ipsum sapere et amare in quantum ens; sed in quantum anima dare alteri sapere, posse, amare... Et utrum quo primo sapit homo, sit immortale, inquirendum esse iuxta nostram philosophiam.* The soul-book is not merely adjacent to the sapor doctrine. It asks whether that by which the human being first knows/tastes is immortal.

**Leaf 803 (sapere before spiritus)**: *Sapere enim est antequam esse calidum, tenue, et mobile* — "to know/taste is prior to being hot, subtle, and mobile." This puts the gustatory verb before the hot, subtle, mobile spirit in the order of explanation. The corporeal spirit is the medium through which cognition operates outwardly, but *sapere* is not reducible to the pneuma's heat, subtlety, or motion.

**Leaves 810-812 (sapere and hidden knowledge distinguished)**: Liber XIV Caput V Articulus IV separates common knowledge that operates with the corporeal spirit from hidden knowledge that remains when spirit departs. The soul does not know itself except *abdita notitia* (leaf 810); the knowledge that remains is what flows from above and is hidden, while common object-derived science is lost when spirit departs, *quo cum sapiebat* (leaf 811); during body-formation the soul is overwhelmed by ignorance of itself yet is never deprived of *secreta notitia sui* (leaf 812). This does not retract the sapor reading, but it prevents collapsing all cognition into embodied *sapere*.

**Leaf 814 (religion begins in hidden tasting of God)**: post-separation cognition is *sapientia intuitiva*, not window-vision but *per obiecti tactum internum aut per ideam a superis defluentem*. The same leaf turns the hidden-knowledge register toward religion: the human mind, though ignorant of itself because of added passions, *vi secreta Deum sapit et ad Deum contendit*. Campanella immediately names this the beginning and strength of Religion: *Et hoc est Religionis initium et robur*. The sun-heat analogy gives the pansensist counterpart: heat enclosed in earth, forgetful of itself because of supervening passions, still *secreta vi semper sursum sapit et tendit*.

**Leaf 860 (religion tastes truths and archetypal goods)**: Liber XVI confirms that the gustatory register is not only the foundation of cognition but the action of religion itself. Religion emerges from our hearts, teaches us divinely, binds us to divine worship, and is given *ut gustaremus, Deo adhaerentes, omnes veritates* — so that, adhering to God, we would taste all truths. The same leaf describes rapt souls as *Mundi Archetypi bona gustantes*, tasting the goods of the Archetypal World, and returns to the affective axis: *sapimus et amamus atque odimus*.

**Leaf 906 (the beatific climax)**: *intuitus animae* (the soul's intuition) is unlike *intuitus oculi* (the eye's intuition); it sees *per intrinsecationem rei in se, et sui in illam*. The eye-model cannot deliver beatitude because it preserves distance. *Ergo intelligere intuitivum est intrinsecatio, per quam unum fit aliud. Cognoscere enim est fieri rem cognitam.* The continuation Cassirer drops: the soul cannot be beatified in this life because it does not become God here, only the defective representations of him. *Sicut nec per cibi visionem & tactionem cibari, nisi gustetur* — one cannot be nourished by sight or touch of food, only by tasting. Scripture therefore liken beatific vision to eating, *unde deceptus est Mahometes* — whence Muhammad was deceived. Muhammad's error was in register (taking the metaphor as literal sensual feast), not in metaphor; the eating-figure *is* the right figure because both forms of consumption become their object.

## The synthesis: *Potentia → Sapor → Amor* as gustatory process

Read leaf 438's *sapor ex posse, affectio ex sapore* together with the leaf 449-450 triad. The three primalities are not three abstract categories — they are a single process:

- *Potentia*: the capacity to be at all
- *Sapientia (= sapor)*: the actualization of that capacity as self-presence; taste-of-being
- *Amor*: the affection born from the taste; clinging to one's own being

The intellectual primality is literally taste. Wisdom is not abstract cognition; it is the gustatory mode of being-actualized. Loving follows necessarily, because what one tastes-becomes one cannot help adhering to.

Leaf 803 sharpens the order: *sapere* is prior to the hot, subtle, mobile spirit. The pneuma is where knowing/tasting manifests in embodied cognition, not where it originates. This prevents the leaf-28 bodily-alteration evidence from becoming a reduction of *sapere* to saliva, tongue, flesh, or spirit; Campanella's point is that knowing/tasting operates prior to and through those bodily media.

### The Two Registers

Liber XIV Caput V Articulus IV makes explicit that *sapere* and *abdita notitia* are not interchangeable. The soul "knew/tasted" with the spirit (*quo cum sapiebat*, leaf 811), but it does not know itself except by hidden knowledge (*non novit se, nisi abdita notitia*, leaf 810). When spirit fails, common science acquired from corporeal objects is lost; only what flows from above and what is hidden remains (*quae a superis defluit, et quae abdita est*, leaf 811). During embodiment and body-formation, the soul can be overwhelmed by ignorance of itself and yet never be deprived of secret self-knowledge (*secreta notitia sui numquam destitui*, leaf 812).

The bridge is leaf 444's *sensus abditus, quo sese cuncta sapiunt*: hidden sense itself uses the gustatory verb. So the sapor doctrine is not merely body-bound, but its embodied deployment depends on the corporeal spirit and can be lost with that spirit. The hidden self-cognitive register remains. This gives the process-reading a sharper internal articulation: *sapere* names the gustatory cognitive form as it operates through spirit, while *abdita / secreta notitia* names the self-knowledge that persists when the with-spirit operation falls away.

### The Religion Axis

Leaves 814 and 860 show that this is not only a cognitive anthropology. The hidden movement by which the mind *Deum sapit* is named as *Religionis initium et robur*. Religion then acts so that, adhering to God, we would *gustare* all truths. This gives the earlier formula *religio ... reversio ad suum principium* a gustatory mechanics: return to one's principle is not abstract orientation alone, but the hidden tasting of God that becomes explicit in worship, truth, and rapture.

The triad describes being-actualizing-itself through bodily-gustatory self-presence and into adhesion. This is what reading the opened evidence together generates that no single leaf carries on its own. The campaign's glossary has the components (`Affectio enim ex sapore nascitur, sapor ex posse` as a thesis entry; `Potentia / Sapientia / Amor` as a triad entry; `sapere / sapientia / sapor` as a term cluster) but no artifact connects them into the process-reading.

## Why this matters

**Inversion of the visual tradition.** The standard term *visio beatifica* survives in Campanella but with different mechanics. The soul's "vision" of God operates by a different sense entirely. This is genuinely heterodox; the orthodox-scholastic apparatus cannot quite catch it.

**Pansensism is consistent, not a separate doctrine.** Leaf 444's hidden self-taste is the same structure as Liber XIV's pansensist claims (each thing senses itself). Leaf 799 now confirms that the soul-book articulates the active/passive problem through *sapere*: every finite being has knowing/tasting and loving as being, while soul gives another *sapere, posse, amare*. The thesis prediction is therefore no longer only a prediction; the *sensus abditus quo sese cuncta sapiunt* bridge has a Liber XIV structural counterpart.

**Religion as gustatory return.** Leaf 87 defined religion as *reversio ad suum principium*. Leaf 814 and leaf 860 now show how that return works: even enclosed in bodily passions, the mind secretly knows/tastes God and tends toward God; religion then gives a tasted participation in all truths and archetypal goods. This matters for comparing Campanella with other Renaissance or Platonist models because religion is not only law, cult, or vision; it is the explicit unfolding of hidden gustatory self-orientation toward God.

**The Cassirer bridge to Spinoza.** Cassirer's compression takes the leaf-906 climax-formula and lifts it out of the gustatory chain that grounds it. The bridge to Spinoza's *scientia intuitiva* survives at the level of form but is too smooth at the level of substance — the gustatory grounding, the bodily figures, the *mensura sui* limit, the participation argument are all dropped. Spinoza's intuition is more abstract; Campanella's is bodily-figured all the way down.

**Comparison with Bruno.** Bruno's *anima mundi* is one universal soul animating all matter. Liber XV now shows that Campanella does not simply eliminate the *anima mundi*: leaf 824 preserves it as one of the Dominations, a vicarial governor within a monarchic angelological order. That makes the contrast sharper rather than weaker. Bruno's world-soul is monistic and self-sufficient; Campanella's world-soul is subordinated under God-as-monarch, while distributive pansensism still gives each thing its own *sensus abditus*. The bodily figure of taste belongs to Campanella; Bruno reaches for vision (the contracted/explicated Cusanus inheritance) and for poetic union. The `sources/italian/bruno-de-la-causa-principio-et-uno/parts/005b-dialogo-secondo-world-soul-and-life-in-all-things.md` material is the in-repo comparand if the Bruno-Campanella convergence/divergence on cognition-of-God is to be written up.

## What is not yet absorbed

- The Liber XIV to Liber XVI bridge is no longer wholly dark, but it remains incomplete. The review-clean `part-iii-xv-001` through `part-iii-xv-008` lane opens Liber XV leaves 817-824: conservation and governance, ends and defects, cross-world coupling, angelic ministry, Necessity/Fate/Harmony through the assisting orders, and the world-soul as Domination. Leaves 825-857 remain unopened and may still bear on the transition from soul, hidden cognition, and religion's beginning into the explicit Liber XVI treatment of religion, law, virtue, and the prophet-legislator.
- Leaf 823 maps Necessity, Fate, and Harmony through Thrones, Cherubim, and Seraphim. This may become an angelological projection of the `Potentia / Sapientia / Amor` triad, but keep it open: the three mappings are not yet proven equivalent.
- Leaf 821 introduces `tincturas veluti primalitatum propriarum` as a cross-world coupling metaphor. It is adjacent to the sapor and intrinsecatio chain but not identical to either; track whether `tinctura` becomes a stable participation term.
- Leaf 87's *uti per Primalitates suas ad superiora* points forward to Part II Liber VI Cap XI (already opened at 449-450), but Campanella also says *sed de hoc in 2. Parte erit sermo*. The Part II treatment may say more about *how* the primalities relate than the leaf-449 opening reveals.
- Whether the *intrinsecatio / extrinsecatio* coordinate pair at leaf 617 has a gustatory analogue. Probably not — *extrinsecatio* does not fit the eating-figure — but the absence is itself worth noting.
