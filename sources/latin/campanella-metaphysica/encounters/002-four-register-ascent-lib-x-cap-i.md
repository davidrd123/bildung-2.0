# 002 — The Four-Register Contemplative Ascent

## Trigger

Internal: surfaced while tracing the `intrinsec*` family across the volume from [[001-intrinsecatio-cap-ii-art-i]]. The single use of `intrinsecatur & extrinsecatur` in coordination (rather than the descriptive `intrinsecus` adjective) names *God's mode of relation to all things*, where the leaf-906 `intrinsecatio` named *the mind's mode of relation to God in beatitude*. The pair is structurally reciprocal; encounter 001's reading needs this passage as its metaphysical anchor.

There is no direct Cassirer citation for this passage. But Cassirer's Renaissance-background bridge for Spinoza's *scientia intuitiva* would land here if it landed honestly — this is where Campanella does the Christian-Platonist work that Spinoza eventually secularizes.

## Local Context

Lib. X is in *Pars II* of the *Universalis Philosophia* — the *Physica* part, which Campanella has structured around the doctrine of four worlds (or "mundi"): Material, Mathematical (= spatial), Mental, Archetypal. The chapter walks the reader through a diagram of concentric circles that gives the architecture of these worlds (the diagram itself is not on leaf 0617; the text references it as "ut in figura ista vides"). Within that exposition, Campanella inserts a contemplative-pedagogical passage on how the mind ascends THROUGH the four worlds as registers of cognition.

The "intrinsecatur & extrinsecatur" pair belongs to that contemplative pedagogy, not to the cosmological structure as such.

## Verified Latin Passage

From the right column, mid-page, against the JP2 (with long-s normalized and OCR glitches corrected):

> Item quomodo mentes quo magis recedunt à situali Mundo, proficiunt magis: in materiali enim agnoscunt, quomodo in uno, & ex uno sunt absque distinctione, & propensione ad plura, nisi ab extrinsecis existentiis. In Mathematico quomodo Deus unus intrinsecatur, & extrinsecatur omnibus, & continet, & servat absque sui divisione, & passione omnia facillime: & quomodo ideare imaginanti convenit verius omnia. In Metaphysico ad divinitatem proprius accedit processiones intraneas investigando, & essentiationes, & egressiones. In Archetypo tandem unam divinitatis essentiam, trinam proprietatem, & virtutes, & dependentias omnium ab ipso, per ipsum, ut in ipso. Et glorificatur Mens nostra in huius intuitu, comprehensione, & fruitione per elevationem.

## Working Translation

> Likewise, the more minds recede from the situal World, the more they progress: for in the material [register] they recognize how things are in one, and from one, without distinction or inclination toward many — except from extrinsic existences. In the Mathematical [register, they recognize] how the one God is *intrinsecated and extrinsecated into all*, and contains and preserves all without division of Himself or passion, most easily; and how to *ideate* suits the imaginer more truly [than to receive] all [things]. In the Metaphysical [register, the mind] approaches divinity more closely by investigating the inward processions, the essentiations, and the emanations. In the Archetypal [register] at last [it grasps] the one essence of divinity, the threefold property, and the powers, and the dependencies of all *from Him, through Him, as in Him*. And our Mind is glorified in the intuition, comprehension, and fruition of this by elevation.

## Structural Observation

The four-register ascent is a Christian-Platonist staircase. Each register answers to a faculty and a mode:

| Register | What the mind grasps | Mode |
|---|---|---|
| Material (`materialis`) | One source, no distinctions yet; multiplicity comes from outside | recognition of unity-pre-distinction |
| Mathematical (`Mathematicus`) | God's intrinsecation-and-extrinsecation into all things; ideation as the imaginer's proper act | the immanence/transcendence pair |
| Metaphysical (`Metaphysicus`) | Trinitarian processions, essentiations, emanations | investigation of inward divine life |
| Archetypal (`Archetypus`) | The divine essence itself; threefold property; "from, through, in Him" | direct grasp — quoting Romans 11:36 |

The closing line — *glorificatur Mens nostra in huius intuitu, comprehensione, & fruitione per elevationem* — uses *intuitu* and *fruitione*, the same vocabulary that the leaf-906 chapter on beatitude uses for the cognitive form of beatific vision. The ascent of Lib. X Cap. I culminates in the same act that Lib. XVII Cap. II Art. I names as `intrinsecatio`.

## Why This Matters for Encounter 001

The reciprocity is now legible. At the *Mathematical* register, God *intrinsecates and extrinsecates* into things — He is at once inside them and outside them. At the *Archetypal* climax, the mind achieves *intuitus / comprehensio / fruitio* of the divine essence. Then in Lib. XVII Cap. II Art. I, this culminating mind-act is given its proper name: *intrinsecatio*, "through which one becomes the other." The mind's intrinsecation into God mirrors God's intrinsecation into creation. Each is a movement of becoming-one-with that does not cancel the difference (the ablative-and-accusative chiasmus from encounter 001 says this for the soul; "absque sui divisione & passione" says it for God).

This is the Christian-Platonist double-movement Cassirer's Spinoza-prefiguration reading needs as its background. It is the same doctrine Cusanus calls *coincidentia oppositorum*; the same doctrine Aquinas summarizes as *Deus est intus omnibus, et extra omnia*; the same doctrine Bruno radicalizes into infinite indwelling. Campanella's contribution is the substantive coinage — *intrinsecatio* as a single technical term that names a cognitive act on the mind's side that ontologically mirrors the divine act on God's side.

- The phrase `& quomodo ideare imaginanti convenit verius omnia` is grammatically dense; the working translation treats `verius` adverbially modifying `convenit` and reads the structure as `ideare convenit imaginanti verius [quam recipere] omnia`, but the precise force should be re-checked once we have other Campanella passages on `ideare`.
