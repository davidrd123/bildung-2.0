## Latin

Unum ex Dominationibus systemata mundialia omnia regere tanquam Dei Vicarium; sydera centralia prima singula singulis Dominationibus gubernari. Horum Planetas Virtutibus; horum Lunas Potestatibus. Corpora vero elementaria ab eisdem et a Principatibus, et Archangelis, et Angelis; singulas species rerum in eis ab iisdem, et individua quomodo. Articulus III.

Profecto si unus est Mundus et Deus, pro Maiestate utitur Ministris, oportet unam intelligentiam toti Mundo praesidere, quam veteres vocaverunt Animam Mundi, praesertim Platonici et sanctus Basilius et Greg. Nyssenus et Augustinus. Hanc autem puto esse unum ex Dominationibus, ultimum erga secundam Hierarchiam. Hic quidem Dominatio non alicui corpori magno, ut caelo vel soli vel terrae, assistit, sed totum complet spatium Mathematicum, basim corporum omnium substentatam [?] et rationabiliter. Siquidem spatium omnia implet et penetrat corpora; et unitas deitatis per vicariam unitatem regit, non autem ex templo per plurimas, ut servetur ordo Monarchicus divinae unitatis repraesentativus.

Unde Philosophi doctiores unam intellectum praeficiunt cunctis corporibus; rudiores vero plures absque ordine ad unum, sicuti si rex in uno regno suo et distante, qualis est mundus corporeus in regione dissimilitudinis, praeponeret iudices in singulis civitatibus absque Prorege uno.

Si autem plures sunt mundi, ut Democraticis et Pythagoricis interdum apparet, tunc etiam Mentem unam sui ministram Deus dictis mundis praefecerit. Omnes enim isti mundi sub uno ambitu contineri oportet, si ab uno sunt Deo; ergo et ab uno vicario Dei. Ac quomodo una mens tot corpora magna regit, cum sphaera activitatis Angelorum unum mundum non complectatur, nec possit simul idem Angelus esse in caelo et in terra? Sed forsitan est in toto spatio ambiente caelum et terram; vel si in una parte per se per ministros extenditur in caeteris, sicuti Papa Romae assistens per Episcopos ubique terrarum totum gubernat Christianum orbem; et profecto nulla Resp. neque regnum tam belle gubernatum mundi repraesentat, sicut Ecclesia Christi.

Sed quod compleat spatium facilius coniecturo. Non enim Dominationes et primae Hierarchiae Angeli sphaeram activitatis habent quantulam ministri inferiores, sed longe maiorem. Trismegistus autem putat mundum esse in mente aliqua, et spatium pro mente ponit. Quod tamen non satis probatur, cum basis corporum sit. Disputavimus de hoc in 1. P. contra eos qui spatium faciunt Deum.

Sive autem sint mundi alii, sive quodlibet sidus suus mundus unus, profecto oportet distribuere suos singulis Dominationes. Cum enim compertum sit plura sydera parva circa magna circumferri, et iterum circa parva alia minora, consequens est ut gradatim regnent Angeli.

Et in mundo nostro, quod constat ex Sole et Venere et Mercurio et Tellure et Marte, et Iove et Saturno Solem circundantibus, a quo convertuntur in suos gyros, solis radiorum effluxionibus et spatio, ubicunque tangunt, quidquid tangunt, convertentibus quorsum ipse se convertit Sol; utique ordo est ut in Sole ipso unus Angelus vocatus Dominatio principetur. In stellis vero errantibus circa Solem intra certos terminos, prout medietas disci solaris, quae porrecta foras plus movere potest quam partes obliquae et laterales, cunctis scilicet Planetis dictis sigillatim cuilibet suus unus assistat Virtus.

Unde Iesus de Novissimis temporibus loquens dixit: virtutes caelorum movebuntur. Virtus ergo a Dominatione regulatur in existentiis, sicut facultas a primalitate in rerum essentiis. Ac Planetarum Planetis, quales sunt quatuor Medicei in Iovis systemate et duo in Saturnio et Luna in Tellure, et alii invisibiles, sicut existimare convenit, circa Martem et circa Venerem et circa Mercurium, aliquando nobis, sicut et illi nuper manifestandi, dabimus unicuique suam Potestatem. Et quamvis Potestatis sit coercere hostiles Daemones, nihilominus ab hoc munere gubernationis non sunt exempti primores eorum; et cum depellant lunulae istae tenebras Planetarum, et in tenebris potentiae hostiles latitent, ut dixit Iesus, non inepte in his ministrant Potestates.

Porro inter systemata haec parva insunt Tellus et Ignis, corpora prima; et in his Virtutes in esse conservantes magnorum corporum intelligimus; et praeter Virtutes adhuc insuper Potestates adversus cacodaemones, et insuper Principatus et Archangelos et Angelos. Etenim et secunda corpora magna in quibus aliquae universitates animatae servantur, habent suas virtutes et potestates et principatus et Angelos. Nam Apostolus horum inspector principatus et potestates aeris agnoscit, et Deum huius aeris, qui forsitan est primus cacodaemon. Et cum corporum ubique sint mali Angeli, et bonos in esse censendum est ad ipsorum expugnationem et correctionem.

Post hos sunt Angeli generationi et mutationi rerum assistentes, tanquam opifices. Et quidem cuilibet speciei unum assignaverunt Platonici et Christiani Theologi; et internos quasi praecedunt Archangeli tanquam magistri opificum dictorum. Supra Archangelos vero principantur qui proprii Principatus vocantur: hi regunt [causationes?][?] et necessitatis influxum inter eos. Archangeli vero ordinem Causativum et Fatum, quo coordinantur. Angeli exequuntur harmonice iuxta praefixas ideas ad certos fines in cunctis generibus et speciebus.

## English

That one from the Dominations rules all worldly systems as Vicar of God; that the first central stars, each one singly, are governed by single Dominations. Their Planets [are governed] by Virtues; their Moons by Powers. But elementary bodies [are governed] by the same and by Principalities, Archangels, and Angels; and how the single species of things in them [are governed] by the same, and individuals. Article III.

Indeed, if there is one World and God uses Ministers for his Majesty, it is necessary that one intelligence preside over the whole World, which the ancients called the Soul of the World, especially the Platonists and St. Basil, Gregory of Nyssa, and Augustine. I think this is one from the Dominations, the last toward the second Hierarchy. This Domination indeed does not assist some great body, such as heaven or the sun or earth, but completes the whole Mathematical space, [as] the basis of all bodies [sustained?][?] and rationally. Since space fills all things and penetrates bodies, the unity of deity rules through a vicarious unity, not from the temple through many [rulers], so that the Monarchic order representing divine unity may be preserved.

Hence the more learned Philosophers set one intellect over all bodies; but the cruder [set] many without order toward one, just as if a king in his one and distant kingdom, such as the corporeal world is in the region of dissimilarity, appointed judges in single cities without one Pro-king.

But if there are many worlds, as sometimes appears to the Democriteans and Pythagoreans, then God will also have set one Mind, his minister, over the said worlds. For all those worlds must be contained under one surrounding ambit, if they are from one God; therefore also [they are ruled] by one vicar of God. And how does one mind rule so many great bodies, when the sphere of Angels' activity does not embrace one world, nor can the same Angel be at once in heaven and on earth? But perhaps it is in the whole space surrounding heaven and earth; or if it is in one part through itself, it is extended in the others through ministers, just as the Pope, assisting at Rome, governs the whole Christian world through Bishops everywhere on earth. And indeed no Republic or kingdom represents the world so beautifully governed as the Church of Christ.

But I would more easily conjecture that it completes space. For Dominations and Angels of the first Hierarchy do not have a sphere of activity as small as the lower ministers, but a far greater one. Trismegistus, however, thinks the world is in some mind and puts space in place of mind. Yet this is not proved enough, since [space] is the basis of bodies. We disputed about this in Part 1 against those who make space God.

But whether there are other worlds, or whether any given star is its own one world, it is certainly necessary to distribute its Dominations to each. For since it has been discovered that many small stars are carried around great ones, and again smaller ones around small ones, it follows that Angels reign by degrees.

And in our world, which consists of the Sun and Venus and Mercury and Earth and Mars, and Jupiter and Saturn surrounding the Sun, from which they are turned into their own circuits, with the outflows of the sun's rays and space turning wherever they touch, whatever they touch, in the direction in which the Sun itself turns: certainly the order is that in the Sun itself one Angel, called a Domination, should hold rule. But in the wandering stars around the Sun within certain bounds, insofar as the middle of the solar disk, extended outward, can move more than the oblique and lateral parts, namely in all the said Planets one Virtue should assist each individually.

Hence Jesus, speaking of the Last times, said: the powers of the heavens will be moved. Therefore Virtue is regulated by Domination in existences, just as faculty [is regulated] by primality in the essences of things. And to the Planets' planets, such as the four Medicean [stars] in the system of Jupiter, and two in the Saturnian [system], and the Moon in Earth, and other invisible ones, as it is fitting to think, around Mars and around Venus and around Mercury, to be manifested to us sometime, as those [were] recently, we will give to each its own Power. And although it belongs to Power to restrain hostile Demons, nevertheless their foremost [members] are not exempt from this office of governance; and since these little moons dispel the shadows of the Planets, and hostile powers lurk in shadows, as Jesus said, Powers do not minister unsuitably in these.

Furthermore, among these small systems are Earth and Fire, the first bodies; and in these we understand Virtues conserving the being of great bodies; and besides Virtues, also Powers against cacodemons, and further Principalities and Archangels and Angels. For also the secondary great bodies in which some animated universals are preserved have their own Virtues, Powers, Principalities, and Angels. For the Apostle, inspector of these, acknowledges principalities and powers of the air, and the God of this air, who perhaps is the first cacodemon. And since evil Angels are everywhere [among] bodies, it must also be judged that good ones exist for their expugnation and correction.

After these are Angels assisting the generation and mutation of things, as workers. And indeed Platonists and Christian Theologians assigned one to each species; and Archangels, as if internal, precede them as masters of the said workers. Above Archangels, however, those who are properly called Principalities hold rule: these govern [causations?][?] and the influx of necessity among them. Archangels [govern] the Causative order and Fate, by which they are coordinated. Angels execute harmonically according to prefixed ideas toward certain ends in all genera and species.

## Note

This part makes the gap's astronomical and angelological payoff explicit. Campanella maps angelic offices onto the post-Galilean sky: the Sun has a Domination, planets have Virtues, moons have Powers, and even the Medicean stars enter the angelic governance scheme. The world-soul is not absorbed into God; it is treated as a vicarial Domination ruling mathematical space and worldly systems under divine monarchy.
