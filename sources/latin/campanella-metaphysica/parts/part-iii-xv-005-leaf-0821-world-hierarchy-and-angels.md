## Latin

[Fragmentum continuans folium praecedens:] intelligeremus situm non esse essentiativum rerum ita, ut non sit tellus nisi in suo situ tellus, neque ignis nisi in hac peripheria; et quomodo propensiones contrariae pariunt difficultatem moventibus et mobilibus. Ceterum idem unusque est mundus materialis et situalis, et iste illius ornatus. Quapropter licet ille prior sit, non tamen istum gubernat dirigitque ad proprium finem, sed solummodo basim existentiae et coessentiationem praebet.

Verum autem mundus materialis sit propter Mathematicum, aegre novimus. Etenim Mathematicus videtur quidem prior ut basis huius, non ut dirigens hunc gubernansque propter se; et tamen gaudet continere hunc, ut materia formas; et propter se invicem ista tria videntur esse et copulari mutuis emolumentis. Dant enim alii basim existentiae, alii ornatum suis basibus et complementum. Haec tamen omnia videntur esse propter Mentalem Mundum, a quo reguntur, sicuti omnia corpora nostra a nostris animabus.

Copulantur quidem symbolis Entitatibus. Etenim materia formis dat existentiae locum. Formae autem materiae ornatum, et similes sunt illi istae per formas materiales. Absque enim figuris, dilatationibus, constrictionibus, et numeris, divisionibusque, quibus materia formis assimilatur, per formas nimirum materiales immaterialibus, non fieret copula. Neque materia spatio uniretur, nisi per dimensiones, in utrisque [consistentissimas?][?], sed ibi corporaliter, hic incorporaliter sese commensurantes mutuo. At neque mens Mathematico et corporali mundo uniretur, nisi in his etiam tincturas veluti primalitatum propriarum agnosceret. Copulantur autem Archetypo per apprehensionem potestativi, per cognoscitivi visionem, et volitivi desiderium. Ideae autem Archetypi mundi maxime sunt causa copulationis mentium et aliorum mundorum ob similitudinem ex eis protensam usque ad corpora corporumque formas.

Manifestat autem hoc nedum natura, sibi in cunctis systematis consimilis, sed etiam Ars. Quandoquidem nihil ipsa facit, quin et natura ipsam imitetur. Videmus enim piscem catenam, et loricam, et Episcopum, similes catenis et Loricis et Episcopis, arte humana prius factis; et sepias et pileos et novaculam marinam, similes atramentario et pileo et novaculae artis. Idem in herbis et lapidibus experiere. Non absque admiratione nuper vidimus herbam repraesentantem totam Christi passionem, in foliis crucem, in floribus clavos tres ex centro egredientes, in limbo coronam spineam, in stipite lanceas, ex internodiis vero funes, quibus ligatus fuit Christus, exeuntes; et nuper in Germania natus est puer cum corona spinea et stigmatibus Christi.

Unde vis ideativa et imaginativa toti naturae indita videtur copulans formansque res omnes. Neque enim casu res viles fiunt, sed prius praeconcipiente virtute artificiosissima vitali res omnes permeante et ideante, ut Gregorius Nyssenus monet: ex illa nimirum Sapientia, quae omnium est artifex, omnem habens virtutem, attingente a fine usque ad finem fortiter, et disponente omnia suaviter, ut ait Salomon; et ex illo spiritu, quo emisso omnia creantur, et renovatur facies terrae, et quo ablato deficiunt, sicut David canebat.

Igitur mundus mentalis regitur a mundo Archetypo propter ipsius Archetypi gloriam, non propter indigentiam, sed propter indigentiam mentalis. Mentalis autem ob sui gloriam, non propter indigentiam, regit corporalem; gloria autem est [facere?][?] et causando Deum [imitari?][?]. Omne ergo posterius ad prius ordinatur, et a priori regitur primo; omne autem prius ad primum.

Distinctio Angelorum assistentium et ministrantium, et de ipsorum officiis sigillatim; et num aliqui cadere possint qui mundo Archetypo assistunt, et utrum sint tot gradus inter supremum Angelum et Deum, quot inter infimam creaturam et nihilum. Cap. II. Artic. I.

Propterea igitur Daniel vidit alios Angelos assistentes Deo, alios vero ministrantes. Assistunt enim contemplando in Archetypo arcana deitatis; ministrant exprimendo ea in corporali mundo. Assistunt dum reguntur; ministrant dum regunt. Cum vero ministrant non impediuntur a contemplando, quoniam contemplatio est regula ministerii, et prout vident, ita operantur, ut D. Thomas et Iamblichus etiam docuere.

Sed profecto quorum nomina non dicunt respectum ad exteriora, sed primalitates tantum ut a Deo sunt, illi dicenda sunt assistere, id est Seraphim, Cherubim, et Throni: quorum est Deo inhaerere per amorem, per scientiam et per potentiam. In Throno enim divina maiestas sedet, iudicat, quiescit. Dominationes autem videntur ministrare: dominari enim super quoscunque est respectivum ad subditos. Virtutum quoque est operari; Potestatum pugnare contra adversarios et custodire suos. Principatuum est primum esse in operibus gubernationem exequentium. Archangelum est esse principalem inter missos ad operandum iuxta iussiones divinas. Angelum vero est simpliciter in ministerium mitti.

Dominationes ergo Monarchae sunt ac reges independentes ab omni ministrante, dependentes autem solum ab assistentibus, praesertim a Thronis, in quibus iudicantur quae sunt agenda decretis divinis. Dominationes vero imperant quae iudicata sunt; Virtutes exequuntur; Potestates tollunt impedienda; Principatus operantibus praesunt; Archangeli dirigunt opifices; Angeli operantur tanquam ministri infimi erga singularia.

## English

[Fragment continuing the preceding leaf:] we understood that situation is not essentiative of things in such a way that earth would not be earth except in its own situation, nor fire except in this periphery; and how contrary propensions produce difficulty for movers and mobiles. For the rest, the material and situational world is one and the same, and this [situational world] is the ornament of that [material world]. Therefore, although that one is prior, it does not govern this one and direct it to its proper end, but only provides the basis of existence and coessentiation.

But whether the material world exists for the sake of the Mathematical, we hardly know. For the Mathematical does indeed seem prior as the basis of this one, not as directing and governing this one for its own sake; and yet it rejoices to contain this one, as matter [contains] forms. And these three seem to exist and to be coupled for one another through mutual advantages. For some give the basis of existence, others ornament and complement to their bases. Yet all these seem to exist for the sake of the Mental World, by which they are ruled, just as all our bodies [are ruled] by our souls.

They are coupled by symbolic Entities. For matter gives to forms the place of existence. Forms, however, give ornament to matter, and these are similar to that through material forms. For without figures, dilatations, constrictions, numbers, and divisions, by which matter is assimilated to forms, namely through material forms to immaterial ones, the coupling would not happen. Nor would matter be united to space except through dimensions, [most consistent?][?] in both, but there corporeally, here incorporeally, mutually commensurating themselves. But neither would mind be united to the Mathematical and corporeal world unless it recognized in these too something like tinctures of its own primalities. They are coupled to the Archetype, however, through apprehension of the potestative, through vision of the cognitive, and through desire of the volitive. But the Ideas of the Archetypal world are especially the cause of the coupling of minds and of the other worlds, because of the likeness stretched from them all the way to bodies and bodily forms.

Nature, not only similar to itself in all systems, manifests this; so also does Art. Since Art makes nothing that Nature does not also imitate. For we see the chain-fish, the cuirass-fish, and the bishop-fish, similar to chains, cuirasses, and bishops first made by human art; and cuttlefish, hats, and the sea-razor, similar to the inkwell, the hat, and the razor of art. You will experience the same in plants and stones. Recently, not without admiration, we saw a plant representing the whole Passion of Christ: in its leaves the cross, in its flowers three nails going out from the center, in its border the crown of thorns, in its stem lances, and from the internodes coming out the ropes with which Christ was bound; and recently in Germany a boy was born with the crown of thorns and the stigmata of Christ.

Hence an ideative and imaginative force placed in all nature seems to couple and form all things. For base things do not happen by chance, but with a most artful vital power first preconceiving, permeating all things and ideating, as Gregory of Nyssa warns: from that Wisdom, namely, which is the artisan of all things, having every virtue, reaching strongly from end to end and disposing all things sweetly, as Solomon says; and from that spirit which, when sent forth, creates all things and renews the face of the earth, and when taken away, they fail, as David used to sing.

Therefore the mental world is ruled by the Archetypal world for the glory of the Archetype itself, not because of [the Archetype's] need, but because of the need of the mental [world]. But the Mental [world], for its own glory, not because of need, rules the corporeal; glory, however, is to [act?][?] and, by causing, to [imitate?][?] God. Therefore everything later is ordered to what is prior, and is first ruled by what is prior; but everything prior [is ordered] to the first.

The distinction of assisting and ministering Angels, and of their offices one by one; and whether any can fall who assist the Archetypal world, and whether there are as many grades between the highest Angel and God as between the lowest creature and nothing. Chapter II. Article I.

Therefore Daniel saw some Angels assisting God, and others ministering. For they assist by contemplating the secrets of deity in the Archetype; they minister by expressing them in the corporeal world. They assist while they are ruled; they minister while they rule. But when they minister they are not impeded from contemplating, since contemplation is the rule of ministry, and as they see, so they operate, as St. Thomas and Iamblichus also taught.

But in fact those whose names do not speak a respect toward exterior things, but only primalities as they are from God, should be said to assist, that is, Seraphim, Cherubim, and Thrones; their office is to adhere to God through love, through science, and through power. For on the Throne the divine majesty sits, judges, and rests. But Dominations seem to minister, for to dominate over anyone is relative to subjects. It belongs to Virtues also to operate; to Powers to fight against adversaries and guard their own; to Principalities to be first in the works of those executing governance. It belongs to an Archangel to be principal among those sent to operate according to divine commands. But it belongs to an Angel simply to be sent into ministry.

Therefore Dominations are monarchs and independent kings from every ministering [order], but dependent only on the assisting [orders], especially on Thrones, in whom the things that must be done are judged by divine decrees. Dominations command what has been judged; Virtues execute; Powers remove impediments; Principalities preside over those operating; Archangels direct the workers; Angels operate as the lowest ministers with respect to singulars.

## Note

This part completes Article III's hierarchy of worlds and immediately turns it into angelology. The material/situational/mathematical worlds are not simply ranked abstractions; they are coupled by symbolic entities, dimensions, and likenesses reaching from archetypal ideas into bodies. The angelic hierarchy then maps the same governance logic: assisting orders contemplate the Archetype, ministering orders express what they see in the corporeal world.
