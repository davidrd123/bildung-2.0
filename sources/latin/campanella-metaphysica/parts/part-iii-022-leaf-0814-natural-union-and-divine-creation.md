## Latin

**Responsio ad propositas quaestiones. Quod mens unitur corpori propter bonum futurum, non praesens, ad secretam Dei gloriam; et non producitur a mente alia, sed a Deo. Articulus II.**

Mentem uniri corpori naturaliter, et tamen inferri a Deo, dicimus. Deus enim et natura sunt agentia subordinata, sicut principale et instrumentarium, a quibus idem effectus manat. Neque violenter immitti dicimus, neque sponte sola in corpus, sed naturaliter, quia sic nascitur et creatur a Deo ut in corpore humano habitet.

Finem propter quem vilissimo corpori Deus nobilem includit animam physiologus non intelligit, qui tantum esse et modum quo corpori inest scit. Fines autem et origines rerum metaphysicus investigat, theologia revelat. Videmus hominem esse animal religiosum et sapientissimum, eiusque intelligentiam superexcedere elementa omnia; intelligimus igitur a Deo ad sui gloriam conditum.

Cur in corpore claudatur naturaliter non intelligimus nisi ob bonum utriusque. Animae bonum est ut meritum acquirat serviens Deo in exercitio corporali, quod perpetua militia est contra mala obiecta malasque passiones. Corpori bonum est ut per talem coniunctionem et obedientiam finem sibi comparet altiorem et diviniorem quam sibi ex natura convenit.

Credita de corporis resurrectione et instauratione cum anima sunt revelata fides; naturaliter hoc non novimus. Naturaliter solum novimus animam post mortem corporis meliora sperare.

Anima, ut sentiat et intelligat, non indiget sensoriis neque corpore secundum esse suum, sed secundum quod clausa est corpore indiget sensoriis, fenestris, et foraminibus. Spiritus liber his non eget. Stultum est dicere mentem uniri corpori ut videat et audiat per oculos et aures, quasi homo in carcere poneretur ut fiat sapiens aspiciendo per cancellos.

Ipsa in aperto tota est sensus et intellectus undique, et post separationem longe melius intelligit et discurrit. Eius intellectio tunc est sapientia intuitiva, non per vitrum fenestrae sed per obiecti tactum internum aut per ideam a superis defluentem.

Deus propter exercitium et ludum suum hoc fecit ad meritum mentis humanae. Sicut solis calor in terra occluditur et multa entia facit sui oblitus ob supervenientes passiones, et tamen secreta vi semper sursum sapit et tendit, sic animus humanus, licet seipsum ignoret ob passiones additas, vi secreta Deum sapit et ad Deum contendit. Hoc est religionis initium et robur.

Animam a Deo derivamus. Licet Deus potuisset dare animae ut producat animam sicut spiritus spiritum et calor calorem, tamen formatio corporis non est a corpore formante sed a spiritu instrumentaliter operante, Deo agente tribus influxibus per ministerium angelorum. Superveniens mens igitur est a Deo, non a mente generante.

## English

Campanella answers that mind is united to body naturally and still by God. God and nature are subordinate agents, principal and instrumental, producing one effect. The mind is not forced into body, nor does it simply choose body; it is created by God in such a way that it is born to inhabit a human body.

The physiologist cannot know the final reason for this union; metaphysics investigates ends and origins, theology reveals them. Since the human is religious and wise, and its intelligence exceeds the elements, it is made by God for God's glory. The good seems twofold: the soul gains merit by bodily exercise, a perpetual warfare against bad objects and passions; the body gains a higher end through obedience to such a soul.

The soul does not need senses and body for its being or knowing as such. It needs sensory windows only because it is enclosed in body. To say the mind is put into body in order to see through eyes is like saying someone is put in prison to become wise by looking through bars. When free, the soul is wholly sense and intellect from every side; after separation it knows better, by intuitive wisdom, inner touch of the object, or idea flowing from above.

The hidden upward tendency becomes the beginning of religion. As solar heat enclosed in earth forgets itself through added passions but secretly tends upward, so the human mind, though ignorant of itself because of added passions, secretly tastes God and strives toward God.

Human mind is from God, not from parent-mind. The body and spirit are formed instrumentally, under divine and angelic action; the supervening mind is added by God.

## Note

The phrase to retain is that the enclosed mind `vi secreta Deum sapit`. This folds the earlier sapor/sapientia chain into religion: religion begins because the mind secretly tastes God even while buried in bodily passions. The separated soul's knowledge by "internal touch" also continues the intrinsecation thread without naming it.
