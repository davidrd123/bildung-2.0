## Latin

**Responsio ad propositas quaestiones. Quod mens unitur corpori propter bonum futurum, non praesens, ad secretam Dei gloriam; et non producitur a mente alia, sed a Deo. Articulus II.**

Ad primam quaestionem dicimus: Mentem uniri corpori naturaliter, et cum infertur, ergo non a Deo, negamus consequentiam. Deus enim et Natura sunt agentia subordinata, sicut principale et instrumentarium, a quibus idem effectus manat. Neque dicimus violenter immitti, neque sponte sola in corpus, sed naturaliter, quia sic nascitur et creatur a Deo ut in corpore humano habitet. Neque in institutione rerum quaeritur miraculum, ait Augustinus, sed quid Natura rerum habeat.

Quod autem Augustinus ait animas corpori naturaliter copulari quia amant regere corpus, Platonicum est. Si enim non praenoverunt cum infunduntur, ergo nec desideraverunt, nisi forsitan instinctu, ut aranea parvula statim ac nascitur non edocta texit retia, et nidus[?] ambulat; et hoc Danthes[?] annuit. Finis autem propter quem vilissimo corpori Deus nobilem includit animam, Physiologus non intelligit, qui tantum esse et modum quo corpori inest scit. Fines autem et origines rerum Metaphysicus investigat, Theologia revelat.

Quoniam ergo videmus hominem esse animal religiosum et sapientissimum, eiusque intelligentiam superexcedere elementa omnia, intelligimus a Deo ad sui gloriam conditum, ut etiam Philosophi et Poetae cecinerunt, et animam eius non esse ab elementis sed supra. Sed cur in corpore claudatur naturaliter non intelligimus, nisi ob bonum utriusque. Quod autem sit hoc bonum, videtur sapientibus esse bonum animae, videlicet ut meritum acquirat ita serviens Deo et in exercitio corporali, quod perpetua militia est contra mala obiecta malasque passiones, agnoscendo quae sint praeparata vincentibus bona; corpori vero, ut per talem coniunctionem et obedientiam finem sibi comparet altiorem et diviniorem quam sibi ex sui natura convenit.

Verum, quia ipsum quasi instrumentum est et propter aliud, non videtur aeternum. Quicquid ergo credimus de corporis resurrectione et instauratione cum anima est revelata fides. Naturaliter enim hoc non novimus, sed solum quod anima post mortem corporis meliora speret. Rerum autem circulatio aeterna, si vera essent, non nisi restaurationem indicaret ad hanc dispositionem, non ad diviniorem vel ad specificam. Dicimus ergo naturale esse quod facit Deus secundum consuetum ordinem. Et cum etiam ceterarum rerum formas neque ab elementis neque a generantibus fieri posse docuimus, proptereaque Angelorum ministerio ideas exprimimus, multo minus animas humanas a genitoribus esse putandum est.

Et addidimus: animam, ut sentiat et intelligat, non indigere sensoriis neque corpore secundum esse suum; sed secundum quod clausa est corpore, indiget sensoriis et fenestris et foraminibus.

Qui autem liber est spiritus, his non eget. Quapropter stultum est dicere spiritum aut mentem uniri corpori ut videat et audiat per oculos et aures, quia idem esset ac si quis diceret hominem in carcere poni ut fiat sapiens aspiciendo per cancellos, uti etiam sanctus Ambrosius et alii Patres docuerunt, et Platonici. Ipsa enim in aperto tota est sensus et intellectus et undique; et post separationem longe melius intelligit et discurrit; et eius intellectio tunc est sapientia intuitiva, et non per vitrum fenestrae, sed per obiecti tactum internum aut per ideam a superis defluentem.

Et quaerenti cur unitur corpori ubi minus bene sapit, nescio quid dicam nisi quod Deus propter exercitium et ludum suum hoc fecit ad meritum mentis humanae. Sic et solis calor in terra occluditur et facit Entia multa, sui oblitus ob supervenientes passiones, et tamen secreta vi semper sursum sapit et tendit. Sic etiam animus humanus, licet seipsum ignoret ob passiones additas, tamen vi secreta Deum sapit et ad Deum contendit. Et hoc est Religionis initium et robur, ut infra videbimus.

Ad tertiam, quamvis physice ita dicendum foret, tamen altius contemplantibus animae divinitatem, a Deo illam derivamus. Et licet Deus potuisset dare animae ut producat animam, sicut spiritus spiritum et calor calorem, tamen cum formatio corporis non a corpore formante sit, sed a spiritu, et quidem nesciente formationis partes et usus, sed operante instrumentaliter, Deo agente tribus influxibus per ministerium quoque Angelorum; et spiritus sit a generante, ut calor a calore effuso et corpus ex corpore deciso. Caloris enim est generatio, non corporis, ingenito spiritu. Formatio autem est ab idea. Propterea arbitramur et supervenientem mentem esse a Deo, et non a Mente generante.

Solis enim instrumentis primis, calori et frigori, dedimus sui multiplicationem, et quidem in virtute ideae primae, sicut in superioribus declaravimus, ubi etiam patuit quod homo generat hominem instrumentaliter, et caper caprum. Neque enim homo neque caper, et multo minus seminalis spiritus, scit formationem et partes et usus generati hominis et capri. Concedimus physice esse principales causas; sed cum videamus secundum primalitates esse insufficientes, necesse est addere causas principaliores. Homo ergo generat hominem quoniam semen emittit, in quo est spiritus, totius generationis instrumentalis causa viva adscita[?], quae etiam remanet ut anima perficienda tandem a Mente, a Deo postmodum indita, et non forte in ipsa seminatione, ut Albertus Magnus et Avicenna putant. ## English

**Response to the proposed questions: that mind is united to body for a future good, not a present one, toward the secret glory of God; and that it is not produced by another mind, but by God. Article II.**

To the first question we say: Mind is united to body naturally; and when it is introduced, therefore it is not from God. We deny the consequence. For God and Nature are subordinated agents, like principal and instrument, from which the same effect flows. Nor do we say that it is sent in violently, nor by mere spontaneity into the body, but naturally, because it is born and created by God in such a way that it inhabits a human body. Nor, Augustine says, is a miracle sought in the institution of things, but what the Nature of things has.

But what Augustine says, that souls are naturally joined to body because they love to rule body, is Platonic. For if they did not know in advance when they are infused, then they did not desire either, unless perhaps by instinct, as the little spider, as soon as it is born, without being taught, weaves nets, and the nestling[?] walks; and Danthes[?] hints at this. But the end for which God encloses a noble soul in a most base body the Physiologist does not understand, since he knows only the being and the mode by which it is in the body. But the Metaphysician investigates the ends and origins of things; Theology reveals them.

Therefore, since we see that the human being is a religious and most wise animal, and that its intelligence exceeds all the elements, we understand that it was founded by God for his glory, as Philosophers and Poets too have sung, and that its soul is not from the elements but above them. But why it is naturally enclosed in body we do not understand, unless for the good of both. What this good is seems to the wise to be the soul's good, namely that it acquire merit by serving God in this way and in bodily exercise, which is a perpetual warfare against bad objects and bad passions, by recognizing what goods have been prepared for those who conquer; but for the body, that through such conjunction and obedience it should gain for itself a higher and more divine end than belongs to it from its own nature.

But truly, because the body itself is as it were an instrument and exists for the sake of another, it does not seem eternal. Therefore whatever we believe about the resurrection and restoration of the body with the soul is revealed faith. For naturally we do not know this, but only that the soul hopes for better things after the death of the body. But an eternal circulation of things, if these things were true, would indicate only a restoration to this disposition, not to a more divine or specific one. Therefore we call natural what God does according to the accustomed order. And since we have taught that the forms of other things too cannot be made either by the elements or by the generators, and therefore that we express ideas by the ministry of Angels, much less should human souls be thought to be from parents.

And we added: that the soul, in order to sense and understand, does not need sensories or body according to its own being; but according as it is enclosed in body, it needs sensories and windows and openings.

But the spirit that is free does not need these. For this reason it is foolish to say that spirit or mind is united to body so that it may see and hear through eyes and ears, because this would be the same as if someone said that a human being is put in prison so that he may become wise by looking through bars, as Saint Ambrose and other Fathers, and the Platonists, also taught. For the soul itself, in the open, is wholly sense and intellect and on every side; and after separation it understands and reasons far better; and its intellection then is intuitive wisdom, and not through the glass of a window, but by the internal touch of the object or by an idea flowing down from higher beings.

And to one asking why it is united to body, where it knows/tastes less well, I do not know what I should say except that God did this for his own exercise and play, toward the merit of the human mind. So too the heat of the sun is enclosed in the earth and makes many Beings, forgetful of itself because of supervening passions, and yet by a secret force it always knows/tastes upward and tends upward. So also the human mind, although it is ignorant of itself because of added passions, nevertheless by a secret force knows/tastes God and strives toward God. And this is the beginning and strength of Religion, as we will see below.

To the third, although this would have to be said in this way physically, nevertheless, for those contemplating the divinity of the soul more deeply, we derive it from God. And although God could have given to soul that it produce soul, just as spirit [produces] spirit and heat [produces] heat, nevertheless, since the formation of the body is not from the body forming, but from spirit, and indeed from spirit not knowing the parts and uses of formation, but operating instrumentally, while God acts by three influxes also through the ministry of Angels; and since spirit is from the generator, as heat from diffused heat and body from cut-off body. For generation belongs to heat, not to body, when spirit has been engendered. But formation is from the idea. For this reason we judge that the supervening mind too is from God, and not from the Mind generating.

For we gave self-multiplication only to the first instruments, heat and cold, and indeed in the power of the first idea, as we declared above, where it was also clear that a human being generates a human being instrumentally, and a goat [generates] a goat. For neither the human being nor the goat, and much less the seminal spirit, knows the formation and the parts and uses of the generated human being and goat. We concede that they are physically principal causes; but since we see that they are insufficient according to the primalities, it is necessary to add more principal causes.
