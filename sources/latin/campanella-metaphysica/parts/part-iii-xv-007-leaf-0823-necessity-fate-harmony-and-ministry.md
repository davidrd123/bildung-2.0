## Latin

Qua ratione Necessitas, Fatum, et Harmonia, antequam effluant in ministros et in Mundum corporeum, praesint in mentali assistentium, attributa Thronis, Cherubinis, et Seraphinis. Item ministros ex ministerio lucrari apud Deum, et quod conveniat ipsis ad motum et quietem artificiosa agere corpora, et ad transmutationem et formationem per influxus magnos, quamvis ipsi non dent formas, nec transmutent propria vi. Artic. II.

Igitur primorum Angelorum est regi a Mundo Archetypo et regere mundum Angelicum; per eos enim ac hos influentia divina promanat. Et per Thronos Necessitas Entitatis et operationis et coexistentiae; per Cherubinos Fatum sive series et coordinatio ipsorum inter se et ad inferiora, Deo dante, intelligunt et effundunt; per Seraphinos vero Harmonia et concentus ipsorum inter se et ad inferiores et ad Deum tradatur; et hoc incorporali ratione, ex qua ad corporalem mundum deferuntur ob egressum, et cum egressu mentalis ad corporalem, mittente Archetypo Verbo.

Et quoniam missio divina non est violenta unquam, ergo cum voluptate, ergo cum emolumento missorum. Ergo in hac missione, qui dicuntur ministrare, beatitatis partem habent, dum scilicet essentiae participio participium etiam causationis adiungunt. Quidquid ergo non faciunt corpora, neque corporum vires calor et frigus per se in natura, illud efficiunt Angeli in hac egressione.

Dixit enim D. Iesus quod ingrediuntur et egrediuntur et pascua quibus in beatitate servantur invenient. Pertinent autem ad mentalem mundum et Angeli et animae humanae; nec propterea sunt eiusdem speciei cum Angelis, sicut putat Origenes. Gradibus enim specificis differunt, proptereaque istae in corpora illabuntur, illi vero in incorporeis permanent. Ergo mentes corporatae sunt in causa ut Angeli egrediantur tanquam ad cognatas res ipsarumque curam gerant. Nec quidem per dissimillima omnino sit egressio, idcirco tincturis primalitatum, viam parantibus, omnia corpora decorantur. Quemadmodum et mentes humanae corporibus per vias stratas primalitatibus spiritus; spiritus autem per sanguinem corpulentis colligitur.

Invenimus quidem corporibus motum et quietem ac simul artificium et rationem, qua moventur ad certos fines quos ipsa non amant, ut cum planta facit fructus propter hominem, dum propter se laborat. Inveniuntur et pugnae inter contraria, usquequaque effusis agentia viribus in invicem, ut sibi quodque pariat regnum; dumque hoc faciunt in rationabilem modum [ducuntur?][?] productionem, ita ut mirere quomodo in stercore vermes tanta ratione fiant, et ubique res omnes miro artificio, etiam ubi nulla providentia inesse videri quasi debebat. Ergo et ad motum et ad pugnam, quatenus effectum cum ratione sortiuntur, corpora ab intelligentiis aguntur.

Unde sanctus Augustinus administrari a Deo corpora per spiritus existimavit, et corpora crassiora per subtiliora; at quidem instrumentaliter. August. enim docet solum Deum agere et non agi, sola corpora, quo corpora, agi et non agere, Angelos vero agere et agi.

At quidem non modo rationem movendorum esse ab Angelis et faciendorum, sed ipsas formas videtur Avicebronti. Non enim forma contracta in materiam individualem potest multiplicari, cum incommunicabiliter subsistat eadem numero; neque species enim ab individuo; neque potest esse plus quam est individuum. Igitur a spirituali vi formae in corporibus imprimuntur. Et Avicenna et Plato et Trismegistus formas ab incorporea intelligente anima effluere statuunt: quod Aphrod. et Themistius et Simplicius aliquando probarunt cum intellectum activum faciunt divinum, ut in quaestionibus Philosophicis declaravimus.

Nos autem formationem rationabilem ab Angelis facimus iuxta ideas quas de Mundo Archetypo in corporalem deferunt, sigillantque per secundorum corporum formas, actas Necessitate, Fato, et Harmonia. Et quoniam omnia motu conficiuntur delectu virtutum, distribuere licet iam gubernatum Angelicum sigillatim.

Utrum autem mali Daemones formationibus rerum praesint sub ducatu bonorum, quemadmodum Platonici plures asserunt, inquirentes dubitavimus: maxime autem Muscarum, Culicum, Colubrorum, et earum quae Naturae obsunt humanae. Nec visum est absurdum eos Ministros etiam in his facere tanquam colligentes materialia et tanquam formantes, cum ideas in Deo non intueantur; et gaudere in eo ministerio quo nobis adversantes res producuntur et Deo per ministerium bonorum. Unde Belzebub forsitan ex hoc ministerio rex muscarum interpretatur.

## English

By what reason Necessity, Fate, and Harmony, before they flow out into ministers and into the corporeal World, preside in the mental [world] of the assisting [angels], attributed to Thrones, Cherubim, and Seraphim. Likewise, that ministers gain from ministry before God, and that it belongs to them to act artfully on bodies toward motion and rest, and toward transmutation and formation through great influxes, although they themselves do not give forms nor transmute by their own power. Article II.

Therefore it belongs to the first Angels to be ruled by the Archetypal World and to rule the Angelic world; for through them and these, divine influence flows forth. And through Thrones, Necessity of Entity, operation, and coexistence; through Cherubim, Fate, or the series and coordination of themselves among one another and toward inferiors, with God giving, they understand and pour forth; but through Seraphim, Harmony and their concord among themselves and toward inferiors and toward God is handed down. And this [happens] by incorporeal reason, from which they are carried down toward the corporeal world because of egress, and with the egress of the mental to the corporeal, the Archetypal Word sending.

And since divine mission is never violent, therefore [it happens] with pleasure, therefore with advantage to those sent. Therefore in this mission those who are said to minister have a share of beatitude, since, namely, to participation in essence they also join participation in causation. Therefore whatever bodies do not do, nor the powers of bodies, heat and cold, do through themselves in nature, Angels effect that in this egression.

For the Lord Jesus said that they enter and go out and will find pastures by which they are preserved in beatitude. But both Angels and human souls belong to the mental world; nor for that reason are they of the same species as Angels, as Origen thinks. For they differ by specific grades, and for that reason these [human souls] slip into bodies, while those [Angels] remain in incorporeal things. Therefore embodied minds are a cause for Angels to go out as toward kindred things and to take care of them. Nor indeed is the egress through things altogether dissimilar; for that reason all bodies are adorned with tinctures of the primalities preparing the way. In the same way, human minds are bound to bodies through ways laid down by the primalities of spirit, and spirit is bound through blood to corporeal things.

We indeed find in bodies motion and rest, and at the same time artifice and reason by which they are moved to certain ends that they themselves do not love, as when a plant makes fruits for the sake of the human being while it labors for itself. Battles among contraries are also found, agents pouring out their powers everywhere into one another, so that each may prepare a kingdom for itself; and while they do this they are [led?][?] toward production in a rational mode, so that you marvel how worms are made with such reason in dung, and how everywhere all things [are made] by marvelous artifice, even where it seemed as if no providence ought to be present. Therefore bodies are acted upon by intelligences both toward motion and toward battle, insofar as they obtain an effect with reason.

Hence St. Augustine thought that bodies are administered by God through spirits, and grosser bodies through subtler ones; but indeed instrumentally. For Augustine teaches that only God acts and is not acted upon; bodies alone, insofar as they are bodies, are acted upon and do not act; but Angels act and are acted upon.

Yet Avicebron seems [to hold] not only that the reason of things to be moved and made comes from Angels, but the forms themselves. For a form contracted into individual matter cannot be multiplied, since it subsists incommunicably, the same in number; nor [can] species [come] from the individual; nor can it be more than the individual is. Therefore forms are imprinted in bodies by a spiritual power. And Avicenna, Plato, and Trismegistus held that forms flow from an incorporeal intelligent soul; Aphrodisias, Themistius, and Simplicius sometimes approved this when they make the active intellect divine, as we declared in the Philosophical questions.

But we make rational formation come from Angels according to the ideas that they carry down from the Archetypal World into the corporeal, and they seal [things] through the forms of secondary bodies, driven by Necessity, Fate, and Harmony. And since all things are accomplished by motion through a selection of powers, it is now permitted to distribute angelic governance one by one.

But whether evil Demons preside over the formations of things under the leadership of good [Angels], as many Platonists assert, we doubted while inquiring; especially [over the formations] of flies, gnats, snakes, and those things that harm human Nature. Nor did it seem absurd that they act as Ministers even in these matters, as collecting materials and as forming, since they do not intuit ideas in God; and that they rejoice in that ministry by which things adverse to us are produced, and [yet] to God through the ministry of the good. Hence perhaps Beelzebub is interpreted from this ministry as king of flies.

## Note

This part ties the Part II great-influences vocabulary directly into Liber XV angelic governance: Necessity, Fate, and Harmony pass through Thrones, Cherubim, and Seraphim before reaching corporeal formation. It also keeps the ministry thesis precise: angels do not create forms by their own power, but carry archetypal ideas into corporeal sealing through secondary bodies. The Beelzebub passage is a first concrete demonological opening inside the gap.
