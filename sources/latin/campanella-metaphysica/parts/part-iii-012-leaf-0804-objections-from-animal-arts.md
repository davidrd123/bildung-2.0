## Latin

Anima per separationem a corpore non moritur, sed perficitur. Apparitiones animarum post mortem in totis transactis saeculis, ut ex historiis sacris et profanis scimus, animas vivere post corpus et semper probant.

Appendix. Cum operationes non cognovisset Aristoteles sed eas modo quae spiritui animalium communes sunt, et posuerit animam actum corporis, consequens est ut immortalem animam non cognoverit. Cum vero facit eam immistam, sumit etiam thesin Anaxagorae, qui de totius mundi autore loquitur. Solus Deus est ita activus quod nullo pacto passivus; alii vero intellectus omnes patiuntur et agunt.

**Rationes difficiles contra praestantiam et immortalitatem mentis humanae proponuntur dissolvendae. Caput V. Articulus I.**

Contra animae immortalitatem et divinitatem multa insurgunt argumenta. Primo, brutis insunt quaecumque exponunt hominem artes. Habent medicinam, clysterem, phlebotomiam, purgationem per vomitum, et oculos acuere marathro. Habent artem militarem pisces turmatim proeliantes sub ducibus et excubiis. Grues, corvi, apes, simiae habent politicam monarchicam; formicae democraticam; locustae anarchiam populariter viventes.

Fabricantur domos et nidos aves et apes, araneae retia. In venatione exercentur pisces, araneae, et ferae multae. Equi multi castitatem observant erga matrem, et omnia sciunt quae pertinent ad propriam vitam non secus ac homo quae ad suam. Quamvis animalia serviant hominibus, id accidit vilioribus, non generosis elephantibus et leonibus; hac ratione etiam homines viles serviunt nobilioribus.

Item habent animalia religionem: elephas baptizat se a coitu et peccato, adorat lunam sicut multi gentiles, et genua flectit. Herbae quoque religionem habent: heliotropium ad solem convertitur quadam religione; salices et clivaria ipsum consequuntur comarum conversione. Potest inter apes esse religio, nam et fucos habent qui sunt sacerdotes.

Praeterea quomodo scimus ea carere literis, religione, et scientia, cum videamus colloqui, mutuo dare et reddere voces? Formicas in aestate congregare pro hyeme; ergo discunt hoc ab aliis, et extat inter eos doctrina et religio. Salomon ad scholam formicae nos mittit.

Praeterea rationis participia esse omnia in Physiologia ostendimus. Utimur sermone et politia, quae absque ratione exerceri non possunt; utimur insidiis et stratagematis. Canis leporem insectans praecidit illi viam. Cancer lapillo conchilia aperta retinet, ut carne vescatur. Araneae plenae syllogismis sunt in captione muscarum, et vulpes insidiis contra gallinas.

## English

Caput IV ends by saying that separation from body perfects rather than destroys the soul, and that apparitions of souls after death, known from sacred and profane histories, continually prove post-bodily life. Aristotle failed because he knew only operations common to animal spirit and treated the soul as act of body; when he made intellect unmixed, he borrowed Anaxagoras's thesis about the author of the world.

Caput V then stages the hardest objections. If animal arts are like human arts, why infer human superiority or immortality? Animals have medicine, purgation, military discipline, politics, monarchy, democracy, popular anarchy, houses, nests, webs, hunting, and chastity. Their service to humans proves little, since lower humans also serve nobler humans.

The objection even extends religion to animals and plants. Elephants purify themselves, adore the moon, and bend the knee. Heliotropes turn to the sun; bees may have priests. Since animals converse and exchange voices, how do we know they lack letters, religion, and teaching? Solomon sends us to the school of the ant.

The strongest version of the objection uses Campanella's own pansensism: if all things participate in reason, and if animals use speech, politics, traps, and syllogistic stratagems, then humans may differ only by more and less, not by order.

## Note

This is a valuable stress test because Campanella lets the animal case get very strong. Caput II used animal excellence to sharpen human superiority; Caput V now uses the same material against him. The leaf also shows that pansensism is not a harmless ornament: it creates real pressure on the immortality proof.
