# Part III 008: The Human Soul by Right Metaphysics

## Latin

Anima non sapit sensu vel intellectu quatenus est corpus, alioquin non nisi corpora saperent; neque quatenus calor, alioquin frigus non sentiret neque spatium. Ergo quatenus ens, quoniam omne ens sentit. Accidentia autem non sentiunt, quia accidentia sunt; neque circulus sentit, sed res circulata; neque paternitas sentit aut alia relatio, sed res relativa.

Propterea dicimus animam esse aliquid subsistens in cunctis rebus. Nec quia subsistit est immortalis: nam et aer subsistit per se et lapis, nec tamen immortales sunt. Quapropter anima brutorum immortalis non est, licet subsistens.

Animae est scire, posse, et appetere quatenus est ens, non quatenus anima, quandoquidem omnia entia his primalitatibus constitui declaravimus. Quatenus autem anima est sentiens et appetens, ostendit se non esse relationem, neque actionem et passionem, neque formam passivam, neque temperiem, neque harmoniam, neque aptitudinem, sed ens subsistens vel inexistens activum, aliquid per se potens licet in alio.

Quatenus anima habet animare, est id quod dat alteri minus potenti, minus sapienti, et minus amanti potentiam, sapientiam, et amorem ab intrinseco. Respectu ergo corporis dicitur anima; respectu sui vero, ens.

Ex corpore et anima non fit unum simpliciter, neque compositum similare ex materia et forma, sed unum decompositum, ut molendinum ex aqua, lapidibus molaribus, et homine, aut textrinum ex texente, tela, filis, radio, et ceteris partibus. Quapropter frustra quaeritur unitas simplex in animali, in quo apparet multiformitas.

**Animam humanam esse immortalem secundum rectam metaphysicam.**

Superiora argumenta, quibus ostendimus hominis divinitatem excedentem animalia robustiora et calidiora, procul dubio ostendunt hominem non a sole et elementis tantum originem habere, sed ab ente diviniori, quod non sit contractum ad esse calidum, frigidum, et corporeum, sed in sola entitate permaneat, quae est potestas, sapientia, et amor.

Sed non adhuc immortalem; quia infinitus non est. Ergo potest habere id a quo corrumpatur. Illa autem consequuntur immortalitatem individualem quae carent contrario et subiecto. Propterea, si humana mens non est corpus nec compositum ex dissolubilibus, et caret contrario et subiecto, incorruptibilis sit.

Quod anima careat contrario patet: ex eius operationibus ostendimus ipsam esse supra elementa, et tamen constare ex primalitatibus sicut angeli. Primalitates non possunt dissolvi propter co-inexistentiam mutuam in invicem, identitatem realem, et quia sunt absque contrario.

## English

The soul does not know by sense or intellect insofar as it is body; otherwise only bodies would know bodies. Nor does it know insofar as it is heat; otherwise it would not sense cold or space. It knows insofar as it is being, because every being senses. Accidents and relations do not sense: the circular thing senses, not circularity; the father senses, not paternity.

Therefore soul is something subsisting in all things. But subsistence alone does not make it immortal, since air and stone also subsist. The souls of beasts may be subsisting without being immortal.

To know, to be able, and to desire belong to soul insofar as it is being, because all beings are constituted by the primalities. As animating, soul gives power, wisdom, and love from within to what is less powerful, less wise, and less loving. In relation to the body it is called soul; in relation to itself, being.

The animal is not one simply, nor a simple matter-form composite, but a decomposite unity: like a mill made from water, millstones, and a miller, or a loom made from weaver, cloth, threads, reed, and the other parts. It is useless to seek simple unity where multiformity is manifest.

The positive metaphysical proof begins from human divinity but does not stop there. Human excellence shows that the human being is not from sun and elements alone, but from a more divine being not contracted to hot, cold, or corporeal being. Still, because the human soul is not infinite, this does not yet prove immortality. Individual immortality belongs to things lacking contrary and subject. If the human mind is not body, not made of dissoluble components, and lacks contrary and subject, then it is incorruptible.

## Note

This leaf ties pansensism directly to the primalities: soul knows, can, and desires insofar as it is `ens`, not insofar as it is a special animal faculty. The key constructive move is that the animal is a natural decomposite unity, not a simple Aristotelian form/matter compound. The immortality proof now has its exact criterion: no contrary, no subject, no dissoluble composition.
