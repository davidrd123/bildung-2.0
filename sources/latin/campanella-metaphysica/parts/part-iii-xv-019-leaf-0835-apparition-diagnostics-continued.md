## Latin

8. Praefulgidaeque imagines. 9. Ignisque eorum indivisus, habens circa se vel ante se vel retro quam plurimam multitudinem. 10. Stabilitatis quoque particeps. 11. Fulgor eorum permanens, sed quasi in oleo. 12. Materiam consumunt paulatim. 13. Angelorum caterva vallati apparent praecurrentium, vel sequentium, vel circumstantium. 14. A Diis ostendunt se dependere. 15. Splendorem emittunt subtiliorem purioremque quam tolerari queat, non tamen adeo intolerabilem sicut Dii. 16. Praebent bona ampliora, sed particularia magis quam Dii, et cognitionem et potentiam non simpliciter, sed ad hoc, et ibi, et tunc, et hoc pacto.

Angeli autem: 1. apparent mitiores; 2. immutabiles, sed minus quam Archangeli, in magnitudine, forma, figura, et actione; 3. adhibent quieti et ordini motum moderatum; 4. pulchritudinem inferioris gradus sub Archangelis; 5. celeritatem cum motu et effectum post dictum; 6. forma et lux minor istorum, et divisa magis; 7. effigiesque eorum speciem servant eamdem, excepto quod a notitiae plenitudine agnitionis discretione deficiunt; 8. fulgidi apparent; 9. ignem divisum praeferunt, sed in ideis absolutissimis; 10. qui stabiliter movetur; 11. et permanet in alio; 12. solvunt a materia et ad sublimia revocant; 13. opera ordini suo congrua praeferunt; 14. declarant se ab Archangelis dependere; 15. tolerabilem cum suo lumine perficiunt aeris mixtionem, sacerdotibus attingibilem; 16. bonaque praebent determinata magis.

Daemones apparent: 1. metuendi; 2. mutabiles magnitudine et figura; 3. cum turbatione ordinem transgrediente; 4. cum pulchritudine in speciebus determinatis secundum rationes essentiae; 5. celeriores in actionibus quam sunt effectu et veritate; 6. in magnitudine et forma divisa magis et minori; 7. spectacula exilia et quasi caduca habent; 8. turbidum ignem; 9. divisumque magis; 10. instabilem; 11. mixtos in se habent Mundi vapores, ferunturque instabiliter praeter vel secundum Mundi motum; 12. diligenter exornant materiam; 13. boni Daemones opificia sua, et bona quae tribuunt, prae se ferunt; Daemones [ultores?][?] suppliciorum species, aliique Daemones pravi occurrunt circumdati feris letiferis voracibusque; 14. patefaciunt sese superioribus ministrare Angelis; 15. nihil eis apparentibus totus aer compatitur, nec fit circumfusus subtilior, neque praecurrit lumen aerem anticipans, circumlucetque minime.

Heroes: 1. verendi apparent minus; 2. mutabilesque, ut fere Daemones, alias aliter; 3. cum motu et festinantia; 4. pulchritudinem in determinatis speciebus secundum fortitudinis actum habent; 5. magnifice incedunt ad effectus, et tardius perficiunt quam Daemones; 6. angustioreque forma, aspectuque magnanimo; 7. exilioribus spectaculis; 8. mixtoque lumine ex pluribus; 9. igneque consimili Daemonibus, sed non aequaliter; 10. sed properantiori; 11. generativae spirituum compositiones eis admiscentur, circa quas et ipsi commoventur; 12. captant materiam, competentes ubique mensuras strenue commodeque ipsam curant; 13. tacet hic Iamblichus de Heroibus; 14. ostendunt se superioribus subiacere alia ratione; 15. cum apparent, plagae quaedam terrae commoventur, sonitusque circumsonat, aer non fit subtilior, accessibilisque est sacerdotibus. Dona Reipublicae terrenae fiunt.

Principatus apparent: 1. stupendi; 2. immutabiles; 3. cum stabilitate constantiaque in se ipsis; 4. cum pulchritudine principali et nativa; 5. cum authoritate et imperio veniunt ad agendum; 6. praegrandes et vasti; 7. spectacula faciunt perspicua; 8. splendent purius; 9. igne apprime lucido; 10. et quieto; 11. permanent eodem modo, mundanum quod habent ostendentes; 12. astant materiae supereminentes, et hoc pacto sese ostendunt; 13. provincias Mundi quasdam secum demonstrant; 14. praefecturam circa Mundum ostendunt; 15. circumcurrit eos visorum circumvolutio plurimorum difficilis toleratu, et volumen eiusmodi circa eos est mundanum.

Principes apparent: 1. noxii et infesti; 2. multiformiter variantur; 3. cum tumultu; 4. ficta pulchritudine adornata et elaborata; 5. cum emphasi agunt, et minori tamen effectu; 6. inflati, superbique et insolentes; 7. spectaculis obscuris; 8. splendore ex dissimilibus contrariisque confuso; 9. cum aliqua opacitate; 10. tumultuosi; 11. materialibus liquoribus pleni; 12. impletos se penitus materia monstrant; 13. asserunt materiae conditionem ordine carentem; 14. praefecturam ostendunt circa materiam; 15. volumen terrenum circa eos, neque supermundana neque elementari subtilitate.

Animae apparent: 1. similes Heroibus, licet minores; 2. mutabiliores; 3. cum minori stabilitate et ordine; 4. cum pulchritudine in rationibus determinatis, sed divisis magis, sub tamen specie comprehensis; 5. cum mobilioribus et debilioribus actionibus; 6. effigie inaequaliores et minores; 7. spectaculis umbrosis; 8. igne pleno ex multis generationibus admixto divisoque; 9. multiformique ex multis Mundi naturis; 10. contendunt trahuntque in generationem; 11. superfluis maculis alienisque speciebus repletae; 12. cum sunt purae, seorsum a materia; impurae, involutae materialibus; 13. purificata igneam ostendit figuram, puram et micantem lumine stabili, gaudens benefactis. Impura vero, labensque deorsum, trahit signa vinculorum et poenarum, et spirituum compositione gravatam, turbationibus materiae occupatam, seque subdentem imperio Daemonum generationi faventium; 14. declarantque se infima loca tenere; 15. aer eis connatus videtur.

Dii affectiones et amorem divinum animorum, et laetitiam, et perfectionem quasi conciliant; Archangeli contemplationem intellectualium summopere puram immutabilemque potentiam; Angeli rationalem cognitionem, virtutemque ordinatam; Daemones appetitum generationis; Heroes studium civilis gubernationis; Principatus motus mundanos; Principes materiales, animo naturae, operumque fatalium; Animae appetitum generationis, et studium corporum curandorum.

## English

8. and pre-shining images. 9. And their fire [is] undivided, having around itself, or before itself or behind, a very great multitude. 10. It is also participant in stability. 11. Their brightness is permanent, but as if in oil. 12. They consume matter gradually. 13. They appear surrounded by a troop of Angels running before, or following, or standing around. 14. They show that they depend on the Gods. 15. They emit a splendor subtler and purer than can be tolerated, yet not so intolerable as the Gods. 16. They provide more ample goods, but more particular than the Gods, and cognition and power not simply, but for this, and there, and then, and in this manner.

Angels, however: 1. appear gentler; 2. immutable, but less than Archangels, in magnitude, form, figure, and action; 3. they add moderated motion to quiet and order; 4. [they have] beauty of a lower grade under the Archangels; 5. swiftness with motion, and effect after the word; 6. their form and light [are] less, and more divided; 7. and their likenesses preserve the same species, except that they fall short of the fullness of knowledge in the discretion of recognition; 8. they appear shining; 9. they display divided fire, but in the most absolute ideas; 10. which is moved stably; 11. and remains in another; 12. they release from matter and recall to higher things; 13. they display works congruent with their order; 14. they declare that they depend on Archangels; 15. with their light they make a tolerable mixture of air, reachable by priests; 16. and they provide goods that are more determinate.

Demons appear: 1. to be feared; 2. mutable in magnitude and figure; 3. with disturbance transgressing order; 4. with beauty in determinate species according to the reasons of essence; 5. swifter in actions than they are in effect and truth; 6. more divided and lesser in magnitude and form; 7. they have slender and as if falling spectacles; 8. turbid fire; 9. and more divided; 10. unstable; 11. they have vapors of the World mixed in themselves, and they are borne unstably apart from or according to the motion of the World; 12. they diligently adorn matter; 13. good Demons display their works and the goods they distribute; Demons [avengers?][?] [display] species of punishments, and other depraved Demons come surrounded by deadly and devouring beasts; 14. they reveal that they minister to superior Angels; 15. when they appear, the whole air in no way suffers together, nor does it become subtler around them, nor does a light run before anticipating the air, and it least of all shines around.

Heroes: 1. appear less to be revered; 2. and mutable, almost as Demons, otherwise in another way; 3. with motion and haste; 4. they have beauty in determinate species according to an act of strength; 5. they advance magnificently toward effects, and complete [them] more slowly than Demons; 6. and with a narrower form and magnanimous aspect; 7. with more slender spectacles; 8. and with mixed light from several [lights]; 9. and with fire very similar to Demons, but not equally; 10. but more hurried; 11. generative compositions of spirits are mixed with them, around which they themselves too are moved; 12. they seize matter, and everywhere they vigorously and fittingly care for its competent measures; 13. Iamblichus is silent here concerning Heroes; 14. they show that they are subject to superiors by another reason; 15. when they appear, certain regions of the earth are moved, and a sound resounds around; the air does not become subtler, and it is accessible to priests. Gifts of the earthly Republic are made.

Principalities appear: 1. astonishing; 2. immutable; 3. with stability and constancy in themselves; 4. with principal and native beauty; 5. they come to acting with authority and command; 6. very large and vast; 7. they make spectacles clear; 8. they shine more purely; 9. with fire especially bright; 10. and quiet; 11. they remain in the same mode, showing what worldly [thing] they have; 12. they stand by matter, supereminent, and in this way show themselves; 13. they demonstrate certain provinces of the World with themselves; 14. they show a prefecture around the World; 15. a revolution of very many visions difficult to tolerate runs around them, and a volume of this sort around them is worldly.

Princes appear: 1. harmful and hostile; 2. they are varied in many forms; 3. with tumult; 4. adorned and elaborated with fictive beauty; 5. they act with emphasis, and yet with lesser effect; 6. inflated, proud, and insolent; 7. with obscure spectacles; 8. with splendor confused from dissimilar and contrary things; 9. with some opacity; 10. tumultuous; 11. full of material liquids; 12. they show themselves completely filled with matter; 13. they assert a condition of matter lacking order; 14. they show a prefecture around matter; 15. an earthly volume around them, with neither supermundane nor elemental subtlety.

Souls appear: 1. similar to Heroes, although smaller; 2. more mutable; 3. with lesser stability and order; 4. with beauty in determinate reasons, but more divided, yet comprehended under a species; 5. with more mobile and weaker actions; 6. in likeness more unequal and smaller; 7. with shadowy spectacles; 8. with fire full from many generations, mixed and divided; 9. and multiform from many natures of the World; 10. they strive and drag toward generation; 11. filled with superfluous stains and alien species; 12. when they are pure, apart from matter; impure, wrapped in material things; 13. purified [soul] shows a fiery figure, pure and flashing with stable light, rejoicing in benefits. But the impure [soul], falling downward, draws signs of bonds and punishments, weighed down by the composition of spirits, occupied by disturbances of matter, and subjecting itself to the command of Demons favoring generation; 14. and they declare that they hold the lowest places; 15. the air seems connate to them.

The Gods as if reconcile affections and divine love of souls, and joy and perfection; Archangels [reconcile] contemplation of intellectual things, exceedingly pure and immutable power; Angels [reconcile] rational cognition and ordered virtue; Demons [reconcile] appetite for generation; Heroes [reconcile] zeal for civil governance; Principalities [reconcile] worldly motions; Princes [reconcile] material [things], with the soul of nature and of fatal works; Souls [reconcile] appetite for generation and zeal for caring for bodies.

## Note

This part continues Article III's apparition taxonomy through Archangels, Angels, Demons, Heroes, Principalities, Princes, and Souls. The catalog is valuable because it gives Campanella a concrete diagnostic vocabulary for discerning mental orders: stability, light, matter, bodily atmosphere, sound, motion, and the kind of good or temptation each order brings.
