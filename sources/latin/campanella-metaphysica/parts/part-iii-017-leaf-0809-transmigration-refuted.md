## Latin

Si brutis cerebrum et instrumenta alia data sunt quae insipienti animae conveniunt, non in poenam sed secundum eorum naturam ita factum esse dicamus oportet. Poena et vindicta non est nec prodest nisi sciat patiens causam passionis culpam. Nemo hoc scit, nec lex Dei hoc docet nos in poenam corporatos. Moyses eorum animam ponit in sanguine et factam ex elementis; humanam vero a Deo inspiratam docet.

Mahometis dicta falsa esse credimus, quoniam in ceteris est falsus ac fabulosissimus. Neque immundis spiritibus credendum est, qui fingunt se esse animas defunctorum ut transmigrationem suadeant. Quando virtute sacramentorum coguntur, mox fatentur se esse diabolos inferni.

Blasius et Franciscus imperant animalibus in virtute Dei, cui omnia obediunt, ad miraculum et doctrinam nobis, ut nos factos Deo amicos cunctis posse rebus dominari intelligamus. Animalia pro nobis esse scimus, sicut et plantas pro animalibus. Ergo ad nostram correctionem ipsa corrigi intelligendum est; asinam Balaam loqui, serpentem vero Evae incluso daemone.

Maxime intelligendum est impossibilem esse animarum transmigrationem. Vel in omnia transmigrant vel in quaedam. Si in quaedam, assignanda sunt illa et ratio. Si commensuratio corporum id facit, anima elephantis non transibit in corpus formicae et apis, sed in cetum vel rhinocerotem aut struthionem. Sed elephantus sapientissimus est, struthio ignorantissimus, apis et formica sapientes. Ergo ratio non constat.

Quomodo potest commensurari tanta animae entitas tam exiguo corpusculo? Non est anima indivisibilis quantitative ut punctum, sed essentialiter, quoniam essentia eius una est. Essentia autem animae praescribit sibi corpus. Non enim anima est propter corpus suum domicilium et instrumentum, sed corpus propter animam.

Qua ratione immortales sunt animae boum et canum, eadem sunt formicarum et aranearum; ab his ad pulices, pediculos, vermiculos facilis est transitus. Ergo in has sordities immittemus animas humanas. Item eadem ratione in plantis, herbis, lapidibus, metallis fiet transmigratio, quoniam et in his sensus est.

Potest quidem Deus animas hominum in corpora brutorum immittere, sicut olim daemones in porcos; sed hoc non est secundum naturam quam investigamus, sed secundum iudicia Dei supra ordinem naturae consuetum.

Cum palam sit spiritum tenuem esse brutorum animam, et nulla operatio divinior doceat aliam mentem immortalem eis inesse, consequens est transmigrationem hanc naturalem non admitti. Homo dicitur fieri bestia moraliter, quando mores bestiales sectatur.

## English

Campanella rejects the idea that brute bodies are punishments for fallen souls. Punishment is useless unless the sufferer knows the guilt that causes the suffering. Neither experience nor divine law teaches that we were embodied as punishment. Moses places animal life in the blood and elements, while teaching that the human soul is breathed in by God.

He also rejects Islamic animal-paradise stories and demonic testimony. Unclean spirits pretending to be dead human souls cannot be trusted; under sacramental compulsion they confess themselves demons. Saints commanding animals, like Blasius and Francis, show God's power and human dominion under God, not transmigration.

Natural transmigration is impossible. If souls migrate only into some bodies, the rule must be given. If bodily commensuration rules it, elephant souls should move into large animals, not bees or ants; but elephants, ostriches, bees, and ants do not map cleanly by size and intelligence. A soul is not an indivisible point that fits anywhere. Its essence prescribes the body, because body is for soul, not soul for body.

The theory also collapses downward. If oxen and dogs have immortal souls, then so do ants and spiders, and then fleas, lice, worms, plants, stones, and metals, since sense appears in all of them. God could miraculously place human souls in beasts, but that is above ordinary nature. Natural evidence shows brute soul as thin spirit; no divine operation shows immortal mind in them.

## Note

The refutation depends on the same pansensist premise that created the problem. Because sense extends into insects, plants, stones, and metals, transmigration cannot use sensation as the boundary for immortal soul. Campanella's compromise remains: animal spirit survives naturally in air, while human mind has divine operations and a body proportioned to that mind.
