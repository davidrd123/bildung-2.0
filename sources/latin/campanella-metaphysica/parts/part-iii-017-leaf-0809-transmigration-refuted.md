## Latin

Nam et pueri ob spiritus crassitiem et cerebri carnositatem, alii vero ob angustiam cellularum et fuligines multas, sunt rudes non secus ac ferae. Ego vero, inquam, hunc sermonem esse divinatorium vel revelatum a Deo aliquo. Nam nos rerum naturas investigando non debemus aliud asserere quam quod in rebus videmus. Si ergo brutis cerebrum et instrumenta alia data sunt, quae insipienti animae [conveniunt?], utique non in poenam, sed secundum eorum naturam ita factum esse dicamus oportet.

Praeterea, poena et vindicta non est, nec prodest, nisi sciat patiens causam passionum culpam. At nemo hoc scit, nec lex Dei hoc docet nos in poenam corporatos. A Deo autem revelationem non habemus pro brutis. Sed Moyses eorum animam ponit in sanguine et factam ex elementis; humanam vero a Deo inspiratam docet. Christus vero in ipsorum salute nihil dixit, nisi quod provideat eis Deus sicut liliis et herbis quoad praesentem vitam.

Quae vero dicit Mahometes falsa esse credimus, quoniam in ceteris est falsus ac fabulosissimus; et culices, pediculos, et ranas beatificat in paradiso indignissime. Denique neque immundis spiritibus credendum est, qui fingunt se esse animas defunctorum, ut transmigrationem suadeant, quoniam in multis respondent falsum; et quando virtute sacramentorum coguntur, mox fatentur se esse Diabolos inferni. Neque puellae scirent ista fingere, neque atra bilis, ut fingit Aristoteles et Galenus. Nominat enim viros occisos quos puella vexata numquam audivit; et narrant quae illa penitus ignorat, et loquuntur peregrina lingua; et ego expertus ista loquor.

Blasius autem et Franciscus imperant animalibus in virtute Dei, cui omnia obediunt; et ad miraculum et doctrinam nobis, ut nos factos Deo amicos cunctis posse rebus dominari intelligamus. Scimus quoque animalia pro nobis esse, uti et plantas pro animalibus. Ergo ad nostram correctionem ipsa corrigi intelligendum est; et loqui asinam Balaam, serpentem vero Evae, incluso Daemone.

Sed adhuc maxime intelligendum est esse impossibilem animarum transmigrationem. Nam vel in omnia transmigrant, vel in quaedam. Si in quaedam, assignanda sunt illa et ratio qua in ipsa solum. Si enim commensuratio corporum id facit, tunc anima elephantis non transibit in corpus formicae et apis, sed in cete vel rhinocerotem aut struthionem. Constat autem sapientissimum esse elephantum, ita ut etiam litteras Graecas didicerit, si Plinio credimus; struthionem vero ignorantissimum, apem vero et formicam sapientes. Ergo potius anima elephantis transit in apem quam in struthionem. At quomodo potest commensurari tanta animae entitas tam exiguo corpusculo?

Si autem quis dicat indivisibilem esse animam, ac proinde posse in maximo et minimo corpore claudi, hic non videtur animae vim agnoscere, quoniam non est indivisibilis quantitative ut punctum, sed essentialiter, quoniam essentia eius una est, nil dissecta. Essentia autem animae praescribit sibi corpus. Non enim anima est propter corpus suum domicilium et instrumentum, sed potius corpus propter animam. Ergo, quoniam animalium alia est magnitudo et figura; figura autem exprimit affectum regnantis et fabricantis animae, ut dictum est, ergo magnitudo indicat entitatis et virtutis molem.

Nec quia homo est elephanto minor, non sit melior, quoniam figura hominis, et cerebri copia, et fabrica, et operationes eius praestantiam significant. Quapropter non videtur possibile quod anima hominis includatur in culice, neque culex in homine, sicuti nec ars citharizandi in acu, nec suendi in aratro.

Item, qua ratione immortales sunt animae boum et canum, eadem sunt et formicarum et aranearum, in quibus industria relucet multa. Ab his autem ad pulices, pediculos, et vermiculos facilis est transitus. Ergo in hasce sordities immittemus animas humanas. Item in ventre hominis generantur vermes multi, et in capite pediculi. Ergo multi erunt homines in uno homine, quoniam in omnibus illis est anima immortalis.

Item, qua ratione in vermiculis est anima humana, eadem erit et in plantis et in herbis, nam et haec animalia inversa et immobilia sunt Platoni et sensitiva Pythagorae. Sed hoc concedit Ovidius transformans homines non modo in bruta, sed etiam in plantas, Pythagoram sequutus. At cum et in lapidibus insit sensus, ut Magnes et Stellaria[?] ostendunt, et metallorum augmenta et vegetationes et sympathiae, etiam in saxa et metalla fiet transmigratio. Et hoc concedit Ovidius atque canit de multis, sicut de uxore Loth scriptura sancta testatur. Hoc autem naturale non est, sed miraculosum et divinum iudicium. Nam et in glacie et in balneo Sanctus Gregorius animas reperit, purgationis gratia.

Posset quidem Deus animas hominum in corpora brutorum immittere, sicut olim Daemones in porcos; sed hoc non est secundum Naturam, quam investigamus, sed secundum iudicia Dei supra ordinem Naturae consuetum.

Pythagoras autem in Legibus id docet et per Theologiam forte responsis Daemonum deceptus, et affinitate corporis brutorum et humanorum. Nam et amico meo Diabolus hanc opinionem suadere conatus est eo quod reperiantur homines rudiores brutis. Aristoteli autem convenit haec opinio ponenti aeternitatem mundi et animorum immortalitatem, et auferenti infinitum numero et magnitudine, sed tamen ipsam reprobat non advertens.

Praeterea videmus muscas et apes necatas mori aqua; deinde vero ad solem vel ad tepidum furnum reviviscere, quoniam spiritus eorum in aquam versus erat, nil laesis organis. At deinde a calore attenuatur, et rursus animat muscam. Tunc profecto oportebit animam immortalem, quae discesserat, redire in muscam. Quis hoc probet? Cur enim et in nos non redit? Et quomodo sex mensibus gelata manet in avibus Islandiae? Patet etiam ex urina in pulvere paulo post innumera nasci pulices. Ergo innumerae animae a Deo cur creantur, aut ab aliis corporibus migrant? Quod si extant animalia quaedam absque anima immortali, voluntarie affirmatur quaedam ipsam habere.

Tandem, cum palam sit spiritum tenuem esse brutorum animam, et nulla operatio divinior doceat nos aliam mentem immortalem eis inesse, consequens est ut transmigratio haec naturalis non admittatur. Sed dicitur homo fieri bestia, quando mores bestiales sectatur, forte apud Pythagoram moraliter.

## English

For children too, because of the thickness of their spirit and the fleshiness of their brain, and others because of the narrowness of the cells and many sooty fumes, are rough no differently from wild beasts. But I, he says, call this speech divinatory or revealed by some God. For in investigating the natures of things we ought not to assert anything other than what we see in things. Therefore if brain and other instruments have been given to beasts which [suit?] an unwise soul, surely we must say that this was done not as punishment, but according to their nature.

Furthermore, punishment and vengeance do not exist and are of no use unless the one suffering knows that fault is the cause of the sufferings. But no one knows this, nor does the law of God teach that we were embodied as punishment. From God, moreover, we have no revelation on behalf of beasts. But Moses places their soul in the blood and teaches that it was made from the elements; but he teaches that the human soul was breathed in by God. Christ, however, said nothing about their salvation, except that God provides for them as for lilies and plants with respect to the present life.

But we believe that the things Muhammad says are false, because in other things he is false and most fabulous; and he beatifies gnats, lice, and frogs in paradise most unworthily. Finally, unclean spirits should not be believed, since they pretend that they are souls of the dead in order to persuade [people of] transmigration, because in many things they answer falsely; and when they are compelled by the power of the sacraments, they soon confess themselves to be devils of hell. Nor would girls know how to feign these things, nor black bile, as Aristotle and Galen imagine. For [the spirit] names murdered men whom the vexed girl has never heard; and they narrate things she utterly does not know, and speak in a foreign tongue; and I speak these things from experience.

Blasius and Francis, however, command animals in the power of God, whom all things obey; and this is for miracle and teaching for us, so that we may understand that we, made friends of God, can rule over all things. We also know that animals exist for us, as plants exist for animals. Therefore the animals themselves must be understood to be corrected for our correction; and Balaam's ass spoke, but Eve's serpent [spoke] with a Demon enclosed.

But this above all must still be understood: that the transmigration of souls is impossible. For either they migrate into all things, or into some. If into some, those things and the reason why into those alone must be assigned. For if commensuration of bodies does this, then the soul of an elephant will not pass into the body of an ant or bee, but into a whale, rhinoceros, or ostrich. But it is agreed that the elephant is most wise, so that it even learned Greek letters, if we believe Pliny; while the ostrich is most ignorant, but the bee and ant are wise. Therefore the soul of an elephant passes rather into a bee than into an ostrich. But how can such great entity of soul be commensurated with such a tiny body?

But if someone says that the soul is indivisible and therefore can be enclosed in the greatest and the smallest body, he does not seem to recognize the power of the soul, because it is not indivisible quantitatively like a point, but essentially, since its essence is one, dissected not at all. But the essence of the soul prescribes a body for itself. For the soul does not exist for the body, its dwelling and instrument, but rather the body exists for the soul. Therefore, since there is one magnitude and figure for some animals and another for others, and figure expresses the affection of the ruling and fabricating soul, as was said, magnitude too indicates the mass of entity and power.

Nor, because the human being is smaller than the elephant, is he therefore not better, since the human being's figure, abundance and structure of brain, and operations signify his excellence. For that reason it does not seem possible that the soul of a human being be included in a gnat, nor a gnat in a human being, just as neither the art of playing the lyre [can be] in a needle, nor the art of sewing in a plough.

Likewise, by the reason by which the souls of oxen and dogs are immortal, those of ants and spiders are too, in which much industry shines forth. From these, moreover, there is an easy transition to fleas, lice, and little worms. Therefore we will send human souls into these filths. Likewise many worms are generated in the belly of a human being, and lice on the head. Therefore there will be many human beings in one human being, since in all those things there is an immortal soul.

Likewise, by the reason by which a human soul is in little worms, it will also be in plants and herbs, for these too are inverted and immobile animals for Plato, and sensitive for Pythagoras. But Ovid concedes this, transforming human beings not only into beasts but also into plants, having followed Pythagoras. But since sense is also present in stones, as the magnet and Stellaria[?] show, and the growths, vegetations, and sympathies of metals, transmigration will also happen into rocks and metals. And Ovid concedes this and sings of many cases, just as Holy Scripture testifies concerning Lot's wife. This, however, is not natural, but miraculous and divine judgment. For Saint Gregory too found souls in ice and in a bath for the sake of purgation.

God could indeed send the souls of human beings into the bodies of beasts, just as once [he sent] Demons into pigs; but this is not according to Nature, which we are investigating, but according to the judgments of God above the accustomed order of Nature.

Pythagoras, however, teaches this in the Laws and perhaps through theology was deceived by answers of Demons, and by the affinity of the bodies of beasts and human beings. For a Devil also tried to persuade a friend of mine of this opinion, because human beings rougher than beasts are found. This opinion fits Aristotle too, since he posits the eternity of the world and the immortality of minds, and removes the infinite in number and magnitude; yet he rejects it without noticing.

Furthermore we see flies and bees killed, dead in water, and then revived in the sun or at a warm oven, because their spirit had been turned into water while the organs were not harmed at all. But then it is attenuated by heat and again animates the fly. Then surely the immortal soul that had departed would have to return into the fly. Who would prove this? For why does it not return into us too? And how does it remain frozen for six months in the birds of Iceland? It is also clear from urine in dust that innumerable fleas are born a little later. Therefore why are innumerable souls created by God, or why do they migrate from other bodies? But if it is affirmed voluntarily that certain animals exist without an immortal soul, then [it is likewise arbitrary to say] that certain ones have it.

Finally, since it is clear that a thin spirit is the soul of beasts, and no more divine operation teaches us that another immortal mind is present in them, it follows that this natural transmigration is not admitted. But a human being is said to become a beast, perhaps morally among the Pythagoreans, when he follows bestial habits.

## Note

This corrected file preserves the refutation's downward pressure: if animal industry proves immortal soul, the argument spreads to insects, worms, plants, stones, and metals. Campanella can allow miraculous divine insertion of souls into bodies, but rejects transmigration as a natural doctrine.
