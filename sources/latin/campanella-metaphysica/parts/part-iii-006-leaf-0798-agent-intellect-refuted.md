## Latin

Cum ipsa sit substantia, ens, non perciperet substantias nisi esset de illorum natura. Sed recipiens denudatum intelligitur a natura recepti secundum id quod recipit, non secundum totum esse. Oculus albus est, sed albedine simplicissima, qualis inest diaphanis rebus; albedo autem quovis colore tingitur propter suam simplicitatem, similiter et lux. Potest ergo quod recipit non esse id quod recipit in eo gradu, sed in alio simpliciori.

Sensus igitur noster corporeus est in mediocritate virium, et percipit ea quae nos excedunt et movent. Cetera vero discursu a simili; qui autem simile novit is idem est qui discurrit ad ignotum, virtus videlicet sentiens.

Quoniam Aristoteles non cognovit quid deferat sensibilia ad sentientem animam, et quid ea de materia abstrahat, debuisset ponere sensum agentem sicut posuit intellectum, et longe magis. Nos autem lucem tingi vultibus rerum docuimus et in oculum insilire; odores esse tenuem substantiam ab exhalante calore delatam; sonum esse motum per aerem communicatum. Idcirco neque intellectus agens poni debet.

Ut enim abstrahatur ab individuis simulachrum, non requiritur opus, sed languor operis. Particularia sensibilia sensum movent; et quia particularitates sunt paucae, id vero in quo conveniunt est multum, quia in omnibus est, ideo remanet in sentiente virtute ipsum universale potius quam particularia. A longe video hominem et non Petrum, quia Petri singularitas, ut facies et color, non potest usque ad me suas vires extendere.

Abstractio ergo vana est. Nam et oculus videt colorem non viso sapore; ergo etiam ipse abstrahit. Et quare? Quia non est aptus pati a sapore. Sic sensus distans, quem vocant intellectum, moveri nequit a distantibus nisi languide.

Quapropter rationes Peripateticorum quibus probant animae immortalitatem vanissimae sunt et sibi mutuo adversae. Non probant subsistentiam animae dum eam ponunt ut formam. Qui ponunt ipsam abstractam a corpore non faciunt hominem intelligere, sed intelligi. Qui dicunt esse formam secundum essentiam, non autem secundum potentias, non probant quod volunt: potentia non potest ab essentia separari nisi cogitatu nostro.

Ratio Aristotelis, qua probat animae immortalitatem, nulla est. Si propriam habeat operationem in qua corpus non communicat, sequitur quod sit separabilis, non quod incorruptibilis. Falsum est etiam quod anima propriam habeat operationem in qua corpus non communicat: ipsi enim intelligere est pati; forma autem non patitur nisi compatiatur subiectum.

## English

Campanella argues that reception does not require the receiver to be identical with what it receives in the same grade. A white or transparent eye can receive colors because its own whiteness or transparency is simple; light too receives colors. The receiver may be of a simpler degree than the received thing.

Our bodily sense sits in a middle range of powers and perceives what exceeds and moves it. From the similar it reasons to the unknown; the same sensing power that knows the similar also makes the inference. If Aristotle needed an agent intellect because he did not know how sensible things reach the soul, then he should have posited an agent sense even more.

Campanella supplies physical media instead: light is tinted by the faces of things and leaps into the eye; odors are thin substance carried by exhaling heat; sound is motion communicated through air. No agent intellect is needed. The universal remains in the sensing power not because a separate intellect abstracts it, but because particularities are weak or few while the common feature is repeated in many cases.

Seeing "human" from far away is not abstraction by an immaterial faculty. It is failure to receive Peter's singular details at that distance. The eye also "abstracts" color from taste simply because it is not fit to suffer from taste. Distant sense, which the Aristotelians call intellect, is moved weakly by distant things.

The Peripatetic proofs of immortality therefore fail. If soul is a form, they do not prove subsistence. If they separate intellect from body, they no longer make the human being understand. If they split essence from powers, they split what cannot really be separated. And even a proper operation would prove separability at most, not incorruptibility.

## Note

The key methodological move is anti-abstractionist. Universality is produced by sensory weakness, distance, repetition, memory, and comparative operation, not by a separate agent intellect stripping matter away. Campanella keeps the becoming-the-object model, but relocates it inside a physically mediated sensing spirit.
