## Latin

Ad gubernatum hominum non modo bonos sed etiam malos Angelos destinatos esse: illos ad bonorum dispensationem, et imperandum malis; hos ad malorum executionem, et exercitium virtutis, et meritum.

**ARTICULUS V.**

Non modo autem Angeli boni, sed etiam mali pertinent ad gubernatum hominum ob duas causas: videlicet, ob exercitium virtutis, et ob iustitiam. Dum enim a malis alliciuntur ad sui cultum blanditiis, atque minis, vexationibus et muneribus, fit qui resistit fortis animo, et fidelior Deo, et eius implorat auxilium, et desiderium arripit revertendi ad suam originem. Item intelligit sibi meliorem deberi vitam supra corporalem, ad ipsamque erigitur. Si enim nobiscum negotiantur mentes abstractae, utique ad ipsarum societatem pertineamus est necessum.

Praeterea cum Angeli rectores hominum aliquid sunt executuri poenarum contra homines, et caeteras creaturas, utuntur cacodaemonibus, tanquam lictoribus, tortoribus, carcerariis, carnificibus. Unde quemadmodum non est bonum in nobis quod Angelorum ministerio non fiat bonorum: neque enim caelum, neque elementa intendunt nobis benefacere, et fortunationem, praesertim quae fit per doctrinam et virtute. Ergo oportet a causis intelligentibus fiant. Sic non est malum, quod Daemonum malorum arte et vi non infligatur, ut rectissime consideravit Chrysostomus in Matt. et Aug. in Psal. 77. et eminentiores Theologi: nam et Iob ab Angelo malo affligitur: et per Angelos malos plagae Aegyptiis infliguntur, teste David: et quae patiebatur fluxum sanguinis, a Iesu curata, testimonium habet, quoniam a Sathana alligata huic malo fuerat.

Ergo et Angeli, qui percuderunt Senacherib, et Sodomam et populum a David numeratum, fuerunt boni quidem, ut testatur scriptura. Sed qui iusserunt ex Dei imperio talia fieri: qui vero executi sunt malum, mali erant, unde docte Augustinus agnovit, omnem legionem Diabolorum ab uno Angelo bono corrigi, et nihil posse eos mali facere, nisi quod boni praecipiunt, aut sinunt fieri [in corporalibus?][?], prout in Archetypo mundo edocentur. Unde cum revelationem praeceptum non habent expressum, est inter bonos etiam concertatio voluntatum, et inter malos executio.

Neque enim Angelus regni Persarum obstitit Michaeli per se pugnando contra eum, sed subditi sui cacodaemones impediebant liberationem Iudaeorum: quam Michael procurabat, volente ipso Angelo, ut per Iudaeorum magisterium Persae, quos ipse regebat, docerentur doctrina Dei: cacodaemones vero de Iudaeorum vexatione solummodo delectati, imperium exequebantur non propter salutem Persarum. Quapropter nullum malum permittitur, aut imperatur a Deo, suisque bonis ministris, nisi propter bonum aliquod. Econtra vero executores mali nihil mali faciunt, nisi quia gaudent alienis malis; et cum aliquid boni faciunt, ob maius malum inde sequuturum faciunt.

Cognovit etiam antiquitas malorum inter se pugnam. Nam Martem, et Apollinem, aliosque Deos Homerus introducit faventes Troianis contra Graecos; Iunonem vero, Minervam, et alios Deos, faventes Graecis contra Troianos, cultum proprium utrosque praetendentes; et alios aliorum malum. Utitur enim Deus ad modificationem negotiorum mortalium bonis ac malis Angelis, et ipsorum pugna, per Necessitatem, Fatum et Harmoniam. Si quis recte intueatur quam similia sunt inter se omnia opera Dei, utque ab uno. Ergo mundus mentalis consimiliter ut corporalis analogice gubernatur: unde et Hierarchia humana Angelica similis est.

Hominem esse in Mundo mentali et corporali secundum Naturam, Angelos vero et diabolos in mentali secundum Naturam, in corporali secundum operationem: et qua ratione Natura non beat, nec existentia in mentali Mundo, sed author Naturae per auxilium supernaturale, elevans usque ad Archetypum, et de scala mundorum et Systematum.

**ARTICULUS VI.**

Porro, neque nobiscum [tractarent?][?] Angeli et Daemones, nisi nos pertineremus ad Mundum ipsorum. Quapropter in duplici Mundo est homo: secundum mentem namque nullo spatio corporali, nullisque parietibus continetur: sed simul est in Caelo, et in Terra, et in Italia, et in Gallia, et in America, quocunque mens sua ictu oculi percurrit, exparaturque intelligendo, et appetendo, et potendo. Secundum corpus vero non est nisi in tanto spatio, quanta est sua exiguitas: in carcere detinetur, et vinculis, ita ut non possit esse, neque ire eo, quo et ad quas res sua intellectio, et voluntas pertingit, saepe plus spatii occupare, quam termini sui corporis habent; animo autem occupat mille Mundos,

## English

That not only good Angels but also evil Angels are destined for the governance of human beings: the former for the dispensing of goods and for commanding the evil; the latter for the execution of evils, and for the exercise of virtue and merit.

**ARTICLE V.**

But not only good Angels, but also evil ones, belong to the governance of human beings for two causes: namely, for the exercise of virtue and for justice. For while they are enticed by evil ones to worship of themselves by blandishments, threats, vexations, and gifts, one who resists becomes strong in soul and more faithful to God, and implores his help, and seizes the desire of returning to his origin. Likewise he understands that a better life above the corporeal is owed to him, and he is lifted toward it. For if abstract minds transact with us, it is surely necessary that we belong to their society.

Moreover, when Angels ruling human beings are about to execute something of punishments against human beings and other creatures, they use cacodemons as lictors, torturers, jailers, executioners. Hence, just as there is no good in us that does not happen through the ministry of good Angels, for neither heaven nor the elements intend to benefit us and [give] fortune, especially that which happens through doctrine and virtue; therefore these things must happen from intelligent causes. So there is no evil that is not inflicted by the art and force of evil Demons, as Chrysostom in Matthew and Augustine on Psalm 77 and the more eminent Theologians considered most rightly. For Job too is afflicted by an evil Angel; and through evil Angels the plagues are inflicted on the Egyptians, with David as witness; and the woman who suffered a flux of blood, cured by Jesus, has testimony that she had been bound to this evil by Satan.

Therefore also the Angels who struck Sennacherib, Sodom, and the people numbered by David were indeed good, as Scripture testifies. But they were those who commanded such things to be done from God's command; those, however, who executed the evil were evil, whence learnedly Augustine recognized that every legion of Devils is corrected by one good Angel, and that they can do nothing evil except what the good command or permit to be done [in corporeal things?][?], according as they are taught in the Archetypal world. Hence, when they do not have an expressed command by revelation, there is also a contest of wills among the good, and execution among the evil.

For the Angel of the kingdom of the Persians did not oppose Michael by fighting against him through himself, but his subject cacodemons impeded the liberation of the Jews, which Michael was procuring, with that Angel himself willing it, so that through the teaching of the Jews the Persians whom he was ruling might be taught the doctrine of God. But the cacodemons, delighted only by the vexation of the Jews, were executing the command not for the salvation of the Persians. Therefore no evil is permitted or commanded by God and his good ministers except on account of some good. On the contrary, evil executors do nothing evil except because they rejoice in others' evils; and when they do something good, they do it on account of a greater evil that will follow from it.

Antiquity also knew the battle of evil beings among themselves. For Homer introduces Mars, Apollo, and other Gods favoring the Trojans against the Greeks, but Juno, Minerva, and other Gods favoring the Greeks against the Trojans, both sides putting forward their own worship, and some [seeking] the evil of others. For God uses good and evil Angels and their battle for the modification of mortal affairs, through Necessity, Fate, and Harmony. If someone rightly intuits how similar all the works of God are among themselves, and how they are from one. Therefore the mental world is governed analogically in a way very similar to the corporeal; whence the human hierarchy too is similar to the Angelic.

That the human being is in the mental and corporeal World according to Nature, but Angels and devils in the mental [World] according to Nature, in the corporeal according to operation; and by what reason Nature does not beatify, nor existence in the mental World, but the author of Nature through supernatural aid, elevating as far as the Archetype; and concerning the ladder of worlds and Systems.

**ARTICLE VI.**

Furthermore, Angels and Demons would not [deal?][?] with us, unless we belonged to their World. Therefore the human being is in a double World: according to mind he is contained by no corporeal space and by no walls; but he is at once in Heaven, on Earth, in Italy, in Gaul, and in America, wherever his mind runs in the blink of an eye, and is spread out by understanding, desiring, and being able. But according to body he is only in as much space as his littleness is: he is detained in prison and chains, so that he cannot be or go there, where and to what things his intellection and will extend, often occupying more space than the limits of his body have; but in soul he occupies a thousand Worlds,

## Note

This part continues the angelic-governance scheme into the role of evil angels. Evil ministers are not autonomous counter-governors: they execute punishments and temptations under permission, while the human exercise of virtue and desire for return to origin are sharpened through resistance. The end of the leaf opens Article VI and breaks mid-sentence at the mind's capacity to occupy "a thousand Worlds"; the continuation is leaf 0827.
