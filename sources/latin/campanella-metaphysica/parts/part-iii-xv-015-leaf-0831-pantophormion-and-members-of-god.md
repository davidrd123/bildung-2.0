## Latin

est creatus potius, et anima Mundi; cui subsunt omnes Dii, id est, Angeli. Facit deinde Iamblichus plures ordines iuxta signorum caelestium numerum, et facies 36. De materia quod sit a Deo producta itidem Iamblichus contestatur: de cuius parte vitali caelestes constituit sphaeras; alteram partem generabilia convertit in corpora.

Quid de comparatione membrorum, quasi Dei, ad membra Mundi Mercurius non recte.

**ARTICULUS III.**

Non videtur autem Mercurius recte vocare Dei membra Vitam, Immortalitatem, Necessitatem, Providentiam, Naturam, Animam, et Mentem: non quia hoc vocabulo utitur, cum non possit meliora habere, et ipsum per metaphoricam analogiam intelligat; sed quoniam vita immortalitasque sunt unum membrum. Quod enim per se vita est, immortalitas est, etiam formaliter consideratum, nedum materialiter. At Necessitas non membrum, sed membrorum compago dici poterat, ipsaque unitas summa. Et ista duo ad primam spectant primalitatem.

Providentia autem ad secundam, sed cum non respiciat exteriora quibus providetur, dicenda erat quatenus est, in sorte membri, potius Sapientia, qua providet, nisi velit haec membra non absolute, sed cum respectu ad exteriora agnoscere. Nam cum addit Naturam, Animam, et Mentem, esse Dei Membra, potius extrinsecus Deum quam intrinsecus considerat. Ipse enim Naturam elementis, Animam animalibus, Mentem hominibus largitur; non autem dat quod non habet: habet autem eminenter et causaliter, et hoc donum spectat ad Amorem, tertiam primalitatem. Ipse enim ex placito Dei necessitatem, ex voluntate effectus derivat.

Mercurium recte praefecisse Angelos rerum gubernatui, et Pantophormion formationibus, et qua ratione; non tamen sufficienter, nec secundum [nos?][?] nec secundum providentiam ad alia systemata.

**ARTICULUS IV.**

Cum mundi membris praeficit Deos, sub quibus Daemonas et Heroas agnoscit, tanquam executores divini operis; et Pantophormion in sphaerarum Republica, id est, virtutem formatricem, quam Colchodeam vocat Avicenna, nos autem Angelum praesidentem opificibus et formatoribus Angelis, bene quidem se habet. At et stellarum Angelos et aliorum systematum Gubernatores agnoscere debuisset. Recte etiam in Caelis tanquam in sigillis formas rerum videtur docere; in Angelis sicut in discipulis; in Deo sicut in praeceptore; in corporibus inferioribus sicut in receptivis; in calore et frigore sicut instrumentis; quae ipse non docuit, nisi de sphaeris et formatore, quem tanquam discipulum ponere oportebat. Neque formas illas caelestes, sive asterismorum sive Decanorum super 36 imaginibus Zodiaci, ita sese habere in Caelo putandum, ut non nisi instrumentalia sigilla sint inferiorum.

Etenim ad alias stellas et systemata aliam habent configurationem; et si qua Entia sunt in Luna et in Venere, aliam habent, atque nos, lucis participationem, cum unus dies Lunaris quindecim tellurales contineat, et variatiores, quoniam soli est vicinior in synodis et distantior in oppositionibus quam tellus; sic de caeteris. Et cum Saturnus Solem nobis quadrato respicit, alios Planetas non quadrato, sed alio aspectu respicit, ita ut mirificentissima sit sapientia Dei, quae eisdem instrumentis simul ad res innumeras et maxime varias concinne utatur; et eadem configuratio et constellatio alia sit singulis in scenis, in quibus divitias suae virtutis repraesentat per Angelorum ministerium et corporum instrumentationem Deus magnus Dominus, et Rex magnus super omnes Deos. Quapropter non solum admittimus Pantoformii 36 facies, sed longe plurimas. Utrum autem sint quales ponuntur Aegyptiis, Babyloniis, et Arabibus, nondum examinavimus; nec, nisi Deo revelante, scire potuisse putamus, quemadmodum etiam Plato de tota Astrologica re opinatur.

## English

was rather created, and [is] the soul of the World; under which, produced by God, all Gods, that is, Angels, are subject. Then Iamblichus makes several orders according to the number of the celestial signs and the 36 faces. Likewise Iamblichus bears witness concerning matter, that it was produced by God: from its vital part he constitutes the celestial spheres; the other part he turns, as generable things, into bodies.

What Mercury did not rightly [say] concerning the comparison of members, as if [members] of God, to the members of the World.

**ARTICLE III.**

But Mercury does not seem rightly to call Life, Immortality, Necessity, Providence, Nature, Soul, and Mind the members of God: not because he uses this word, since he cannot have better ones, and understands it through metaphoric analogy; but because life and immortality are one member. For what is life through itself is immortality, even considered formally, much less materially. But Necessity could have been called not a member, but the joining of members, and the highest unity itself. And those two pertain to the first primality.

Providence, however, pertains to the second; but since it does not regard exterior things for which provision is made, insofar as it is in the class of a member it should rather have been called Wisdom, by which it provides, unless he wishes to recognize these members not absolutely, but with respect to exterior things. For when he adds Nature, Soul, and Mind as Members of God, he considers God more extrinsically than intrinsically. For God gives Nature to the elements, Soul to animals, Mind to human beings; but he does not give what he does not have. He has it eminently and causally, and this gift pertains to Love, the third primality. For he derives necessity from God's pleasure, effects from will.

That Mercury rightly set Angels over the governance of things, and Pantophormion over formations, and by what reason; nevertheless not sufficiently, neither according to [us?][?] nor according to providence toward other systems.

**ARTICLE IV.**

When he sets Gods over the members of the world, under whom he recognizes Demons and Heroes as executors of the divine work, and Pantophormion in the Republic of the spheres, that is, the formative power, which Avicenna calls Colchodea, but which we call the Angel presiding over the artificers and formative Angels, this indeed stands well. But he should also have recognized Angels of the stars and Governors of other systems. He also seems rightly to teach forms of things in the Heavens as in seals; in Angels as in disciples; in God as in teacher; in lower bodies as in recipients; in heat and cold as in instruments. These things he did not teach, except concerning the spheres and the former, whom it was necessary to place as a disciple. Nor should those celestial forms, whether of asterisms or of Decans over the 36 images of the Zodiac, be thought to have themselves in Heaven in such a way that they are only instrumental seals of inferior things.

For they have another configuration toward other stars and systems; and if there are any Beings on the Moon and Venus, they have a participation of light different from ours, since one Lunar day contains fifteen terrestrial [days], and [the variations are] more varied, because it is nearer to the sun in conjunctions and farther away in oppositions than the earth is; so with the rest. And when Saturn regards the Sun by a square aspect with respect to us, it regards other Planets not by a square but by another aspect, so that the wisdom of God is most wondrous, using the same instruments at once for innumerable and most various things fittingly; and the same configuration and constellation is different in individual scenes, in which God, the great Lord and great King above all Gods, represents the riches of his virtue through the ministry of Angels and the instrumentation of bodies. Therefore we admit not only the 36 faces of Pantoformius, but far more. Whether they are such as they are posited by the Egyptians, Babylonians, and Arabs we have not yet examined; nor do we think they could have known except by God's revealing, as Plato too opines concerning the whole astrological matter.

## Note

This part tightens the Mercury critique by forcing the reported Hermetic vocabulary back through Campanella's own primality scheme: Life/Immortality and Necessity belong to the first primality, Providence to Wisdom, and the outward gift of Nature/Soul/Mind to Love. It also expands the form-giving problem beyond the 36 decans: Pantophormion is accepted only as an instrumental-formative office inside a much wider angelic governance of plural star systems.
