## Latin

Deus dedit mentibus virtutem continendi corpora in suo officio et iustitia. Iustum enim quod pulchrum; pulchrum est boni apparentia. Bonum autem est deteriora melioribus subesse: corpus spiritui, spiritus menti, mens Deo. Sic enim naturae melius servantur.

Hanc iustitiam perdidit animus cum Deo subditus esse noluit, a quo servatur. Ergo non fabula est peccatum angelorum et Adae in posteros traductum, sed ex arcanis naturae relucens veritas. Dedit Deus menti humanae ut imperare corpori, animalibus, et cunctis rebus se deterioribus possit, imperio tamen quod Dei imperium non impediat.

Imperium rationale et castum habet homo super res cunctas, et omnis superior super inferiorem. Amittit vero quando in suo ordine et dotibus a Deo datis non permanet. Cecidit homo ab hoc ordine, alioquin non tanta mala ipsum apprehendissent, nec corporis passionibus obediret ita turpiter ut homo asino et eques equo contra iustitiam et pulchritudinem eius epiphaniam.

Igitur cum homo agnoscat iustum, pulchrum, et bonum, et tamen non quod vult bonum agat, consequens est ut agnoscat se indigere superiori auxilio. Quemadmodum trahitur ad inferiora in dissimilitudinem et alienationem ad corporeum mundum per sensualitatis corporei spiritus illecebras et furias, per hominum pravorum et daemonum suggestiones, et oblatas occasiones, sic etiam ad superiora per reversionem sui ad se suamque originem curari oportet.

Curari debet per oppositas melioresque causas: per illecebras divinarum pulchritudinum oblatarum a mundi gubernatore, per gratiam autoris rerum, per suggestiones angelorum et doctrinas bonorum hominum. Auxilia maiora esse oportet quam depravantes causae, quae potestativum roborent, cognoscitivum illuminent, volitivum purgent sanctificentque.

**Hominem optimum vivendi genus, quo divinum regrediatur in mundum, non posse tenere nisi lege servetur. Articulus II.**

Cognoscit homo sese indigere legibus, id est regulis iustitiae, per quas doceretur quae sectanda sunt et quae declinanda. Post experientiam bonorum et malorum per philosophiam, scientias et artes invenit; in rebus tractandis regulas posuit quales rerum universitas ostendit rationabilissima.

Inventum est Deum lege aeterna, quae verbum Dei est essentialiter, cuncta regere et deducere suos ad fines. A lege aeterna derivatam agnoscimus legem naturalem, qua res omnes proprios in fines per certa media deducuntur, conservationem totius per conservationem partium. Ex lege naturali emanavit lex gentium per modum conclusionis, et lex positiva per modum specificationis praesentibus necessitatibus occurrens.

Experimento didicimus leges istas non sufficere ad bene vivendum in mundo corporali vitam ordinatam ad vitam mentalis mundi. In his legibus homines altercantur, et alii aliud credunt et faciunt; unde scivimus nos indigere Deo legislatore.

## English

God gave minds the power to keep bodies in their office and justice. Justice is the beautiful, beauty is the appearance of good, and good order means lower things subject to higher: body to spirit, spirit to mind, mind to God. The soul lost this justice when it refused subjection to God, by whom it is preserved. Therefore the sin of angels and Adam handed down to descendants is not a fable but a truth shining from the secrets of nature.

Humans were given rational and chaste dominion over bodies, animals, and all lower things, but not a dominion that disrupts God's own order. They lose this dominion when they do not remain in their order and gifts. The body then rules shamefully, as if an ass ruled a man or a horse ruled its rider.

Because humans know the just, beautiful, and good but fail to do the good they want, they need higher aid. Downward alienation comes through the allurements and rages of corporeal spirit, bad humans, demons, and occasions. Upward healing must come through stronger opposite causes: divine beauties, grace, angels, and good teachers. Power must be strengthened, cognition illuminated, will purified and sanctified.

Article II begins from law. Humans need laws, rules of justice teaching what to seek and avoid. Experience, philosophy, sciences, and arts discover rules in things. Eternal law is God's own Word governing all things; natural law derives from it; the law of nations follows as conclusions; positive law specifies for present needs. But these laws do not suffice for a bodily life ordered toward the mental world, because humans dispute and divide over them. Therefore they need God as legislator.

## Note

This leaf connects the fall to metaphysical order rather than only biblical narration. Sin is disorder in the chain body-spirit-mind-God, and religion will be the healing return through stronger contrary aids. The triadic faculties reappear operationally: aid must strengthen `potestativum`, illuminate `cognoscitivum`, and sanctify `volitivum`.
