## Latin

[Fragmentum tituli currentis in extracto: "PARS III. LIB. XV. CAP. II."[?]]

Singulos homines, a singulis Angelis, ultra speciei Angelum, gubernari: et Mundum humanum repraesentare omnes Mundos, et ipsorum gubernatum. Ergo Angelus speciei humanae respondens Angelo omnium Systematum, requirit hominem Unum, Principem totius generis humani, qui a Christo restituitur, cum propter peccata hominum diversitas Principatuum, [Sectarum?][?], non Uno pendentium, introducta fuerit. Et quis ordo Angelorum regnis hominum, quis Provinciis, quis Civitatibus, quis Congregationibus, et [Sectis?][?] praesit, et horum rectores homines duplici custodia gaudere. Et omnis coetus partitur in Religiosos, et Rectores, et Opifices, tribus Hierarchiis Angelorum correspondentes.

**ARTICULUS IV.**

Verum enimvero quoniam omnes species rerum inferiorum videntur esse propter homines, non abs re docuere Theologi cuique homini Angelum custodem unum adesse praeter Angelum speciei: quod non modo Iesus revelavit, cum dixit: Angelos puerorum semper videre faciem Patris de coelis [Matth. 18?][?]. Antiquitas contestatur de Geniis. Unde Socrates sui Genii seu custodis Angeli increpationes et admonitiones sentire sese [dictitat?][?], apud Platonem et Xenophontem. Brutus etiam a proprio Genio promonebatur. Stoici, et Platonici idem profitentur, et Trismegistus.

Nos autem etiam proprio nomine vocati a custode Angelo imminentia mala praemonente, edocemur. De bonis autem instruimur certis instigationibus, quas nemo in se non advertit. Vocibus enim circa hoc parum indigemus. Sancti viri doctissimi inter Christianos id etiam quotidie probant: ut nullatenus dubitare viro rationali amplius sit.

Cum praeterea de Mentalis Mundi substantiis in corporibus humanis hospitentur: profecto aequum est, ut egrediantur Angeli ad harum custodiam et gubernatum, donec ad Mentale Mundum revertantur, divisum in assistentes et ministrantes, donec perficiatur opus Dei. Et quoniam Epilogus Mundi est homo non corporalis modo, sed etiam mentalis: quidquid enim in eis est, continet in se homo: consequens est, ut humanae speciei gubernatio repraesentet totius Mundi gubernationem.

Ergo quoniam unus erat Mundus subditus Deo, omniaque eveniebant secundum praefixum ordinem ab exordio rerum: cadente autem homine in a Deo aversionem, res cunctae, quasi a proprio originali decore desciverunt; ut etiam probat S. Clemens, et Chrysost. Regimen totius generis humani ab uno omnium Patre simul rege ac sacerdote divisum est. Et sicut dicit Salomon: Propter peccata terrae multi principes eius. Idcirco non ab uno homine Dei Vicario regitur totum, uti aequum erat, genus nostrum.

Principatus ergo divisi sunt inter homines: sed adhuc remanet unus Princeps Angelus totius humani generis, non autem unus homo Princeps; erit tamen aliquando, uti speramus. Nam Papam unum restituit Christus, ut in libro de Monarchia Messiae probatum est. Quot autem sunt Principes terrae, tot etiam sunt Principatus Angelici: unde apud Danielem Angelus regni Persarum introducitur resistens Angelo regni Iudaeorum.

Sub Angelo, Principatu dicto, unius regni quasi sub una dominatione militant plures Archangeli, praefecti Provinciis eiusdem Regni vel Civitatibus: et omnibus congregationibus, sive religiosorum, sive laicorum praesidet Archangelus unus. Quapropter Rex, et Princeps, et Comes, et iudex Civitatis, et Dux militiae, et Magister generalis unius religionis, vel ordinis religiosorum, et Abbas unius conventus, et quilibet in dignitate constitutus, duplici custodia vallatur Angelica; altero S. Angelo personae, altero officii seu regiminis: et quidem maioris, vel minoris, vel subordinatae virtutis, prout sunt et ipsi magistratus. Similiter et homines singuli singulos habent Angelos maioris, vel minoris perfectionis, pro naturalium individualium dignitate, et fine, ad quem ordinantur.

Et in cunctis hominum nationibus triplex ordo correspondens supremo assistentium, videlicet ordo religiosorum primae Hierarchiae assistenti divinis persimilis, et eiusdem Hierarchiae Seraphinis; alius est ordo rectorum Provinciae, et Civitatis, ut iudices et Consiliarii, et praeceptores, et Duces, correspondens secundae Hierarchiae, quae est prima ministrantium et iubentium, et secundo insuper ordini primae, idem Cherubinis: scientia enim regit et dirigit. Tertius est ordo exequentium, et servientium in Mechanicis, cuiusmodi sunt pugnatores, et opifices, et agricolae, qui correspondet tertiae Hierarchiae Angelorum, et tertio ordini primae, qui est Thronorum: potestas enim executiva iustitiae ipsorum congruit, et robori.

Est advertendum, quod Proclus in libro commentorum in Alcibiadem, non modo probat animam humanam non esse de essentia Dei: quia turbationibus plena, et ignorantia, et finitudine; sed etiam probat Angelum custodem non esse particulam animae humanae vel ipsam animam: neque [enim?][?] quod secundum essentiam melius accipitur ad deterius complendum. Et cum perseveret custos, anima vero nunc ira aestuet, nunc maeritudine, et variis agitatur modis, non convenit cum custode in essentia et informatione corporali. Praeterea ait Iamblichus, non elevabit nos custos supra fatum corporabile ad Deum, si pars animae generationi immersae esset.

Probant etiam Platonici, animas defunctorum non esse Daemones: neque Deos erraticos, ut Proclus ait. Sed revera sunt erratici Dii, id est, Angeli, qui suum principatum non servarunt, nec ordinem. Neque enim putandum malos natura a summo bono Deo creari potuisse, nec contra seipsum: ergo ex electione, et errore, cum essent Dii, ceciderunt, ut Christiana pandit Philosophia.

## English

[Running-title fragment in the extract: "Third Part. Fifteenth Book. Chapter II."[?]]

That individual human beings are governed by individual Angels, beyond the Angel of the species; and that the human World represents all Worlds and their governance. Therefore the Angel of the human species, corresponding to the Angel of all Systems, requires one Human Being, Prince of the whole human race, who is restored by Christ, since because of the sins of human beings a diversity of Principalities and [Sects?][?] not depending on One has been introduced. And which order of Angels presides over the kingdoms of human beings, which over Provinces, which over Cities, which over Congregations and [Sects?][?]; and that the human rulers of these rejoice in a double guardianship. And every assembly is divided into Religious, Rectors, and Workers, corresponding to the three Hierarchies of Angels.

**ARTICLE IV.**

But truly, since all species of inferior things seem to exist for human beings, Theologians have taught not without reason that one guardian Angel is present to each human being, besides the Angel of the species. This not only Jesus revealed, when he said that the Angels of children always see the face of the Father in heaven [Matt. 18?][?]. Antiquity bears witness concerning Genii. Hence Socrates [said repeatedly?][?] that he sensed the rebukes and admonitions of his Genius or guardian Angel, in Plato and Xenophon. Brutus also was forewarned by his own Genius. The Stoics and Platonists profess the same, and Trismegistus.

We too, called even by our own name by a guardian Angel forewarning imminent evils, are taught. But concerning goods we are instructed by certain instigations, which no one does not notice in himself. For we have little need of voices concerning this. Most learned holy men among Christians prove this daily too, so that there should no longer be any doubt at all for a rational man.

Moreover, since substances of the Mental World are hosted in human bodies, it is surely fitting that Angels go out for their guardianship and governance until they return to the Mental World, divided into assistants and ministers, until the work of God is completed. And since the human being is the Epitome of the World not only in corporeal mode but also in mental mode, for whatever is in them the human being contains in himself, it follows that the governance of the human species represents the governance of the whole World.

Therefore, since there was one World subject to God, and all things happened according to the prefixed order from the beginning of things, but when the human being fell into aversion from God, all things, as if from their own original beauty, departed, as St. Clement and Chrysostom also prove, the rule of the whole human race by one Father of all, at once king and priest, was divided. And as Solomon says: because of the sins of the earth, its princes are many. Therefore our whole race is not ruled by one human being, Vicar of God, as was fitting.

Principalities, therefore, have been divided among human beings; but there still remains one Angelic Prince of the whole human race, but not one human Prince; yet there will be one someday, as we hope. For Christ restored one Pope, as was proved in the book On the Monarchy of the Messiah. But however many Princes of the earth there are, so many Angelic Principalities there also are: hence in Daniel the Angel of the kingdom of the Persians is introduced resisting the Angel of the kingdom of the Jews.

Under the Angel called Principality, of one kingdom as if under one domination, many Archangels serve as soldiers, prefects over the Provinces of the same Kingdom or over Cities; and one Archangel presides over all congregations, whether of religious or of laypeople. Therefore a King, Prince, Count, judge of a City, military Leader, general Master of one religion or order of religious, Abbot of one convent, and anyone constituted in dignity is enclosed by a double Angelic guardianship: by one holy Angel of the person, by another of the office or governance; and indeed of greater, lesser, or subordinate power, according as the magistrates themselves are. Likewise individual human beings have individual Angels of greater or lesser perfection, according to the dignity of the individual natural things and the end to which they are ordered.

And in all nations of human beings there is a triple order corresponding to the supreme [hierarchy] of assistants, namely the order of religious, very similar to the first Hierarchy assisting divine things and to the Seraphim of the same Hierarchy. Another is the order of rulers of Province and City, such as judges, Counselors, teachers, and Leaders, corresponding to the second Hierarchy, which is the first of those ministering and commanding, and in addition to the second order of the first [Hierarchy], the same [as] Cherubim: for knowledge rules and directs. The third is the order of those executing and serving in the Mechanical [arts], of which sort are fighters, workers, and farmers, which corresponds to the third Hierarchy of Angels and to the third order of the first [Hierarchy], which is of the Thrones: for the executive power of justice agrees with their [office] and with strength.

It must be observed that Proclus, in the book of commentaries on the Alcibiades, not only proves that the human soul is not from the essence of God, because it is full of disturbances, ignorance, and finitude; but also proves that the guardian Angel is not a particle of the human soul or the soul itself; nor [indeed?][?] is what is better according to essence taken for completing what is worse. And since the guardian persists, but the soul now burns with anger, now with sadness, and is agitated in various ways, it does not agree with the guardian in essence and corporeal informing. Moreover Iamblichus says that the guardian would not raise us above corporeal fate to God, if it were part of the soul immersed in generation.

The Platonists also prove that the souls of the dead are not Demons, nor wandering Gods, as Proclus says. But in reality there are wandering Gods, that is, Angels, who did not preserve their principality or order. Nor should it be thought that evil beings could have been created by nature by the highest good God, nor against himself; therefore by choice and error, when they were Gods, they fell, as Christian Philosophy reveals.

## Note

This part carries the governance scheme from celestial systems into human political and social order. The article makes human governance representative of world-governance: one angelic prince remains for the whole human race, but human rule is divided because of sin, with Christ's restoration of one Pope named as the hoped-for repair. The page also opens the double-guardian doctrine for persons and offices, and ends by separating guardian Angels from human souls against Proclus and Iamblichus.
