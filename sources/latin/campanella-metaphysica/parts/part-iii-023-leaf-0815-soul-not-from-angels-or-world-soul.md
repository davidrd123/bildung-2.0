## Latin

[Fragmentum continuans folium praecedens:] in adventu animae et noviter fieri statim ab ea. Si enim ipsa nudae materiae unitur, ad quid praefuit spiritus et laboriosa organizatio? Si ut mens aliam faciat ex ea, quid oportuit eam recte factam corrumpi? Et cur Mens, si subito tam divinum opus facit et praecedens delet, non etiam subito fractum crus reficit, eoque magis cum dominari in corpus iam didicerit? Et cur a fuligine et bile discursus et scientia adimitur tam potentissimae causae, subito ex nihilo corporeitatem, quam ipsa non habet, et mixtionem et sensualitatem largienti? Manifeste quoque, abeunte mente, remanent organa omnia in mortuo per febrem. Ergo non sequuntur ipsam, sed corporeitatem a spiritu organizatam virtute animae Mundi, ut dictum est.

Quapropter homo generat hominem, quia corpus et spiritum sentientem adeo producit, quod tandem Deus perficit opus eius, non autem destruit. Et mirum quomodo Peripatetici possint hominem ita generare, cum Aristoteles per motum generantis remanentem in semine faciat hominem ex menstruo, semenque expelli doceat. Ergo non remanet eius vis. Omitto quod motio illa est inanis; nam subito dependitur, ut patet in virgae exemplo ab eo adducto. Formatio autem et anima vigoratur. Item, si permaneret, cum stupida sit motio et non Ens, opificium sic mirificum nullo potest efformare modo. Nobis autem patet hominis ab homine generatio, et ranae ex non rana, ubi nulla est motio generantis, sed formatio fit iuxta ideam ab anima Mundi, caloris opera utente, sicut in seminibus.

Ad quartum: non ergo homo est imperfectior brutis, quippe qui generat corpus et spiritum sicut bruta, et insuper disponit ad susceptionem mentis a Deo, qui non nisi perfectissimo opificio eam immittit. Ratio valet contra praefatos opinantes[?].

Ad quintum: Deus cooperatur Naturae, non peccato, ut dictum est in prima parte, quoniam Enti, non autem nihilo, communicat; Ens facit, non defectum.

Ad sextum: perfecta est Natura serviens Deo; perfectum est Dei opus, qui Naturam fecit et eadem utitur ad hominis fabricam. Nihil enim ipsa facit sine Deo, ut dictum est in secunda parte.

Ad septimum et octavum dicimus animam esse partem hominis et in seminis potentia receptiva, non autem activa, contineri. Agit enim seminalis spiritus opificium capax mentis a Deo infundendae. Partes autem corporeae et animales sunt in semine actu explicandae in opus, quoniam sunt ex viribus Naturae. Mens autem excedit Naturam et a Deo expectatur. De augmento et decremento secundum spiritum dictum est in praecedenti sermone.

Ad nonum: vera est sententia, sed profecto spiritus humanus efformat organa aptanda humanae menti. Cum enim absque symbolo non uniantur, ille huic deservit, teste Augustino. Praeterea videmus mores iuxta temperiem et organa ostendi in homine. Ergo Mens, licet dominatum gerat, non tamen nisi politicum, quoniam advenit operi facto et non ipsa facit, et venit pugnatura hic, alibi vero regnatura. Idcirco non semper organa ut ipsa vellet habet. Quis enim fervens iecore non vellet frigidiusculum habere? Et cellas capitis ampliores, in quibus spiritus posset melius discurrere? Spiritus generans facit organa similia suo principio pro materiae et instrumentorum conditione. Formatio autem est ab anima Mundi et Angelis, qui norunt quae instrumenta cui animae debeantur. Numquid et in generatione ranae praeest semper rana generans organa? Item domus non fit ab habitatore ignaro, sed a domificatore artis perito. Sic corpora non ab habitante anima sed ab artificibus praedictis.

Propterea Thomas non probat quod Albertus docet animam a principio esse calorem, et mox vegetalem, et mox sensualem, et tandem Rationalem, ut eadem anima sibi faciat omnia organa. Potest enim Mens uti factis.

Ad decimum: rite probatur corpus ab anima efformari, sed non ab anima geniti, sed generantis, vel a communi anima Mundi. Immo ab hac magis, quae scit melius opus formationis nobis ignotum, ut domus a domificatore, non ab habitatore.

Ad undecimum: viva dicimus semina vita animali, non mentali, et ab ea corpus organizari iuxta ideam, quam administrant virtutes Angelicae sub anima Mundi. Deum vero, quod perfectissimum est et ab eis dari non potest, adiungere, ipsam videlicet Mentem. Neque enim Mens forma aut formatio est, sed Ens divinum, non nisi creatori tribuendum opus. Et haec quidem probabiliter loquuti sumus, ut in tanta re potuimus.

**Appendix, quod anima non sit ab Angelis neque ab anima Mundi.**

Hic notare libet quod Angelus aut anima Mundi non producunt nostram animam. Nam ex nihilo non possunt; ex seipsis vero facerent similes seipsis, et sic anima esset angelus et non Mens inclusa ad animandum corpus. Anima vero Mundi non facit animam per decisionem, quia non est partibilis, neque per sui diffusionem, ut calor calorem, quoniam non habet contrarium cuius gratia velit multiplicari, neque corruptibilitatem quare velit instaurari. Item essent omnes animae eiusdem speciei. Ostendimus autem ferinas esse a sole. Item non intelligeret anima nostra supra animam Mundi, neque ad Deum intuitu et religionem elevaret. Item Religio immediate tendit in Deum et tradita est nobis a Deo, non ab anima Mundi neque ab Angelis nisi ut instrumentis Dei. Ergo anima ipsa fit a Deo et non ab his.

## English

[Fragment continuing the preceding leaf:] at the coming of the soul, and newly made at once by it. For if the soul itself is united to bare matter, why did spirit and laborious organization come first? If [mind comes] so that it may make another [organization] from it, why was it necessary that the organization rightly made be corrupted? And why, if Mind suddenly makes so divine a work and deletes the preceding one, does it not also suddenly repair a broken leg, especially since it has already learned to dominate the body? And why are discourse and knowledge taken away by soot and bile from such a most powerful cause, which suddenly from nothing grants corporeity, which it itself does not have, and mixture and sensuality? Clearly too, when mind goes away, all the organs remain in one dead through fever. Therefore they do not follow the mind itself, but corporeity organized by spirit through the power of the soul of the World, as was said.

For this reason the human being generates a human being, because he produces body and sensing spirit to such a degree that God at last perfects his work, but does not destroy it. And it is strange how the Peripatetics can generate a human being in this way, since Aristotle makes the human being from menstrual blood through the motion of the generator remaining in the seed, and teaches that the seed is expelled. Therefore its force does not remain. I omit that that motion is empty; for it is immediately suspended, as is clear in the example of the rod brought forward by him. But formation and soul are strengthened. Likewise, if it remained, since it is a stupid motion and not a Being, it could in no way form such a marvelous work. But for us the generation of a human being from a human being is clear, and of a frog from a non-frog, where there is no motion of a generator, but formation happens according to the idea from the soul of the World, using the work of heat, just as in seeds.

To the fourth: therefore the human being is not more imperfect than beasts, since he generates body and spirit just as beasts do, and in addition disposes [the body] for receiving mind from God, who sends it in only to the most perfect work. The argument holds against the aforesaid thinkers[?].

To the fifth: God cooperates with Nature, not with sin, as was said in the first part, because he communicates to Being, not to nothing; he makes Being, not defect.

To the sixth: Nature serving God is perfect; the work of God, who made Nature and uses it for the fabric of the human being, is perfect. For Nature itself does nothing without God, as was said in the second part.

To the seventh and eighth we say that soul is part of the human being and is contained in the receptive potency of the seed, but not the active potency. For the seminal spirit carries out a work capable of mind to be infused by God. The corporeal and animal parts, however, are in the seed actually, to be unfolded into the work, because they are from the powers of Nature. But Mind exceeds Nature and is awaited from God. Concerning increase and decrease according to spirit, it was said in the preceding discourse.

To the ninth: the opinion is true, but certainly the human spirit forms organs to be adapted to the human mind. For since they are not united without a symbol, that spirit serves this mind, Augustine being witness. Moreover we see that habits are shown in the human being according to temperament and organs. Therefore Mind, although it bears dominion, bears it only politically, because it comes to a work already made and does not itself make it, and it comes to fight here, but elsewhere to reign. For this reason it does not always have the organs as it would wish. For who, burning with liver, would not wish to have it a little cooler? And broader cells of the head, in which the spirit could discourse better? The generating spirit makes organs similar to its principle according to the condition of matter and instruments. But formation is from the soul of the World and Angels, who know which instruments are owed to which soul. Is there always a generating frog presiding over organs in the generation of a frog? Likewise a house is not made by the ignorant inhabitant, but by a house-builder skilled in the art. So bodies are not [made] by the inhabiting soul, but by the aforesaid artificers.

Therefore Thomas does not prove what Albert teaches, that the soul from the beginning is heat, and soon vegetative, and soon sensual, and finally Rational, so that the same soul makes all organs for itself. For Mind can use things already made.

To the tenth: it is rightly proved that the body is formed by soul, but not by the soul of the generated thing, but of the generator, or by the common soul of the World. Indeed rather by this latter, which knows better the work of formation unknown to us, as a house [is made] by the house-builder, not by the inhabitant.

To the eleventh: we say that seeds are alive by animal life, not mental life, and that the body is organized by it according to the idea, which the Angelic powers administer under the soul of the World. But God adds what is most perfect and cannot be given by them, namely Mind itself. For Mind is neither form nor formation, but a divine Being, a work to be attributed only to the creator. And indeed we have spoken these things probably, as we could in so great a matter.

**Appendix: that the soul is not from Angels or from the soul of the World.**

Here it is pleasing to note that an Angel or the soul of the World does not produce our soul. For they cannot [produce] from nothing; but from themselves they would make things similar to themselves, and thus the soul would be an angel and not a Mind enclosed to animate the body. But the soul of the World does not make soul by cutting off, because it is not divisible, nor by diffusion of itself, as heat [makes] heat, because it has no contrary for the sake of which it would want to multiply, nor corruptibility for the sake of which it would want to be restored. Likewise all souls would be of the same species. But we have shown that beast-souls are from the sun. Likewise our soul would not understand above the soul of the World, nor would it raise [itself] to God by intuition and religion. Likewise Religion tends immediately into God and has been handed down to us by God, not by the soul of the World nor by Angels except as instruments of God. Therefore the soul itself is made by God and not by these.
