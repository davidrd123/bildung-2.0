## Latin

Mirabilia in brutis sunt quia bruta sunt; item bruta serviunt homini, non autem homo brutis. Licet homo serviat homini, non autem sic vi et studio actus alieno, sed propria voluntate, quam volare nullus potest. Machabaei mortem alacriter suscipiunt potius quam Antiocho regi obediant; Mucius ignem non timet neque Porsennam pro patriae libertate.

Equus indiget freno et calcaribus, simius flagello; dolore et voluptate rapiuntur et assuescunt. Homo vero spernit dolores et blanditias, et ratione utitur superiori, qua nec infans nec canis suaderi possunt ut ieiunent et ut plant disciplinam [?].

Religionem proprie inesse brutis negandum videtur. Haec enim est cultus veri Dei cum despectione praesentis vitae propter aeternam a Deo adipiscendam. Bruta praesentibus modo inhiare bonis novimus, et nihil de Deo et aeternitate inquirere. Elephas lunam adorat quoniam in nocturnis itineribus ab ea beneficium accipit; baptizatur a coitu ad sordes corporis abstergendas, non animae.

Solis et lunae herbae sequuntur motum, quoniam moventur illorum viribus et quadam symbola suavitate. Magnes spectat polum; haec omnia fiunt a consensu et dissensu rerum, non religione.

Animalia garritu, volatu, et gestibus significant aliqua instinctu sensuali, non intendendo oracula dare, sed proprias passiones sectando. Pecus et bos multum comedentes indicant pluviam, quoniam sentiunt in aere illam parari a vaporibus et frigore.

Bruta, cum passionibus corporis operam attentiorem dent quam nos, melius agnoscunt passiones aeris easque praedicunt. Qui hernia laborant aut cicatricibus prius praevident tempestatem et frigus quam ceteri, quoniam facilius ab aere patiuntur. In somnis quoque plura nobis ab aere et caelo significantur quam in vigilia, quoniam spiritus non vacat negotiis externis.

Prophetia animalium est ab aere in spiritu aereo affecto ab initiis et causis futurorum. Hominis vero non solum ab aere et caelo, sed ab angelis et Deo; nec modo de pluviis, tempestatibus, et bello, sed de regnorum interitu, nominibus futurorum hominum, gestis, Deo, paradiso, inferis, et arcanis.

Naturam non deesse in necessariis fatemur: ubi rebus socialibus necessarium est sermonem, legem, obedientiam, praeceptum, haec animalibus non negamus secundum similitudinem nostram. Sed ad aliam vitam post mortem dirigi negamus. Omnia sentiunt Creatorem secreto sensu; at per artem et cultum nonnisi homines aliam sperant vitam.

## English

Animal wonders are wonderful because animals are beasts; they do not put beasts on the human level. Beasts serve humans, while humans do not serve beasts. When humans serve humans, they can still act by their own will, which no one can take away. The Maccabees choose death over obedience to Antiochus; Mucius does not fear fire or Porsenna when liberty is at stake.

Animals need reins, spurs, whips, pain, pleasure, and habituation. Humans can despise pain and blandishment by higher reason. A dog carrying letters or meat home is not thereby rational in the human way; it acts by affected sense and habit in one limited line, while humans can carry instructions and gifts to countless friends and go where they have never been by verbal indication alone.

Campanella denies religion to beasts properly so called. Religion is worship of the true God with contempt of present life for the sake of eternal life. Elephants honor the moon because it helps them travel at night; their washing after intercourse cleans bodily filth, not the soul. Plants turning to the sun and magnets facing the pole are consensual and sympathetic motions of things, not religion.

Animal "prophecy" is likewise natural sensitivity. Beasts attend more closely to bodily passions than humans do, and so they read atmospheric changes better. Dreams also receive more from air and heaven because the spirit is not occupied with external business. Human prophecy, by contrast, comes not only from air and heaven but from angels and God, and concerns kingdoms, named future persons, God, paradise, hell, and divine secrets.

Campanella grants social law, speech, obedience, and signs in animals when needed for their common life. What he denies is direction toward another life after death. All things secretly sense the Creator, but only humans hope for another life through art and cult.

## Note

The line is sharper than simple "animals lack reason." Animals can have speech, social law, weather prophecy, and a secret sense of the Creator. What they lack is religion as self-directed cult of God ordered to eternal life, and prophecy as angelic or divine disclosure rather than atmospheric sensitivity.
