## Latin

Si animae erant beatae ante corporis clausuram, numquam cupivissent corporis carcerem. Si miserae, quaerenda est miseriarum causa. Si, ut Origenes inquit, quia peccarunt, stultum est putare eas potuisse peccare si Deo fruebantur, cuius boni summi amor numquam excidit. Si ex veri intuitu ad opinabilia transeunt ut cadant, impossibile videtur: in Deo enim omnia vera.

Neque si erant in quodam statu naturali, sicut angeli, et peccarunt, bene solvitur. Tunc Deus immittit eas in corpora tanquam in poenas aeternas aut purgatorias. Non aeternas, quia corpus moritur; neque purgatorias, quia corpus est occasio peccandi magis. Quomodo Deus purgat et lavat luto maculas mentium?

Si in poenam immittuntur, oportuisset non tollere illis memoriam delitiarum praeteritarum et status inclyti quem perdiderunt; poena enim non est in eo qui nescit quod bonum amiserit. Quapropter rationabilis sententia est quod animae a Deo creentur et simul corporibus immittantur; non ex Dei substantia, quoniam Deus partibilis non est; neque ex alia materia, cum sit incorporea; ergo ex nihilo.

**Quaestiones iterum: cur anima immortalis unitur corpori, et utrum anima producat animam cum semine sicut spiritus producit spiritum. Caput VI. Articulus I.**

Quaeritur: si naturaliter anima unitur corpori, ergo non est Deus eius causa sed natura; si violenter, homo est violenter homo; si voluntarie, ergo anima amat corpus ut bonum, in quo tamen multa incurrit mala. Remanet quaestio cur nobilissimum ens ignobilissimo uniatur.

Item si anima indiget spiritu et sensoriis ad cognitionem rerum, cur separatur a corpore, et quomodo post separationem cognoscit? Si vero non indiget spiritu et sensoriis, cur unitur ei cum detrimento?

Cur sicut spiritus facit spiritum, non et anima animam per traductionem, ut sentit Apollinaris? Alioquin homo non generaret hominem, sed materiam. Item compositum generatur; ergo anima et corpus ab homine generante, alioquin non esset genitor. Deus etiam in caestibus et adulteriis cooperaretur immittens animam in tali coitu.

Anima est pars hominis; ergo continetur in semine. Simul crescunt et manifestantur vires animae et corporis per operationes paulatim perfectas; ergo habent eandem originem. Organa corporis fiunt conformia animae affectibus: leporis anima paravit organa ad fugiendum, leonis ad pugnandum, avis ad volandum. Ergo ipsa est in semine humano, figuram rectam et manus fabricatura.

## English

Campanella closes the preexistence question by rejecting the soul-prison account. If souls were blessed before embodiment, they would not choose the prison of body. If miserable, the cause must be explained. If they sinned while enjoying God, the claim is absurd, since enjoyment of the supreme good would not fall away. If embodiment is punishment, memory of the lost state should not have been removed; punishment is not punishment to one who does not know what good was lost.

The more reasonable position is that souls are created by God and placed together with bodies. They are not pieces of God's substance, because God is not divisible; not made from some mental matter, because incorporeal; therefore created from nothing.

Caput VI then reopens the hard questions. If soul is naturally united to body, does nature rather than God cause it? If violently, is the human being human by violence? If voluntarily, why would soul love a body in which it suffers so many evils? If soul needs spirit and senses for knowing, how does it know after separation? If it does not need them, why join them at a loss?

The generation problem follows. Why should soul not generate soul through seed as spirit generates spirit? Otherwise the parent generates only matter. Since soul is part of the human composite and body-organs seem shaped by soul's affections, perhaps the soul is contained in seed and translated from parent to child.

## Note

This leaf turns from immortality to ensoulment. Campanella has established that the human mind survives body, but now has to explain why a divine, immortal mind is joined to body at all and why it is not simply generated by parents. The questions are useful because they force his creationism to handle biology rather than bypass it.
