[[section: Postrema]]

POSTREMA.

[apparatus note] This final page is not a continuous argumentative passage. It is a printer's/postscript correction page containing a dense list of page-and-line errata plus one omitted passage keyed to page 95, `Dubitatio I`. The page-image OCR is very noisy and should not be treated as a reliable item-by-item correction list without visual collation against the image.

Legible structure:

1. Corrections keyed first to the `Epistola Typographi ad Lectorem`, then to many page-and-line locations in the work.
2. An omitted passage keyed to page 95, `Dubitationis I`, to be supplied from the index prefixed to the work.
3. The omitted passage concerns distinguishable powers or acts of cognition: one power/act for knowing whiteness, another for knowing hardness or liquefaction; someone can know hardness without thereby knowing whiteness, as in the case of one born blind; from this it is inferred that no thing has so many known attributes as our mind, since many more are known in it than in any other thing.
4. A later correction concerns the claim that, although nothing is admitted in the self except mind, it does not follow that seeing and touching are operations of mind in the bodily sense; the intended point concerns only the thought of seeing and touching, as is experienced every day in dreams.
5. The page closes with additional short page-and-line corrections through the end of the work.
