## Opening Finding

Gassendi says Descartes skipped the parent objection under cover of the same alleged inconvenience. Gassendi had allowed that the mind depends on a being other than itself, but as something once produced, not as something continuously produced anew. The proof that the mind is not from itself was unnecessary; parents remain the obvious producing cause. Descartes' reply that the mind is thinking and has the idea of God does not remove parents, because parents or their minds were also thinking things with the idea of God. To answer otherwise Descartes would have to deny that the parents had minds, or thinking minds, or the idea of God, or enough objective reality in that idea to account for the child's mind.

## Working Translation

I had consequently objected that you could indeed gather that you depend on a being different from you, but in such a way that this was understood, not as the being by which you are produced anew and continuously, but as the being by which you were once produced. But you thought this saved by alleging the very same inconvenience. Yet should you really, under the same pretext, have passed over what I objected about parents?

For you had asked in this way: from what, then, would I be? From myself, perhaps? Or from parents? Or from something else? I first said that there was no need for you to prove that you are not from yourself. I supposed, of course, that you neither could nor should have doubted this, since you remembered that you had not always existed, unless perhaps you could think that from eternity you had slept a sleep without dreams and then finally woke up spontaneously when you first began to think, forgetful of the producing cause. And among so many things manifest to you, this could not escape you: nothing is the cause of itself.

Next I asked why you would not think that you are from your parents, by whom you seemed to have been so manifestly produced. And because you had anticipated the point by saying that you are a thinking thing, having the idea of God in you, and that there must be at least as much in the cause as in the effect, I pressed in this way: were not your parents, or their minds, since this distinction had to be made because you were speaking as Mind, also thinking things and things having the idea of God?

Moreover, you answered nothing to this; and in truth you could not have answered, except by denying that your parents, when they generated you, had a mind; or that they did have one, but not a thinking one; or that they did have a thinking one, but not one having the idea of God; or that they did have one, but not containing at least as much objective reality as you, or as the mind which you then received and afterward recognized, even while dwelling within the womb, that you had.

## Notes

- Gassendi keeps the distinction between original production and continuous production at the center of the reply.
- The proof that the mind is not from itself is treated as unnecessary because non-self-causation is already among Descartes' manifest commitments.
- The dreamless-eternal-sleep aside mocks how little work Descartes' memory of not always existing should need to do.
- Parents return as obvious producing causes, and the burden shifts to Descartes to show why thinking parent-minds with the idea of God cannot satisfy the causal maxim.
- The causal rule about as much in the cause as in the effect is turned against Descartes: parent minds may already contain the relevant idea-content.
