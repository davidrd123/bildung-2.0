## Opening Finding

Descartes rejects Gassendi's category objection by widening `proprietas` to any attribute or predicable. Necessary existence, he says, is a property in God in the strictest sense because it belongs only to God and in him alone makes part of essence. God's existence therefore should not be compared with the triangle's existence: God is his own being, while triangle is not. Descartes also preserves a graded contrast between possible existence and necessary existence. Possible existence is a perfection in the idea of a triangle; necessary existence is a perfection in the idea of God. On that basis he denies that the proof begs the principle and says the demonstration of God's existence is parallel to, but simpler and clearer than, the triangle angle theorem.

## Working Translation

RESPONSE.

Here I do not see of what genus of things you want existence to be, nor why it cannot be called a property just as much as omnipotence, provided the name "property" is taken for any attribute, or for everything that can be predicated of a thing, as it must plainly be taken here.

Indeed, necessary existence is really a property in God, taken in the strictest mode, because it belongs to him alone and in him alone makes part of essence.

Therefore the existence of a triangle should not be compared with the existence of God, because it plainly has a different relation to essence in God than in the triangle.

Nor is it any more a begging of the principle that existence is numbered among the things that pertain to God's essence than that the equality of three angles with two right angles is counted among the properties of the triangle.

Nor is it true that essence and existence in God can be thought one without the other as they can in the triangle, because God is his own being, but the triangle is not.

Yet I do not deny that possible existence is a perfection in the idea of a triangle, just as necessary existence is a perfection in the idea of God. For it makes that idea more excellent than the ideas of those chimeras whose existence is supposed to be impossible.

Therefore you have not weakened the force of my argument in the least in any respect, and you remain always deceived by that sophism which you say could so easily be solved by me.

As for the things you add, I have already answered them sufficiently elsewhere. And you are plainly mistaken when you say that existence is not demonstrated of God as it is demonstrated of the triangle that its three angles are equal to two right angles. The reason of both is parallel, except that the demonstration proving existence in God is much simpler and clearer.

Finally I pass over the rest, because while you say that I explain nothing, you yourself explain nothing, and prove nothing except only that you can prove nothing.

## Notes

- Descartes broadens `proprietas` to any attribute or predicable.
- Necessary existence is a property in God in the strict sense: unique to God and part of essence.
- The existence of triangle is not comparable with the existence of God because God is `suum esse`, while triangle is not.
- Possible existence can be a perfection in the idea of triangle; necessary existence is a perfection in the idea of God.
- Chimeras mark the impossibility contrast: possible existence makes an idea better than ideas whose existence is supposed impossible.
- Descartes rejects the `petitio principii` charge and claims parity with the triangle theorem, with the God-existence demonstration simpler and clearer.
- The response mostly asserts a category difference rather than developing one; Gassendi's next `Instantia` should press the broad use of property, attribute, and genus.
