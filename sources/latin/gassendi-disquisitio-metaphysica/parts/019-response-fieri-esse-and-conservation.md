## Opening Finding

Descartes answers by making explicit the distinction Gassendi anticipated: causes `secundum fieri`, which explain a thing's coming-to-be, and causes `secundum esse`, which explain its continued being. Architect and father are causes only in the first sense; God is cause of creatures in both. Descartes also recasts Gassendi's parent-regress as irrelevant because the question concerns causes in being, not causes in becoming, and he embraces the amplification of human perfections as the very force that proves we were made by God.

## Working Translation

Responsio.

When you deny that we need the continuous influx of the first cause in order to be conserved, you deny a thing which all metaphysicians affirm as manifest, but about which the unlearned often do not think, because they attend only to causes according to becoming and not according to being.

Thus the architect is the cause of the house, and the father of the son, only according to becoming; and therefore, when the work is completed, it can remain without a cause of this sort. But the sun is the cause of the light proceeding from it, and God is the cause of created things not only according to becoming but also according to being. Therefore he must always flow into the effect in the same way, in order to conserve it.

This is openly demonstrated from what I explained about the independence of the parts of time, which you try in vain to evade by proposing the necessity of sequence which exists between the parts of time considered in the abstract. That is not the question here, but rather time or duration of the enduring thing, whose individual moments you do not deny can be separated from neighboring ones, that is, that the enduring thing can cease to be at each individual moment.

And when you say that there is a force in us sufficient to secure that we persevere, unless a corrupting cause intervenes, you do not notice that you attribute to the creature the perfection of the creator, namely that it perseveres in being independently of another, and to the creator the imperfection of the creature, namely that by a positive action he must tend toward non-being if he ever wishes to bring it about that we cease to be.

As for what you add about progress to infinity, namely that it is not absurd for such a progress to be given, this is afterward weakened by you yourself, for you confess that it is absurd in causes so connected with one another that the lower cannot act without the higher. The question here is only about such causes, namely causes in being, not causes in becoming, as parents are. Therefore Aristotle's authority does not oppose me here.

Nor does what you say about Pandora oppose me. For you confess that all the perfections which I notice in human beings can be increased by me by various degrees, to such a point that afterward I see them to be such that they cannot fall within human nature. This is wholly sufficient for me to demonstrate God's existence. For I urge and contend that this very force of amplifying all human perfections to the point that they are known to be more than human would not have been in us unless we had been made by God.

But that it does not appear to you that I have demonstrated this most evidently does not surprise me at all, because I have not so far noticed that you have correctly perceived any of my reasons.

## Notes

- `Secundum fieri / secundum esse` is now explicit. Descartes grants examples like architect and father only for becoming, not for continued being.
- Conservation depends on continual divine influx. Descartes treats independent persistence as a creator-level perfection, not a creaturely power.
- The temporal argument is restated as duration of the enduring thing, not abstract sequence among parts of time.
- Parent-regress is rejected as a regress among causes in becoming, while Descartes says the proof concerns causes in being.
- Descartes accepts the amplification of human perfections but makes it evidence of divine origin: the mind's power to exceed the human would not exist unless God made us.
