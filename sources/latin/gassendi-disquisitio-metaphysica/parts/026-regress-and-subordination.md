## Opening Finding

Gassendi says Descartes attacks the infinite-regress objection only after bypassing the parent objection. The issue was not an infinite regress of causes in being, or essentially subordinated causes, but an infinite regress of causes in becoming, accidentally subordinated, like father, grandfather, great-grandfather, and so on. In such a series, later descendants can act and become causes even when earlier parents no longer exist. Descartes' reply works only by conflating causes in being with essentially subordinated causes and by ignoring the parent-series Gassendi actually raised.

## Working Translation

Passing over this, you attack what I said about progress to infinity. In order that it may further appear how neatly you act, one must know only what is at issue. When you were going to prove that you were produced not by parents or by any other cause than God, you used that argument familiar among the Doctors: if there is another cause, one can ask about it whether it is from itself or from another. For if it is from itself, it is clear from what has been said that it is God itself, because, and so on. But if it is from another, the same question will again be asked about this other, whether it is from itself or from another, until at last one comes to the ultimate cause, which will be God.

I replied: but if the cause was parents, that cause could be not from itself but from another, and that again from another, and so on to infinity. At this point, what is more manifest than that the question had been set up about an infinite progress of causes in becoming, or accidentally subordinated causes, as they say, and not about causes in being, or essentially subordinated causes?

And lest this be doubtful, I consequently explained the matter and declared that you had to prove an infinite progress absurd, not in essentially subordinated causes, or causes in being, since the question was not about those, but in accidentally subordinated causes, or causes in becoming. Such are parents: father, grandfather, great-grandfather, great-great-grandfather, great-great-great-grandfather, great-great-great-great-grandfather, and the rest. Even when these do not exist, sons, grandsons, great-grandsons, and the others can act and can likewise become causes.

Now you say that what I add about progress to infinity, namely that it is not absurd for such a progress to be given, is afterward weakened by myself. How so? Because I confess that it is absurd in causes so connected with one another that the lower cannot act without the higher. And is this not neat? I said that it is absurd in those causes; therefore I weakened what I added when I said that it is not absurd in other causes so ordered that, when the prior one has been destroyed, the one that depends on it survives and can act? But of course this is your mode of inference.

You go on: "For the question here is only about such causes, namely causes in being, not causes in becoming, as parents are." But is the question not rather about causes in becoming, as parents are? Yet I am tired of pressing further. I note instead that you slip by cleverly when you confuse what you call causes in being with what they call essentially subordinated causes, as if causes subordinated in this way were not really in becoming, not only with respect to conservation, but especially with respect to first production. Meanwhile you say that the question concerns causes in being and not in becoming, and at the same time you quietly evade what I mentioned about Aristotle for no other purpose than to show that these matters are not so open by natural light as you assert, if indeed Aristotle did not lack the light of nature.

## Notes

- Gassendi reasserts the exact scope of the regress objection: parentage is a series of causes in becoming, not the essentially subordinated causal series Descartes needs.
- The scholastic distinction is accepted as vocabulary but turned against Descartes' use of it.
- Accidentally subordinated causes can survive the destruction of prior causes and themselves act or become causes.
- Descartes' reply is treated as a conflation: causes in being are slid into essentially subordinated causes, while the actual parent-series objection is ignored.
- Aristotle remains evidence against natural-light obviousness, not an authority replacing proof.
