# Parts

This directory keeps longer sequential translation chunks for the *Disquisitio* campaign.

The campaign began as citation-level grounding for Cassirer's Vol. 2 Gassendi chapter, but sequential translation is now an experiment in its own right. Parts should still begin near a real source pressure point and route findings back into `journal.md`, `glossary.yaml`, and the Vol. 2 crosswalk when relevant.

## Parts

- `001-dubitatio-iii-adventitious-ideas-and-sun.md`: opening of `In Meditationem III, Dubitatio III`, from Gassendi's adventitious-origin title through Descartes' reply and the beginning of Gassendi's rejoinder.
- `002-instantia-born-blind-and-idea-as-image.md`: Gassendi's rejoinder on the born-blind test, Descartes' definition of idea, and the difference between true idea/image and necessary inference.
- `003-analogical-knowledge-substance-and-god.md`: close of Dubitatio III and opening of Dubitatio IV, moving from image versus inference to substance, objective reality, and analogical ideas of God.
- `004-response-intellect-imagination-and-substance.md`: Descartes' reply to Dubitatio IV and Gassendi's counter on intellect, imagination, substance, and accidents.
- `005-first-humans-and-the-idea-of-god.md`: close of Dubitatio IV, giving Gassendi's genealogy of the idea of God and his critique of infinity as enlarged finite conception.
- `006-cause-effect-and-objective-reality.md`: opening of Dubitatio V, where Gassendi redirects the causal axiom toward material cause and demotes objective reality to representation, likeness, symmetry, relation, or mode.
- `007-reality-modality-and-impressed-ideas.md`: Gassendi's `Instantia I`, turning objective reality into modality, representation, and seal/wax impression.
- `008-self-idea-and-material-things.md`: `Dubitatio VI` and Descartes' reply on self-idea, reflected cognition, and whether corporeal ideas can come from the mind alone.
- `009-material-existence-and-self-action.md`: Gassendi's rejoinder on obvious material existence, mental images, self-action, and Descartes' spinning-top example.
- `010-god-infinite-substance-and-infinite-worlds.md`: `Dubitatio VII` and Descartes' reply on the idea of God, infinite substance, amplification, and infinite worlds.
- `011-private-demonstration-and-cartesian-circle.md`: opening of Gassendi's `Instantia` after Dubitatio VII, restating Descartes' proof and attacking it as private, falsely abstracted, and circular.
- `012-major-proposition-and-royal-road.md`: Gassendi's critique of the major proposition, opposing the public royal road through the universe to Descartes' private enclosed idea and reducing objective reality to relation or non-being.
- `013-minor-assumption-and-divine-attributes.md`: Gassendi's critique of the minor premise, tracing divine ideas to words and instruction while treating substance, infinity, eternity, and divine attributes as not clearly comprehended.
- `014-sub-assumption-paralogism-and-infinite-worlds.md`: Gassendi's critique of the sub-assumption, conceding divine excess while denying `in solo Deo` and restating the infinite-worlds counterproof as a paralogism test.
- `015-infinite-representation-and-human-idea.md`: close of the `Instantia` after Dubitatio VII, where Gassendi attacks Descartes' claim that the human idea represents the whole infinite and rejects the triangle analogy.
- `016-defect-comparison-and-actuality.md`: `Dubitatio VIII` and Descartes' response, deflating the proof from self-defect into finite comparison and distinguishing what is actual in an idea from what is actual in the represented thing.
- `017-true-idea-equivocation-and-infinite-leap.md`: Gassendi's `Instantia` after Dubitatio VIII, attacking equivocation over `vera idea`, the leap from more-perfect to infinite, and the objective-being circle.
- `018-conservation-creation-and-causal-regress.md`: `Dubitatio IX`, separating production from conservation, challenging temporal recreation, allowing generative regress, and explaining divine perfections through finite composition and amplification.
- `019-response-fieri-esse-and-conservation.md`: Descartes' response to Dubitatio IX, making the cause `secundum fieri` / `secundum esse` distinction explicit and defending continuous divine conservation.
- `020-instantia-ix-fieri-esse-and-sunlight.md`: opening of Gassendi's `Instantia` after Dubitatio IX, rejecting the inference from cause-in-being vocabulary and using sunlight as a failed model for conserving the same individual thing.
- `021-time-nature-and-duration.md`: Gassendi's critique of Descartes' temporal premise, treating time as obscure, rejecting private/internal duration, and distinguishing common moments from separable time-parts.
- `022-time-demonstration-and-petitio-principii.md`: Gassendi's formal reconstruction of Descartes' time argument and first objections, from false premise and heterogeneous deduction to the charge of begging the question.
- `023-force-action-and-natural-light.md`: Gassendi's fifth and sixth objections to the time argument, challenging the same-force claim, creation ex nihilo, and the appeal to natural light.
- `024-perseverance-and-positive-action.md`: Gassendi's return to Descartes' response about creaturely perseverance, continuous influx, and whether divine positive destruction implies imperfection.
- `025-parents-and-thinking-causes.md`: Gassendi's renewed parent-cause objection, arguing that parents or their minds are thinking causes with the idea of God.
- `026-regress-and-subordination.md`: Gassendi's clarification that the disputed infinite regress concerns accidentally subordinated causes in becoming, not essentially subordinated causes in being.
- `027-amplification-and-necessity.md`: close of the `Instantia` after Dubitatio IX, rejecting the inference from amplified beyond-human perfections to necessary divine existence.
- `028-impressed-idea-and-likeness.md`: `Dubitatio X`, pressing Descartes' claim that the idea of God is an innate artisan's mark impressed in the mind and that creation implies divine likeness.
- `029-response-indivisible-essence-and-apelles.md`: Descartes' response to Dubitatio X, defending indivisible essences, the triangle analogy, Apelles' mark, and limited image-likeness.
- `030-first-two-syllogisms.md`: opening of Gassendi's `Instantia` after Dubitatio X, recasting the Meditation's closing movement and its first two arguments in syllogistic form.
- `031-third-syllogism-and-partial-cognition.md`: Gassendi's third syllogistic reconstruction, turning Descartes' non-comprehending touch of divine perfections into a partial-cognition critique of the idea of infinite being.
- `032-fourth-syllogism-and-nondeception.md`: Gassendi's fourth syllogistic reconstruction, showing divine non-deception being stored for later use as if already demonstrated.
- `033-indivisible-essence-and-accidents.md`: Gassendi's reply to Descartes' indivisible-essence defense, arguing that distinct ideas represent accidents and circumstances while essence remains confusedly hidden.
- `034-divine-essence-and-essential-attributes.md`: Gassendi applies the accident/essence critique to God's essence, essential attributes, a posteriori knowledge, and the confusion of idea with thing-in-itself.
- `035-acarus-microscope-and-idea-growth.md`: Gassendi uses microscope, telescope, distance, painting, and seal analogies to argue that increased distinctness adds to the idea.
- `036-triangle-and-amplification.md`: Gassendi blocks Descartes' triangle analogy and rejects the all-at-once idea of infinite being as either inconsistent with divine impression or question-begging.
- `037-apelles-and-artisan-mark.md`: Gassendi returns to Apelles, separating a mark of expert workmanship from an image that represents the maker's person or likeness.
- `038-image-likeness-and-human-shaped-god.md`: Gassendi clarifies image-likeness, prototype/copy conformity, and why imperfect human thinking cannot represent divine thought enough for natural demonstration.
- `039-disagreement-and-triangle.md`: Gassendi rejects the triangle analogy for human disagreement about God and argues that a divine impressed idea should be uniform across minds.
- `040-meditatio-iv-judgment-error-and-final-causes.md`: opening of `In Meditationem IV`, where Gassendi challenges Descartes' account of fallible judgment, defends final causes as natural-theological evidence, and records Descartes' response.
- `041-negative-idea-and-clear-distinct-circle.md`: Gassendi's `Instantia` on the negative idea of nothing, participation in non-being, the evaded fallible-judgment objection, and the clear-and-distinct circle.
- `042-final-causes-use-and-efficient-cause.md`: Gassendi identifies use and end, uses the bridge analogy to route final causes toward efficient cause, and contrasts manifest bodily ends with obscure proximate efficient causes.
- `043-sense-isolation-and-corporeal-images.md`: close of the `Instantia` after `Dubitatio I`, testing Descartes' sense-deprived mind and defending corporeal images as occasions and steps toward knowledge of God.
- `044-judgment-faculty-and-universal-perfection.md`: `Dubitatio II` and Descartes' response on the created judging faculty, part/whole perfection, error-liability, positive imperfection, and negation of greater perfection.
- `045-positive-imperfection-and-smallness.md`: Gassendi's `Instantia` after `Dubitatio II`, separating defect from smallness and attacking Descartes' positive-imperfection and body-covered-with-eyes replies.
- `046-will-intellect-and-error.md`: `Dubitatio III` and Descartes' response on whether error arises because will or judgment extends more widely than intellect.
- `047-intellectual-operations-and-blind-will.md`: opening sections of Gassendi's `Instantia` after `Dubitatio III`, restoring judgment and reasoning to intellect and treating will as a blind power following intellectual presentation.
- `048-descartes-examples-and-wills-scope.md`: Gassendi applies the rebuilt faculty map to Descartes' thin-body mind and poisoned-apple examples, arguing that will and understanding remain coextensive at the same level.
- `049-indifference-freedom-and-libentia.md`: section VI of the same `Instantia`, distinguishing indifference from freedom, rooting will's flexibility in intellect's, and introducing `libentia` for necessary but glad adherence to the supreme good.
- `050-corrected-apprehension-and-perseverance.md`: section VII of the same `Instantia`, explaining correction of will through corrected apprehensions or notions and answering how we avoid persevering in error.
- `051-falsity-judgment-and-false-gold.md`: close of the same `Instantia`, locating falsity properly in judgment as privation of conformity and using false gold to complete the will/intellect account.
- `052-clear-perception-and-missing-method.md`: `Dubitatio IV` and Descartes' response, shifting from the cause of error to the still-missing method for recognizing genuine clear and distinct perception.
- `053-preface-minute-reading-and-promised-method.md`: opening of the `Instantia` after `Dubitatio IV`, defending piecewise examination and asking where the promised method was actually delivered.
- `054-circle-and-counterfeit-clarity.md`: Gassendi's formal reconstruction of the clear-and-distinct circle and the resulting need to distinguish genuine clarity from counterfeit clarity.
- `055-survey-four-rules-and-residual-doubt.md`: close of the `Instantia` after `Dubitatio IV`, testing exhaustive survey and the four method rules as still lacking a criterion that nothing has been omitted.
- `056-material-essence-universals-and-triangle.md`: opening of `In Meditationem V`, where Gassendi treats universal essences and the mental triangle as abstractions from singular sensory materials rather than real natures outside intellect.
- `057-response-immutable-essence-and-true-triangle.md`: Descartes' response to `Dubitatio I`, defending God-dependent immutable mathematical truths and the prior idea of the true triangle.
- `058-material-essence-thinness-and-corpuscles.md`: opening of Gassendi's `Instantia` after the response, challenging Descartes' thin treatment of material essence and naming corpuscular explanation as the richer physical route.
- `059-immutable-eternal-natures-and-possibles.md`: Gassendi presses God-dependent eternal natures into a production dilemma, rejects non-existing true natures, and recasts universal natures as intellect-formed notions from singulars.
- `060-geometrical-ideas-and-innate-faculty.md`: close of the same `Instantia`, answering Descartes' geometrical-idea argument and reducing innate ideas to an innate faculty for knowing things from singulars.
- `061-existence-perfection-and-the-triangle-analogy.md`: `Dubitatio II`, where Gassendi attacks the proof from divine essence to existence by denying that existence is a perfection and by testing the triangle, mountain, horse, Pegasus, and rhombus comparisons.
- `062-response-necessary-existence-and-divine-essence.md`: Descartes' response to `Dubitatio II`, defending necessary existence as a strict divine property and distinguishing God's relation to essence from the triangle's.
- `063-property-attribute-and-transcendental-existence.md`: opening of Gassendi's `Instantia` after `Dubitatio II`, distinguishing physical property from logical attribute and treating existence as transcendental rather than a special property.
- `064-idea-state-and-real-state-paralogism.md`: Gassendi locates the ontological proof's paralogism in a changed supposition between the idea-state and real-state, using dialectical examples and the golden mountain.
- `065-assumption-divine-nature-and-existence-in-idea.md`: close of Gassendi's `Instantia` after `Dubitatio II`, attacking Descartes' minor assumption as existence in the idea confused with existence in the thing itself.
- `066-certainty-truth-and-the-skeptics.md`: `Dubitatio III` and Descartes' response, testing whether mathematical certainty depends on prior knowledge of a truthful God by appeal to geometry, atheists, mathematicians, and skeptics.
- `067-skeptics-hypotheses-and-natural-certainty.md`: Gassendi's `Instantia` after `Dubitatio III`, arguing that skeptics challenged hypotheses and dogmatic arrogance, not apparent mathematical results, and that natural certainty depends on evidence.
- `068-intellection-imagination-and-degree.md`: opening of `In Meditationem VI`, where Gassendi treats intellection and imagination as one cognitive power differing by degree of distinctness, effort, confusion, and slackening.
- `069-response-two-modes-and-true-idea-of-mind.md`: Descartes' response to `Dubitatio I`, defending intellection and imagination as two modes of operation and locating the true idea of mind in thought alone.
- `070-chiliagon-name-and-distinct-perception.md`: Gassendi's first `Instantia` section after Descartes' response, reducing clear chiliagon-understanding to clarity of name or definition unless distinct perception can grasp parts one by one.
- `071-mind-alone-and-corporeal-accommodation.md`: Gassendi's second `Instantia` section, arguing that Descartes has not proved mind can understand without imagination and clarifying corporeal images as accommodations rather than adequate divine representations.
- `072-sense-appearance-and-judgment.md`: `Dubitatio Secunda` and Descartes' response, distinguishing passive sensory appearance from judgment while Descartes redirects the issue to external-object truth.
- `073-prejudice-tower-and-external-truth.md`: Gassendi's `Instantia` after `Dubitatio Secunda`, answering Descartes' prejudice charge through probability, corrected tower evidence, and the challenge of external truth.
- `074-separability-mind-body-and-subtle-body.md`: `Dubitatio Tertia`, where Gassendi attacks the inference from separate clear ideas to real separability and says Descartes has proved at most distinction from the gross body, not pure incorporeality.
- `075-response-separability-and-subtle-body.md`: Descartes' short response to `Dubitatio Tertia`, insisting that the proof concerns every body, however subtle, and that Gassendi confuses intelligibility with real being.
- `076-paralogism-and-divine-power.md`: opening of Gassendi's `Instantia` after the response, reversing Descartes' confusion charge and attacking the private divine-power principle behind the separability proof.
- `077-gross-body-subtle-body-and-precision.md`: Gassendi's rejoinder that Descartes' own wording targets the gross body and that considering mind precisely as thinking thing or substance does not exclude subtle body or prove incorporeality.
- `078-formal-proof-and-divine-power.md`: Gassendi reconstructs Descartes' separability proof as a troubled syllogism and argues that absolute divine power leaves philosophy for theology while ordinary divine power adds nothing to natural separability.
- `079-criterion-triangle-and-complete-things.md`: Gassendi asks for a criterion distinguishing separables from inseparables and reconstructs Descartes' complete-entity reply to the triangle-property objection.
- `080-complete-substances-and-inadequate-cognition.md`: Gassendi argues that Descartes begs the question by treating the disputed mind/body pair as complete substances and that human inadequate cognition cannot exclude an indivisible nexus.
- `081-assumption-special-extension-and-concrete-subject.md`: Gassendi applies the criterion critique to Descartes' assumption, arguing that the human mind case turns on special thinking and special extension in the same disputed subject, not on generic body or cross-subject abstraction.
- `082-parity-proof-and-intellectual-separation.md`: Gassendi closes the third doubt by parodying Descartes' proof, showing that the same clear-and-distinct form can be mirrored to prove mind is not incorporeal if intellectual separation is confused with real separation.
- `083-corporeal-species-and-unextended-mind.md`: `Dubitatio IV`, where Gassendi asks how an unextended mind can receive or use a corporeal species of body and argues that neither diffusion through the body nor location in a brain-point secures incorporeality.
- `084-response-pure-intellection-and-corporeal-species.md`: Descartes' response to `Dubitatio IV`, separating pure intellection from corporeal species, treating imagination as applying mind to an extended corporeal species, and denying that union with body makes mind extended.
- `085-intelligible-species-and-separated-mind.md`: opening of Gassendi's `Instantia` after `Responsio IV`, arguing that Descartes evades the question whether intellectual cognition of body needs an incorporeal species and pressing the case of separated mind without brain.
- `086-aspectual-clarity-and-thinking-thing.md`: section II of the same `Instantia`, where Gassendi answers Descartes' blind-man/sun reply by treating `res cogitans` like `res calefaciens`: an aspectual predicate, not knowledge of nature.
- `087-shared-blindness-and-hidden-mind.md`: section III of the same `Instantia`, where Gassendi denies that shared human blindness about mind lets Descartes infer there is nothing hidden beyond thought.
- `088-nonextension-bucephalus-and-private-principle.md`: section IV of the same `Instantia`, where Gassendi treats non-extension as a negative marker, corrects the Bucephalus `Musica`/`Musca` comparison, and recasts Descartes' self-idea as private principle rather than public demonstration.
- `089-mind-body-mixture-and-pain.md`: `Dubitatio V`, where Gassendi tests Descartes' sailor-in-ship contrast, quasi-mixture, and confused pain/hunger/thirst modes against incorporeal and indivisible mind.
- `090-response-union-and-nonimagined-parts.md`: Descartes' response to `Dubitatio V`, rejecting comparison between mind-body union and body-body mixture and denying that parts must be imagined in mind because mind understands bodily parts.
- `091-doubts-objections-and-idea-containment.md`: opening of Gassendi's `Instantia` after `Responsio V`, arguing that doubts against conclusions are objections against reasons, that `toto genere diversa` repeats the disputed question, and that Descartes' earth-magnitude reductio confuses representative/objective presence with real containment.
- `092-optical-species-and-representing-parts.md`: continuation of the same `Instantia`, adding the retinal, optic-nerve, brain, and separated-mind species argument that any representation of parts must retain some part-structure, even if much smaller than the represented object.
- `093-remaining-heads-and-brief-response.md`: Gassendi's `Praeteritio` on the remaining Sixth Meditation heads and Descartes' brief response, with sensory reliability, dreams, divine non-deception, and human weakness treated as passed-over rather than fully contested.
- `094-passed-over-contradiction-and-prolixity.md`: Gassendi's `Instantia` after the `Praeteritio` response, reopening the waking/dreaming certainty argument and answering Descartes' charge that non-contradiction plus prolixity exposes a lack of reasons.
- `095-conclusio-and-friendship.md`: Gassendi's closing `Conclusio`, lowering the authority of his own judgment through the taste analogy and preserving friendship while leaving Descartes free to disregard the objections.
- `096-response-true-gassendi-and-easy-answers.md`: Descartes' response to the `Conclusio`, separating the polemical "flesh" from the true Gassendi while maintaining that no reason or conclusion was raised that he could not easily answer.
- `097-final-answer-mind-flesh-and-demonstration.md`: final answer after Descartes' response, rejecting the mind/flesh split and restating that the dispute concerns the force of Descartes' demonstrations, not the truth of God or the immortality of souls.
- `098-postrema-errata-and-omitted-passage.md`: final `POSTREMA` apparatus page, recording a noisy errata/corrigenda sheet and its legible omitted page-95 passage without treating it as a normal objection-response unit.
