## Why This Batch

This batch tests whether Bruno should be handled with a full translation
journal discipline. The opening is not doctrinal exposition yet; it is patronal,
emblematic, and defensive. That matters because the work presents itself as a
philosophical cargo moving under protection before it presents its explicit
claims about cause, principle, and the one.

NOLANO

DE LA CAUSA, PRINCIPIO ET UNO

AL MOLTO ILLUSTRE
SIGNOR DI MAUVISSIERO
STAMPATO IN VENEZIA
Anno M.D.LXXXIIII.


EPISTOLA PROEMIALE

scritta al molto illustre
Signor Michel di Castelnovo,
Signor di Mauvissiero, Concressalto e di Ionvilla,
Cavalier de l'ordine del Re Cristianissimo,
Conseglier nel suo privato Conseglio,
Capitano di cinquanta uomini d'arme,
ed Ambasciator alla Serenissima
Regina d'Inghilterra.


MOLTO illustre ed unico cavalliero, se io drizzo gli occhi de la
considerazione a rimirar la vostra longanimità, perseveranza e sollecitudine,
con cui, gionto ufficio ad ufficio, beneficio a beneficio, m'avete vinto,
legato e stretto, e con cui solete superar ogni difficultà, scampare ogni
periglio, condure a fine ogni degnissimo dissegno; vegno a comprendere come a
voi propriamente convegna quella generosa divisa, con cui il vostro terribil
cimiero v'adornate: là dove quel liquido umore che, stillando a goccia a
goccia con dolcezza ferisce, con perseveranza lungamente et altamente
imprime, intenerisce, incava, doma, rompe e spiana un certo denso, aspro, duro
e ruvido sasso.

## Working Translation

GIORDANO BRUNO
THE NOLAN

ON CAUSE, PRINCIPLE, AND THE ONE

TO THE VERY ILLUSTRIOUS
SIGNOR OF MAUVISSIERE
PRINTED IN VENICE
Anno M.D.LXXXIIII.


PROEMIAL EPISTLE

written to the very illustrious
Signor Michel of Castelnovo,
Signor of Mauvissiero, Concressalto, and Ionvilla,
Knight of the Order of the Most Christian King,
Councillor in his private Council,
Captain of fifty men-at-arms,
and Ambassador to the Most Serene
Queen of England.


Very illustrious and singular knight, if I direct the eyes of consideration to
behold your longanimity, perseverance, and solicitude, with which, adding
office to office and benefit to benefit, you have conquered me, bound me, and
held me fast, and with which you are accustomed to overcome every difficulty,
escape every danger, and bring every most worthy design to completion, I come
to understand how properly that generous device belongs to you, with which you
adorn your terrible crest: there, that liquid humor which, dripping drop by
drop, wounds gently, and by perseverance impresses long and deeply, softens,
hollows, tames, breaks, and levels a certain dense, rough, hard, and rugged
stone.

## Translation Pressure

`Nolano` is more than a place label. It is Bruno's self-signature, and for now
`the Nolan` keeps that slightly strange authorial posture visible.

The title terms `causa`, `principio`, and `uno` should remain literal for the
moment. `Principle` may eventually need to carry origin, beginning, or source,
but the title should not be harmonized before the dialogues teach the range.

The dedication's first movement is emblematic. `Divisa`, `cimiero`, `liquido
umore`, `stilla`, and `sasso` stage a device in which persistent, gentle force
works on hardness. That is rhetoric, but it may also foreshadow Bruno's idea of
philosophical force acting under resistance.

The sentence wants to remain long. Breaking it into modern short units would
make the prose easier but would also erase the accumulating pressure of
obligation, praise, and image.

## Calibration Result

Bruno needs a journaled translation pass, not only a cleanup pass. The source
prose asks for:

- source-local batches with source spans named
- a running glossary for terms under pressure
- notes on rhetorical scene as well as doctrine
- caution around modernizing names and philosophical vocabulary too quickly

For a full-work goal, the next practical choice is whether to translate the
remaining front matter first or use `Dialogo primo` as the first doctrinal
calibration.
