# Bruno Translation Journal

## 2026-05-08 - Source-Local Scaffold Opened

Opened a source-local scaffold under
`sources/italian/bruno-de-la-causa-principio-et-uno/` rather than promoting the
work directly into `texts/`.

The source is a Liber Liber e-text based on Giovanni Aquilecchia's 1973 Einaudi
edition. It is original-language Italian, with early modern forms and Latin
front-matter pressure, but it is not a diplomatic 1584 print transcription. The
full local copy in `source/full/` is the working authority for this scaffold.

The first split follows the text's own units:

- metadata and table of contents
- title page and proemial epistle
- arguments of the five dialogues
- `Ai principi de l'universo`
- the five dialogues

The split files are line-normalized working files. They preserve textual order
and section boundaries but not page evidence. If this becomes a full campaign,
page witness work should be named explicitly rather than assumed.

## 2026-05-08 - Calibration: Proemial Epistle Opening

Completed a first calibration batch:
`parts/001-proemial-epistle-opening-calibration.md`.

The opening shows immediately that Bruno should not be handled as simple
cleanup or thin translation. The dedication begins in patronage, emblem, and
defensive rhetoric before turning toward doctrine. That framing matters because
the work is already staging its speech as protected, endangered, and borne
through hostile conditions.

Terms under pressure from the first batch:

- `causa`
- `principio`
- `uno`
- `contemplazion reale`
- `longanimità`
- `divisa`
- `cimiero`
- `liquido umore`

Current decision: use a richer, Erkenntnisproblem-style journal discipline for
any sustained Bruno work. The prose is periodic, figurative, and philosophically
loaded enough that a thin translated-text pass would lose the reasons the work
matters.

Next decision point: choose whether the first full goal should start with the
front matter and `argomenti`, or move directly into `Dialogo primo` after this
calibration.

## 2026-05-08 - Front Matter Translation Pass

Added working translation drafts for the remaining front matter:

- `parts/001-title-and-proemial-epistle.md`
- `parts/002-argomenti.md`
- `parts/003-ai-principi-de-l-universo.md`

The `argomenti` confirm that the front matter is a structural map, not
secondary apparatus. Bruno announces the whole conceptual order before the
dialogues unfold: cause/principle, form/matter, act/potency,
substance/accident, universal soul, matter's excellence, and the one.

The verse threshold confirms that vision and unveiling are not incidental
images. The poems link love, sight, truth, darkness, time, and the one before
the dialogue form begins.

Source issues noticed in the Liber Liber e-text:

- `efficiente,etè`
- `partietescrementi`
- `Orcoetavaro`

These look like joined e-text forms rather than meaningful Bruno spellings. I
separated the obvious words in translation, but they should be reviewed against
a better witness if this work is promoted.

Latin note: the translations in `003-ai-principi-de-l-universo.md` are
provisional. They preserve image order and conceptual pressure, but the Latin
verse should receive a dedicated check before any public or canonical version.

Next work: translate `Dialogo primo` as the first doctrinal calibration and
then write a state note before continuing to Dialogues 2-5.
