## What This First Block Shows

Bruno should be pursued with a journaled translation method, not a simple text
cleanup method. The front matter and first dialogue establish the rhetorical
conditions for the work before the doctrine begins: patronage, danger,
shipwrecked philosophical cargo, light and vision, mixed comic-serious form,
correction rather than revenge, and opposition to pedantic word-authority.

The `argomenti` are especially important. They preview the actual doctrinal
sequence:

- cause and principle
- efficient, formal, and final cause
- universal soul
- form and matter
- act and potency
- substance and accident
- the one
- coincidence of contraries

`Dialogo primo` does not yet argue those points directly. It clears the field
for them.

## Translation Risks

The Latin verse in `003-ai-principi-de-l-universo.md` is provisional and should
be checked again before any canonical or public edition.

The Liber Liber e-text contains some likely joined-word artifacts, including
`efficiente,etè`, `partietescrementi`, and `Orcoetavaro`. The working
translations separate the obvious forms, but a promoted text project should
verify them against a better witness.

Several names remain source-form rather than normalized:

- Mauvissiero / Mauvissiere
- Michel di Castelnovo
- Alessandro Dicsono Arelio
- Polihimnio / Poliinnia

This is intentional for now. Name normalization should be done as a separate
editorial pass.
