# Terminology Note: Complicato / Esplicato

Status: source-local terminology note. Do not treat this as promotion to a
stable English term.

Date: 2026-05-08

## Current Decision

Keep `complicated` / `explicated` in the source-local working translation for
now.

The choice is intentionally technical. `Complicato` does not mean ordinary
English "confusing" or "overly complex." In Bruno's Cusan pressure, it means
folded, gathered, or comprehended into unity. `Esplicato` means unfolded,
distributed, or expressed in determinate finite modes.

That makes the current pair partly awkward and partly useful. It preserves the
Latin-root pressure and keeps Bruno's relation to `complicare` / `explicare`
audible, but it risks misleading a reader who hears only modern everyday
English.

## Local Evidence

- `parts/007c-dialogo-quarto-matter-contains-forms.md`: matter has no fixed
  dimensions "so that it may have all"; forms and dimensions come from its
  womb rather than from outside.
- `source/raw/007-dialogo-quarto.txt:130`: the superior/inferior matter
  contrast uses `esplicatamente` and `complicatamente`.
- `source/raw/007-dialogo-quarto.txt:154-156`: Polihimnio names Aristotle as
  preferring `esplicazione` of form to `implicazione`; Dicsono answers that
  expressed and sensible explication is consequent on actuality, not its
  principal reason.
- `source/raw/008-dialogo-quinto.txt:26`: the supreme one is
  `complicatamente` one, immense, infinite, and is `esplicatamente` in sensible
  bodies where act and potency are distinct.
- `source/raw/008-dialogo-quinto.txt:36`: ascent to knowledge complicates the
  multitude; descent into production explicates unity.
- `source/raw/008-dialogo-quinto.txt:74-78`: final unity is not an explicated
  singularity, but `complicante e comprendente`; the denary, centenary, and
  millenary examples show unity that contains more without ceasing to be one.

## Alternatives

`enfolded` / `unfolded`

Best for reader clarity, especially if the text is later prepared for TTS or a
public-facing reader. The cost is that it hides Bruno's Latin-root technical
pressure and weakens the link to `implicazione`.

`implicated` / `explicated`

Strong for the fourth dialogue, where Bruno explicitly works with forms held
within matter before expression. The cost is that `implicated` in modern
English often sounds legal, causal, or accusatory rather than folded-within.

`complicated` / `explicated`

Strongest for source-local discipline and Cusan continuity. The cost is that
it needs a note, because `complicated` now carries the wrong everyday sense.

## Use Rule

For the source-local Bruno scaffold, keep `complicated` / `explicated` and keep
the glossary entries `open`.

For a later audiobook or public reader, decide deliberately between:

- retaining `complicated` / `explicated` with an introductory terminology note,
  or
- shifting the running English to `enfolded` / `unfolded` while preserving the
  Italian in notes.

Do not silently mix the pairs passage by passage. Bruno is building an
argument through repeated pressure, and the reader needs to hear the recurrence.
