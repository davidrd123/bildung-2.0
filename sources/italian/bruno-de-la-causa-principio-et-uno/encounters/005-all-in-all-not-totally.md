## Why This Passage

This is Bruno's most useful guardrail against an over-simple "everything is
everything" reading. He affirms all-in-all, but immediately adds mode and
totality constraints.

## Core Passage

The universe contains all being and all modes of being. Each thing has all
being, but not all modes of being, because many forms are incompossible in the
same subject. Therefore all is in all, but not totally and not in every mode in
each thing. Every thing is one, but not in one mode.

The later summary repeats the constraint: whatever is in the universe is
everywhere according to the mode of its capacity; each thing contains the whole
world-soul in its own way, but not totally.

## Mechanism

Unity is substantial; difference belongs to mode, accident, figure, complexion,
condition, and capacity.

The formula is not identity without difference. It is one substance under
finite modal constraints.

## Pressure

Track these terms together:

- `tutto in tutto`
- `non totalmente`
- `non omnimodamente`
- `unimodamente`
- `modo`
- `capacita`

The translation should not smooth `omnimodamente` away. It is the local guard
against a flat monism.

## Use

Use this encounter whenever Bruno's one-substance language starts to sound too
easy. The passage gives the limit: each thing has all being, but only according
to its determinate mode and capacity.

## Open Questions

- Does `all being` in each thing require a later explanatory note for readers
  who will otherwise hear numerical totality?
- Should `capacity` become the preferred rendering for `capacita` in this zone,
  or does "mode of its capacity" need a different English form?
