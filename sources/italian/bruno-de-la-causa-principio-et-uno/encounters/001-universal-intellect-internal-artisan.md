## Why This Passage

This is Bruno's most concentrated account of the universal physical efficient.
The universal intellect is not merely an external maker. It is the intimate
faculty of the world-soul, the world's internal artisan, forming matter from
within.

## Core Passage

Bruno calls the universal intellect the intimate, most real, proper faculty and
potential part of the world-soul. It fills the whole, illuminates the universe,
directs nature to produce its species, impregnates matter with forms, and
explicates what is confused in matter's womb.

The strongest image is vegetal and embryological: the artisan works from
inside the seed, root, trunk, branches, buds, leaves, flowers, and fruits; in
animals it works from seed and heart outward through bones, cartilage,
arteries, pores, fibers, and nerves.

## Mechanism

Efficient causation is both external and internal.

It is external because the efficient is not one part among the produced
compounds. It is internal because its operation does not stand outside matter
like an artisan carving wood from without. It works in matter, through matter,
and from the inside of natural generation.

## Pressure

This passage holds together several terms that must not be flattened:

- `intelletto universale`
- `anima del mondo`
- `causa efficiente`
- `artefice interno`
- `esplicare`
- `seno della materia`

The translation should keep `internal artisan` rather than smoothing to
"immanent cause." Bruno is making a causal claim through an artisan image, and
the artisan image remains active.

## Use

Use this encounter when tracking Bruno's difference from a merely mechanical or
external efficient cause. It is also the strongest source-local bridge to later
work on form as internally organized production rather than imposed shape.

## Open Questions

- How far can `internal artisan` be compared with later organismic or process
