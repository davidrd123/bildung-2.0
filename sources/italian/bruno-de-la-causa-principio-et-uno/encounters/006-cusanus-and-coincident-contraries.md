## Why This Passage

This is the explicit Cusanus passage and the strongest local evidence for the
`complicato` / `esplicato` terminology note.

## Core Passage

Bruno asks what is more contrary than straight and curved, yet at the minimum
arc and minimum chord they agree, and at the maximum the infinite circle and
infinite straight line coincide. He names Cusanus directly as the one who
divinely noted the geometrical secret.

The passage continues through the missing figures: triangle, square, acute and
obtuse angle, hot and cold, generation and corruption, love and hate. The final
claim is that whoever wants the greatest secrets of nature should contemplate
the minima and maxima of contraries. Profound magic is knowing how to draw out
the contrary after finding the point of union.

The final unity is then defined as not explicated under number and
distribution, but complicating and comprehending.

## Mechanism

Contraries do not merely alternate. At their minimum, maximum, source, or
transition point, they reveal a shared principle.

This is not the disappearance of difference in ordinary finite cases. It is a
method for finding unity by following contraries to the points where their
opposition ceases to be final.

## Pressure

The three missing diagrams matter because the argument is partly visual. A
