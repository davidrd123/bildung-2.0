# Dialogo Terzo, Absolute Act, Potency, and the One

[[speaker: Dicsono Arelio]]
What will you say of death, corruption, vices, defects,
monsters? Do you wish that these too have place in that which is the whole,
which can be and is in act all that is in potency?

[[speaker: Teofilo]]
These things are not act and potency, but defect and impotence, which
are found in explicated things because they are not all that they can be, and
they strive toward what they can be. Hence, not being able to be together and at
one stroke so many things, they lose one being in order to have the other; and
sometimes they confound one being with another, and sometimes are diminished,
lacking, and maimed by the incompatibility of this being and that, and by the
occupation of matter in this and that. Now, returning to the proposition, the
first absolute principle is greatness and magnitude, and is such magnitude and
greatness that it is all that it can be. It is not great with such greatness
that it can be greater, nor that it can be lesser, nor that it can be divided,
as every other greatness that is not all that it can be; therefore it is
greatness maximum, minimum, infinite, impartible, and of every measure. It is
not greater, by being minimum; it is not minimum, by being the same maximum; it
is beyond every equality, because it is all that it can be. What I say of
greatness, understand of everything that can be said: because it is similarly
goodness that is every goodness that can be; it is beauty that is all the
beautiful that can be; and there is no other beautiful that is all that it can
be except this one. One is that which is all and can be all absolutely. In
natural things beyond this we see no thing that is other than what it is in act,
according to which it is what it can be by having a species of actuality;
nevertheless, not even in this single specific being is any particular ever all
that it can be. Here is the sun: it is not all that the sun can be; it is not
everywhere the sun can be, because when it is eastern to the earth, it is not
western, nor meridian, nor of another aspect. Now if we wish to show the mode in
which God is sun, we will say, because he is all that he can be, that he is
together east, west, meridian, midnight, and of whatever point of the whole
convexity of the earth; whence, if we wish to understand that this sun moves and
changes place, either by its own revolution or by that of the earth, because it
is not actually in one point without potency to be in all the others, and
therefore has aptitude to be there, if then it is all that it can be and
possesses all that it is apt to possess, it will be together everywhere and in
all. It is so thoroughly most movable and swiftest that it is also most stable
and most immobile. Therefore among divine discourses we find that it is called
stable forever and swiftest, running from end to end; because that is understood
as immobile which in the same instant departs from the point of east and has
returned to the point of east, besides being seen no less in east than in west
and any other point of its circuit. For this reason there is no more reason for
us to say that it departs and returns, has departed and returned, from that
point to that point, than from any other of the infinite points to the same.
Hence it will come to be all and always in the whole circle and in any part of
it; and consequently every individual point of the ecliptic contains the whole
diameter of the sun. And thus one individual comes to contain the divisible;
this does not occur by natural possibility, but by supernatural possibility, I
mean when it is supposed that the sun were that which is in act all that it can
be. Such absolute power is not only what the sun can be, but what every thing is
and what every thing can be: potency of all potencies, act of all acts, life of
all lives, soul of all souls, being of all being. Hence it is said deeply by
the Revealer: "He who is sends me; He who is says thus." Therefore what
elsewhere is contrary and opposite is in him one and the same, and every thing
in him is the same. So discourse through differences of times and durations as
through differences of actuality and possibility. Therefore he is not an ancient
thing and is not a new thing; for which reason the Revealer well said: "first
and last."

[[speaker: Dicsono Arelio]]
This most absolute act, which is the same as most absolute
potency, cannot be comprehended by the intellect except by way of negation. It
cannot, I say, be grasped either insofar as it can be all or insofar as it is
all. For when the intellect wishes to understand, it will need to form the
intelligible species, to make itself similar, to measure itself together with
and equal itself to that species. But this is impossible, because the intellect
is never so much that it cannot be greater, and that, being immense from every
side and mode, cannot be greater. There is, then, no eye that can approach or
has access to so very high a light and so very deep an abyss.

[[speaker: Teofilo]]
The coincidence of this act with absolute potency has been described
very openly by the divine spirit where it says: "The darkness will not be
darkened from you. Night will be illuminated like day. As its darkness, so also
its light." Concluding, then, you see how great is the excellence of potency,
which, if it pleases you to call it reason of matter, which the vulgar
philosophers have not penetrated, you can treat more highly, without detracting
from divinity, than Plato in his Republic and the Timaeus. Those men, for
having raised the reason of matter too high, have been scandalous to some
theologians. This has happened either because those did not declare themselves
well, or because these did not understand well, because they always take the
signified of matter according as it is subject of natural things only, as men
nourished in the sentences of Aristotle; and they do not consider that matter
is such among others that it is common to the intelligible and sensible world,
as they say, taking the signified according to an analogical equivocation.
Therefore, before opinions are condemned, they should be very well examined,
and languages should be distinguished as meanings are distinguished. For
although all sometimes agree in one common reason of matter, afterward they are
different in the proper one. And as far as belongs to our proposition, it is
impossible, taking away the name of matter, however captious and wicked a wit
may be, that there be found a theologian who can impute impiety to me for what
I say and understand of the coincidence of potency and act, taking both terms
absolutely. Hence I would infer that, according to such proportion as it is
permitted to speak, in this simulacrum of that act and that potency, because it
is in specific act all that it is in specific potency, because the universe,
according to such a mode, is all that it can be, whatever one may wish as to
numerical act and potency, it comes to have a potency that is not absolute from
act, a soul not absolute from the animated. I do not say the composite, but the
simple; whence the universe has a first principle that is understood as the
same, no more distinctly material and formal than can be inferred from the
similitude of the aforesaid absolute potency and act. Hence it will not be
difficult or burdensome to accept in the end that the whole, according to
substance, is one, as perhaps Parmenides understood, ignobly treated by
Aristotle.

[[speaker: Dicsono Arelio]]
You wish, then, that although descending by this ladder of
nature there is double substance, one spiritual, the other corporeal, in sum
both the one and the other are reduced to one being and one root.

[[speaker: Teofilo]]
If it seems to you that this can be tolerated by those who do not
penetrate more than so far.

[[speaker: Dicsono Arelio]]
Very easily, provided you do not rise above the terms of
nature.

[[speaker: Teofilo]]
This has already been done. If, while not having that same sense and
mode of defining divinity which is common, we have a particular one,
nevertheless it is not contrary nor alien to that one, but perhaps clearer and
more explicated according to the reason that is not above our discourse, from
which I did not promise to abstain.

[[speaker: Dicsono Arelio]]
Enough has been said of the material principle according to the
reason of possibility or potency; please prepare yourself tomorrow for the
consideration of the same according to the reason of being subject.

[[speaker: Teofilo]]
So I will do.

[[speaker: Gervasio]]
Until we see one another again.

[[speaker: Polihimnio]]
With good birds.

End of the Third Dialogue.
