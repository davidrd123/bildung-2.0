# Title and Proemial Epistle

GIORDANO BRUNO

THE NOLAN

ON CAUSE, PRINCIPLE, AND THE ONE

TO THE MOST ILLUSTRIOUS

SIGNOR OF MAUVISSIERE

PRINTED IN VENICE

Anno M.D.LXXXIIII.

PROEMIAL EPISTLE

written to the most illustrious

Signor Michel of Castelnovo,

Signor of Mauvissiero, Concressalto, and Ionvilla,

Knight of the Order of the Most Christian King,

Councillor of his Privy Council,

Captain of 5 men-at-arms

and Ambassador to the Most Serene

Queen of England.

Most illustrious and singular knight, if I turn the eyes of consideration to
behold your longanimity, perseverance, and solicitude, with which, adding office
to office, benefit to benefit, you have conquered me, obligated me, and held me
fast, and are accustomed to overcome every difficulty, escape from whatever
danger, and bring all your most honorable designs to completion, I come to see
how properly that generous device belongs to you, with which you adorn your
terrible crest: where that liquid humor, which gently wounds while it drips
continually and often, by force of perseverance softens, hollows, tames,
breaks, and levels a certain dense, rough, hard, and rugged stone.

If, on the other side, I bring back to mind how, leaving your other honorable
deeds aside, by divine ordinance and high providence and predestination you are
my sufficient and solid defender against the unjust outrages that I suffer,
where a truly heroic spirit was needed in order not to lower its arms, despair,
and give itself up as conquered by so swift a torrent of criminal impostures;
with which the envy of the ignorant, the presumption of sophists, the detraction
of the malicious, the murmuring of servants, the whispers of hirelings, the
contradictions of domestics, the suspicions of the stupid, the scruples of
informers, the zeal of hypocrites, the hatreds of barbarians, the furies of
plebeians, the rages of the populace, the complaints of those struck back, and
the voices of those chastised have hurled themselves against me with all their
force; where nothing else was lacking but a discourteous, mad, and malicious
feminine indignation, whose false tears are accustomed to be more powerful than
any swollen waves and rigid tempests of presumptions, envies, detractions,
murmurings, betrayals, angers, indignations, hatreds, and furies; then I see you
as a solid, firm, and constant rock, which, rising and showing its head out of
the swollen sea, is not at all chipped, moved, or shaken by angry sky, by the
horror of winter, by violent shocks of swollen waves, by shrieking aerial
storms, or by the violent breath of north winds, but all the more grows green
again and is encrusted and reclothed with substance like itself. You, then,
endowed with a double virtue, through which the liquid and pleasant drops are
most powerful and the rigid and tempestuous waves most vain, through which
against the drops the fortunate stone becomes so weak, and against the floods
the troubled rock rises so powerful, are the one who likewise makes himself a
secure and tranquil harbor for the true Muses, and a ruinous rock upon which the
false fortifications of the impetuous designs of their enemy sails come to
vanish. I, then, whom no one could ever accuse as ungrateful, no one has blamed
as discourteous, and of whom no one can justly complain; I, hated by fools,
despised by the vile, blamed by the ignoble, vilified by scoundrels, and
persecuted by bestial spirits; I, loved by the wise, admired by the learned,
magnified by the great, esteemed by the powerful, and favored by the gods; I,
for such great favor already received from you, nourished, defended, liberated,
kept safe, and maintained in port, as one who through you has escaped from a
perilous and great tempest, consecrate to you this anchor, these ropes, these
broken sails, and these wares, dearer to me and in future more precious to the
world, so that through your favor they may not be submerged by the unjust,
turbulent, and hostile Ocean that is mine. These, hung in the sacred temple of
Fame, as they will be powerful against the arrogance of ignorance and the
voracity of time, will likewise render eternal testimony of your unconquered
favor, so that the world may know that this generous and divine offspring,
inspired by high intelligence, conceived by regulated sense, and brought forth
by the Nolan Muse, has not through you died in its swaddling clothes, and
further promises itself life while this earth, with its living back, shall keep
turning toward the eternal aspect of the other sparkling stars.

Here is that species of philosophy in which certainly and truly is found what
is sought in vain in the contrary and diverse philosophies. And first, with the
greatest brevity, I present to you in five dialogues everything that seems to
belong to the real contemplation of cause, principle, and one.
