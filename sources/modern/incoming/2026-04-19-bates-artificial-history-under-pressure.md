## Why this source matters now

Three things survive the initial localization:

### 1. Bates gathers several already-live repo surfaces in one place

The contents alone show that this is not generic AI-history packaging. It has
direct chapters on:

- Kant's critique of automatic reason
- Von Uexkull
- Lotka
- Wittgenstein on thinking
- Turing and error
- exosomatic culture
- Leroi-Gourhan on exteriorization

That does not make the book authoritative. It does make it a sharper reserve
surface than a generic contemporary AI title.

### 2. The pressure is contemporary-synthetic, not source-substitutive

Bates matters here only as a possible interlocutor on how these lines are being
retold and reconnected now. He does **not** replace:

- Kant
- Uexkull
- Lotka
- Leroi-Gourhan
- or the repo's own primary contact with any of them

The value would be bounded comparison, not delegated understanding.

### 3. `sources/modern/` is the right home

The April 19 continuation's strongest architectural correction was that this
kind of work should fill out `sources/modern/` under the existing apparatus,
not spawn a parallel repo. Bates is a good test case for that narrower
decision:

- contemporary
- English
- synthetic
- potentially useful
- still clearly below campaign status
