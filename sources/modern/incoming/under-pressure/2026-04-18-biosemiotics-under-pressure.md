- `open biosemiotics because the vocabulary sounds right`
- `treat Hoffmeyer as already-confirmed authority`
- `use biosemiotics to reopen Peirce, Schelling, or LLM ontology by drift`

The live issue is narrower:

- the repo already has a strong direct Uexkuell line on the page
- older repo work keeps tagging the Sebeok / Hoffmeyer / Kull line as a real
  descendant pressure

  if a bounded modern synthesis is ever needed downstream of Uexkuell, then

## What is checkable in named texts

The following kinds of claims are checkable in principle by reading named
texts:

- that Hoffmeyer's books explicitly read Uexkuell and Peirce together
- that Hoffmeyer treats semiosis as constitutive of life, not as a merely
  human add-on
- that Hoffmeyer uses terms such as `semiotic scaffolding` or
  `code-duality`

  Peircean biosemiotics

## 2026-04-23 - bounded Hoffmeyer first contact from Langer pressure

This pass follows the closer Langer chapter 4 pass. The test was narrow:
whether Hoffmeyer actually gives the kind of downstream biosemiotic surface that
earlier notes only suspected.

### Read surface

- `sources/modern/hoffmeyer-biosemiotics/source/normalized/010-on-biosemiotics.txt`
- `sources/modern/hoffmeyer-biosemiotics/source/normalized/011-surfaces-within-surfaces.txt`

  `sources/modern/hoffmeyer-biosemiotics/source/normalized/012-sign-and-cause.txt`

The normalized files are a browsing layer, not quotation-grade authority.
Exact citation still needs the PDF witness.

### 1. The expected field pressure is really present

Hoffmeyer does explicitly frame biosemiotics as a synthesis surface for Peirce,
Uexkuell, and Bateson, and he does not treat sign-processes as merely human or
linguistic.

Evidence anchors:

- `010-on-biosemiotics.txt:7`: the opening names Peirce, Uexkuell, and Bateson
  in connection with life grounded in semiotic processes
- `010-on-biosemiotics.txt:15`: cultural sign processes are treated as special
  instances of broader biosemiosis
- `010-on-biosemiotics.txt:17`: life on Earth is described through an
  evolutionary semiosphere
- `010-on-biosemiotics.txt:41`: any bridge between human and natural worlds
  requires semiotic competence in nature itself

So the earlier on-disk orientation was directionally right: Hoffmeyer is a real
downstream synthesis surface, not just attractive vocabulary.

### 2. Human language remains special, but no longer defines semiosis

Hoffmeyer preserves human specificity by reserving symbolic reference and
language for a distinctive human capacity. He does not preserve it by making
sign-use itself uniquely human.

Evidence anchors:

- `010-on-biosemiotics.txt:23`: the semiosphere begins with life and depends on
  inside/outside asymmetry, membranes, self-reference, and other-reference
- `010-on-biosemiotics.txt:27`: reductionism is avoided only if sign and
  meaning are not narrowed to human linguistic symbols
- `010-on-biosemiotics.txt:109`: life and signs, not atoms alone, are proposed
  as fundamental phenomena for biology

This is the main difference from Langer. Langer dislodges language-monopoly
inside human rationality; Hoffmeyer dislodges it by lowering semiosis into
living organization.

### 3. Skin and membrane are the real operators

The second chapter is not a decorative "surface" metaphor. Hoffmeyer's operative
claim is that living boundaries are interpretive interfaces.

Evidence anchors:

- `011-surfaces-within-surfaces.txt:6`: skin is introduced as an atmosphere of
  encounter and as a place where encounters become knowledge
- `011-surfaces-within-surfaces.txt:16`: personhood is placed in the skin as a
  diversified information-exchange organ
- `011-surfaces-within-surfaces.txt:25`: skin keeps the world physically away
  while making it psychologically present through biological meanings
- `011-surfaces-within-surfaces.txt:88`: meaning is already beginning in skin
  processes before conscious interpretation
- `011-surfaces-within-surfaces.txt:137`: membrane is treated as semiotic
  bridge across inside/outside asymmetry
- `011-surfaces-within-surfaces.txt:225`: life requires feedback between
  other-reference and self-reference

This gives the strongest concrete bridge from Langer's perceptual
form-giving to biosemiotics: both refuse passive reception, but Hoffmeyer makes
the interface biological.

### 4. Chapter 3 adds causation and information pressure

The first pass into chapter 3 shows a second major hinge: Hoffmeyer uses
Peircean final causation and semiotic causation to resist a biology reduced to
efficient causes plus reified information transfer.

Evidence anchors:

- `012-sign-and-cause.txt:19`: purpose is separated from broader final
  causation
- `012-sign-and-cause.txt:151`: Hoffmeyer names `semiotic causation` as his
  preferred term for the biological phenomenon
- `012-sign-and-cause.txt:179`: the Central Dogma becomes the entry point into
  the information critique
- `012-sign-and-cause.txt:187`: biochemical information is distinguished from
  Shannon information
- `012-sign-and-cause.txt:203`: the transport metaphor turns information into
  a thing-like package
- `012-sign-and-cause.txt:217`: information must be understood by an
  interpreting system and therefore can be misunderstood
- `012-sign-and-cause.txt:219`: information as cue/interpreting-system
  relation is not itself transportable

This is likely the next pressure if Hoffmeyer gets another pass: not "life is
meaning" as a slogan, but the exact causal and informational work required to
make that claim non-metaphorical.

### 5. Relation to Langer

The bridge is now clearer:

- Langer: meaningful form is wider than discursive language, and some symbolic
  articulation must be read as configuration rather than translated into a
  dictionary
- Hoffmeyer: living systems themselves operate through sign-relations mediated
  by interpretive boundaries, feedback, and organism-specific stakes

Shared pressure:

- both reject language as the monopoly form of meaning
- both reject passive reception
- both require an interpreting structure rather than a bare signal

Non-identity:

- Langer remains primarily a theory of human symbolic form, perception, and art
- Hoffmeyer is making a biological and causal claim about life
- moving from the first to the second requires Peirce/Uexkuell/membrane work,
  not just the word `meaning`
