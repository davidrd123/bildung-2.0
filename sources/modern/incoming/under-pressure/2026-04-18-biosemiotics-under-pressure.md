## What is actually under pressure

The live issue is not:

- `open biosemiotics because the vocabulary sounds right`
- `treat Hoffmeyer as already-confirmed authority`
- `use biosemiotics to reopen Peirce, Schelling, or LLM ontology by drift`

The live issue is narrower:

- the repo already has a strong direct Uexkuell line on the page
- older repo work keeps tagging the Sebeok / Hoffmeyer / Kull line as a real
  descendant pressure
- the April 13 extraction already kept one procedural survivor alive:
  if a bounded modern synthesis is ever needed downstream of Uexkuell, then
  Hoffmeyer may be the more honest first move than opening a full Peirce line
- there is still no direct contact with the downstream synthesis texts

So the real question is:

- can this downstream line be held in view without being imported as a live
  explanatory framework?

This note says yes.

## What is checkable in named texts

The following kinds of claims are checkable in principle by reading named
texts:

- that Hoffmeyer's books explicitly read Uexkuell and Peirce together
- that Hoffmeyer treats semiosis as constitutive of life, not as a merely
  human add-on
- that Hoffmeyer uses terms such as `semiotic scaffolding` or
  `code-duality`
- that a code-biology alternative exists and is presented as distinct from
  Peircean biosemiotics
- that Sebeok and Kull write programmatic statements about the field

Those are the claims a direct encounter could actually confirm or refuse.

## What is only field-shape or reader-perception

The following claims should be treated as orientation only until direct contact
or better evidence exists:

- that Hoffmeyer is the field's "main point person"
- that one clean ranking across Sebeok / Kull / Hoffmeyer / Barbieri captures
  the field
- that reader visibility maps neatly onto institutional centrality
- that Hoffmeyer is the normal entry point for readers in any strong empirical
  sense

These may be directionally right, but they are not the kind of claim the repo
should silently inherit.

A safer provisional orientation is to hold apart field-roles rather than rank
"importance":

- Hoffmeyer: on-disk downstream synthesis surface and cheapest first check
- Sebeok: historically central / institution-forming figure
- Kull: programmatic and historiographic orientation surface
- Barbieri: clearest later fault-line test if the code-vs-Peircean split is
  real

## Why keep this note now

Without a localized holding note, the biosemiotics line stays in one of two bad
states:

- atmospheric chat authority
- or scattered residue across extractions, deepresearch, and distillations

This note keeps the pressure visible while keeping it below promotion.

## First confirming texts

### 1. Best immediately available first contact: Hoffmeyer

The books actually on disk are:

- `Signs of Meaning in the Universe`
- `Biosemiotics`
- `A Legacy for Living Systems`

So the first honest confirming move, if any, is one bounded Hoffmeyer
introduction or opening chapter.

The reason to start there is not that Hoffmeyer has already been established
as the field's most important figure. The reason is narrower:

- he is the only downstream synthesis surface already localized on disk
- he is the cheapest way to test whether the current field-sketch is even
  stated that way from inside the tradition

That would let the repo test:

- whether the Uexkuell + Peirce framing is really how Hoffmeyer states the
  field
- whether Hoffmeyer's vocabulary does any real work on live repo pressure
- whether this is a useful downstream synthesis or only attractive atmosphere

### 2. Strongest later split test, not yet localized: Hoffmeyer vs. Barbieri

The most useful field-split test would be:

- Hoffmeyer introduction vs. one Barbieri introduction

Reason:

- if the Peircean-vs-code-biology split is real, it should be visible quickly
  there
- this tests a substantive fork in the field, not just a reputation claim

But that second surface is **not yet on disk**, so it remains a future test,
not an immediate next move.

### 3. Later programmatic field-shape test, also not yet localized:
Hoffmeyer vs. Sebeok or Kull

This would test the weaker and more field-shaped claim that Hoffmeyer is best
understood as a downstream synthesizer, while Sebeok and Kull occupy different
historical or programmatic roles.

It is worth naming, but it is subtler than the Hoffmeyer / Barbieri split.

## What this does not license

This note does **not** license:

- citing `biosemiotics` into live `texts/` work
- treating Hoffmeyer as already-confirmed bridge authority for Uexkuell
- reopening Peirce because the field map feels persuasive
- importing `semiosis is coextensive with life` as a repo-level principle
- using this note to justify LLM or moral-status speculation
