# Clark Markov Blanket Control Case

Purpose: hold Andy Clark's 2017 Markov blanket paper as a bounded control case
for the current Hoffmeyer / biosemiotics pressure.

This is not a promoted encounter. It is a note saying why the paper is worth
keeping close while the repo tests what biosemiotics adds over non-semiotic
active-inference and process-ontology vocabularies.

## Status

This paper is a **control case**, not a source-verified conclusion.

The PDF has been locally identified and a raw text extraction has been made for
search/reference. The extraction is layout-noisy and should not be treated as
cleaned text.

## Verified Locally

Checked from the local PDF metadata and first pages:

- Author: Andy Clark.
- Title: "How to Knit Your Own Markov Blanket: Resisting the Second Law with
  Metamorphic Minds."
- Publication context: *Philosophy and Predictive Processing*, edited by
  Thomas Metzinger and Wanja Wiese, 2017.
- Length: 19 PDF pages.
- Keywords include active inference, autopoiesis, EEE cognition, embodied
  cognition, extended mind, free energy minimization, Markov blanket,
  prediction error minimization, and process ontology.
- The opening argument responds to Hohwy's claim that Markov blankets create a
  tension with embodied, enactive, and extended cognition.
- Clark's stated counterclaim is that biological Markov blankets are multiple
  and mutable rather than unique and stationary.
- Section 2 explicitly connects biological agency, resistance to the second law,
  and the free energy principle.
- A footnote in section 2 explicitly links the formulation to autopoiesis.

## Why It Matters Here

The paper is worth ingesting because it gives the project a strong non-semiotic
comparison point for the Hoffmeyer line.

The current Hoffmeyer work has been circling:

- life as entropy resistance without being reducible to ordinary mechanism
- the boundary problem of organism, cell, symbiont, and superorganism
- autopoiesis and self-maintaining organization
- metamorphosis and life-cycle identity
- extended phenotype and niche-construction pressures
- process ontology against fixed organism concepts

Clark appears to work through much of this same terrain using Markov blankets,
free energy minimization, active inference, process ontology, and EEE cognition
rather than signs, interpretants, code-duality, or final causation.

That makes the comparison useful. If biosemiotics earns its keep, it should earn
it over a framework this strong, not over a reductive straw opponent.

## Control Question

What does biosemiotics explain, preserve, or articulate that Clark's
active-inference vocabulary does not?

Candidate differentiators to test:

- final causation or semiotic causality as more than teleonomic persistence
- the interpretant as a real third term rather than model/world coupling
- code-duality and the digital capacity for negation or abstraction
- sign-processes as causal organization rather than merely statistical
  dependency or self-evidencing model structure

## Holds

The following claims should not be promoted without a closer read:

- that Clark's framework has no semiotic residue at all
- that active inference is simply dyadic in contrast to Peircean triadicity
- that Friston/Clark have no serious equivalent for teleology or finality
- that Hoffmeyer's vocabulary adds explanatory power rather than metaphysical
  decoration

Those are exactly the questions this control case is meant to keep open.
