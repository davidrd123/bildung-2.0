## What is actually under pressure

The April 10 chat narrowed the Peirce question correctly.

The live issue is not `read Peirce because Peirce is important`.

The live issue is narrower:

- is the generative-chain apparatus really close to a doubt / belief / inquiry structure, or only superficially so
- does the current experiment protocol line up with Peirce's account of abduction and inquiry, or only borrow some vocabulary
- if sign language is used at all, what level of Peirce's sign theory is needed before the use stops being decorative

## First-priority texts

These are the smallest published anchors that appear genuinely pressured:

### 1. `The Fixation of Belief` (1877)

Why:

- this is where the doubt / belief / irritation structure is stated in a form the current repo questions can actually test
- it is the first place to check whether the chain logic is inquiry-like in Peirce's sense or only resembles it from outside

### 2. `How to Make Our Ideas Clear` (1878)

Why:

- this is the immediate companion to `Fixation`
- it sharpens the practical meaning test without requiring late manuscript reconstruction

### 3. The 1903 Harvard lectures on pragmatism / abduction

Why:

- this is the strongest published anchor for the abduction question
- the April 10 chat's LLM / abduction claims should stand or fall here before anything later or more fragmented is invoked
