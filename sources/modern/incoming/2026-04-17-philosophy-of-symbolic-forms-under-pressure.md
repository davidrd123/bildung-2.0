## What is actually under pressure

The live pressure is not:

- `read all of Philosophy of Symbolic Forms because the chats keep naming it`
- `Cassirer now explains all cross-project plurality`
- `symbolic forms should become active repo architecture`

The live pressure is narrower:

- `Erkenntnisproblem` already holds a later language / symbolic-forms bridge as
  real reserve pressure
- the current `Substanzbegriff` probe made the presently source-earned Cassirer
  center much clearer without reaching the later symbolic-forms layer
- recent chats keep reaching for symbolic forms when they want a stronger
  account of irreducible world-disclosure modes, naming crisis, and
  constitution beyond exact-science history

So the real question is:

- can that later Cassirer pressure now be localized source-side without letting
  chat appetite decide too much too early?

This note says yes.

## Why localize now

Without a localized source surface, the symbolic-forms line stays in one of two
bad states:

- atmospheric chat authority
- or abstract reserve pressure with no exact source to touch

Localizing the German source fixes that without widening the project.

## First-priority surface

The first honest probe surface is **Volume I: `Die Sprache`**.

Reason:

- the strongest already-earned reserve pressure is the later language /
  symbolic-forms bridge
- Volume I is the smallest surface that can test that pressure directly
- it lets the repo ask whether symbolic constitution is really needed for
  current work, rather than importing the whole three-volume architecture at
  once

Likely first bounded surface:

- chapter I
- then, only if needed, the later chapters on relation-forms and judgment

## Second-priority surface

The next real reserve surface is **Volume III: `Phänomenologie der Erkenntnis`**.

Reason:

- current repo pressure around anti-psychologism, representation,
  objectivation, symbolic constitution, and modern physics could eventually
  need direct contact here
- but that need is not yet cleaner than the Volume I pressure

So Volume III should be localized and held ready, not activated first.

## Third surface, held below activation

**Volume II: `Das mythische Denken`** is clearly relevant to the larger Cassirer
program, but it is not the first surface current repo work is demanding.

That does not demote its importance in Cassirer.

It only means:

- current project pressure is not yet strongest there
- opening it first would be architecture appetite outrunning source demand

## What this does not license

This note does **not** license:

- a full `Philosophie der symbolischen Formen` campaign
- importing `symbolic form` as an active repo-wide explanatory term

Until then, keep this source localized, source-side, and below campaign level.

## 2026-04-17 - bounded read confirmation from Volume I

A first bounded direct read of the new localized Volume I surface confirmed the
basic ordering of this note.

Read surface:

- `source/raw/010-einleitung-und-problemstellung.txt`
- `source/raw/011-kapitel-i-das-sprachproblem-in-der-geschichte-der-philosophie.txt`

### What the read confirmed

- Volume I is the correct first probe surface.
- The live gain is not "symbolic forms explain everything," but a more exact
  account of language as a constitutive mode of objectivation.
- The introduction is much closer to the current repo's earned Cassirer line
  than recent chat rhetoric was: it extends the move from thing / substance to
  relation / symbol / function without yet requiring the whole three-volume
  architecture.

### What sharpened on contact

1. The introduction explicitly removes objectivity from naive copy theory.
   `Intellektuelle Symbole` are not passive images of a given being; their
   value lies in the ordered unity they produce.
2. The move from scientific symbol to plural symbolic form is made in terms of
   different energies of objectivation, not in terms of loose perspectives or
   interchangeable frameworks.
3. Chapter I makes the language pressure much cleaner historically. The line
   through Herder and especially Humboldt is not just "language matters," but:
   language is a formative medium through which objectivity is constituted.
4. Humboldt is the key local pivot. Languages are not different labels laid
   over one ready-made world; they are `Verschiedenheiten der Weltansichten
   selbst`.

### What this does not license

- not a full opening of all three volumes
- not importing `symbolic form` as a free repo-wide explanation
- not treating myth or culture-plurality as the first payoff of this scaffold
- not displacing the currently source-earned `Substanzbegriff` line

### Practical result

The actual first symbolic-forms question should stay narrow:

- when current work reaches for symbolic forms, does it really need Cassirer's
  account of language as constitutive objectivation and world-view formation?

That question is now source-localized enough to test directly.

The first verified working surfaces for that test are now:

This still does **not** amount to a full normalized Volume I layer. It means:

- Intro I is now continuously available as a working surface

  whole historical survey of chapter I
