# Von Foerster: On Constructing a Reality

**Source**: Heinz von Foerster, "On Constructing a Reality" (1973), lecture at the Fourth International Conference on Environmental Design Research
**Status**: open
**Triggered by**: the ratio/intellectus turn in Erkenntnisproblem Parts 10-13; the `term:hinzutretende` evidence chain in zeitmauer; Barrett's constructed-categories thesis surfacing independently from the Cusanus material

## Overview

Nine pages. The paper moves from perceptual experiments (blindspot, scotoma, alternates, comprehension) through a neurophysiological tour to a formal claim about cognitive closure, and ends with an ethical imperative. The postulate — "the environment as we perceive it is our invention" — is not solipsism. The paper's real argument is that cognition is recursive computation of descriptions of descriptions, doubly closed (operating on its own operators), and that the only escape from solipsism is the relation between autonomous observers: Reality = Community.

## Passage 1: The Postulate

### Formal (von Foerster)

> "Again, the discovery we all have to make for ourselves is the following postulate: *the environment as we perceive it is our invention.*"
>
> "However, neither M. Jourdain nor my friends have as yet made another discovery, and that is when M. Jourdain speaks, may it be prose or poetry, it is he who invents it, and likewise when we perceive our environment, it is we who invent it."

### Prose

We do not receive the environment. We produce it. Not in the sense that nothing exists beyond us, but in the sense that what we *perceive* — color, sound, warmth, pattern, meaning — is computed by the nervous system from undifferentiated signals. The postulate is epistemological, not ontological: it concerns what we can know about the relation between knower and known, not whether anything exists independently.

### Cusan register

*Assimilatio*, not *similitudo*. The mind does not copy the world's form into itself (passive resemblance). It discovers its own forms in the material it encounters (active self-measurement). What we call "perceiving the environment" is the mind's conjectural activity — *conjectura* — constructing a world-picture from within, using its own organizational lawfulness as the instrument. The environment "as perceived" is not the environment "as it is," because *ratio* can only find what its own categories permit. But this is not defeat. It is the condition of all genuine knowing: *docta ignorantia* at the perceptual level.

### Residue (Postulate)

| Direction | What survives | What's lost |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| von Foerster → Cusa | The active construction thesis. The anti-representationism. | The neurophysiological ground. The experiments. The humor. |
| Cusa → von Foerster | The *positive* epistemic status of construction — it's not error but the only genuine knowing. The name (*assimilatio*) for what von Foerster can only describe operationally. | The mathematical precision of Cusa's *conjectura*. The theological horizon (mind as image of divine creative activity). |

## Passage 2: The Blindspot

### Formal (von Foerster)

> "Note that this localized blindness is not perceived as a dark blotch in our visual field (seeing a dark blotch would imply 'seeing'), but this blindness is not perceived at all, that is, neither as something present, nor as something absent: whatever is perceived is perceived 'blotch-less'."

### Prose

The blindspot is not a hole you can see. It is an absence that does not register as absence. You don't know what you're missing, and you don't know that you don't know. The visual field fills itself in seamlessly. Perception computes a complete world from incomplete data, and the completeness is indistinguishable — from the inside — from a world that actually is complete.

### Cusan register

This is *docta ignorantia* made visible — or rather, made invisible in exactly the right way. Cusa's claim: *ratio*, operating through comparison and excluded middle, cannot perceive its own limits from within. It produces a complete picture precisely by not registering what exceeds its categories. The blindspot is not a failure of the eye. It is a structural feature of any instrument that constructs its world through finite operations. The polygon approximating the circle does not perceive itself as lacking curvature. It *is* a complete polygon.

The deeper resonance: Cusa says the discovery of the blindspot — the moment when *ratio* recognizes its own incompleteness — is not the end of knowing but the *beginning* of a higher knowing (*intellectus*). Von Foerster's scotoma therapy confirms this: blindfolding the patient (removing the visual scaffold) restores motor function by shifting attention to proprioceptive channels. The cure for the blindspot is not better seeing but a different mode of engagement.

### Jünger register

The blindspot is the Zeitmauer's opening condition. Jünger's §47: "events no longer fit the historical frame and its concepts." The naming crisis is a civilizational blindspot — not the absence of names but the inability to perceive the absence. Where we say "peace" there may be war. The vocabulary fills in the gap seamlessly, and the seamlessness is the danger. Von Foerster's blindspot experiment is the perceptual miniature of what Jünger diagnoses at historical scale.

### Anchors

- `zm:8` — measurable knowledge destroys what it measures. The blindspot is *structurally invisible* to the instrument that harbors it.
- `zm:47`, `zm:48` — naming crisis. Historical naming fails without the failure being perceived as failure.
- Erkenntnisproblem Part 10 (pp. 37-40) — Cusa's *docta ignorantia* as the positive discovery of ratio's limits.

## Passage 3: The Principle of Undifferentiated Encoding

### Formal (von Foerster)

> "The response of a nerve cell does not encode the physical nature of the agents that caused its response. Encoded is only 'how much' at this point on my body, but not 'what'."
>
> "Since the physical nature of the stimulus — its *quality* — is not encoded into nervous activity, the fundamental question arises as to how does our brain conjure up the tremendous variety of this colorful world as we experience it any moment while awake, and sometimes in dreams while asleep."

### Prose

Every receptor is blind to quality. Rods, cones, taste buds, touch receptors — all respond only to intensity at a location. The nervous system receives a field of "how much" and computes from it a world of "what." Color, sound, warmth, pain are not transmitted. They are computed. The quality you experience is the system's own organizational contribution, not a copy of the stimulus.

### Cusan register

The nerve cell encodes *quantitas* but not *quidditas*. It measures magnitude at a point but is constitutively blind to essence. This is *ratio* at the cellular level: comparison of intensities, determination of proportions, but never the apprehension of the thing's own nature.

Yet the organism does experience quality. Color *is* perceived. Sound *is* heard. Warmth *is* felt. Where does the quality come from? From the organization of the computation itself. The system's own *complicatio* — the enfolded principle from which the entire sensory manifold is *explicatio*, unfolded — supplies the qualitative character that the stimulus does not carry.

This is Cusa's strongest claim about *intellectus*: it does not receive quality from outside. It discovers quality as its own organizational form reflected in the quantitative data that *ratio* provides. The carbuncle (Erkenntnisproblem Part 11) glows not because light enters it from without, but because its own *quidditas* — its characteristic activity — is luminous. The organism perceives color not because color is "out there" but because its own computational organization generates color as the form of its engagement with electromagnetic radiation.

### Speculative mathematical note

Von Foerster's principle *suggests* a functor-like structure, though the paper does not formalize it this way. If one imagined a category of physical stimuli (objects: stimulus types; morphisms: intensity changes) and a category of experiences (objects: qualia; morphisms: qualitative transitions), the nervous system would play the role of something that preserves *relational structure* (proportions between intensities map to proportions between experiences) while discarding *substance* (the physical nature of the stimulus is not carried over). This is suggestive of Cassirer's *Substanzbegriff → Funktionsbegriff*: what is preserved across the transformation is the relational structure, not the material identity. But the analogy is speculative — von Foerster's paper does not establish the structure-preservation formally, and the Eilenberg & Mac Lane encounter (when drafted) is where this parallel should be tested rather than asserted.

### Residue (Undifferentiated Encoding)

| Direction | What survives | What's lost |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| von Foerster → Cusa | The blindness to quality. The computational construction of the qualitative world. | The *name* for what quality is (quidditas). The positive account of how the mind contributes quality (complicatio/explicatio). |
| Cusa → von Foerster | The carbuncle image. The claim that quality is the mind's own form, not an import. | The neurophysiological mechanism. The experimental basis. The cell-level specificity. |
| von Foerster → Eilenberg & Mac Lane | The structure-preservation without substance-preservation. | The full mathematical apparatus. The naturality condition. The commutative diagram. |
| Eilenberg & Mac Lane → von Foerster | The formal criterion for when structure-preservation is "natural" (basis-independent). | The biological ground. The ethical consequence. Everything that makes this about *life* rather than algebra. |

## Passage 4: Cognition as Recursive Computation

### Formal (von Foerster)

> COGNITION → computing descriptions of a reality
>
> [then corrected to:]
>
> COGNITION → computing descriptions of ↺
>
> "where the arrow turning back suggests this infinite recursion of descriptions of descriptions of descriptions... This formulation has the advantage that one unknown, namely, 'reality' is successfully eliminated."

### Prose

Cognition does not terminate in a description of reality. It terminates in a description — which becomes the input for the next computation. The process is recursive: descriptions of descriptions of descriptions, with no final referent outside the recursion. "Reality" is eliminated not by denying that anything exists but by recognizing that the recursion never reaches a terminal description that would *be* the reality it describes.

### Cusan register

This is *scientia infinita*. Knowledge never reaches the object. It approximates without limit. Each conjecture (*conjectura*) becomes the material for the next conjecture. The polygon adds sides indefinitely but never becomes the circle. Cusa's solution: recognize the recursion as the *form* of knowing, not as a failure to reach the object. The approximation-process *is* the genuine knowledge. The recursion is not a defect but the mind's own infinity reflecting itself.

Von Foerster eliminates "reality" from the formula. Cusa would not — he would say the eliminated term is precisely what *drives* the recursion. The circle is what the polygon is always aiming at. Remove the circle and the polygon has no reason to add sides. The unattainable object is not eliminated; it is the *motor* of the recursion. This is the residue between the two registers: von Foerster's is cleaner, Cusa's is truer to the phenomenology of inquiry.

### Anchors

- Erkenntnisproblem Part 15 (pp. 57-60) — *scientia infinita*: "what is presupposed in every question is at the same time the light that guides us to what is asked"
- Exegesis thread `the-dialectic` — Dick's recursive model-revision. Each entry revises the model, producing the anomaly that demands the next entry. The recursion is the Exegesis.
- `sources/modern/rosen-closure-efficient-causation.md` — closure to efficient causation. Von Foerster's recursive arrow is the epistemic version of Rosen's organizational loop.

## Passage 5: Closure (The Torus)

### Formal (von Foerster)

> [Figure 18: the organism as doubly closed computational system — sensory surface (SS), motor surface (MS), neuropituitary (NP), with neural bundles computing across synaptic gaps]
>
> "Note the double closure of the system which now recursively operates not only on what it 'sees' but on its operators as well."
>
> [Figure 19: the torus — Figure 18 wrapped around two axes of circular symmetry]
>
> **Postulate of Cognitive Homeostasis**: "The nervous system is organized (or organizes itself) so that it computes a stable reality."
>
> "This postulate stipulates 'autonomy', i.e., 'self regulation', for every living organism... This is precisely what the doubly closed, recursively computing torus does: it regulates its own regulation."

### Prose

The nervous system is closed in two ways. First, it operates on what it senses — the external loop through sensory and motor surfaces. Second, it operates on *its own operators* — the neuropituitary modifies the synaptic chemistry, changing how the system computes. The system doesn't just compute a world; it computes the rules by which it computes the world.

Wrapped into a torus, this double closure means: there is no input that is not already shaped by the system's own prior computation, and there is no computation that is not itself subject to modification by the system's own endocrine regulation. Autonomy — self-regulation — is not a property added to the organism. It *is* the organism's organizational form.

### Cusan register

This is *intellectus* — the faculty that comprehends not only its objects but its own operations. *Ratio* computes within fixed categories (the external loop). *Intellectus* grasps the generating principle of those categories and can modify them (the internal loop through the neuropituitary). The torus is the topological form of the ratio/intellectus relation: one loop for discursive operation on data, another loop for reflexive operation on the operations themselves.

The Postulate of Cognitive Homeostasis — "the nervous system organizes itself so that it computes a stable reality" — is *assimilatio* in its strongest form. The mind doesn't merely find its own forms in the world. It *stabilizes* the world around its own organizational invariants. What we call "reality" is the eigenvalue of the recursive computation — the fixed point at which the system's own self-modification converges.

Cusa's *coincidentia oppositorum* applies here: the observer and the observed are not two separate things brought into contact, but two aspects of one doubly-closed process. The knower is what the torus does when it operates on its sensory surface. The known is what the torus does when the sensory surface operates on it. The coincidence of these two — the point where observing and being observed are the same act — is the torus itself.

### Diagrammatic

```
External loop (ratio):
  SS → [compute] → MS → [act on world] → SS
  (sense → process → move → sense again)

Internal loop (intellectus):
  NP → [modify synapses] → [compute] → NP
  (regulate → change the rules of computation → regulate again)

Torus: both loops simultaneously, sharing the computational tissue.
No input that isn't already shaped by prior computation.
No computation that isn't subject to self-modification.
```

### Residue (Closure)

| Direction | What survives | What's lost |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| von Foerster → Cusa | The double closure. The self-modification of operators. The eigenvalue interpretation. | The *names* for the two loops (ratio/intellectus). The positive epistemic meaning of the convergence (scientia infinita, not just homeostasis). |
| Cusa → von Foerster | The claim that self-modification is not just stability but *genuine knowing* — intellectus discovers the generating principle. | The topological form (the torus). The neurophysiology. The endocrine mechanism. |
| von Foerster → Rosen | Both name closure. Von Foerster's is epistemic (the system computes its own operators). Rosen's is organizational (the system produces its own efficient causes). | Rosen adds the impossibility claim (disputed). Von Foerster adds the ethical consequence. |
| von Foerster → Uexküll | Von Foerster's torus is Uexküll's Funktionskreis at a deeper level — not just the circuit between organism and world, but the circuit between the organism and its own mode of circuiting. | Uexküll adds the plurality of worlds (many Umwelten). Von Foerster's single-organism closure needs Uexküll's overlapping-worlds picture to escape solipsism — which is exactly what Passage 6 does. |

## Passage 6: Reality = Community

### Formal (von Foerster)

> "According to the Principle of Relativity which rejects a hypothesis when it does not hold for two instances together, although it holds for each instance separately... the solipsistic claim falls to pieces when besides me I invent another autonomous organism."
>
> "There must be a third that is the central reference. It is the relation between Thou and I, and this relation is IDENTITY:
>
> **Reality = Community**"
>
> "**The Ethical Imperative**: Act always so as to increase the number of choices."
>
> "**The Aesthetical Imperative**: If you desire to see, learn how to act."

### Prose

Von Foerster's argument is not a logical refutation of solipsism. It is a *decision*. The Principle of Relativity is not a logical necessity — it is something the observer is free to adopt or reject. If I reject it, "my reality is my dreams and nightmares, my language is monologue, and my logic mono-logic." If I adopt it, then neither I nor the other can be the center. A third reference is needed: the relation between Thou and I. Von Foerster calls this relation Identity, and equates it with reality: Reality = Community.

The ethical and aesthetical imperatives follow *from the choice*, not from a proof. "Act always so as to increase the number of choices" is the consequence of having *chosen* the relational principle over the solipsistic one. "If you desire to see, learn how to act" closes the circle: perception is not passive reception but active engagement, and the quality of engagement is an ethical matter. The paper ends in ethics, not in epistemology — and this is deliberate.

### Cusan register

*Coincidentia oppositorum* at the social level. Two knowers, each a closed torus, each computing a stable world. Neither can contain the other (each is autonomous). Neither can be reduced to the other (each has its own organizational closure). The relation between them — the *tertium* that is neither I nor Thou but the bond — is the *coincidentia* from which both are unfolded.

Cusa's *non aliud* — "the not-other" — names something structurally adjacent: that which is neither this nor that but the principle from which both emerge. Von Foerster arrives at this through a voluntary adoption of the Principle of Relativity. Cusa arrives at it through negative theology. The resemblance is real — both treat reality as relational rather than substantial, and both hold that the relation is prior to the relata. But there is a difference in force: von Foerster's argument rests on a choice that could be refused; Cusa's rests on a metaphysical claim about the structure of being. Whether these are the same claim at different depths or genuinely distinct claims is an open question this encounter should not pretend to settle.

### Jünger register

The Hinzutretende — the one who comes in addition — is von Foerster's second autonomous organism. When the interpreter enters the functional circle, solipsism breaks. The observer changes what is observed not by distorting it but by *constituting the relation* without which there is no reality, only private computation. Jünger's figure is the ethical version of von Foerster's formal argument: the Hinzutretende doesn't add facts. He adds *community* — the third thing that makes the facts real.

### Anchors

- `zm:6`, `zm:7`, `zm:9` — the Hinzutretende evidence chain. Von Foerster's "Reality = Community" is the formal ground for why the interpreter-figure is not a distortion but a condition of reality.
- Escolios §325 — "Every truth is tension between contrary evidences demanding simultaneous obedience." The relation between autonomous knowers *is* the truth — not either one's private construction.
- Erkenntnisproblem Part 13 (pp. 47-52) — *coincidentia oppositorum* as methodological principle.

## Residue Matrix (whole paper)

### What no single register captures

The *convergence* between von Foerster's "Reality = Community" and Cusa's *non aliud*. Both treat reality as relational rather than substantial — neither the observer nor the observed but the relation from which both emerge. Von Foerster arrives at this through the Principle of Relativity (a voluntary adoption). Cusa arrives at it through negative theology (a metaphysical argument). The two routes are independent. Whether the convergence is structural identity or family resemblance is itself an open question — see below. What is clear is that neither route can state in the other's vocabulary what it adds: von Foerster adds the choice, Cusa adds the metaphysical ground.

The Erkenntnisproblem is the text that could hold both routes simultaneously, because Cassirer's project is precisely to trace how theological categories become epistemological tools without either being reduced to the other. The encounter produces its sharpest residue when placed next to the Cusanus chapter — not as confirmation, but as a bidirectional translation where each register reveals what the other hides.

### What the Cusan register adds that von Foerster can't say

- **Quidditas**: the nerve cell encodes quantity but not quality. Cusa names the quality: *quidditas*. The concept exists in the Cusan vocabulary. It does not exist in the neurophysiological vocabulary. This is not a decorative addition. It is a term for something the formal register can only point to negatively.
- **Scientia infinita**: von Foerster eliminates "reality" from the recursion. Cusa would say the eliminated term is the motor. The unattainable object *drives* the approximation. Removing it from the formula is algebraically clean but phenomenologically false.
- **Non aliud**: von Foerster's "Identity" between Thou and I. Cusa's name for it carries six centuries of theological precision about what it means for a relation to precede its terms.

### What von Foerster adds that Cusa can't say

- **The blindspot**: the structural invisibility of the instrument's own limits. Cusa knows *docta ignorantia* but doesn't have the perceptual demonstration.
- **The torus**: the topological form of double closure. Cusa describes *intellectus* operating on its own operations but can't show the *shape* of that reflexivity.
- **The ethical imperative**: "Act always so as to increase the number of choices." Cusa has no ethics of choice-maximization. The ethical consequence of constructivism is von Foerster's own contribution.
- **The experiments**: blindspot, scotoma, alternates, comprehension. The empirical ground that makes the constructivist thesis testable rather than merely assertable.

## Open Questions (status: open)

### Is von Foerster's "Reality = Community" the same claim as Cusa's *non aliud*?

Family resemblance, probably not identity. Von Foerster's argument is horizontal: two autonomous observers, neither containing the other, generating a shared reality through their relation. Cusa's *non aliud* is vertical: the principle from which all determinations unfold, prior to any specific pair of knowers. The horizontal version needs the vertical to ground it (why should the relation between observers have this constitutive power?). The vertical version needs the horizontal to make it concrete (how does the principle actually appear in the world of finite knowers?). **Working assessment: complementary, not identical. The encounter should hold both (75% confidence).**

### Does the Cusan register reveal anything about von Foerster that the formal register hides?

Yes — the *scientia infinita* point. Von Foerster's elimination of "reality" from the recursive formula is algebraically satisfying but it removes the motor of inquiry. Cusa's version (the circle that the polygon approximates without reaching) preserves the *directionality* of the recursion. The recursion is not aimless computation. It is *approximation toward* something. The Cusan register adds final causality to a system that von Foerster describes only in terms of efficient and formal causality. **This is a genuine finding, not a decorative addition.**

### What happened when this was tested against Erkenntnisproblem Part 45?

Tested against the next Skepticism tranche (printed pp. `176-179`, PDF pp. `200-203`). This is the first place where the encounter changed the reading rather than merely echoing it. Cassirer's Montaigne now says, in effect, that knowledge gives us not the form of things but the specific character of the organ affected; that the same underlying stream becomes different qualities in different instruments; and that a new sense would open entire regions of being that our present organization keeps shut. Von Foerster's blindspot and Undifferentiated Encoding sharpen this from a generic skeptical topos into a structural claim: perception does not simply miss parts of a ready-made world, but constructs a world under the conditions of the organ. What changed in the reading was the center of gravity. The passage no longer reads chiefly as a complaint about sensory unreliability, but as an argument about organ-conditioned mediation and the impossibility of treating our sensory organization as the measure of being. **Working assessment: first validation achieved; the encounter changed the read at this seam, while broader status remains open until it proves equally generative beyond Montaigne.**

### How does this connect to Bogdanov?

Von Foerster's nerve cell (encodes quantity, not quality) is Bogdanov's neutral complex at the cellular level. The nerve cell is the limiting case where organizing and disorganizing processes balance — it transmits magnitude without organizational gain or loss. The *organism* (the torus) is the organized complex: the whole is practically greater than the sum of its parts because the double closure generates quality from quantity. Bogdanov's activities-resistances framework could formalize what von Foerster leaves at the descriptive level: the torus is an organized complex whose boundary (sensory/motor surfaces) is simultaneously the zone of construction and destruction, exactly as in Bogdanov's drop/atmosphere analysis. **Not yet tested. Marked for future Bogdanov encounter cross-reference.**
