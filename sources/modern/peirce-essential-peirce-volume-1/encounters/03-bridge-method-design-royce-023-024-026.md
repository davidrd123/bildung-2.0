## Why These Are Bridges

These essays do not form one doctrinal block, but they keep the campaign from
jumping too quickly from algebra to cosmology.

- `023` turns logic into the theory of methods.
- `024` tests how and when an axiom may become questionable.
- `026` brings the index / outward-clash problem into a critique of Royce and
  Hegelian dialectic.

Together, they discipline the later metaphysical sequence.

## What They Clarified

### 1. Logic is the method of methods

In `023`, logic is not the art of rumination. Peirce defines the university
task as method-transfer: adapting the methods of one science to another
without losing the discipline of either. Darwin, Maxwell, Wundt, Galton, and
others appear as examples of method migration.

Project pressure:

- cross-project synthesis should move methods, not just analogies
- a useful bridge note should specify what is being transferred and what would
  count as misuse
- the repo's strongest comparisons are methodological before they are thematic

### 2. Axioms can be doubted only with a guiding idea

In `024`, Peirce does not recommend casual skepticism. Established laws should
be treated as exact while that discipline is needed; later, with a guiding idea
in hand, their exactitude can become a legitimate question.

The bridge into cosmology is precise: if laws themselves are facts, and if
facts call for explanation, then the laws of nature may need a natural history.
Chance becomes a way to make room for the growth of law, not a license for
arbitrariness.

Project pressure:

- do not question a working frame merely to appear radical
- a frame becomes questionable when the material supplies a better question
- "chance" in this arc means lawful room for novelty and habit-taking, not
  randomness as an explanatory end point

### 3. Dialectic fails when it ignores resistance

In `026`, Peirce praises Royce's style but attacks the method where it turns
inward. The key objection is that thought does not settle itself by the play of
assent and doubt alone. Belief is forced by a world of action and reaction.

This ties directly back to `025`: an index is required because a proposition
must designate what it is about. General description cannot do all the work.
The "outward clash" is not an extra psychological anecdote; it is a condition
for reference and correction.

Project pressure:

- dialectical neatness is not enough; the work must be hit by sources, cases,
  and anchors
- genuine doubt is productive, but make-believe doubt is method theater
- handles and evidence items matter because they keep claims exposed to
  particularity

## Decision

No separate bridge glossary is earned. The useful record is procedural:
method-transfer, guided doubt, and outward-clash constraints should shape the
later encounter notes, but they should not become new repo doctrine.
