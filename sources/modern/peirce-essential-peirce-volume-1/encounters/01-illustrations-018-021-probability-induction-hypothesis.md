## Why This Tranche

`Fixation of Belief` and `How to Make Our Ideas Clear` establish the local
problem: inquiry moves from real doubt to belief, belief is a habit of action,
clarity lies in conceivable practical bearings, and reality is tied to the
eventual settlement of inquiry.

Essays `018-021` complete the first `Illustrations of the Logic of Science`
surface by adding the missing machinery:

- probability as the quantitative treatment of logic
- induction as a sampling procedure with its own validity conditions
- order of nature as a problem for logic rather than a vague guarantee
- deduction, induction, and hypothesis as distinct inference forms

That makes the arc useful as a bounded source surface without opening a general
Peirce campaign.

## What The Arc Clarified

### 1. Probability is not private confidence

In `018`, probability is treated primarily as a feature of classes of
inferences or consequences, not as a loose degree of subjective assurance about
an isolated event. A single non-repeatable case creates pressure precisely
because probability becomes intelligible through a long run.

The important local result is that logic is not only a matter of individual
correctness. The long-run structure of probability forces Peirce toward the
unlimited community: logicality requires interests wider than one's own finite
case.

Project pressure:

- this supports the repo's preference for evidence chains over private
  confidence
- it also warns against treating `probable` as a feeling of plausibility
- the community condition is stronger than "collaboration"; it is built into
  the logic of inference when the individual case is finite

### 2. Induction is sampling under discipline

In `019`, Peirce rejects the idea that induction can be assigned a simple
probability by imagining all possible worlds or treating ignorance as an even
chance. Synthetic inference is not reducible to deduction plus a probability
calculus.

What can be known is the trustworthiness of the procedure: how a sampling
process behaves in the long run when it is aimed at a real class. This shifts
the center from "how probable is this conclusion?" to "how well controlled is
this way of reaching a conclusion?"

Project pressure:

- the repo's `open` and `tentative` states should be read procedurally, not
  merely psychologically
- a reading batch is stronger when the sampling condition is visible: what
  passages were selected, why they count, and what would test them
- "more examples" is not automatically stronger evidence if the character was
  selected after the fact

### 3. The order of nature is not the warrant for lazy induction

In `020`, Peirce attacks the appeal to uniformity of nature as the sole warrant
of induction. The essential points are more exact: the character should be
predesignated or at least predetermined, the sample must be random with respect
to the target class, and accidental agreements in a sample cannot by themselves
ground a real induction.

The essay also makes the mind-world fit explicit. Space, time, force, and
simple laws are not just observed givens; they are conceptions the mind is
adapted to form. Natural selection is offered as a partial explanation, but
Peirce leaves a remainder rather than pretending the fit is fully explained.

Project pressure:

- the source-contact method should separate predesignated questions from
  patterns noticed after reading
- an attractive cross-project resonance is often only a hypothesis until a
  second sample tests it
- mind-world fit can be used as pressure, but not as a settled metaphysical
  guarantee

### 4. Hypothesis is not weak induction

In `021`, Peirce gives the rule / case / result scheme:

- deduction applies a rule to a case and gets a result
- induction infers a rule from case and result
- hypothesis infers a case from rule and result

The decisive distinction is that induction extends from observed phenomena to
similar phenomena, while hypothesis posits something of another kind in order
to explain what is observed. That makes hypothesis bolder and weaker in one
sense, but not disposable. Historical existence, hidden causes, and scientific
theories often cannot be replaced by induction.

Project pressure:

- many cross-project connections start as hypothesis, not induction
- the right discipline for those connections is not to pretend they are already
  sampled regularities, but to state the question before testing, choose tests
  fairly, and count failures
- abduction-like moves need a verification loop, not rhetorical force

## Relation To The Earlier Two Essays

With `016` and `017`, the arc now reads as:

- `016`: inquiry begins in real doubt and seeks fixed belief under the pressure
  of an external permanency
- `017`: clarity means knowing the conceivable practical bearings of a concept,
  and reality is what inquiry would ultimately settle on
- `018`: probability turns logic into a long-run, community-bound discipline
- `019`: induction cannot be reduced to subjective probability; its force lies
  in controlled synthetic procedure
- `020`: nature's order must be handled through sampling discipline and
  predesignated characters, not invoked as a blanket warrant
- `021`: deduction, induction, and hypothesis name different transformations of
  rule, case, and result

This is enough Peirce for the local questions about belief, clarity,
probability, induction, order, and hypothesis. It does not yet license a
Peircean rewrite of the repo architecture.
