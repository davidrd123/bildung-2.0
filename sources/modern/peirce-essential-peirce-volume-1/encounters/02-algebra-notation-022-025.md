## Why This Tranche

These two algebra papers are best read together. `022` derives logic from
belief-habit, inference, leading principle, and criticism; `025` asks what a
notation must contain if reasoning is to become inspectable.

The arc matters because it makes Peirce's semiotic vocabulary appear under
technical pressure from logic, not as a general cultural framework.

## What The Arc Clarified

### 1. Logic begins as habit under criticism

In `022`, Peirce starts from nervous action, irritation, habit formation, and
belief. A judgment represents a belief-habit; inference is the development of
belief under such a habit; logical criticism asks whether the habit would tend
from true premises to true conclusions.

The crucial move is that logic is not merely a catalog of forms. It exists
because inference-habits can be valid or invalid when measured against the
long-run settlement of belief.

Project pressure:

- a method is stronger when it names the habit it is using
- a note is not only a conclusion; it is a record of the passage that licensed
  it
- criticism should ask what kind of inference habit is being repeated

### 2. The copula is an instrument of criticism

Peirce's algebraic signs are not decorative formalism. They let him separate a
drawn inference from the leading principle that would justify it. That matters
because an enthymeme can feel complete while its governing habit remains
unstated.

Project pressure:

- hidden premises should be surfaced before a pattern becomes structural
- a thread should not be treated as settled merely because the local connection
  feels natural
- formalization is useful only when it exposes what must be checked

### 3. A notation needs tokens, indices, and icons

In `025`, Peirce argues that a sufficient logical notation needs conventional
general signs, demonstrative signs, and resemblance signs. Tokens give
generality; indices designate the subject of discourse; icons or diagrams make
relations observable enough to reason on.

The point is local and technical: the sign triad is demanded by the work of
deductive notation. It is not yet a warrant to redescribe the whole repo in
Peircean terms.

Project pressure:

- handles function partly as indices: they let the work point to a particular
  anchor rather than merely describe it
- diagrams and tables matter when relations need to be inspected rather than
  asserted
- general vocabulary without anchoring is weak; anchoring without relational
  display is also weak

## Decision

Keep `token`, `index`, and `icon` source-local for now. The useful pressure is
not the vocabulary itself, but the demand that a working notation combine
generality, pointing, and inspectable relation.
