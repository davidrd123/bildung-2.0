## Why This Tranche

The Monist sequence takes the architectonic wager from `030` and tests it as a
cosmological series: necessity is loosened by chance, continuity becomes a law
of mind, matter is interpreted through habit and deadened mind, and evolution
is finally sorted into chance, necessity, and love.

## What The Arc Clarified

### 1. Tychism is not laziness about causes

`031` rejects strict necessitarianism, but not explanation. The claim is that
absolute law cannot account for novelty, variety, growth, or the historical
coming-to-be of law itself. Chance is introduced to make room for a principle
of generalization and habit-taking.

Project pressure:

- unexplained variety should not be hidden under a too-neat system
- chance can mark the site where a pattern has not yet become law
- do not use "chance" to stop inquiry; use it to keep novelty from being
  falsely assimilated

### 2. Synechism makes mind temporal and spread out

`032` states the law of mind as the continuous spreading of ideas. Ideas lose
intensity as they gain generality and become welded with other ideas.
Continuity is not ornamental: it supports Peirce's account of consciousness,
memory, personality, and the relation between mental action and logical
inference.

Project pressure:

- a living thread is not a point; it has temporal spread and changing
  intensity
- generality is gained by diffusion, not by fiat
- personality and community appear here as continuities of feeling and idea,
  but this remains Peirce's speculative source claim

### 3. The mind-body bridge is a test of the architecture

`033` pushes the scheme through physical detail: molecular theory, protoplasm,
habit, and the relation between psychical and physical aspects. The important
methodological result is not the technical physics itself, which needs PDF and
historical checking, but the demand that an idealist account should not fear
mechanism if mechanical law is itself habit become rigid.

The ending extends the law of mind toward corporate personality. Peirce treats
groups, churches, and societies as possible sites of something like personal
consciousness when feelings are connected closely enough to influence one
another.

Project pressure:

- collective agency should be recorded cautiously, as a pressure, not assumed
  as a given
- a group thread becomes real only when its internal continuities and effects
  can be shown
- [visual candidate] a map from feeling -> habit -> general idea -> corporate
  personality may be useful if this pressure recurs

### 4. Agapism is Peirce's corrective to chance and mechanism

`034` distinguishes evolution by fortuitous variation, by mechanical
necessity, and by creative love. The third is not sentiment as decoration. It
is an account of growth through sympathetic attraction to a not-yet-possessed
idea, made possible by continuity of mind.

The essay also attacks greed and rehabilitates sentiment as judgment of the
sensible heart. This is powerful but high-risk material for the repo: it can
help name a kind of development by attraction and care, but it can also become
too easy an ethical vocabulary if lifted out of Peirce's technical arc.

Project pressure:

- not all development is selection pressure or mechanical consequence
- attraction to an emerging form can be a real motor of inquiry
- ethical language should be tied to source behavior and practice, not used as
  a warm gloss over weak evidence

## Decision

`tychism`, `synechism`, and `agapism` are real repeated pressures inside
Peirce, but they are not repo terms yet. They stay source-local in this
encounter layer. No glossary file is created from them until another source or
active text problem forces cross-source use.
