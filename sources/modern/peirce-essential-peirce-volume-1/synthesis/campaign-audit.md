## What Peirce Contributes Here

Peirce now contributes five source-local pressures to the repo:

1. Inquiry and method are habits under criticism, not private confidence.
2. Induction and hypothesis require different disciplines: sampling for one,
   fair predictive testing for the other.
3. A working notation needs general signs, particular pointing, and
   inspectable diagrams or likenesses.
4. System-building should survey available materials before turning one good
   idea into architecture.
5. Evolutionary metaphysics gives a high-risk but useful vocabulary for
   chance, habit, continuity, and growth by attraction.

The most durable repo-facing contribution is procedural: Peirce strengthens
the demand that claims remain tied to evidence, sampling conditions, handles,
tests, and the particularity of source contact.

## What Remains Tentative

- `tychism`, `synechism`, and `agapism` are repeated and structurally real
  inside Peirce, but they are not cross-source repo terms.
- The triad of firstness, secondness, and thirdness is useful for reading this
  volume, but it is not a repo ontology.
- Corporate personality, objective idealism, and matter as effete mind remain
  speculative Peirce positions, not project commitments.
- The image-grounded files are suitable for bounded reading and encounter
  work, but any quotation-bearing or diagram-bearing use still needs the PDF.
