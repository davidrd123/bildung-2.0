About the Author
Blaise Agüera y Arcas is a VP and Fellow at Google, where he is the
CTO of Technology & Society and founder of Paradigms of Intelligence
(Pi). Pi is an organization working on basic research in AI and related
fields, especially the foundations of neural computing, active inference,
sociality, evolution, and Artificial Life. In 2008, Blaise was awarded MIT’s
TR35 prize. During his tenure at Google, he has innovated on-device
machine learning for Android and Pixel, invented Federated Learning (an
approach to decentralized model training that avoids sharing private data),
and founded the Artists and Machine Intelligence program. A frequent
public speaker, he has given multiple TED talks and keynoted NeurIPS. He
has also authored numerous papers, essays, op-eds, and chapters, as well as
two previous books, Who Are We Now? and Ubi Sunt.
