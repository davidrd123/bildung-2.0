## Decision

Natural science part III completes the source-local core of Weyl's main text,
but it should still not be promoted as a final Weyl object until the appendices
have been encountered.

Sections 22-23 show what world-picture results from Weyl's method of symbolic,
experiment-coupled, theory-level construction. Matter is not a simple substrate
and causality is not a simple determinist chain. Both become historically and
methodologically transformed: matter passes through atom, force, field, ether,
conservation law, and quantum complementarity; causality passes through
function-law, causal structure, statistical law, entropy, time-direction,
freedom, and biological purposiveness.

The promoted Weyl object should eventually carry this pressure:

> Weyl's physical world-picture replaces substance and simple determinism with
> a symbolic, field-theoretic, statistical, and agency-indexed account in which
> law remains central but no longer exhausts causality, matter, life, or the I.

## What Sections 22-23 Established

- Matter is de-substantialized. The old demand for a persisting substance-place
  is transformed through atomism, force-centers, field theory, ether history,
  conservation laws, and quantum complementarity.
- The field is not another thing inside empty space. It changes the ontology
  of space itself: no empty container stands over against field and matter.
- Conservation laws become the modern carrier of substance-pressure, but Weyl
  refuses to make them metaphysically invulnerable. Even energy conservation is
  held open to possible theoretical revision.
- Quantum complementarity displaces the old polarity of matter and force. Light
  and electrons appear as wave or corpuscle depending on the observation
  arrangement.
- Causality is not identical with function-law. Functional dependencies are
  indifferent to which term determines which; causal understanding requires a
  cut between conditions and event.
- The causal cut is partly practical and agent-indexed. The experimenter treats
  the conditions as the part of the event under control, and the concept of
  cause is tied to will and self-understanding.
- Helmholtz's account makes causal law a methodological norm: not an inductive
  empirical fact, but the demand to understand everything.
- Relativity gives causality a factual physical form as causal structure:
  light cones replace simultaneity strata, and Kant's third analogy of
  simultaneous community falls away.
- Probability becomes original beside strict law. The ergodic attempt to
  reduce probability to residence-time inside exact law loses physical force
  under quantum mechanics.
- Entropy gives the main statistical law of time-direction, but reversible
  elementary laws do not themselves contain the arrow of time. The direction
  enters through probability judgments governed by the causal orientation of
  earlier as given ground of later.
- Freedom is not rescued by law-breaking. Weyl rejects both crude determinism
  and crude voluntarist exceptions to physics. The open place lies where being
  is open to sense in the I.
- Biological purposiveness is problem-setting, not optional projection. The
  hand is for grasping and the eye is for seeing; Darwinian explanation can
  begin only after such purposive structures are recognized.
- Biology is autonomous but not methodologically alien. Its laws are laws of
  specific complication, discovered by induction, comparison, experiment, and
  theoretical construction.
- The final boundary of natural science is not ordinary mind-body interaction
  but the double position of the I: natural individual and meaning-giving
  consciousness.

## Architecture Of The World-Picture

Part III has a two-step architecture.

Section 22 asks what becomes of substance. Weyl's answer is that substance does
not disappear; it migrates. It is no longer a self-identical stuff but a series
of technical carriers: identifiable atom, force-center, field-knot, boundary in
field law, conserved quantity, quantum state, and observation-dependent
phenomenon.

Section 23 asks what becomes of causality. Weyl's answer is again migratory
rather than eliminative. Causality is displaced from a direct metaphysical bond
between individual events into natural law, causal structure, probability,
entropy, experimental agency, and the practical-metaphysical self-understanding
of the I.

The result is not positivism. Weyl repeatedly rejects explanations that merely
rename or dismiss metaphysical pressure. Matter, causality, chance, freedom,
and purposiveness are not eliminated; they are redistributed across the
scientific world-picture and its boundary with lived agency.

## Relation To Earlier Campaigns

The mathematical chapters established symbolic access to structures that cannot
be directly surveyed. Chapter III added invariance, transformation groups, and
objective structure. Natural science part I showed that symbolic objectivity
must be physically coupled and return to subjective-absolute givenness at
possible contact points. Natural science part II showed how measurement,
concept-formation, and theory-formation make that coupling responsible to
experience.

Part III now gives the ontology permitted by that method. The world-picture is
symbolic and constructive, but it is not arbitrary. It is constrained by field
equations, conservation laws, relativity, thermodynamics, probability,
measurement, quantum complementarity, biological function, and the boundary of
the I.

This is the strongest current shape of the Weyl dossier:

- symbolic construction is necessary because the objective world is not
  directly intuited
- invariance disciplines symbolic freedom
- measurement and experiment tie symbols to reality
- theory-systems, not isolated laws, are empirically accountable
- matter and causality are historical pressure points, not fixed categories
- law remains central but must coexist with statistics, time-direction,
  purposiveness, and the unresolved status of the I

## Promotion Recommendation

Do not yet create the final promoted Weyl natural-science dossier.

The appendices are not peripheral. Appendix A returns to the structure of
mathematics, appendix C updates quantum physics and causality, appendix E treats
physics and biology, and appendix F treats the physical world. Those appendices
are likely to revise the exact shape of the promoted synthesis.

Do, however, treat part III as a completed source-local checkpoint. If a
temporary promoted pressure note is needed, it should say:

> The natural-science campaign shows that Weyl's symbolic construction reaches
> objectivity through invariance, measurement, theory-level correction, and a
> world-picture in which substance and causality survive only as transformed
> scientific and methodological structures.

## Translation State

Sections 22-23 are working encounter translations, not publication
translations. Every active page in the range was visually checked against page
images, and the cleaned German was corrected where OCR damaged quotes,
notation, references, formulas, page anchors, or bibliographic entries.

Remaining risks:

- Weyl's references to quantum mechanics are historically layered; appendix C
  should be read before treating the section-23 probability account as his
  final word.
- `Schöpfung` should not be over-theologized. In section 23 it names the
  structural placement of the improbable past required by forward-directed
  probability judgment.
- `Zweckmäßigkeit` should not be flattened into either modern biological
  function or metaphysical teleology. Weyl uses it as a problem-setting
  category that causal biology must first recognize and then explain.
- `Gesicht` at the Fichte/Schelling close remains pressure-bearing. `Vision`
  is usable in English, but the German should be preserved where the double
  sense of face/sight/light matters.
