## Worked Examples

Historical explosion and historian's hindsight:

In `Прерывное и непрерывное`, Lotman describes the present as a point between
two asymmetric directions. Looking backward, the past narrows toward the
present like a cone; looking forward, the audience faces a fan of unrealized
possibilities. At the moment of explosion, causality and probability no longer
determine which path will be selected. After the selection, causal chains resume
and the observer re-describes the random outcome as if it had been the only
possible one.

Lotman's examples include the Chekhov gun that may or may not fire, the dead
soldier whose accidental death cuts off whole chains of possible futures, the
early death of Nikolai Turgenev as a lost literary future, and the historical
description that removes chance from wars, revolutions, and other explosive
events. The historian then constructs a coherent line from retained facts to
the endpoint and risks giving the endpoint the false aura of destiny.

Duel as bounded unpredictability:

In `Момент непредсказуемости`, Lotman makes the duel example explicit. Pushkin's
description of Lensky's possible futures places the reader before a bundle of
potential trajectories at the moment Onegin and Lensky approach with raised
pistols. Lotman then compares this to the duel between Pushkin and Dantes. At
the instant before the shot, there is no predetermined future in lived reality;
there is a bounded set of equally probable futures. A slip on the trampled snow
or a tremor of the hand would have sent both Pushkin's life and Lensky's plot
onto other paths.

This example also gives the dossier its first internal scope check. One can
say that Pushkin in Petersburg in the 1830s was doomed if one is describing the
large-scale series of his last years. One cannot say that he was already doomed
at the moment when he took the pistol in his hand. The degree of predictability
changes with the series into which the event is inserted.

Historian as backward prophet:

The same section gives the exact "historian as prophet predicting backward"
formulation through Pasternak's line about Hegel. Lotman uses it to define the
historian's double position. From the future, the historian already knows the
real chain of events; by imaginatively returning to the past, he presents those
known results as if they were predictions. Lotman compares the historian to a
theater spectator seeing a play for the second time: the ending is already
known, but the spectator can re-enter the present tense of the staged action
and simulate not knowing. In this process chance disappears from history.

Artistic ending and rereading:

In `Феномен искусства`, Lotman shifts the same operation into art. The artwork
has an ending, and that endpoint becomes the point from which the whole plot is
read again. The title or ending changes meaning as the reader moves through the
work; the ending requires a return to the beginning. Sequential reading is
converted into simultaneous memory, and the formerly contingent sequence is
revalued.

The examples are `Пиковая дама`, `Чайка`, `Евгений Онегин`, and `Василий
Теркин`. Serial publication sharpens the case: a chapter may be published
before later chapters are even written, so author and reader occupy a world in
which the future is not yet known. Later memory reorganizes that open process
into a meaningful whole.

Repeated reception:

Lotman adds a third example type: repeating the same recording or rereading a
text is not zero-information repetition, because the receiver has changed. The
same external text becomes active in a different receiving intelligence. This
connects retrospective inevitability to dynamic memory rather than only to
historical narration.

## Mechanism Or Lens

Mechanism.

The operation is:

1. A present state opens onto a bounded field of possible futures.
2. An explosive moment selects one path by chance, not by causal necessity.
3. The unrealized paths then separate in meaning from the realized path.
4. Once selected, the path enters ordinary chains of explanation.
5. Memory, narrative, or historical description then returns to the pre-event
   point and reconstructs the realized path as if it were necessary.
6. The unrealized paths are demoted from possible futures into impossibilities,
   mistakes, omissions, or irrelevant noise.

The mechanism is not simply "hindsight bias." It is a semiotic transformation:
the observer's later model changes the event's meaning by converting temporal
openness into retrospective necessity.

## Layer

Mixed: mechanism, historical diagnosis, and literary reading.

The mechanism is the temporal asymmetry between forward possibility and
backward reconstruction. The historical diagnosis is Lotman's warning that
historians and cultures often remove chance from explosive events. The literary
reading is the role of endings, titles, rereading, and artistic memory in
turning sequence into simultaneous meaning.

The slide matters. Art does not merely illustrate the historical mechanism; it
is one of the domains where Lotman thinks the mechanism becomes visible in a
controlled form.

## Scope Of Applicability

Strong scope:

- historical description after revolutions, wars, deaths, and other explosive
  events
- bounded event analysis where the scale of the series matters, as in
  Pushkin's duel considered as one shot rather than as two doomed years
- literary works with marked endings, significant titles, serial composition,
  or strong rereading effects
- cultural memory when it reorganizes a past event around the present endpoint
- interpretive situations where a later observer has more structure than an
  earlier participant

Bounded scope:

- the future fan is never infinite; Lotman says the future excludes what cannot
  enter the given system
- gradual processes and anonymous technical development may be described with
  more legitimate continuity than explosive events
- retrospective reconstruction can be careful when it explicitly preserves the
  unrealized alternatives and marks its own endpoint pressure
- changing the scale changes the degree of predictability; this is a scope
  rule, not a loophole

## Where It Does Not Apply

Do not use this mechanism for every act of historical explanation. It does not
mean causality is unreal, that all narratives are falsifications, or that every
outcome was equally possible before it happened.

It is also weak for purely synchronic description, for cases where the field of
possibility is not at stake, or for technical processes whose development is
mostly slow, cumulative, and anonymous. Lotman himself treats the history of
technology as more hospitable to gradual explanation than personal artistic or
political history.

Do not use the mechanism across scales without marking the shift. "Pushkin was
doomed in the last two years of his life" and "Pushkin was doomed at the moment
he took the pistol" are different claims for Lotman. The first may be a
large-series judgment; the second erases the bounded unpredictability of the
event.

## Risk / Overreach

The false doctrine would be: "history always lies by making chance look
necessary."

Lotman's better claim is narrower. Retrospective description tends to erase
explosive unpredictability unless the historian actively preserves it. Careful
historical work is not impossible; it must resist the observer's built-in
temptation to read backward from the result as if the result were the cause.

The second overreach risk is aesthetic: treating every artwork as a neat
machine whose ending explains all earlier parts. Lotman's own serial-publication
examples block that simplification, because the open future can be real for the
author as well as for the reader.

The third overreach risk is scale substitution: treating a large-scale
predictability claim as if it explained away the chance structure of a
particular explosive event.

## Comparator

Peirce's tychism is the strongest mechanism-layer comparator to check next: if
chance is a real ingredient in becoming, then Lotman's explosion should not be
reduced to ignorance inside an already determined chain.

Whitehead is the second comparator: the asymmetry between past actuality and
future possibility should constrain how far Lotman's backward-cone / forward-fan
model can be generalized.

Eco is the literary comparator: rereading, title, ending, and model-reader
constraints should help distinguish legitimate retrospective interpretation
from over-reading.

Status of comparator: named but not source-checked in this dossier. That is why
this passes locally for Lotman but does not yet promote a repo-wide term.

## Counter-Check

Weakening evidence would include:

- Lotman treating retrospective transformation as only a defect of bad
  historians rather than as a general memory mechanism.
- A later Lotman passage where explosion is reduced to speed or violence rather
  than unpredictability.
- Historical cases where the relevant actors preserved the alternative futures
  clearly enough that later reconstruction does not erase contingency.
- Literary cases where an ending does not reorganize earlier material and where
  rereading does not change the receiver's information state.
