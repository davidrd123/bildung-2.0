## Why This Passage

The May 6 extractions treated Part I as Lotman's first compact mechanism
sequence: text has three functions; autocommunication turns messages into codes;
rhetoric generates meaning by forced translation between heterogeneous systems;
and iconic rhetoric shows the same mechanism across mirrors, theater, painting,
and behavior. This packet tests whether that sequence is really present in the
Russian files before the reader drafts or chat extractions are used as method
evidence.

## Stable Raw-Source Findings

§1, `Три функции текста`: Lotman explicitly rejects a transmission-only model
as the whole of semiosis. Adequate transfer requires artificial simplification,
reduced memory, and near identity of codes; artificial languages model only one
function of language. The creative function appears where translation is
asymmetrical, where codes intersect without being identical, and where mixed
translatability / untranslatability produces an increment of meaning. The same
chapter then adds memory: the text is not only a generator of new meanings but a
condenser of cultural memory, capable of accumulating later contexts.

§2, `Автокоммуникация`: The raw text supports the reader claim that Lotman is
not merely adding a self-address channel to Jakobson's model. In `Я - ОН`,
information moves between persons under a constant code; in `Я - Я`, new codes
are introduced, information grows by transformation and reformulation, and the
same person occupies sender and receiver positions. Formal, even asemantic,
structures can reorganize meaning. A real poetic text is not simply `Я - Я`;
it oscillates between `Я - ОН` and `Я - Я`, with codes becoming messages and
messages becoming codes. Tatyana and Lensky are used as examples of texts
functioning as codes for self- and world-interpretation. Culture itself can be
viewed as a collective autocommunication, but Lotman warns that real cultures
contain both communication types even when one orientation dominates.

§3, `Риторика - механизм смыслопорождения`: The raw text supports the strongest
Part I mechanism claim. Human and collective consciousness are heterogeneous:
at minimum, they require differently organized systems exchanging texts. One
system operates discretely, through segmented chains; the other is continual,
with meaning spread across an n-dimensional semantic space. Exact translation
between these systems is impossible, but approximate equivalence under a shared
cultural-psychological and semiotic context is what produces new semantic
relations. A trope is therefore not ornament; it is an adequacy relation between
mutually noncomparable meaningful elements inside a context. Later in the
chapter, rhetoric is extended beyond art into scientific modeling, analogy, and
the generative side of hypothesis formation. The whole text can become a
`большое слово`, and rhetorical structure is treated as an added, foreign
organization that works through tension with the organic structure of the text.

§4, `Иконическая риторика`: The raw text supports the extraction's claim that
iconic rhetoric is not a side example but a cross-media continuation of the
same mechanism. Doubling is named as an ontological precondition for turning
the world of objects into a world of signs. Double doubling, especially through
mirrors in painting, makes the semiotic process conscious by exposing the
difference between object, image, and mode of representation. Lotman states
that a rhetorical utterance cannot simply be re-expressed non-rhetorically
because rhetorical structure belongs to content, not just expression. Theater
then functions as an intermediate code and `код-переводчик` between life and
the visual arts. The life / theater / visual-art triangle becomes a field of
symbolic exchange, where double belonging to different semiotic systems creates
new meanings.

## Residue / Strain Report

- The raw OCR is visibly noisy in diagrams, formulas, italicized foreign
  citations, names, and some page-heading transitions. This pass confirms
  argumentative structure, not exact wording.
- §2 starts at the end of raw `005` and continues in raw `006`. Reader and TTS
  file boundaries are operationally clean, but source-facing references should
  remember that the printed chapter crosses the split.
- `смысловое пятно` is clear in context, but `semantic patch` may be too neat;
  a later checked packet should decide whether `patch`, `spot`, or `blur`
  better preserves Lotman's spatial metaphor.
- `код-переводчик` should not be flattened too quickly. `translator-code`
  preserves the code as active mediator; `translation-code` sounds more natural
  but may weaken the agency of the theatrical intermediary.
- The scientific-rhetoric passage in §3 is strong enough to keep as a source
  candidate, but exact quotation should wait for scan checking because nearby
  OCR noise affects technical terms.

## Adversarial Note

This inventory may over-unify Part I by reading four chapters as one clean
mechanism sequence. The raw text supports the sequence, but Lotman also shifts
between communication theory, poetics, cultural typology, rhetoric, visual
semiotics, and historical examples without always preserving strict layer
boundaries.

Difficulty type: contextual + methodological.

What would weaken the inventory: a scan-backed reread showing that one of the
chapters works mainly as local poetics rather than as part of the broader
mechanism; or a later Lotman passage that treats `автокоммуникация`, `риторика`,
and `промежуточное кодирование` as separate analogies rather than linked
operations.

## What This Sharpens

The May 6 extraction's core Part I reading is source-supported at the raw-OCR
level. The strongest reusable result is not a broad claim about the
semiosphere, but a smaller mechanism family:

1. meaning grows where codes are non-identical;
2. self-address works by introducing a second code and restructuring the self;
3. trope/rhetoric makes incompatibility generative inside a context;
4. iconic and theatrical mediation show the same code-translation pressure
   across nonverbal media.

This is strong enough to guide the next checked packets, especially the
Hoffmeyer code-duality crosswalk and a later Langer presentational-forms check.
