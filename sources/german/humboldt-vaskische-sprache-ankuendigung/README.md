# Humboldt - *Ankuendigung einer Schrift ueber die Vaskische Sprache*

Wilhelm von Humboldt, *Ankuendigung einer Schrift ueber die Vaskische
Sprache und Nation, nebst Angabe des Gesichtspunctes und Inhalts derselben*.
Printed in *Wilhelm von Humboldts Gesammelte Schriften*, Band III, Erste
Abteilung: *Werke III*, edited by Albert Leitzmann. Berlin: B. Behr's
Verlag, 1904, pp. 288-299.

## What This Is

A small German source-language landing zone for the short Vaskische
announcement cited by Cassirer at PSF I p. 99 and used again in *Die
Kantischen Elemente in Wilhelm von Humboldts Sprachphilosophie*.

This is not a Basque/Humboldt campaign. It exists to close the minor PSF I
chapter I source-use gap and to provide the exact language-as-mediation passage
that Cassirer quotes through Steinthal.

## Current Finding

The article begins at GS III p. 288 and is followed by *Essai sur les langues
du nouveau Continent* at p. 300. In the local IA PDF, the checked article range
uses `pdf_page = printed_page + 12`.

The working normalized excerpt covers the passage where language is described
as `Vermittlerin` between infinite and finite nature and between individuals,
then as the basis for the `Ich` / `Du` relation. This is the compact passage
Cassirer uses for the chapter I Humboldt/Kant opening.
