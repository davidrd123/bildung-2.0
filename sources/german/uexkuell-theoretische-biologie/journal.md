## 2026-04-01 — Review note after encounters 01-02

**Cross-project pattern**

The repo now has at least three distinct modes of opacity under active pressure:

- `zeitmauer`: lexical opacity — German compounds and term-clusters that resist clean English mapping
- Bogdanov: conceptual opacity — philosophically dense claims stated in comparatively plain language
- Uexküll: architectural opacity — a family of terms whose internal structure carries the argument (`Merk` / `Wirk` / `Um` + `Welt`)

This matters because the same broad method is holding across all three cases: the residue appears exactly where English becomes too fluent too early.

**Method consequence**

The bounded source-campaign form used here is not just a convenience for Uexküll. It is now a reusable intermediate tier for source work that has outgrown single encounters but has not yet earned a full `texts/` project.

## 2026-04-01 — Encounter 03 worked

**What this tranche clarified**

- The many-worlds claim holds up under direct textual pressure. Uexküll is not merely saying that different animals interpret one shared neutral world differently; biology is asked to begin from a plurality of lawful subject-worlds.
- The `Merk*` family is now explicitly architectural. `Merkzeichen` is defined as the just-noticeable change in the content of attention, and `Merkmal` appears as what those signs become in the built world.
- The observer chapter blocks both naive objectivism and naive empathy. We do not gain the foreign subject's own `Merkzeichen`; we reconstruct a foreign Umwelt from our own objectified qualities under constraint.
- The late move to an `übersubjektive` lawfulness matters. It keeps the chapter from collapsing into a loose perspectivism and points forward toward the harder question of fit and law that encounter 04 should reopen through `Fügung`.

**Decision for now**

- Keep the glossary lean. Encounter 03 earned stronger notes on existing terms, not a wider term-list.
- Keep `Umwelttunnel` out of the glossary for now even though the term appears at the edge of this tranche; the later culmination still deserves to carry that pressure.

**Implication**

The bounded campaign has passed its first real stress test. The density here is clearly beyond a one-off source encounter, but it still does not require a full sequential translation lane. That is evidence that the intermediate form is doing real methodological work rather than just postponing a decision.

**Review notes**

## 2026-04-01 — Encounter 04 worked

**What this tranche clarified**

- `Fügung` is now under direct pressure as operative fit, not vague harmony. The wasp/prey example makes clear that Uexküll means an exact asymmetrical relation between organism, counterpart, and act.
- The closure of action in a `Funktionskreis` happens through the destruction of the relevant `Merkmal`, either objectively in the world or subjectively through inhibition.
- `Merkding`, `Wirkding`, and `Gegengefüge` show the object being recut from the standpoint of the subject. This is stronger than a general contrast between biologically relevant and physically complete description; the object is actively repartitioned into subject-relative roles.
- Encounter 03's `übersubjektive` lawfulness now returns in a harder idiom: `Planmäßigkeit` is named as a `Naturkraft` that binds according to rules, and `Weisheit` is explicitly detached from what the organism knowingly contains in its `Merkorgan`.

**Decision for now**

- Tighten the evidence notes for `Fügung` and `Planmäßigkeit`, but do not widen the glossary yet.
- Keep `Merkding`, `Wirkding`, and `Gegengefüge` local to the encounter until the late pages show whether they are truly recurring terms rather than this tranche's own analytic machinery.

**Implication**

The Uexküll line is no longer only a critique of neutral object-description. It is now also a positive account of how a subject cuts, binds, and closes its world. That makes encounter 05 less a new departure than a culmination.

## 2026-04-01 — Encounter 05 worked

**What this tranche clarified**

- `Umwelt` reaches its late form here. It is not a sealed private sphere but part of a dense weave of overlapping and partly contradictory worlds.
- `Umwelttunnel` gives the temporal form of an Umwelt from birth to death and makes clear that objects become tunnel-specific things.
- `Einpassung` is broader than ordinary adaptation. The late pages tie it to layered forms of self-service and other-service, and to the problem of bodies belonging to many worlds under different cuts.
- `Funktionskreis` is elevated from local circuit to invariant. Uexküll now calls it the one unwandelbare factor that bears and forms the world-weave itself and treats the functional circles as active nature-plans.

**Decision for now**

- Add `Umwelttunnel` back into the glossary, but keep it open and untranslated.
- Tighten `Umwelt` and `Funktionskreis` rather than widening the glossary further.
- Treat the Jennings material on page `222` as spillover context, not as the conceptual center of the encounter.

**Implication**

The initial five-encounter queue has now done what it needed to do. Uexküll entered as a bounded source campaign and proved that this intermediate form can hold real conceptual density without prematurely becoming a full translation project.
