## Scope

- Lines `255-368`
- Source: `../../../books/Die Antiquiertheit des Menschen - Gunther Anders.txt`

## Why This Tranche

This is the campaign's obvious center of gravity.

Anders names here the shame before self-made things, but he does more than coin a phrase:

- the contrast between made and born becomes existential
- the human being is judged against its own products
- the old Promethean pride flips into inferiority
- the first objections establish what this shame is not

## Current Pressure

The risk here is immediate fluency. `Prometheische Scham` is close enough to English that it can look already solved, even though the surrounding argument makes it stranger: the shame is directed not at thinghood, but at not being thing enough.

The tranche also pressures the relation between `creator` and `creatum`, creature and product, birth and manufacture. That network matters more than any single neat English phrase.
