## Why This Tranche

This is the campaign's worldlessness test.

Anders is not using Beckett as ornament. The opening sections of `Sein ohne Zeit` give him a way to name:

- negative parable as form
- the non-world of modern existence
- continued existence without directed action
- waiting as the misinterpretation of merely staying there

## Current Pressure

This tranche risks looking like a detachable literary essay, but it likely belongs to the same apparatus-world diagnosis as the other encounters. The point is not Beckett commentary for its own sake; it is a phenomenology of stalled historical life.

The phrase `Sein ohne Zeit` itself will probably resist smooth English because it names both a temporal and world-structural deprivation.
