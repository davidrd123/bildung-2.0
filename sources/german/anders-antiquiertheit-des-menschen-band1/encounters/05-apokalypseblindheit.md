## Why This Tranche

This is the bomb opening and the point where Anders's moral and methodological claims become explicit.

The first sections already force the core issues:

- bagatellized objects require overstatement
- philosophy must leave the academic idiom when the object demands it
- the `Wie`-question gives way to the `Ob`-question
- humanity becomes collectively killable
- `Apokalypseblindheit` names the failure to live proportionally to that fact

## Current Pressure

The difficulty here is scale. The rhetoric can look inflated if detached from Anders's claim that understatement is already built into the epoch. So the opening must be read as a theory of truthful exaggeration, not as style alone.

This tranche will also show whether `Apokalypseblindheit` can be translated at all without losing the link between blindness, imagination, and the refusal of fear.
