## Campanella Pressure

Leaf 0829 opens its Trismegistus dossier with Word, mind, divine generation, and the sensible world:

> Mercurius ... cum Deum omnipotentem rerum omnium autorem celebrasset, ac Hylem, mundum sensibilem, et hominem natos: qui tamen non nati, erant in verbo Dei vita et Deus. Deumque cum verbo suo, per quod fecit omnia, genuisse tertiam mentem, quae Deus est, et spiritusque Numen, ut ipse inquit.

This is not just Greek Corpus Hermeticum pressure. For Campanella's reading surface, Ficino's Latin `Pimander` matters most.

## Extracted Latin Anchors

Poimandres as divine mind:

> Sum inquit ille Pimander mens diuinae potentiae: ac tu uide quid uelis: ipse uero tibi ubique adero.

Light, voice, and Word:

> Inde fumus quidam manus in sonitum erumpebat: ex sonitu uox egrediebatur: quam ego luminis uocem existimabam ex hac luminis uoce uerbum factum prodiit.

Mind, Word, Father:

> Lumen illud ego sum mens deus tuus ... Mentis uero germen uerbum lucens dei filium. ... quod in te uidet & audit uerbum domini, mens aut pater deus. Neque enim distant adinuicem: horum unio uitae est.

The second mind / demiurgic mind:

> Mens autem deus utriusque sexus ... uita & lux cum uerbo suo mentem alteram opificem peperit qui quidem deus ignis atque spiritus numen.

Seven governors and fate:

> Septem deinceps fabricauit gubernatores: qui circulis mundum sensibilem complectuntur: eorumque dispositio fatum uocatur.

Human being as image and double nature:

> pater omnium intellectus uita & fulgor existens. hominem sibi similem procreauit ... Deus enim reuera propria forma nimirum delectatus. opera eius omnia usui concessit humano.

> Quando causam solus homo ex uniuersis terrenis animantibus duplicis naturae censetur: mortalis quidem propter corpus: immortalis autem propter hominem ipsum substantialem.

## Working Takeaway

Ficino gives Campanella the vocabulary bridge:

- `Pimander` as `mens diuinae potentiae`
- luminous `verbum` as offspring of mind
- father/mind/word language that Campanella can Christianize into Word and third mind
- `mens altera opifex` as the world-forming divine mind
- seven governors whose disposition is called fate
- human being as likeness/image, mortal by body and immortal by essential human

This extract explains why leaf 0829 can move so quickly from Word and mind to world, human being, fate, and governance. The source layer is already compressed in Ficino's Latin.
