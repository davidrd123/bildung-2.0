## Campanella Pressure

Leaf 0830 compresses two statue passages that are separated in the Asclepius:

> ... sicut Deus ad sui similitudinem creavit Deos caelestes ... ita homo ad sui imaginem etiam facit, sicuti moris erat eorum, qui statuas loquentes, et responsa dantes in Aegypto constituebant ...

> ... quoniam animas dare non possunt, evocant animas daemonum, vel Angelorum, easque includunt imaginibus et statuis: quae et benefaciendi, et malefaciendi ex inclusis numinibus sortiuntur potestatem ...

> Praeterea illiciuntur divinis corporalibus rebus, symbolum habentibus cum eis: sunt enim lapides et plantae et metalla, aromata, et harmonia, cantusque vim divinam habentes ...

The source pressure is exact: fabricated gods, animated statues, divine/demonic souls inserted into images, herbs/stones/aromatics/hymns/harmony, and human beings as makers of gods.

## Extracted Latin Anchors

Asclepius 24, animated statues:

> Statuas dicis o Trismegiste? TRISME. Statuas: o Asclepi. videsne: quatenus tu ipse diffidas. statuas animatas / sensu et spiritu plenas / tanta et talia facientes. statuas futurorum prescias / easque forte vates omnes somnijs multisque alijs rebus predicentes ...

Asclepius 37, art of making gods:

> inuenerunt artem qua deos efficerent. cui inuente / adiunxerunt virtutem de mundi natura conuenientem / eamque miscentes et (quoniam animas facere non poterant) euocantes animas demonum / vel angelorum / eas indiderunt imaginibus sanctis / diuinisque misterijs. per quas solas idola et benefaciendi et malefaciendi vires habere potuissent.

Asclepius 38, divine materials and `deorum fictor`:

> Constat o Asclepi: de herbis / de lapidibus / de aromatibus / vim diuinitatis naturalem in se habentibus. et propter hanc causam: sacrificija frequentibus oblectantur hymnis / et laudibus / dulcissimis sonis in modum celestis harmonie concinentibus.

> ... sic deorum fictor est homo.

Asclepius 39-40, Heimarmene, necessity, order:

> quam ergo partem rationis ειμαρμενη vel fata incolunt o Trismegiste ... Hec itaque ειμαρμενη & necessitas / ambe sibi inuicem indiuiduo connexe sunt glutino ...

> Hec ergo tria: ειμαρμενη id est fatum / necessitas / ordo / vel maxime dei nutu sunt effecta ...

## Critical Check

Nock-Festugiere vol. II confirms the same structure in sections 24, 38, and 39-40. The Bude text is especially useful for locating Campanella's two-fold compression:

- `statuas animatas sensu et spiritu plenas` in Ascl. 24
- `deorum fictor est homo` in Ascl. 38
- `Heimarmene, necessitas, ordo` in Ascl. 39-40

## Working Takeaway

Campanella's leaf 0830 is not only repeating the famous statue passage. It welds together:

- Ascl. 24: animated, speaking, prophetic statues
- Ascl. 37: art of making gods by summoning demons/angels into images
- Ascl. 38: herbs, stones, aromatics, hymns, harmony, and `deorum fictor est homo`
- Ascl. 39-40: the fate/necessity/order triad from the same governance context

This is the highest-pressure extract for the Campanella tranche because it explains why leaf 0830 treats statue fabrication as part of governance rather than as a decorative Hermetic curiosity.
