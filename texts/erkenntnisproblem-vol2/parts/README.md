# Parts

Vol. 2 part files should follow the Vol. 1 paragraph-cluster pattern but include explicit status lines from the beginning.

Each part should open with a short finding paragraph, written after the translation is drafted. This paragraph names what the tranche actually showed; it is not a preview and not a summary of coverage.

If one passage, contrast, or argumentative move carries the tranche, name it as
the structural center in the opener or notes. Do not add a separate heading
unless the material needs it.

Use re-entry hooks lightly. A hook is an unresolved pressure worth returning to at higher resolution: a consciously compressed quotation, a primary-source question, a justification Cassirer leaves implicit, or a German construction the first English pass cannot yet fully carry. Hooks are not a checklist and do not have to be solved in the same part.

Suggested first-file shape:

````markdown
# 001-bacon-opening-and-the-critique-of-understanding

Cassirer opens Vol. 2 by distinguishing Bacon's empirical program from the constructive mathematical natural science that closed Vol. 1. Bacon's strength and limit appear together: he attacks the self-enclosure of the understanding, but he initially frames nature as something to be forced into testimony rather than as a lawful order that answers thought from within.
````

Do not place commentary inside the English block. If commentary is needed, put it under `Notes`, `Re-entry hooks`, or `journal.md`.

Substantive Latin, French, or other source-language footnote quotations should
be translated in the draft-footnote layer when they carry evidence. Pure
bibliographic references can remain source identifications. Any deferral of a
substantive quotation must be marked with a reason and a re-entry hook.

When hooks across consecutive parts repeatedly point to the same primary source or justification problem, that is the signal for a bounded reading note in `reading/`, not for overloading the part file.
