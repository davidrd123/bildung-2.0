# Campanella Qualification Of Part 018

Status: short Cassirer-side synthesis note, written after the Campanella source-campaign back-port. No new Latin opened here.

## Why This Note Exists

The Part 018 footnotes and glossary now record the source checks, but they do not by themselves carry the argument-form of the Campanella chain. The useful synthesis is not only that Cassirer cites the right passages. It is that the opened Campanella leaves show why the citation is both strong and over-compressed.

## Working Finding

Cassirer is right to use Campanella as a Renaissance source for early Spinozan intuition as becoming-one. Leaf 906 gives the exact formula `intrinsecatio, per quam unum fit aliud`, and leaf 402 confirms that exterior intellection means suffering from external things and becoming them.

But Cassirer's Part 018 paragraph becomes too smooth if this is allowed to stand for Campanella's whole theory of cognition. The opened Latin distinguishes registers:

- bodily taste and altered sensory entry: leaves 23 and 28
- exterior/object intellection as becoming: leaf 402
- hidden essential self-intellection as not becoming: leaf 402
- finite intrinsecation under the `mensura sui` limit: leaf 438
- hidden self-love and hidden self-sense: leaf 444
- divine immanence and transcendence as `intrinsecatur & extrinsecatur`: leaf 617
- beatific intrinsecation as the nominalized climax: leaf 906

So the corrected Cassirer-side use is: Campanella supports the formal bridge to Spinoza's early intuition, but the bridge should be read as register-bound rather than as a free-standing doctrine that all knowing is self-dissolution into the object.

## The Hidden-Self Bridge

The most promising unresolved bridge is not only `intrinsecatio`. It is the `abditus` family:

- leaf 402: `intellectione abdita per suam essentiam`
- leaf 444: `Amor abditus, quo se cuncta amant`
- leaf 444: `sensus abditus, quo sese cuncta sapiunt`

This cluster links hidden self-intellection, hidden self-love, and hidden self-sense. It may be the deeper Campanella structure beneath Cassirer's Spinoza comparison: finite beings are not merely dissolved into objects; they also have a hidden self-relation through which participation in divine being is known, loved, and tasted.

Do not promote this yet into a full dossier. It is a strong re-entry point if a later Spinoza section requires the relation between self-activity, love of God, and intuitive cognition to be traced back through Campanella.
