# Leibniz: Function, Symbol, and Harmony

Status: bounded chapter note for Book V, chapter 2. Keep chapter-local until Tschirnhaus supplies contrast evidence.

## Chapter Finding

Cassirer's Leibniz chapter answers the Spinoza close by changing the form of the problem. Spinoza had made mathematical order the form of absolute being, then exposed the anthropomorphic limit of that move: being was still explained through space-intuition and thought. Leibniz does not solve this by simply removing the human standpoint. He makes cognition's mediating forms themselves into the route to objectivity.

The chapter's center is the expansion of function into symbol and harmony. The function concept replaces the search for simple elements; symbolic expression replaces copy theory; the individual fact remains inexhaustible for finite analysis; monadology then interprets the individual substance as a unique law of development. Harmony is the chapter-close name for the agreement of these different ideal standpoints, not an added metaphysical ornament.

## Evidence Path

- Parts 031-032: Leibniz begins from truth, not from Descartes's self-consciousness or Spinoza's God as immediate starting point. Judgment requires predicate-in-subject inclusion, but finite cognition needs analysis, signs, and progressively clarified conditions.
- Parts 033-034: The youthful thought alphabet and combinatorics are not enough. Cassirer locates the decisive advance in the function concept: mathematics becomes an inquiry into forms of connection rather than an inventory of elements.
- Parts 035-036: The continuum forces the break with mereological reduction. The simple is not a smallest part inside the composite but an ideal ground or rule of generation; continuity becomes an objective logical postulate.
- Part 037: Force extends continuity into real temporal occurrence. Derivative force makes the present state tend toward and pre-involve the next, while work and living force give heterogeneous processes a common measurable form.
- Parts 038-039: Leibniz's truth concept becomes semiotic. Ideas, characters, differentials, work-magnitudes, hypotheses, and appearances are valid by relation-preserving expression, not likeness. The individual fact remains an endless task, but a rule of progress can make contingent truth valid.
- Part 040: The limit of finite logical analysis becomes monadology and harmony. Primitive force names the rule of an individual developmental series, and harmony names the accord among the ideal ways of considering through which being can be presented and interpreted.

## What This Is Not

This is not the Hobbes pattern repeated. Hobbes's self-created principles become absolute powers that bind their creator. Leibniz's constructed and symbolic forms do become binding, but Cassirer does not present this as a failure of self-created authority. He presents it as the necessary route by which reality becomes intelligible without becoming a copied object.

This is not the Spinoza pattern repeated either. Spinoza's geometry becomes the norm of being and then shows its anthropomorphic limit. Leibniz keeps the mediation visible: space, time, extension, and motion are grounded ways of considering, and concepts themselves are more or less perfect signs.

Nor is this a cross-book Cassirer thesis yet. The chapter gives a powerful Leibnizian answer, but the next chapter has to show whether that answer survives outside Leibniz's harmony-metaphysics.
