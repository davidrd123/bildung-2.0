# Gassendi To Hobbes: From Experience-Riddle To Constructive Method

This note bridges the Gassendi and Hobbes chapters after the completed *Disquisitio metaphysica* source campaign. It is bounded to `parts/007-016` and the local Gassendi source apparatus; it should not yet be promoted into a general thesis about empiricism, rationalism, or Cassirer's whole Vol. 2 arc.

## Local Finding

The Gassendi-Hobbes transition is not a simple move from empiricism to materialism, or from sensory origins to body. Cassirer's sharper contrast is methodological.

Gassendi exposes the problem that any empiricism tied to modern physics must solve: experience cannot remain passive sensory reception, because scientific objects require comparison, correction, abstraction, enlargement, naming, and relation. His own system repeatedly invokes these active functions but cannot ground them from its image/species theory.

Hobbes supplies the missing methodological form by making knowledge constructive. To know is to generate the object from conditions fixed by thought: cause becomes the aggregate of conditions, geometry becomes the model of demonstrability, names stabilize constructed judgments, and even nature is first reconstructed through the destruction-of-the-world thought experiment. But Cassirer's Hobbes chapter then shows a new failure: the products of construction harden into absolute powers, especially body, matter, motion, sovereign convention, and external pressure.

So Hobbes answers Gassendi's gap only halfway. He replaces passive experience with construction, but he cannot keep the constructed object from becoming an independent metaphysical thing again.

## Gassendi Side

The Gassendi chapter and the *Disquisitio* campaign show the same pressure from two directions.

- In `parts/007-011`, Cassirer argues that Gassendi's modern-scientific commitments outrun his sensory criterion: perception becomes mediated motion-sign, atomism requires scientific reason, space and time have objective validity beyond sensory action, and the scientific sun idea requires active mental enlargement.
- The *Disquisitio* source campaign confirms that this is not only Cassirer's external criticism. Gassendi repeatedly distinguishes proper idea/image from inference, analogy, consequence, abstraction, comparison, and amplification, while still needing those operations to explain science, substance, God, geometry, mind, and body.
- The late *Disquisitio* parts sharpen the image/species problem: even intellectual representation of extension seems to require some part-structure, and a merely negative self-description such as non-extension does not yield positive knowledge of mind.
- Gassendi's strongest methodological formula is negative: Descartes has not publicly demonstrated what he claims. But Gassendi's own positive account remains split between sensory images and operations the sensory model cannot fully explain.

## Hobbes Side

The Hobbes chapter begins exactly where Gassendi's weakness lies: the passive origin of knowledge is replaced by generation from conditions.

- `parts/012-013`: cause becomes the complete condition-aggregate whose positing makes the effect necessary. Knowledge is a priori where we can generate a content from its conditions. Geometry is demonstrable because we make its figures.
- `parts/014`: names and signs do not merely label things. They fix the course of thought, preserve rules in memory, and turn a particular insight into a universal judgment.
- `parts/015`: the world-destruction thought experiment negates the finished external world so that space, time, division, and body can be reconstructed from necessary possessions of consciousness.
- `parts/016`: the chapter closes by naming the reversal. Reason creates principles, but those principles separate from their conditions of genesis and return as absolute powers over thought and action.

This makes Hobbes the immediate methodological answer to Gassendi: where Gassendi leaves `experience` as an unexplained passage from object into I, Hobbes starts by asking what thought itself must construct, posit, divide, count, and name before any object can become knowable.

## What Changes In The Transition

The object changes status.

For Gassendi, the object is still something external that affects us, sends species, or leaves signs from which the mind reconstructs a cause. The riddle is how that external effect becomes a connected object of experience.

For Hobbes, the object of knowledge is initially something thought has generated or reconstructed from conditions. The riddle is how this generated order can claim reality without becoming a dogmatic absolute.

The role of sense changes.

For Gassendi, sense is the foundation and threatened criterion of knowledge. Error enters through judgment when it misassigns the sensory appearance to the thing in itself.

For Hobbes, sense and memory are only knowledge of fact. Science concerns consequences. Sensation can be called the principle of principles, but only as the internal phenomenon from which inquiry begins; it is then explained through motion and pressure from bodies.

The role of language changes.

Gassendi's *Disquisitio* often tests whether a term such as `idea`, `clear and distinct`, `substance`, or `objective reality` is being stretched beyond its legitimate image or inference base.

Hobbes makes naming part of the machinery of reason. Words are not innocent labels; they are tools by which mental sequence becomes publicly stable, countable, repeatable, and universal.

The method problem changes.

Gassendi asks for a criterion: how do we distinguish genuine clear knowledge from counterfeit clarity, real separability from conceptual separation, or proper idea from analogy?

Hobbes offers a criterion of generation: we know what we can construct from conditions. But Cassirer then tests whether this collapses into addition/subtraction, arbitrary convention, or body as absolute substance.

## Pressured Materials Beyond Descartes

This bridge pressures several nearby materials.

- Bacon: Hobbes transforms Bacon's knowledge-from-causes formula by replacing inner powers, forms, and appetites with condition-aggregates and mathematical relations. Gassendi sits between them: more physics-aware than Bacon, still not constructive enough for Hobbes.
- Epicurus and Democritus: Gassendi's sensory canonics and atomism become unstable once atomism itself needs non-sensory conceptual criteria.
- Aristotle and Scholasticism: Gassendi's species language and substance/accident model persist even where he modernizes perception by motion and optics.
- Galileo: Hobbes is Cassirer's stronger philosophical reception of Galilean method. Gassendi receives modern physics as material to be philosophically appropriated; Hobbes tries to turn its method into a universal logic.
- Spinoza: the next chapter tests whether genetic definition can avoid Hobbes's split between generated rational order and absolute body by identifying real ground and ground of knowledge more radically.
- Leibniz: the later Leibniz chapter will test the Hobbesian construction ideal by asking for deeper analysis of possibility, symbol, continuity, and the individual rather than stopping at construction by addition and subtraction.

## Rival Reading

A competent rival reading would say that Hobbes does not answer Gassendi at all. On that view, Hobbes is simply another materialist psychology: sensation still comes from external bodies pressing on organs, and body remains the real subject of philosophy. The constructive method would then be a logical overlay on the same basic metaphysical realism Gassendi never escapes.

The current bridge does not deny that. It says Cassirer makes the difference lie in the path of grounding. Gassendi begins from sensory contact and cannot justify the active forms that science requires. Hobbes begins from constructive generation and then falls back into metaphysical absolutizing. The failures rhyme, but they are not the same failure.

## Weakening Evidence

This bridge would weaken if later or source-level Hobbes work showed that Cassirer's constructive Hobbes is mostly imposed by Cassirer, while Hobbes's own texts consistently subordinate construction to body as first reality.

It would also weaken if a fuller Gassendi source campaign in the *Syntagma* or *Physica* showed a stronger positive account of mental operation than the *Disquisitio* and Cassirer's chapter currently expose: for example, a robust theory of comparison, sign-use, or abstraction that does more than append active functions to sensory species.

Finally, the bridge should be revised if Spinoza or Leibniz shows that Cassirer treats Hobbes less as the answer to Gassendi and more as a separate rationalist line whose real role is to mediate Spinoza's genetic definition.

## Re-entry

Use this note when returning to:

- Gassendi source work in the *Syntagma* / *Physica*: test whether Gassendi has a stronger positive method than the current bridge grants.
- Hobbes source work in `De Corpore`, `De Homine`, `Leviathan`, or `Elements of Law`: test whether construction or body has priority in Hobbes's own sequence.
- Spinoza Parts 020-022: test whether genetic definition transforms the Hobbes answer or merely intensifies it.
- Leibniz Parts 031-040: test whether symbolic cognition and function repair the hardening of constructed principles into absolute things.
