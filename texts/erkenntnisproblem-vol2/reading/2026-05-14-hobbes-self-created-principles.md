# Hobbes: Self-Created Principles Hardening Into Absolute Powers

## Local Finding

Across the Hobbes chapter, Cassirer repeatedly shows the same structural reversal. Hobbes begins from the autonomy of reason: knowledge is possible where thought generates, constructs, names, counts, and orders its own contents. But once these principles are created, they detach from the acts and conditions that produced them. They return as absolute powers that bind reason, action, and nature.

This is not merely a charge of inconsistency. It is Cassirer's way of locating Hobbes's philosophical originality and limit at once: Hobbes liberates thought from given things and inherited authority, then lets the products of that liberation harden into a new authority.

## Evidence Path

Part 012 opens the Hobbes chapter by transforming cause from substantial form or inner force into the complete aggregate of conditions. The Galilean-Hobbesian cause is constructible and conditional, not occult.

Part 013 generalizes this into method. Knowledge is secured where a content can be generated from its conditions: geometry is demonstrable because we construct its figures; physics must imitate that procedure by deriving appearances from possible causes. But Hobbes's logic narrows connection to decomposition and recomposition, addition and subtraction.

Part 014 transfers the issue to names and signs. Truth seems to dissolve into arbitrary names and conventions, but Cassirer recovers the rationalist motive: words stabilize judgment and let a particular insight become universally valid. The key phrase `veritas in dicto, non in re consistit` marks truth as belonging to what is said or asserted, not to things apart from judgment.

Part 015 carries the constructive method into nature philosophy. Hobbes negates the finished external world in order to reconstruct it from the necessary possessions of consciousness: space, time, division, and body. Yet body then hardens into absolute substance, and real motion becomes first and absolute as though the mediating construction had become dispensable.

The Lyon quotation in Part 015 is part of the evidence, not merely secondary ornament. It names the latent idealist consequence: Hobbes's geometrical universe remains something of the thought that engenders it. This supports the local finding while also marking a later cross-volume hinge for Kantian/transcendental comparison.

Part 016 closes the pattern in perception and psychology. Sensation is first the `Prinzip der Prinzipien`, because inquiry into phenomena must begin within phenomena, memory, and inner experience. Then Hobbes explains sensation by a remote material source: external bodies pressing on organs. The chapter's final sentence makes the pattern explicit: the product of reason separates from the conditions out of which it arose and becomes absolute reality.

## Chapter Boundary

This note is local to Hobbes. Bacon's chapter turned Baconian concepts against Bacon; Gassendi's chapter made experience the ground-riddle rather than the solution. Hobbes's chapter has a different shape: reason's self-creation becomes reason's new bondage.

The political analogy is not external decoration. Cassirer explicitly links Hobbes's derivation of political domination from free acts of will with his theoretical derivation of knowledge from self-created principles. In both cases, free positing produces a power that then binds the one who posited it.

The world-destruction thought experiment should be kept available as an annotation candidate for later comparison with the Kantian chapters. Do not turn it into an external phenomenology claim from this chapter alone.

The same restraint applies to Cassirer's later systematic vocabulary. Hobbes's failure looks close to a substance/function problem: he reaches constructive function and lets it fall back into substantial absoluteness. But this note should not yet treat the chapter as evidence for Cassirer's whole `Substanzbegriff und Funktionsbegriff` position. That comparison has to be earned by the Spinoza, Leibniz, and later Kant material.

## Next Test

Spinoza should test whether this pattern persists or is transformed. If Spinoza refuses the split between generated rational order and absolute substance, then Hobbes's problem is chapter-local: a failure to hold construction and substance together. If Spinoza repeats the hardening of rational product into absolute power, then the issue becomes a broader problem in the development of rationalism.

Do not promote the Hobbes pattern to a cross-book Cassirer thesis until the Spinoza opening supplies contrast evidence.
