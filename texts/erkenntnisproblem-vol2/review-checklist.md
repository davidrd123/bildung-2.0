# Review Checklist

Use this checklist for Vol. 2 maintenance passes and preflight review.

This is a discipline aid, not a completeness contract. Use it to notice whether a pass stayed honest about what it did and did not reach.

## Per Tranche

- Verify the corrected German against the PDF or JP2 scan for the active tranche only.
- Check paragraph boundaries, footnotes, hyphenation repairs, and mid-sentence carryover at page breaks.
- Keep the English as draft translation, not summary or commentary.
- Check for two distinct failure modes: gloss-creep, where translation turns into paraphrase, and compression, where fluent English silently drops quotation, texture, or connective tissue.
- Put explanatory commentary outside the English block.
- Translate substantive Latin, French, or other source-language footnote quotations when they carry evidence; leave only bibliographic footnotes as source IDs.
- Write the opening finding paragraph after the translation pass, not before it.
- Leave at least one explicit re-entry hook when the tranche exposed unresolved pressure; use `none` only when that is a real judgment.
- Update `journal.md` with what the tranche clarified, its structural center when one is visible, a routing decision for any pressure that should move elsewhere, and `Decision for now`.
- Add or revise glossary entries only when a term is doing repeated structural work.
- Keep source-bound notes in `journal.md`; put speculative cross-volume or cross-project notes in `reading/` until earned.

## Periodic Maintenance

- Run a quality-zone check after each major chapter or after roughly 10 parts, whichever comes first.
- Audit earlier parts after a vocabulary cluster stabilizes.
- Treat the translating agent's own source-facing review as the mandatory second pass before moving forward. External or sub-agent review is optional pressure-testing for targeted risks, not final verification; independently check source-language details before marking a tranche clean.
- Strengthen thin glossary entries so they say what the term does philosophically, not only which English options are available.
- Check PDF/printed-page/scan alignment whenever page maps cross front matter, blank pages, or the index gap.
- Consider a bounded second-pass memo after a closed chapter or book if accumulated hooks need resolution; do not create a standing memo layer by habit.
- Do not build viewer/export infrastructure until the part format has been tested by multiple batches.

## Quality Questions

- Does the English preserve the argumentative structure without mechanically copying German period length?
- Does the English preserve quotations, examples, and connective texture where they carry the argument, or did it silently compress them?
- Is the local register right: empirical method, rationalist metaphysics, psychological analysis, Newtonian science, or Kantian critique?
- Are Latin, French, and historical technical terms kept visible where they carry the argument?
- Are substantive Latin or French footnote quotations translated, or explicitly marked with a reason and re-entry hook if deferred?
- Does the opening paragraph state what the tranche clarified, not merely what pages it covered?
- Does the journal name the structural center of the batch when the material has one?
- Does each routing decision say why a pressure stays in the journal, moves to the glossary, becomes a reading note, becomes a thread, or remains unpromoted?
- Do the re-entry hooks name the next resolution point without pretending to resolve it?
- Has the German block preserved Cassirer's main prose at full density, even when documentary footnotes or page spillover are handled selectively?

## Open-Ended Progress Gate

Do not begin an open-ended forward run until the current part discipline has
passed repeated correction. The minimum gate is:

- corrected German checked against PDF or JP2 scans
- substantive quoted footnotes translated or explicitly routed
- draft English preserving argumentative texture
- opening finding paragraph written after translation
- journal entry with structural center, routing decision, hooks, and decision for now
- glossary kept empty or updated only by earned repeated pressure
- any external or sub-agent review verified against the source where it touches source-language detail

Speed is allowed only after the first few tranches pass this gate without
substantive review corrections.

For an open-ended `/goal` or other burst run, the translating agent's own
source-facing review creates `controlled draft` status, not automatic parity
with tranches that have also gone through separate iterative pressure. After a
multi-tranche burst, run a chapter-level calibration before commit or status
elevation: at minimum, spot-check claimed scan paragraph boundaries and review
the load-bearing closing tranche against German, Latin/source-language notes,
and routing decisions. Record whether the result is `self-reviewed`,
`probe-reviewed`, or fully second-pass reviewed.
