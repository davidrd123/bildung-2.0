## Claim

Weyl's symbolic construction becomes responsible to reality through
measurement, concept-formation, and whole-theory correction. Physics is not
free formal invention, but neither is it passive copying of what is simply
given.

## Method

Measurement translates reality into exact symbolic determination. It requires
equality, order, additivity, reproducible units, and often implicit laws of
nature. Exactness comes from symbolic and operational structure, not from raw
sensory sharpness.

Concept-formation determines what is to be measured. Mass, momentum, force,
field, charge, and spectrum are not names for obvious sensory traits. They are
constructive concepts won through experimental reaction, hidden structure, and
lawful decomposition and recomposition.

Theory-formation makes the whole system the unit of empirical responsibility.
Individual laws are not checked in isolation; the whole nexus of geometry,
concepts, laws, correction procedures, and predictions meets experience.

## Reality-Contact

The objective world-picture is symbolic and coordinate-relative, while
immediate givenness is subjective-absolute. That does not make the objective
less real. It means objectivity is invariant convertibility plus possible
return to experience.

Weyl's anti-surplus rule matters here: a theoretical addition that can make no
possible difference in givenness is epistemologically idle.

## Use Limits

Do not flatten Weyl into positivism. He does not reduce theory to observations.
The theory-system can contain hidden elements and ideal structures, but those
structures must be operationally disciplined and corrigible as a whole.
