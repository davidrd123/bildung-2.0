## Claim

Weyl does not eliminate substance and causality. He redistributes them across
modern physics, probability, experimental agency, and the boundary problem of
life and the I.

## Matter

Matter is no longer a simple substrate occupying empty space. It is transformed
through atomism, force-centers, field theory, ether history, conservation
laws, quantum states, and complementarity. The field is not another thing in a
container; it changes the ontology of space itself.

Conservation laws become the modern carrier of substance-pressure, but Weyl
does not make them metaphysically immune to revision.

## Causality And Chance

Causality is not identical with function-law. A functional equation does not
decide which term determines which. Causal understanding involves a cut between
conditions and event, and that cut is partly practical and agent-indexed.

Quantum mechanics relocates causality. The wave-state evolves under strict
law, while measurement outcomes are governed by primary probability. Chance is
not merely ignorance once quantum probability is in view.

Entropy and probability ground time-direction, but reversible elementary laws
do not by themselves contain the arrow of time.

## Freedom

Freedom is not rescued by arbitrary law-breaking. Weyl rejects both crude
determinism and crude voluntarist exception. Appendix E adds that mutation and
few-photon perception open a recognizable non-causal element, but that is not
enough. Vital, psychic, and spiritual factors would need symbolic
representation inside a theory of reality.

## Use Limits

Do not use Weyl as a simple indeterminist. His position keeps strict law,
primary probability, thermodynamic statistics, agency, and the unresolved I
problem together.
