## Claim

Chapter III and natural science part I show how Weyl's symbolic construction
becomes objective. Objectivity is not direct intuition and not privileged
coordinates. It is invariance under the relevant transformation group,
physically coupled to fields, instruments, and possible return to lived
givenness.

## Core Pattern

Geometry first breaks the equation between intuitive Euclidean space and
mathematical structure. Projective, affine, metric, topological, and
color-space examples show that geometry is a family of possible relation
systems.

Objectivity then enters through transformation. A structure is objective when
its content survives the appropriate group of coordinate changes or
automorphisms. Coordinates remain necessary, but their arbitrariness is
controlled by invariance.

Physics adds the stronger test. Metric structure becomes real only when it is
coupled to clocks, rods, light, inertial motion, field equations, matter, and
possible experimental contact. The metric field acts on matter and receives
reaction from matter.

## Why It Matters

This dossier modifies the chapter-II symbolic-access claim. Symbolic access to
totality is not enough. In geometry and physics, symbolic structure must define
the transformations under which it remains invariant and the physical
operations through which it can return to experience.

## Use Limits

Do not over-modernize Weyl's local-frame and gauge-like passages into later
physics vocabulary without source evidence. The source-local synthesis keeps
them historically tied to Weyl's own geometry-to-physics bridge.
