## Claim

Weyl gives a strong rule for diagrams and symbolic schemata: draw the limited
symbol sharply, but know the level where it is valid.

Appendix D is the clearest case. Chemical valence diagrams are not primitive
truths and not disposable fictions. They are a useful layer whose foundation is
lowered into quantum mechanics through electron spin, antisymmetry, invariant
theory, and resonance.

## The Hierarchy

Weyl's chemical hierarchy is:

- quantum-mechanical structures
- invariant-theory structures
- valence diagrams

Discovery historically moves from surface diagram down toward foundations.
Explanation moves from deeper structure back up toward usable diagrams.

## Repo Rule

This is directly reusable beyond chemistry:

> A diagram becomes dangerous only when it forgets its mediating layer.

The rule also applies to genetic aggregates, chromosomal linkages, and any
project-side visual schema. Provisional diagrams are valuable when clear,
limited, and source-indexed.

## Use Limits

Do not promote every historical chemical detail. The reusable claim is about
mediated symbolic validity, not about using Weyl as a current chemistry source.
