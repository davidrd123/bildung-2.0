## Definition

The thread names a narrow Weylian pattern: theoretical reason can have a real
need for totality without being able to convert totality into an object of
direct intuition. Symbolic construction is the disciplined middle: it represents
what cannot be simply seen.

This is not a general celebration of symbols. The thread is valid only where
symbolic mediation is tied to constraint, construction, consistency, or some
other discipline that prevents the symbol from becoming mystical possession or
empty manipulation.

After the full source-local campaign, the thread is no longer chapter-II-only.
It remains open because cross-source reuse can still weaken it, but it is now
promoted as a Weyl pattern.

## Grounding In Chapter II

`weyl:math-02:8` pressures the thread negatively. Set theory satisfies the
totality need by treating possible sequences, subsets, and properties as
already completed objects. Weyl grants the power of the move but marks its
ontological cost.

`weyl:math-02:9` gives the intuitionist correction. Brouwer refuses completed
infinite totalities and returns mathematical truth to construction, choice
sequence, and the continuum as becoming.

`weyl:math-02:10` gives the formalist correction to the correction. Hilbertian
formalism admits transfinite moves as rules over formal signs, but only under
the demand of consistency. This does not restore direct truth; it licenses a
symbolic game.

`weyl:math-02:11` states the pattern explicitly. Set-theoretic foundation is
naive realism because it misses the passage from the given to the transcendent.
Brouwer is idealism because he restricts truth to the intuitively given.
Axiomatic formalism tries to leave the material of the given behind and present
the transcendent, but only in symbol.

## Current Use

Use this thread when a source forces the question: how can thought legitimately
work with a whole it cannot directly possess?

Good candidates for future testing:

- Cassirer on symbolic forms and scientific objectivity
- Peirce on sign, object, and interpretant
- Hoffmeyer on semiotic causality and code-duality
- Lotman on translation between non-identical semiotic systems
- mathematical structuralism or category-theoretic objecthood

These are candidates only. They do not become evidence for the thread until a
source-local encounter makes the relation specific.

## Whole-Book Extension

Chapter III extends the thread from totality to objectivity by invariance.
Natural science extends it to measurement, concept-formation, and
whole-theory correction. The appendices extend it to mediated diagrams,
quantum probability, heredity, inward understanding, and evolution.

The stronger form is:

> Symbolic access is legitimate only when disciplined by construction,
> invariance, measurement, theory-level correction, morphology, or another
> source-specific constraint.

## Rival Reading

The rival reading is that this thread over-philosophizes a technical episode in
foundations of mathematics. On that view, set theory, intuitionism, and
formalism are historical positions with local technical stakes; "symbolic
access to totality" is too broad and risks absorbing the precision that made the
chapter useful.

Keep that objection active. The thread stays tentative until it survives reuse
without becoming a loose metaphor.

## Weakening Evidence

The thread weakens if:

- cross-project uses fail to preserve the distinction among objectification,
  intuition, and disciplined formal symbol
- the thread starts explaining too much too easily
- the word `symbolic` starts replacing the specific constraints Weyl requires:
  invariance, measurement, morphology, and theory-level correction
