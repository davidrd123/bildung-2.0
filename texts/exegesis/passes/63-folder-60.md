# Pass 63: Folder 60 (Early 1981)

Folder 60 is a small but revealing early-1981 bridge. Dick uses it to do two things at once: sketch a purified origin-story for Christianity around sacrament, anamnesis, and archaic vision, and then reread `VALIS` not as raw testimony but as the artistic completion of 2-3-74. The result is one of the clearest statements that the novel mattered more than the vision because it turned revelation into durable form.

Folder 60 contains 7 indexed entries. Two of them, [60:A-9] and [60:A-44], contribute no recoverable substantive text in the abridged ebook.

---

## [60:A-1] - The Christianity That Should Have Been

**Claim:** Dick imagines a radically different Christian origin: Qumran, Orphic and Pythagorean sacrament, mushroom bread and broth, and a flash of light that restores pre-fallen perception of archetypal ideas. This is not antiquarian ornament. It is Dick's way of redesigning Christianity around anamnesis, meta-abstraction, and direct vision rather than around attenuated orthodoxy. The imagined "Zadokite scrolls" are therefore a fictional vehicle for what the Exegesis itself has been claiming all along.

**Threads:** `fiction-as-prophecy` `thomas-anamnesis` `2-3-74`

**Drift:** Folder 54 had just argued that `VALIS` and Gnosticism succeeded where self-generated speculation failed. Folder 60 turns that same pressure backward and imagines the sacred archive that would have encoded the real thing from the start.

**Echoes:** Back to [54:N-20] (Gnosticism as living epistemology), [53:C-14-D-12] (anamnesis, pulley, and vertical rescue), and [57:Q-14] where the fish-sign and light are again treated as the operative sacramental trigger.

**Sources:** Zadokite scrolls, sacred mushroom, Orphic rites, pre-fallen man, phosphene activity, archetypal ideas, meta-abstraction.

## [60:A-15], [60:A-35], and [60:A-37] - `VALIS` as the Artistic Completion of 2-3-74

**Claim:** Dick says something extreme and important here: `VALIS` is more important than 2-3-74 because it converts the vision into art. The saving figure is not hidden in the novel's theology but in Horselover Fat himself, the fool who sees Christ. Art transfigures Dick's own doubt into pistis. That is why the novel survives as part of the macromind even if Dick the man remains skeptical. The late fiction thread reaches a new form here: revelation is not finished when it happens; it is finished when it becomes a work.

**Threads:** `fiction-as-prophecy` `2-3-74` `thomas-anamnesis`

**Drift:** Folder 81 had made creation itself grace. Folder 60 sharpens that claim by subordinating the original event to its artistic rendering and by openly identifying Fat, Jack Isidore, and Thomas as the novel's real Christ-bearing figure.

**Echoes:** Back to [81:K-230-258] (creation as completion), [54:N-15] (`VALIS` as complete success), and [73:29-32] (`Owl` as critique of salvation-through-gnosis), forward to [84:8] where Dick will treat the paradoxical structure of `VALIS` itself as a waking device.

**Sources:** H. Fat, Jack Isidore, Thomas, fool sees Christ, pistis, art more important than vision.

## [60:A-34] - God in Doubt, Music, Cat, and Grief

**Claim:** Dick condenses several late intuitions here. God is everywhere because every atom yields infinity. Doubt is not the opposite of the road to God but one form of it. And grief is disclosed as something even higher than agape, a fourth revelation beyond power, wisdom, and love. This is a small entry, but it shows the late archive trying to move from cosmic system toward immediate sacramental recognition.

**Threads:** `logos-holy-spirit` `the-dialectic`

**Drift:** Folder 75 will extend this into a broader doctrine of world as lens or transduction of God. Folder 60 states the compact experiential premise first.

**Echoes:** Back to [75:D-129] (God recovered through cat, music, and lost particulars), [55:D-110-115] (agape within passion), and [79:I-95-110] (beauty as the irreducible remainder).

**Sources:** doubt, infinity, music, cat, grief beyond agape, sacramental world.

## Folder 60 - Summary Observations

**What changes in Early 1981:** Dick turns from explaining the vision to preserving it in artistic and sacramental form.

**What crystallizes here:** a reimagined archaic Christianity of anamnetic vision, `VALIS` as the artistic completion of 2-3-74, and the fool as the true Christ-bearer.

**Method note:** Folder 60 is a strong late statement that art is not secondary packaging. It is the second half of revelation.
