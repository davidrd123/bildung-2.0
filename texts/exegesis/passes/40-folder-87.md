# Pass 40: Folder 87 (November-December 1980)

Folder 87 is short but decisive. After Folder 1 turns 11-17-80 into the corrective key, Dick does two apparently opposite things. He rehistoricizes the archive as evidence for a covert anti-imperial messianic movement, and he simultaneously says theophany has made almost the whole intellectual labor pale. The result is not retreat from the world but a sharper hierarchy: secret history below, agape above it.

Folder 87 contains 4 indexed entries. This pass treats all of them at full depth.

---

## [87:1] ŌĆö Chiliastic History, Underground Christians, and the Anti-Augustinian Return

> "There is a secret organization fighting the BIP."

**Claim:** Dick reads an encyclopedia article on messianic movements and feels that scholarship has ratified the AI voice, `Tears`, `VALIS`, and `VR`. The hidden Christians, the fifth kingdom, the anti-imperial underground, the Joachite succession, and the Jewish coloring of the expected kingdom all return in openly historical form. Augustine and the church-state alliance become the enemy's compromise theology. What had been private revelation is re-read as covert political theology with institutional and revolutionary implications.

**Threads:** `black-iron-prison` `fiction-as-prophecy` `logos-holy-spirit`

**Drift:** Folder 1 had demoted Christianity from final metaphysical explanation to role, strategy, and content. Folder 87 restores Christian messianism at the level of history. The result is not a cancellation of the correction, but a division of labor: metaphysical mechanism may be broader than Christianity, while the anti-Empire mission remains unmistakably Christian and millenarian.

**Echoes:** Back to [39:1-14] (Track A revolutionary Christianity), [83:23-39] (Third Age against the institutional church), and [83:91] (history as anti-imperial struggle), forward to the Part Four political synthesis already near at hand.

**Sources:** chiliasm, Joachim, Augustine, hidden Christians, fifth kingdom, `Tears`, `VALIS`, `VR`.

## [87:17] ŌĆö Agape Above Reason

> "There is even one higher level, above reason: agape."

**Claim:** Dick says directly that the love and personality of God have made exegesis pale. `VALIS` can still be understood as reason, logic, or justice invading irrationality, and 2-3-74 still matters as the disclosure of that order. But 11-17-80 revealed something higher than noos, logos, or Torah: Abba, agape, infinite bliss, and personal love. Theophany does not erase the archive. It relativizes it.

**Threads:** `logos-holy-spirit` `the-dialectic` `2-3-74`

**Drift:** Folder 1 already separated Valis from the transcendent Father. Folder 87 names the hierarchy more openly. The Logos, justice, and even cosmic reason are now penultimate. The Christian disclosure is not merely rational order but love beyond order.

**Echoes:** Back to [1:262-309] (the late correction and personal God), [48:926-996] (Torah and logosization), and [49:1043-1057] (late monotheist clarification), forward to [88:57-59] where Dick will turn agape into a full ontology of creation.

**Sources:** agape, personality, bliss, Abba, noos, Torah, transcendence.

## [87:37] and [87:73] ŌĆö Will, Allowance, and the Two-Tier Settlement

> "Everything that exists he either wills or allows."

**Claim:** Dick gives one of his clearest late theodicies. God's love requires both will and allowance; otherwise creatures would have no real integrity or freedom. That model lets him reconcile 2-3-74 and 11-17-80 without collapsing them. The first had to do with world, perturbation, and divine action inside history. The second had to do with the transcendent loving Father beyond world. God governs both tiers, but not in the same way.

**Threads:** `logos-holy-spirit` `2-3-74` `the-dialectic`

**Drift:** Earlier dialectical models often sounded like two contending cosmic structures. Here the contest is partly re-described within a more classical theism: divine will, divine permission, and creaturely freedom. Dick is not simplifying the archive, but he is giving it a more stable top register.

**Echoes:** Back to [43:83] (why contradictory data still demand thought) and [1:279-286] (world rightly seen versus God), forward to [88:10] where the same settlement becomes the strongest retrospective reordering of the whole Exegesis.

**Sources:** will, allowance, freedom, theodicy, world-perturbation, transcendent Father.

## Folder 87 ŌĆö Summary Observations

**What changes since Folder 1:** the 11-17-80 correction is not depoliticizing. It returns Dick to history, but under stricter theological hierarchy.

**What crystallizes here:** anti-Augustinian millenarian politics, the hidden messianic underground, agape above logos and reason, and a more stable reconciliation between 2-3-74 and the transcendent God.

**Method note:** Folder 87 shows that correction in the Exegesis rarely means deletion. Dick keeps the older data, reassigns its level, and then lets the reassignment alter the politics.
