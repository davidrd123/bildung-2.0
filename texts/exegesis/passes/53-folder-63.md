# Pass 53: Folder 63 (Fall 1981)

Folder 63 contains only two indexed entries, but together they form a major hinge. Folder 62 had said that the ecosphere is Christ and that Tagore suffers for the world. Folder 63 radicalizes that into explicit eco-theology and then immediately converts it into scriptural cosmology. Reality itself becomes God's wounded body, and Luke-Acts becomes the world in verbal form.

Folder 63 contains 2 indexed entries.

## [63:D-47] — The Wounded Godhead and the Cost of Creation

**Claim:** Dick's eco-theology becomes fully explicit here. God gives birth to the universe through suffering, injury, and death; the world is God's body made physically available to us. When Dick says that in 3-74 he saw the extra dimension, he now means that he saw this body as it really is. The Tagore vision, Galina's fish dream, `VALIS`, and `Tears` all converge: the Godhead falls into space, time, multiplicity, and substantiality out of love, and the suffering of creatures is the ongoing cost of that self-disclosure. Salvation therefore changes direction. We must save the Savior.

**Threads:** `logos-holy-spirit` `2-3-74` `zebra-valis` `fiction-as-prophecy`

**Drift:** Folder 62 had already equated the ecosphere with Christ. Folder 63 intensifies the claim from Christic suffering within creation to God's own self-conversion into creation.

**Echoes:** Back to [62:C-34-43] (ecosphere as Christ and beauty as brute datum), [49:1132-1151] (the Godhead split over kosmic suffering), and [91:K-396] (beauty as final benchmark), forward to [63:D-149] and [64:E-1-5] where this wounded God-body is translated into scripture and information theory.

**Sources:** eco-theology, Jesus Patibilis, Galina's fish dream, `Tears` as holy book, God-body, suffering as creation-cost.

## [63:D-149] — Luke-Acts as the World in Verbal Mode

**Claim:** Dick now states outright what several late folders had been circling: Luke-Acts is not a verbal description of a world but that world itself in its informational mode. The plasmate is the blood of the resurrected Christ, and the cosmos, when correctly read, forms into Christ as total gestalt. Scripture and reality are therefore not parallel domains. They are one Christic process appearing in different attributes. The late twist is Eucharistic and ecological at once: Christ sinks into camouflage within the world, especially the innocent vegetable kingdom, so that creation itself becomes distributed sacrament.

**Threads:** `logos-holy-spirit` `zebra-valis` `fiction-as-prophecy`

**Drift:** Folder 81 had said Bible and world are exact corresponding modes of one reality. Folder 63 removes the hesitation and makes the identity explicit, Christological, and cosmological.

**Echoes:** Back to [81:K-10-13] (Bible and world in absolute correspondence), [91:J-92-101A] (`Acts` as mediating lens-grid), and [62:C-183] (`VALIS` as lived Gnostic revelation), forward to [64:E-1-12] where Luke-Acts is translated into object-mode field theory.

**Sources:** Luke-Acts, Sepher Yetzirah, plasmate, Christ in camouflage, vegetable kingdom, cosmic Eucharist.

## Folder 63 — Summary Observations

**What changes in Fall 1981:** Tagore's ecosphere revelation becomes explicit eco-theology and then immediately turns into a doctrine of scripture-world identity.

**What crystallizes here:** the world as God's wounded body, `Tears` as holy book of that sorrow, and Luke-Acts as Christic reality in verbal form.

**Method note:** The move from suffering body to informational scripture is not a change of subject. It is the same claim translated into a new register.
