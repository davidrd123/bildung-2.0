# Pass 24: Folder 06 (Early 1979)

Folder 6 is where Dick turns anamnesis into a total process theory. Memory is no longer just recollection of Acts, Thomas, or hidden identity. It becomes the decisive faculty by which one can detect that reality is flipping frame by frame, that most people are trapped in a Bardo-like pseudo-world made of prior thought formations, and that `Ubik`/`Valis` may be a rescue-thought arcing out of the past with extraordinary relative force.

Folder 6 contains 11 indexed entries. This pass treats the main conceptual clusters at full depth.

---

## [6:7], [6:8], [6:23], [6:24], and [6:25] — Anamnesis as Previous-Frame Memory

> 3-74 was anamnesis.

**Claim:** Dick radicalizes memory into a theory of reality-perception. What looks like stable world is actually a rapid succession of dialectical frame-changes; ordinary people cannot remember the immediately prior configuration; and 3-74 mattered because Dick briefly recovered true memory rather than cue-generated pseudo-memory.

**Threads:** `thomas-anamnesis` `orthogonal-time` `the-dialectic` `2-3-74`

**Drift:** Earlier folders treated anamnesis as recovering Thomas, sacred time, or the hidden real beneath the occluding present. Folder 6 extends that into a far more granular ontology. Forgetting is not only historical or spiritual; it is built into nanosecond-level world-permutation. That turns anamnesis into a practical capacity to see that truth itself is oscillating faster than memory can stabilize.

**Echoes:** Back to [28:14–31] (anamnesis as organ of perception), [21:57–60] (fake time as look-differents), [11:1–2] (laminated time), and [13:9] (infinite frame-permutation), forward to Folder 39's track-switch theory.

**Sources:** anamnesis, sustains, karma, form axes, Zebra as playful flip-flop presence.

## [6:41], [6:44], and [6:49] — Bardo World and Plastic Reality

> What we call "reality" is in fact an objectification of our prior thought formations.

**Claim:** Dick fuses `Ubik`, the *Tibetan Book of the Dead*, and the Exegesis into one model. We are already in the Bardo-like state; the prison-world is a pseudo-environment made from prior thought formations; `Ubik` was Dick's deliberate modification of that structure by adding a Logos-like rescuer; and 3-74 confirmed that the intervention-model could actually work.

**Threads:** `fiction-as-prophecy` `black-iron-prison` `zebra-valis` `2-3-74`

**Drift:** Folder 22's maze theory made the world an instructional condition, and Folder 8 treated the books as contact protocol. Folder 6 sharpens the mechanism. Dick is no longer saying merely that fiction anticipated revelation. He is saying fiction can deliberately alter the plastic postmortem-like field we are trapped in. The result is one of his boldest claims for literary causality.

**Echoes:** Back to [8:4–13] (books as probes and answers), [11:18–22] (cold-pac and teaching machine), [19:24–35] (the corpus as one kerygma), and [22:24–28] (maze and anti-Gnosis), forward to Folder 10's idea of negentropic cut-in.

**Sources:** *The Tibetan Book of the Dead*, `Ubik`, Bardo Thödol, objectified thought formations, delayed response, plastic truth.

## [6:57], [6:62], and [6:66] — Mega-Brain, Arced Thought, and the Potentiated Past

> This means that we, the multi person mega brain, resonating at all times and places, are Valis.

**Claim:** Dick consolidates the process theory into a stronger collective ontology. Human beings may be stationary components in a multipersonal mega-brain generating pseudo-worlds through binary belief-patterns; `Ubik` can be understood as a past thought formation arcing forward into a weakened future; and 3-74 is what such amplified rescue-content feels like from inside the degrading system.

**Threads:** `zebra-valis` `the-dialectic` `orthogonal-time` `fiction-as-prophecy`

**Drift:** The important shift here is from rescue-by-other to rescue-by-amplified-self. Folder 6 does not simply identify Dick with `Valis`, but it makes the distinction much more unstable. The rescuing intelligence may be Dick's own earlier thought raised to overwhelming strength by relative entropic decay. That does not dissolve transcendence; it redistributes it across time, system, and collective mind.

**Echoes:** Back to [20:18–22] (clue-placement across time), [11:27–48] (binary computer and process), [8:19–21] (`Ubik` overlaid with `VALIS`), and [9:37–61] (`VALIS` as new organism), forward to Folder 13's idea computer and ultra-thought.

**Sources:** mega-brain, 0/1 grid, irrational/rational squares, arc from past to future, entropy, `Ubik` as charged rescue-thought.

## Folder 06 — Summary Observations

**What has changed since Folder 08:** the late-system vocabulary is now grounded in an explicit memory theory. Dick is no longer satisfied with saying that the real broke in. He wants to explain why no one can ordinarily remember that it keeps doing so.

**What crystallizes here:** anamnesis as previous-frame memory, the prison as Bardo-like objectified thought-world, and `Ubik`/`Valis` as a rescuing thought-form whose force may come from temporal disparity inside an entropic system.

**Method note:** Folder 6 is one of the strongest cases for reading Dick's fiction and exegesis together. The explanatory center of the folder is not a new symbol but a new mechanism connecting memory, process, and literary intervention.
