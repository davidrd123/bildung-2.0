# Pass 04: Folder 25 (January or February 1977)

Folder 25 is where the mid-period system starts speaking in its own proper names. The mimicking entity is now Zebra. The world is explicitly dualized into blind ananke and wise arranging intervention. Messages, signals, and causal chains become one operational theory, and Dick's fiction is reread not just as anticipation but as partial prior contact with the same reality.

Folder 25 contains 11 indexed entries. This pass gives full-depth treatment to the major conceptual clusters and light annotation to the methodological coda.

---

## [25:1] — Zebra Gets Its Name

> Although it is evident that the information emanated from Zebra (which is my nickname for the mimicking entity)...

**Claim:** Dick gives the mimicking entity its durable mid-period name and imagines humans as cells, carriers, or functions inside its concealed body. Zebra is not identical with the universe but fashions itself within it, threading its signals and causal adjustments through a larger hostile or indifferent order.

**Threads:** `zebra-valis` `the-dialectic` `black-iron-prison` `logos-holy-spirit`

**Drift:** Folder 24 still oscillated between Brahmanic monism and the shy hidden God. Here Dick decisively limits the monism. Zebra is a golden thread in dross, not all things equally. That sharpens the dualism and gives him a way to explain evil without making the salvific intelligence responsible for the whole world. Naming the entity also matters methodologically: once it has a name, it can accumulate functions, messages, and a body.

**Echoes:** Back to [24:83] and [24:85–87] (mimicry and concealed intervention), [24:47] (the reality of the prison-world), forward to [25:11] (messages as programming) and [25:16] (Zebra inserting itself between us and determinism).

**Sources:** Steganography, Plato's *Timaeus*, Nixon as example of limited providential intervention.

## [25:3–4] — Arrangement, Corpus Christi, and the Growing Brain

> Zebra = Plato's noös = Holy Wisdom = Christ

**Claim:** Zebra's essential activity is arrangement. Dead or inert things can be seen entering a living process, fitted into a growing corpus that is at once Holy Wisdom, Corpus Christi, noösphere, and composite brain. Dick interprets 3-74 as his own temporary incorporation into that structure.

**Threads:** `zebra-valis` `logos-holy-spirit` `the-dialectic` `2-3-74`

**Drift:** The mimicry model now becomes constructive rather than merely covert. Zebra is not only hiding in the world; it is arranging the world into kosmos out of anomie. This is one of Dick's strongest syntheses of Christian, Platonic, and Teilhardian vocabularies. The pressure of providence becomes legible as the growth of a sentient structure. The insight that "all it can do is arrange things" becomes a virtue rather than a limitation.

**Echoes:** Back to [24:42] (reweaving of persons and events), [24:65–66] (the hidden absolute landscape), and [24:87] (the shy God close by), forward to [25:9] (the builder identified with the edifice it builds) and later VALIS/brain imagery.

**Sources:** Plato's noös, Colossians 1, Teilhard de Chardin, Augustine, Fibonacci geometry.

## [25:7] and [25:9] — Creator, Recreator, and the Secret Growth of Kosmos

> The one to turn to is the Savior who arranges and not the Creator.

**Claim:** Dick accepts experienced duality as his starting point: creator and recreator may be separate beings, aspects of one being, or two poles bound by love, but salvation lies with the wisdom that enters chaos and slowly builds krasis within it.

**Threads:** `the-dialectic` `logos-holy-spirit` `zebra-valis`

**Drift:** This is a more disciplined dualism than the earlier prison-world language. The creator is not simply demonized; he may be blind rather than evil. The focus shifts from denunciation to allegiance: whatever the ultimate metaphysics, the power worth trusting is the arranger, the stranger God, the builder of coherence. [25:9] pushes this toward a self-completing deity that stands outside linear time and retroactively creates itself.

**Echoes:** Back to [23:111–112] (strife and love), [24:1] (Brahman/Vishnu/Christ translation), and [25:1] (opposing subject), forward to later VALIS theology and the Tractates' paired-principle formulations.

**Sources:** Vishnu and Shiva, Joseph Campbell on Egyptian divine partnership, Empedocles, Teilhard.

## [25:11], [25:16], and [25:19] — Signals, Grace, and Mimicked Causality

> Zebra uses these messages to communicate with all of us.

**Claim:** Dick gives his most operational account yet of providence. Zebra programs and guides human beings through embedded signals, then breaks deterministic chains by inserting its own living body between us and the blind mechanism of the prison-world. Even apparent causality may be a salvific simulation.

**Threads:** `zebra-valis` `black-iron-prison` `logos-holy-spirit` `fiction-as-prophecy`

**Drift:** Earlier folders treated the gold fish sign, dream-books, and Runciter-style messages as mysterious clues. Here they become components of a general model. Zebra does not necessarily smash causality; it can imitate it so well that grace appears as ordinary sequence. This preserves both intervention and camouflage. Theologically, this is strong providence without overt miracle. Existentially, it means freedom arrives as deprogramming from astral determinism.

**Echoes:** Back to [4:106] (the fish sign as catalytic release), [24:85–87] (occupation by the mimicking entity), [25:1] (messages within the body), and forward to the later VALIS signal-system.

**Sources:** *Ubik* and Runciter's messages, astral determinism, divine providence, the fish sign.

## [25:21] and [25:24] — Ubik, Tears, and Backdated Revelation

> I am staggered at how close I came to Zebra.

**Claim:** Dick rereads his fiction and concludes that revelation clarified rather than created his central insights. *Ubik*, *Flow My Tears*, *Stigmata*, and related novels had already mapped the prison-world, contending powers, hidden messages, and partial saving intrusion long before he could name Zebra.

**Threads:** `fiction-as-prophecy` `zebra-valis` `black-iron-prison` `the-dialectic`

**Drift:** Folder 24 had already called 3-74 the apotheosis of his lifelong themes. Folder 25 goes further by naming which books got closest and in what way. The rereading is no longer vague. *Ubik* is singled out as especially near Zebra; *Flow My Tears* clarifies prison and slavery; *Stigmata* stages the conflict of the two powers. Dick is not claiming literary genius alone but partial unconscious contact with the same structure he later experienced directly.

**Echoes:** Back to [24:15] (the two themes verified), [4:41] (life behaving like the novels), and [4:135] (his fiction as a priori map), forward to [28:33] and the late novels that will feed back into the Exegesis again.

**Sources:** *Ubik*, *Flow My Tears, the Policeman Said*, *The Three Stigmata of Palmer Eldritch*, *Galactic Pot-Healer*.

## Folder 25 — Summary Observations

**What has changed since Folder 24:** the mimicking entity is no longer just described but named, localized, and given operational roles. Dick's cosmology becomes more dualistic, more procedural, and more confident about how signals, providence, and fiction fit together.

**What crystallizes here:** Zebra as a durable term, arrangement as the essence of divine action, grace as disguised intervention in causality, and Dick's novels as increasingly specific precognitive maps of the same metaphysical terrain.

**Method note:** Folder 25 is dense enough that grouped full-depth sections work better than atomized annotation. The entries are short, but many of them only make sense as one continuous system-build.
