## [78:H-1] — `Bishop Archer` as Californian Commedia

**Claim:** Dick conceives `Bishop Archer` as a Bay Area spiritual quest disguised as secular intellectual life. Tim Archer begins inside a Heidegger-Sufi-Commedia framework, then wrecks himself by rushing from one scheme to the next, from Barefoot's tolerant mysticism to Zadokite and Anokhi extremity. The point is not to preach theology directly, but to stage the modern search for the immediately present Christ through a sequence of cultural and doctrinal masks.

**Threads:** `fiction-as-prophecy` `orthogonal-time` `logos-holy-spirit`

**Drift:** Folder 77 had reclassified the late trilogy as art. Folder 78 shows what that means in practice. Dick is not just revising doctrine. He is designing a novel-form capable of carrying the search without naming it too early.

**Echoes:** Back to [77:G-8-11] (the corpus as one artistic vision), forward to [79:I-36-81] where `Bishop Archer` becomes the actual capstone of the trilogy, and to [80:I-115-124] where Dick formalizes the hidden Commedia structure and vertical ascent.

**Sources:** Heidegger, Sufism, Binswanger, Dead Sea scrolls, Anokhi mushroom, Barefoot as rational mystic counterpoint.
