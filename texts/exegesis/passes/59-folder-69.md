# Pass 59: Folder 69 (December 1981)

Folder 69 is only one entry, but it records a sharp recoil that matters for the late archive. Immediately after turning revelation into political commitment, Dick rejects the guilt-and-duty version of Christianity that had long produced weakness, shame, and servility in him. The entry reads like a counter-pressure against the more ascetic or punitive strands of his own late theology.

Folder 69 contains 1 indexed entry.

## [69:I-8] - Against Hair-Shirt Christianity

**Claim:** Dick bluntly rejects Christianity as internalized abasement. Shame, guilt, permanent obligation, and deference to others are now named as one coercive package. In their place he asserts self-respect, earned worth, and masculine adulthood. This is not a systematic theological proposal, but it is an important late datum because it shows that even after justification and grace language have intensified, Dick still experiences a rival Christianity centered on humiliation and dependence.

**Threads:** `the-dialectic`

**Drift:** Folder 55 had held YHWH and ecstasy together, but the cost was renewed moral pressure. Folder 69 records the backlash. Dick refuses to let Christian seriousness collapse back into hair-shirt self-hatred.

**Echoes:** Back to [81:K-221-225] (justification as completion rather than accusation), [55:D-85] (the moral YHWH encounter), and [55:D-110-115] (ecstasy hidden inside Christian passion), forward to [73:29-32] where the search for salvation through gnosis is mocked from within.

**Sources:** Nietzsche, shame, guilt, self-respect, adulthood, rejection of abasement.

## Folder 69 - Summary Observations

**What changes in December 1981:** the late archive openly resists one of Christianity's most punitive emotional forms.

**What crystallizes here:** Dick can still use Christian categories only if they do not annihilate dignity.

**Method note:** Folder 69 is a good reminder that the Exegesis does not move in one direction. Every apparent consolidation throws off its own corrective.
