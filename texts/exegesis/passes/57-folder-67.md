# Pass 57: Folder 67 (December 1981)

Folder 67 is only one entry long, but it matters because it converts the Tagore/ecosphere revelation into decisive political will. Dick says the old lifelong schism in him has been healed. For the first time he can act against the government without ambivalence because he now experiences political resistance as religious obedience rather than as merely secular dissent.

## [67:12] - The Tagore Vision Heals the Political Schism

**Claim:** Dick now says outright that the Tagore vision solved a problem far older than the Exegesis. It healed the split that had divided his loyalties since the 1950s and gave him wholehearted commitment to anti-government political action. The crucial point is not activism by itself but sanction: he acts because he knows this is what God wants. The old split between religious allegiance and political resistance collapses.

**Threads:** `the-dialectic` `black-iron-prison` `2-3-74`

**Drift:** Folder 62 had already made the ecosphere-Christ revelation political. Folder 67 is where that public horizon becomes inwardly settled decision rather than merely an interpretive possibility.

**Echoes:** Back to [62:C-56-57] (Reagan-Caesar and the ragtag believers), [55:D-70] (eco-theology made public), and [87:1] (the covert anti-imperial struggle), forward to [68:L-10] where Dick identifies the whole anti-nuclear field as the palpable apocalyptic situation of 2-3-74.

**Sources:** Tagore vision, healed schism, anti-government action, religious sanction, divided allegiance.

## Folder 67 - Summary Observations

**What changes in December 1981:** Dick's public theology becomes practical allegiance.

**What crystallizes here:** the Tagore vision no longer means only creaturely holiness or ecosphere-Christ. It becomes the ground for choosing sides.

**Method note:** Folder 67 shows how late Dick's political claims work. They are not added onto revelation from the outside. They are what revelation becomes once it enters ordinary action.
