# Pass 58: Folder 68 (December 1981)

Folder 68 makes the December 1981 turn explicit. What had been visionary, inferential, or typological is now said to be historically palpable. Dick identifies the anti-nuclear field as the visible form of the apocalyptic struggle disclosed in 2-3-74 and treats Reagan-era America as the moment when the masks finally come off.

Folder 68 contains 2 indexed entries.

---

## [68:L-10] and [68:L-12] - Armageddon Made Palpable

**Claim:** Dick now says that the revealed apocalyptic situation of 2-3-74 has become ordinary political visibility. Armageddon is no longer only supernaturally seen; it is directly perceptible in the struggle against the "demonic trinity" of nuclear reactors, nuclear waste, and nuclear weapons. The Christic Institute becomes the concrete organizational form of the Christian revolutionary struggle he had previously seen only in vision. Reagan's accession marks the historical point at which both sides openly declare themselves.

**Threads:** `black-iron-prison` `the-dialectic` `2-3-74`

**Drift:** Folder 67 gave Dick inward resolution. Folder 68 gives him an outward object. Revelation is no longer mainly retrospective decoding; it is the lens by which current history becomes legible.

**Echoes:** Back to [67:12] (healed allegiance and religious sanction), [89:103-119] (`VALIS` as revolutionary apparatus), and [62:C-56-57] (Reagan-Caesar and anti-imperial eco-politics), forward to [54:J-14] where the hidden Christian underground is redescribed as the group we ourselves become when seen outside time.

**Sources:** Armageddon, demonic trinity, Christic Institute, Reagan, masks off, palpable apocalypse.

## Folder 68 - Summary Observations

**What changes in December 1981:** 2-3-74 is converted into present-tense historical discernment.

**What crystallizes here:** the late anti-imperial reading is no longer metaphorical. Empire names a visible political field, and the Christian underground is treated as an actually emerging activist formation.

**Method note:** Folder 68 is one of the clearest places where Dick treats revelation as public diagnosis rather than private metaphysics.
