# Pass 60: Folder 73 (December 1981)

Folder 73 is tiny, but it plays an outsized methodological role. Dick turns `Owl in Daylight` into a parody of the Exegesis itself: a holy-fool anti-Faust story in which the search for gnosis may be futile, the alien breakthrough remains real, and the proper outcome is not esoteric mastery but political action. The folder ends with Dick retracting one of his own over-heated myth-history readings and insisting that the real issue is how reality itself behaves.

Folder 73 contains 3 indexed entries.

---

## [73:29] and [73:32] - `Owl` as Holy-Fool Corrective

**Claim:** Dick uses `Owl` to mock and preserve the Exegesis at the same time. Owl is a holy fool whose superiority complex about hidden signs becomes part of the joke, and the whole Faustian hunt for salvation through gnosis is indicted as restless futility. Yet Dick does not simply cancel the alien or the plasmate. The alien mind still intervenes and still brings Owl to a "fourth period." The correction is instead about outcome: the real resolution is not private illumination but relinquishment of elitist art and movement toward public antiwar resistance.

**Threads:** `fiction-as-prophecy` `the-dialectic`

**Drift:** Folder 55 had already made `Owl` the self-indicting literary version of the exegesis as Chinese finger trap. Folder 73 sharpens that by making the novel itself an anti-Faust parody of gnosis.

**Echoes:** Back to [55:D-84] (`Owl` as anti-model of the exhausted exegesis), [69:I-8] (recoil from punitive seriousness), and [43:83] (the defense of irresolution), forward to [54:L-9] where Dick again says the maze can only be escaped by the paradoxical act of returning.

**Sources:** `Owl in Daylight`, Faust, holy fool, alien mind, fourth period, antiwar action.

---

## [73:54] - Withdrawal from Esoteric Overreading

**Claim:** Dick briefly spins an elaborate mythic reading in which true Christians are a hidden Celtic-Orphic commonwealth beneath imperial false Christianity, then abruptly rejects the whole construction as nonsense. What matters is not one more secret genealogy but the way reality itself behaves. This is a major methodological recoil. Dick is willing to keep the war-language, but he refuses here to let the archive dissolve into arbitrary occult overlays.

**Threads:** `the-dialectic`

**Drift:** The December 1981 political-apocalyptic entries had intensified hidden-history language. Folder 73 tests that language, overheats it, and then cuts it back.

**Echoes:** Back to [68:L-10] (the apocalyptic situation made palpable), [55:3-2] (ethical secrecy rather than decorative esotericism), and [83:138] (the exegesis as memory-work rather than system-building), forward to [54:N-20] where Gnosticism is retained only insofar as it names the primacy of knowing and repair.

**Sources:** Christianity versus Empire, invisible commonwealth, nonsense, reality's behavior, methodological recoil.

---

## Folder 73 - Summary Observations

**What changes in December 1981:** Dick begins actively parodying his own salvation-through-gnosis machinery.

**What crystallizes here:** the holy-fool correction, the futility of purely Faustian search, and the refusal to let mythic overreading replace attention to the behavior of reality itself.

**Method note:** Folder 73 vindicates the atlas method. Dick's late archive is not just system-building but system-testing, including the testing of its own literary and esoteric habits.
