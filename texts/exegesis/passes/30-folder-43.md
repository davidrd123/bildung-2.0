## [43:46] and [43:83] — Irreality, Perturbation, and the Love of the Enigma

> Irreality and perturbation are the two perplexities which confront us.

**Claim:** Dick makes the strongest methodological defense yet of the Exegesis as a philosophical practice. Conflicting realities, memory-historical disagreement, and fleeting traces of another world are not embarrassments to be explained away; they are the exact sort of data that require a radical metaphysical overview. The enigma is simultaneously acosmic, intelligent, and structured, and thinking has to remain supple enough to hold all three.

**Threads:** `2-3-74` `the-dialectic` `orthogonal-time`

**Drift:** Folder 42 had nearly collapsed into self-reproach and psychological reduction. Folder 43 does not deny pathology, but it re-situates it. If reality is undergoing revision, schizophrenia-like casualties are not accidental side effects of bad thinking; they may be the human cost of a world whose data no longer fit together. The Exegesis then becomes not indulgence but one long attempt to keep conceptual courage under those conditions.

**Echoes:** Back to [18:23] (the psychosis hypothesis), [13:4–12] (scramble and irreducible instability), [39:30] (oscillation truth), and [44:12] (progressive dialogue with obliging reality), forward to Folder 44's staged psychosis cross-examination.

**Sources:** schizophrenia, contradictory realities, memory/history mismatch, reality testing, enigma as riddle.

## Folder 43 — Summary Observations

**What has changed since Folder 42:** self-reduction gives way to a principled defense of remaining with the contradiction rather than fleeing it.

**What crystallizes here:** the Exegesis as reality-testing under impossible conditions, the possibility that pathology is structurally induced by revisionary reality, and a methodological willingness to let the enigma remain multiple.
