# Thomas / Anamnesis

Dick's sense that a prior self — "Thomas," a 1st-century Christian — awakened within him during 2-3-74. Anamnesis (Platonic "unforgetting") is the mechanism.

## Core Claim

"I am not PKD; I am Thomas." The Philip K. Dick personality is a "memory-less mask." Thomas is the authentic self, a time-traveler or reincarnation from Rome c. 70 A.D. The Exegesis itself is Thomas's attempt to understand the world he finds himself in.

## Anamnesis

Memory as an organ of perception — not metaphorically but literally. Through anamnesis, Dick perceives the "one organism" across thousands of years. Forgetting (amnesia) is the normal state; remembering is the breakthrough.

## Complications

- The Erasmus episode (letter to Claudia Bush, Feb 1975): Dick identifies the animating spirit as Erasmus, not Thomas
- Dick alternates between taking Thomas literally (time-travel) and metaphorically (Jungian archetype, past life)
- Connection to the bicameral mind theory (Julian Jaynes)
