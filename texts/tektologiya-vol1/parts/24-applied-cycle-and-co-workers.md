# Part 24: Applied Cycle and Co-Workers (front.08 ¶6-9)

This later clean portion of `front.08` resumes after the damaged break and turns from architecture to historical situation. Bogdanov says he has already been using tektological methods in political economy and the development of ideologies without always naming them as such; that the years of worldwide disorganization have changed the timeliness of the project; and that in Russia his long-standing hope for co-workers has at last begun to be realized.

## Why This Batch

- `front.08 ¶6` says Bogdanov's prior economic and ideological work already contained tektological methods and should feed a new application-cycle.
- `front.08 ¶7` argues that the years of great disorganization and great organizational attempts have made scientific organization-questions historically urgent.
- `front.08 ¶8-9` says collaborators have finally appeared in Russia and names some of the live application-fields, ending in a dedication to those co-workers.

### Passage B (front.08 ¶7)

**Russian (lightly normalized from the FB2)**

```text
Если девять лет назад моя попытка оказалась идеологически несвоевременной, то теперь уже дело обстоит совсем иначе. Пережитые годы — годы великой дезорганизации, как и великих организационных попыток, — породили во всем мире острую потребность в научной постановке вопросов организации. Развиваются частичные прикладные науки этого типа — об организации мастерской, об организации предприятия, учреждения вообще, армии… Все сильнее начинают чувствовать недостаточность и шаткую эмпиричность этих попыток, необходимость расширить задачу, хотя не доходят до мирового ее масштаба и универсальных закономерностей…
```

**Draft**

```text
If nine years ago my attempt proved ideologically untimely, matters now stand altogether differently. The years we have lived through, years of great disorganization as well as of great organizational attempts, have engendered throughout the world an acute need for a scientific posing of organizational questions. Partial applied sciences of this type are developing, the organization of the workshop, the organization of the enterprise, the institution in general, the army... More and more strongly people are beginning to feel the inadequacy and shaky empiricism of these attempts, and the need to broaden the task, though they do not yet reach its world-scale and universal regularities.
```

### Passage C (front.08 ¶8-9)

**Russian (lightly normalized from the FB2)**

```text
К счастью, по-видимому, все же не придется ждать, пока европейцы самостоятельно повторят то, что уже сделано. По крайней мере в России я с большой радостью могу констатировать, что моя надежда на присоединение товарищей соработников, наконец, оправдалась. Ряд молодых — и даже не только молодых — ученых определенно пошли по пути тектологического исследования, применяя его методы и наиболее установленные выводы к различным живым вопросам практики и науки: о государственно-хозяйственном плане, о программах и приемах педагогики, об анализе переходных экономических форм, о социально-психологических типах и проч. В литературе пока результаты еще по количеству невелики, но работа идет — работа жизненная, упорная и убежденная.

Приветствуя товарищей по делу, я посвящаю им эту книгу.

19 ноября 1921 года. Москва.
```

**Draft**

```text
Fortunately, it seems, we shall nevertheless not have to wait until Europeans independently repeat what has already been done. At least in Russia I can state with great joy that my hope for the joining of co-workers has at last been fulfilled. A number of young, and not only young, scholars have definitely set out along the path of tektological investigation, applying its methods and its most established conclusions to various living questions of practice and science: the state-economic plan, the programs and methods of pedagogy, the analysis of transitional economic forms, socio-psychological types, and so forth. In literature the results are still small in quantity, but the work is going on, living, stubborn, and convinced work.

Greeting these comrades in the common task, I dedicate this book to them.

November 19, 1921. Moscow.
```

## Notes

- This section makes clear that tektology is not only a general theory waiting for applications. Bogdanov claims he has already been applying its methods across economic and ideological work, even when not naming them explicitly.
- The line about a `new cycle of works` matters because it shows the present book as a base rather than as a finished terminus.
- The historical-timeliness claim is now reversed. What had been ideologically premature is said to have become urgently timely through years of disorganization and attempted reorganization.
- The named application-fields matter for the whole local read. `State-economic plan` and pedagogy are not later accidents; Bogdanov himself presents them here as active tektological fronts.
- The collaborator note is also structurally important. It is the answer to the solitude of `front.06`: by `1921`, Bogdanov says the first cohort of co-workers is real.
- Because of the damaged witness between `front.08 ¶4a` and this later clean portion, this batch resumes after a visible gap rather than pretending continuity through the missing sentence and bibliographic interruption.
