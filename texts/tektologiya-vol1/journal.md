Set up a dedicated `tektologiya-vol1` project rather than forcing Bogdanov into the `zeitmauer` or `erkenntnisproblem` shape.

Reasons:

- the FB2 is already structured enough to extract directly
- the main text is hierarchical rather than flat: volume, chapters, lettered groups, numbered `§` units

- the source is cleaner than raw OCR but still noisy enough that active-tranche checking is preferable to premature whole-book cleanup

Initial structure:

- reproducible FB2 extractor

- calibration batch under `parts/`

## 2026-04-01 - Calibration Batch

Started with `1.1` and `2.a.1`.

Why this pair:

- `1.1` states the broad claim of the whole project: all human activity can be read from the organizational point of view
- `2.a.1` pressures the first genuinely technical vocabulary around organization, disorganization, purposiveness, and `complex`

Open decisions at this stage:

- whether the project-level English should lean toward `Tektology`, `Tektologiya`, or the fuller `Universal Organizational Science`
- whether `комплекс` can safely remain `complex`
- whether `организованность / дезорганизованность` should stay visibly awkward in English
- how much of Bogdanov's terminological repetition should be preserved before the English turns inert

Current bias:

- keep the English plain, systematic, and slightly hard rather than smoothing it into management prose
- preserve repeated technical stems where the argument depends on them
- keep glossary expansion lean until recurrence becomes structural rather than merely noticeable

## 2026-04-01 - Review Note

The residue is now clearer.

In `zeitmauer`, the resistance lies chiefly in terms that do not map cleanly. Here the English often maps well enough. The heavier pressure lies in what the mapped language is asked to do. `Complete unorganizedness is a concept without meaning`, the reversibility of `activity` and `resistance`, and above all the claim that mathematics is a restricted `tektology of neutral complexes` are all philosophically denser than their surface wording suggests.

That means Bogdanov is likely to matter less as a glossary-pressure project than as a project of conceptual calibration: he keeps redefining what counts as a legitimate universal description without announcing a metaphysical register change each time.

## 2026-04-01 - Review Note: Cross-Project Convergence

The wave-interference passage in `2.a.2` sharpens a real convergence with Jünger.

Bogdanov's point there is that the `sacred formula of common sense` - `2 + 2 = 4` - holds strictly only in the neutral case, where organizing and disorganizing tendencies balance. Constructive and destructive interference show instead that combination can yield `1 + 1 = 4` or `1 + 1 = 0`. Simple summation is therefore not the general law of reality but a special limiting case.

That is structurally parallel to Jünger's attack on naive counting in `§41`: mechanical addition misses qualitative transitions because it describes only one regime of relation. The two texts arrive independently, in different registers, at the same critique of naive addition.

This looks like a real future bridge-point for cross-project synthesis, but it does not need immediate architectural treatment here.

## 2026-04-01 - Review Note: Residue Type and the Goethe/Leibniz Split

The difference between `zeitmauer` and Bogdanov is beginning to look methodologically significant.

- In `zeitmauer`, the strongest resistance is lexical and imagistic. The pressure lives in compounds, metaphors, and perceptual vocabulary that resist clean formal equivalence.
- In Bogdanov, the strongest resistance is conceptual and structural. The pressure lives in claims about universal combination, neutrality, and organization that can sound merely technical until their philosophical reach becomes visible.

This maps cleanly onto the existing Goethe/Leibniz diagnostic:

- `zeitmauer` pressures the Goethean side of the method
- Bogdanov pressures the Leibnizian side

The fact that both produce genuine residue, but of opposite kinds, is itself evidence that the split is not a decorative frame but a live working diagnostic for the project.

## 2026-04-01 - Proving Passages in 2B and 2C

The next test held.

`2.b` and `2.c` are not compact leaf units like `2.a.1-2.a.3`, but monolithic blocks. For calibration purposes it made more sense to translate decisive proving passages than to imitate whole-unit completion prematurely.

What `2.b` clarified:

- Bogdanov's method is stricter than a general plea for interdisciplinarity
- the sequence is explicit: relative analysis of complexes, symbolic abstraction, cross-domain comparison, law, deduction, experiment
- mathematics remains the standing model of abstraction, but only as a restricted province, because mathematical thinking itself is treated as an organizational process subordinate to general tektology
- the claim that tektology is the `completion of the cycle of the sciences` is one of the strongest scope-statements so far

What `2.c` clarified:

- the `organizational point of view` is now explicitly a machine both for re-posing any scientific question and for generating questions that no special science can pose within its own boundaries
- the cephalopod-eye case and the extension of Le Chatelier's law are not casual examples; they are Bogdanov's evidence that trans-domain organizational problems are scientifically legitimate
- Bogdanov does not place tektology beside philosophy as a peer discourse; he treats philosophy as a precursor whose valid content must be absorbed, tested, organizationally interpreted, and thereby deprived of philosophical status

Current conclusion:

- Bogdanov continues to yield conceptual residue at the same density
- the project has therefore earned continuation beyond `2.a`
- but the right pace remains selective, because the larger chapter-level units need proving-passage handling before any attempt at continuous whole-unit translation

## 2026-04-01 - Structural Mechanisms in 3A

Chapter 3 begins a new kind of pressure.

Up to `2.c`, Bogdanov's main burden lay in increasingly large claims stated in comparatively plain language. In `3.a.1`, `3.a.3`, and `3.a.4`, he starts building the compact internal mechanism-vocabulary of tektology itself. That changes the translation task slightly: the issue is no longer mainly whether a plain sentence hides a metaphysical claim, but whether the coined mechanism-terms can be carried over without domesticating them into explanatory prose.

What this batch clarified:

- `conjugation` works because it preserves the sheer formal breadth of the primary act of joining
- `ingression` works provisionally because Bogdanov really is naming a general mechanism of linkage through common or intermediary elements, not a one-off image
- `deingression` is ugly, but probably usefully ugly: it marks that disorganization is being treated as a formally corresponding counter-mechanism, not as vague destruction
- `3.a.4` makes boundary and environment newly load-bearing; the same apparent breakup can be reproductive, destructive, or roughly neutral depending on the activities counted and the medium in which the process occurs

Current conclusion:

- the English should stay slightly harder here
- chapter 3 has earned real glossary expansion, because Bogdanov is no longer just reusing common words under pressure but introducing the compact internal grammar of his system
- the next decision is whether to backfill `3.a.2` as the bridge on common links and chain connection, or move to the shorter `3.a.6-3.a.7` now that the main mechanism-terms are in place

## 2026-04-01 - Chain Connection as Bridge

`3.a.2` answered the local structural question directly.

It is not merely a glossary backfill after `conjugation`, nor just a preface to `ingression`. It is the hinge that makes the mechanism-grammar explicit:

- `conjugation` gives the primary act of joining
- `chain connection` gives the form in which conjugated complexes remain linked
- `ingression` gives the operative method for producing such linkage when direct common elements are absent or insufficient

What this section clarified:

- `common` means coincidence, not mere similarity
- homogeneous and contrastive linkages are not different logical species; both organize through common links
- the degree of organization depends on the development of the local `bond`, the aggregate of coinciding elements
- `chain connection` is not only an objective relation but also a form of thought about organized combinations; where common links are not perceptually available, thought constructs them theoretically

Current conclusion:

- `3.a.2` was load-bearing and had to come next
- the first mechanism-grammar of chapter 3 is now in place
- the next clean move is no longer backward clarification but forward continuation

- [visual candidate] symmetry / asymmetry as two forms of chain connection
