## Main Movement

The completed book now reads as a staged enlargement of one crisis rather than as a loose sequence of metaphysical reflections.

### 1. `Fremde Vogel` - aperture and symptom

The book opens by loosening ordinary confidence in the given world. The strange bird is not yet a doctrine; it is an index that something can appear inside the shared field without fitting the inherited frame.

### 2. `Messbare und Schicksalszeit` - the first wedge

The first closed argumentative unit moves from astrology, augury, and the church year into catastrophe, planning, medicine, tragedy, happiness, periodicity, and political uniformity. The point is not astrology as subject matter. The point is that measurable time and fateful time are not two calendars but two orders of reality, and that the dominance of the measurable flattens what it cannot register.

The bounded reread confirmed that this part widens in rings until the issue is no longer occult knowledge but civilizational form.

### 3. `Humane Einteilungen` - diagnosis becomes consequence

The next large movement asks what happens when historical and human categories themselves begin to fail. The title phrase appears inside the argument; the time wall becomes a threshold rather than a decorative title; future things arrive before historically binding names can catch them. The central result here is not merely historical pessimism. It is that the categories by which history understands itself have become dated events inside a larger shift.

### 4. `Siderische Einteilungen` - the scale shift

The book then moves into geological, cosmic, technical, and experimental registers. The wall is no longer only a boundary of historical sense; it becomes the place where earth-history, technique, apparatus, experiment, and transformed human types begin to overlap. This is where `Weltplan`, `Urgrund`, invisible democracy, world-language, and species-level intervention become load-bearing.

Here the crisis is no longer only about how time is divided. It becomes a crisis of world-form: the field of perception, judgment, action, and reception is being recut under conditions that historical and political vocabulary do not master.

### 5. `Urgrund und Person` - late recomposition

The closing unit does not simply abandon the earlier argument for religion or for metaphysical depth. Its actual movement is fourfold:

- withdrawal of the personal gods and persistence of institutions
- church as institution distinguished from the wider metaphysical total situation, with law, faith, and emptiness forming one late diagnostic field
- worker, materialism, mother-earth, and nihilism as transit rather than endpoint
- sacred time, earth-experiment, spirit, the late return of `der Hinzutretende`, cultic abbreviation, and hope as the final recomposition

The second pass confirmed that the close lands as hard-won orientation, not soft consolation.

## Strongest Findings Now Earned

### 1. The book's central problem is a crisis of time that becomes a crisis of world-form

`thread:time-crisis` is the strongest local dossier because it tracks the widening movement accurately. What begins as the measurable/fateful distinction becomes a problem of naming, planning, apparatus, technical mediation, and species-level intervention. The crisis is not exhausted by chronology; it reaches the conditions under which a world is disclosed and organized.

### 2. The time wall is a threshold, not a date

The title phrase now has internal evidence. It names a boundary where historical categories, historical naming, and historical legitimacy begin to fail. The wall is dangerous because it marks contact with what outlines itself before accepted language or settled method can receive it.

### 3. Naming lag is structural, not accidental

The book repeatedly insists that new realities become visible before the historically binding words for them exist. This is not just a stylistic problem. It is one of the book's deepest method claims, and it directly explains why the local glossary needed to keep some terms open rather than force elegant English too early.

### 4. Technique and experiment are not treated as external tools alone

By the later middle of the book, apparatuses, experimentation, display, and advance showing no longer sit outside a previously neutral world. They help organize what can be seen, accepted, and acted upon. This is the strongest local basis for `thread:predecided-world`, though that dossier still needs scale discipline when it is compared beyond Junger.

### 5. `Urgrund` and `Weltplan` are strongest as reopened pressures, not as settled doctrines

The completed run did not settle these terms once and for all. It gave them evidence chains and structural weight, but the late chapters keep reopening them through earth-power, gap, sacred time, transit, and experiment. Their force comes partly from refusing premature closure.

### 6. The book closes in orientation, not in system

The ending prefers hope to fear, but only after withdrawal, emptiness, catastrophe, species-risk, and spiritual mismatch have all been traversed. That makes the coda neither naive optimism nor mere apocalyptic warning.

## What Remains Open

### 1. Some of the deepest terms remain methodologically open

The completed run did not justify closing:

- `An der Zeitmauer`
- `Bruchstelle`
- the full English force of `Urgrund`
- the precise status of `Weltplan`

This is not a failure of completion. It is part of what the book itself teaches about naming under threshold conditions.

### 2. The book must not be simplified into one late doctrine

The completed run still resists several flattenings:

- generic anti-modern complaint
- generic church apologetics
- mythic restorationism
- simple anti-science position
- pure apparatus determinism

Each of these captures a real pressure in the book and then overstates it.

### 3. The local theological and metaphysical vocabulary is not export-ready by default

`Urgrund`, the father/mother reversal, the church passages, the closing spirit-language, and the hope/fear coda are all real and important. But they should not yet be exported as repo-wide claims without bounded local checks, because their force depends heavily on Junger's own late movement.

## What Is Mature Enough For Controlled Comparison

### 1. `thread:time-crisis`

This is now stable enough for controlled comparison because it is anchored in a large evidence chain and has already survived one bounded reread of Part 2 and one of the closing unit.
This does not yet mean every middle stretch has been reread as a closed unit; it means the main dossier is now strong enough to support bounded comparison without pretending the whole book has been second-passed evenly.

### 2. `thread:predecided-world`

This is mature enough only at the level of problem-structure: plan, measure, built form, apparatus, and advance display can pre-cut the field in which later action and judgment occur. It should still be compared with strong scale discipline rather than as if every other author were saying the same thing.

### 3. The naming-crisis line

The claim that realities arrive before the inherited vocabulary that can bind them is one of the strongest exportable problem-structures in the completed run.

### 4. The constitutive-versus-flattening problem of measure

This is mature enough for controlled comparison, especially where later notes might be tempted to treat all measure, planning, and construction as uniformly destructive. The local `Zeitmauer` run clearly distinguishes between real ordering work and later flattening or capture, even when the distinction remains tense.

## What Should Remain Local For Now

- the full wall-crossing and sacred-time doctrine
- the late church and cult passages in their strongest form
- any attempt to turn Junger into a direct theory of `Umwelt`
- any attempt to turn the completed run into a fully general apparatus theory
- any reading that makes the book's late hope equivalent to a solved metaphysics
