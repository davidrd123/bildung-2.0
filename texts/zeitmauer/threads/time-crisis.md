# Time Crisis

Handle: `thread:time-crisis`

The main thread so far in `texts/zeitmauer`: the collision between measurable time and qualitative or fateful time.

## Core Path

- `evidence:term:schicksalszeit:zm:4` grounds the first explicit contrast.
- `zm:9:church-year` crystallizes the difference by moving from astrology into the church year.
- `zm:13:schicksalsuhr` extends that move by naming the horoscope as an uneven clock.
- `zm:14:abdichtung` widens the problem from interpretation to planning, ceremony, and the need to seal life toward the side of fate.
- `zm:25:plan-freedom` pushes the crisis into insurance, plan-accounting, and the immediate language of freedom.
- `zm:28:periodicity` extends the crisis into flattened rhythms, denied sacrifice, and catastrophe.
- `zm:31:so-sein-gleichsein` closes the part where the crisis becomes one of singular form versus uniformity and mass fate.
- `thread:mumford-bridge` parallels the same crisis historically through clock-time and the record.
- `thread:goethe-leibniz-frame` grounds the deeper split between formal measure and participatory seeing.

## Current Claim

`thread:time-crisis` is no longer just a local theme about astrology. It names a broader conflict over what kinds of reality can be measured without being destroyed by measurement.
