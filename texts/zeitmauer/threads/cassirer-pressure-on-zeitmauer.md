## What Cassirer Vol. I actually gives

Cassirer's completed *Erkenntnisproblem* Vol. I tracks how cognition moves from substantial and ontological pictures toward relation, rule, function, and exact science. The decisive breakthrough is that measurement, mathematical construction, and functional relation are *positive conditions of exact science* — they make lawful phenomenal knowledge possible, not merely reduce reality.

That is the level at which Cassirer enters Zeitmauer work: epistemic-historical constitution of objectivity. Not apparatus-world, not infrastructure, not fully symbolic-formal.

## Four operational constraints

### 1. Measure is constitutive before it becomes flattening

Zeitmauer notes cannot let "plan," "measure," "rectangle," and "cadastral cut" sit in a uniformly negative register. Cassirer shows that functional construction and measurement are first the *positive* securing of lawful relation. Any Zeitmauer reading that lets "measurement destroys reality" stand without distinction loses the key tension between exact science itself and the later totalizing or mechanized uses Jünger attacks.

Applies to: `zm:110:world-plan`, `zm:112:right-angle`, `zm:128:invisible-democracy`, `zm:146:world-plan-research`.

### 2. Weltplan needs an anti-reification test

Cassirer's arc repeatedly shows what goes wrong when methodological gains harden into metaphysical guarantees — substances, powers, or hidden contents. That puts direct pressure on every `Weltplan` usage: is it naming a relational ordering-claim, or has it hardened into a quasi-substantial hidden agent?

The test: can the passage's `Weltplan` be restated as a claim about how ordering works, or does it require a hidden planner/substance to function?

Applies to: `term:weltplan`, `zm:110:world-plan`, `zm:146:world-plan-research`.

### 3. Naming crisis is not license for anti-concept drift

Cassirer gives a concrete answer to failed inherited language: not raw immediacy, not mantic or poetic contact, but *new concept-work*. The Leonardo threshold shows that the appeal from words to things does not end in sensation-rule but in a reformed logic and a new conception of concept.

That does not cancel Jünger's naming crisis. It pressures the repo not to read that crisis as if the only alternative to dead vocabulary were pre-conceptual immediacy.

Applies to: `zm:51:namenlos`, the naming-crisis line in `thread:time-crisis`.

### 4. Urgrund cannot function as a free depth-intensifier

Cassirer's completed arc shows that re-securing methodological gains through deep grounds, substances, or powers is the recurrent failure mode of the volume (Bruno, Descartes, Malebranche). That does not cancel Jünger's `Urgrund`, but it means the term has to prove its explanatory necessity each time it appears. It cannot serve as an atmospheric intensifier for depth without earning its ground.

Applies to: `term:urgrund`.

## What Cassirer Vol. I does not reach

- `Schicksalszeit` and the wall-crossing doctrine — no fateful or qualitative time in Vol. I
- The apparatus/registry/uptake line (`zm:148-155`) — nothing about advance social acceptance, state registry, invisible democracy, or technical media as world-cutting. Those remain Jünger/Anders claims.
- `Umwelt` plurality — Vol. I gives historically reconstituted objectivity, not organism-bound many-worlds

## Predecided-world thread correction

The current `thread:predecided-world` dossier says (line 26): "With Cassirer, the naming crisis is not lexical accident but the breakdown of one world-order and the approach of another."

That is partly right but overstated for Vol. I alone. Cassirer's contribution to the predecided-world thread should stay at the epistemic-historical level: cognition is historically constituted through concept, law, and method, so the field of knowing is never neutrally given. That is genuine predecision pressure. But it is not yet the same kind of predecision as Uexküll's organism-bound world-cut or Anders's apparatus-world. The three layers are:

1. Cassirer: epistemic-historical constitution of objectivity
2. Uexküll: organism-bound functional world-cut
3. Anders/Jünger: apparatus- and infrastructure-level preformatting

Treating all three as the same kind of "predecision" would erase the scale-differences the dossier has otherwise been careful to maintain.

## Symbolic anticipation as a middle category

Some Jünger passages — `zm:70:projected-spirit`, `zm:129:night-view`, `zm:130:world-language` — may be strongest when read as *symbolic anticipation* rather than as either mere metaphor or exact cognition. Cassirer gives a precise middle category for this: natural philosophy symbolically anticipates what later exact science secures, but the anticipation is not yet the science.

That does not diminish these passages. It gives them a more precise role: strong diagnostics of epochal change that do not need to be mistaken for already exact knowledge.
