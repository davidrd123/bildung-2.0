## Why this unit

`Meßbare und Schicksalszeit` is the first complete argumentative unit in the project and already has enough local apparatus to support a real second pass:

- the part is closed at `§31`
- the main glossary decisions have already been tested across multiple registers
- the handle layer already records evidence chains and thread traversal
- the journal has already identified the widening spiral of the part and the textile thread

The right second-pass question here is therefore not:

- is each English sentence acceptable?

It is:

- does the English preserve the part's widening movement from astrology into civilizational diagnosis, or does it leave the reader with a loose heap of striking analogies?

## Pressure Clusters

### 1. `§§4-13` - Astrology as the wedge, not the subject

Main risk:

- the opening can be reread as a defense of astrology in the narrow sense instead of as the first wedge by which Jünger opens the distinction between measurable and fateful time

What has to stay visible:

- chess, augury, horoscope, church year, and `Schicksalsuhr` are all test cases for a larger contrast
- `Schicksalszeit` is qualitative and uneven, not merely another chronology
- `der Hinzutretende` is not just an expert or consultant, but the figure who adds assurance, interpretation, and relation to what circulation alone cannot ground

Draft-level watchpoints:

- explanatory language that turns analogy into illustration
- English that makes `der Hinzutretende` sound like a specialist role instead of an added interpretive presence
- wording that makes `Schicksalszeit` sound like private destiny or psychology rather than a mode of time

### 2. `§§14-19` - Plan, seam, and the limits of knowledge

Main risk:

- the middle of the part can flatten into a general critique of planning or a generic science-versus-spirit contrast, when Jünger is actually defining neighboring houses, seam-points, and intermediate chambers

What has to stay visible:

- the plan needs sealing toward the side of fate
- catastrophe exposes the difference between thinking and steering
- `Zwischengemächer` and the seam-image are methodological, not decorative
- `Weltplan` and `Menschenplan` are not simple metaphysical ornaments; they pressure the limits of specialized competence

Draft-level watchpoints:

- smoothing architectural language into abstraction
- phrasing that turns `Denken und Lenken` into a bland cognitive distinction
- sentences where the office/function contrast or knowledge/wisdom contrast loses force

### 3. `§§20-25` - The person under fateful time

Main risk:

- the person-scale center of the part can get dissolved into moral reflection or existential rhetoric, when these sections actually show what `Schicksalszeit` does to character, tragedy, return, happiness, omen, and freedom

What has to stay visible:

- Peter is the strongest person-scale formulation of fate's disposal over the individual
- tragedy weaves `Schicksalszeit`; measurable time knows only accidents
- `Wiederkehr` and `Wiederholung` are not ornamental variants but different temporal orders
- `§24-25` condense the conflict into omen, command, plan, insurance, and the immediate language of freedom

Draft-level watchpoints:

- theological overstatement around `Weltgewissen`
- English that lets the return/repetition distinction collapse into vague recurrence
- language that weakens the cell, seam, and bricked-up-window imagery in `§23`

### 4. `§§26-31` - Revolutionary symptom and political closure

Main risk:

- the close of the part can be read as one more anti-modern complaint or as a residual astrology defense, instead of as the political and anthropological hardening of the whole arc

What has to stay visible:

- astrology returns here as dissatisfaction and revolutionary symptom from below
- `wahr werden` names a historical change in optics, not just later verification
- singularity and elemental inclination culminate in `So-Sein`
- the part closes against leveling, `Gleichsein`, and Leviathan, not in favor of liberal individualism

Draft-level watchpoints:

- smoothing `von unten` into neutral social description
- translating `wahr werden` in a way that loses the historical-perceptual shift
- wording in `§31` that makes the political close sound merely sociological rather than ontological-anthropological

## Term Watchpoints

### `term:schicksalszeit`

The label `fateful time` does not need to be reopened by default. What needs checking is whether the local English keeps:

- unequal hours rather than neutral sequence
- dispositional force over persons and events
- the ability to move across liturgical, tragic, methodological, and political registers

### `term:hinzutretende`

The current rendering is defensible because it keeps addition visible. The reread question is whether any local sentence quietly turns the figure into:

- expert
- priest
- analyst
- interpreter

when the point is the added relation itself.

### `term:da-sein-so-sein`

The current awkwardness is productive. The reread should guard against any drift toward:

- `existence / essence`
- purely sociological identity-language
- political paraphrase that loses the metaphysical pressure

### `term:bruchstelle`

Do not use this pass to force settlement. `Bruchstelle` matters to the broader book, but it is not what this closed part earns by itself.

## Image Watchpoints

- textile language is structural, not decorative: warp, weft, sealing, and insertion all belong to the part's model of time
- architectural language is structural, not decorative: houses, chambers, seams, and walls do real conceptual work
- optical language is structural, not decorative: sharpening sight, inner optics, and synoptic grasp all govern what can count as truth
