# Section 36: Politics and Vacuity (§801-820, pp. 142-145)

Here the political line grows more contemptuous without becoming abstract: collectivity, confession, vocation, and policy all keep exposing the emptiness that asks to be filled from the outside.

---

## §801
> El individuo se declara miembro de una colectividad cualquiera, con el fin de exigir en su nombre lo que le avergüenza reclamar en el propio.

**Close:** The individual declares himself a member of some collectivity with the aim of demanding in its name what he is ashamed to claim in his own.

**Free:** The individual declares himself part of some collectivity in order to demand in its name what he would be ashamed to demand in his own.

`tags: collectivity, shame, demand`

---

## §802
> La prolijidad suele ser el aparato defensivo de las ideas insignificantes.

**Close:** Prolixity is often the defensive apparatus of insignificant ideas.

**Free:** Prolixity is often the defensive machinery of insignificant ideas.

`tags: prolixity, idea, defense`

## §803

**Close:** A stupidity does not cease to be such because someone dies for it.

`tags: stupidity, martyrdom, idea`

## §805
> Para una sociedad que vive entre estadísticas, sospechar que cada unidad es persona única y destino propio resulta perturbador y alarmante.

**Close:** For a society that lives among statistics, to suspect that each unit is a unique person and a proper destiny is perturbing and alarming.

**Free:** For a society that lives among statistics, it is disturbing and alarming to suspect that each unit is a unique person with his own destiny.

`tags: statistics, person, destiny`

## §806
> Los tontos piensan que el único modo de liberar a un individuo de sus embrollos psicológicos es hablándole de ellos.

**Close:** Fools think that the only way to free an individual from his psychological entanglements is by talking to him about them.

**Free:** Fools think the only way to free a person from his psychological tangles is to keep talking to him about them.

`tags: psychology, fool, liberation`

**Free:** Every being lies scattered in fragments across a life, and there is no way for our love to gather all of it back up.

> Nunca hubo felicidad tan libre de amenazas que nos atreviéramos a volverla a vivir.

**Free:** No happiness has ever been so free from threat that we would dare to live it over again.

## §812
> El católico a quien preocupa la suerte de la Iglesia ha dejado de ser católico.

**Close:** The Catholic to whom the fate of the Church causes concern has ceased to be Catholic.

**Free:** The Catholic who worries about the fate of the Church has already ceased to be Catholic.

**Exegesis:**
The line does not reject care for the Church in every sense; it strikes at the shift from belonging to management anxiety.

`tags: Catholic, Church, anxiety`

---

## §813
> El individuo que tenga una vocación auténtica es reaccionario. Cualesquiera que sean las opiniones que abrigue. Es demócrata el que espera que lo exterior le fije metas.

**Close:** The individual who has an authentic vocation is reactionary, whatever opinions he may harbor. Democrat is the one who waits for the exterior to fix his goals.

**Free:** Anyone with an authentic vocation is reactionary, whatever opinions he may hold. A democrat is the one who waits for the outside world to assign him his aims.

**Exegesis:**
Reaction here names interior authority rather than party doctrine. Vocation itself is anti-democratic insofar as it refuses to let exterior process assign ends.

`tags: vocation, reactionary, democracy`

---

## §814
> La "política" es actividad propiamente democrática, porque sólo deja de ser mal necesario para el que piensa que el hombre es producto artificial del hombre. Para el reaccionario la política es actividad subalterna.

**Close:** "Politics" is activity properly democratic, because it ceases to be a necessary evil only for the one who thinks that man is the artificial product of man. For the reactionary politics is subordinate activity.

**Free:** "Politics" is the properly democratic activity because it stops being a necessary evil only for those who think man is man's artificial product. For the reactionary, politics is subordinate work.

`tags: politics, democracy, subordination`

## §818
> El pueblo no se casa sino con ideas prostituidas.

**Close:** The people marry none but prostituted ideas.

**Free:** The people will marry only prostituted ideas.

**Exegesis:**
This is not just contempt for mass opinion. It says public life attaches itself only to ideas already vulgarized, thinned, and made marketable.

`tags: people, idea, prostitution`
