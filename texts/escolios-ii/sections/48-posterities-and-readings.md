# Section 48: Posterities and Readings (§1041-1060, pp. 181-184)

What matters in this stretch is less possession than angle: how the world is lit, inherited, misread, and carried forward.

---

## §1041
> Los benefactores de nuestra inteligencia no son aquellos que enriquecen el inventario intelectual del mundo, sino los que modifican el ángulo de iluminación de las cosas.

**Close:** The benefactors of our intelligence are not those who enrich the intellectual inventory of the world, but those who modify the angle of illumination of things.

**Free:** The real benefactors of our intelligence are not those who add more items to the world's inventory, but those who change the angle from which things are lit.

**Exegesis:**
This is a strong criterion for intellectual greatness in Gómez Dávila: not accumulation, but alteration of light.

`tags: intelligence, illumination, benefactors`

---

## §1042
> Para que una cultura fecunde otra cultura, es preciso que se conozca mal. El saber histórico dificulta la imitación creadora.

**Close:** For one culture to fecundate another culture, it is necessary that it be known badly. Historical knowledge hinders creative imitation.

**Free:** For one culture to fertilize another, it has to be known imperfectly; too much historical exactitude obstructs creative imitation.

**Exegesis:**
The claim is not anti-knowledge in general. It distinguishes between historical exactness and the fertile misprision from which new form can arise.

`tags: culture, imitation, history`

## §1044

**Close:** The history of spirit is not a dialectical process, but dialogue, along the thread of time, among individuals congenitally constrained to unpredictable visions of the world.

**Free:** The history of spirit is not a dialectical process but a dialogue running through time among individuals bound from birth to unpredictable visions of the world.

## §1045
> La ambición de "transformar el mundo" no emborracha sino a las épocas carentes de quienes lo pinten, lo describan, o lo piensen, con talento.

**Close:** The ambition to "transform the world" intoxicates only the epochs lacking those who paint it, describe it, or think it, with talent.

**Free:** The ambition to "transform the world" intoxicates only those ages that lack people capable of painting, describing, or thinking it with talent.

`tags: world, talent, transformation`

## §1046
> La ciudad desaparece, mientras el mundo entero se urbaniza. La ciudad occidental fue persona. Hoy, la hipertrofia urbana y el centralismo estatal la desintegran en mero hacinamiento inánime de viviendas.

**Close:** The city disappears while the entire world urbanizes. The Western city was a person. Today urban hypertrophy and state centralism disintegrate it into mere inanimate crowding of dwellings.

**Free:** The city disappears even as the whole world urbanizes. The Western city was once a person; today urban hypertrophy and state centralism dissolve it into a lifeless heap of dwellings.

**Exegesis:**
The contrast is not city versus countryside, but person versus aggregation. Urban expansion destroys the city precisely by universalizing its shell.

`tags: city, urbanization, state`

**Free:** One belongs either to Hegel's posterity or to Schopenhauer's. There is no third line.

`tags: Hegel, Schopenhauer, posterity`

## §1048
> La irrupción de la historia no-europea en la tradición de Occidente es un episodio de la vida intelectual del xix. Los partícipes de esta tradición no son herederos forzosos de esa historia y sólo pueden heredarla respetando las condiciones intelectuales de su ingreso al patrimonio de Occidente. En otros términos, puede haber sinólogos en Occidente, verbigracia, pero no taoístas.

**Close:** The irruption of non-European history into the tradition of the West is an episode of the intellectual life of the nineteenth century. The participants in this tradition are not forced heirs of that history and can inherit it only by respecting the intellectual conditions of its entry into the patrimony of the West. In other terms, there can be sinologists in the West, for example, but not Taoists.

**Free:** The entry of non-European history into the Western tradition is a nineteenth-century intellectual episode. Western participants are not compelled to inherit that history, and can inherit it only by respecting the intellectual conditions under which it entered the Western inheritance. In other words: the West may have sinologists, but not Taoists.

**Exegesis:**
The line is severe, but the point is formal. Inheritance is never simple acquisition; it remains answerable to the conditions under which a foreign corpus became legible in the receiving tradition.

`tags: West, inheritance, sinology`

## §1051
> Así como la interpretación alegórica, o anagógica, o tropológica, permitía lecturas literarias al cristiano de ayer, así la interpretación económica se las permite hoy al marxista piadoso.

**Close:** Just as allegorical, or anagogical, or tropological interpretation permitted literary readings to yesterday's Christian, so economic interpretation permits them today to the pious Marxist.

**Free:** Just as allegorical, anagogical, and tropological reading once gave the Christian a whole range of literary readings, economic interpretation now does the same for the pious Marxist.

**Exegesis:**
The comparison is methodological rather than devotional. Marxism is treated here as a total hermeneutic capable of generating sanctioned readings everywhere it turns.

`tags: interpretation, marxism, hermeneutics`

**Free:** Hegel, philosopher of the Prussian state? Philosopher of the French Revolution? As if every French Revolution did not end in a Prussian state.

## §1060
> Una frase perfecta puede caer en el olvido, pero escapó a la muerte.

**Close:** A perfect sentence may fall into oblivion, but it escaped death.

**Free:** A perfect sentence may be forgotten, but it has already escaped death.

**Exegesis:**
The survival at stake is not reception but form. Perfection does not guarantee remembrance; it guarantees that the sentence once achieved something imperishable.

`tags: sentence, perfection, memory`
