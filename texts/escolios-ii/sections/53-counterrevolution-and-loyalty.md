# Section 53: Counterrevolution and Loyalty (§1141-1160, pp. 196-200)

The stretch moves from historical theory into a more atmospheric register: night, thirst, loyalty, value, and counterrevolution are all treated as ways of restoring lost dimensionality.

---

## §1141
> Tal es la complejidad de los hechos históricos que toda teoría encuentra casos a que aplicarse.

**Close:** Such is the complexity of historical facts that every theory finds cases to which to apply itself.

**Free:** Historical facts are so complex that every theory can find cases to apply itself to.

`tags: history, theory, complexity`

---

## §1142
> Las naciones tienen dos modalidades nobles de existencia: ascenso o decadencia, y una modalidad vulgar: prosperidad.

**Close:** Nations have two noble modalities of existence, ascent or decadence, and one vulgar modality, prosperity.

**Free:** Nations have two noble modes of existence, ascent and decadence, and one vulgar one: prosperity.

`tags: nations, nobility, prosperity`

---

## §1143
> Las revoluciones no son las locomotoras, sino los descarrilamientos de la historia.

**Close:** Revolutions are not the locomotives of history, but the derailments of history.

**Free:** Revolutions are not history's locomotives but its derailments.

`tags: revolution, history, derailment`

---

## §1144
> Quien nos traiciona nunca nos perdona su traición.

**Close:** He who betrays us never forgives us for his betrayal.

**Free:** Whoever betrays us never forgives us for having been betrayed.

`tags: betrayal, forgiveness, resentment`

---

## §1145
> Volver a escribir nobles páginas no será posible mientras no le devolvamos su otra dimensión al universo.

**Close:** Writing noble pages again will not be possible while we do not restore to the universe its other dimension.

**Free:** It will not be possible to write noble pages again until we restore the universe to its other dimension.

`tags: writing, universe, nobility`

---

## §1146
> La contra-revolución permanente consiste en rasparle permanentemente al misterio el barniz de inteligibilidad con que permanentemente pretenden ocultarlo.

**Close:** Permanent counterrevolution consists in permanently scraping from mystery the varnish of intelligibility with which they permanently pretend to hide it.

**Free:** Permanent counterrevolution consists in ceaselessly scraping from mystery the varnish of intelligibility with which people ceaselessly try to hide it.

**Exegesis:**
Counterrevolution is defined here neither politically nor nostalgically, but as a discipline of de-simplification.

`tags: counterrevolution, mystery, intelligibility`

---

## §1147
> El período histórico no es acomodo historiográfico. Período histórico es el lapso durante el cual se desarrollan las consecuencias de un acto libre.

**Close:** The historical period is not a historiographic arrangement. Historical period is the span during which the consequences of a free act unfold.

**Free:** A historical period is not a historiographical convenience. It is the span during which the consequences of a free act work themselves out.

`tags: period, history, free-act`

---

## §1148
> El alma del objeto es la forma perfecta de sí mismo que el objeto evoca.

**Close:** The soul of the object is the perfect form of itself that the object evokes.

**Free:** The soul of an object is the perfect form of itself that the object calls forth.

`tags: object, soul, form`

## §1149
> Las promesas de la vida no defraudan sino a quien cree que aquí se cumplen.

**Close:** The promises of life disappoint only him who believes that they are fulfilled here.

**Free:** Life's promises disappoint only the man who thinks they are fulfilled here.

`tags: life, promise, disappointment`

## §1150
> Nuestra sed sólo oye aquí el rumor del agua.

**Close:** Our thirst hears here only the murmur of water.

**Free:** Here our thirst hears only the sound of water.

## §1151
> Basta abrir nuestras ventanas a la noche, para que atice las cenizas calcinadas de nuestra alma el hálito de misteriosas primaveras.

**Close:** It is enough to open our windows to the night for the breath of mysterious springs to fan the calcined ashes of our soul.

**Free:** One need only open the windows to the night for the breath of mysterious springs to stir the burnt ashes of the soul.

`tags: night, spring, soul`

## §1152
> La noche vuelve canto los agrios gritos del día.

**Close:** Night turns the day's bitter cries into song.

**Free:** Night turns the harsh cries of the day into song.

`tags: night, song, day`

## §1153
> Hay seres que no ven en cada amanecer sino la coyuntura de una traición.

**Close:** There are beings who see in each dawn nothing but the occasion of a betrayal.

**Free:** Some people see in every dawn only the occasion for a betrayal.

`tags: dawn, betrayal, beings`

## §1154
> La lealtad es la música más noble de la tierra.

**Close:** Loyalty is the noblest music of the earth.

**Free:** Loyalty is the noblest music on earth.

**Exegesis:**
The claim is not ornamental. Loyalty is treated as a formal harmonizing principle, not merely as a moral virtue among others.

`tags: loyalty, music, nobility`

## §1155
> La ideología se separa pronto del interés que la engendra y corre mundo por cuenta propia.

**Close:** Ideology soon separates from the interest that engenders it and goes out into the world on its own account.

**Free:** Ideology soon detaches itself from the interest that generated it and begins to circulate on its own.

`tags: ideology, interest, circulation`

## §1156
> Breves convulsiones bastan para abatir los edificios del espíritu, mientras que nuestra natural vileza ampara los éxitos técnicos.

**Close:** Brief convulsions suffice to bring down the edifices of the spirit, while our natural vileness shelters technical successes.

**Free:** Brief convulsions are enough to bring down the edifices of the spirit, while our native vileness protects technical success.

`tags: spirit, technology, collapse`

## §1157
> El historiador tan sólo relativiza, cuando atribuye al valor erróneamente el condicionamiento de su aparición empírica.

**Close:** The historian relativizes only when he erroneously attributes to value the conditioning of its empirical appearance.

**Free:** The historian relativizes only when he mistakenly attributes to value the conditions of its empirical appearance.

`tags: historian, value, relativism`

---

## §1158
> La colocación de un valor en el sitio de su orto histórico no afecta su validez. Indica, tan sólo, en qué latitudes nació y desde cuáles se observa.

**Close:** The placing of a value in the site of its historical dawn does not affect its validity. It indicates only in what latitudes it was born and from which it is observed.

**Free:** Locating a value at the site of its historical sunrise does not affect its validity. It only shows in what latitudes it was born and from which ones it is seen.

**Exegesis:**
Historical placement is here distinguished sharply from historical reduction. Origin clarifies perspective without dissolving validity.

`tags: value, history, validity`

---

## §1159
> El individualismo doctrinario allana el suelo, para que el colectivismo edifique.

**Close:** Doctrinaire individualism levels the ground so that collectivism may build.

**Free:** Doctrinaire individualism clears the ground for collectivism to build on.

`tags: individualism, collectivism, groundwork`

## §1160
> Historia crítica y pensamiento reaccionario provienen del mismo acto intelectual que descubrió la radical diversidad del hombre.

**Close:** Critical history and reactionary thought come from the same intellectual act that discovered the radical diversity of man.

**Free:** Critical history and reactionary thought arise from the same intellectual act: the discovery of man's radical diversity.

`tags: critical-history, reaction, diversity`
