# Section 33: Values and Colonization (§741-760, pp. 132-135)

This stretch keeps turning modern self-assurance inside out: ideology governs by blinding, metaphysics dies when read as code, and education proves itself by what it cannot survive.

---

**Close:** Man, in order to govern, blindfolds himself with ideologies.

**Free:** To govern, man blindfolds himself with ideologies.

`tags: ideology, government, blindness`

## §742
> La metafísica pierde su validez al tomar sus fórmulas a la letra.
> No pudiendo hablar sino "poetice", la pretensión a la univocidad la corrompe.
> La metafísica no puede pasar de una fórmula a otra fórmula, sino de una intuición a otra intuición.
> Las deducciones, en metafísica, son chuscadas verbales.

**Close:** Metaphysics loses its validity when its formulas are taken literally.
Since it can speak only *poetice*, the pretension to univocity corrupts it.
Metaphysics cannot pass from one formula to another formula, but from one intuition to another intuition.
Deductions, in metaphysics, are verbal clownings.

**Free:** Metaphysics loses validity the moment its formulas are taken literally.
Because it can speak only *poetice*, the ambition to univocity corrupts it.
Metaphysics moves not from formula to formula but from intuition to intuition.
In metaphysics, deductions are verbal stage business.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the clearest statements yet that high discourse is not disqualified by figuration but destroyed by literalism. Metaphysics survives only if its formulas are treated as vehicles of intuition rather than as mechanically deducible propositions.

`tags: metaphysics, poetry, intuition`

## §743
> El dogma de la inspiración verbal entregó los evangelios al adversario.
> El análisis crítico desemboca en una construcción "racionalista", cuando sus innegables conclusiones, en lugar de ser interpretadas por el exégeta católico con categorías religiosas, son obligatoriamente rechazadas para que otros las interpreten con categorías profanas.

**Close:** The dogma of verbal inspiration delivered the Gospels to the adversary.
Critical analysis ends in a "rationalist" construction when its undeniable conclusions, instead of being interpreted by the Catholic exegete with religious categories, are obligatorily rejected so that others may interpret them with profane categories.

**Free:** The dogma of verbal inspiration handed the Gospels over to the adversary.
Critical analysis ends in a "rationalist" construction when its undeniable conclusions are compulsively rejected instead of being interpreted by the Catholic exegete through religious categories, leaving them to be interpreted by others through profane ones.

**Exegesis:**
The argument is not anti-critical. It is anti-defensive literalism: reject critical findings on the wrong grounds, and one cedes the whole interpretive field to profane categories.

`tags: Gospel, exegesis, inspiration`

## §746

**Close:** Values are not citizens of this world, but pilgrims of other skies.

**Free:** Values are not citizens of this world but pilgrims from other heavens.

**Exegesis:**

## §753
> La filosofía, sin ser cumulativa, sólo avanza asumiendo en cada etapa la totalidad de su pasado.

**Close:** Philosophy, without being cumulative, advances only by assuming at each stage the totality of its past.

**Free:** Philosophy is not cumulative, but it advances only by taking up the whole of its past at each stage.

**Exegesis:**
This is a strong anti-novelty claim. Philosophy advances not by discarding its past as superseded, but by re-assuming it whole.

`tags: philosophy, past, advance`

## §756
> El hombre moderno no es tanto víctima de posesión demoníaca como de colonización por ideas bobas.

**Close:** Modern man is not so much the victim of demonic possession as of colonization by stupid ideas.

**Free:** Modern man is less the victim of demonic possession than of colonization by stupid ideas.

**Exegesis:**
The aphorism lowers the supernatural pitch without softening the judgment. The modern mind is not mostly possessed by devils; it is occupied by banality.

## §757
> Sostener que "todas las ideas son respetables" no es más que una inepcia pomposa.
> Sin embargo, no hay opinión que el apoyo de un número suficiente de imbéciles no obligue a aguantar.
> No disfracemos nuestra impotencia en tolerancia.

**Close:** To maintain that "all ideas are respectable" is nothing more than pompous stupidity.
Nevertheless, there is no opinion that the support of a sufficient number of imbeciles does not oblige us to put up with.
Let us not disguise our impotence as tolerance.

**Free:** To claim that "all ideas are respectable" is only pompous nonsense.
Still, there is no opinion that the support of enough imbeciles does not force us to endure.
Let us not dress up impotence as tolerance.

`tags: ideas, tolerance, impotence`

## §758
> La inteligencia no consiste en encontrar soluciones, sino en no perder de vista los problemas.

**Close:** Intelligence does not consist in finding solutions, but in not losing sight of the problems.

**Free:** Intelligence lies not in finding solutions but in never losing sight of the problems.

**Exegesis:**
This line is methodological and local at once. Intelligence is defined by attention under pressure, not by premature closure.

`tags: intelligence, problem, solution`
