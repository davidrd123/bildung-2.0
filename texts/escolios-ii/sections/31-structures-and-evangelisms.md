# Section 31: Structures and Evangelisms (§701-720, pp. 127-129)

Not argument in the abstract here, but pressure: theology scavenged from rubbish, society treated as structure rather than mechanism, and history trusted least where it sounds most coherent.

---

## §701
> El católico progresista recolecta su teología en el basurero de la teología protestante.

**Close:** The progressive Catholic gathers his theology in the garbage dump of Protestant theology.

**Free:** The progressive Catholic scavenges his theology from Protestant theology's rubbish heap.

`tags: Catholicism, Protestantism, progressivism`

---

## §702
> Los valores, como el alma, nacen en el tiempo, pero no le pertenecen.

**Close:** Values, like the soul, are born in time, but they do not belong to it.

**Free:** Values, like the soul, come to birth in time without belonging to time.

`tags: values, soul, time`

---

## §703
> Lejos de parecerse a nuestra relación con proposiciones, nuestra relación con los valores se asemeja a nuestra relación con personas.

**Close:** Far from resembling our relation to propositions, our relation to values resembles our relation to persons.

**Free:** Our relation to values is much less like our relation to propositions than like our relation to persons.

**Exegesis:**
This turns value away from mere statement and toward encounter. Values are not primarily things we verify, but realities before which one stands in a personal mode.

`tags: values, propositions, persons`

---

## §704
> Las sociedades no son ni mecanismos, ni organismos, sino estructuras.
> Podemos, por lo tanto, sostener a priori que el número de formas sociales correctas es inferior al de combinaciones posibles.
> Los programas políticos suelen ser un tejido de atropellos a la gramática social.

**Close:** Societies are neither mechanisms nor organisms, but structures.
We can, therefore, hold a priori that the number of correct social forms is smaller than that of possible combinations.
Political programs are usually a fabric of assaults on social grammar.

**Free:** Societies are neither machines nor organisms, but structures.
We may therefore say in advance that the number of socially right forms is smaller than the number of possible combinations.
Political programs are usually woven out of violences against social grammar.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the volume's strongest anti-constructivist statements. Possibility is not authority; most combinatory freedom in politics is simply violation of an order one does not first understand.

`tags: society, structure, political-program`

## §706
> Las metáforas son metafísicas silvestres.
> Las metafísicas son metáforas de herbario

**Close:** Metaphors are wild metaphysics.
Metaphysicses are herbarium metaphors.

**Free:** Metaphors are wild metaphysics.
Metaphysical systems are metaphors pressed flat in a herbarium.

**Exegesis:**
The aphorism runs both ways. Metaphor already thinks more deeply than rhetoric admits, while system is what happens when living metaphor is dried, classified, and preserved.

`tags: metaphor, metaphysics, system`

---

## §707
> Mientras no les surjan defensores a las cosas que importan, no tenemos por qué temer que las ataquen.

**Close:** So long as defenders do not arise for the things that matter, we have no reason to fear that they will attack them.

**Free:** Until the things that matter acquire defenders, we need not fear attacks on them.

`tags: defense, importance, corruption`

## §715
> Jesucristo no lograría hoy que lo escucharan, predicando como hijo de Dios, sino como hijo de carpintero.

**Close:** Jesus Christ would not succeed today in making them listen to him by preaching as son of God, but as son of a carpenter.

**Free:** Jesus Christ would not get a hearing today by preaching as the Son of God, but as the carpenter's son.

**Exegesis:**
The line measures modern hearing by what it finds socially authentic rather than theologically true. It is less about Christology than about degraded criteria of credibility.

`tags: Christ, authenticity, hearing`

## §718
> Así como en el siglo xix la ética disolvió al protestantismo, así en el siglo xx el catolicismo está siendo disuelto por la sociología.

**Close:** Just as in the nineteenth century ethics dissolved Protestantism, so in the twentieth century Catholicism is being dissolved by sociology.

**Free:** Just as ethics dissolved Protestantism in the nineteenth century, so sociology is dissolving Catholicism in the twentieth.

**Exegesis:**
The analogy is sharp because it names dissolution by translation. A religion is not refuted directly; it is thinned into another discourse that leaves its form standing while hollowing its substance.

`tags: Protestantism, Catholicism, sociology`
