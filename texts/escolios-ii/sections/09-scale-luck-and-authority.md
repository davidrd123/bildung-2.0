# Section 09: Scale, Luck, and Authority (§181–200, pp. 38–41)

These pages keep stripping prestige away from modern certainties.

---

## §181
> Sus obras envanecen al hombre, porque olvida que si lo
> que hace es suyo, no es suyo el tener la capacidad de
> hacerlo.

**Close:** His works make man vain, because he forgets that if what he does is his, it is not his to have the capacity to do it.

**Free:** Our works puff us up because we remember that the product is ours and forget that the power to make it never was.

`tags: works, vanity, capacity`

## §182
> La prosa se corrompe cuando se propone, en lugar de ser
> simplemente inteligible, ser convincente.
> Debemos decir sin esguinces lo que pensamos, pero dejar
> que el lector se convenza solo.

**Close:** Prose is corrupted when it proposes, instead of being simply intelligible, to be convincing. We must say without sprains what we think, but leave the reader to convince himself alone.

**Free:** Prose goes bad when it tries to persuade instead of simply being clear. We should say what we think without contortion and let the reader do his own convincing.

**Exegesis:**
The target is not rhetoric as such, but coercive prose. Once style turns from intelligibility to managed assent, the sentence begins to limp. The oddness of *sin esguinces* matters: prose should not move like something sprained.

`tags: prose, clarity, persuasion`

## §183
> El diálogo pervierte a sus participantes.
> O porfían por pugnacidad, o conceden por desidia.

**Close:** Dialogue perverts its participants. Either they wrangle out of combativeness, or concede out of sloth.

**Free:** Dialogue corrupts its speakers: they either keep fighting from sheer pugnacity or start yielding from fatigue.

`tags: dialogue, pugnacity, sloth`

## §184
> Más de un milenio duró el periodo de la historia europea
> durante el cual la salvación social fue posible. Y varias
> veces conseguida.
> Pero en tiempos democráticos, o cesáreos, tan sólo
> podemos salvar el alma.
> Y eso no siempre.

**Close:** More than a millennium lasted the period of European history during which social salvation was possible. And several times achieved. But in democratic, or Caesarean, times we can only save the soul. And not always.

**Free:** For more than a millennium Europe lived in a period when social salvation was possible, and sometimes actually achieved. In democratic or Caesarean ages, at most the soul can still be saved, and not always.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the starkest historical downgrades in the volume. The loss is not merely political order but the possibility that a whole social form might participate in salvation rather than leaving everything to private remnant-work.

`tags: salvation, Europe, democracy`

**Free:** The man of action misleads the intellectual trying to interpret him precisely because action leaves no time for subtlety.

## §186
> Indignado con el burgués que "tranquiliza su conciencia"
> dando limosna de su propio peculio, el católico de izquierda
> se propone hacerlo abnegadamente repartiendo el peculio
> ajeno.

**Close:** Indignant with the bourgeois who "quiets his conscience" by giving alms from his own purse, the left-wing Catholic proposes to do so abnegatingly by distributing someone else's purse.

**Free:** Offended by the bourgeois who quiets his conscience with alms from his own money, the left Catholic nobly proposes to quiet his conscience with other people's money.

`tags: left-catholic, alms, hypocrisy`

## §187
> La ciencia política seguirá reducida a un incoherente
> catálogo de accidentes históricos, mientras no exista una
> axiología que le fije fines y una biología política que le
> suministre medios.

**Close:** Political science will remain reduced to an incoherent catalogue of historical accidents so long as there does not exist an axiology that fixes its ends and a political biology that supplies its means.

**Free:** Political science will remain a heap of historical accidents until it has both an axiology to determine ends and a political biology to determine means.

**Exegesis:**
He is refusing both empty norm-talk and empty realism. Ends without an account of human material are blind; means without ends are mechanical.

`tags: political-science, axiology, means`

## §194
> La sociedad moderna abriga el peculiar propósito de
> cambiar sistemáticamente las autoridades sociales por
> autoridades políticas.
> Es decir: las instancias civilizadoras por cargos
> administrativos.

**Close:** Modern society harbors the peculiar purpose of systematically changing social authorities into political authorities. That is to say: civilizing instances into administrative posts.

**Free:** Modern society keeps trying to replace social authorities with political ones, that is, living civilizing authorities with administrative offices.

**Exegesis:**
This is a sharp formula for bureaucratic modernity. Administration does not merely add itself to inherited social forms; it displaces them.

`tags: authority, administration, civilization`

## §195
> La propaganda, en Rusia como en China, selecciona tan
> intencionalmente los argumentos burdos y las
> falsificaciones obvias que es menester atribuir el triunfo del
> comunismo al desdén con que trata la inteligencia de las
> muchedumbres.

**Close:** Propaganda, in Russia as in China, selects crude arguments and obvious falsifications so intentionally that one must attribute the triumph of communism to the disdain with which it treats the intelligence of the multitudes.

**Free:** Russian and Chinese propaganda chooses such crude arguments and such blatant falsifications that communism's triumph seems to depend on the contempt it shows for mass intelligence.

`tags: propaganda, communism, masses`

## §197
> Ya que explicar es identificar, el conocimiento no es
> explicativo donde la individualidad es su objeto.

**Close:** Since to explain is to identify, knowledge is not explanatory where individuality is its object.

**Free:** If explanation works by identification, then wherever individuality is the object, knowledge cannot be fully explanatory.

**Exegesis:**
This is a compact epistemological rule. Explanation tends toward subsumption; individuality resists being reduced to a repeatable case.

`tags: explanation, individuality, knowledge`
