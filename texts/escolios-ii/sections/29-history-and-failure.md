# Section 29: History and Failure (§661-680, pp. 120-123)

Here the pages become harsher about modern success: news, anonymity, suffrage, revolution, and bureaucracy all keep wearing the face of progress while hollowing out judgment and form.

---

**Free:** Marxist scholasticism reached the fifteenth century in a single leap.

## §667
> Las explicaciones económicas ocupan la zona media de la historia.
> Entre la zona superficial ocupada por las interpretaciones psicológicas y la zona profunda que ocupa la investigación fenomenológica de las estructuras mentales.

**Close:** Economic explanations occupy the middle zone of history.
Between the superficial zone occupied by psychological interpretations and the deep zone occupied by phenomenological inquiry into mental structures.

**Free:** Economic explanations occupy history's middle zone, between the surface level of psychological interpretations and the deeper level of phenomenological inquiry into mental structures.

**Exegesis:**
This is not an outright dismissal of economic explanation. It is a rank judgment: economics belongs to a real but limited stratum, and error begins when that middle zone claims the whole.

`tags: history, economics, rank`

## §668
> Donde los cambios sociales se aceleran y donde el anonimato crece, las costumbres, hijas del tiempo y de la vigilancia del vecino, se desmoronan y perecen.
> Siendo ellas el mecanismo de ajuste entre la ley y la ética, su desaparición coloca a la conciencia inerme y desnuda ante el estado.
> Por otra parte, como los cambios sociales rápidos y el anonimato urbano raen, alisan, lijan, al individuo, la sociedad que confía más arduos fallos a cada conciencia, es la que produce individuos menos capaces de dictarlos.

**Close:** Where social changes accelerate and anonymity grows, customs, daughters of time and of the neighbor's vigilance, crumble and perish.
Since they are the adjustment mechanism between law and ethics, their disappearance places conscience unarmed and naked before the state.
On the other hand, since rapid social changes and urban anonymity scrape, smooth, and sand down the individual, the society that entrusts harder judgments to each conscience is the one that produces individuals least capable of dictating them.

**Free:** Where social change accelerates and anonymity expands, custom, child of time and neighborly surveillance, crumbles away.
Because custom is the mediating mechanism between law and ethics, its disappearance leaves conscience naked and defenseless before the state.
At the same time, the very society that asks each conscience for harder judgments is busy sanding the individual flat and making him less able to render them.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the volume's strongest accounts of modern moral exposure. The disappearance of custom does not liberate conscience into heroic maturity; it leaves conscience isolated before the state while also producing flatter people.

`tags: custom, conscience, state`

## §674
> El incorregible error político del hombre de buena voluntad es presuponer cándidamente que en todo momento cabe hacer lo que toca.
> Aquí, donde lo necesario suele ser lo imposible.

**Close:** The incorrigible political error of the man of good will is to presuppose naively that at every moment it is possible to do what is fitting.
Here, where what is necessary is usually the impossible.

**Free:** The incorrigible political error of the man of good will is to suppose, naively, that at every moment one can do what ought to be done.
Here necessity is usually the impossible.

**Exegesis:**
Good will is not enough because political life is constrained by conditions it does not choose. The aphorism is aimed at moral innocence that mistakes obligation for immediate possibility.

`tags: politics, good-will, impossibility`

## §675
> La sociedad moderna se envilece tan aprisa que cada nueva mañana contemplamos con nostalgia al adversario de ayer.
> Los marxistas ya comienzan a parecernos los últimos aristócratas de Occidente.

**Close:** Modern society debases itself so quickly that each new morning we contemplate yesterday's adversary with nostalgia.
The Marxists already begin to seem to us the last aristocrats of the West.

**Free:** Modern society degrades itself so fast that every new morning we look back nostalgically on yesterday's adversary.
The Marxists already begin to look like the last aristocrats of the West.

**Exegesis:**
The sentence is not praising Marxism. It measures the speed of debasement by the grotesque fact that yesterday's enemy can start to look like a lost nobility.

`tags: modernity, debasement, Marxism`

## §677
> Las verdaderas revoluciones no se inician con su estallido público, sino terminan con él.

**Close:** True revolutions do not begin with their public outbreak, but end with it.

**Free:** Real revolutions do not begin with their public eruption; they end there.

**Exegesis:**
The public event is demoted from origin to disclosure. What matters has already happened before the explosion becomes visible.

`tags: revolution, public-event, origin`

## §678
> Ver las apariencias como hechos estéticos - mirar la realidad como hecho religioso.

**Close:** To see appearances as aesthetic facts - to look at reality as religious fact.

**Free:** See appearances as aesthetic facts; look at reality as a religious fact.

**Exegesis:**
The aphorism is almost a method note. It asks for a double discipline: form at the level of appearance, religion at the level of reality.

`tags: appearance, reality, religion`
