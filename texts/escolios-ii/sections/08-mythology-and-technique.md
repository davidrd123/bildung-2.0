# Section 08: Mythology and Technique (§166–180, pp. 35–38)

Here the tone gets tighter and stranger. Science, religion, technique, sermon, anthem, and culture all come under the same suspicion: what they claim to explain often turns out to be a displaced mythology.

---

## §166
> Las teorías científicas no se vuelven fuerzas históricas
> sino al actuar como ideologías de movimientos religiosos.

**Close:** Scientific theories do not become historical forces except by acting as ideologies of religious movements.

**Free:** Scientific theories only start moving history when they begin functioning like religious ideologies.

`tags: science, history, religion`

---

## §167
> La humanidad disfraza sus hambres religiosas con
> máscara de codicias terrestres.

**Close:** Humanity disguises its religious hungers with the mask of earthly greeds.

**Free:** Human beings dress their religious hungers in the costume of worldly appetites.

`tags: religion, hunger, greed`

---

## §168
> La ciencia se degrada fácilmente en mitología de los
> tontos.

**Close:** Science easily degrades into mythology of fools.

**Free:** Science easily sinks into a mythology for fools.

`tags: science, mythology, fools`

---

## §169
> La historia es más un conflicto entre mitologías que
> entre codicias.

**Close:** History is more a conflict between mythologies than between greeds.

**Free:** History is less a clash of appetites than a clash of mythologies.

**Exegesis:**
This revises a great deal of modern reductionism in one stroke. What looks like material conflict often depends on rival sacred narratives, even when those narratives are dressed as economics or politics.

`tags: history, mythology, conflict`

---

## §170
> La originalidad deliberada es el perfil de lo que los
> demás piensan.

**Close:** Deliberate originality is the profile of what the others think.

**Free:** Deliberate originality usually turns out to be a silhouette cast by prevailing opinion.

`tags: originality, conformity, opinion`

---

## §171
> Los que profesan que lo noble es vil acaban predicando
> que lo vil es noble.

**Close:** Those who profess that the noble is vile end by preaching that the vile is noble.

**Free:** Whoever starts by debunking the noble usually ends by canonizing the base.

`tags: noble, vile, inversion`

## §172
> La inteligencia no consiste en el manejo de ideas
> inteligentes, sino en el manejo inteligente de cualquier idea.

**Close:** Intelligence does not consist in the handling of intelligent ideas, but in the intelligent handling of any idea.

**Free:** Intelligence lies not in having smart ideas ready-made, but in handling any idea intelligently.

`tags: intelligence, ideas, handling`

---

## §173
> La inepcia y la sandez de la palabrería episcopal y
> pontificia nos turbarían, si nosotros, cristianos viejos, no
> hubiésemos aprendido, felizmente, desde pequeños, a
> dormir durante el sermón.

**Close:** The ineptitude and silliness of episcopal and pontifical palavering would disturb us if we, old Christians, had not fortunately learned from childhood to sleep during the sermon.

**Free:** The stupidity of episcopal and papal chatter would disturb us much more if old Christians had not, thankfully, learned as children how to sleep through sermons.

`tags: episcopal-talk, sermon, old-Christians`

## §174
> Cuando oímos los acordes finales de un himno nacional,
> sabemos con certeza que alguien acaba de decir tonterías.

**Close:** When we hear the final chords of a national anthem, we know with certainty that someone has just said foolish things.

**Free:** The last bars of a national anthem reliably tell you that someone has just finished talking nonsense.

`tags: anthem, nationalism, foolishness`

---

## §175
> Llamamos hoy anti-inconformismo la actitud del que
> comparte corajosamente opiniones mayoritarias y cumple
> cabalmente sus funciones fisiológicas.
> El anticonformista, al defecar, se cree sobre una
> barricada.

**Close:** Today we call anti-nonconformism the attitude of one who courageously shares majority opinions and fully performs his physiological functions. The anticonformist, while defecating, believes himself on a barricade.

**Free:** What now passes for bold anti-conformism is merely the courageous sharing of majority opinion plus bodily candor. The pseudo-rebel imagines himself on a barricade while performing a bodily function.

**Exegesis:**
The joke matters because it names a modern habit: treating private transgression or mere bluntness as public courage. The political metaphor survives, but only as parody.

`tags: anticonformism, majority, parody`

---

## §176
> Dios es el término con que le notificamos al universo
> que no es todo.

**Close:** God is the term with which we notify the universe that it is not all.

**Free:** "God" is the word by which we inform the universe that it does not exhaust reality.

**Exegesis:**
This is among the cleanest metaphysical lines in the volume. God functions here not as one more item within the whole, but as the name by which the whole is denied total sufficiency.

`tags: God, universe, totality`

---

## §177
> El verdadero Dios respira más auténticamente en la
> plebe divina de los Indigitamenta que en ese espectro de la
> teología moderna que no es más que el perfil de las colinas
> éticas destacándose sobre un horizonte desierto.

**Close:** The true God breathes more authentically in the divine plebs of the Indigitamenta than in that specter of modern theology which is nothing more than the profile of ethical hills standing out against a deserted horizon.

**Free:** The true God breathes more genuinely in the divine rabble of the Indigitamenta than in the ghost of modern theology, that mere outline of ethical hills against an empty horizon.

**Exegesis:**
The point is not a literal return to Roman cult. Gómez Dávila is saying that even a crowded pagan divine register can feel more alive than the thinned ethical abstraction of modern theology.

`tags: God, Indigitamenta, modern-theology`

---

## §178
> Las "culturas" no son recíprocamente incomprensibles
> las unas a las otras, como universos incomunicados.
> Ni mutuamente transparentes, como si consistieran en
> proposiciones lógicas.
> Sino diáfanas y opacas a la vez, como individuos entre sí.

**Close:** "Cultures" are not reciprocally incomprehensible to one another, like incommunicated universes. Nor mutually transparent, as if they consisted in logical propositions. Rather they are at once diaphanous and opaque, like individuals to one another.

**Free:** Cultures are neither sealed worlds nor perfectly translatable propositions. They are translucent and opaque at once, the way persons are.

**Exegesis:**
This is a very good rule of comparison. It blocks both radical incommensurability and naïve transparency. Cultural understanding is personal in kind: partial, real, and never total.

`tags: culture, comparison, opacity`

---

## §179
> El técnico se cree un ser superior, porque sabe lo que,
> por definición, cualquiera puede aprender.

**Close:** The technician thinks himself a superior being because he knows what, by definition, anyone can learn.

**Free:** The technician mistakes himself for a superior being because his expertise is, in principle, universally teachable.

`tags: technician, superiority, teachability`

---

## §180
> Los viejos libros de ciencias naturales no interesan sino
> al historiador de la ciencia.
> En las ciencias humanas, la inteligencia no cumple su
> tarea sino asumiendo por su cuenta y riesgo, en todo
> momento, el pasado entero de esas ciencias.

**Close:** Old books of natural science interest no one but the historian of science. In the human sciences, intelligence fulfills its task only by assuming on its own account and risk, at every moment, the entire past of those sciences.

**Free:** Old natural-science books are mainly for historians of science. In the human sciences, by contrast, intelligence does its work only by continually taking the whole past of those disciplines upon itself, at its own risk.

**Exegesis:**
This is another distinction that matters beyond the aphorism itself. In the human sciences the past is not obsolete background but active inheritance. Intelligence there is inseparable from responsible recollection.

`tags: natural-science, human-science, inheritance`
