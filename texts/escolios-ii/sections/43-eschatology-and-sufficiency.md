# Section 43: Eschatology and Sufficiency (§941-960, pp. 165-168)

These pages hold together the strongest theological and methodological pressure in the whole run so far: eschatology without historicism, exegesis without literalism, and ethics without self-sufficiency.

---

## §941
> La eternidad sólo aflora en el tiempo cuando lo vivimos como duración ontológicamente amenazada. Todo es efímero en una duración asegurada.

**Close:** Eternity surfaces in time only when we live it as ontologically threatened duration. Everything is ephemeral in a secured duration.

**Free:** Eternity surfaces in time only when we live time as duration ontologically under threat. Within a secured duration, everything becomes ephemeral.

**Exegesis:**
The line makes vulnerability a condition of depth. Security does not stabilize being; it thins it.

`tags: eternity, time, threat`

---

## §942
> El catolicismo es la suma de formas impuestas a un propósito tenaz por los repetidos intentos de abolirlo. El catolicismo no es una sucesión de pactos con el mundo, sino una estela de naufragios triunfales.

**Close:** Catholicism is the sum of forms imposed on a tenacious purpose by repeated attempts to abolish it. Catholicism is not a succession of pacts with the world, but a wake of triumphant shipwrecks.

**Free:** Catholicism is the set of forms forced upon a tenacious purpose by repeated attempts to abolish it. It is not a succession of pacts with the world, but the wake left by triumphant shipwrecks.

**Exegesis:**
This is a powerful anti-adaptation image. Catholicism endures not by neat compromise, but through surviving repeated wrecks without losing its purpose.

`tags: Catholicism, form, shipwreck`

## §946
> Ser reaccionario no es creer en determinadas soluciones, sino tener un sentido agudo de la complejidad de los problemas.

**Close:** To be reactionary is not to believe in certain solutions, but to have a sharp sense of the complexity of problems.

**Free:** Being reactionary does not mean believing in certain solutions, but possessing an acute sense of how complex the problems are.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the cleanest non-programmatic definitions in the volume. Reaction is cast as intellectual and moral acuity before complexity, not as attachment to a platform.

`tags: reactionary, complexity, solution`

## §947
> La sociedad capitalista se enriqueció acoplando la ignorancia de un empresario astuto, que dirige, a la ciencia de un técnico estulto, que realiza. El socialismo pretende enriquecerse confiando la dirección al técnico.

**Close:** Capitalist society enriched itself by coupling the ignorance of an astute entrepreneur, who directs, with the knowledge of a stupid technician, who executes. Socialism pretends to enrich itself by entrusting direction to the technician.

**Free:** Capitalist society enriched itself by coupling the ignorance of a shrewd entrepreneur, who directs, with the knowledge of a foolish technician, who carries things out. Socialism hopes to enrich itself by handing direction over to the technician.

`tags: capitalism, socialism, technician`

## §948
> Como si la noción de inspiración fuese inspirada, la Iglesia supeditó su exégesis a una definición postulacional de la noción. En lugar de definirla empíricamente de modo acorde con la índole de los textos inspirados.

**Close:** As though the notion of inspiration were inspired, the Church subordinated its exegesis to a postulational definition of the notion, instead of defining it empirically in a way accordant with the nature of the inspired texts.

**Free:** As if the notion of inspiration itself were inspired, the Church subordinated its exegesis to a postulated definition of the notion instead of defining it empirically in accord with the character of inspired texts.

**Exegesis:**
The criticism is subtle and internal. The problem is not the notion of inspiration as such, but insulating the concept from empirical encounter with the texts it is supposed to illuminate.

`tags: inspiration, Church, exegesis`

## §951
> Rasgo típico no es el que tenga una particular frecuencia, sino el que tiene una particular importancia. La estadística no reemplaza la intuición.

**Close:** A typical trait is not the one that has a particular frequency, but the one that has a particular importance. Statistics does not replace intuition.

**Free:** A typical trait is not what appears most frequently, but what carries particular importance. Statistics cannot replace intuition.

**Exegesis:**
This is a methodological warning against quantitative reduction. Frequency is not typicity.

`tags: typicity, statistics, intuition`

## §954
> Aun cuando la definición construya en realidad la clase, la clase parece construir la definición. Basta construir una clase, en efecto, eligiendo una relación cualquiera común a varios individuos, para despertar en ellos un sentimiento de coparticipación, que convierte la definición de la clase en clase definida. La conciencia de clase no es reflejo de una situación, sino reflexión sobre ella.

**Close:** Even when the definition really constructs the class, the class seems to construct the definition. It suffices indeed to construct a class, choosing any relation common to several individuals, to awaken in them a feeling of co-participation that converts the definition of the class into a defined class. Class consciousness is not reflection of a situation, but reflection upon it.

**Free:** Even when the definition in fact constructs the class, the class appears to construct the definition. It is enough to build a class by selecting any relation common to several individuals in order to awaken in them a feeling of participation that turns the class's definition into a defined class. Class consciousness is not the reflection of a situation, but reflection upon it.

`tags: class, definition, consciousness`

## §960
> Debemos condenar, a veces, los intentos del hombre de ocultar su miseria. Y condenar, a veces, a quienes los condenan.

**Close:** We must sometimes condemn man's attempts to hide his misery. And sometimes condemn those who condemn them.

**Free:** We must sometimes condemn man's attempts to hide his misery, and sometimes condemn those who condemn those attempts.

**Exegesis:**
The final move is deliberately double. Moral judgment remains necessary, but it too must stay under judgment.

`tags: misery, judgment, double-condemnation`
