# Section 28: Mystery and Diagnosis (§641-660, pp. 117-120)

The next stretch turns diagnosis sharper: mystery educates, transcendence interrupts immanence, and the modern order finally appears less as confusion than as a machinery whose very functioning degrades.

---

## §641
> Como el intelectual no es inventor de ideas, sino mero usuario, el lucero que orienta sus empresas es siempre el de la víspera.

**Close:** Since the intellectual is not an inventor of ideas, but a mere user, the star that guides his enterprises is always that of the previous evening.

**Free:** Because the intellectual invents no ideas of his own and merely uses them, the star guiding his enterprises is always yesterday's.

`tags: intellectual, ideas, delay`

> Nada más fácil que engañar la primera vez al alma noble.

**Close:** Nothing is easier than deceiving the noble soul the first time.

**Free:** Nothing is easier than deceiving a noble soul the first time.

`tags: nobility, deception, innocence`

## §643
> Todo lo que le haga sentir al hombre que el misterio lo envuelve lo vuelve más inteligente.

**Close:** Everything that makes man feel that mystery envelops him makes him more intelligent.

**Free:** Whatever makes a man feel himself surrounded by mystery makes him more intelligent.

**Exegesis:**
Mystery is not anti-intellectual here. It is what restores proportion, and with it a sharper rather than duller intelligence.

`tags: mystery, intelligence, proportion`

## §647
> La democratización del erotismo sirvió, por lo menos, para mostrarnos que la virginidad, la castidad, la pureza, no son solteronas agrias y morbosas, como lo creíamos, sino vestales silenciosas de una limpia llama.

**Close:** The democratization of eroticism served, at least, to show us that virginity, chastity, purity, are not sour and morbid old maids, as we thought, but silent vestals of a clean flame.

**Free:** The democratization of eroticism at least showed us that virginity, chastity, and purity are not the sour, morbid spinsters we took them for, but silent vestals tending a clear flame.

**Exegesis:**
The sentence does not defend moralism by scolding. It tries to recover a symbolic dignity that modern caricature had turned into pathology.

`tags: eroticism, chastity, purity`

## §655
> Los tontos se dividen en dos clases: los que "quieren ser como los demás"; los que "no quieren ser como los demás".

**Close:** Fools divide into two classes: those who "want to be like everyone else"; those who "do not want to be like everyone else."

**Free:** Fools come in two kinds: those who want to be like everyone else, and those who build their identity on not wanting to be like everyone else.

**Exegesis:**
The aphorism refuses both conformity and counterfeit singularity. The two kinds of folly are mirror images, not opposites.

`tags: folly, conformity, singularity`

## §656
> Si extraemos de la sola inmanencia los principios de la acción inmanente, desatamos catástrofes.
> La imaginación no inventa verdades sino bajo influjos trascendentes.

**Close:** If we extract from immanence alone the principles of immanent action, we unleash catastrophes.
Imagination does not invent truths except under transcendent influences.

**Free:** If we try to derive the principles of action from immanence alone, we set catastrophes loose.
Imagination invents no truths except under transcendent influence.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the volume's starkest anti-immanent claims. Action severed from transcendence does not become free and mature; it becomes catastrophic.

`tags: immanence, transcendence, catastrophe`

## §657
> No es imposible que en los batallones clericales al servicio del hombre todavía se infiltren algunos quintacolumnistas de Dios.

**Close:** It is not impossible that in the clerical battalions at the service of man some fifth-columnists of God may still infiltrate themselves.

**Free:** It is not impossible that among the clerical battalions enlisted in man's service a few fifth-columnists of God still slip through.

`tags: clericalism, God, infiltration`

---

## §658
> El marxismo, más que diagnóstico, es síntoma.

**Close:** Marxism is, more than diagnosis, symptom.

**Free:** Marxism is less a diagnosis than a symptom.

**Exegesis:**
The sentence reverses explanatory prestige in a single stroke. Marxism is not first the instrument by which history is read, but one of the phenomena history itself must explain.

`tags: Marxism, symptom, diagnosis`

---

## §659
> No es al simple fracaso del mundo moderno a lo que hoy asistimos, sino al fracaso de su éxito.

**Close:** It is not the simple failure of the modern world that we witness today, but the failure of its success.

**Free:** What we are witnessing today is not the mere failure of the modern world, but the failure produced by its very success.

**Exegesis:**
This is sharper than conservative lament. The problem is not that modernity failed to achieve its ends, but that its ends themselves have ripened into their own indictment.

`tags: modernity, success, failure`

---

## §660
> La burocracia no asusta porque paralice, sino porque funciona.

**Close:** Bureaucracy does not frighten because it paralyzes, but because it functions.

**Free:** Bureaucracy is frightening not because it jams, but because it works.

**Exegesis:**
The line strips away the consoling image of bureaucratic incompetence. Its danger is not mere inefficiency, but the cold competence with which it can carry out a degraded order.

`tags: bureaucracy, function, fear`
