# Section 38: Christendom and Paradox (§841-860, pp. 149-152)

The final stretch of this run is more constructive than it first appears: discipline, rank, paradox, Empire, and Christendom all serve as ways of refusing both egalitarian simplification and theocratic fantasy.

---

## §841
> El ciudadano honesto, para participar en los conflictos sociales de este siglo, debe pedir un fusil que dispare simultáneamente en direcciones antagónicas.

**Close:** The honest citizen, to participate in this century's social conflicts, must ask for a rifle that fires simultaneously in antagonistic directions.

**Free:** An honest citizen who wants to take part in this century's social conflicts needs a rifle that fires in opposite directions at once.

`tags: citizen, conflict, antinomy`

**Free:** The socialist does not understand that those who foresee socialism's triumph are reactionaries precisely because they foresee it.

**Close:** One must not count among the partisans of inequality those who reject economic equality because they know that only fortune distinguishes them.

**Free:** We should not count as defenders of inequality those who reject economic equality only because they know fortune is the sole thing distinguishing them.

`tags: inequality, fortune, motive`

## §844
> Las individualidades vigorosas no aparecen sino en el seno de severas disciplinas colectivas.

**Close:** Vigorous individualities appear only in the bosom of severe collective disciplines.

**Free:** Strong individualities appear only within severe collective disciplines.

**Exegesis:**
This is the same anti-flattening claim seen from the other side. Discipline is not the enemy of individuality but its condition.

`tags: individuality, discipline, collectivity`

## §846
> Buscar la "verdad fuera del tiempo" es la manera de encontrar la "verdad de nuestro tiempo". El que busca la "verdad de su tiempo" encuentra los tópicos del día.

**Close:** To seek the "truth outside time" is the way to find the "truth of our time." He who seeks the "truth of his time" finds the commonplaces of the day.

**Free:** Seeking the "truth outside time" is how one finds the "truth of our time." Whoever seeks the "truth of his time" finds only the day's cliches.

## §847
> Con la Independencia feneció la autenticidad espiritual de América. Capaz, durante el período colonial, de adaptar las formas mediterráneas a los nuevos paisajes y aún de dar una modulación propia al barroco, posteriormente sólo copia con docilidad plebeya las modas del día. La originalidad limitada, pero auténtica, de provincia española, que tuvo durante la Colonia, se convirtió en el plagio cursi peculiar a los barrios pobres.

**Close:** With Independence America's spiritual authenticity came to an end. Capable, during the colonial period, of adapting Mediterranean forms to new landscapes and even of giving the baroque a modulation of its own, it later copies only the fashions of the day with plebeian docility. The limited but authentic originality of a Spanish province that it had during the Colony turned into the tacky plagiarism peculiar to poor neighborhoods.

**Free:** America's spiritual authenticity died with Independence. During the colonial period it could adapt Mediterranean forms to new landscapes and even give the baroque a modulation of its own; afterward it merely copied the fashions of the day with plebeian docility. The limited but authentic originality it possessed as a Spanish province turned into the tacky plagiarism peculiar to poor neighborhoods.

**Exegesis:**
The provocation is not anti-American in the loose sense. It is about the loss of mediated inheritance: a provincial originality rooted in transmission gives way to docile fashion-copying.

`tags: America, Independence, originality`

## §850
> La inteligencia es espontáneamente aristocrática, porque es la facultad de distinguir diferencias y de fijar rangos.

**Close:** Intelligence is spontaneously aristocratic because it is the faculty of distinguishing differences and fixing ranks.

**Free:** Intelligence is spontaneously aristocratic because it is the faculty that distinguishes differences and assigns ranks.

**Exegesis:**
This is not social snobbery disguised as epistemology. It is an argument that intelligence itself works by discrimination, not by leveling.

`tags: intelligence, aristocracy, rank`

## §854
> Rango cultural y rango estético de la obra de arte son categorías distintas. El rango cultural depende de la calidad de la cultura a que la obra pertenece, el rango estético depende de la sola calidad de la obra. Obras de bajo rango estético pueden tener alto rango cultural, porque llevan consigo los valores de la cultura insigne a que pertenecen. Inversamente, obras de rango cultural bajo, pueden ser redimidas por su excelencia estética.

**Close:** Cultural rank and aesthetic rank of the work of art are distinct categories. Cultural rank depends on the quality of the culture to which the work belongs; aesthetic rank depends on the work's quality alone. Works of low aesthetic rank can have high cultural rank because they carry with them the values of the distinguished culture to which they belong. Conversely, works of low cultural rank can be redeemed by their aesthetic excellence.

**Free:** The cultural rank and the aesthetic rank of a work of art are different categories. Cultural rank depends on the quality of the culture to which the work belongs; aesthetic rank depends only on the quality of the work itself. A work low in aesthetic rank may still have high cultural rank because it bears the values of an eminent culture; conversely, a work culturally low may be redeemed by aesthetic excellence.

**Exegesis:**
This distinction matters well beyond art history. It separates intrinsic excellence from the weight of the civilizational world a work carries with it.

`tags: art, culture, aesthetics`

## §855
> El universo no es sistema, es decir: coherencia lógica, sino estructura jerárquica de paradojas.

**Close:** The universe is not system, that is to say: logical coherence, but hierarchical structure of paradoxes.

**Free:** The universe is not a system, that is, logical coherence, but a hierarchical structure of paradoxes.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the book's central metaphysical refusals. Reality is not saved by coherence but articulated by paradox under hierarchy.

`tags: universe, paradox, hierarchy`

## §859
> El cristianismo es radicalmente adverso a la teocracia. Una sociedad convertida en Iglesia no prefigura el reino de Dios. Dibuja, al contrario, su caricatura satánica. La Iglesia reclama la paralela existencia del Imperio. Personalmente, sólo creo legítimo un mundo que presidan, desde tronos simétricos, Pontífice Romano y Emperador Germánico.

**Close:** Christianity is radically adverse to theocracy. A society converted into Church does not prefigure the kingdom of God. It sketches, on the contrary, its satanic caricature. The Church claims the parallel existence of Empire. Personally, I only believe legitimate a world presided over, from symmetrical thrones, by Roman Pontiff and German Emperor.

**Free:** Christianity is radically opposed to theocracy. A society turned into Church does not prefigure the kingdom of God; it sketches its satanic caricature. The Church requires the parallel existence of Empire. Personally, I count legitimate only a world presided over from symmetrical thrones by the Roman Pontiff and the German Emperor.

**Exegesis:**
Whatever one makes of the final political image, the governing claim is clear: Christianity resists fusion with the social totality, and theocracy is named not as fulfillment but as caricature.

`tags: Christianity, theocracy, Empire`

## §860
> La cristiandad nunca pretendió, ni lo pretenderá si resucita, ser el reino de Dios, sino una sociedad de pecadores cristianos.

**Close:** Christendom never pretended, nor will it pretend if it resurrects, to be the kingdom of God, but rather a society of Christian sinners.

**Free:** Christendom never claimed, and if it rose again would not claim, to be the kingdom of God, but only a society of Christian sinners.

**Exegesis:**
The line closes the run by lowering pretension without lowering aspiration. Christendom is not the kingdom; it is a temporal order marked by sin and yet shaped by Christianity.

`tags: Christendom, kingdom-of-God, sinners`
