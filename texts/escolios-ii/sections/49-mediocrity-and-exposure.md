# Section 49: Mediocrity and Exposure (§1061-1080, pp. 184-187)

The governing pressure here is exposure: what publics reward, what democracies flatten, and how mediocrity reveals itself when it can no longer hide inside slogans.

---

## §1061
> A ciertos escritores les abriría uno, de buena gana, las ventanas del alma, porque huelen a moho.

**Close:** To certain writers one would gladly open the windows of the soul, because they smell of mold.

**Free:** Some writers make one want to throw open the windows of their souls, because they smell of mildew.

`tags: writers, soul, mildew`

---

## §1062
> La desigualdad meramente económica no dura. Ni merece durar.

**Close:** Merely economic inequality does not last. Nor does it deserve to last.

**Free:** Purely economic inequality never lasts, nor does it deserve to.

`tags: inequality, economy, duration`

---

## §1063
> Comerciantes, banqueros, industriales, no son fenómenos típicos de la sociedad burguesa, puesto que existen en las más diversas sociedades. El típico fenómeno burgués es el intelectual.

**Close:** Merchants, bankers, industrialists, are not typical phenomena of bourgeois society, since they exist in the most diverse societies. The typical bourgeois phenomenon is the intellectual.

**Free:** Merchants, bankers, and industrialists are not the typical products of bourgeois society, since they exist in all sorts of societies. The typical bourgeois phenomenon is the intellectual.

**Exegesis:**
The target is not commerce as such, but the intellectual figure produced under bourgeois conditions: abstracted, justificatory, and socially central in a specifically modern way.

`tags: bourgeois, intellectual, society`

---

## §1064
> La ética que no mande renunciar es un crimen contra la dignidad a que debemos aspirar y contra la felicidad que podemos obtener.

**Close:** The ethic that does not command renunciation is a crime against the dignity to which we ought to aspire and against the happiness that we can obtain.

**Free:** Any ethic that does not command renunciation is a crime against the dignity we should seek and the happiness we might attain.

`tags: ethics, renunciation, dignity`

---

## §1065
> Tantos públicos distintos existen hoy que cualquier libro, por mediocre que sea, encuentra iletrados que seduzca.

**Close:** So many distinct publics exist today that any book, however mediocre it may be, finds illiterates whom it seduces.

**Free:** There are now so many different publics that any book, however mediocre, can find illiterates to seduce.

`tags: publics, mediocrity, readership`

## §1066
> Cuando el grueso público se aficiona a las artes, la expresión estética se simplifica en puro impacto.

**Close:** When the broad public takes a liking to the arts, aesthetic expression simplifies itself into pure impact.

**Free:** Once the mass public takes up the arts, aesthetic expression gets simplified into sheer impact.

`tags: art, public, impact`

## §1067
> En arte, como en política, mientras más bajo hoy se apunte mejor se atina.

**Close:** In art, as in politics, the lower one aims today the better one hits.

**Free:** In art as in politics, the lower one aims today, the surer the hit.

`tags: art, politics, standards`

## §1068
> No hay muerto más muerto que el escritor de talento que se creyó genio.

**Close:** There is no dead man deader than the talented writer who believed himself a genius.

**Free:** No dead writer is deader than the talented man who mistook himself for a genius.

`tags: writer, genius, delusion`

---

## §1069
> El tumulto en torno de una obra de arte no es hoy indicio de importancia estética, sino de aprovechamiento político.

**Close:** The tumult around a work of art is today not an indication of aesthetic importance, but of political exploitation.

**Free:** Tumult around a work of art today signals not aesthetic importance but political use.

**Exegesis:**
Public noise around art is no longer evidence of weight. It is evidence that the work has been seized for another register.

`tags: art, politics, publicity`

---

## §1070
> La facilidad es el premio del genio y el castigo del mediocre.

**Close:** Facility is the reward of genius and the punishment of the mediocre.

**Free:** Ease is genius's reward and the mediocre man's punishment.

`tags: genius, mediocrity, ease`

---

## §1071
> Los mediocres nos salvamos cuando somos tan mediocres que logramos verlo.

**Close:** We mediocrities save ourselves when we are so mediocre that we manage to see it.

**Free:** We mediocre people are saved when we are mediocre enough to recognize it.

**Exegesis:**
The saving move is not excellence but lucidity. Self-knowledge becomes the one form of escape still open to mediocrity.

`tags: mediocrity, self-knowledge, salvation`

---

## §1072
> Los partidarios de la sociedad igualitaria suelen ser siempre chiquitos.

**Close:** The partisans of egalitarian society tend always to be small.

**Free:** The supporters of egalitarian society always seem to be little men.

`tags: equality, resentment, scale`

## §1073
> La historia no ofrece más cómico espectáculo que el del historiador progresista peleando, indignado, con la historia.

**Close:** History offers no more comic spectacle than that of the progressive historian fighting, indignant, with history.

**Free:** History offers no more comic spectacle than the progressive historian quarreling indignantly with history itself.

`tags: history, progressivism, comedy`

## §1074
> A la mayoría de los historiadores se les trasluce, al juzgar, el arrabal donde los criaron.

**Close:** In the case of the majority of historians, one sees through, when they judge, the suburb in which they were raised.

**Free:** In the judgments of most historians, one can still see the outskirts that raised them.

`tags: historians, judgment, origin`

## §1075
> Medio globo hoy se encarga de patentizar los vicios de la democracia despótica, y medio los de la democracia liberal.

**Close:** Half the globe today undertakes to make manifest the vices of despotic democracy, and half those of liberal democracy.

**Free:** Half the globe now exists to make plain the vices of despotic democracy, and the other half those of liberal democracy.

`tags: democracy, globe, vice`

---

## §1076
> La prosperidad material envilece menos que los requisitos intelectuales y morales para lograrla.

**Close:** Material prosperity debases less than the intellectual and moral requisites for attaining it.

**Free:** Material prosperity debases less than the intellectual and moral requirements demanded in order to attain it.

`tags: prosperity, morality, intellect`

---

## §1077
> Desde el instante en que una opinión cristaliza en texto, ya no lo juzga el oído cómplice de los contemporáneos, sino la pétrea mirada de lo muertos.

**Close:** From the instant in which an opinion crystallizes into text, it is no longer judged by the accomplice ear of contemporaries, but by the stony gaze of the dead.

**Free:** The moment an opinion hardens into text, it is no longer judged by the accomplice ear of contemporaries but by the stony gaze of the dead.

**Exegesis:**
Writing changes the tribunal. Publication removes a statement from the warm complicity of the present and places it before harsher, less bribable judges.

`tags: writing, judgment, posterity`

---

## §1078
> Contra la humildad de las tareas que la vida le asigna, nadie protesta tan ruidosamente como el incapaz de desempeñar otras.

**Close:** Against the humility of the tasks that life assigns him, no one protests so noisily as the man incapable of performing others.

**Free:** No one protests so noisily against the humble tasks life assigns him as the man incapable of doing any others.

`tags: task, humility, incapacity`

---

## §1079
> Podemos pedir misericordia. ¿Pero con qué derecho reclamamos justicia?

**Close:** We can ask for mercy. But with what right do we claim justice?

**Free:** We may ask for mercy. But by what right do we demand justice?

`tags: mercy, justice, right`

## §1080
> El actual sentimentalismo social es tan grotesco como el sentimentalismo erótico de antaño. El intelectual revolucionario es la dama de las camelias del nuevo teatro.

**Close:** The current social sentimentalism is as grotesque as the erotic sentimentalism of former times. The revolutionary intellectual is the lady of the camellias of the new theater.

**Free:** Today's social sentimentalism is as grotesque as yesterday's erotic sentimentalism. The revolutionary intellectual is the Lady of the Camellias of the new stage.

`tags: sentimentalism, intellectuals, theater`
