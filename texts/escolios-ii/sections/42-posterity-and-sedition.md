# Section 42: Posterity and Sedition (§921-940, pp. 162-165)

What dominates here is not just polemic but measure: admiration, justice, posterity, and the Gospel are all protected against ideological misreading.

---

## §921
> El Mesías anunciado por los profetas de la democracia decimonónica resultó meramente un aborto del Anticristo.

**Close:** The Messiah announced by the prophets of nineteenth-century democracy turned out to be merely an abortion of Antichrist.

**Free:** The Messiah proclaimed by the prophets of nineteenth-century democracy turned out to be merely an Antichristic miscarriage.

`tags: democracy, Messiah, Antichrist`

---

## §922
> Al revés de lo que les acontece a los jóvenes, entre los viejos sólo el imbécil dice estupideces.

**Close:** Contrary to what happens to the young, among the old only the imbecile says stupidities.

**Free:** Unlike the young, among the old only the imbecile says stupid things.

`tags: age, youth, stupidity`

## §923
> Esa liberación de la humanidad que cantó el siglo xix no resultó ser más que el turismo internacional.

**Close:** That liberation of humanity sung by the nineteenth century turned out to be nothing more than international tourism.

**Free:** The nineteenth century's hymn to humanity's liberation turned out to mean little more than international tourism.

`tags: liberation, nineteenth-century, tourism`

## §924
> El católico progresista decretó que el ateísmo consiste en dudar de la divinidad del hombre.

**Close:** The progressive Catholic decreed that atheism consists in doubting the divinity of man.

**Free:** The progressive Catholic has decreed that atheism means doubting man's divinity.

`tags: progressive-Catholic, atheism, divinity`

## §925
> El progresismo cristiano es una borrachera de traición.

**Close:** Christian progressivism is a drunkenness of betrayal.

**Free:** Christian progressivism is a drunken binge of betrayal.

`tags: progressivism, Christianity, betrayal`

## §926
> Nada de lo que sinceramente admiramos en un individuo depende de él.

**Close:** Nothing of what we sincerely admire in an individual depends on him.

**Free:** Nothing we sincerely admire in an individual finally depends on him.

`tags: admiration, individual, gift`

---

## §927
> La obra de arte lograda difiere radicalmente de la obra de arte fallida, pero la fallida difiere poco de la lograda. Condenar es más difícil, en las artes, que elegir.

**Close:** The successful work of art differs radically from the failed work of art, but the failed one differs little from the successful one. To condemn is more difficult, in the arts, than to choose.

**Free:** The successful work of art differs radically from the failed one, but the failed one differs very little from the successful. In art, condemnation is harder than choice.

**Exegesis:**
The line is about proximity, not indulgence. Artistic failure lies dangerously close to success, which is why judgment here is harder than taste.

`tags: art, failure, judgment`

---

## §928
> Al que anda a caza de una explicación cabal del mundo aconsejémosle que la invente. Para que corra menos riesgo de creer en ella.

**Close:** To the one who goes hunting for a complete explanation of the world, let us advise that he invent it. So that he runs less risk of believing in it.

**Free:** Whoever goes hunting for a total explanation of the world should be advised to invent one himself, so that he runs less risk of believing it.

`tags: explanation, world, invention`

## §929
> Cuando navegamos en océanos de imbecilidad, la inteligencia necesita el auxilio del buen gusto.

**Close:** When we navigate in oceans of imbecility, intelligence needs the aid of good taste.

**Free:** When we sail in oceans of imbecility, intelligence needs good taste as an ally.

`tags: intelligence, good-taste, imbecility`

## §930
> La justicia ha sido uno de los motores de la historia, porque es el nombre que asume la envidia en boca del querellante.

**Close:** Justice has been one of the motors of history because it is the name envy assumes in the mouth of the litigant.

**Free:** Justice has been one of history's motors because it is the name envy takes in the mouth of the plaintiff.

**Exegesis:**
The sentence does not deny justice in every sense. It warns that the rhetoric of justice is often the public mask envy learns to wear.

`tags: justice, envy, history`

## §931
> El siglo xix no vivió más angustiado con sus represiones sexuales que el siglo xx con su liberación sexual. Obsesión idéntica, aun cuando de signo contrario.

**Close:** The nineteenth century did not live more anguished by its sexual repressions than the twentieth century by its sexual liberation. Identical obsession, even if of contrary sign.

**Free:** The nineteenth century was no more anguished by its sexual repressions than the twentieth has been by its sexual liberation. The obsession is the same, even if the sign has changed.

`tags: sexuality, nineteenth-century, obsession`

## §932
> Anhelemos que triunfen, pasajeramente, momentos nobles de la rutina histórica. Pero no caigamos en la necedad de esperar la abolición terrestre de su giro monótono.

**Close:** Let us long that noble moments of the historical routine triumph, passingly. But let us not fall into the foolishness of expecting the earthly abolition of its monotonous turn.

**Free:** Let us hope that noble moments may triumph, for a time, within history's routine. But let us not fall into the folly of expecting the earthly abolition of its monotonous rotation.

`tags: history, nobility, monotony`

**Free:** When intellectuals finally fall silent, literature may yet rise again.

## §935
> El problema más circunscrito interesa eternamente, si un hombre inteligente lo trató.

**Close:** The most circumscribed problem interests eternally, if an intelligent man treated it.

**Free:** Even the most circumscribed problem interests forever if an intelligent man handled it.

`tags: problem, intelligence, eternity`

## §936
> La posteridad mira con ironía la obra de los revolucionarios victoriosos, pero no ha aprendido aún a citar sin quejumbre el nombre de los revolucionarios vencidos.

**Close:** Posterity looks with irony on the work of victorious revolutionaries, but it has not yet learned to cite without whining the name of defeated revolutionaries.

**Free:** Posterity views the work of victorious revolutionaries ironically, but it still has not learned to utter the names of defeated revolutionaries without complaint.

`tags: posterity, revolution, defeat`

---

## §937
> Tanto la percepción auténtica de cualquier esplendor terrestre, como la legitimidad de nuestro acatamiento, están condicionadas por la admisión previa de su radical dependencia.

**Close:** Both the authentic perception of any earthly splendor and the legitimacy of our obedience are conditioned by the prior admission of its radical dependence.

**Free:** Both authentic perception of any earthly splendor and the legitimacy of our reverence for it depend on first admitting its radical dependence.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the clearest formulas for earthly splendor in the volume. Glory is not denied, but it is made legitimate only through dependence.

`tags: splendor, dependence, legitimacy`

**Free:** Impartiality forbids injustice, not combat.

**Free:** If the reactionary never awakens inside the conservative, then he was only a paralyzed progressive.

## §940
> Quienes tildan de sedicioso al Evangelio, confunden burlescamente la negación revolucionaria de la sociedad con la negación apocalíptica del mundo.

**Close:** Those who brand the Gospel seditious burlesquely confuse the revolutionary negation of society with the apocalyptic negation of the world.

**Free:** Those who brand the Gospel seditious ludicrously confuse the revolutionary negation of society with the apocalyptic negation of the world.

**Exegesis:**
The distinction is essential. The Gospel's apocalyptic pressure does not simply translate into political sedition.

`tags: Gospel, apocalyptic, sedition`
