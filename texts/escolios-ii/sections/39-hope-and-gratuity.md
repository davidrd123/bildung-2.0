# Section 39: Hope and Gratuity (§861-880, pp. 153-156)

This run keeps hope on a short leash: no restoration, no Future with a capital letter, and no greatness that can finally be claimed as merit.

---

**Close:** It is not a restoration that the reactionary longs for, but a new miracle.

**Free:** What the reactionary longs for is not restoration but a new miracle.

`tags: reactionary, restoration, miracle`

## §866
> Cada valor es individual. Imprevisible, por lo tanto, casual, e irreemplazable. (Nadie reconstruirá las tragedias perdidas de Esquilo).

**Close:** Each value is individual. Unpredictable, therefore, contingent, and irreplaceable. (No one will reconstruct the lost tragedies of Aeschylus).

**Free:** Every value is individual, and therefore unpredictable, contingent, and irreplaceable. No one will reconstruct Aeschylus' lost tragedies.

**Exegesis:**
The parenthesis matters. Value is not interchangeable because what is lost is not reproducible by general procedure, only mourned as singular absence.

`tags: value, individuality, irreplaceable`

## §868
> Esperar no entontece fatalmente, si no esperamos en un futuro con mayúscula. Abrigar la esperanza de un nuevo esplendor terrestre no es ilícito, siempre que esperemos un esplendor herido, endeble, mortal. Podemos amar sin culpa lo terrestre, mientras recordemos que amamos una arcilla fugitiva.

**Close:** Hoping does not fatally make one stupid, if we do not hope in a Future with a capital letter. To harbor the hope of a new earthly splendor is not illicit, so long as we expect a splendor wounded, frail, mortal. We can love the earthly without guilt while we remember that we love a fugitive clay.

**Free:** Hope does not fatally stupefy us, provided we do not place it in a capital-F Future. There is nothing illicit in hoping for a new earthly splendor, so long as we expect a splendor that is wounded, fragile, mortal. We may love the earthly without guilt, so long as we remember that what we love is fugitive clay.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the volume's best calibrations of terrestrial hope. The prohibition falls not on hope itself, but on eschatologizing the future into an idol.

`tags: hope, future, earth`

## §875
> La indisolubilidad del pacto entre la Antigüedad y el Cristianismo se patentizó con la rebelión simultánea de la mentalidad moderna contra el espíritu antiguo y contra el espíritu cristiano.

**Close:** The indissolubility of the pact between Antiquity and Christianity became patent with the simultaneous rebellion of the modern mentality against the ancient spirit and against the Christian spirit.

**Free:** The indissoluble pact between Antiquity and Christianity became evident when the modern mentality rebelled simultaneously against the ancient spirit and the Christian spirit.

**Exegesis:**
This treats the modern revolt as double and therefore diagnostic. Its target is not one inheritance but the alliance between classical measure and Christian transcendence.

`tags: Antiquity, Christianity, modernity`

## §879
> Ser cristiano no es profesar una falsa humildad. Es confesar que todo lo eximio es gratuito.

**Close:** To be Christian is not to profess a false humility. It is to confess that everything excellent is gratuitous.

**Free:** To be Christian is not to put on false humility, but to confess that whatever is excellent is sheer gift.

**Exegesis:**
The turn from humility to gratuity matters. Christianity is not self-belittlement but acknowledgment that excellence comes as gift rather than possession.

`tags: Christianity, humility, gratuity`
