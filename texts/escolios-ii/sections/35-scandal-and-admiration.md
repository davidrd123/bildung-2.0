# Section 35: Scandal and Admiration (§781-800, pp. 139-142)

The next pages keep moving between disgust and measure: what educates is not self-expression but discipline by the dead, and what remains credible is whatever still scandalizes.

---

## §781
> El moderno de ayer tan sólo creía en la idoneidad de una definición naturalista del hombre. El moderno de hoy la siente ya idónea.

**Close:** Yesterday's modern merely believed in the suitability of a naturalistic definition of man. Today's modern already feels it suitable.

**Free:** The modern of yesterday merely believed a naturalistic definition of man was adequate; the modern of today already feels it as adequate.

`tags: modernity, naturalism, feeling`

---

## §782
> Una existencia colmada es aquella que entrega al sepulcro, después de largos años, un adolescente que la vida no envileció.

**Close:** A fulfilled existence is one that delivers to the tomb, after long years, an adolescent whom life did not debase.

**Free:** A fulfilled life is one that, after many years, still hands the grave an adolescent whom life did not debase.

`tags: existence, adolescence, debasement`

---

## §783
> La experiencia del hombre que "ha vivido mucho" suele reducirse a unas anécdotas triviales con que adorna una imbecilidad incurable.

**Close:** The experience of the man who "has lived much" usually reduces itself to a few trivial anecdotes with which he adorns an incurable imbecility.

**Free:** The experience of the man who "has lived a lot" usually shrinks to a few trivial anecdotes with which he decorates an incurable stupidity.

`tags: experience, anecdote, stupidity`

---

## §784
> Temblemos si no sentimos, en este abyecto mundo moderno, que el prójimo, cada día, es menos nuestro semejante.

**Close:** Let us tremble if we do not feel, in this abject modern world, that the neighbor each day is less our fellow.

**Free:** Let us tremble if, in this abject modern world, we do not feel that our neighbor is becoming less and less our fellow creature.

**Exegesis:**
The sentence treats moral numbness itself as the emergency. The danger is not merely social cruelty, but losing even the ability to register the diminishing resemblance between ourselves and the neighbor.

`tags: neighbor, resemblance, modernity`

---

## §785
> Cuando la libertad deja de ser sumisión a los más altos valores de la época, para convertirse en derecho de expresar nuestra individualidad insignificante, más vale la disciplina del cuartel socialista.

**Close:** When liberty ceases to be submission to the highest values of the epoch in order to become the right to express our insignificant individuality, the discipline of the socialist barracks is preferable.

**Free:** When freedom stops meaning submission to an age's highest values and becomes the right to express our insignificant individuality, the discipline of the socialist barracks is almost preferable.

**Exegesis:**
This is not praise of barracks discipline. It is a brutal contrast meant to humiliate a degraded notion of freedom that has collapsed into the sanctification of trivial self-expression.

`tags: freedom, individuality, discipline`

---

## §786
> Observar la vida es demasiado interesante para perder el tiempo viviéndola.

**Close:** To observe life is too interesting to waste time living it.

**Free:** Life is too interesting to observe for us to waste all our time merely living it.

`tags: life, observation, irony`

## §787
> No es enfrentándola a la vida como podemos sacarle filo a nuestra inteligencia, sino enfrentándola a la inteligencia de los grandes muertos.

**Close:** It is not by confronting it with life that we can put an edge on our intelligence, but by confronting it with the intelligence of the great dead.

**Free:** We sharpen our intelligence not by throwing it against life, but by throwing it against the intelligence of the great dead.

`tags: intelligence, dead, education`

## §788
> El hombre cultivado no es el que anda cargado de contestaciones, sino el que es capaz de preguntas.

**Close:** The cultivated man is not the one who goes around loaded with answers, but the one who is capable of questions.

**Free:** The cultivated man is not the one weighed down with answers, but the one capable of questions.

`tags: cultivation, question, answer`

## §789
> Comprender no es rozar la frase, sino asirla. Para mayor claridad, debe ortografiarse comprehender.

**Close:** To understand is not to graze the phrase, but to seize it. For greater clarity, it ought to be spelled *comprehend*.

**Free:** Understanding is not brushing against a sentence, but grasping it. For clarity's sake, the word ought to be spelled *comprehender*.

**Exegesis:**
The joke is philological and real at once. To understand is not verbal contact but possession, taking hold.

`tags: understanding, phrase, grasp`

## §790
> La importancia de un acontecimiento es inversamente proporcional al espacio que le dedican los periódicos.

**Close:** The importance of an event is inversely proportional to the space newspapers devote to it.

**Free:** The importance of an event is inversely proportional to the amount of newspaper space it receives.

`tags: event, newspaper, importance`

## §791
> La actitud de los que recusan la historicidad de Jesús es semejante a la de los padres de la tradición evangélica. El personaje les pareció a ambos tan extraño, que aquellos, al tropezar con él en un texto, negaron su existencia, y éstos, al conocerlo en carne y hueso, proclamaron su divinidad.

**Close:** The attitude of those who reject the historicity of Jesus resembles that of the fathers of the evangelical tradition. The personage seemed to both so strange that those, upon stumbling over him in a text, denied his existence, while these, upon knowing him in flesh and blood, proclaimed his divinity.

**Free:** Those who deny the historicity of Jesus stand in an oddly similar relation to him as the fathers of the evangelical tradition. He seemed so strange to both that the former, meeting him in a text, denied he had existed, while the latter, meeting him in the flesh, proclaimed his divinity.

`tags: Jesus, historicity, Gospel`

## §792
> Sólo anunciando la inminencia del escatón se evita que la prédica escatológica degenere en progresismo soso. La ambigüedad condiciona el acierto.

**Close:** Only by announcing the imminence of the eschaton does one avoid escatological preaching degenerating into insipid progressivism. Ambiguity conditions rightness.

**Free:** Only by announcing the nearness of the eschaton can eschatological preaching be kept from degenerating into bland progressivism. Ambiguity is a condition of accuracy.

**Exegesis:**
The aphorism protects eschatology from domestication. Remove imminence, and the thing dissolves into one more program of gradual improvement.

`tags: eschatology, progressivism, ambiguity`

---

## §793
> La historia se emancipa al fin, como las ciencias, cuando renuncia a buscar "causas". La búsqueda del "por qué", en historia como en física, esconde metafísicas vergonzantes.

**Close:** History finally emancipates itself, like the sciences, when it renounces searching for "causes." The search for the "why," in history as in physics, hides shamefaced metaphysics.

**Free:** History comes into its own, like the sciences, when it gives up hunting for "causes." The search for the "why," in history no less than in physics, hides embarrassed metaphysics.

`tags: history, cause, metaphysics`

## §794
> El lector contemporáneo sonríe cuando el cronista medieval habla de "paladines romanos", pero se queda serio cuando el marxista diserta sobre la "burguesía griega" o el "feudalismo americano".

**Close:** The contemporary reader smiles when the medieval chronicler speaks of "Roman paladins," but remains serious when the Marxist dissertates on the "Greek bourgeoisie" or on "American feudalism."

**Free:** The modern reader smiles at the medieval chronicler speaking of "Roman paladins," yet keeps a straight face when the Marxist lectures on the "Greek bourgeoisie" or "American feudalism."

`tags: anachronism, Marxism, reader`

## §795
> Toda obra importante nos hace recorrer tres etapas: admiración, desilusión, admiración.

**Close:** Every important work makes us traverse three stages: admiration, disillusionment, admiration.

**Free:** Every important work makes us pass through three stages: admiration, disillusion, admiration again.

`tags: work, admiration, disillusion`

## §796
> Irrespetar la individualidad es el objeto de la educación. Del olvido de verdad tan obvia proviene, en parte, la crápula moderna.

**Close:** To disrespect individuality is the object of education. From forgetting so obvious a truth there comes, in part, modern debauchery.

**Free:** The object of education is to disrespect individuality. Part of modern dissolution comes from forgetting so obvious a truth.

**Exegesis:**
The target is not personhood but self-enclosure. Education exists to break the idol of private singularity, not to flatter it.

`tags: education, individuality, modernity`

---

## §797
> Cada día me parece menos verosímil que las exigencias de la razón coincidan con la esencia del universo. No confío en lo que no sea escándalo.

**Close:** Each day it seems to me less plausible that the demands of reason coincide with the essence of the universe. I do not trust anything that is not scandal.

**Free:** Each day it seems less plausible to me that the demands of reason coincide with the universe's essence. I trust nothing that is not scandal.

**Exegesis:**
The second sentence explains the first. He trusts what interrupts our demand for congruence, not what too easily satisfies it.

`tags: reason, universe, scandal`

**Close:** The intelligent man usually fails because he does not dare to believe in the true size of human stupidity.

**Free:** Intelligent men often fail because they do not dare to believe how large human stupidity really is.

> El proletariado tiende hacia la vida burguesa, como los cuerpos hacia el centro de la tierra.

**Close:** The proletariat tends toward bourgeois life, like bodies toward the center of the earth.

**Free:** The proletariat tends toward bourgeois life the way bodies fall toward the center of the earth.
