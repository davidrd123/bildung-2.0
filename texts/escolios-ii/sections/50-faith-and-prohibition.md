# Section 50: Faith and Prohibition (§1081-1100, pp. 188-190)

Writing, faith, taboo, memory, and discipline keep crossing here until the closing line relocates conflict from the battlefield to the soul.

---

## §1081
> Para tener talento, Beaumarchais tampoco hizo más que darse el trabajo de nacer.

**Close:** In order to have talent, Beaumarchais did no more either than take the trouble to be born.

**Free:** To have talent, Beaumarchais too did nothing but take the trouble to be born.

`tags: talent, Beaumarchais, birth`

---

## §1082
> El pueblo, al cabo de unos años, olvidaría el nombre de los demagogos ilustres, si sus sucesores no obligaran al contribuyente a costearles ritos conmemoratorios. La memoria popular sólo hospeda nombres de reyes.

**Close:** The people, after a few years, would forget the name of illustrious demagogues, if their successors did not oblige the taxpayer to finance commemorative rites for them. Popular memory lodges only the names of kings.

**Free:** After a few years the people would forget the names of illustrious demagogues if their successors did not force taxpayers to pay for commemorative rites. Popular memory houses only the names of kings.

**Exegesis:**
What endures in popular memory is not whoever shouts loudest, but whoever bore a form sturdy enough to survive propaganda's upkeep.

`tags: memory, demagogues, kings`

## §1083
> Nunca hay demasiados escritores, sino demasiada gente que escribe.

**Close:** There are never too many writers, but too many people who write.

**Free:** There are never too many writers; there are only too many people writing.

`tags: writers, quantity, distinction`

## §1084
> Las soluciones que el hombre encuentra resultan siempre menos interesantes que los problemas. Las únicas soluciones interesantes son las que Dios se reserva.

**Close:** The solutions that man finds always turn out less interesting than the problems. The only interesting solutions are those that God reserves to himself.

**Free:** The solutions man discovers always prove less interesting than the problems. The only truly interesting solutions are the ones God keeps to himself.

**Exegesis:**
The aphorism is not anti-intellectual. It demotes closure and re-privileges depth, reserve, and the part of reality that refuses to become manageable.

`tags: problems, solutions, God`

---

## §1085
> El resorte de la historia de la filosofía no es la esperanza de descifrar el enigma, sino el empeño de lograr una precisión creciente en el análisis de los problemas.

**Close:** The spring of the history of philosophy is not the hope of deciphering the enigma, but the effort to achieve a growing precision in the analysis of problems.

**Free:** What drives the history of philosophy is not the hope of solving the enigma, but the effort to gain ever greater precision in analyzing the problems.

**Exegesis:**
This is close to a programmatic sentence for the project as a whole. Precision in the problem matters more than theatrical claims of final solution.

`tags: philosophy, problems, precision`

## §1086
> Un poco de polvo sobre un texto ahuyenta al lector común. Como si un soplo de inteligencia no dejara el mármol limpio.

**Close:** A little dust upon a text frightens off the common reader. As if a breath of intelligence did not leave the marble clean.

**Free:** A little dust on a text scares off the common reader, as if a breath of intelligence could not leave the marble clean.

`tags: texts, readers, intelligence`

## §1087
> Sólo la fe permite raciocinios que no dependen de postulados. Que sólo prolongan evidencias.

**Close:** Only faith permits reasonings that do not depend on postulates. That only prolong evidences.

**Free:** Only faith permits lines of reasoning that do not rest on postulates, but simply prolong evidences.

**Exegesis:**
The claim is compressed almost to the point of fracture. Faith is presented not as irrational leap against evidence, but as the condition for carrying evidence farther than explicit postulational systems can.

`tags: faith, reasoning, evidence`

## §1092
> El talento del escritor no está en describir un personaje, un paisaje, una escena, sino en hacernos creer que lo hizo.

**Close:** The writer's talent does not lie in describing a character, a landscape, a scene, but in making us believe that he did it.

**Free:** A writer's talent does not lie in describing a character, a landscape, or a scene, but in making us believe he has done so.

`tags: writer, talent, illusion`

## §1093
> Petrarca es el padre del intelectual. Y de quienes intentamos no serlo.

**Close:** Petrarch is the father of the intellectual. And of those of us who try not to be one.

**Free:** Petrarch is the father of the intellectual, and of those of us who try not to become one.

**Exegesis:**
The joke works because it denies any clean escape. Even the refusal of the intellectual type remains genealogically implicated in it.

`tags: Petrarch, intellectuals, genealogy`

## §1094

**Close:** The stupidities that a genius says barely shadow the relief of his texts. But the fool who repeats them dies crushed under their weight.

**Free:** The foolish things a genius says only lightly shade the relief of his texts. The fool who repeats them dies crushed beneath them.

`tags: genius, stupidity, repetition`

## §1098
> Humanidad es el rango a que asciende el animal que acata prohibiciones.

**Close:** Humanity is the rank to which the animal ascends that obeys prohibitions.

**Free:** Humanity is the rank attained by the animal that obeys prohibitions.

`tags: humanity, rank, prohibition`

## §1099
> La importancia de la moral está menos en lo que prohibe que en el hecho mismo de prohibir.

**Close:** The importance of morality lies less in what it prohibits than in the very fact of prohibiting.

**Free:** Morality matters less for what it prohibits than for the simple fact that it prohibits.

**Exegesis:**
The form of moral limit matters prior to its specific content. Prohibition itself is treated as one of the conditions by which the merely animal is made human.

`tags: morality, prohibition, form`

**Free:** We should not praise war between men, but the war within man.
