# Section 30: Justice and Aggiornamento (§681-700, pp. 123-126)

The pressure here falls on political and theological vocabulary at once: justice, demagogy, poetry, prophecy, and ecclesial adaptation are all tested for whether they still answer to transcendence or merely to fashion.

---

## §681
> Por justicia social se entiende dar a cada cual lo que no es suyo.
> Alienum cuique tribuere.

**Close:** By social justice is understood giving to each what is not his.
*Alienum cuique tribuere.*

**Free:** "Social justice" comes to mean giving each person what is not his.
*Alienum cuique tribuere.*

**Exegesis:**
The aphorism is built as an anti-definition. By inverting the Roman formula, it treats the modern phrase not as fulfillment of justice but as its deliberate corruption.

`tags: justice, social-justice, inversion`

## §684
> No es justo exigirle a una burguesía amenazada que no tenga miedo.
> Podemos pedirle, en cambio, que pierda el miedo de tenerlo.

**Close:** It is not just to demand of a threatened bourgeoisie that it have no fear.
We can ask of it, instead, that it lose the fear of having it.

**Free:** It is unjust to demand that a threatened bourgeoisie feel no fear.
What we may ask instead is that it cease being ashamed of being afraid.

`tags: bourgeoisie, fear, justice`

## §687
> No apelar a Dios, sino a su justicia, nos lleva fatalmente a emplazarlo ante el tribunal de nuestros prejuicios.

**Close:** To appeal not to God but to his justice leads us fatally to summon him before the tribunal of our prejudices.

**Free:** When we appeal not to God but to his justice, we end by hauling him before the tribunal of our own prejudices.

**Exegesis:**
The line rejects the tactic of saving divine seriousness by translating it into one moral attribute. Once God is approached chiefly through "justice," he is made answerable to our prior standards.

`tags: God, justice, prejudice`

## §689
> Enemigo del rey-dios como del demos-dios, el cristianismo no debe celebrar ni la apoteosis del césar, ni la apoteosis de la plebe.

**Close:** Enemy of the king-god as of the demos-god, Christianity ought to celebrate neither the apotheosis of Caesar nor the apotheosis of the plebs.

**Free:** As enemy alike of the god-king and the god-people, Christianity should celebrate neither the apotheosis of Caesar nor that of the crowd.

**Exegesis:**
This is a precise anti-idolatrous political claim. Christianity is placed against both vertical and horizontal idolatries when either claims divinity for political form.

`tags: Christianity, Caesar, plebs`

---

## §690
> De Dios no se habla con alguna exactitud y seriedad sino en poesía.

**Close:** Of God one speaks with any exactness and seriousness only in poetry.

**Free:** One speaks of God with any real exactness and seriousness only in poetry.

**Exegesis:**
The aphorism is not romantic inflation. It says that seriousness about God requires a language more exacting than discursive prose usually permits.

`tags: God, poetry, exactness`

## §694
> La humanidad no necesita al cristianismo para construir el futuro, sino para poder afrontarlo.

**Close:** Humanity does not need Christianity in order to build the future, but in order to be able to face it.

**Free:** Humanity does not need Christianity to build the future, but to be able to face it.

**Exegesis:**
The sentence strips Christianity of programmatic prestige without stripping it of necessity. Its role is not to engineer tomorrow but to make confrontation with tomorrow humanly possible.

`tags: Christianity, future, courage`

## §696
> La advertencia paulina de "permanecer en el estado a que fuimos llamados" encrespa al tonto, para quien la escatología consiste en la mudanza de la humanidad de un barrio a otro barrio, a través de una universidad.

**Close:** The Pauline warning to "remain in the state to which we were called" bristles the fool, for whom eschatology consists in the moving of humanity from one neighborhood to another, by way of a university.

**Free:** Paul's warning to "remain in the state to which we were called" irritates the fool, for whom eschatology consists in moving humanity from one neighborhood to another by way of a university.

**Exegesis:**
The target is not learning but redemptive administration. Eschatology becomes ridiculous when it is imagined as a managed relocation project for the species.

`tags: Paul, eschatology, university`

## §700
> En lugar de una teología del cuerpo místico, los teólogos actuales enseñan una teología de la masa mística.

**Close:** In place of a theology of the mystical body, current theologians teach a theology of the mystical mass.

**Free:** In place of a theology of the mystical body, today's theologians teach a theology of the mystical crowd.

**Exegesis:**
The contrast is not merely witty. It names a shift from organic and sacramental order toward sheer aggregated quantity under religious language.

`tags: theology, mystical-body, mass`
