# Section 54: Historicity and Judgment (§1161-1180, pp. 200-203)

This pocket is unusually tight: historicity, judgment, hierarchy, and ridicule are all pressed into one argument about how history is to be seen without being flattened.

---

## §1161
> El historiador progresista y demócrata vierte en los odres multiformes de la historia el mismo líquido incoloro, inodoro, e insípido.

**Close:** The progressive and democratic historian pours into the multiform wineskins of history the same colorless, odorless, and tasteless liquid.

**Free:** The progressive democratic historian pours the same colorless, odorless, tasteless liquid into history's many-shaped skins.

`tags: historian, democracy, flattening`

---

## §1162
> La historicidad no es evolución, ni dialéctica, ni progreso. Ni germen que crece, ni aproximación a una meta. La historicidad no es definible. Meramente ejemplarizable.

**Close:** Historicity is not evolution, nor dialectic, nor progress. Neither a germ that grows, nor approximation to a goal. Historicity is not definable. Merely exemplifiable.

**Free:** Historicity is neither evolution nor dialectic nor progress. It is neither a growing germ nor an approach to a goal. Historicity cannot be defined, only exemplified.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the section's governing refusals. Historicity is denied every developmental formula that would make it manageable in advance.

`tags: historicity, progress, example`

---

## §1163
> Para el individualismo doctrinario, la ética es iusnaturalista o sociológica. Para el individualismo historicista, la ética no es ni absolutista, ni relativista. Ni lo primero, porque la percepción del valor es relativa a situaciones concretas. Ni lo segundo, porque desde cada situación se percibe un absoluto.

**Close:** For doctrinaire individualism, ethics is natural-law or sociological. For historicist individualism, ethics is neither absolutist nor relativist. Not the former, because the perception of value is relative to concrete situations. Not the latter, because from each situation an absolute is perceived.

**Free:** For doctrinaire individualism, ethics must be either natural-law or sociological. For historicist individualism, ethics is neither absolutist nor relativist: not the first, because value is perceived in concrete situations; not the second, because from each situation one perceives an absolute.

**Exegesis:**
The contrast is the crucial thing here. History does not abolish the absolute; it alters the situation from which it is seen.

`tags: ethics, individualism, historicism`

## §1166
> La historia coordina paradójicamente las coordenadas de proposiciones espacio-temporales con las coordenadas absolutas del juicio.

**Close:** History coordinates paradoxically the coordinates of space-temporal propositions with the absolute coordinates of judgment.

**Free:** History paradoxically coordinates the coordinates of space-time propositions with the absolute coordinates of judgment.

**Exegesis:**
History is neither pure relativity nor pure timelessness here, but the paradoxical coordination of both.

## §1178
> Que las "civilizaciones sean mortales" es el mayor consuelo del que hoy vive.

**Close:** That "civilizations are mortal" is the greatest consolation of the man who lives today.

**Free:** The thought that civilizations are mortal is today's man's greatest consolation.

**Exegesis:**
The line is less historical observation than relief-structure. Civilizational mortality becomes consolation precisely for someone living under a civilization he cannot honor.

`tags: civilization, mortality, consolation`
