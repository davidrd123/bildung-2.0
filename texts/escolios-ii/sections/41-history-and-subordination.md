# Section 41: History and Subordination (§901-920, pp. 159-162)

These pages keep tightening the relation between value and history: values are discoveries, not inventions; history has center rather than route; and politics keeps turning means into ends.

---

## §901
> "Anarquía feudal" es el apodo con que el terrorismo democrático denigra el único período de libertad concreta que conozca la historia.

**Close:** "Feudal anarchy" is the nickname with which democratic terrorism denigrates the only period of concrete liberty that history may know.

**Free:** "Feudal anarchy" is the label democratic terrorism uses to denigrate the only period of concrete liberty history may have known.

`tags: feudalism, liberty, democracy`

---

## §902
> Los valores no son indemostrables porque sean postulados, sino porque son presentaciones. No tienen fecha de invención como las ideas, sino de descubrimiento como los continentes. La axiología es un conocimiento empírico, histórico, y particular, de absolutos.

**Close:** Values are not indemonstrable because they are postulates, but because they are presentations. They do not have a date of invention like ideas, but of discovery like continents. Axiology is an empirical, historical, and particular knowledge of absolutes.

**Free:** Values are not indemonstrable because they are postulates, but because they are presences. They have not a date of invention, like ideas, but of discovery, like continents. Axiology is an empirical, historical, and particular knowledge of absolutes.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the clearest statements yet that values belong to encounter rather than construction. They are discovered historically without therefore becoming relative.

`tags: values, axiology, discovery`

## §904
> No es en hacer coincidir nuestras evidencias en lo que debemos empeñarnos, sino en subordinarlas.

**Close:** It is not in making our evidences coincide that we ought to strive, but in subordinating them.

**Free:** We should strive not to make our evidences coincide, but to subordinate them.

**Exegesis:**
This is a major methodological line. The task is not flattening plurality into agreement, but learning rank among evidences.

`tags: evidence, subordination, method`

## §906
> La escatología hebrea y la profana han sido históricas. La cristiana, en cambio, jamás lo fue. Aún la impaciencia escatológica de la generación apostólica no esperaba la plenitud de la historia, sino la clausura apocalíptica del tiempo.

**Close:** Hebrew and profane eschatology have been historical. Christian eschatology, by contrast, never was. Even the eschatological impatience of the apostolic generation did not await the fullness of history, but the apocalyptic closure of time.

**Free:** Hebrew and profane eschatologies have been historical; the Christian one never truly was. Even the apostolic generation's impatience did not await history's fulfillment, but time's apocalyptic closure.

`tags: eschatology, history, time`

## §908
> Si la historia tuviera sentido, la Encarnación sobraría.

**Close:** If history had meaning, the Incarnation would be superfluous.

**Free:** If history already had meaning in itself, the Incarnation would be unnecessary.

**Exegesis:**
The point is drastic and theological. Meaning is not read off history's own logic; if it were, incarnation would add nothing essential.

`tags: history, Incarnation, meaning`

## §914
> Las ideologías optimistas comienzan fusilando por amor. Para sanar a la humanidad. Y terminan fusilando por rencor. Porque la humanidad resulta insanable

**Close:** Optimistic ideologies begin by shooting out of love. To heal humanity. And they end by shooting out of rancor. Because humanity turns out unhealable.

**Free:** Optimistic ideologies begin by shooting out of love, to heal humanity. They end by shooting out of rancor, because humanity proves incurable.

**Exegesis:**
The aphorism exposes the continuity between benevolent coercion and punitive cruelty. The motive darkens, but the gun remains.

`tags: ideology, violence, optimism`
