# Section 23: Vigency and Partiality (§546-557, pp. 101-102)

This cluster keeps asking what survives fashion: inherited books, unfashionable passions, punctuated speech, named faith, and the admitted limits of any honest standpoint.

---

## §546
> Cada generación sólo entiende unos pocos libros de la
> biblioteca que hereda.

**Close:** Each generation understands only a few books from the library it inherits.

**Free:** Every generation really understands only a small handful of the books it inherits.

`tags: generation, books, inheritance`

## §547
> El historiador marxista, para mayor tranquilidad, debe
> omitir cautelosamente de su bibliografía los libros del
> historiador serio.

**Close:** The Marxist historian, for greater tranquility, must cautiously omit from his bibliography the books of the serious historian.

**Free:** For the sake of his own peace, the Marxist historian must carefully leave the serious historian out of his bibliography.

`tags: Marxism, historian, bibliography`

## §548
> Las doctrinas se defienden mejor contra argumentos
> contundentes que contra el más leve desdén.

**Close:** Doctrines defend themselves better against forceful arguments than against the slightest disdain.

**Free:** Doctrines can survive strong arguments more easily than the faintest note of disdain.

`tags: doctrine, argument, disdain`

---

## §549
> Religión y ciencia no deben firmar pactos de límites, sino
> tratado de desconocimiento recíproco.

**Close:** Religion and science ought not to sign pacts of limits, but a treaty of reciprocal unknowing.

**Free:** Religion and science should not negotiate border treaties, but accept a treaty of mutual non-knowledge.

**Exegesis:**
The line refuses both easy harmony and simple jurisdictional division. What is being proposed is not peace through delimitation, but discipline through acknowledgment that each leaves part of the other genuinely unknown.

`tags: religion, science, limits`

---

## §550
> Sólo los problemas de su tiempo le parecen importantes
> al tonto.

**Close:** Only the problems of his time seem important to the fool.

**Free:** To the fool, only the problems of his own time seem important.

`tags: time, fool, importance`

## §551
> Educar es enseñar a apasionarse por lo que carece de
> vigencia.

**Close:** To educate is to teach one to become passionate about what lacks vigency.

**Free:** Education is teaching someone to care passionately for what no longer has currency.

**Exegesis:**
The point is not antiquarianism. What lacks vigency here is precisely what market time, fashion, and public urgency no longer ratify, but what may still form a life.

`tags: education, vigency, passion`

## §552
> A lo más que puede aspirar el hombre que se conoce es a
> ser lo menos repugnante posible.

**Close:** At most, the man who knows himself can aspire to be as little repugnant as possible.

**Free:** The most a self-knowing man can hope for is to become as little repugnant as possible.

`tags: self-knowledge, humility, repugnance`

## §553
> La frase que no se puntúa a sí misma es informe.

**Close:** The sentence that does not punctuate itself is formless.

**Free:** A sentence that cannot punctuate itself has no form.

`tags: sentence, punctuation, form`

## §554
> Los fervientes del ecumenismo olvidan que ser cristiano no
> consiste sólo en tener fe en Dios, sino en tener fe en el Dios en
> que se debe tener fe.

**Close:** The fervent supporters of ecumenism forget that being Christian consists not only in having faith in God, but in having faith in the God in whom one ought to have faith.

**Free:** Ecumenical enthusiasts forget that Christianity means not only believing in God, but believing in the God one ought to believe in.

**Exegesis:**
Whatever one makes of the claim, its structure is clear: the issue is not bare theism but determinate confession. Generic religious agreement is treated as an evasion of the concrete God Christianity names.

`tags: ecumenism, Christianity, confession`

## §555
> El sociólogo expresa, con número estupendo de decimales,
> hechos cuyas unidades no logra contar en unos casos y no
> logra, en otros, ni siquiera definir.

**Close:** The sociologist expresses, with a stupendous number of decimals, facts whose units in some cases he cannot count and in others cannot even define.

**Free:** The sociologist reports, with an impressive number of decimals, facts whose units he sometimes cannot count and sometimes cannot even define.

`tags: sociology, quantification, definition`

## §556
> Postulado básico de la democracia: la ley es la conciencia
> del ciudadano.

**Close:** Basic postulate of democracy: the law is the conscience of the citizen.

**Free:** Democracy's basic postulate is that the law serves as the citizen's conscience.

`tags: democracy, law, conscience`

## §557
> El individualismo es una posición de repliegue, desde la
> cual renovar el combate es siempre posible. Pero no es posición
> cuya conquista asegure la posesión venturosa y pacífica del
> territorio humano.

**Close:** Individualism is a position of withdrawal, from which it is always possible to renew the combat. But it is not a position whose conquest assures the fortunate and peaceful possession of human territory.

**Free:** Individualism is a fallback position from which the fight can always be renewed, but it is not a position whose capture secures the peaceful and fortunate possession of human ground.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the more careful political lines in the run. Individualism is granted a tactical dignity as retreat, but denied the status of a final homeland.

`tags: individualism, retreat, combat`
