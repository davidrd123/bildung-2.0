# Section 16: Tragedy, Mystery, and Order (§361-390, pp. 70-75)

The pressure here shifts from public polemic to underlying structure: tragedy, mystery, value, and the forms of order a rationalized world can no longer recognize.

---

## §361
> La historia debe relatarse como tragedia, no como
> desacierto.

**Close:** History must be narrated as tragedy, not as blunder.

**Free:** History should be told as tragedy, not as a series of mistakes.

**Exegesis:**
This is a compact refusal of the modern moralizing habit that treats the past as error waiting to be corrected. Tragedy preserves seriousness where the language of blunder trivializes.

`tags: history, tragedy, error`

---

## §364
> Donde no haya intención no hay estructura, sino hecho.
> Toda estructura es la suma de condiciones axiológicas
> formales a priori de un propósito.

**Close:** Where there is no intention there is no structure, but fact. Every structure is the sum of the formal axiological conditions *a priori* of a purpose.

**Free:** Without intention there is no structure, only brute fact. Structure is the set of formal value-conditions that make a purpose possible in advance.

`tags: structure, intention, value`

## §365
> Verificar una proposición sólo sería posible si de una
> proposición falsa no pudiesen deducirse consecuencias
> verdaderas.

**Close:** Verifying a proposition would only be possible if true consequences could not be deduced from a false proposition.

**Free:** A proposition could be verified only if false premises never yielded true consequences.

`tags: verification, logic, truth`

## §366
> Devolvamos a la noche la positividad que le niega
> nuestra astronomía insuficiente. Nuestra más urgente tarea
> es la de reconstruir el misterio del mundo.

**Close:** Let us restore to the night the positivity that our insufficient astronomy denies it. Our most urgent task is to reconstruct the mystery of the world.

**Free:** We must return to night the positive reality our thin astronomy refuses it. Our most urgent task is to rebuild the world's mystery.

**Exegesis:**
This is not anti-science in the cheap sense. It says that a merely explanatory astronomy can describe the night and still fail to acknowledge what the night positively is.

`tags: night, mystery, astronomy`

## §368
> La pesquisa de estructuras confunde validez y vigencia.
> La serie de configuraciones empíricas de una estructura con
> la estructura a priori que las sustenta. Juristas,
> economistas, sociólogos, etc., proceden como si la lógica,
> verbigracia, fuese un capítulo de psicología.

**Close:** The search for structures confuses validity and force in effect. It confuses the series of empirical configurations of a structure with the *a priori* structure that sustains them. Jurists, economists, sociologists, etc., proceed as if logic, for example, were a chapter of psychology.

**Free:** Structural inquiry keeps confusing validity with historical currency, and empirical configurations with the *a priori* structure beneath them. Jurists, economists, and sociologists proceed as if logic itself were just another branch of psychology.

**Exegesis:**
The complaint is not against structure as such, but against flattening levels. Once the sustaining form is confused with its empirical episodes, everything becomes sociology.

`tags: structure, validity, sociology`

## §372
> La "sociedad racional" no se opone a la "tradicional",
> como lo coherente a lo ilógico. Sino como lo animal a lo
> humano. "Racional", en este contexto, significa ahitarse sin
> decencia y copular sin trabas.

**Close:** "Rational society" is not opposed to the "traditional" as the coherent to the illogical, but as the animal to the human. "Rational," in this context, means to glut oneself without decency and copulate without restraints.

**Free:** The so-called rational society is not the coherent opposite of the traditional one, but its animal opposite. Here "rational" means gorging without decency and coupling without restraint.

**Exegesis:**
He is not using "rational" neutrally. The target is a rationalization that strips away the human form of conduct while congratulating itself on coherence.

`tags: rational-society, tradition, animality`

## §373
> La ejecución de un acto cualquiera, en una sociedad
> "tradicional", está condicionada por la totalidad de
> categorías que la conciencia de esa sociedad reconoce. En
> una sociedad "racional", al contrario, cada acto sólo admite
> las condiciones privativas de la categoría a que pertenece.

**Close:** The execution of any act whatever, in a "traditional" society, is conditioned by the totality of categories recognized by the consciousness of that society. In a "rational" society, on the contrary, each act admits only the conditions proper to the category to which it belongs.

**Free:** In a traditional society, any act is conditioned by the whole field of categories the society recognizes. In a rationalized society, each act is allowed to answer only to the narrow conditions of its own category.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the best concrete definitions in the range of what rationalization destroys: not action itself, but the way every act once remained answerable to a larger human whole.

`tags: traditional-society, rationality, categories`

## §374
> Ojalá resucitaran los "filósofos" del xviii, con su
> ingenio, su sarcasmo, su osadía, para que minaran,
> desmantelaran, demolieran, los "prejuicios" de este siglo.
> Los prejuicios que nos legaron ellos.

**Close:** Would that the "philosophers" of the eighteenth century might resurrect, with their wit, sarcasm, and audacity, so that they might mine, dismantle, and demolish the "prejudices" of this century. The prejudices they bequeathed to us.

**Free:** If only the eighteenth-century "philosophers" would return with their wit, sarcasm, and audacity, so they could demolish this century's prejudices, the very prejudices they left us.

`tags: eighteenth-century, prejudice, inheritance`

## §384
> Sin severo entrenamiento epistemológico, no podemos
> emprender la conquista del derecho a la superstición.

**Close:** Without severe epistemological training, we cannot undertake the conquest of the right to superstition.

**Free:** Without severe epistemological training, we cannot begin to win the right to superstition.

**Exegesis:**
The sentence is funny because it reverses the progressive story. Serious training is required not to escape superstition, but to earn the right to something modern rationality dismisses too easily.

`tags: epistemology, superstition, right`

## §388
> Atribuir a la obra de arte la subjetividad de la
> imaginación que la crea, equivale a confundir proposición
> lógica con juicio psicológico.

**Close:** To attribute to the work of art the subjectivity of the imagination that creates it is equivalent to confusing a logical proposition with a psychological judgment.

**Free:** To attribute to the artwork the subjectivity of the imagination that produced it is like confusing a logical proposition with a psychological judgment.

**Exegesis:**
The work may arise from imagination, but it is not reducible to the maker's subjectivity. Otherwise art collapses into biography.

`tags: art, imagination, subjectivity`
