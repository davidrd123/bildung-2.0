# Section 17: Authenticity and Mystery (§391-420, pp. 75-80)

This stretch keeps opposing two modern reductions at once: authenticity reduced to mere sincerity, and explanation reduced to whatever can survive on the public surface.

---

## §391
> La estética romántica no se equivocó al enseñar que la
> obra de arte es expresión de su autor, pero yerra al tomar
> por criterio de valor la autenticidad de la expresión. El
> valor no depende de la autenticidad de la expresión, sino
> de la calidad de la persona. La sinceridad del imbécil
> carece de importancia.

**Close:** Romantic aesthetics was not mistaken in teaching that the work of art is expression of its author, but it errs in taking the authenticity of the expression as a criterion of value. Value depends not on the authenticity of the expression, but on the quality of the person. The sincerity of the imbecile lacks importance.

**Free:** Romantic aesthetics was right that a work expresses its maker, but wrong to make authenticity of expression the measure of value. Value depends on the quality of the person, not on sincerity alone. The fool's sincerity counts for nothing.

**Exegesis:**
This is a clean attack on a still-current confusion. Authenticity may be real, but it is not yet a criterion. Expression matters only once the person expressed is worth expressing.

`tags: aesthetics, authenticity, sincerity`

**Free:** Physiology and sociology have partitioned psychology between them. Personal life has been abolished, like the Polish state.

`tags: psychology, physiology, sociology`

## §396
> Las instituciones sociales se quebrantan, cuando
> tecnifican su funcionamiento para acrecentar su eficacia. El
> hombre, en efecto, no acata dócilmente sino lo misterioso.
> El terror es, ineludiblemente, el substituto racional de las
> ceremonias irracionales.

**Close:** Social institutions are broken when they technify their functioning in order to increase their efficacy. Man, in effect, docilely obeys only what is mysterious. Terror is, unavoidably, the rational substitute for irrational ceremonies.

**Free:** Social institutions crack when they technicize themselves in the name of efficiency. Man submits docilely only to what still carries mystery; once ritual is rationalized away, terror becomes its replacement.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the strongest institutional claims in the volume. Rational efficiency does not simply improve institutions; it hollows out the symbolic form that made obedience possible without force.

`tags: institutions, mystery, terror`

## §404
> Las fuerzas de la naturaleza son epifanías religiosas,
> pero no divinas. Entre el mundo profano y el mundo divino,
> hay un mundo sagrado.

**Close:** The forces of nature are religious, but not divine, epiphanies. Between the profane world and the divine world, there is a sacred world.

**Free:** Natural forces are religious epiphanies without being divine. Between the profane and the divine there is a sacred middle world.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the clearest statements in the range that sacred presence is neither reducible to bare nature nor identical with God. The middle layer matters.

`tags: sacred, nature, divinity`

## §418
> El universo no resulta de lectura difícil porque sea texto
> hermético, sino porque es texto sin puntuación. Sin la
> entonación adecuada, ascendente o descendente, su sintaxis
> ontológica es ininteligible.

**Close:** The universe is not difficult to read because it is a hermetic text, but because it is a text without punctuation. Without the proper intonation, ascending or descending, its ontological syntax is unintelligible.

**Free:** The universe is hard to read not because it is esoteric, but because it is unpunctuated. Without the right rising or falling intonation, its ontological syntax cannot be understood.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the best hermeneutic images in the volume. Reality is not hidden by secrecy so much as by the difficulty of learning its inflection.

`tags: universe, reading, syntax`

## §419

> públicas nos parecen deliberadas. Hasta que descubrimos,
> asombrados, que cree en ellas.

**Close:** Since the democrat's electoral skill seems to us proof of intelligence, the stupidities of his public declarations seem deliberate to us. Until we discover, astonished, that he believes in them.

**Free:** Because the democrat's electoral skill looks like intelligence, we assume the nonsense in his public statements must be strategic, until we discover with surprise that he actually believes it.

`tags: democracy, intelligence, stupidity`
