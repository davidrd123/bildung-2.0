# Section 10: Sacred Remnant (§201–220, pp. 42–45)

By the end of this run the page has turned toward whatever still survives reduction.

---

## §201
> No es fácil discernir si el periodismo contemporáneo es
> un cínico propósito de lucrarse envileciendo al hombre o un
> apostolado "cultural" de mentes irremediablemente
> incultas.

**Close:** It is not easy to discern whether contemporary journalism is a cynical purpose of profiting by debasing man or a "cultural" apostolate of minds irremediably uncultured.

**Free:** It is hard to tell whether contemporary journalism is cynical profit from debasement or a sincere cultural mission undertaken by the incurably uncultured.

`tags: journalism, culture, debasement`

---

## §202
> La filosofía se vuelve más sensata cuanto más se
> aproxima a la literatura.
> La prosa limpia es el escollo de la especulación
> extravagante.

**Close:** Philosophy becomes more sensible the more it approaches literature. Clean prose is the reef of extravagant speculation.

**Free:** Philosophy grows saner as it moves nearer literature. Clean prose is where extravagant speculation runs aground.

**Exegesis:**
This does not subordinate philosophy to belles-lettres. It says that clean prose disciplines thought by refusing to let speculation hide inside fog.

`tags: philosophy, literature, prose`

---

## §203
> La lucidez, en el siglo xx, tiene por requisito la
> abdicación a la esperanza.

**Close:** Lucidity, in the twentieth century, has as its requisite abdication of hope.

**Free:** In the twentieth century lucidity seems to require renouncing hope.

`tags: lucidity, hope, twentieth-century`

## §204
> Muchos creen que el enunciado lacónico es dogmático y
> estiman la generosidad de una inteligencia proporcional a la
> prolijidad de su prosa.

**Close:** Many believe the laconic statement dogmatic and esteem the generosity of an intelligence proportional to the prolixity of its prose.

**Free:** Many take brevity for dogmatism and measure an intelligence's generosity by how long-winded its prose is.

`tags: brevity, dogmatism, prose`

## §205
> El individuo tiene que pensar en el idioma de una
> tradición filosófica, así como tiene que expresarse en el
> idioma de una colectividad lingüística.
> La originalidad sólo depende del sesgo del espíritu.

**Close:** The individual has to think in the language of a philosophical tradition, just as he has to express himself in the language of a linguistic collectivity. Originality depends only on the slant of the spirit.

**Free:** The individual thinks within a philosophical idiom just as he speaks within a linguistic one. Originality lies only in the spirit's inflection.

`tags: tradition, originality, language`

---

## §206
> Una cultura muere cuando nadie sabe en qué consiste, o
> cuando todos creen saberlo.

**Close:** A culture dies when nobody knows what it consists in, or when everyone believes they know.

**Free:** A culture dies either when no one can say what it is or when everyone is sure they can.

**Exegesis:**
This is a very Gómez Dávila sentence: death comes both from loss of consciousness and from glib over-possession. Either opacity or total self-certainty can kill a culture.

`tags: culture, death, self-knowledge`

---

## §207
> El mundo moderno censura con acrimonia a quienes le
> "voltean la espalda a la vida". Como si fuese posible saber
> con certeza que voltearle la espalda a la vida no sea volver
> la cara hacia la luz.

**Close:** The modern world acrimoniously censures those who "turn their back on life." As if it were possible to know with certainty that turning one's back on life might not be turning one's face toward the light.

**Free:** The modern world bitterly reproaches those who "turn their back on life," as though one could know for certain that turning away from life is not actually turning toward light.

`tags: life, light, modern-world`

---

## §208
> Los conflictos sociales, en una sociedad sana, se
> plantean entre sectores funcionales, en una sociedad
> enferma entre estratos económicos.

**Close:** Social conflicts, in a healthy society, arise between functional sectors; in a sick society, between economic strata.

**Free:** In a healthy society social conflict runs between functions; in a sick one it collapses into economic class war.

`tags: social-conflict, healthy-society, economy`

## §209
> Teniendo el hombre en la trastienda del alma un animal
> agazapado, aún una sociedad justa necesitaría protegerse de
> la perversidad humana.
> La coacción social no es consecuencia de la historia
> social, sino de la naturaleza del hombre.

**Close:** Since man keeps in the back room of the soul a crouching animal, even a just society would need to protect itself from human perversity. Social coercion is not a consequence of social history, but of the nature of man.

**Free:** Because a crouching animal remains in the soul's back room, even a just society would still need protections against human perversity. Social coercion comes not from historical accident but from human nature.

`tags: human-nature, coercion, justice`

**Free:** Even in Arcadia one ought to keep an eye on utopian dreamers.

## §211
> "Necesidad histórica" es el nombre del último avatar del
> más sanguinario dios azteca.

**Close:** "Historical necessity" is the name of the latest avatar of the bloodiest Aztec god.

**Free:** "Historical necessity" is only the latest incarnation of the bloodiest Aztec deity.

**Exegesis:**
The line is savage because it treats historical necessity not as sober analysis but as sacrificial cult under a modern label.

`tags: historical-necessity, sacrifice, aztec-god`

## §212
> Si los paleontólogos no yerran al afirmar que el
> hominida apareció hace veinte millones de años, el
> progresista tendrá que esperar aproximadamente otro tanto
> para que el hombre cambie su perversidad vieja por una
> nueva.

**Close:** If paleontologists are not mistaken in affirming that the hominid appeared twenty million years ago, the progressive will have to wait approximately another such amount for man to exchange his old perversity for a new one.

**Free:** If hominids really appeared twenty million years ago, the progressive may need to wait about that long again before man trades one perversity for another.

`tags: progressivism, paleontology, perversity`

## §213
> No acusemos al moderno de haber matado a Dios. Ese
> crimen no está a su alcance.
> Sino de haber matado a los dioses.
> Dios sigue intacto, pero el universo se marchita y se
> pudre porque los dioses subalternos perecieron.

**Close:** Let us not accuse the modern man of having killed God. That crime is not within his reach. But rather of having killed the gods. God remains intact, but the universe withers and rots because the subordinate gods perished.

**Free:** We should not accuse the modern of killing God, which lies beyond his power, but of killing the gods. God remains untouched; the universe withers because the lesser divine presences have died.

**Exegesis:**
This is a major religious turn. The damage modernity does is not to transcendence itself, but to the intermediate texture by which the world remained inhabited and numinous.

`tags: God, gods, disenchantment`

---

## §214
> No valía la pena negar granos de incienso a los altares
> de Augusto, que al fin y al cabo algo tenía de dios, para
> acabar celebrando con ditirambos blasfematorios a la
> muchedumbre, que nada tiene de divino.

**Close:** It was not worth denying grains of incense to the altars of Augustus, who after all had something of a god, only to end by celebrating with blasphemous dithyrambs the multitude, which has nothing divine.

**Free:** It was hardly worth refusing incense to Augustus, who at least had something divine about him, only to wind up singing blasphemous dithyrambs to the crowd, which has nothing divine at all.

`tags: Augustus, multitude, divinity`

---

## §215
> Para sofocar su angustia ante la trascendencia que lo
> invade, el hombre inventa una mitología de larvas
> subconscientes.
> Nada más tranquilizante que reducir a fermentaciones
> internas las evidencias que espantan.

**Close:** In order to suffocate his anguish before the transcendence that invades him, man invents a mythology of subconscious larvae. Nothing is more tranquilizing than reducing to internal fermentations the evidences that terrify.

**Free:** To smother the anguish caused by invading transcendence, man invents a mythology of subconscious larvae. Nothing soothes more than reducing frightening evidence to inner ferment.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the places where anti-reductionism becomes openly religious. Psychologizing the sacred is comforting not because it explains better, but because it domesticates what frightened us.

`tags: transcendence, subconscious, reductionism`

---

## §216
> La verdad del cristianismo no se aloja en su mitología,
> pero está en el mito como la Presencia en la hostia.

**Close:** The truth of Christianity is not lodged in its mythology, but it is in the myth as the Presence in the host.

**Free:** Christianity's truth does not reside merely in its mythology, yet it is present in myth the way Presence is in the host.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the strongest sacramental analogies in the run so far. Myth is neither literal container nor disposable shell. It is the site of presence.

`tags: Christianity, myth, presence`

---

## §217
> Nuestro deber actual no está tanto en obedecer a
> determinadas reglas éticas como en salvar la conciencia de lo
> sagrado.

**Close:** Our present duty lies not so much in obeying certain ethical rules as in saving the consciousness of the sacred.

**Free:** Our task now is less to obey a set of ethical rules than to preserve the sense of the sacred.

**Exegesis:**
This is a true hinge line. Ethics is not denied, but subordinated to a more prior task: preventing the disappearance of sacred consciousness itself.

`tags: sacred, duty, ethics`
