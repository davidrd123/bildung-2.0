# Section 32: Imagination and Survival (§721-740, pp. 130-132)

This range moves faster: intelligence, myth, love, history, and transcendence keep being tested for whether they can survive flattening without becoming slogans.

## §721
> Ciencia recién adquirida y zapatos recién comprados crujen.

**Close:** Newly acquired knowledge and newly bought shoes creak.

**Free:** Freshly acquired learning creaks like newly bought shoes.

`tags: knowledge, novelty, creak`

`tags: imagination, epoch, repertoire`

## §723
> Las sociedades igualitarias estrangulan la imaginación, para ni siquiera satisfacer la envidia.

**Close:** Egalitarian societies strangle the imagination, only not even to satisfy envy.

**Free:** Egalitarian societies strangle the imagination, and not even in a way that truly satisfies envy.

`tags: equality, imagination, envy`

## §724
> El mito es el modo único de expresar verdades simples.

**Close:** Myth is the sole mode of expressing simple truths.

**Free:** Myth is the only way simple truths can be expressed.

**Exegesis:**
The line reverses the usual prestige order. Myth is not what obscures simplicity, but what alone can carry it without flattening it.

`tags: myth, truth, simplicity`

**Free:** What sometimes makes us feel superior is not our knowledge, but the quality of our ignorance when measured against someone else's knowledge.

## §728
> El amor es transmutación del campo erótico, que se produce cuando hay desequilibrio entre sus polos.
> Entre iguales sólo hay cópula.

**Close:** Love is a transmutation of the erotic field that occurs when there is disequilibrium between its poles.
Among equals there is only copulation.

**Free:** Love is the transmutation of the erotic field that occurs when there is an imbalance between its poles.
Among equals there is only copulation.

**Exegesis:**
The aphorism is not praising domination. It is insisting that love begins where the erotic field is transformed into something irreducible to symmetrical exchange.

`tags: love, eroticism, transmutation`

## §732
> No existe texto sinóptico que la crítica radical no haya atribuido alguna vez a la primera generación cristiana, como proyección retrospectiva de su fe.
> Salvo los que implican una escatología inminente.

**Close:** There exists no synoptic text that radical criticism has not at some time attributed to the first Christian generation as retrospective projection of its faith.
Except those that imply an imminent eschatology.

**Free:** There is no synoptic text that radical criticism has not at some point assigned to the first Christian generation as a retrospective projection of its faith, except the texts that imply an imminent eschatology.

**Exegesis:**
The exception is the point. Radical criticism becomes selective at precisely the place where the texts resist later domestication.

`tags: criticism, Gospel, eschatology`

**Free:** Many phenomena that look economic in origin prove biological in the end, because economics merely conditioned the social predominance of certain biological factors.

## §734
> La objetividad en las ciencias naturales es postulado, mientras que en las ciencias históricas es tarea.

**Close:** Objectivity in the natural sciences is postulate, whereas in the historical sciences it is task.

**Free:** In the natural sciences objectivity is a postulate; in the historical sciences it is a task.

**Exegesis:**
This is a concise methodological distinction. Historical objectivity is not given at the outset as a condition of method, but earned through labor against one's own partisanship and simplifications.

`tags: objectivity, natural-science, history`

## §738
> La ignorancia es la condición necesaria y suficiente de la generalización en historia.
> Conocer un hecho es descubrir que ninguna ley lo explica.

**Close:** Ignorance is the necessary and sufficient condition of generalization in history.
To know a fact is to discover that no law explains it.

**Free:** Ignorance is the necessary and sufficient condition for generalizing in history.
To know a fact is to discover that no law explains it.

**Exegesis:**
The claim is deliberately excessive, but its target is clear: historical knowledge grows more singular as it deepens, not more easily subsumable under law.

`tags: history, generalization, law`

## §740
> La imaginación es el único lugar en el mundo donde se puede habitar.

**Close:** Imagination is the only place in the world where one can dwell.

**Free:** Imagination is the only place in the world one can truly inhabit.

**Exegesis:**
After so many attacks on fantasy and ideology, this lands with force. The imagination condemned elsewhere is the imagination reduced to propaganda or wish; here it names the only inhabitable interiority left.

`tags: imagination, dwelling, interiority`
