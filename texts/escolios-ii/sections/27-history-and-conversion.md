# Section 27: History and Conversion (§621-640, pp. 114-116)

These pages keep shifting the ground under judgment: value has no rulebook, history cannot dissolve nature, and reason stops short of the conversions that matter.

---

## §621
> Todo individuo piensa hoy que el mundo anda mal porque no lo escuchan.

**Close:** Every individual today thinks the world goes badly because it does not listen to him.

**Free:** Nowadays everyone thinks the world is going wrong because no one is listening to him.

`tags: individual, world, grievance`

## §622
> Ciertas cosas sólo son interesantes vividas, otras sólo lo son imaginadas.

**Close:** Certain things are interesting only when lived, others only when imagined.

**Free:** Some things matter only if one lives them; others only if one imagines them.

`tags: experience, imagination, interest`

## §623
> Como el valor no obedece a reglas, nunca sabemos anticipadamente si el espíritu que invocamos es el de un demonio o el de un dios.
>
> La caza de valores es aventura en la cual sólo se arriesga sin temblar el ignorante.

**Close:** Since valor does not obey rules, we never know in advance whether the spirit we invoke is that of a demon or of a god.
The hunt for values is an adventure in which only the ignorant risks himself without trembling.

**Free:** Because courage obeys no rulebook, we never know beforehand whether the spirit we summon is demonic or divine.
The pursuit of value is an adventure at which only the ignorant can fling themselves without trembling.

**Exegesis:**
This is not a cult of decision for its own sake. The point is risk under conditions where no procedural guarantee can tell us in advance whether what we invoke is saving or corrupting.

`tags: value, courage, risk`

## §624
> No demos a nadie la ocasión de ser vil.

**Close:** Let us give no one the occasion to be vile.

**Free:** Give no one the chance to behave basely.
He will use it.

`tags: vileness, occasion, conduct`

## §625
> "Todos los versos" no "han sido hechos".
> Cada cultura, sin embargo, es individuación del espíritu, donde la virtualidad abstracta del hombre se limita a la virtualidad de un tipo humano.
> La virtualidad del hombre de una cultura dada se agota, por lo tanto, y la humanidad reitera durante siglos un presente estéril.
> Mil indicios sugieren que la caravana se interna, nuevamente, en un "Sahara of the higher intelligence".

**Close:** "All the verses" have not "been made."
Each culture, nevertheless, is an individuation of spirit, where the abstract virtuality of man is limited to the virtuality of a human type.
The virtuality of the man of a given culture is exhausted, therefore, and humanity reiterates for centuries a sterile present.
A thousand signs suggest that the caravan is again entering a "Sahara of the higher intelligence."

**Free:** "All the verses" have not "already been written."
Each culture is still an individuation of spirit in which man's abstract possibilities are narrowed to the possibilities of a particular human type.
Those possibilities can therefore be exhausted, and humanity may spend centuries repeating a sterile present.
A thousand signs suggest that the caravan is once more entering a "Sahara of the higher intelligence."

**Exegesis:**
The opening refusal of poetic exhaustion turns immediately into a civilizational warning. New forms remain possible, but cultures can still narrow what counts as human until intelligence wanders through a sterile landscape.

`tags: culture, spirit, intelligence`

**Free:** The same stupidity gets taken up again and again, provided it changes its outfit each time.

`tags: stupidity, fashion, repetition`

## §627
> Mirada de historiador es la que ve el significado del individuo, sin convertir al individuo en su mero significado.

**Close:** The historian's gaze is the one that sees the individual's significance without converting the individual into his mere significance.

**Free:** A historian's eye sees what an individual means without reducing him to nothing but that meaning.

**Exegesis:**
This is a compact rule for historical judgment. Significance must be seen, but it must not be allowed to consume the individual person who bears it.

`tags: history, individual, significance`

## §628
> No es cierto que el hombre "no tenga naturaleza, sino historia".
> La naturaleza humana es la suma de todo lo que el hombre, en la historia, no puede negarse a reconocer como deber sin mentirse a sí mismo.

**Close:** It is not true that man "has no nature, but history."
Human nature is the sum of everything that man, in history, cannot refuse to recognize as duty without lying to himself.

**Free:** It is false that man has no nature, only history.
Human nature is made of everything that, within history, man cannot deny as duty without becoming dishonest toward himself.

**Exegesis:**
The aphorism refuses both abstract nature-talk and historicist dissolution. Nature is not outside history, but neither is it cancelled by history; it appears as those obligations we cannot renounce without self-falsification.

`tags: nature, history, duty`

## §631
> La razón corrige los errores lógicos, pero los errores espirituales sólo son corregibles por una conversión de la persona.
>
> Las evidencias presuntas se desvanecen en silencio, cuando las contemplamos desde un nivel espiritual más alto.

**Close:** Reason corrects logical errors, but spiritual errors are corrigible only by a conversion of the person.
Presumed evidences vanish in silence when we contemplate them from a higher spiritual level.

**Free:** Reason can correct logical mistakes, but spiritual errors are corrected only by the conversion of the person.
Supposed evidences quietly evaporate once we behold them from a higher spiritual plane.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the volume's clearest refusals of intellectualism. Logic has its jurisdiction, but certain falsehoods persist until the person himself is altered.

`tags: reason, spiritual-error, conversion`

## §635
> La sabiduría se reduce a no olvidar jamás, ni la nada que es el hombre, ni la belleza que nace a veces en sus manos.

**Close:** Wisdom reduces itself to never forgetting either the nothingness that man is or the beauty that is sometimes born in his hands.

**Free:** Wisdom comes down to never forgetting either man's nothingness or the beauty that sometimes comes to birth in his hands.

**Exegesis:**
The sentence is balanced between abasement and praise. Wisdom is not choosing one truth against the other, but refusing to forget either human nullity or human making.

`tags: wisdom, nothingness, beauty`

## §637
> No llamemos realidad la pesadilla cotidiana, sino esa alusión que aflora en la fugitiva voz de un verso.

**Close:** Let us not call reality the daily nightmare, but rather that allusion which surfaces in the fugitive voice of a verse.

**Free:** Let us not call reality the daily nightmare, but the allusion that rises for an instant in the passing voice of a line of verse.

**Exegesis:**
Reality is displaced from the obvious to the allusive. The point is not escapism but a reversal of prestige: what everyday life calls solid may be the least real thing in sight.

`tags: reality, verse, allusion`

## §638
> Como los sociólogos proyectan sobre el pasado el esquema formalizado de las circunstancias en que viven, no esperemos que un subjetivismo tan crudo produzca ideas, sino fábulas.
> La sociología es un apólogo prosaico improvisado por mitólogos pedantes.

**Close:** Since sociologists project onto the past the formalized schema of the circumstances in which they live, let us not expect such a crude subjectivism to produce ideas, but fables.
Sociology is a prosaic apologue improvised by pedantic mythologists.

**Free:** Because sociologists project onto the past the formalized pattern of the circumstances they themselves inhabit, we should expect from such crude subjectivism not ideas but fables.
Sociology is a prosaic apologue improvised by pedantic myth-makers.

`tags: sociology, projection, fable`

## §639
> Solo esquivaron el desengaño de todo sueño colmado los que emprendieron la única empresa racional de la historia: conquistar en Judea un sepulcro vacío.

**Close:** Only those escaped the disenchantment of every fulfilled dream who undertook the only rational enterprise in history: to conquer in Judea an empty tomb.

**Free:** Only those escaped the disillusion of every fulfilled dream who undertook the only rational enterprise in history: the conquest of an empty tomb in Judea.

**Exegesis:**
The aphorism yokes rationality to resurrection rather than to disenchantment. Its shock lies in relocating the only fully rational enterprise away from prudence and toward an empty tomb.

`tags: rationality, resurrection, Judea`

---

## §640
> Si el universo es sistema, no puede haber evidencias que se contradigan.
> ¿Pero quién nos asegura que lo sea?

**Close:** If the universe is system, there cannot be evidences that contradict one another.
But who assures us that it is?

**Free:** If the universe were really a system, contradictory evidences could not arise.
But who has guaranteed that it is one?

**Exegesis:**
This is a direct check on explanatory ambition. Contradiction may indict our thinking, but it may also indict the hidden assumption that reality must present itself as a closed, coherent system.

`tags: universe, system, contradiction`
