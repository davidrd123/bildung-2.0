# Section 55: Taste and Demythologization (§1181-1200, pp. 203-206)

The final pages of this run stay close to the project's own recurrent line: taste, judgment, duration, and myth are all defended against flattening moralism, sociology, and explanatory vulgarity.

**Free:** To keep from being brutalized, it is enough to avoid the conversations of the young and the amusements of adults.

## §1185
> El profano que castra las colmenas de la filosofía sucumbe bajo el aguijón de sus abejas.

**Close:** The profane man who castrates the beehives of philosophy succumbs beneath the sting of its bees.

**Free:** The profane man who castrates philosophy's beehives ends by succumbing to the sting of its bees.

**Exegesis:**
The image is grotesque on purpose. Reduction does not neutralize philosophy; it provokes a worse encounter with what one tried to domesticate.

`tags: philosophy, profanation, bees`

## §1196
> Las auténticas leyes sociológicas no son recetas que predicen lo que ha de acontecer en determinadas circunstancias, sino fórmulas que indican lo que en determinadas circunstancias difícilmente acontece.

**Close:** Authentic sociological laws are not recipes that predict what is to happen in certain circumstances, but formulas that indicate what in certain circumstances happens only with difficulty.

**Free:** Genuine sociological laws are not recipes predicting what will happen in certain circumstances, but formulas indicating what under those circumstances hardly happens at all.

**Exegesis:**
This is a methodological inversion worth noticing. Law is strongest not when it predicts the obvious, but when it marks the improbabilities that define a field.

`tags: sociology, law, prediction`

## §1199
> Desmitologizar es empresa de quienes cándidamente suponen que, verbigracia, la Ascensión es episodio astronáutico para el creyente.

**Close:** To demythologize is the enterprise of those who naively suppose that, for example, the Ascension is an astronautic episode for the believer.

**Free:** Demythologizing is the enterprise of those who naively suppose that, say, the Ascension is for the believer an episode in astronautics.

**Exegesis:**
The mockery lands on literalizing reduction. Demythologization mistakes symbolic truth for failed reportage.

`tags: demythologization, Ascension, myth`
