# Section 45: Naturalness and Judgment (§981-1000, pp. 172-174)

The hundred-mark closes on judgment again: against the fetish of the natural, against false futures, and against every rhetoric that mistakes naked immanence for seriousness.

---

## §981
> La posteridad no va a entender qué hazaña es la mera sensatez en este siglo demente.

**Close:** Posterity is not going to understand what a feat mere sanity is in this demented century.

**Free:** Posterity will never understand what an achievement mere sanity is in this deranged century.

`tags: posterity, sanity, century`

---

## §982
> El hecho clave de este siglo es la explosión demográfica de las ideas bobas.

**Close:** The key fact of this century is the demographic explosion of stupid ideas.

**Free:** The key fact of this century is the population explosion of stupid ideas.

`tags: century, ideas, explosion`

---

## §983
> El elogio irrestricto de lo "natural" es escapatoria para eludir la obligación de distinguir entre las cosas naturales.

**Close:** Unrestricted praise of the "natural" is an escape route for eluding the obligation to distinguish among natural things.

**Free:** Indiscriminate praise of the "natural" is a way of escaping the obligation to distinguish among natural things.

`tags: natural, distinction, praise`

---

## §984
> El hombre no está encarcelado, se encarcela.

**Close:** Man is not imprisoned, he imprisons himself.

**Free:** Man is not in prison; he imprisons himself.

`tags: man, prison, self`

---

## §985
> Desmistifiquemos el futuro, que repetirá las eternas rutinas de la historia, para que no se convierta en asilo de sangrientas utopías.

**Close:** Let us demystify the future, which will repeat the eternal routines of history, so that it does not become asylum of bloody utopias.

**Free:** Let us demystify the future, since it will repeat history's eternal routines, so that it does not become a refuge for bloody utopias.

`tags: future, utopia, history`

---

## §986
> El que es partidario de la igualdad sin ser envidioso, sólo puede serlo porque es bobo.

**Close:** He who is partisan of equality without being envious can only be so because he is foolish.

**Free:** Anyone who supports equality without being envious can only do so because he is a fool.

`tags: equality, envy, foolishness`

## §987
> Mientras sobreviva el soldado, el total envilecimiento se dificulta.

**Close:** So long as the soldier survives, total debasement becomes difficult.

**Free:** As long as the soldier survives, total debasement becomes harder.

`tags: soldier, debasement, survival`

---

## §988
> La ontología es la ciencia de las apariencias, la axiología, la ciencia de la realidad.

**Close:** Ontology is the science of appearances, axiology the science of reality.

**Free:** Ontology is the science of appearances; axiology is the science of reality.

**Exegesis:**
This is one of the book's sharpest reversals of philosophical prestige. Reality is accessed not first through being, but through value.

`tags: ontology, axiology, reality`

---

## §989
> Las sentencias, el día del Juicio, serán menos terminantes y enfáticas que las de cualquier periodista sobre cualquier tema.

**Close:** The sentences on the Day of Judgment will be less categorical and emphatic than those of any journalist on any subject.

**Free:** The judgments on Judgment Day will be less categorical and emphatic than those any journalist pronounces on any topic.

`tags: Judgment-Day, journalism, judgment`

---

## §990
> Las ideologías son secreción democrática. Ya que el hombre común ni admite que le sea propuesto fin distinto de su interés personal, ni que se lo propongan con limpio cinismo.

**Close:** Ideologies are democratic secretion, since common man admits neither that an end different from his personal interest be proposed to him, nor that it be proposed with clean cynicism.

**Free:** Ideologies are a democratic secretion, since the common man admits neither that an end different from his personal interest be proposed to him nor that it be proposed with clean cynicism.

`tags: ideology, democracy, self-interest`

---

## §991
> Tanto individualismo como colectivismo son repercusiones sociales de la creencia en la inmortalidad del alma. El individuo se vierte hacia adentro, se examina, se observa y descubre su individualidad, o se vierte hacia afuera, se proyecta, se dispersa y se confunde con una colectividad, según crea, o no crea, en un incorruptible tribunal.

**Close:** Both individualism and collectivism are social repercussions of belief in the immortality of the soul. The individual pours himself inward, examines himself, observes himself and discovers his individuality, or pours himself outward, projects himself, disperses himself and confuses himself with a collectivity, according as he believes, or does not believe, in an incorruptible tribunal.

**Free:** Both individualism and collectivism are social repercussions of belief in the immortality of the soul. A person pours inward, examining and observing himself until he discovers individuality, or pours outward, projecting and dispersing himself until he is confused with a collectivity, depending on whether he believes in an incorruptible tribunal or not.

`tags: individualism, collectivism, immortality`

## §992
> Para no tener que acuartelar finalmente a la sociedad, hay que acuartelar al ejército dentro de ella.

**Close:** In order not to have finally to quarter society, one must quarter the army within it.

**Free:** If one does not want to quarter society in the end, one must quarter the army within society instead.

`tags: army, society, quartering`

## §993
> Cualquier superioridad, en tiempos democráticos, pasa el tiempo disculpándose.

**Close:** Any superiority, in democratic times, spends its time apologizing.

**Free:** In democratic times, any superiority spends its life apologizing for itself.

`tags: superiority, democracy, apology`

## §994
> No habiendo sino dos tipos lícitos de fiesta: regocijo popular y pompa aristocrática, las fiestas son grotescas en este siglo de clase media.

**Close:** There being only two licit kinds of feast, popular rejoicing and aristocratic pomp, festivities are grotesque in this middle-class century.

**Free:** Since there are only two legitimate kinds of festivity, popular rejoicing and aristocratic pomp, festivities are grotesque in this middle-class century.

`tags: feast, middle-class, pomp`

## §995
> Una verdad no se anula porque exista a un nivel más hondo otra verdad que ignore.

**Close:** A truth is not annulled because at a deeper level there exists another truth that it ignores.

**Free:** A truth is not cancelled because, at a deeper level, there exists another truth it does not know.

`tags: truth, depth, annulment`

---

## §996
> La juventud navega sin notarlo en un mar de conformismo. En cada ola que la arrastra sólo observa la breve espuma que la diferencia de las otras y no la marea común que las empuja a todas.

**Close:** Youth sails in a sea of conformism without noticing it. In each wave that drags it, it observes only the brief foam that differentiates it from the others and not the common tide that pushes them all.

**Free:** Youth sails without noticing it in a sea of conformism. In each wave carrying it along, it notices only the brief foam that distinguishes it from the others, never the common tide pushing them all.

**Exegesis:**
The image is exact because it grants difference without granting independence. Foam is real, but not self-moving.

`tags: youth, conformism, sea`

---

**Free:** Explanations are only a pastime; representations alone are serious.

`tags: explanation, representation, seriousness`

## §998
> Las ideas que menos influyen en política son las políticas.

**Close:** The ideas that influence politics least are the political ones.

**Free:** The ideas that least influence politics are political ideas.

`tags: ideas, politics, influence`

## §999
> La más odiosa retórica es la del individuo que ya no ve los hechos sino en su desnudez intramundana, pero habla de ellos como si aún viera allí reflejos de un transmundo.

**Close:** The most odious rhetoric is that of the individual who no longer sees facts except in their intramundane nakedness, but speaks of them as if he still saw there reflections of a transworld.

**Free:** The most odious rhetoric is that of the man who now sees facts only in their intramundane nakedness but still speaks of them as if he perceived there reflections of a world beyond.

**Exegesis:**
The disgust is aimed at borrowed elevation. Intramundane reduction becomes truly odious when it continues to borrow the prestige of a transcendent language it no longer believes.

`tags: rhetoric, intramundane, transmundo`
