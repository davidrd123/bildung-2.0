# Part 99: Galilei, Conservation of Matter and Motion as a Mathematical Object

Cassirer now carries the Galilean reversal through. Early modern thought had first reduced scholastic concepts to names in the name of sense; Galileo's own analysis turns the result back on perception itself. Whatever cannot be redeemed in pure quantitative determination counts as mere name. From that recast concept of matter follows the conservation thesis: absolute generation and destruction become scientifically unthinkable. The same line then admits movement itself as a fully legitimate mathematical object and rejects every claim that physics needs a special method opposed to mathematics.

**English (draft)**

```text
... Cassirer now makes the Galilean inversion explicit. Early modern science had first reduced scholastic concepts to mere names in the name of sense; Galileo's own analysis now turns the result back on perception itself. Whatever cannot be redeemed in pure quantitative determination counts as mere name. Even tactile resistance and weight drop out of the strict concept of body so long as geometry remains the leading form of mathematical determination.

From this follows the conservation thesis. Once matter is fixed as the enduring identical correlate of law, absolute generation and destruction become scientifically unintelligible. Apparent creation and annihilation are reinterpreted as relative displacements within the homogeneous totality of matter. A fact is no longer whatever immediate observation says it is; its scientific standing depends on systematic coherence with the whole of phenomena.

The same shift carries into the concept of motion. Movement itself becomes a fully legitimate mathematical object, and Galileo rejects every demand for a special physical method opposed to the mathematical one. The hydrostatic dispute sharpens the contrast: universal law yields determinate cases, whereas qualitative explanation proliferates distinctions without necessity.
```

**Working Commentary**

```text
1) The strongest turn here is the reversal of the old nominalism charge: immediate perception itself becomes `Name` unless it can be grounded quantitatively.

2) Weight and even tactile resistance are withheld from the strict body-concept at this stage. The leading interest is still logical and geometrical.

3) Conservation is not added empirically from outside. It follows directly from the homogeneous, enduring concept of matter.

4) Experience is redefined: not every observation counts as fact, only what can be held in systematic agreement with the whole of phenomena.

5) Motion is now admitted without remainder into mathematics, and the demand for a separate `physische Methode` is treated as a regression into qualitative ambiguity.
```
