# Part 90: Kepler, Force as Function, and the Arithmetic of Forces

Cassirer now gives the Kepler force-line its methodological center. Cause no longer means an inner driver hidden behind motion, but a system of mathematically determinable conditions. That move excludes supersensible interventions from physics, expels Aristotelian teleology on the same ground, and roots force in number, statics, and lawful derivability rather than in desire, entelechy, or living animation. Kepler cannot yet produce the full arithmetic of basic forces, but Cassirer now treats him as having clearly sketched the ideal of modern dynamics before the empirical gravity discussion begins.

**English (draft)**

```text
... as magnitudes dependent on the given numerical elements of the orbit. The closer analysis distinguishes three groups of such elements: the measure of the force belonging to a planet at a given segment of its path is determined by the size of the radius vectors for the points of the orbit, by the length of the traversed arc, and by the time required to cover that path. What is sought is not the inner driving cause of motion; the very concept of cause dissolves into a complex of mathematical conditions. Cassirer says this formulation already governs Kepler's later theory of gravitation step by step. It also excludes supersensible and immaterial interventions from nature, because only within bodily extension can fixed measure and exact causality be applied. Any appeal to an outside agent would introduce a geometric uncertainty into a universe whose true divine constitution requires each element to be derivable from the others in demonstrative fashion.

From this same standpoint Aristotelian teleology is expelled from physics. The opposition of heavy and light bodies, with their natural motions to center and periphery, violates the new demand for logical and mathematical homogeneity between cause and effect. Force is therefore no longer grounded in vague analogy with sensible desire, but in the pure law of number. Kepler can thus say that he replaces Aristotle's heavenly theology and metaphysics with a heavenly philosophy and physics that already includes a new arithmetic of forces.

Cassirer then makes the historical point sharply. Kepler appeals above all to statics and the lever law to show the possibility of the new science. Statics, from Archimedes through Stevin, gave the only secure point of attachment for this thought, though its significance reaches far beyond that domain. Kepler has not yet fulfilled the demand he poses: his laws give the geometric outline of the cosmos without reaching the arithmetic of the basic forces from which it is formed. But the ideal of modern dynamics is already sketched here, and the relation to Newton is now clear. What Newton later executes scientifically, Kepler has already brought to methodological clarity. The next step is his empirical explanation of gravity through Gilbert's magnetism.
```

**Working Commentary**

```text
1) The decisive shift here is that cause becomes function. Cassirer no longer treats force as a hidden entity behind motion, but as a mathematically articulable dependency among orbital magnitudes.

2) This is why external spiritual agency is excluded. The issue is not a flat materialism; it is that any extra-worldly intervention would destroy the demonstrative derivability demanded by exact science.

3) The banishment of Aristotelian teleology follows from the same rule. Cause and effect must be homogeneous enough to enter one mathematical order.

4) The `Arithmetik der Kräfte` line matters because it roots force in number and statics rather than in entelechy or desire. Kepler still falls short of the full science he is aiming at, but the target is now unmistakable.

5) Newton appears here not as an abrupt replacement but as the later execution of a methodological demand Kepler has already made explicit.
```

**Notes**

- `Kraftbegriff und Funktionsbegriff` is the clearest local formula yet for Cassirer's recasting of cause within exact science.
- The line about geometric uncertainty is worth keeping visible; it is one of the sharpest statements so far of why outside agency becomes inadmissible.
- The Gilbert handoff is now earned: gravity is about to become the concrete test case for this force/function framework.
