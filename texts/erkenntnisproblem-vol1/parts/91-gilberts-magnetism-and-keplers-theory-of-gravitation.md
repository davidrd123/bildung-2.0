# Part 91: Gilbert's Magnetism and Kepler's Theory of Gravitation

Cassirer now makes the gravity line concrete by way of Gilbert. Gilbert's break consists in refusing to derive motion from privileged places, directions, or vague substantial affinities; attraction must be rooted in bodies themselves and treated as a general natural cause. Kepler deepens this in two ways: attraction becomes a force between cosmic masses rather than only between a body and its parts, and magnetism helps force the transition from psychic animation to mathematically law-governed nature. Gravity is then redefined as reciprocal interaction, not one-sided tendency, and the real question shifts from "what force is" to the law according to which it varies.

**English (gloss)**

```text
... what Kepler and Galileo chiefly value in Gilbert is the method of the work. Gilbert himself had proclaimed a "new way of philosophizing," and Cassirer says the decisive starting point is the fight against the dominance of the ontological concept-system. Aristotle had turned merely relative directions like up and down into a real opposition of forces, and had never sharply drawn the line between subject and object. The substantial affinities once used to explain magnetic attraction were therefore both vague and infertile.

Gilbert replaces this with an empirical theory of magnetic and electric force and with the discovery of terrestrial magnetism. Gravity is no longer explained by orientation toward a privileged world-center. No point in the universe has physical precedence; place as such does nothing. Natural force belongs to bodies themselves. Gravity thus becomes a general natural cause with a definite range and measure, weakening with distance, and Gilbert imagines it mediated by a subtle fluidum in analogy with magnetism and electricity.

Kepler takes over Gilbert's theory but deepens it in two decisive ways. First, attraction becomes a force operating between cosmic masses, not merely between a body and its own parts. That is why he can apply gravitation to the earth-moon relation and anticipate the basic thought of the Newtonian tide theory. Second, magnetism itself now helps Kepler break with animism. The phenomena push him, as he says, from the side of spirit to the side of nature. Because both the acting body and the mediating medium are fixed quanta, they belong to mathematics and nature. Gravity must therefore be conceived as reciprocal interaction. The earth draws the stone, but the stone also draws the earth. The question is no longer the old metaphysical one of inner powers, but the law according to which this relation varies.
```

**Working Commentary**

```text
1) Gilbert matters here less as a detached figure than as the historical medium through which gravity is stripped of privileged place and substantial affinity.

2) The anti-ontological line stays active. "Up" and "down" are no longer allowed to harden into real opposing powers.

3) Kepler's first deepening is cosmological. Attraction now binds distinct masses and begins to reorganize the whole structure of the heavens.

4) The second deepening is methodological. Magnetism does not preserve animism; it pushes Kepler out of it, because acting body and medium are both fixed quanta inside one mathematical order.

5) Reciprocity is the key gain at the end of the tranche. Once gravity is defined as interaction, the dominant question shifts from force-substance to force-law.
```

**Notes**

- Gilbert's attack on privileged place is one of the clearest local breaks with Aristotelian natural place physics.
- The Bruno comparison matters because it shows how much Kepler changes even within the anti-Aristotelian camp.
- The cut-off now points straight to the law-question and the correlate concept of matter.
