At this point, however, what matters above all is to grasp the motive of comparison in all its sharpness. The fundamental law of all abstractive thinking is, as now becomes clear, the strict separation of the elements toward which it is directed. Here cognition means individuation: the apprehension of the particular as a particular demands above all that its distinction from all other possible contents standing over against it be secured. The oppositions to which thought is led in this its first achievement are therefore irreconcilable from its own standpoint, because the form of cognition itself consists in an act of opposition.2) But once this first phase has been reached, another demand necessarily already arises within the sphere of science itself. The completion of knowledge is first accomplished when it succeeds in finding a principle from which, in unbroken sequence, the totality of possible particular positings can fully develop and be surveyed in the necessity of its connection. This principle lies prior to all particular oppositions, since these are first supposed to unfold from it through determinate conditions superadded to it. Here the delimitations of the particular no longer confront us as originally given barriers, rigid and unalterable; rather, we try to see them arise before us.

**Notes**

- This tranche makes the faculty distinction explicit: mathematics can no longer remain grounded in `ratio`, but passes to `intellectus` and `visus intellectualis`.
- Cassirer now contrasts abstractive thinking with constructive or generative grasp: the former isolates fixed oppositions, the latter comprehends the rule that yields an entire series of forms.
- The angle-construction passage is the clearest statement so far that `coincidence` names a continuous lawful transition, not the collapse of distinct terms into an undifferentiated unity.
- The closing return to mysticism is not a retreat from mathematics but the point at which Cassirer makes their shared logical form explicit.
- The tranche stops mid-sentence at the printed page `52` boundary, where Cassirer is about to name the mediating category that links mathematical and metaphysical development.
