## Promoted claims

### 1. The strongest methodological gain is the negative criterion for unearned synthesis

The most stable yield from the chat is not one more positive connection but a sharper description of what false synthesis looks like from the inside:

- everything maps too quickly
- translation pressure runs in one direction only
- the borrowed vocabulary arrives as a template rather than being generated by the source conditions that first produced it
- no friction appears, only harmonization

This is a real project criterion now. It sharpens the existing guardrails by naming the failure mode more concretely: beautiful total consonance is itself evidence that the immune system should be firing.

The specific case discussed in the chat matters less than the criterion it exposed. The theological mapping was useful precisely because it demonstrated the collision capability without the filtration discipline.

### 2. The structured representation proposal is a real infrastructure candidate

The most practical new idea in the chat is the proposal to represent conceptual development not as a proposition tree but as a generative chain.

The live form is:

- `position`
- `internal pressure`
- `operation`
- `yield`
- `generated problem`
- `conditions of application`

This is stronger than the glossary because it preserves why a concept arose, what insufficiency forced it, what it costs, what it opens, and under what conditions it is valid. That matters directly for the Cusanus material, where concepts like `docta ignorantia`, `assimilatio`, and `coincidentia oppositorum` are not portable keywords but constrained moves inside a sequence of transformations.

This proposal is now worth holding as the densest plausible next piece of earned infrastructure. But it remains unbuilt. If pursued, it should begin with Parts `6-16`, reread specifically for the chain of transformations that forced each move.

### 3. Peirce is a real bridge candidate for the formal-systems gap, but only as a trigger-conditioned reserve

The chat's most significant positive addition to the map is not "Peirce belongs everywhere" but something more exact:

- Peirce is a specific candidate for the meaning/formalism junction
- the German line through Leibniz -> Kant -> neo-Kantians -> Cassirer and the Anglophone line through Leibniz -> Peirce / Whitehead may be parallel unfoldings from a common root
- the formal-systems gap may lie less inside the German tradition than in the unheld space between those lines

This is not yet a project finding. It is a trigger condition.

What can be retained now:

- when the Erkenntnisproblem reaches Leibniz, this question becomes live
- if the McCulloch / formal-systems side forces the meaning junction directly, Peirce becomes newly relevant
- if Peirce enters, he should enter through specific texts, not as a corpus-scale ambition

The important discipline is negative: do not promote Peirce into active framework before one of those triggers arrives.

### 4. The practice claim matters more than the historical anecdote

The tail's final move is strongest when detached from the romance of the anecdote. Whether every detail about Peirce's Kant reading is exactly right is secondary. The durable claim is methodological:

- sustained habitation inside a difficult primary text produces a different order of thought than rapid interpretive surveying
- the difference is not just quantity of detail but mode of formation
- this is close to the project's own working practice and helps explain why the translation work keeps generating findings that survey prose alone cannot

This should be retained as a method observation, not as hero narrative.
