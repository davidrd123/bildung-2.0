# 2026-04-08 - Ferrari 1997 on Cassirer and the logical-empiricist field

Purpose: extract the most useful field-placement gains from Massimo Ferrari's chapter in the 1997 Cassirer science volume for the current `Erkenntnisproblem` work.

Inputs:

- Massimo Ferrari, `Über die Ursprünge des logischen Empirismus, den Neukantianismus und Ernst Cassirer`
- `reading/2026-04-08-ihmig-on-cassirer-scientific-progress.md`
- `reading/2026-04-08-cassirer-reception-gap-and-1997-volume-priorities.md`

Constraint:

- this is a bounded working note, not a full reconstruction of the origins of logical empiricism

## Main gain

Ferrari is useful because he breaks a bad binary:

- Cassirer is not simply outside the formation of logical empiricism
- Schlick, Reichenbach, and even early Carnap are not simply anti-Kant or anti-neokantian from the start

The result is a much better placement line for the current project:

- Cassirer belongs inside the same continental problem-field
- the real issue is not simple opposition, but different ways of transforming Kant and the neo-Kantian science tradition

## Promoted claims

### 1. The standard anti-Kant origin story is too crude

Ferrari opens by stressing that newer work on logical empiricism had already begun revising the official story:

- the field cannot be understood well if it tells itself only as anti-metaphysical rupture
- historical reconstruction changes the picture of its origins
- the continental phase matters, especially the relations to neo-Kantianism, phenomenology, conventionalism, and relativity debates

Project use:

- do not treat Cassirer as a figure simply superseded by a wholly external logical-empiricist line
- treat the current method questions as belonging to a shared debate about science, constitution, objectivity, and the fate of the a priori

### 2. Early Schlick is much closer to neo-Kantian science-philosophy than the standard story allows

Ferrari's Schlick section is the sharpest immediate payoff.

What he argues:

- Schlick's early work is unintelligible if one sees only Mach and not the neo-Kantian and conventionalist background
- the relevant background includes Lange, Helmholtz, Riehl, the Marburg school, and parts of Hönigswald
- Schlick shares with Marburg-style thought the emphasis on the fact of science, the need to revise epistemology in light of scientific development, and the conception of scientific objects as embedded in networks of laws and relations

Ferrari then makes the divergence more exact:

- Schlick can move with Cassirer as far as law-concept, relation, and the constructive status of space
- he parts company over the synthetic productivity of pure thought and over the status of the given
- this is why Ferrari treats Schlick's realism as non-dogmatic but still significantly anti-Marburg at the crucial point

Project use:

- the Schlick/Cassirer relation should be read as near-alignment with a decisive break, not as wholesale foreignness

### 3. Reichenbach remains closer to Cassirer than his anti-Kant polemics suggest

Ferrari's Reichenbach section is the most useful companion to the current Cassirer method work.

The strongest point is:

- Reichenbach's standard complaint that Kant ignored the historical development of science does not really hit Marburg and still less Cassirer
- with Cassirer, Reichenbach shares the attempt to relativize the a priori and move it toward a scientific-analytic method
- the key commonality is the stress on constitutive principles whose role persists even as their concrete scientific content changes

Ferrari does not collapse them into one another:

- Reichenbach moves further toward convention and scientific analysis
- Cassirer remains more explicitly transcendental and is said to lag behind some later epistemological mediations

But the convergence is still real and important:

- Cassirer is not the static defender of an unmodified synthetic a priori
- he is already moving toward an invariant-theoretical account of experience

Project use:

- this note supports reading Cassirer as a live interlocutor in the relativity / geometry / constitutive-principles debate
- it also sharpens why older Schlick/Reichenbach material is acquisition-worthy for this project

### 4. Early Carnap also belongs to this same continental field

Ferrari's Carnap section matters less for immediate local use, but it reinforces the general placement claim.

The key point is:

- the `Aufbau` should not be read simply as crude positivist or foundationalist reduction
- its background includes neo-Kantian, phenomenological, and constitutive themes
- Carnap is still a German philosopher formed inside a strongly Kant-inflected atmosphere

Project use:

- keep early Carnap in the outer field of possible comparison
- do not let later textbook images of logical empiricism flatten the earlier continental formation

## What this changes for current Cassirer work

### 1. Cassirer should not be read as a dead branch

For the science-method line, Ferrari makes it harder to tell a simple succession story:

- Cassirer versus logical empiricism
- then logical empiricism wins

That picture is too simple. The more accurate view is:

- Cassirer participates in the same problem-space
- later figures split from him at identifiable pressure points

### 2. The real pressure point is the status of constitutive activity

Across Schlick and Reichenbach, Ferrari repeatedly returns to one central issue:

- how far can one preserve constitutive principles without preserving a stronger transcendental framework?

This is the best compressed way to understand Cassirer's placement in the field.

### 3. Older interlocutors matter more than generic later Cassirer commentary

This note confirms the acquisition strategy already recorded elsewhere:

- if we want the denser "older serious scholarship" layer, Schlick / Reichenbach / Hilbert-adjacent material will likely teach more than another generic Cassirer collection
