## 1. Young Cassirer is a method-discoverer, not just a method-applier

The strongest correction to the generic Cassirer gloss is simple:

- Volume I does not read like a finished symbolic-forms system being projected backward onto earlier thinkers
- it reads like a reconstruction of how concepts generate successors through internal pressure, insufficiency, and transformation

The volume is therefore less usefully described as `young neo-Kantian Cassirer applying a framework` and more usefully as `Cassirer discovering the logic of conceptual development through historical reconstruction`.

## 2. The Cusanus line is more radical than a generic neo-Kantian gloss predicts

The opening book does not merely anticipate later Marburg positions.

What the local work actually showed:

- cognition is defined through measuring, proportioning, and determination
- `similitudo` gives way to active `assimilatio`
- incompletability becomes a positive cognitive principle
- Cusanus matters by transformed questioning and staged development, not by topic-match

So the safer local claim is:

- Cusanus is not just a prelude to neo-Kantian constitutive theory
- he is one of the places where Cassirer finds the deeper engine that later neo-Kantianism will inherit and rework

## 3. Reification is a discriminating diagnostic, not a slogan about `Substanz vs Funktion`

The completed volume does not support only the broad formula `substance-thinking gives way to function-thinking`.

What it supports more sharply is:

- methodological gains repeatedly harden back into substances, powers, guarantees, or fixed objects
- Cassirer tracks this hardening as a recurring failure mode
- the resulting diagnostic is fine-grained enough to sort cases rather than only condemn them in bulk

That stronger version is what made the later cross-project experiment use real rather than decorative.

## 4. Bayle is method-bearing, not an appendix

The received high-level Cassirer story often centers Cusanus, Galileo, Descartes, and then stops.

The completed local run showed something stronger:

- Bayle is the internal critic who tests whether rational syntheses have actually earned their warrant
- he preserves critique while sacrificing constructive theoretical reason
- he is therefore a limit-case inside the volume's own argument, not just a late historical add-on

This is why Bayle became method-bearing for later experiment work rather than staying only a chapter summary point.
