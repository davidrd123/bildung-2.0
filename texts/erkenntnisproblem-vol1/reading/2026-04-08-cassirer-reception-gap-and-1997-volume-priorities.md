# 2026-04-08 - Cassirer reception gap and 1997 volume priorities

Purpose: sort what seems to be missing from the current Cassirer secondary packet and identify which chapters in the 1997 science volume are actually worth reading for the live `Erkenntnisproblem` work.

## Main judgment

The present packet is not thin because there was no serious scholarship around Cassirer.

It is thin because the most useful older material is likely to live in dispersed forms:

- adjacent interlocutors rather than explicit Cassirer monographs
- reviews and controversy pieces rather than late survey books
- journal literature and collected volumes rather than one continuous school tradition
- exile-broken and postwar-distributed reception rather than a stable line of direct heirs

The local packet itself supports this reading:

- Ferrari stresses Cassirer's problematic reception history and the late "Cassirer-Renaissance"
- Rudolph stresses that Cassirer did not generate a strong succession of direct "children"
- the 2016 collection reads like a field rebuilding itself through reception work and Nachlass publication

## What seems to be missing

For current project purposes, the most likely missing "older serious scholarship" is not generic Cassirer appreciation but the following kinds of material:

### 1. Direct interlocutors on the same science-method problems

Most wanted:

- Schlick
- Reichenbach
- Hönigswald
- Riehl
- Bauch
- Hilbert-adjacent and Erlanger-Program-adjacent materials

These matter because they pressure the same cluster Cassirer is inhabiting: geometry, relativity, scientific objectivity, constitutive principles, and the fate of the a priori.

### 2. Review-era responses to major Cassirer books

Most wanted:

- responses to `Substanzbegriff und Funktionsbegriff`
- responses to `Das Erkenntnisproblem`
- responses to `Zur Einsteinschen Relativitätstheorie`

These would likely give a denser picture of how Cassirer was actually received at the time than later "Cassirer studies" volumes do.

### 3. Warburg / Hamburg / exile-distributed context

Most wanted:

- work that places Cassirer inside the Warburg-Hamburg intellectual field
- material showing what broke after exile and why continuity of reception weakened

This would help explain the structural gap between Cassirer's importance and the patchiness of the later scholarship.

### 4. Immediate postwar reception fragments

Most wanted:

- pieces where Cassirer is used, argued with, or absorbed without becoming the center of a school
- early Goodman / Bourdieu / Habermas-side reception traces only when they still illuminate the break in direct continuity

These are not first-line for tranche work, but they matter for understanding why the later field looks rebuilt rather than continuous.

## 1997 volume - priority map

The 1997 volume is not just reserve context. It contains several chapters that look like genuine old-style method interlocutors.

### Use now

#### 1. Karl-Norbert Ihmig, `Hilberts axiomatische Methode und der Fortschritt in den Naturwissenschaften`

Best chapter for the live method line.

Why it matters:

- explicitly about scientific progress, continuity, rupture, and Cassirer's epistemology
- connects Cassirer to Hilbert, axiomatics, and the problem of non-cumulative development
- looks strongest for the current question: how to read `Erkenntnisproblem` as a changing theory of scientific objectivity rather than as a doctrinal sequence

#### 2. Massimo Ferrari, `Über die Ursprünge des logischen Empirismus, den Neukantianismus und Ernst Cassirer`

Best chapter for field placement.

Why it matters:

- reconstructs the Schlick / Carnap / Reichenbach / Marburg background
- directly pressures the relation between Cassirer and the continental origins of logical empiricism
- helps locate what kind of interlocutor Cassirer really is

### Keep nearby

#### 3. Enno Rudolph, `Raum, Zeit und Bewegung`

Best genealogical chapter for the Leibniz -> Kant -> Cassirer -> Reichenbach line.

Why it matters:

- sharp for space / time / relativity background
- useful if the current science-method line needs a deeper prehistory
- probably less immediately actionable than Ihmig or Ferrari

#### 4. Ion-Olimpiu Stamatescu, `Cassirer und die Quantenmechanik`

Best physics-facing chapter.

Why it matters:

- good on causality, continuity, symbolization, and the non-positivist/non-naive-realist reading of physics
- more useful for Cassirer's philosophy of modern physics than for the Renaissance material directly

### Read conditionally

#### 5. Hans Günter Dosch, `Against Philosophy - auch gegen Ernst Cassirer?`

Good orientation piece, but less central than the four above.

Why it matters:

- useful on positivism, theory-ladenness, anti-relativism, and Cassirer as ally against crude empiricism
- strongest as orientation rather than as a direct tool for current tranche work

#### 6. Gerold Prauss, `Zum Problem der nichteuklidischen Geometrien`

Potentially useful as a hard counterreading.

Why it matters:

- directly criticizes Cassirer on geometry
- worth using if the geometry issue becomes active enough to need adversarial pressure rather than supportive framing

### Later only

- Martina Plümacher on Gestalt psychology
- Brigitte Lohff on vitalism
- Volker Schürmann on anthropology and naturphilosophy

These are real chapters, but not the first reads for the current `Erkenntnisproblem` line.

## Working read order

If one bounded pass through the 1997 volume is wanted for current use:

1. Ihmig
2. Ferrari
3. Rudolph
4. Stamatescu
5. Dosch
6. Prauss only if needed as counterpressure
