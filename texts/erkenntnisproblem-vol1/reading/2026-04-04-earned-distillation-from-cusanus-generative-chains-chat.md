## Promoted claims

### 1. The first Cusanus node needed sharpening at the level of cognition itself

The strongest earned gain from the chat is not a new outward mapping but a refinement of the opening node in the Cusanus chain.

What the close reading clarified:

- the initial pressure is not only theological but epistemic
- `Erkennen` is defined as `Messen`, and `Messen` as a `Gleichung` between sought content and known elements
- concept and language are therefore bound to proportion, differentiation, and `Andersheit`
- the resulting blockage is double: the absolute becomes inaccessible, and the creature becomes unintelligible as well
- the crux formula is the paradox of reaching `Inhaltsbestimmung` through the removal of every finite `Bestimmtheit`

This matters because it makes the later reversal internally demanded rather than externally supplied. The creature is not revalued by a sentimental turn back toward finitude. It returns because the first position undermines the very conditions of cognition on which it relied.

### 2. The German is carrying reasoning through five distinct compression layers

The most durable philological gain from the chat is the compression claim, now sharpened into five mechanisms rather than one broad observation:

- compound nouns encode structural twinning
- verb prefixes encode directional argument
- reflexive constructions encode self-movement
- periodic sentence structure enacts deductive suspension and resolution
- etymological transparency lets Cassirer argue through word choice itself

The strongest examples in the opening passage were:

- compound twinning like `Gottesbegriff` / `Erkenntnisbegriff`
- directional verbs like `Hinwegschreiten`
- reflexives like `sich konzentrieren` and `sich spiegeln`
- the `Bestimmtheit` / `Bestimmung` distinction
- the long `da ... so` measuring sentence, whose logical force depends on delayed verbal resolution

The project-level conclusion is now more exact: English can preserve compound structure partially, but it weakens directional and reflexive force, flattens the temporal experience of the periodic sentence, and often converts etymologically live reasoning into opaque terminology.

### 3. A construction-kit glossary is a real infrastructure candidate

The chat produced one new infrastructure idea that survives review: a glossary organized by roots, prefixes, and recurring constructions rather than by isolated dictionary entries.

This would matter because German philosophical vocabulary in the Cusanus chapter is visibly combinatorial. Once roots like `Wesen`, `Stimme`, `Schein`, `stehen`, `halten`, and `setzen` and prefixes like `hin-`, `über-`, `fort-`, `ent-`, `ver-`, and `zer-` are tracked in context, new compounds stop arriving as wholly new units.

This is not yet built. But it is now a real candidate because it would answer a pressure repeatedly generated by the primary work rather than by speculative architecture.

### 4. The reasoning diary and residue analysis are legitimate outputs, not side effects

The chat sharpened a method point that belongs inside the project:

- close translation produces findings about the source text
- it also produces findings about what does and does not cross between German and English
- that residue is not waste; it is data about the boundary between languages and between modes of reasoning

This does not justify turning the project into free-floating AI exegesis. It does justify treating the translation process itself as an additional evidentiary layer when the losses and gains are concrete and source-bound.

### 5. The project now needs three explicit operational modes

The late self-correction in the chat is worth keeping because it gives a clean sorting rule:

- `survey` mode maps possible collisions and future directions
- `verification` mode checks shared understanding of material already encountered at lower resolution
- `analytical` mode works at sentence-level pressure against primary text and is the only mode that earns promotion

This distinction should govern later modern-framework collisions. The problem in the chat was not that survey happened, but that survey-speed hypotheses briefly wore analytical clothing.
