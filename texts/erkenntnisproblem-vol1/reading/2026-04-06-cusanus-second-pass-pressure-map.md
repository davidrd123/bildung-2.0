## Why this unit

This is the smallest late-Cusanus arc that closes a full conceptual movement:

- `complicatio` / `explicatio` move from theology into finite cognition
- mathematics stops serving theology merely as symbol and becomes `mathematica perfectio`
- `ratio` yields to `intellectus`
- `emanatio` is reread as deduction
- `non aliud` and `scientia infinita` turn the whole sequence back into consciousness and inquiry

The right question for a second pass here is therefore not "is each sentence acceptable English?" but:

- does the English preserve the transfer from one register to the next, or does it leave the reader with a heap of separate doctrines?

## Pressure Clusters

### 1. Part 12 - From comparison to mediation

Main risk:

- the English can read like generic history of ideas rather than like Cassirer's sharper question about the logical category that makes the theology/mathematics connection fruitful

What has to stay visible:

- early Cusan mathematics as symbolic comparison
- later Cusan mathematics as productive for mathematics itself
- `mathematica perfectio` as a real reversal, not a decorative tag

Draft-level watchpoints:

- overly inert phrasing around the paradox of union
- constructions that flatten "fruitful for mathematical insight" into static description

### 3. Part 14 - From `emanatio` to logical relation

- wording that softens the shift from doctrine to operation
