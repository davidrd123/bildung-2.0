## 1. Phase map

The visible journal runs from `2026-04-01` through `2026-04-08`, with an extreme density spike on April 8.

Read as a document, it moves through five regimes:

1. startup protocol
   - `Notes Separation`
   - `Scaffold`
   - `Intake Method`
   - `Calibration Batch`
   - the journal begins by setting bias, container rules, and intake procedure before it becomes a tranche log
2. tranche-by-tranche discovery
   - the dominant form is `What this tranche clarified / Decision for now`
   - the working unit is not a chapter in the abstract but an earned local arc whose boundary is discovered in motion
3. apparatus-mutation
   - `Reading-note distillation promoted into working status`
   - `First-pass Cusanus generative chain built`
   - `Close-reading capture gap named and local ledger added`
   - `Context packages and Bruno chain externalized`
   - the journal starts recording not just textual gains but changes in the apparatus required to keep those gains from disappearing
4. high-frequency structural pressure
   - late `Naturphilosophie`, exact science, mathematics, and the Book III run
   - the journal becomes more repetitive in form and more explicit about not promoting glossary terms while structural pressure is still rising
5. post-completion repair regime
   - `Ninety-Eighth Naturphilosophie Tranche`
   - `Repair pass opened on Leonardo / early Kepler` and following entries
   - the journal stops being mainly a discovery surface and becomes a boundary-and-integrity surface

## 2. Deferred decision changes function across the run

The journal's restraint is not one continuous rule with one stable meaning.

Early:

- restraint means `do not settle too soon because the local term is still being specified`
- glossary additions still happen actively (`Geisteswissenschaften`, `conjectura`, `Wiedererinnerung`, etc.)

Middle:

- restraint means `keep on watch until recurrence clarifies whether the pressure is local or chapter-bearing`
- this is strongest in the early `Naturphilosophie` run around `Weltorganismus`, `Potenz`, `Substanz`, and related terms

Late:

- restraint means `the pressure is real but no longer mainly lexical`
- the repeated formula `still no glossary promotion` shifts from caution to method
- by the Bayle close, the journal says this directly: the pressure remained `architectonic`

Working judgment:

- the late thinness of the glossary is not only a gap
- it is also evidence that the deepest late pressures were often being carried as structures, relations, and method-lines rather than as stable lexical handles

## 3. Corrections are epistemic, not clerical

The journal repeatedly treats metadata, boundaries, and expected continuations as fallible until re-earned.

Recurring correction types:

- page-boundary and divider corrections
  - `Introduction Endpoint Correction`
  - the `60/61` Cusanus reopening
  - `Skepticism Concludes, Book II Opens` with the `203` correction
- false-edge corrections
  - a continuation expected from the surrounding structure turns out not to be what the source is actually doing
  - the mathematics-to-Descartes threshold is the clearest late case
- source-absence or compression corrections
  - bounded rereads and new local files are introduced when memory or existing notes are no longer enough to preserve the live pressure

This means the journal's procedural lesson is stronger than `be careful with metadata`.

It is:

- section boundaries, OCR assumptions, anticipated continuations, and even the shape of the working apparatus remain revisable until the text re-earns them

## 4. Persistent unresolved pressures

Some of the most important issues do not resolve into stable glossary entries or final local decisions.

Persistent pressure lines:

- `Wissenschaft`, `Geist`, `Geisteswissenschaften`
- Bruno's `Minimum` / `Grenze` / irrational / continuum problem
- `Erfahrung`, `Relation`, `Hypothese`, `Gesetz`, `Kraft`
- reasoning-experience loss in English where syntax carries method

The important point is not that these were missed.

It is that unresolvedness becomes a standing operating condition of the journal:

- some pressures remain alive precisely because closing them early would falsify the text

## 5. What changes after Volume I is complete

The completion entry does not end the journal. It changes its job.

Before completion:

- the journal mainly records discovery, local pacing, and delayed stabilization

After completion:

- the journal tracks `honest repair boundary`
- it keeps `audit`, `close review`, and `session ledger` synchronized
- it distinguishes English repair from German completeness
- it protects the claim that the volume is complete while still allowing local repair

So the final lesson of the second pass is:

- `journal.md` is not only a translation log or decision surface
- it is also the record of how the subproject learned to distrust premature stability in terms, boundaries, apparatus, and completion-status itself
