# Secondary sources for seven thinkers in their source languages

**The most striking finding across all seven figures is a shared structural gap**: for each thinker, the secondary literature in the source language is thinner than expected on the *specific* text in question, even where the broader oeuvre is well studied. Jünger's *An der Zeitmauer*, Cassirer's *Erkenntnisproblem* Vol. 1, and the PKD *Exegesis* lack dedicated book-length treatments entirely. The strongest conceptual-vocabulary-centered scholarship exists for Anders and Uexküll; the weakest for Jünger's late work and Cassirer's early historiography.

---

## 1. Jakob von Uexküll — German-language sources on *Theoretische Biologie*

The German secondary literature has grown substantially since 2000 but clusters around biography, political critique, and semiotic reframing — **not sustained explication of the conceptual architecture** of *Theoretische Biologie* itself. No monograph systematically unpacks the Merkzeichen → Merkmal → Merkding hierarchy, the Gegenstand/Objekt distinction, or Planmäßigkeit as Naturkraft rather than theology.

### Essential

**Thure von Uexküll (ed.), *Kompositionslehre der Natur: Biologie als undogmatische Naturwissenschaft*** (Ullstein/Propyläen, 1980). Selected writings with Thure's 50-page introduction reframing Jakob's biology as sign theory. The single most influential secondary interpretation in German; all biosemiotic reception passes through this text. Transforms rather than merely explicates the original terminology, but indispensable.

**Rudolf Langthaler, *Organismus und Umwelt: Die biologische Umweltlehre im Spiegel traditioneller Naturphilosophie*** (Georg Olms, 1992). The most dedicated German philosophical treatment — reads the Umweltlehre against Aristotle through Kant. Rare book-length study that treats Uexküll's system as a philosophical-conceptual architecture, not a biographical subject. Directly relevant to the organism-environment relation in *Theoretische Biologie*.

**Gottfried Schnödl & Florian Sprenger, *Uexkülls Umgebungen: Umweltlehre und rechtes Denken*** (meson press, 2021; Open Access). The most sustained recent German engagement with Uexküll's conceptual system. Argues that **Planmäßigkeit, Einpassung, and the closed-Umwelt doctrine** are inseparable from reactionary political thought. Directly engages key terminology — essential as critical counterpoint even if the political-ideological lens is not the reader's primary concern.

**Florian Mildenberger, *Umwelt als Vision: Leben und Werk Jakob von Uexkülls*** (Franz Steiner, 2007). The Referenzwerk — first full scholarly biography, based on 43 archives. Includes a 60-page secondary bibliography. Stronger on biographical documentation than conceptual explication, but universally cited and indispensable as factual foundation.

### Important

**Friedrich Brock, "Die Grundlagen der Umweltforschung Jakob von Uexkülls und seiner Schule"** (*Zoologischer Anzeiger* Suppl. 12, 1939). A 52-page exposition by Uexküll's closest disciple and Hamburg successor — the most detailed contemporary account of how the conceptual system was operationalized as a working research program. Period piece but essential for grasping the terminology from the inside.

**Thure von Uexküll, "Die Zeichenlehre Jakob von Uexkülls"** in *Die Welt als Zeichen* (Severin und Siedler, 1981). The ~47-page definitive semiotic re-reading. Systematically translates Funktionskreis, Merkwelt, Wirkwelt into sign-theoretic terms.

**Kristian Köchy, "Plessners Biophilosophie als Erweiterung des Uexküll-Programms"** in *Zwischen den Kulturen* (Karl Alber, 2015). 40-page essay treating Uexküll's work as a conceptual "Programm" that Plessner's *Stufen des Organischen* extends.

**Mildenberger & Herrmann (eds.), *Uexküll: Umwelt und Innenwelt der Tiere*** (Springer, 2014). Critical commentary edition of the 1909 text with modern commentary on Funktionskreis, Umweltlehre, and Kant connections. Essential for tracing conceptual development toward the 1928 *Theoretische Biologie*.

### Key scholars

**Florian Mildenberger** (Europa-Universität Viadrina) — primary biographer. **Kristian Köchy** (Universität Kassel) — leading biophilosopher treating Uexküll as a philosophical program. **Gottfried Schnödl** (Leuphana) and **Florian Sprenger** (Ruhr-Universität Bochum) — critical rereading of Umweltlehre and politics. **Torsten Rüting** (Hamburg) — custodian of the Jakob von Uexküll-Archiv. **Thure von Uexküll** (1908–2004) — decisive for semiotic reinterpretation.

⚠️ **Flag**: A book-length study that takes the 1928 *Theoretische Biologie* as primary text and explicates the full conceptual architecture — Fügung, Einpassung, Umwelttunnel, the Merk- family — remains a **major desideratum**. Carlo Brentari's *Jakob von Uexküll* (Springer, 2015) is the closest sustained conceptual analysis but is in English (originally Italian), not German.

## 2. Ernst Jünger — German-language sources on *An der Zeitmauer*

The dedicated literature on this text is **remarkably sparse**. Despite Elfriede Jelinek's judgment that it is Jünger's "intellektuell vielleicht tiefsinnigstes Werk," scholarship overwhelmingly gravitates toward the Frühwerk (*In Stahlgewittern*, *Der Arbeiter*). The Spätwerk remains under-researched.

### Essential

**Peter Koslowski, *Der Mythos der Moderne: Die dichterische Philosophie Ernst Jüngers*** (Fink, 1991). The most philosophically ambitious German monograph on Jünger. Reads the oeuvre as "dichterische Philosophie" — chapters on the Erdgeschichte/Weltplan distinction, the dialectic of mythical and scientific temporality, and the "Historiker des Posthistoire." Directly engages the conceptual core of *An der Zeitmauer*.

**Peter Koslowski, "Die Rückkehr des Titanen Mensch zur Erde"** in Müller/Segeberg (eds.), *Ernst Jünger im 20. Jahrhundert* (Fink, 1995). The **single most important dedicated scholarly essay** on *An der Zeitmauer* — 30 pages analyzing the Titan figure, the end of linear Geschichte, Da-Sein/So-Sein, and Schicksalszeit vs. measurable time. Canonical close reading.

**Detlev Schöttker, Adnoten to *An der Zeitmauer*** (Klett-Cotta, 2013; 2nd ed. 2019). The scholarly annotated edition by the foremost living expert on Jünger's archives. Source identifications, intertextual references, and interpretive notes for all 186 numbered sections. Indispensable starting point for any close reading.

**Wojciech Kunicki, "An der Zeitmauer (1959)"** in Schöning (ed.), *Ernst Jünger-Handbuch* (Metzler, 2014). The authoritative Handbuch article — the standard academic reference entry in Germanistik.

### Important

**Martin Meyer, *Ernst Jünger*** (Hanser, 1990). The first comprehensive Werkbiographie (**698 pp.**), praised as "das mit Abstand beste, gründlichste und genaueste Buch" on Jünger. The chapter "Sein als Zeit" is directly relevant.

**Andrea Benedetti, "Schicksalszeit, Zeitalter des Wassermannes und ewige Gegenwart"** in Hagestedt/Benedetti (eds.), *Totalität als Faszination* (De Gruyter, 2018). Directly addresses Schicksalszeit, the Romantik reception (Novalis, Görres), and the tension between mythical and scientific temporality.

**Helmuth Kiesel, *Ernst Jünger: Die Biographie*** (Siedler, 2007). The standard academic biography by the leading Jünger scholar — treats *An der Zeitmauer* in its intellectual-biographical context (genesis, relationship to Teilhard de Chardin, Spengler, reception).

**Steffen Martus, *Ernst Jünger*** (Sammlung Metzler, 2001). The standard academic introduction, unusual in giving equal weight to the Spätwerk. Good entry point with comprehensive bibliography.

### Key scholars

**Detlev Schöttker** (ZfL Berlin) — editor of annotated edition; leading archival researcher. **Peter Koslowski** (†2012) — philosopher who produced the key essay and monograph. **Helmuth Kiesel** (Heidelberg, em.) — canonical biographer. **Matthias Schöning** (Konstanz) — editor of the Metzler Handbuch. **Andrea Benedetti** — important work on Schicksalszeit and mythico-temporal categories.

⚠️ **Flag**: There is **no book-length German monograph** devoted exclusively to *An der Zeitmauer*. The dedicated treatment essentially reduces to one 30-page essay (Koslowski 1995), one Handbuch article, and one annotated edition. A comprehensive Einzeluntersuchung remains a major desideratum. The recent Anthropocene-inflected rediscovery may produce new work.

## 3. Ernst Cassirer — German-language sources on *Das Erkenntnisproblem*, Vol. 1

The Cassirer-Forschung has overwhelmingly concentrated on the *Philosophie der symbolischen Formen* and the Davos disputation with Heidegger. **The *Erkenntnisproblem* has been treated as a "scholarly preliminary"** rather than a major philosophical achievement. Ralf Konersmann called it "ein philosophischer Schlüsseltext des 20. Jahrhunderts neu zu entdecken."

### Essential

**Massimo Ferrari, *Ernst Cassirer: Stationen einer philosophischen Biographie*** (Meiner, 2003; trans. from Italian). Contains the chapter **"Genese und Struktur des Erkenntnisproblems"** — the single most important treatment of EP's genesis, structure, and method. Shows how Cassirer's reading of Renaissance natural philosophy goes beyond Marburg neo-Kantianism. Ferrari is the indispensable scholar for this text.

**Massimo Ferrari, "'Wachstum' oder 'Revolution'? Ernst Cassirer und die Wissenschaftsgeschichte"** (*Berichte zur Wissenschaftsgeschichte* 35:2, 2012). The most focused German-language essay on Cassirer's historiography of science. Examines "growth" vs. "revolution," the treatment of Renaissance natural philosophy and Galilei, and the relationship to Koyré and Kuhn. Directly addresses the key concepts requested.

**Arno Schubbach, *Die Genese des Symbolischen: Zu den Anfängen von Ernst Cassirers Kulturphilosophie*** (Meiner, 2016). A **465-page** Habilitationsschrift tracing the path from EP to symbolic forms, based on extensive Beinecke Library archival work. The most thorough recent study of how EP's method of functional/relational thinking becomes the foundation for everything that follows.

**Thomas Knoppe, *Die theoretische Philosophie Ernst Cassirers*** (Meiner, 1992). The most rigorous systematic analysis of Cassirer's theoretical philosophy from Marburg foundations through EP. Demonstrates how the *Erkenntnisproblem* embodies the Marburg program while simultaneously transcending Cohen's narrower framework.

### Important

**Enno Rudolph, *Ernst Cassirer im Kontext: Kulturphilosophie zwischen Metaphysik und Historismus*** (Mohr Siebeck, 2003). Contains "Cassirers Rezeption des Renaissancehumanismus" and "Substanz als Funktion: Leibnizrezeption als Kantkritik bei Cassirer" — both directly address EP Vol. 1's Renaissance philosophy and the substance-to-function transition.

**Hector José González Ríos, "Vom Erkenntnisproblem bis zur Philosophie der symbolischen Formen: Ernst Cassirer als Leser von Cusanus"** (*Coincidentia* 4:2, 2014). Rare German-language essay directly tracing Cassirer's evolving reading of **Cusanus** from EP Vol. 1 to the symbolic forms.

**Birgit Recki, *Cassirer*** (Reclam, 2013). The most authoritative concise German introduction by the editor of the *Gesammelte Werke*. Contextualizes EP within Cassirer's intellectual biography and the Marburg years.

**Dorothea Frede & Reinold Schmücker (eds.), *Ernst Cassirers Werk und Wirkung*** (WBG, 1997). Contains Rudolph's essay on Cassirer's Renaissance humanism plus contributions by Habermas, Recki, and Krois.

### Key scholars

**Massimo Ferrari** (Turin) — the essential scholar for EP specifically, though Italian by training. **Birgit Recki** (Hamburg) — editor of the Hamburger Ausgabe; foremost institutional authority. **Enno Rudolph** (Lucerne, em.) — key on Renaissance reception and historiographic method. **Arno Schubbach** — most important recent archival-philological study. **Christian Möckel** (1952–2023, HU Berlin) — editor of Nachlass volumes.

⚠️ **Flag**: **No German-language monograph is devoted exclusively to *Das Erkenntnisproblem* Vol. 1.** EP's Renaissance material (Cusanus, Bruno, Campanella, Patrizzi) is almost exclusively treated in Rudolph's essays and the González Ríos article. A dedicated study of EP's historiographic method and its anticipation of later developments in history and philosophy of science remains a major desideratum.

## 4. Alexander Bogdanov — Russian-language sources on *Тектология*

The Russian secondary literature is significant but clusters around three moments: the 1967–72 "rehabilitation" (Setrov, Takhtajan), the **1989 republication** and its 1995 follow-up, and the 2016–2023 wave (Loktionov, Kleiner). The richest sources are paratextual — introductions and commentaries to editions of *Тектология* itself.

### Essential

**А. Л. Тахтаджян (Takhtajan), "Тектология: история и проблемы"** (*Системные исследования: Ежегодник*, Наука, 1971). THE foundational Russian scholarly analysis — a **77-page** essay by a world-class botanist that was the first major Soviet rehabilitation. Systematically maps tektology's organizational laws (подбор, ингрессия, эгрессия, дегрессия) and demonstrates structural anticipation of cybernetics and general systems theory. Canonical starting point for all subsequent scholarship.

**Г. Д. Гловели (Gloveli), introductory essay and commentary to the 2003/2023 critical edition of *Тектология*** (Финансы, 2003; reissued URSS, 2023). Gloveli is the preeminent textual scholar. His extensive apparatus traces the intellectual genealogy of tektological concepts (подбор, organizational crisis, structural stability), identifies sources and precursors, and provides the most detailed historiographic overview. **The standard scholarly edition.**

**М. В. Локтионов (Loktionov), *Александр Богданов между марксизмом и позитивизмом*** (Институт философии РАН, 2018). The most important recent book-length monograph — analyzes the tension between Marxist foundations and Machian/empirio-critical influences that produced tektology's unique philosophical stance. The closest thing to a monographic treatment of Bogdanov's theoretical system.

**М. В. Локтионов (ed.), *Александр Александрович Богданов*** (РОССПЭН, 2021). The most comprehensive recent collective volume, in the prestigious "Философия России первой половины XX века" series. Essays by leading philosophers actualizing Bogdanov's ideas, including tektology's relation to systems thinking.

### Important

**М. И. Сетров (Setrov), "Об общих элементах тектологии А. Богданова, кибернетики и теории систем"** (1967). The pioneering essay that first systematically demonstrated structural parallels between tektology, cybernetics, and systems theory. Historically crucial — opened the path for all subsequent rehabilitation.

**Н. Н. Моисеев (Moiseev), "Тектология А. А. Богданова — современные перспективы"** (*Вопросы философии* No. 8, 1995). Evaluates tektology from the standpoint of modern systems theory. Key nuance: Bogdanov anticipated cybernetic ideas including feedback, but was **not** a direct mathematical precursor. Places Bogdanov alongside Mendeleev and Vernadsky.

**В. Н. Садовский (Sadovsky), "История создания, теоретические основы и судьба эмпириомонизма А. А. Богданова"** (afterword to *Эмпириомонизм*, Республика, 2003). By one of the most important Soviet/Russian systems philosophers — the most authoritative analysis of the philosophical foundations from which tektology emerged.

**М. В. Локтионов, "А. А. Богданов как основоположник общей теории систем"** (*Философия науки и техники*, 2016). Argues Bogdanov should be credited as the founder of general systems theory before Bertalanffy. Systematically maps tektological concepts onto cybernetics (Wiener, Ashby), GST, synergetics, and autopoiesis.

**Г. Б. Клейнер (Kleiner), "Системная методология Александра Богданова"** (*Вопросы экономики* No. 3, 2023). The most current high-profile engagement, projecting tektology onto systemic economic theory.

### Key scholars

**Г. Д. Гловели** (Institute of Economics, RAS) — premier textual scholar; prepared all major edition commentaries. **М. В. Локтионов** (Institute of Philosophy, RAS) — leading current specialist. **А. Л. Тахтаджян** — foundational 1971 rehabilitation. **В. Н. Садовский** — Soviet/Russian systems philosophy; co-editor of 1989 edition. **Н. Н. Моисеев** — academician, systems modeler. **В. В. Попков** — afterword to 2003 edition; Тектологический альманах co-editor.

⚠️ **Flag**: There is **no comprehensive Russian monograph that reconstructs tektology purely as a theoretical system** — systematically unpacking подбор (progressive vs. conservative), organizational crisis, structural stability, and the дегрессия mechanism at monographic length. The tektology → Soviet cybernetics lineage is widely asserted but rarely demonstrated with detailed conceptual rigor. The special issue of *Вопросы философии* (No. 8, 1995) remains the richest concentrated source.

## 5. Nicolás Gómez Dávila — Spanish-language (and German) sources on the *Escolios*

Only **two book-length monographs** exist worldwide: Kinzel's in German (2003) and Abad Torres's in Spanish (2008). Colombian academic reception was slow — Gómez Dávila was largely ignored by the philosophical establishment in his lifetime — but has accelerated sharply since the **2013 centenary**, centered at the Javeriana, La Sabana, and the Andes.

### Essential (Spanish)

**Franco Volpi, *El solitario de Dios*** (Villegas Editores, 2005). Originally the prologue to the 2005 *Obra Completa*. The single most important interpretive text — reads Gómez Dávila not primarily as a political reactionary but as a genuinely philosophical thinker engaged with nihilism and the classical tradition. Interprets the "implicit text" as the ideal, perfect, merely imagined work in which the propositions are prolonged and fulfilled. **Indispensable starting point.**

**Alfredo Abad Torres, *Pensar lo implícito: en torno a Gómez Dávila*** (CRIE, Pereira, 2008). The first Spanish book-length scholarly study. Engages with the **form of the escolio as philosophically necessary**, gnosticism and modernity, the death of art as ontological-theological category. Abad is the leading Colombian academic specialist.

**Juan Fernando Mejía Mosquera (ed.), *Facetas del pensamiento de Nicolás Gómez Dávila*** (Pontificia Universidad Javeriana / Instituto Caro y Cuervo, 2018). The most philosophically sophisticated collective volume — product of a sustained seminar. Essays address aesthetics, fragmentary writing as philosophical practice, the relationship between *Notas*/*Textos* and the *Escolios*, and literary form. Engages seriously with form AND content.

**Bogdan Piotrowski (ed.), *Nicolás Gómez Dávila: Homenaje al centenario*** (Universidad de La Sabana, 2017). Centenary congress proceedings covering sources, conceptual dimensions, and reception. Best overview of the field as a whole.

### Important (Spanish)

**Francisco Pizano de Brigard, "Semblanza de un colombiano universal"** (*Revista del Colegio Mayor de Nuestra Señora del Rosario* No. 542, 1988). The foundational interpretive essay by Gómez Dávila's closest friend. Identifies the "implicit text" as the passage in *Textos I* where the theory of reaction and anthropological-metaphysical vision are laid out. The most cited hypothesis about the texto implícito.

**Guillermo Hoyos Vásquez, "Don Nicolás Gómez Dávila, pensador en español y reaccionario auténtico"** (*Arbor*, CSIC, No. 734, 2008). Phenomenological reading by the leading Colombian Husserlian — reads the "authentic reactionary" stance as analogous to phenomenological *epoché*, not political program.

**Michaël Rabier, "La cuestión literaria en la obra de Nicolás Gómez Dávila"** (*Perífrasis* No. 10, 2014). Directly addresses the literary question — Gómez Dávila's relationship to literature as form. Also: "Biblioteca gomezdaviliana" (*Revista Interamericana de Bibliotecología*, 2013) mapping actual sources.

**Nicolás Barguil Vallejo, *El arabesco de la inteligencia: para una poética de Nicolás Gómez Dávila*** (Pontificia Universidad Javeriana, 2013). Rare literary-critical study of the escolio as literary genre — the aesthetic dimensions and formal strategies (rhythm, semantic doubleness, intertextual echoes). **Uniquely addresses "aphoristic form as philosophically necessary."**

### German-language reception

**Till Kinzel, *Nicolás Gómez Dávila: Parteigänger verlorener Sachen*** (Edition Antaios, 2003; 5th ed. Lepanto Verlag, 2023). **The only book-length monograph on Gómez Dávila in any language** at first publication. Systematically covers all dimensions of the thought. Five editions make it the standard reference work.

**Karolinger Verlag translations** (Vienna, 1987–2020) — the vehicle of German reception, culminating in *Sämtliche Scholien zu einem inbegriffenen Text* (2020), the complete scholies numbered 1–10,370. Germany is where Gómez Dávila was first taken seriously as a *thinker*.

**Martin Mosebach (ed.), *Das Leben ist die Guillotine der Wahrheiten*** (Eichborn / Die Andere Bibliothek, 2006). Selection with important introduction by the Georg-Büchner-Preis laureate reading Gómez Dávila as a "Fragmentariker" whose books "open in concentric circles, not linearly."

### Key scholars

**Franco Volpi** (†2009, Padova) — foundational philosophical interpreter. **Alfredo Abad Torres** (Pereira) — leading Colombian specialist. **Juan Fernando Mejía Mosquera** (Javeriana) — seminar organizer; editor of *Facetas*. **Till Kinzel** (TU Berlin) — author of the only monograph. **Michaël Rabier** (France) — bibliographical and literary-formal specialist. **Enver Torregroza** (U. del Rosario) — phenomenological anthropology.

⚠️ **Flag**: The formal-literary dimension is especially underdeveloped. Despite Gómez Dávila's insistence that "in philosophy, whatever is not a fragment is a fraud," there is very little sustained analysis of *why* the aphoristic form is philosophically necessary or of the formal strategies by which the escolios work. Translation studies are almost nonexistent — the German reception (the most developed outside Spanish) raises questions about close vs. free translation that remain unaddressed.

## 6. Günther Anders — German-language sources on *Die Antiquiertheit des Menschen*, Bd. I

Anders scholarship has undergone a **remarkable renaissance since 2012** — the founding of the Internationale Günther Anders-Gesellschaft, the 2023 establishment of the Freiburg Forschungsstelle, and ongoing Nachlass publications at C.H. Beck have dramatically expanded the field.

### Essential

**Konrad Paul Liessmann, *Günther Anders: Philosophieren im Zeitalter der technologischen Revolutionen*** (C.H. Beck, 2002; latest ed. 2020). THE definitive German intellectual monograph. Liessmann had personal contact with Anders. Chapters directly address **prometheische Scham**, the Phantom/Matrize analysis of mass media, and the antiquatedness thesis. Closely engages Anders's conceptual vocabulary and the Heidegger relationship.

**Christian Dries, *Die Welt als Vernichtungslager*** (transcript, 2012; Open Access). The most theoretically ambitious secondary work — develops a critical theory of modernity drawing on Anders's *Antiquiertheit*, Arendt's *Vita activa*, and Jonas's *Prinzip Verantwortung*. Centers on the "Pandynatos-Prinzip" and the Herstellen/Vorstellen gap.

**Margret Lohmann, *Philosophieren in der Endzeit: Zur Gegenwartsanalyse von Günther Anders*** (Fink, 1996). The most extensive single-author monograph devoted to Anders's Gegenwartsanalyse. **370 pages** systematically reconstructing the philosophical argumentation of both *Antiquiertheit* volumes. Treats Anders as a serious philosopher, not merely a cultural critic.

**Ludger Lütkehaus, *Philosophieren nach Hiroshima*** (Fischer, 1992; rev. as *Schwarze Ontologie*, zu Klampen, 2002). Four studies covering Anders's nihilism, negative Kontingenzphilosophie, and the Heidegger inversion. Establishes the "schwarze Ontologie" framework. Strong on the relationship to Heidegger.

**Christian Dries, *Günther Anders*** (UTB/Fink, 2009). The standard academic introduction — systematic overview from early anthropological writings to the *Antiquiertheit*. Covers prometheisches Gefälle, Herstellen/Vorstellen gap, negative Anthropologie.

### Important

**Helmut Hildebrandt, *Weltzustand Technik*** (Metropol, 1990). The only book-length systematic comparison of Anders's and Heidegger's philosophies of technology. Essential for understanding how *Antiquiertheit* responds to and inverts Heidegger's "Frage nach der Technik."

**Marcel Müller, *Von der Weltfremdheit zur Antiquiertheit*** (Tectum, 2012). First monograph specifically devoted to Anders AS philosophical anthropologist. Traces continuities from the 1920s "Weltfremdheit" concept through to the 1956 work. Addresses whether *Die Antiquiertheit* qualifies as Philosophische Anthropologie in the Scheler-Plessner-Gehlen disciplinary sense.

**Christian Dries, *ad Günther Anders: Exerzitien für die Endzeit*** (EVA, 2023). The most recent monograph, drawing on extensive Nachlass research. Proposes a reassessment ("Neubewertung") based on previously unpublished manuscript material.

**Christian Dries, "Von der Weltfremdheit zur Antiquiertheit des Menschen"** — 100-page afterword in Anders, *Die Weltfremdheit des Menschen* (Beck, 2018). Functions as the definitive study of Anders's development from early negative anthropology to the *Antiquiertheit*.

**Liessmann (ed.), *Günther Anders kontrovers*** (Beck, 1992). Proceedings of the first international Anders symposium (Vienna, 1990). Multi-author landmark volume that opened serious academic discussion.

### Key scholars

**Christian Dries** (Universität Freiburg) — head of the Günther-Anders-Forschungsstelle since 2023; Obmann of IGAG; the dominant current figure. **Konrad Paul Liessmann** (Vienna, em.) — founding IGAG chairman; personal acquaintance of Anders; author of the standard monograph. **Ludger Lütkehaus** (Freiburg, em.) — early systematic philosopher of Anders. **Reinhard Ellensohn** and **Kerstin Putz** (Vienna/ÖNB) — co-editors of posthumous works and Nachlass researchers.

⚠️ **Flag**: Despite the renaissance, there is **no comprehensive commentary or Werkinterpretation devoted exclusively to *Die Antiquiertheit des Menschen, Band I*** as a standalone philosophical text — akin to what exists for *Sein und Zeit* or *Negative Dialektik*. All monographs treat it within broader engagements with Anders's oeuvre. The December 2025 first complete English translation (University of Minnesota Press) may catalyze further work.

## 7. Philip K. Dick — English-language sources on *The Exegesis*

The Exegesis's **8,000-page length**, resistance to systematization, and position between disciplines (philosophy, theology, psychology, literary criticism) means no single field has claimed it. The best scholarship is explicitly interdisciplinary and based on direct archival access.

### Essential

**Erik Davis, *High Weirdness: Drugs, Esoterica, and Visionary Experience in the Seventies*** (MIT Press / Strange Attractor, 2019). The single most important scholarly work — approximately one-third devoted to Dick. Davis had **direct archival access**, co-edited and annotated the 2011 *Exegesis*. Reads the Exegesis as "postmodern mystical writing," directly addressing recursive self-mapping, spiral epistemology, and the paranoia/pattern-recognition dialectic.

**Pamela Jackson & Jonathan Lethem (eds.), *The Exegesis of Philip K. Dick*: editorial apparatus** (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2011). Not merely a primary text but a critical edition. The introduction by Jackson & Lethem is a landmark essay on the Exegesis as literary-philosophical object. Annotations by eight scholars (Critchley, Davis, Hayles, Kripal, McKee, Doyle, Erickson, Gill) constitute the first sustained multi-perspectival engagement. Richard Doyle's afterword reads the Exegesis as an initiatory text. **The only scholarship based on all 8,000 unpublished pages.**

**Simon Critchley, "Philip K. Dick, Garage Philosopher"** (*The Stone* / NYT, 2012; expanded in *Bald*, Yale UP, 2021). By the **Hans Jonas Professor of Philosophy at The New School** and an Exegesis annotator. Takes Dick seriously as a philosopher who, using secondary sources and encyclopedias, produced "rapid chains of ideas" with "systematic coherence." Reads Dick's paranoid epistemology through Gnostic, Kantian, and Deleuzian lenses. The key entry point for understanding the Exegesis as philosophy.

**Gabriel McKee, *Pink Beams of Light from the God in the Gutter*** (University Press of America, 2003). The first book-length treatment of Dick's religious thought. McKee (Harvard Divinity School) traces the theological arc across the entire career — three sections on early, middle, and VALIS-era writing. Later served as an Exegesis annotator.

### Important

**Alexander Dunst & Stefan Schlensag (eds.), *The World According to Philip K. Dick*** (Palgrave Macmillan, 2015). Part IV, titled "EXEGESIS," contains three directly relevant chapters: Davis's "The Hymn of Philip K. Dick" (gnostic dimensions and writing practice), Doyle's "Stairway to Eleusis" (transformative philosophical practice), and Burton's "From Exegesis to Ecology" (systems-theory lens). **Densest concentration of Exegesis-specific academic essays.**

**Laurence A. Rickels, *I Think I Am: Philip K. Dick*** (University of Minnesota Press, 2010). Dense psychoanalytic-philosophical reading through Lacanian/Freudian theory — covers psychic reality, psychosis, Christian mysticism. Highly original but extremely challenging prose. Essential for psychoanalytic approaches.

**Kyle Arnold, *The Divine Madness of Philip K. Dick*** (Oxford UP, 2016). Psychobiography by a clinical psychologist arguing Dick did *not* have schizophrenia. Engages the Exegesis deeply as the primary document of Dick's post-1974 inner life. Important for the **paranoia vs. genuine pattern recognition** question.

**David Lapoujade, *Worlds Built to Fall Apart: Versions of Philip K. Dick*** (University of Minnesota Press, 2024; orig. Minuit, 2021). A Deleuzian philosopher reads Dick's oeuvre through "delirium" as a philosophical mode. Engages the Exegesis as "psychic autobiography" while maintaining that Dick's mysticism always retains sociopolitical critique. The most recent major philosophical treatment.

**Jeffrey J. Kripal, *Mutants and Mystics*** (University of Chicago Press, 2011). Extended analysis of VALIS and Dick's visionary experience within the "Realization" and "Authorization" mythemes — the discovery that one is being written by a larger story. Important for the **recursive self-mapping** theme. Kripal supervised Davis's PhD and served as an Exegesis annotator.

### Key scholars

**Erik Davis** (Rice PhD; independent) — co-editor, annotator, *High Weirdness*; the central figure. **Pamela Jackson** (UC Berkeley) — co-editor who led archival work. **Simon Critchley** (The New School) — most prominent professional philosopher engaged with Dick. **Jeffrey J. Kripal** (Rice) — institutional legitimator; supervised Davis. **Richard Doyle** (Penn State) — Exegesis afterword; ecological/consciousness reading. **Gabriel McKee** (Harvard Divinity) — pioneer of theological analysis. **N. Katherine Hayles** (UCLA/Duke) — cybernetic/posthuman framework; annotator.

⚠️ **Flag**: **There is no single book-length monograph devoted exclusively to the Exegesis as a philosophical/theological text.** Davis's *High Weirdness* comes closest but covers three subjects. The best Exegesis-specific scholarship consists of dispersed essays and chapters. The spiral, recursive, self-contradictory structure of the 8,000-page archive — the very feature that makes it philosophically interesting — also makes it resistant to conventional scholarly treatment. Only ~1,000 pages are published; the rest requires archival visits. A sustained monograph on the Exegesis's epistemological structure, thread architecture, and cosmological system remains the most obvious gap.

## Cross-cutting observations

Three patterns emerge across all seven cases. First, **the most conceptually engaged scholarship often comes from outside the thinker's home discipline**: a botanist (Takhtajan) for Bogdanov, a philosopher of religion (Davis) for Dick, an Italian philosopher (Ferrari) for Cassirer's German text, and a philosopher (Koslowski) rather than a Germanist for Jünger. Disciplinary homes can paradoxically inhibit close conceptual reading.

Second, **critical editions and paratextual apparatus** — Schöttker's Adnoten, Gloveli's commentary, the 2011 Exegesis annotations, Thure von Uexküll's introductions — frequently constitute the best available secondary scholarship, especially where dedicated monographs are absent.

Third, every thinker except Anders shows a **persistent gap between cultural reputation and dedicated conceptual scholarship in the source language**. The text that made each thinker important is often the text least studied on its own terms. This suggests a systematic tendency in academic reception: once a thinker enters the canon, scholars engage with what the thinker *means for* other fields rather than with what the thinker actually *said*.
