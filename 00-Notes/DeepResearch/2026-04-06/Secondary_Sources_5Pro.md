Below is a research-first bibliography keyed to your use-case: secondary work that stays as close as possible to the author’s own lexicon rather than flattening it into external summaries. Where a field is genuinely thin, I say so; where an item is really an annotated edition or archival gateway rather than “secondary” in the narrow sense, I flag it.

## 1) Jakob von Uexküll — German-language secondary sources

German conceptual work on Uexküll definitely exists, but it is relatively compact; the strongest material is dispersed across dissertations, essays, and commentary-rich editions rather than one dominant monographic tradition. ([RWTH Publications][1])

**Essential**

1. **Charlotte Helbach, *Die Umweltlehre Jakob von Uexkülls: Ein Beispiel für die Genese von Theorien in der Biologie zu Beginn des 20. Jahrhunderts* (1989).** Still the foundational German study of how Umweltlehre is built as a biological theory; the best older starting point if you want Uexküll’s terminology treated as a system rather than as later reception. ([RWTH Publications][1])

2. **Thure von Uexküll, “Die Umweltlehre Jakob von Uexkülls und die Wissenschaften vom Menschen” (1979).** Strong from inside the vocabulary itself, especially for Innenwelt/Umwelt, sign-relations, and the extension of Uexküll’s categories into anthropological and medical thinking. ([Merkur][2])

3. **Thure von Uexküll, “Die Zeichenlehre Jakob von Uexkülls” (1981).** One of the most useful German pieces for the sign-theoretical side of the Merk-family and for the internal logic of object-relations in Uexküll. ([DNB Portal][3])

4. **Thomas Potthast, “Lebensführung (in) der Dialektik von Innenwelt und Umwelt – Jakob von Uexküll, seine philosophische Rezeption und die Transformation des Begriffs ‘Funktionskreis’ in der Ökologie” (2014).** Directly valuable on Innenwelt/Umwelt and Funktionskreis, especially if you want to keep the term from dissolving into a loose ecological metaphor. ([Universität Tübingen][4])

5. **Florian Mildenberger und Bernd Herrmann, “Die zentrale Bedeutung des Werks *Umwelt und Innenwelt der Tiere* mit Stellenkommentar” (2014).** Not limited to *Theoretische Biologie*, but one of the best close-commentary pieces around the Uexküllian lexicon and its textual articulation. ([Springer][5])

**Important**

1. **Manfred D. Laubichler, “Theoretische Biologie” (2020).** A concise, authoritative entry that frames the book as an anti-mechanistic biology of perceiving and acting subjects; useful as an orientation before diving into the denser specialist pieces. ([Springer][6])

2. **Florian Höfer, *Die Notwendigkeit der Kommunikation: Die Missachtung eines Phänomens bei Jakob von Uexküll* (2007).** Specialized, but worthwhile because it tests the limits of Umweltlehre from within rather than replacing it with an external vocabulary. ([DNB Portal][3])

3. **Katja Kynast, “Kinematografie als Medium der Umweltforschung Jakob von Uexkülls” (2010).** Helpful for linking the conceptual system to method and observation, especially around Merkwelt/Wirkwelt as research practice rather than only doctrine. ([edoc][7])

**Key scholars**

Charlotte Helbach, Thure von Uexküll, Thomas Potthast, Bernd Herrmann, Florian Mildenberger, Manfred D. Laubichler, Katja Kynast, Florian Höfer. ([RWTH Publications][1])

## 2) Ernst Jünger — German-language secondary sources on *An der Zeitmauer*

If you insist on *An der Zeitmauer* rather than Jünger’s oeuvre in general, this is the thinnest German-language field on your list: the best material is spread across annotated editions, handbook entries, and a handful of essays, not a deep monographic tradition. ([Springer][8])

**Essential**

1. **Wojciech Kunicki, “An der Zeitmauer (1959)” in *Ernst Jünger-Handbuch* (2014).** The best concise German map of the book itself. ([Springer][8])

2. **Anja Keith und Detlev Schöttker (Hrsg.), *Ernst Jünger: An der Zeitmauer. Mit Adnoten von Detlev Schöttker* (2019).** Strictly speaking an edition rather than a monograph, but indispensable because the annotations do much of the close-reading work the secondary literature otherwise leaves undone. ([ZfL Berlin][9])

3. **Peter Koslowski, “Die Rückkehr des Titanen Mensch zur Erde und das Ende der ‘Geschichte’. Jüngers Essay *An der Zeitmauer* (1959)” (1995).** One of the very few dedicated essays on the book as a philosophical text of history and time. ([Springer][10])

4. **Heinz-Albert Becker, “Sanduhr- und Schicksalszeit. Temporäres im Werk Ernst Jüngers” (2000).** Essential for Schicksalszeit versus measurable time, and for how Jünger folds mythic temporality into historical consciousness. ([DNB Portal][11])

5. **Alexander Honold, “Der Sand in den Uhren. Ernst Jüngers Poetik der fünfziger Jahre zwischen Naturgewalt und Zeitgenossenschaft” (2012).** The strongest item I found for situating *Zeitmauer* inside the time-questions of Jünger’s 1950s prose. ([De Gruyter Brill][12])

**Important**

1. **Andrea Benedetti, “’Schicksalszeit’, ‘Zeitalter des Wassermannes’ und ‘ewige Gegenwart’: Ernst Jüngers Rezeption der deutschen Romantik … zwischen Mythos, Moderne und Postmoderne” (2018).** Very useful on Schicksalszeit and the tension between mythic and scientific temporality. ([De Gruyter Brill][13])

2. **Harro Segeberg, “Schreiben an der ›Zeitmauer‹. Der Autor und sein Mythos” (1996).** Helpful on the self-staging and intellectual position of the late essay. ([Springer][10])

3. **Ingo Stöckmann, “Jüngers Spätwerk” (2012).** Broader than *Zeitmauer*, but useful as a frame for the late vocabulary and its philosophical drift. ([De Gruyter Brill][14])

4. **Stephan Lesker, “’Spätherbst eines Äons’: Krise und Krisenbewältigung im Werk Ernst Jüngers …” (2018).** Good on late Jünger’s crisis consciousness and epochal threshold-thinking. ([De Gruyter Brill][15])

5. **Peter Koslowski, *Der Mythos der Moderne. Die dichterische Philosophie Ernst Jüngers* (1991).** Broader book-length study, but still one of the better German routes into Jünger’s philosophy of time and history. ([Springer][10])

**Key scholars**

Wojciech Kunicki, Detlev Schöttker, Peter Koslowski, Heinz-Albert Becker, Alexander Honold, Harro Segeberg, Ingo Stöckmann, Andrea Benedetti, Stephan Lesker. ([Springer][8])

## 3) Ernst Cassirer — German-language secondary sources on *Das Erkenntnisproblem*

German-language work here is strong, but it concentrates more on Cassirer’s problem-historical and historiographic method than on line-by-line commentary on volume 1 alone; specifically on the Renaissance chapters, the literature is thinner than on the method as such. ([ResearchGate][16])

**Essential**

1. **Rainer A. Bast, *Problem, Geschichte, Form. Das Verhältnis von Philosophie und Geschichte bei Ernst Cassirer im historischen Kontext* (2000).** The major monograph on the philosophy/history relation in Cassirer and still the best single starting point for the method of *Erkenntnisproblem*. ([Duncker & Humblot][17])

2. **Herbert Kopp-Oberstebrink, *Das Verhältnis von Systematik und Historie in Ernst Cassirers philosophie- und wissenschaftsgeschichtlichen Arbeiten: Untersuchungen zum frühen Werk* (2015).** Deep study of the early work with sustained attention to *Erkenntnisproblem*, *Substanzbegriff und Funktionsbegriff*, and the system/history nexus. ([Elekpub][18])

3. **Massimo Ferrari, “Wachstum oder Revolution? Ernst Cassirer und die Wissenschaftsgeschichte” (2012).** A compact must-read on how Cassirer writes the history of science and intellectual development. ([PhilPapers][19])

4. **Martina Plümacher, “Die Einheit der Regel der Veränderung. Zur Bedeutung der Wissenschaftsrezeption für Cassirers Theorie des Wissens” (1996).** Especially useful for your interest in philosophy and science as coequal symptoms of one development. ([Revistas UdeA][20])

5. **Servanne Jollivet, “Die Kulturphilosophie Cassirers in der Historismus-Debatte” (2016).** Best route into Cassirer’s problem-historical method as an answer to historicism rather than as mere erudite survey. ([Filozófia Tanszék][21])

**Important**

1. **Detlev Pätzold, “Ernst Cassirers Philosophiebegriff” in *Kultur und Symbol* (2003).** Good conceptual orientation on Cassirer’s notion of philosophy, including the relation of history, science, and symbolic form. ([DNB Portal][22])

2. **Guido Kreis, *Cassirer und die Formen des Geistes* (2010).** Broad rather than book-specific, but philosophically sharp and very useful once you have the method in view. ([Suhrkamp Verlag][23])

3. **Oswald Schwemmer, *Ernst Cassirer: Ein Philosoph der europäischen Moderne* (1997).** A classic large-scale study with strong attention to the unity of Cassirer’s project. ([WorldCat][24])

4. **Thomas Meyer, *Kulturphilosophie in gefährlicher Zeit. Zum Werk Ernst Cassirers* (2007).** Broader frame, but helpful for situating the early history-of-knowledge project within the whole oeuvre. ([Lit Verlag][25])

**Key scholars**

Rainer A. Bast, Herbert Kopp-Oberstebrink, Massimo Ferrari, Martina Plümacher, Servanne Jollivet, Detlev Pätzold, Guido Kreis, Oswald Schwemmer, Thomas Meyer. ([Duncker & Humblot][17])

## 4) Alexander Bogdanov — Russian-language secondary sources on *Тектология*

Russian-language work on *Тектология* as theory is real, but it is concentrated in a small cluster of essays, the 1995 revival around *Вопросы философии*, and a few recent collections rather than a large standalone monographic tradition. ([Chelovek Nauka][26])

**Essential**

1. **С. Н. Пустильник, “Принцип подбора как основа тектологии А. А. Богданова” (1995).** The most directly relevant item for your interest in *подбор* as a category broader than Darwinian selection. ([Chelovek Nauka][26])

2. **Г. Д. Гловели, “Тектология: генеалогия и историография” (2003).** Essential orientation to the internal genesis of tektology and the way the concept-system hangs together. ([Vestnik TSPU][27])

3. **М. В. Локтионов, “А. А. Богданов как основоположник общей теории систем” (2016).** The best concise Russian article on *Тектология* as a general theory of systems and organization rather than as political biography. ([Philosophy of Science and Technology][28])

4. **Г. Д. Гловели, “Среднерусский марксизм и системное мышление: организационная наука А. А. Богданова” (2021).** Long, substantial essay on organizational science, crisis, and system-thinking in Bogdanov. ([HSE Publications][29])

5. **В. Е. Лепский и Е. С. Слепцов, “Влияние тектологии А. А. Богданова на становление постнеклассической кибернетики саморазвивающихся полисубъектных сред” (2018).** The most useful targeted piece on the line from tektology to later cybernetic/systemic thinking. ([ResearchGate][30])

**Important**

1. **М. В. Локтионов, *Александр Богданов между марксизмом и позитивизмом* (2018).** Not only about tektology, but very helpful for the philosophical stakes behind the organizational turn. ([Institute of Philosophy RAS][31])

2. **Н. Н. Моисеев, “Тектология А. А. Богданова — современные перспективы” (1995).** A classic revival essay on why Bogdanov mattered for later systems thought. ([Dissercat][32])

3. **А. Б. Бахур, “Идеи А. А. Богданова как основание методологии организационно-управленческого моделирования” (2021).** More applied, but good for seeing how the organizational categories continue to be worked with conceptually. ([HSE Publications][33])

4. **К. Н. Любутин и В. Д. Толмачёв, *Александр Богданов: от философии к тектологии* (2005).** The best broader bridge from Bogdanov’s earlier philosophy to the organizational science. ([Google Books][34])

**Key scholars**

М. В. Локтионов, Г. Д. Гловели, С. Н. Пустильник, Н. Н. Моисеев, В. Е. Лепский, Е. С. Слепцов, А. Б. Бахур, К. Н. Любутин, В. Д. Толмачёв. ([Philosophy of Science and Technology][28])

## 5) Nicolás Gómez Dávila — Spanish-language secondary sources, plus German reception

Serious Spanish-language scholarship exists, especially in Colombia, but the field is still relatively small; the best work tends to appear in essay collections, annotated editions, and a handful of articles rather than in a large monographic industry. The German reception is also important, and it did indeed take Gómez Dávila seriously earlier than the Anglophone world did. ([JSTOR][35])

**Essential**

1. **Ernesto Volkening; ed. Alfredo Abad, Francia Elena Goenaga y Efrén Giraldo, *Diario de lectura de los Escolios de Nicolás Gómez Dávila: Cuadernos I y II* (2020).** Probably the single most valuable close-reading document: not a standard monograph, but an extended reading diary of the *Escolios* with scholarly apparatus. ([dokumen.pub][36])

2. **Tomás Felipe Molina Peláez, “Los epígrafes en los *Escolios* de Nicolás Gómez Dávila: hacia una lectura intertextual” (2019).** Essential for the “implicit text,” intertext, and the way the epigraphic apparatus governs reading. ([Revistas Javeriana][37])

3. **“Biblioteca gomezdaviliana: las fuentes bibliográficas del pensamiento de Nicolás Gómez Dávila (I)” (2013).** Crucial for reconstructing the “implicit library,” which is probably the most concrete path toward the implicit text. ([SciELO][38])

4. **Alfredo Abad (comp.), *Entre fragmentos. Interpretaciones gomezdavilianas* (2017).** The best collective volume on form, reading, and the philosophical use of fragment/aphorism; especially strong is Michaël Rabier’s essay on Gómez Dávila’s way of writing and reading. ([Casa de Asterión Ediciones][39])

5. **Juan Fernando Mejía Mosquera (ed.), *Facetas del pensamiento de Nicolás Gómez Dávila* (2018).** Strong Colombian collection; the most useful pieces for your purposes are Francia Elena Goenaga’s essay on the successive formation of the work and the Goenaga/Barguil piece on style. ([JSTOR][35])

**Important**

1. **Francia Elena Goenaga y Nicolás Antonio Barguil Vallejo, “El eclecticismo de un estilo” (2018).** Very good on why the form is not decorative but integral to thought. ([JSTOR][35])

2. **Michaël Rabier, “Una cierta idea de la filosofía: acerca de la recepción y no recepción de Nicolás Gómez Dávila en Francia” (2019).** Useful on Gómez Dávila’s anti-modernism as philosophy rather than mere ideological posture. ([AmeliCA][40])

3. **Silvia Lavina, “La idiosincrasia antimoderna de Nicolás Gómez Dávila” (2012).** Especially relevant because it treats the aphoristic/escolio form as philosophically necessary, not just stylistic. ([Eikasia][41])

4. **Franco Volpi, *Nicolás Gómez Dávila, el solitario de Dios* (2005).** A major interpretive introduction from one of the first high-level non-Colombian readers to take him seriously as a thinker. ([Google Books][42])

5. **Till Kinzel, *Nicolás Gómez Dávila: Parteigänger verlorener Sachen* (2005).** The key German-language reception book and the place to start if you want the early serious German engagement. ([PhilPapers][43])

**Key scholars**

Alfredo Abad, Francia Elena Goenaga, Efrén Giraldo, Tomás Felipe Molina Peláez, Michaël Rabier, Juan Fernando Mejía Mosquera, Franco Volpi, Till Kinzel, Nicolás Antonio Barguil Vallejo. ([JSTOR][35])

## 6) Günther Anders — German-language secondary sources on *Die Antiquiertheit des Menschen*

German-language scholarship on *Die Antiquiertheit des Menschen* is substantial and conceptually serious; unlike the Jünger field, there is a genuine line of work here on philosophical anthropology, media critique, and the human/apparatus relation. ([Zeitgeschichte Digital][44])

**Essential**

1. **Margret Lohmann, *Philosophieren in der Endzeit. Zur Gegenwartsanalyse von Günther Anders* (1996).** One of the earliest major monographs and still important for the architecture of Anders’s diagnosis of the present. ([Google Books][45])

2. **Konrad Paul Liessmann, *Günther Anders: Philosophieren im Zeitalter der technologischen Revolutionen* (2002).** The classic compact German book on Anders; especially good on Promethean shame, apparatuses, virtuality, and technological modernity. ([Google Books][46])

3. **Marcel Müller, *Von der „Weltfremdheit“ zur „Antiquiertheit“. Philosophische Anthropologie bei Günther Anders* (2012).** The best monograph for reading *Antiquiertheit* as philosophical anthropology rather than as mere political warning. ([Dussmann - Das Kulturkaufhaus][47])

4. **Daniel Morat, “Die Aktualität der Antiquiertheit. Günther Anders’ Anthropologie des industriellen Zeitalters” (2006).** The most useful shorter piece on volume I as a structured anthropological critique of industrial modernity. ([Zeitgeschichte Digital][44])

5. **Christian Dries, *Günther Anders* (2009).** Concise but indispensable because Dries is one of the central contemporary Anders scholars and writes with close attention to the conceptual vocabulary. ([University of Freiburg][48])

**Important**

1. **Christian Dries, “Technischer Totalitarismus: Macht, Herrschaft und Gewalt bei Günther Anders” (2013).** Good for extending the anthropological critique into Anders’s theory of power and domination. ([Units][49])

2. **Peter Schmitt, *Medienkritik zwischen Anthropologie und Gesellschaftstheorie. Zur Aktualität von Günther Anders und Theodor W. Adorno* (2020).** The book I would go to for Anders on mass-mediated experience and media critique. ([Günther Anders Gesellschaft][50])

3. **Christina Nurawar Sani, “Nicht-in-der-Welt-sein innerhalb des Auf-der-Welt-seins. Phänomenologische Betrachtungen … im Ausgang von Martin Heidegger” (2017).** Best targeted item I found for the Anders–Heidegger relation from within the phenomenological-anthropological problematic. ([Günther Anders Gesellschaft][51])

4. **Gabriele Althaus, *Leben zwischen Sein und Nichts. Drei Studien zu Günther Anders* (1989).** Older, but still worth knowing as part of the early serious philosophical reception. ([SearchWorks][52])

**Key scholars**

Konrad Paul Liessmann, Christian Dries, Marcel Müller, Margret Lohmann, Daniel Morat, Peter Schmitt, Christina Nurawar Sani, Gabriele Althaus. ([Google Books][46])

## 7) Philip K. Dick — serious work on the *Exegesis*

On the *Exegesis* specifically, the serious literature is still much thinner than the Dick-on-the-novels literature. The real core is a small cluster: the archival editors, Erik Davis, Gabriel McKee, and the essays gathered in the 2015 *World According to Philip K. Dick* volume. ([WorldCat][53])

**Essential**

1. **Erik Davis, “The Hymn of Philip K. Dick: Reading, Writing and Gnosis in the ‘Exegesis’” (2015).** The best single scholarly essay on the *Exegesis* as a textual and philosophical enterprise rather than as pathology. ([Springer][54])

2. **Gabriel McKee, *Pink Beams of Light from the God in the Gutter: The Science-Fictional Religion of Philip K. Dick* (2003).** The strongest book-length study of Dick’s religious thought, drawing on fiction but also on private writings and correspondence. ([Bloomsbury Publishing][55])

3. **Hélène Canaan, “Time and Gnosis in the Writings of Philip K. Dick” (2008).** Very useful for temporality, gnosis, and the recursive structure of Dick’s late thought. ([JSTOR][56])

4. **Richard Doyle, “Stairway to Eleusis, or: Perennially Philip K. Dick” (2015).** Strong on recursive consciousness, psychedelic/theological patterning, and the philosophical stakes of Dick’s late archive. ([Springer][57])

5. **James Burton, “From Exegesis to Ecology” (2015).** Helpful for tracking how recurrent threads in the archive mutate into a broader philosophical-cosmological system. ([Springer][57])

**Important**

1. **Lawrence Sutin (ed.), *In Pursuit of VALIS: Selections from the Exegesis* (1991).** Not a secondary study in the strict sense, but an unavoidable early archival gateway by the scholar-biographer who first brought this material into print. ([Internet Archive][58])

2. **Pamela Jackson und Jonathan Lethem (eds.), *The Exegesis of Philip K. Dick* (2011), with annotations by Erik Davis.** Again not purely secondary, but indispensable because it is the definitive curated presentation of thousands of pages and because Davis’s annotations are interpretively important. ([WorldCat][53])

3. **Alexander Dunst und Stefan Schlensag (eds.), *The World According to Philip K. Dick* (2015).** The most important collected volume for Exegesis-focused scholarship because it contains the Davis, Doyle, and Burton cluster. ([Springer][57])

4. **David G. Robertson, “The Exegesis of Philip K. Dick” (2013).** Short, but useful as a religion-studies reception piece on the archive itself. ([Taylor & Francis Online][59])

**Key scholars**

Erik Davis, Gabriel McKee, Lawrence Sutin, Pamela Jackson, Jonathan Lethem, Richard Doyle, Hélène Canaan, James Burton, Alexander Dunst. ([Springer][57])

---

The genuinely thinnest fields here, on your exact terms, are Jünger on *An der Zeitmauer*, Russian conceptual work on Bogdanov’s *Тектология* as such, and Exegesis-focused Dick scholarship. The strongest source-language conceptual traditions in your list are Uexküll, Cassirer, and Anders; Gómez Dávila sits in the middle—serious and real, but still comparatively small and heavily concentrated in Colombian collections and a few major interpreters. ([Springer][8])

A useful next pass would be to turn this into a ranked reading order for each author—“start here, then widen out”—instead of a flat bibliography.
