## Tier 0 — OG contemporaneous sources (Erkenntnisproblem field)

These are not later scholars looking back. They are witnesses to the original conversation — direct reviewers, same-problem interlocutors, and institutional participants from Cassirer's own generation. Source: `00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-08/Secondary_Sources_OGs_5Pro.md`.

### Direct reviews and critiques

0a. `OG` **Richard Hönigswald, "Substanzbegriff und Funktionsbegriff. Kritische Betrachtungen"** (*Deutsche Literaturzeitung* 33, 1912, cols. 2821-2843 and 2885-2902). The most important old-style direct critique of *Substanzbegriff* from inside German Erkenntniskritik. Two-part review.
   - Where to look: *Deutsche Literaturzeitung* archive, HathiTrust, academic libraries

0b. `OG` **Moritz Schlick, "Kritizistische oder empiristische Deutung der neuen Physik?"** (*Kant-Studien* 26, 1921, 96-111). The essential direct controversy piece — Schlick vs. Cassirer on the Einstein book, in the flagship journal, while the relativity debate was still open.
   - Where to look: De Gruyter / *Kant-Studien* archive, JSTOR

0c. `OG` **Gerard Heymans, "Zur Cassirerschen Reform der Begriffslehre"** (*Kant-Studien* 33, 1928, 109-128). Later contemporaneous assessment of *Substanzbegriff*; one of the few pieces modern scholarship still points back to.
   - Where to look: De Gruyter / *Kant-Studien* archive

0d. `OG` **Uebinger, review of *Das Erkenntnisproblem*** (*Philosophisches Jahrbuch* 22, 1909, 80-82). The clearest early German review of EP itself — almost the only direct contemporaneous reception.
   - Where to look: *Philosophisches Jahrbuch* archive

### Same-problem interlocutors

0e. `OG` **Hans Reichenbach, *Relativitätstheorie und Erkenntnis apriori*** (1920). Explicitly engages Cassirer's just-appeared Einstein book as the first major neo-Kantian attempt on general relativity. Links Cassirer's dissolution of the substance concept to relativity. Also contains Hilbert/implicit-definition material.
   - Where to look: **Project Gutenberg** (free, downloadable now: gutenberg.org/cache/epub/57240)

0f. `OG` **Ewald Sellien, *Die erkenntnistheoretische Bedeutung der Relativitätstheorie*** (1919, *Kant-Studien* Ergänzungsheft 48). Pre-Cassirer neo-Kantian relativity benchmark.
   - Where to look: **Internet Archive** (free: archive.org/details/dieerkenntnisthe00sell)

0g. `OG` **Ilse Schneider (Lengyel), *Das Raum-Zeit-Problem bei Kant und Einstein*** (Springer, 1921). Same-year neo-Kantian relativity book; closest contemporaneous comparandum for Cassirer's Einstein book.
   - Where to look: **Internet Archive** (free: archive.org/details/dasraumzeitprobl00leng)

### Warburg/Hamburg field context

0h. `OG` **Cassirer, "Der Begriff der symbolischen Form im Aufbau der Geisteswissenschaften"** in *Vorträge der Bibliothek Warburg* 1921-1922, pp. 11-39. Cassirer as active participant in the Hamburg field, not as object of commentary.
   - Where to look: Heidelberg digitized historical collections (digi.ub.uni-heidelberg.de)

0i. `OG` **Cassirer, *Individuum und Kosmos in der Philosophie der Renaissance*** (*Studien der Bibliothek Warburg*, Band 10, 1927). The key older Warburg-side item for the Renaissance natural-philosophy line that EP Vol. 1 covers.
   - Where to look: Heidelberg digitized collections, academic libraries

### Reconcentration volumes

0j. `OG` **Raymond Klibansky and H. J. Paton (eds.), *Philosophy and History: Essays Presented to Ernst Cassirer*** (Oxford: Clarendon, 1936). The best old-style volume for seeing Cassirer inside a living field. Edited with Warburg Institute support. Key essays: Huizinga on the concept of history, Edgar Wind on contact between history and natural sciences.
   - Where to look: academic libraries, used bookshops, possibly digitized

0k. `OG` **Paul Arthur Schilpp (ed.), *The Philosophy of Ernst Cassirer*** (Library of Living Philosophers, 1949). Stronger on the science-method side than usually remembered. Key essays: Kaufmann on scientific knowledge, Gawronsky on epistemology of physics, Smart on mathematical concepts, Solmitz on Galileo, Randall on Renaissance thought.
   - Where to look: Library of Living Philosophers series, academic libraries, used bookshops, widely available

## Tier 1 — Immediate (Erkenntnisproblem + Zeitmauer active now)

### Erkenntnisproblem

1. `2` **Massimo Ferrari, "Wachstum oder Revolution? Ernst Cassirer und die Wissenschaftsgeschichte"** (*Berichte zur Wissenschaftsgeschichte* 35:2, 2012). The most focused essay on EP's historiographic method. Directly addresses growth vs. revolution, Renaissance natural philosophy, and the Koyré/Kuhn relationship. Both bibliographies name this as essential.
   - Where to look: journal article, likely available through academic databases or ResearchGate

2. `2` **Massimo Ferrari, *Ernst Cassirer: Stationen einer philosophischen Biographie*** (Meiner, 2003). Chapter "Genese und Struktur des Erkenntnisproblems" is the single most important treatment of EP's genesis and method. Opus names this as indispensable.
   - Where to look: Meiner Verlag, used academic bookshops, WorldCat

3. `2` ⚠️ **Hector José González Ríos, "Vom Erkenntnisproblem bis zur Philosophie der symbolischen Formen: Ernst Cassirer als Leser von Cusanus"** (*Coincidentia* 4:2, 2014). Directly relevant to the Cusanus chain you're building. The only identified essay tracing Cassirer's evolving Cusanus reading from EP to symbolic forms.
   - Where to look: *Coincidentia* journal (Cusanus-Gesellschaft), possibly via PhilPapers or JSTOR
   - ⚠️ verify: journal issue, exact title, author affiliation

4. `2` **Enno Rudolph, "Cassirers Rezeption des Renaissancehumanismus"** in *Ernst Cassirer im Kontext* (Mohr Siebeck, 2003). Directly addresses EP Vol. 1's Renaissance philosophy — Cusanus, Bruno, the substance-to-function transition.
   - Where to look: Mohr Siebeck, academic libraries

5. `2` **Rainer A. Bast, *Problem, Geschichte, Form*** (Duncker & Humblot, 2000). 5Pro names this as the best single starting point for EP's method. The philosophy/history relation in Cassirer.
   - Where to look: Duncker & Humblot, used academic bookshops

### Zeitmauer

6. `2` **Detlev Schöttker (Hrsg.), *Ernst Jünger: An der Zeitmauer. Mit Adnoten*** (Klett-Cotta, 2nd ed. 2019). The annotated edition. Source identifications, intertextual references, and interpretive notes for all 186 sections. Both bibliographies call this indispensable.
   - Where to look: Klett-Cotta, German bookshops, ZVAB

7. `2` **Peter Koslowski, "Die Rückkehr des Titanen Mensch zur Erde"** in Müller/Segeberg (eds.), *Ernst Jünger im 20. Jahrhundert* (Fink, 1995). The single most important dedicated essay on Zeitmauer. 30 pages on the Titan figure, Da-Sein/So-Sein, Schicksalszeit. Both bibliographies agree.
   - Where to look: essay in edited volume, Fink Verlag, academic libraries, ZVAB

8. `2` **Peter Koslowski, *Der Mythos der Moderne: Die dichterische Philosophie Ernst Jüngers*** (Fink, 1991). Book-length study; chapters on Erdgeschichte/Weltplan, mythical vs. scientific temporality. The broader companion to the 1995 essay.
   - Where to look: Fink Verlag, used academic bookshops, ZVAB

9. `4` **Hans Blumenberg, *Der Mann vom Mond: Über Ernst Jünger*** (Suhrkamp, 2007). Sustained confrontation from Blumenberg's estate — unpublished essays combining admiration for Jünger's style with relentless criticism. All three thinker-on-thinker documents flag this.
   - Where to look: Suhrkamp, widely available

## Tier 2 — Next return (Escolios, Tektologiya)

### Escolios

10. `2` ⚠️ **Ernesto Volkening (ed. Abad, Goenaga, Giraldo), *Diario de lectura de los Escolios de Nicolás Gómez Dávila: Cuadernos I y II*** (2020). An extended reading diary of the Escolios with scholarly apparatus. Someone else doing what you're doing — sequential close reading with notes. 5Pro's strongest Escolios find.
    - Where to look: Colombian academic publishers, dokumen.pub has a listing
    - ⚠️ verify: publisher, availability, whether this is accessible outside Colombia

11. `4` **Vittorio Hösle, *Im Dialog mit Gómez Dávila*** (Lepanto Verlag, 2022). A philosopher answering Dávila with counter-aphorisms. The single strongest category-4 result for Escolios. Only the 5Pro thinker-on-thinker document found this.
    - Where to look: Lepanto Verlag, German bookshops

12. `2` **Franco Volpi, *El solitario de Dios*** (Villegas Editores, 2005). The most important interpretive text — reads Gómez Dávila as a philosophical thinker, not merely a political reactionary. Both bibliographies name this as essential.
    - Where to look: Villegas Editores, Latin American bookshops, possibly available as PDF

### Tektologiya

13. `2` **А. Л. Тахтаджян (Takhtajan), "Тектология: история и проблемы"** (*Системные исследования: Ежегодник*, Наука, 1971). The foundational Russian scholarly analysis — 77 pages by a world-class botanist. First major rehabilitation. Both bibliographies call this canonical.
    - Where to look: Russian academic archives, possibly digitized in Russian library systems

14. `2` **С. Н. Пустильник (Pustilnik), "Принцип подбора как основа тектологии А. А. Богданова"** (*Вопросы философии* No. 8, 1995). The most directly relevant item for подбор as an organizational category. 5Pro names this as the key article.
    - Where to look: *Вопросы философии* archive, Russian academic databases

15. `2` **Г. Д. Гловели (Gloveli), introductory essay and commentary to the 2003/2023 critical edition of *Тектология*** (Финансы, 2003; reissued URSS, 2023). The standard scholarly edition with the most detailed historiographic overview.
    - Where to look: URSS publisher (urss.ru), Russian bookshops
