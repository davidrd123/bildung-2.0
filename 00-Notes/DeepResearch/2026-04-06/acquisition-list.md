## Tier 1 — Immediate (Erkenntnisproblem + Zeitmauer active now)

### Erkenntnisproblem

1. ✓ `2` **Massimo Ferrari, "Wachstum oder Revolution? Ernst Cassirer und die Wissenschaftsgeschichte"** (*Berichte zur Wissenschaftsgeschichte* 35:2, 2012). The most focused essay on EP's historiographic method. Directly addresses growth vs. revolution, Renaissance natural philosophy, and the Koyré/Kuhn relationship. Both bibliographies name this as essential.
   - Where to look: journal article, likely available through academic databases or ResearchGate

2. ✓ `2` **Massimo Ferrari, *Ernst Cassirer: Stationen einer philosophischen Biographie*** (Meiner, 2003). Chapter "Genese und Struktur des Erkenntnisproblems" is the single most important treatment of EP's genesis and method. Opus names this as indispensable.
   - Where to look: Meiner Verlag, used academic bookshops, WorldCat

3. ✓ `2` ⚠️ **Hector José González Ríos, "Vom Erkenntnisproblem bis zur Philosophie der symbolischen Formen: Ernst Cassirer als Leser von Cusanus"** (*Coincidentia* 4:2, 2013). Directly relevant to the Cusanus chain you're building. The only identified essay tracing Cassirer's evolving Cusanus reading from EP to symbolic forms.
   - Where to look: *Coincidentia* journal (Cusanus-Gesellschaft), possibly via PhilPapers or JSTOR
   - ⚠️ verify: journal issue, exact title, author affiliation

4. `2` **Enno Rudolph, "Cassirers Rezeption des Renaissancehumanismus"** in *Ernst Cassirer im Kontext* (Mohr Siebeck, 2003). Directly addresses EP Vol. 1's Renaissance philosophy — Cusanus, Bruno, the substance-to-function transition.
   - Where to look: Mohr Siebeck, academic libraries

5. ✓ `2` **Rainer A. Bast, *Problem, Geschichte, Form*** (Duncker & Humblot, 2000). 5Pro names this as the best single starting point for EP's method. The philosophy/history relation in Cassirer.
   - Where to look: Duncker & Humblot, used academic bookshops

Parallel OG reception-field layer:
- direct reviews, same-problem interlocutors, Warburg/Hamburg field context, and reconcentration volumes are tracked separately in `00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-08/Secondary_Sources_OGs_5Pro.md`
- keep that file as a separate reception-history intake surface rather than treating the whole stratum as more urgent than Ferrari/Bast for current EP work

6. ↗ `OG` **Hans Reichenbach, *Relativitätstheorie und Erkenntnis apriori*** (1920). The strongest immediate bridge item from the older Cassirer field: explicit engagement with Cassirer's Einstein book, plus Hilbert/implicit-definition pressure relevant to the science-method line.
   - Where to look: **Project Gutenberg** (free, downloadable now)

7. `OG` **Raymond Klibansky and H. J. Paton (eds.), *Philosophy and History: Essays Presented to Ernst Cassirer*** (Oxford: Clarendon, 1936). Best old-style reconcentration volume for Cassirer inside a living field; especially relevant for Huizinga and Edgar Wind on the history/science relation.
   - Where to look: academic libraries, used bookshops, possibly digitized

8. `OG` **Paul Arthur Schilpp (ed.), *The Philosophy of Ernst Cassirer*** (Library of Living Philosophers, 1949). The later reconcentration point; stronger on the science-method side than usually remembered, with essays directly relevant to Galileo, mathematical concepts, and Renaissance thought.
   - Where to look: Library of Living Philosophers series, academic libraries, used bookshops, widely available

### Zeitmauer

9. ✓ `2` **Detlev Schöttker (Hrsg.), *Ernst Jünger: An der Zeitmauer. Mit Adnoten*** (Klett-Cotta, 2nd ed. 2019). The annotated edition. Source identifications, intertextual references, and interpretive notes for all 186 sections. Both bibliographies call this indispensable.
   - Where to look: Klett-Cotta, German bookshops, ZVAB
   - Local note: current local copy appears to be the 2013 annotated edition rather than the 2019 2nd edition

10. `2` **Peter Koslowski, "Die Rückkehr des Titanen Mensch zur Erde"** in Müller/Segeberg (eds.), *Ernst Jünger im 20. Jahrhundert* (Fink, 1995). The single most important dedicated essay on Zeitmauer. 30 pages on the Titan figure, Da-Sein/So-Sein, Schicksalszeit. Both bibliographies agree.
   - Where to look: essay in edited volume, Fink Verlag, academic libraries, ZVAB

11. ✓ `2` **Peter Koslowski, *Der Mythos der Moderne: Die dichterische Philosophie Ernst Jüngers*** (Fink, 1991). Book-length study; chapters on Erdgeschichte/Weltplan, mythical vs. scientific temporality. The broader companion to the 1995 essay.
   - Where to look: Fink Verlag, used academic bookshops, ZVAB

12. `4` **Hans Blumenberg, *Der Mann vom Mond: Über Ernst Jünger*** (Suhrkamp, 2007). Sustained confrontation from Blumenberg's estate — unpublished essays combining admiration for Jünger's style with relentless criticism. All three thinker-on-thinker documents flag this.
   - Where to look: Suhrkamp, widely available

## Tier 2 — Next return (Escolios, Tektologiya)

### Escolios

13. `2` ⚠️ **Ernesto Volkening (ed. Abad, Goenaga, Giraldo), *Diario de lectura de los Escolios de Nicolás Gómez Dávila: Cuadernos I y II*** (2020). An extended reading diary of the Escolios with scholarly apparatus. Someone else doing what you're doing — sequential close reading with notes. 5Pro's strongest Escolios find.
    - Where to look: Colombian academic publishers, dokumen.pub has a listing
    - ⚠️ verify: publisher, availability, whether this is accessible outside Colombia

14. `4` **Vittorio Hösle, *Im Dialog mit Gómez Dávila*** (Lepanto Verlag, 2022). A philosopher answering Dávila with counter-aphorisms. The single strongest category-4 result for Escolios. Only the 5Pro thinker-on-thinker document found this.
    - Where to look: Lepanto Verlag, German bookshops

15. `2` **Franco Volpi, *El solitario de Dios*** (Villegas Editores, 2005). The most important interpretive text — reads Gómez Dávila as a philosophical thinker, not merely a political reactionary. Both bibliographies name this as essential.
    - Where to look: Villegas Editores, Latin American bookshops, possibly available as PDF

### Tektologiya

16. `2` **А. Л. Тахтаджян (Takhtajan), "Тектология: история и проблемы"** (*Системные исследования: Ежегодник*, Наука, 1971). The foundational Russian scholarly analysis — 77 pages by a world-class botanist. First major rehabilitation. Both bibliographies call this canonical.
    - Where to look: Russian academic archives, possibly digitized in Russian library systems

17. `2` **С. Н. Пустильник (Pustilnik), "Принцип подбора как основа тектологии А. А. Богданова"** (*Вопросы философии* No. 8, 1995). The most directly relevant item for подбор as an organizational category. 5Pro names this as the key article.
    - Where to look: *Вопросы философии* archive, Russian academic databases

18. `2` **Г. Д. Гловели (Gloveli), introductory essay and commentary to the 2003/2023 critical edition of *Тектология*** (Финансы, 2003; reissued URSS, 2023). The standard scholarly edition with the most detailed historiographic overview.
    - Where to look: URSS publisher (urss.ru), Russian bookshops
