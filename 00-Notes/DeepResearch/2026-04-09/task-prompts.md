**Prompt:**

Many threads were written before the volume was finished. Now that Volume I is complete through Bayle, which threads are actually supported by the finished run, which are weaker than they look, and which have new pressure the thread description doesn't yet reflect?

For each thread that references Cassirer or the Erkenntnisproblem in any way, assess:
1. **Confirmed** — the completed volume directly supports the thread's claim, with specific part ranges
2. **Strengthened beyond current description** — the volume gives the thread more than the current write-up credits
3. **Weaker than claimed** — the thread's write-up implies a connection the volume doesn't actually provide at the stated strength
4. **New pressure** — the completed volume opens a question or connection the thread doesn't yet name
5. **Not reached** — the thread references Cassirer but the connection is actually to later volumes or to other Cassirer works, not to EP Vol. I

For threads that don't reference Cassirer at all, note only if the completed volume creates a new connection that would be surprising.

Constraints: be specific about part ranges. Do not invent connections the volume doesn't support. If a thread is simply confirmed as written, say so briefly and move on.

## Task 3: Glossary/term pressure audit

**Context package:**
- `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/journal.md` (full, ~3,552 lines)
- `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/source/glossary.yaml`
- `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/reading/close-reading-ledger.md`

**Prompt:**

Which terms or constructions recurred with enough force across the volume to deserve glossary treatment that the "keep watching" decisions never promoted?

Look for:
1. **Missed promotions** — terms the journal flagged for watching in early tranches that then recurred significantly in later tranches but were never given glossary entries
2. **Ledger patterns without glossary homes** — recurring constructions or pressures in the close-reading ledger that appear across multiple entries but have no corresponding glossary term
3. **Correctly deferred** — terms the journal watched and that genuinely didn't recur enough to justify promotion
4. **Glossary gaps by chapter** — chapters or stretches where the glossary is noticeably thinner than the conceptual density would suggest
5. **Over-represented terms** — glossary entries that got early treatment but didn't carry much weight across the rest of the volume

Constraints: this is an audit, not a proposal to expand the glossary. The project's principle is that premature carving is worse than a thin glossary. Flag what's missing; do not write the entries. Mark each finding as *strong case for promotion*, *borderline*, or *correctly deferred*.

## Task 4: Journal as document

**Context package:**
- `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/journal.md` (full, ~3,552 lines)
- `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/reading/2026-04-08-earned-distillation-from-volume-i.md`
- `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/reading/2026-04-08-whole-run-parts-journal-ledger-scratch-note.md`

**Prompt:**

What does the journal teach that the distillation notes do not capture?

The journal is a sequence of tranche-by-tranche "what this clarified / decision for now" entries. Read it as a document, not as a log. Look for:

1. **Rate of discovery** — where does the journal's pace of new findings accelerate, slow, or stall? What does that rhythm reveal about the text's own structure?
2. **Decision patterns** — the journal repeatedly says "keep watching, do not promote to glossary yet." Where did that restraint pay off and where did it quietly drop?
3. **Turns the distillation missed** — moments where the journal records a surprise, correction, or method shift that the distillation's chapter-summary format flattens
4. **The journal's own method development** — does the form of entries change across the run? Do early entries ask different questions than late ones?
5. **Recurring tensions** — difficulties or unresolved pressures that recur across multiple entries without fully resolving

Constraints: do not restate what the distillation notes already say. Do not summarize chapters. Produce findings about the journal as a working document. If you find nothing the distillation missed, say so honestly.
