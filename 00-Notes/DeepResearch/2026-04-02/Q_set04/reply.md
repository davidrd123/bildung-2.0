Core conclusions:

* **Jünger §11:** the project’s rendering is basically sound but a little dead in English. I’d prefer **“still depth”** over **“a depth at rest,”** and **“derelict mine-workings / ruined shafts”** over **“abandoned mine-shafts.”** The final litotes works better as **“it does not come out empty-handed”** than **“it does not remain without yield.”**
* **`Bruchstelle`:** keeping it **open** is the right call. The evidence chain really does range across **find-site / rupture-site / wound-edge / historical hinge**, so no single English word covers all cases cleanly. `fracture point` is the best forced default; `fault line` and `break point` are weaker.
* **`So-Sein`:** rejecting **`existence / essence`** is defensible as a **translation tactic**, though the project slightly overstates the case when it implies Jünger has no relation to that background. **`what-he-is / that-he-is`** is useful precisely because it stays strange, but it works better as a **glossary pair** than as a compulsory running-translation formula in every sentence.
* **Gómez Dávila §33–34:** the close/free split is doing real work. In §33, **`bagasse`** is exact but too exotic in English, while **`pulp left over`** is readable but too generic; something like **“the spent cane”** preserves the agricultural specificity better. In §34, the triple **`parecen`** is load-bearing; the exegesis is right to hear anti-reductionism, but naming **positivism/materialism** specifically is a little too much.
* **Black Iron Prison dossier:** the project’s arc is good as a **second-pass map**, but a bit too clean as chronology. The late move from prison-as-ontology toward prison-as-occlusion/representation is real, especially after Folders 1, 88, and 90, but Dick never fully stops cycling back into harder prison-language. Likewise, **compassionate return** is a major late corrective, not an uncontested final doctrine.
* **`Hinzutretende` → AI agent:** the cautious version of the analogy is plausible; the bold version is overstated. Jünger’s figure is not just “one who adds meaning without adding facts,” but also a **priestly / typological / sometimes prophetic** mediator, and the stronger repo notes flatten that distinction.

One practical method judgment: **close-only** is still the right default for *Zeitmauer*, but the project would benefit from more explicit **alternate renderings at pressure points**. For the *Exegesis*, a full “plain-English rewrite” would damage the object, but **selective micro-decryption** would help.
