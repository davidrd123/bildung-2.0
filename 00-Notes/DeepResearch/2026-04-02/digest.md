## 3. Best high-level synthesis: Q_set02 followup

`Q_set02/followup.md` is the strongest visionary note in the new set.

Its best contribution is not the larger rhetoric. It is the insistence on **bidirectionality**:

- forward translation alone makes earlier thinkers look like flattering ancestors of present frameworks
- backward translation or detranslation is the corrective
- the real method, if it matures, runs in both directions under evidence

This is the strongest conceptual addition in the note. It sharpens the project's emerging relation between:

- recovery and modernization
- philology and systems thought
- old semantic worlds and modern technical vocabularies

### What to keep from Q_set02 followup

- "bidirectional" or "reversible" translation pressure is real and worth preserving as a live hypothesis
- the civilizational story is better understood as:

> not simple loss, but dispersal, de-self-conscious survival, and rerouting into unexpected media and institutions

- the most interesting long-range possibility is not just recovery, but forcing old and new conceptual worlds to test each other without reducing either side to decorative gloss

### What to be careful with

This note still leans toward the repo's preferred elevated diction. Phrases like:

- `transhistorical concept laboratory`
- `semantic torsion under evidence`

may be good vision-language, but they should not be used as current-state descriptions. The note is strongest when read as directional synthesis, not as status report.

## 5. What actually changes practice

Only a few things here should alter the repo's next moves.

### Method

The strongest methodological demand now on the table is:

- define a repeatable assay workflow on one bounded case before making stronger claims for the method as a whole

The obvious candidate remains a live source slice where:

- the baseline source read exists
- a modern seam already changed the reading once
- multiple frameworks are actually available

Q_set03 names the strongest candidate: the Cassirer/Cusa/Montaigne seam in `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/`, where the von Foerster encounter already changed the read once and Barrett and Eilenberg–Mac Lane are both available as independent paths.

### Language

The best current status language is:

- not "full instrument"
- not "just notes"
- but `prototype bench`

That phrase is better than most of the loftier alternatives because it is both accurate and demanding.

This should remain a working correction, tested in source work, not yet promoted to settled architecture.
