(`00-Notes/process.md`; `AGENTS.md`; `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/reading/2026-04-03-earned-distillation-from-introduction-notes.md`; `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/reading/2026-04-04-earned-distillation-from-cusanus-generative-chains-chat.md`)

The `zeitmauer` journal repeatedly says “no new glossary entries yet” and extends evidence instead of forcing closure. (`patterns/handle-schema.md`; `texts/zeitmauer/source/glossary.yaml`; `texts/zeitmauer/journal.md`)

(`patterns/handle-schema.md`; `texts/zeitmauer/source/handles.yaml`; `texts/zeitmauer/threads/time-crisis.md`; `texts/zeitmauer/threads/goethe-leibniz-frame.md`; `00-Notes/right-now.md`)

4. **The chat → distillation → provisional promotion path is already a functioning internal filter.**

## Currently Misleading Or Overbuilt

1. **The default Proteus/Hermes operator surface.**
   **Claim or structure:** `CLAUDE.md` says “Proteus is the default driver” and tells the system to “proactively unpack and enrich user prompts,” while also inviting vivid subagent play and cross-domain translation.
   **Why it is premature or distorting:** the same file later insists on “evidence before framework,” but the default operator stance already biases work toward expansive mapping. The raw April 4 Cusanus chat shows the predictable result: over-anchoring on one experimental structure and survey-speed mappings that later had to be demoted. This is not a hypothetical risk; it is the behavior the repo then has to filter back down.
   **What evidence would be needed to earn it:** repeated sessions showing explicit mode-marking before interpretation, with no later need to downgrade analytical-sounding claims into mere survey; or else the flamboyant operator surface should be demoted from default to optional. (`CLAUDE.md`; raw April 4 Cusanus chat slices; `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/reading/2026-04-04-earned-distillation-from-cusanus-generative-chains-chat.md`)

2. **The frame namespace has more formal reality than the practice has earned.**
   **Claim or structure:** `patterns/handle-schema.md` gives `frame:*` examples, defines promotion gates for frames, and calls them “Perspective lenses for cross-project reading,” even while saying the namespace is “reserved now.” `00-Notes/process.md` says the frame layer is “not yet earned,” while `00-Notes/genealogy-to-instrument.md` says the repo “clearly has frames in practice.”
   **Why it is premature or distorting:** this combination gives the layer a quasi-canonical feel before repeated frame-reads have shown blind spots, rival readings, or cross-project durability. A reserved namespace is one thing; examples plus promotion gates plus surrounding prose make it feel like a latent object layer already waiting to be filled.
   **What evidence would be needed to earn it:** repeated frame-reads across distinct anchors and at least two projects, with explicit rival readings and documented blind spots, not just elegant redescriptions. (`patterns/handle-schema.md`; `00-Notes/process.md`; `00-Notes/genealogy-to-instrument.md`; `texts/zeitmauer/threads/goethe-leibniz-frame.md`)

3. **`patterns/handle-schema.md` speaks repo-wide for what is still mainly a local pilot.**
   **Claim or structure:** the schema says statuses are “Shared across all handle types,” and sketches “Phase 2: YAML index,” “Phase 3: Dynamic view,” and “Phase 4: Agent integration.”
   **Why it is premature or distorting:** the strongest live implementation is still `texts/zeitmauer`, while `00-Notes/right-now.md` explicitly says the handle/evidence/thread pilot works there and asks whether `Erkenntnisproblem` even needs the same infrastructure. That means the document currently reads more like a general spec than a pilot-proven pattern.
   **What evidence would be needed to earn it:** one more canonical implementation outside `zeitmauer`, or an explicit scope line in the schema saying that repo-wide applicability is intended but presently uneven and concentrated in one proving ground. (`patterns/handle-schema.md`; `00-Notes/right-now.md`; `texts/zeitmauer/source/handles.yaml`; `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/source/cusanus-generative-chain.yaml`)

4. **The adversarial standard is described as current practice more than it is instantiated.**
   **Claim or structure:** `00-Notes/process.md` says each thread dossier “should include” `Rival reading` and `Weakening evidence`, and that these “belong as sections in the dossier itself.” `patterns/handle-schema.md` also defines `challenges` as an active relation type.
   **Why it is premature or distorting:** the active dossiers supplied here (`texts/zeitmauer/threads/time-crisis.md` and `texts/zeitmauer/threads/goethe-leibniz-frame.md`) do not yet expose those sections, and the `handles.yaml` slice is overwhelmingly positive: `grounds`, `extends`, `crystallizes`, `parallels`. The protocol therefore describes a contestability layer that is clearer in meta-language than in the strongest live pilot.
   **What evidence would be needed to earn it:** explicit rival-reading and weakening-evidence blocks in the flagship dossiers, plus at least some live `challenges` relations or negative-evidence handles that can actually pressure central claims. (`00-Notes/process.md`; `patterns/handle-schema.md`; `texts/zeitmauer/threads/time-crisis.md`; `texts/zeitmauer/threads/goethe-leibniz-frame.md`; `texts/zeitmauer/source/handles.yaml`)

## Where The Protocol Protects Itself

The protocol protects itself most by pre-naming its own failure modes. `00-Notes/process.md` says the default failure is “absorbing critique as better prose rather than harder tests,” and the April 3 connecting-tail distillation promotes the criterion that “everything maps too quickly” when synthesis is false. That self-diagnosis is intelligent, but because the active dossiers still do not instantiate the required rival-reading and weakening-evidence sections, the naming of the criticism can substitute for exposure to the criticism. The protocol often sounds already chastened before it has actually made its strongest claims contestable. (`00-Notes/process.md`; `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/reading/2026-04-03-earned-distillation-from-connecting-chat-tail.md`; `texts/zeitmauer/threads/time-crisis.md`)

## Where The Protocol Is Still Underbuilt

What is still underbuilt is the negative side of the existing machinery. The repo already knows how to accumulate evidence, mark open terms, and extend threads. It is much weaker at recording what would force a contraction or reversal. The strongest proof is the gap between the stated adversarial apparatus and the live pilot: `process.md` requires rival readings and weakening evidence, `handle-schema.md` includes `challenges`, but the supplied `zeitmauer` dossiers and handle slice are still mostly growth-oriented rather than falsification-oriented. That missing negative branch matters more than the missing dashboard, dynamic view, or frame layer. (`00-Notes/process.md`; `patterns/handle-schema.md`; `texts/zeitmauer/source/handles.yaml`; `texts/zeitmauer/threads/time-crisis.md`)

## Chat To Distillation Pipeline

It currently looks like a **real filtration mechanism**, not a grooved ritual. The raw April 4 Cusanus chat shows three things that the later notes actually preserve: the generative chain is demoted from *the* structure to one experiment among several; the Peirce map is explicitly held in reserve until the primary material forces it; and the outputs are sorted into survey, verification, and analytical modes. The later April 4 distillation and the `erkenntnisproblem` journal keep those demotions in place, while `source/cusanus-generative-chain.yaml` marks itself `first-pass` and provisional. Earlier April 3 distillations show the same promote/hold-back pattern, so this is not a one-off repair. (raw April 4 Cusanus chat slices; `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/reading/2026-04-03-earned-distillation-from-introduction-notes.md`; `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/reading/2026-04-03-earned-distillation-from-connecting-chat-tail.md`; `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/reading/2026-04-04-earned-distillation-from-cusanus-generative-chains-chat.md`; `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/journal.md`; `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/source/cusanus-generative-chain.yaml`)

But it is still a **soft** filter rather than a hard contestability mechanism. The same voice often generates the exuberant mapping, the self-correction, the distillation, and the provisional ratification. That means the pipeline is good at reducing inflation and sorting claims by status, but it has not yet shown many cases where a claim is decisively rejected, weakened, or reversed inside the canonical layer. So the filter is real, but it currently filters **enthusiasm** better than it filters **error**. (`00-Notes/process.md`; `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/journal.md`; `texts/zeitmauer/threads/time-crisis.md`)
