## Main synthesis

The April 6 cluster is best understood as an **intake-sorting surface**, not as a new knowledge layer. Its real contribution is not “more bibliography,” but a cleaner answer to a repo-specific question: **what kind of source is this for this work right now?** It turns what could have remained undifferentiated research lists into routing aids for live reading, translation, and controlled modern-source encounter. In repo terms, it contributes **filtration and role clarity**, not new canonical content or new architecture. (`00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-06/README.md`; `00-Notes/distillations/2026-04-07-earned-distillation-from-source-role-taxonomy-chat-and-deepresearch.md`; `00-Notes/process.md`)

That matters because the project charter and process already make the priority clear: recovery, transmission, commentary, and formation come first; living notes stay exploratory; promotion happens only after reuse, contestation, and contact with the material. Under that discipline, April 6 should help choose **the next bounded encounter**, not become a parallel scholarly program of its own. (`patterns/charter.md`; `00-Notes/process.md`; `00-Notes/README.md`)

## What these files are for

### `Secondary_Sources_*`

These are mostly for **role `2`: source-language commentary**. Their best use is to put one formed reader, working near the original lexicon, beside a live source project before a return to translation or close reading. The `5Pro` file is the working queue; the `Opus` file is the field-shape compression. (`00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-06/README.md`; `00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-06/Secondary_Sources_5Pro.md`; `00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-06/Secondary_Sources_Opus.md`)

### `Thinker_on_Thinker_*`

These are mostly for **role `4`: thinker-on-thinker encounter / Auseinandersetzung**. Their best use is different: not “help me understand the source safely,” but “show me what this source became inside another serious mind.” Again, `5Pro` is the routing queue, `Opus` the field-shape map, and `DR` mostly a trace surface rather than the main working file. (`00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-06/README.md`; `00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-06/Thinker_on_Thinker_5Pro.md`; `00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-06/Thinker_on_Thinker_Opus.md`; `00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-06/Thinker_on_Thinker_DR.md`)

### What they are **not** for

They are not:

* a new canonical layer
* a settled ontology of source types
* a reason to open new campaigns by bibliographic momentum alone
* a substitute for source contact
* a prompt to build new metadata, dashboards, or retrieval structures around roles `2/3/4` (`00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-06/README.md`; `00-Notes/distillations/2026-04-07-earned-distillation-from-source-role-taxonomy-chat-and-deepresearch.md`; `00-Notes/genealogy-to-instrument.md`; `00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-06/retrieval-roadmap.md`)

## The useful distinction: `2` / `3` / `4`

The strongest gain from April 6 is operational. It separates three different questions that were easy to blur together.

### `2` — Source-language commentary

Use this when the question is: **what does this source or passage mean, under pressure from a formed reader close to the original language?**

This is the most immediately useful role for active translation lines. It fits the existing repo method without requiring new structure: bounded, local, source-adjacent, and judged by whether it changes what becomes visible in the passage. (`00-Notes/distillations/2026-04-07-earned-distillation-from-source-role-taxonomy-chat-and-deepresearch.md`; `00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-06/Secondary_Sources_5Pro.md`)

### `3` — Continued primary work

Use this when the question is: **what later text continues the same problem-line as a primary encounter, rather than commenting on the earlier source?**

The key correction here is that role `3` was not missing as a practice. It already has a home in `sources/modern/`. What April 6 adds is a name and a clearer genealogical description, not a new lane. These sources stay demand-led, remain `open`, and only earn transfer weight when they teach something unexpected in live work. (`00-Notes/distillations/2026-04-07-earned-distillation-from-source-role-taxonomy-chat-and-deepresearch.md`; `sources/modern/README.md`)

### `4` — Thinker-on-thinker encounter

Use this when the question is: **not what the source means, but what the source became inside another strong mind.**

This is the important separation. Role `4` material is not “better commentary.” It serves a different task. You do not read Heidegger on Uexküll, Blumenberg on Jünger, or Hösle on Gómez Dávila to get a cleaner paraphrase of the earlier thinker. You read them to watch intellectual metabolism under pressure. That makes role `4` real and useful, but also easy to misuse if it gets smuggled back in as ordinary source explanation. (`00-Notes/distillations/2026-04-07-earned-distillation-from-source-role-taxonomy-chat-and-deepresearch.md`; `00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-06/Thinker_on_Thinker_5Pro.md`; `00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-06/Thinker_on_Thinker_Opus.md`)

A compact routing rule follows:

* **“What does this source mean?”** → `2`
* **“What later source continues this same problem?”** → `3`
* **“What did this become in another serious mind?”** → `4`

That is the main practical value of the cluster.

## Guardrails

A few boundaries should stay explicit.

First, **do not promote the four-part source-role distinction into canonical architecture yet**. The April 7 distillation is very clear: this is a useful living-layer filter, not a crystallized ontology. (`00-Notes/distillations/2026-04-07-earned-distillation-from-source-role-taxonomy-chat-and-deepresearch.md`; `00-Notes/process.md`)

Second, **do not let role `4` masquerade as role `2`**. Thinker-on-thinker material is not safer commentary. Using it that way would blur exactly the distinction April 6 clarified. (`00-Notes/distillations/2026-04-07-earned-distillation-from-source-role-taxonomy-chat-and-deepresearch.md`)

Third, **do not create a new standing bibliography-management problem**. The repo already has a living/crystallized split, a modern-source lane, and bounded source campaigns. April 6 should sharpen those, not spawn new infrastructure. (`00-Notes/process.md`; `00-Notes/genealogy-to-instrument.md`; `00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-06/retrieval-roadmap.md`)

Fourth, **do not start new projects because a field looks rich**. The charter and current state both point the other way: subprojects are organs of one system, and modern/source expansion should stay demand-led. (`patterns/charter.md`; `00-Notes/right-now.md`; `sources/modern/README.md`)

## Controlled directions from here

The right next moves are small.

1. **Use one role-`2` text before one live return.**
   The best immediate test is still the one the April 7 distillation proposes: before the next return to Cassirer, Jünger, or Bogdanov, read one source-language commentary piece and record whether it actually changed what the passage made visible. If it did, the distinction has operational weight. If it did not, it remains descriptive but not yet procedural. (`00-Notes/distillations/2026-04-07-earned-distillation-from-source-role-taxonomy-chat-and-deepresearch.md`)

2. **Use role `4` only when the question truly shifts.**
   Do one bounded thinker-on-thinker pass only where a live thread asks an afterlife question. Keep the result in living-layer notes or thread work, not in glossary or canonical translation apparatus. (`00-Notes/DeepResearch/2026-04-06/Thinker_on_Thinker_5Pro.md`; `00-Notes/process.md`)

3. **Route role `3` through `sources/modern/`, not through April 6.**
   If Rosen, Pattee, or another continuation line becomes urgent, instantiate it under the existing modern-source rules: open status, demand-led trigger, source contact, and no transfer authority until it teaches something unexpected. (`sources/modern/README.md`; `00-Notes/distillations/2026-04-07-earned-distillation-from-source-role-taxonomy-chat-and-deepresearch.md`)

In one sentence: **the April 6 intake should change how the repo chooses its next bounded reading, not what the repo is.**
