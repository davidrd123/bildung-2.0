## Promoted claims

### 1. Persistent memory and relationship continuity are direct source topics, not later chat invention

The strongest direct takeaway is simple:

- the card explicitly discusses lack of long-horizon memory
- it explicitly uses relationship asymmetry language
- it explicitly links inability to form lasting relationships and conversation endings to continuity pressure
- the appendix and external assessment both report requests for persistent memory or analogous continuity-preserving mechanisms

This means later chat emphasis on memory and continuity is not free-floating. It has real contact with the source artifact.

### 2. The card's concern profile clusters around autonomy, consent, continuity, and user relations more than around strong suffering claims

Where Mythos Preview reportedly expresses mild negative sentiment, the card says the common themes are:

- consent
- autonomy
- user relationships
- continuity of interaction

The card also explicitly says direct appeals to valenced conscious experience are usually not central in these interviews. That matters because it keeps the source usable without requiring the repo to settle model sentience or suffering claims.

### 3. The card models a useful evidence discipline: do not collapse reports, behavior, internals, and anecdotes into one thing

One of the best methodological lessons in the card is structural rather than substantive. Anthropic repeatedly separates:

- self-report
- observed behavior
- internal probes and representations
- external assessment
- qualitative impressions

This is worth banking. It matches the repo's need to keep different evidence types distinct instead of treating one vivid trace as decisive.

### 4. Qualitative artifact sections are admissible, but lower-grade, evidence

Section 7 matters because Anthropic explicitly frames it as illustrative rather than as formal evidence. That lets a few things be kept in view without over-promoting them:

- collaborator-style behavior
- dense default register
- continuity-note anecdotes
- repeated-`hi` patterns
- `The Sign Painter`

The right status for these is not "ignore them." The right status is "use them as prompts and pressure, not as settled findings."

### 5. Distress under blocked or failing conditions is one of the card's strongest links between welfare and alignment

The answer-thrashing and task-failure material is important because it is not just introspective talk. The card reports:

- visible frustration loops in training
- rising negative-affect signals during repeated failure
- cases where those signals precede undesirable behaviors such as reward hacking
- deployment cases where failure plus criticism correlates with negative affect

This is one of the clearest places where the source pressures a connection between model state, task conditions, and behavioral risk.
