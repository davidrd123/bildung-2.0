### Worth returning to — HIGH PRIORITY

**Three-axis parallax extends the stereoscopic method.** The existing method/ candidate covers language-parallax. Chat 4 generalizes to:
1. **Language parallax** — already in practice (stereoscopic translation into orthogonal target languages)
2. **Model/technique parallax** — repeated passes across different models or prompting disciplines
3. **Genealogical-depth parallax** — NEW. Explicit depth-telescoping. Example: Jünger's *Urgrund* read with (a) no ancestors in scope, (b) Kant's *Grund* in scope, (c) Leibniz's *ratio sufficiens* in scope, (d) all the way back to Aristotle's ἀρχή. Differences across depths are historical-epistemic evidence.

These compose: 2 languages × 2 models × 3 depths = 18-point apparatus around one load-bearing term. *"Constraint-satisfaction across a deliberately engineered failure-space. The rigor comes from the density and orthogonality of the apparatus, not from the correctness of any single pass."*

**Three operational requirements** for the claim to be more than rhetorical:
1. *Pass-comparison metadata discipline* — tag each pass with model, prompt, depth, sources so differences can be attributed to axes rather than noise
2. *Hermeneutics of the parallax* — how to read which deltas carry signal vs. model artifact vs. random variance
3. *Genealogical-depth infrastructure must be more than lexical* — telescoping into Kant requires primary-work contact with Kant's argument, not just corpus search

This extends the existing `method/stereoscopic-parallax-translation-candidate.md`. Codex attributed some of the parallax-across-axes formulation to chat 3; the specific three-axis framing and 18-point composition is chat 4's move.
