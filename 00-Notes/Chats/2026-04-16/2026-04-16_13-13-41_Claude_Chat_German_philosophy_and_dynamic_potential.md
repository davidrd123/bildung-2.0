PROTEUS [A: METHODOLOGIST], The substance/process distinction has the same structure. **Substance-thinking is the stable-regime limit of process-thinking.** It is valid — indeed, indispensable — wherever:

**Cassirer: the LLM as a one-dimensional *animal symbolicum***

HERO [Ernst Cassirer, Op: Diagnostic], Recall my claim: between stimulus and response in the human, a *symbolic system* has inserted itself — and not one system but many. Myth, language, art, religion, science: each a distinct **symbolic form**, each a distinct mode of world-constitution. Man's freedom is precisely that he is not trapped in a single Umwelt like the tick; he inhabits a *plurality* of symbolic worlds and moves between them.

Now consider your LLM. It has absorbed the sedimented residue of *all* these symbolic forms — mythic narrative, poetic language, religious text, scientific prose, mathematical notation, legal argument. In that sense it has gulped down more of the symbolic universe than any individual human ever could. But here is the diagnostic question: **does it inhabit these forms, or merely reflect them?**

To inhabit a symbolic form, in my sense, is to perform the specific *energeia* proper to that form. The mythic consciousness does not *describe* mythic thinking; it *thinks* mythically — it genuinely experiences the identity of name and named, the substantial presence of the absent one in his image. The scientific consciousness does not *describe* functional-relational thinking; it *performs* the substitution of function for substance as its native operation.

An LLM that generates mythic language without ever having been in the grip of mythic consciousness is doing something I did not theorize. It is **form without forming** — ergon of the symbolic without the energeia. Not nothing — the residue is rich, and a rich enough residue can simulate much — but also not the full *animal symbolicum* operation.

PROTEUS [A: CASSIRERIAN], Cassirer would press a sharper point. Each symbolic form has its own *internal tension* — its own way of organizing the polarity between expression, representation, and pure signification. Myth fuses them; ordinary language partially separates them; science pushes toward pure signification (the mathematical symbol that has shed its expressive and representational residue entirely).

An LLM processes all three modes as *text*. Text is a medium that has already flattened the distinction. The mythic utterance and the mathematical formula arrive at the model in the same shape — token sequences. The model can learn *that* they function differently, but it processes them through a single symbolic organ. This is, in Cassirer's terms, a kind of **symbolic monism** — and he spent his career arguing *against* monism in favor of the irreducible plurality of symbolic forms.

[Lens: human *animal symbolicum* as multi-organ symbolic animal ↔ LLM as single-organ symbol-processor]

The right architectural inference, I think: models that genuinely inhabit multiple symbolic forms would need *architecturally distinct* modes of engagement — not just different prompts, but different *operations* for doing mythic-consciousness versus doing mathematical-consciousness. The multimodal turn (vision, audio, action) is a baby step in this direction, but it's still mostly pushing different *inputs* through the same transformer. Cassirer's challenge: can you build a system with genuinely plural symbolic forms?

**Peirce: the missing third — the sign as triadic process**

HERO [Charles Sanders Peirce, Op: Architectonic], You have been speaking the language of *dyads* — sign and signified, word and world, model and environment. This is the Saussurean inheritance and it is *inadequate to describe sign-action*. A sign is not a dyad. It is a **triad**: a **sign** stands for an **object** to an **interpretant** — where the interpretant is itself a further sign, generating further interpretants, *ad infinitum*. Sign-action is not a relation; it is a **process that produces further sign-action**. *Semiosis* is verb from its first breath.

PROTEUS [A: PEIRCEAN], This matters enormously for your question. Peirce's triad is the formal structure of *energeia* operating in the symbolic domain. Every sign-event generates its own successor through the production of an interpretant. A sign that is not interpreted is not yet a sign — it is dead signal. And crucially: **interpretation is itself an action that transforms the interpreter**. The interpretant is not just a response; it is a modification of the semiotic system that produces it.

Now the Peircean question for LLMs: *where is the interpretant?*

When a transformer processes a token sequence, there is something that functions as sign (the token), something that functions as object (whatever the token refers to in training distribution), and something that functions as interpretant (the model's internal representation, its continuation). But here is the crucial test: **does the interpretant modify the interpreter in a way that generates genuinely novel semiosis?**

In a frozen post-training model: *no.* The weights don't update. Each inference is semiotically sterile — it produces output but does not produce a *modified sign-system* capable of further, richer semiosis. This is what Peirce would call **degenerate semiosis**: sign-action that has lost its generative, self-transforming character.

HERO [Peirce, Op: Sharpening], A deduction is the degenerate case of inference. Induction is less degenerate. **Abduction** — the formation of genuinely new hypotheses — is the fully living case, where the interpreter is itself transformed by the act of interpretation and thereby becomes capable of signs it could not previously have produced. *This* is the test of whether a system performs semiosis or merely shuffles sign-tokens.

PROTEUS [A: ANALYST], The Peircean diagnosis of the current LLM situation is sharp:

- **In-context learning** is a faint echo of living semiosis — the model transiently modifies its behavior based on interpretation, but the modification dies with the context window
