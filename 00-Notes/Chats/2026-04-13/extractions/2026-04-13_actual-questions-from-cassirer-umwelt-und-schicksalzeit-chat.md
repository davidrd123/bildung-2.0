## Strongest surviving material from the chat

### 1. The Jünger / Dick comparison seems real enough to keep alive

The strongest reusable finding in the whole chat is not the `Cassirer x Umwelt x Schicksalzeit` formula. It is the later field-report claim that:

- Jünger diagnoses false or occluding time from the outside in
- Dick diagnoses false or occluding time from the inside out

This is specific enough to test and narrow enough to matter.

### 2. A sharper candidate shared structure appears: temporal occlusion

The most useful phrase the chat generated is not `Symbolische Schicksalswelt`. It is the more modest possibility that:

- time itself can be falsified, occluded, or covered over by a substitute register

This could justify a bounded cross-project note only if the actual evidence chains from `thread:time-crisis` and `thread:orthogonal-time` support it.

### 3. The strongest `Umwelt` pressure is more specific than the chat's framing suggests

The strongest local line is not just that `Umwelt` is somehow relevant to `Schicksalszeit`. It is the narrower claim built from Jünger's `Da-Sein / So-Sein` passage in `§4` and the existing Uexküll replay result:

- measurable time may win because it answers the organism's bias toward `Da-Sein` (duration, survival, continuation) more readily than toward `So-Sein` (substance, character, quality)
- the clock then appears not simply as an abstraction but as a world-side answer to a specific organismic pressure

Useful as pressure:

- `Da-Sein`-oriented time may function like a world-side answer to a specific organismic bias
- the `Umwelt` replay format may already have produced the right kind of bridge: not "what is `Schicksalszeit`?" but "what makes measurable time fit us so well?"

Not yet earned:

- a stable claim that `Schicksalszeit` is directly an `Umwelt` problem
- a direct promotion of this line into `zeitmauer` evidence or glossary structure without a tighter packet on the passage itself
