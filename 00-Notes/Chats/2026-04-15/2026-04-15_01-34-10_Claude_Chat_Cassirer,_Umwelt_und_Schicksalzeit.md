**Translation as reasoning engine.** The idea that translation isn't for producing readable versions of foreign texts. It's a cognitive forcing function. When you push a passage through a target language with different conceptual architecture, the LLM has to decompose the idea into its actual components and reassemble it in a structure that won't accept the original assembly. That decomposition is where understanding happens. The further extension: doing this into *two* orthogonal languages simultaneously — say German and classical Chinese from English — produces two different residues, and the difference between the residues is higher-order content that neither translation alone reveals. The non-commutation between translation paths is diagnostic of where the source concept is relying on its home language's structure rather than on genuine content. This reframes the entire project: the translations aren't the output. The translations are the *instrument* for producing insight that reading alone can't reach.

**The field/pressure/temperature model.** From the multi-voice synthesis. The repo isn't a knowledge base or a graph. It's a pressure field. Terms have temperature — how much recent evidence has accumulated around them, how many threads are currently crossing them, whether they're under active challenge. The maturity lifecycle is already field-temperature (open terms with rich evidence are hot; stable terms nobody's revisited are cold). Forward provenance enables a decay-and-reactivation model — a finding's activation is computable from how recently it's been cited downstream. The "field-state query" is different from a graph query: not "what's connected to what" but "where is the field densest right now, where has it shifted since last session, where are the asymmetries." The practical form is the replay packet — reading a new passage and reporting not what it says but what it does to the pressure on a term. The first test worked: `Umwelt` got hotter and the pressure shifted from centeredness to world-side planfulness. The vocabulary — pressure, temperature, field-state delta — hasn't been tested on enough passages to know if it's real or atmospheric.
