## Three-register distinction offered to the curio

Lines 369-386 — reading the curio's three existing sections next to each other, Claude noticed they might be tracking *three different kinds of afterlife-after-theoretical-death*:

- **Conceptual distinctions and phrases** (Energeia/Ergon, *Symbolische Prägnanz*, "water/mud/source") = *living diagnostic* — concept stays operational in conversation without ossifying into glossary; promotion would destroy it.
- **Rejected ideas that kept their charge** (orgone, vril, phlogiston, morphic resonance, *élan vital*) = *rejected-but-vivid* — theory died, aesthetic charge survived, often at borders of serious work. Protection needed is against re-elevation into pseudoscience.
- **Imaginal layers of serious thinkers** (Newton's alchemy, Leibniz's hermetic side, Goethe's *Urpflanze*, Grothendieck's *souffle*, Peirce's agape, Cassirer's fourth volume) = *bracketed-but-intact* — theory archived and available; bracket is a later scholarly move, not decay of material. Removal of bracket needs care: scholarship quarantined it *for reasons*, some good and some parochial.

Offered as a noticing, not as architecture. The three registers already exist in the curio as separate sections; curator may already feel the difference. Kept here in case the distinction earns through further entries, or decays.
