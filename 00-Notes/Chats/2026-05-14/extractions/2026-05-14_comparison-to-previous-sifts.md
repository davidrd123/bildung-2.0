# Comparison to Previous Sifts

Status: comparison note only. Not method canon. Purpose: test whether the May
14 Claude Code sift is actually doing the same job as earlier sift practice, and
where the Claude Code format should change the extraction shape.

Compared against:

- April 19 Claude Code sift: `../../2026-04-19/sift-notes.md`
- April 19 Claude Code dossier: `../../2026-04-19/extractions/2026-04-19_structuralism-middle-zone-grothendieck-dossier.md`
- May 5 multi-chat sift: `../../2026-05-05/sift-notes.md`
- May 6 Lotman sift: `../../2026-05-06/sift-notes.md`

## Short Verdict

The May 14 sift is aligned with previous practice at the level of promotion
discipline, but its center of gravity is different.

- **April 19** preserves one correction exemplar plus atelier residue.
- **May 5** invents / names the full-resolution traversal form for live reading.
- **May 6** sorts a source-adjacent theorist into usable method without making
  him foundation.
- **May 14** tracks an operational apparatus proving itself over a long Claude
  Code session.

So May 14 should not try to look like April 19. Its best extraction form is
closer to a session-log / apparatus-proof dossier than to an atelier sift.

## What Carries Forward Cleanly

### 1. Promotion Discipline

All four sifts make the same boundary move: chat is not authority. May 14
preserves this strongly through `candidate-promotions` and
`source-verification-queue`.

The May 14 version is actually more explicit than the older sifts because it
separates:

- already-earned local Vol. II apparatus moves;
- candidates worth testing;
- material to keep below promotion;
- source / theory / product claims needing verification.

That is a useful refinement for Claude Code exports because they often mix
conversation, tool work, and file-change summaries in one stream.

### 2. Strongest-Survivor Logic

April 19's dossier asks: what survived a full-cover read?

May 14 asks the same question, but the survivors are operational rather than
lexical or historical:

- fast audit corrected by primary contact;
- re-entry hooks;
- routing journal;
- correctness gate;
- `mente captus` check;
- Bacon/Gassendi apparatus transfer.

That is the right adaptation. The strong survivor is not one phrase or one
source correction; it is a sequence of apparatus tests.

### 3. Explicit Caution Around Meta-Material

May 14 handles its biggest risk better than April 19 did at first glance. April
19 preserves large attractive historical arcs in the sift and then marks them
held below promotion. May 14 mostly pushes comparable civilizational material
into pointer/candidate/verification files and keeps the main sift operational.

That is probably correct because May 14's civilizational material was less
source-contacted than April 19's Grothendieck correction and less directly
actionable than May 6's Lotman source-reading.

## What Differs From Earlier Sifts

### 1. May 14 Is Colder Than April 19

April 19's sift is a real second-pass atelier reading. It has sections for:

- exemplar event;
- vocabulary emergence;
- charged / portable phrases;
- outside-recognition register;
- physical-discovery epistemology;
- cross-scale patterns;
- middle-zone prose.

May 14's sift is shorter and more procedural. It has fewer charged phrases and
less texture. Some of that is good: the session itself concluded that much of
the meta-conversation should remain calibration rather than artifact. But it
means May 14's `sift-notes.md` functions more as a re-entry map than as an
atelier complement.

If a future pass wants the April 19 style, the missing section would be
something like **"Methodological Delta"**:

- the correctness gate as standards-maintenance;
- negative archaeology;
- polymath-scholar-on-demand;
- selective non-isomorphic rendering at hooks;
- apparatus fields as visible structure.

Those are present now, but dispersed across dossier / candidates / markers
rather than gathered as a textural pass.

### 2. May 14's Unit Is a Session, Not a Day

May 5 and May 6 are day-folder sifts over multiple discrete chats. May 14 is a
single Claude Code session with internal product work, remote-control detours,
translation review, morphology lessons, and meta-conversation.

This matters because a day-folder "actual questions" file works well when
questions are independent strands. In May 14, many questions are sequential
apparatus tests. The actual-questions file therefore reads like a workflow arc,
not a topic map. That is appropriate, but future Claude Code sifts should watch
for this distinction:

- multi-chat day = question map;
- long Claude Code session = apparatus / decision arc.

### 3. Tool Provenance Is Weaker in the Markdown Export

April 19 had raw JSONL plus cleaned markdown in the folder. May 14 currently has
only cleaned markdown in the folder, with tool internals stripped. That makes
line-anchoring conversation reliable, but not tool-result audit.

The May 14 sift correctly caveats this. Future Claude Code sifts should decide
whether to preserve the raw JSONL next to the cleaned markdown when the tool
trace matters.

## What May 14 Adds to the Sift Practice

### 1. Apparatus-Proof Dossier

Previous dossiers focused on source-question survival or correction events. May
14 demonstrates a dossier form for a working apparatus:

- identify the proposed rule;
- identify the gate or test;
- identify the correction it caught;
- identify whether the rule transferred across later material;
- identify when verification should ease.

This is useful beyond Vol. II.

### 2. Companion Artifact Split

May 14 already had two working-synthesis companion files:

- morphology as reference;
- meta-conversation as pointer.

The chat-folder extraction could therefore stay thinner. This suggests a
general rule for future sifts: if a session already produced a good local
artifact, the sift should point to it and describe its role, not re-extract the
same content.

### 3. Candidate Promotions + Verification Queue as Separate Files

May 5 and May 6 already used candidate-promotion and verification-queue files.
May 14 confirms that those files are especially useful for Claude Code exports,
where product claims, source claims, live file changes, and speculative method
claims sit side by side.

## Risks in the May 14 Sift

### 1. It May Under-Preserve the Atelier Layer

The main sift is disciplined but dry. If future-you wants to recover the feel of
the session, April 19 is better. May 14 relies on the meta-markers file and the
dossier to compensate.

This is acceptable only because the session itself decided the meta layer should
remain pointer-only. If that decision changes, write a separate atelier pass
rather than bloating the current sift.

### 2. The Standard Headings Are Starting to Groove

May 5, May 6, and May 14 all use a similar scaffold:

- First Orientation;
- Working Form(s) Discovered;
- Phrases / Operations;
- Strong Re-entry Points;
- Caution Zones;
- Best Next Move.

That is still functional, but it is close to becoming the exact kind of form
grooving the repo warns against. The next sift should justify this shape from
the material or vary it.

For Claude Code sessions specifically, better variable headings might be:

- Operational Arc;
- Corrections That Changed the Work;
- Apparatus Tests;
- Product / Tool Detours;
- What Not To Extract.

### 3. The Candidate-Promotion File Could Over-Signal Importance

Calling something a "candidate promotion" makes it feel more ready than it may
be. May 14 handles this by separating "already earned locally" from "worth
testing" and "keep below promotion." Keep that structure if this file type
continues.

## Practical Recommendation

Keep the May 14 extraction set as-is. It is doing the right job for a Claude Code
session: preserving the operational proof without overpromoting the
meta-conversation.

If doing one more pass, do not expand the whole extraction set. Add only a short
atelier supplement if later work pulls on one of these four phrases:

- negative archaeology;
- polymath-scholar-on-demand;
- selective non-isomorphic rendering;
- apparatus fields as visible structure.

Otherwise let the current form stand and test it on the next Claude Code export.
