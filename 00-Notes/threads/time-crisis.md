# Time Crisis

Handle: `thread:time-crisis`

Pilot cross-project thread for the collision between measurable time and qualitative or fateful time.

## Core Path

- `zm:4` begins the explicit contrast. See `evidence:term:schicksalszeit:zm:4`.
- `zm:9:church-year` crystallizes the argument by moving from astrology to the liturgical year. See `evidence:term:schicksalszeit:zm:9`.
- `zm:13:schicksalsuhr` extends that move by naming the horoscope as `Schicksalsuhr`, the uneven clock. See `evidence:term:schicksalszeit:zm:13`.
- `zm:14:abdichtung` shows the argument widening: planning and ceremony require a "sealing toward the side of fate."
- `note:journal:mumford-bridge` parallels the same problem historically through clock-time, fungibility, and the megamachine.
- `note:journal:goethe-leibniz-frame` grounds the deeper split: participatory perception versus formalized measure.

## Current Claim

`thread:time-crisis` is not just a theme inside `texts/zeitmauer/`. It is one of the repo's main bridges between textual recovery and cross-domain synthesis.

In Jünger, the issue is first presented through astrology, but it quickly becomes larger:

- not just two clocks, but two ontologies of time
- not just prediction, but what kinds of reality can be known without being destroyed by knowing
- not just fate, but the broader crisis of modern planning, technique, and abstraction

The thread becomes strongest when the examples stop looking "occult" and start looking ordinary:

- banknotes and their backing in `zm:7:banking`
- the church year in `zm:9:church-year`
- the horoscope as `zm:13:schicksalsuhr`
- Roman augury and ceremony in `zm:14:abdichtung`
- the record, the `Titanic`, and clock-measured performance in `zm:15`
