# Bounded Survey Encounters As Middle Band

Status: working-layer method note. Not a pattern file. Not authority to open a source campaign by convenience.

Purpose: clarify the legitimate middle band between:

- strict local replay packets
- fully open source campaigns
- free synthesis chats

This note exists because several localized source scaffolds are now on disk, and one April 14 Grothendieck session was productive without cleanly counting as either a replay packet or a source opening.

## Claim

A bounded survey encounter is legitimate when it is treated as a pre-promotion probe:

- one live pressure
- one bounded source surface
- one explicit question
- one explicit limit on what the result can authorize

It is not the same thing as opening a source campaign.

## When To Use It

Use a bounded survey encounter when:

- primary work has generated a real pressure toward a source
- the pressure is too specific to justify a full campaign
- the source has been localized enough to read exact passages
- the question is still about whether the source is needed at all

Good shape:

- `Uexkuell -> Kant KdU §§61-68, 71, 77, 78`
- one narrow `Recoltes` check against a live residue or transmission question
- one `Substanzbegriff` tranche check against an already active Cassirer pressure

## Minimum Controls

- name the pressure before reading
- keep the source surface exact and small
- say in advance what would count as a useful negative
- record at least one resistance or non-fit, not only a contact point
- keep the result in the living layer
- state explicitly that the source remains unopened unless later work forces more

## What It Can Produce

A bounded survey encounter can legitimately produce:

- a stronger or weaker pressure note
- a small confirmation memo
- a bounded experiment packet or collision handoff
- a refusal: the source looked promising, but the pressure was chat-layer only

## What It Does Not License

- not a glossary, journal, or encounter dossier for the surveyed source
- not promotion of the source into a live campaign by reuse drift
- not new standing repo vocabulary
- not whole-author claims
- not repeated return just because the scaffold is cheap to use

If the source keeps being touched, the question changes. At that point either open it honestly or stop.

## Relation To Other Middle Forms

- A `replay packet` is stricter: one local term or pressure, one bounded passage, one explicit output contract.
- A `bounded source-collision note` is lateral: two already earned source surfaces placed together under bounded controls. See [Experiments README](/Users/daviddickinson/Projects/Lora/bildung-2.0/00-Notes/experiments/README.md:46) and [collision-01-time-crisis-orthogonal-time-threshold-and-blindness.md](/Users/daviddickinson/Projects/Lora/bildung-2.0/00-Notes/experiments/2026-04-13/collision-01-time-crisis-orthogonal-time-threshold-and-blindness.md:1).
- A `bounded survey encounter` is upstream of both: it tests whether a scaffolded source should stay dormant, yield one bounded follow-up, or be opened properly.

## Current Use Rule

Use this note when deciding whether a scaffolded source can be touched once without silently becoming active.

Do not use it to justify touching the same source over and over while claiming it is still only a probe.
