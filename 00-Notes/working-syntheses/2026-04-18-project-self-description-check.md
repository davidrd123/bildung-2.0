## Core judgment

The project's **mission** still holds.

`bildung-2.0` is still best described, at charter level, as a reconstruction project for whole knowing: recover buried texts, reconnect severed lineages, and build a practice that forms the reader rather than merely informing them.

What has changed is not the telos but the repo's **self-understanding of its means**.

Recent work has made three things clearer:

1. the repo is no longer best described as mainly a forward-translation engine
2. different texts genuinely require different apparatus shapes
3. bounded probing, method notes, and sidecar spaces now play a larger role in practice than the older summary language makes explicit

So the right move is not immediate charter revision. The right move is to keep the charter stable and periodically refresh the repo's working self-description.

## What still holds from the charter

The following charter-level claims still look sound:

- recovery, not novelty, is the governing orientation
- buried or inaccessible texts remain the main material base
- translation, decryption, commentary, and synthesis are all real transmission work
- formation of the reader remains a real aim, not decorative language
- deferred structure still names a live discipline
- the texts still teach the method more reliably than predeclared frameworks do

Nothing in the recent phase shift falsifies those claims.

## What has changed in practice

The repo now understands itself more clearly as a mixed instrument with several live modes:

- completed translation nodes with ongoing integration pressure
- bounded experiments and replay packets
- scaffolded but dormant source surfaces
- method candidates that are neither architecture nor canon
- a small side-energy space that is explicitly not authority

This is a real change in working reality.

The older summary picture tilted toward:

- translation runs
- journals and glossaries
- handle architecture as the main structural question

The current picture is broader:

- translation still matters, but some major runs are now complete
- queue pressure now often comes from integration, selective probing, and source-near verification
- apparatus pluralism has become explicit rather than accidental
- the living layer now has more differentiated note types than the older two-layer summary suggests

## What does not yet need charter revision

Recent work does **not** yet force any of the following:

- a new mission statement
- a new project motto
- replacement of "reconstruction project for whole knowing"
- a charter-level declaration that the repo is primarily a method project, a knowledge system, or a dynamic instrument
- a charter-level promotion of the newer conversation vocabulary around energies, temperatures, fields, or program-building

Those belong, if anywhere, in living summaries until they survive longer contact.

## One real tension

There is one live pressure worth naming.

The charter still presents the project through its purpose and subprojects, which is right. But the repo now has a more articulated **operational middle** than the charter or even the process note makes easy to see:

- experiments
- extractions
- distillations
- working syntheses
- method notes
- atelier sidecar work
- scaffolded but unopened source surfaces

This does not mean the charter is wrong. It means the repo now needs a clearer living account of how those middle layers support the mission without being mistaken for the mission.

## Recommended discipline

Use a two-speed review rule:

1. **Charter review rarely.** Revisit only when the project's aim, limits, or non-negotiable commitments have changed.
2. **Self-description review periodically.** Revisit after major phase shifts: completed campaigns, new note classes that prove durable, or real changes in what the queue is asking for.

In practice, this means the repo should probably have occasional notes like this one rather than frequent charter churn.

## Provisional answer

If asked today what has changed, the best answer is:

The project is still trying to recover, reconnect, and form through hard text work. What has changed is that it now better understands the range of supporting practices needed to do that honestly: differentiated apparatus, bounded probes, selective source-near testing, and clearer separation between exploratory energy and canonical authority.

That is a revision of self-description, not of mission.
