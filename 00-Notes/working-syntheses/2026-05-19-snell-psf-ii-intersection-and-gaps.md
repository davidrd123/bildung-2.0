# Snell / PSF II Intersection And Gaps

**Status:** Working synthesis. This note integrates the active Snell scaffold,
the PSF II Greek substrate pressure note, and the Hermetic / Trismegistus gap.
It does not promote Snell, PSF II, Greek myth, or the Hermetica to a full
`texts/` campaign.

## Why This Exists

The mythic Greek shelf now has a double pressure source:

- Cassirer PSF II needs Greek material for myth as a symbolic form.
- Snell needs Greek material for the historical discovery of mind from Homer
  through tragedy, philosophy, and science.

Those two pressures overlap, but they are not identical. Snell is mainly
developmental: the Greek mind moves from myth toward concept, selfhood,
history, and science. Cassirer is morphological: myth is a durable symbolic
form with its own structures of thought, intuition, and life.

The methodologically important fact is the non-overlap. Snell reaches forward
into post-mythic concept formation and Hellenistic reception; PSF II reaches
sideways and downward into mystery religion, mythic number/space, Plato's
philosophical myths, and late-antique mythical-religious material.

## PSF II Shapes

Use these as the alignment test:

1. Form of thought: mythical causality, concrete/abstract relation, magic,
   mana, divine agency, and the mythical object.
2. Form of intuition: mythical space, time, number, sacred locality, qualitative
   ordering, and person-bound world articulation.
3. Form of life: mythical I, soul, community, ritual, religion, ethical
   emergence, and the dialectic toward religious or rational consciousness.

A source is PSF II-shaped when it lets one of these forms be observed directly,
not merely when it contains mythic themes.

## Snell Chapters Against PSF II

Strong overlap:

- `011-die-auffassung-des-menschen-bei-homer`: form of life. The mythical I
  before `psyche` becomes a philosophical object.
- `012-der-glaube-an-die-olympischen-goetter`: form of thought. Mythical
  causality and divine personhood.
- `013-die-welt-der-goetter-bei-hesiod`: form of thought. Cosmogony,
  genealogy, and succession of gods as world articulation.
- `014-das-erwachen-der-persoenlichkeit-in-der-fruehgriechischen-lyrik`: form
  of life. The I emerging from mythic anonymity; one of the closest Snell
  parallels to PSF II's dialectic.
- `016-mythos-und-wirklichkeit-in-der-griechischen-tragoedie`: form of life.
  Tragedy as myth under pressure from law, recognition, order, and guilt.
- `018-menschliches-und-goettliches-wissen`: form of thought. Mythical and
  rational knowledge in transition.
- `019-zur-entstehung-des-geschichtlichen-bewusstseins`: form of intuition.
  Historical time emerging out of mythical time.

Medium overlap:

- `015-pindars-hymnos-auf-zeus`: form of life. Mythic genealogy and religious
  praise, but less central for Cassirer's PSF II apparatus than Homer, Hesiod,
  and tragedy.
- `020-mahnung-zur-tugend`: form of life. Ethical reflection emerging from a
  religious-mythical background.

Weak or outside PSF II:

- `017-aristophanes-und-die-aesthetik`: comedy and aesthetic reflection. Useful
  for Snell, not a strong PSF II substrate.
- `021-gleichnis-vergleich-metapher-analogie`: comparison and analogy; closer
  to PSF III and general symbolic cognition.
- `022-die-naturwissenschaftliche-begriffsbildung-im-griechischen`: scientific
  concept formation; PSF III / `Substanzbegriff` territory.
- `023-das-symbol-des-weges`: literary symbol, mostly post-mythic.
- `024-die-entdeckung-der-menschlichkeit`: humanism, post-mythic.
- `025-ueber-das-spielerische-bei-kallimachos`: Hellenistic learned reception,
  not myth as symbolic form.
- `026-arkadien`: literary topos, post-mythic.
- `027-theorie-und-praxis`: theory/practice; weak PSF II, stronger PSF III.

Working summary: Snell and PSF II converge most strongly across chapters
11-20, minus Aristophanes. That is the archaic-classical band where mythical
consciousness is still directly visible and its movement toward rational
consciousness is already underway.

## PSF II-Shaped Text Cluster

This is the focused subset across shelves. It is narrower than the whole
mythic Greek / Snell shelf.

Load-bearing Greek narrative and poetic material:

- Homer, `Iliad` and `Odyssey`: mythical I, heroic personhood, divine causality,
  and the pre-philosophical soul surface.
- Homeric Hymns: sacred locality, divine epiphany, god/place articulation.
- Hesiod, especially `Theogony`: cosmogonic genealogy and succession of gods as
  form of thought.
- Aeschylus, especially the `Oresteia` and `Prometheus Bound`: guilt, divine
  order, civic law, and the dialectic from Erinyes to institutional order.
- Sophocles, especially `Antigone` and `Oedipus Tyrannus`: chthonic/civic law,
  oracle, fate, recognition, and the tragic form of life.
- Euripides, especially `Bacchae`: Dionysian return and the religious pressure
  on rational/civic order.
- Pindar: religious praise, genealogy, and mythic memory around agonistic life;
  useful but secondary.

Mythic-philosophical Plato:

- `Timaeus`: cosmogonic myth and mathematical world-formation.
- `Republic` X / Myth of Er: eschatological ordering, fate, choice, and the
  soul after death.
- `Symposium`: eros myths, Diotima, ascent, and soul/religion transition.
- `Phaedo`: afterlife myth and the soul as form-of-life problem.

Presocratic / mystical surfaces:

- Heraclitus: Apollo, Sibyl, fire, soul, and the religious register of logos.
- Empedocles, especially the `Katharmoi` surface: daimon, transmigration,
  purification, and Orphic-Pythagorean pressure.
- Pythagorean material: number before it becomes purely theoretical; a bridge
  between mythical number and mathematical concept.

Hermetic / late-antique continuation:

- Corpus Hermeticum I, `Poimandres`: revelation-dialogue, Anthropos myth,
  descent and re-ascent of soul.
- `Asclepius`: world as divine, statue-making, Egyptian wisdom, and made gods.
- Stobaean Hermetica, especially `Kore Kosmou`: world-soul / cosmic-mother
  fragments and mythic cosmology.

The Hermetic line is not just "more Greek." It is one of the places where myth
as form of consciousness speaks about itself in a reflective late-antique
register. That makes it PSF II-shaped even though it is not Snell-shaped.

## Snell Beyond PSF II

Snell's later chapters are legitimate for the Snell scaffold, but they should
not be silently recoded as PSF II substrate.

- Aristophanes: comedy, aesthetic reflection, and comic handling of inherited
  myth.
- Callimachus: learned Hellenistic play with myth; reception rather than
  myth-as-form.
- Scientific concept formation: Greek movement toward theory and natural
  science; better handled through PSF III, `Substanzbegriff`, and Greek
  mathematics.
- Humanism, Arcadia, theory/practice: post-mythic afterlives of Greek symbolic
  material.

These are why the shelf name keeps both Cassirer and Snell. Snell's corpus is
broader than the PSF II-shaped subset, and that breadth should remain visible.

## PSF II Beyond Snell

PSF II needs material Snell does not center:

- Mystery religion and ritual primary surfaces.
- Empedoclean purification, Orphic-Pythagorean pressure, and Heraclitus as
  religious thinker.
- Plato's philosophical myths as myth transformed inside philosophy.
- Hermetic / Trismegistus material as late-antique mythical-religious
  self-description.
- Mythical number, space, and time as forms of intuition rather than stages in
  a Greek developmental story.

This is the methodological correction Cassirer supplies against a purely
developmental reading. Myth is not only an earlier stage on the way to thought.
It is a form of symbolic consciousness with its own internal law.

## Working Rule

When a future note says "PSF II Greek substrate," it should mean the focused
PSF II-shaped cluster above. When it says "mythic Greek / Snell substrate," it
can mean the broader Snell-facing corpus. Keeping those two labels distinct
prevents the whole Greek shelf from being overcoded as PSF II material.
