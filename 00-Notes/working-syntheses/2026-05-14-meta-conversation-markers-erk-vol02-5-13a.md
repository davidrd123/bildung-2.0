## Threads that calibrated working register without becoming artifacts

These shaped how the verification work was conducted but did not produce specific apparatus changes. They are pointers, not content:

- **LLM introspection as functional/metaphorical, not phenomenological.** Honest hedge: claims about how "common philosophical German feels different from English" are pattern-recognition descriptions plausible at functional level, not reliable phenomenology.

- **Cognitive resources as intelligence-constitutive (user correction of overly-soft framing).** Reset the framing of what the project is doing: not preserving stylistic preference but maintaining cognitive infrastructure that affects what can be thought.

- **Loss of classical education in English specifically: greater than for German speakers.** German preserves compositional morphology natively; English depended on classical training as the bridge to its Latinate vocabulary, and that bridge eroded post-1950 with mass-education curriculum shifts.

- **Jewish educational tradition as counter-example to "mass education necessarily trades depth for breadth."** Community-sustained depth at scale is possible when the cultural anchor (religious or otherwise) makes the depth obviously worth the cost.

- **Spengler-adjacent worry: democracy and elite culture incompatibility.** Structural argument that elite cognitive infrastructure has historically required insulation (aristocratic, religious, institutional) that democracy dissolves. Hollowing-out has historical precedents that high cultures have survived, but only through community-sustained pockets, not through democratic-political defense.

- **Negative archaeology as the project's actual move.** The translation work doesn't just transmit German content into English; it simultaneously surfaces what English has lost in handling the same concepts. This is the cross-linguistic vantage point no single-language reader has from inside their own language.

- **LLM-as-polyglot-scholar-on-demand (corrected from "bridge between cognitive infrastructures").** Honest revision: the LLM is not bridging native fluencies; it's deploying dense statistical pattern-knowledge of German morphology, Latin etymology, and English Latinate substrate, calibrated to the user's specific question. The capability resembles a polymath scholar's synthesis. What's new is the *availability conditions* — on-demand, dialogic, calibrated — not the capability itself.

- **Lotman on productive asymmetry: minimal pair + translation block.** Lotman's claim is more specific than "more friction is productive." The minimal working semiotic structure is *two mutually non-translatable codes linked by a conventional equivalence-system*. Asymmetry between codes is what generates new meaning; the translation block is what makes the transfer possible despite the asymmetry. Repo connection: `sources/russian/lotman-vnutri-mislyashih-mirov/encounters/2026-05-04-001-minimal-paired-heterogeneity.md` already names the connection to the project's translation-residue practice.

- **Peirce-Lotman comparison: triadic family resemblance, different architecture.** Both reject dyadic semiotics; both insist meaning requires a third term. But Peirce's third term (interpretant) is the *output* of the sign relation, while Lotman's third term (translation block) is the *mechanism* of the inter-code relation. Family resemblance, not architectural identity.

- **Parallel/stereoscopic translation as Lotmanian asymmetry.** The candidate move at `00-Notes/method/stereoscopic-parallax-translation-candidate.md` is already on file; this session's discussion of it added the Lotman-specific framing (the productive case is non-isomorphic codes, not redundant transfers) and the caution that parallel translation should be applied at points of high pressure, not as a general method.
