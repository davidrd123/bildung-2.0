## Working Claim

The live comparison is not "Plato already has Humboldt" and not "Humboldt is a
late natural-name theorist." The sharper seam is a displacement:

- In *Cratylus* 388b-c, the name is an `organon`: a teaching and discriminating
  instrument, capable of dividing things according to how they are.
- In 389-390, this tool frame is governed by a maker/use structure: the
  name-maker or lawgiver looks toward the form of the name, while the
  dialectician is the competent user and overseer.
- In Humboldt § 8, language is explicitly not a finished `Werk (Ergon)` but a
  `Thätigkeit (Energeia)`: the continually repeated work of spirit that makes
  articulated sound capable of thought-expression.
- Cassirer reads this Humboldt formula as a genetic account of language:
  objectivity is held not in the single word as a finished substance, but in
  lawful synthesis, repeated formation, sentence, and activity.

The contact point is therefore methodological. Both source surfaces resist
treating words as arbitrary detached labels. But they secure non-arbitrariness
differently. Plato's local frame asks how the name functions as a correct tool
under dialectical supervision. Humboldt's frame relocates the linguistic object
away from a tool/product and into recurrent living production.

## Cassirer Constraint

Cassirer does not license a naive identification here. In the Cratylus passage
of PSF I, he emphasizes that Plato's dialogue destroys the simple thesis of a
natural correctness of each name. The name remains a beginning point and
vehicle of knowledge, not the possession of the idea itself.

That matters for the Humboldt seam. Cassirer's later Humboldt reading is not a
return to natural names; it is a shift from resemblance to synthesis. The
single word no longer carries a closed concept as a substance. It prompts a
formation, and the sentence becomes the truer bearer of linguistic sense.

## Pressure Point

Use the comparison this way:

1. Cratylus gives the old instrument/problem surface: name as tool, maker,
   user, correctness, nature, discrimination.
2. Humboldt gives the activity surface: language as living work, written and
   analytic residues as incomplete preservation, connected speech as the place
   where the living form appears.
3. Cassirer gives the methodological conversion: the old question of
   word/object correctness becomes a question of symbolic function, synthesis,
   and lawful production.

The comparison is strongest when it stays at this level. It becomes weak if it
claims that Plato's natural-name thesis simply survives in Humboldt, or that
Humboldt's `Energeia` can be reduced to a theory of naming.

## Chapter III Follow-Through

The new chapter III support strengthens the comparison by moving it away from
single-word correctness and toward operations of formation.

Cassirer's 1923 Humboldt essay makes the conversion explicit: Kant's problem
of judgment and objectivity reappears, for Humboldt, as a problem of linguistic
synthesis. Objectivity is not a given thing copied by a word; it is something
won through lawful formation. The sentence, not the isolated word, becomes the
primary linguistic surface because it enacts relation.

The Kawi and *Ortsadverbien* tranches show how Cassirer can carry that into PSF
I chapter III without abstracting away from philology:

- Kawi Drittes Buch makes comparative method depend on agreement of `Form`,
  not just shared word-stock.
- The Kawi space-verb locus shows spatial relation becoming graspable as
  directed act rather than as a static local noun.
- The *Ortsadverbien* pronoun tranche makes `Ich`, `Du`, and `Er` original
  relation-points of speech, not late labels attached to already constituted
  objects.
- The *Ortsadverbien* spatial-deixis tranche keeps place and person together:
  here/there/near/far are not inert coordinates but speech-relative
  orientations.

So the Cratylus pressure is useful precisely because Cassirer does not let it
remain a theory of names. The old question "how can the name be a correct
instrument?" is converted into the question "what operations make an object,
a relation, or an I/Thou position available to speech at all?"

## Rival Reading / Limit

The later movement of the *Cratylus* matters. Cassirer himself points to
438dff. as part of the dialogue's result, where learning things from names is
put under pressure. This weakens any direct "Plato equals Humboldt" line. It
does not weaken the crosswalk itself, because the useful comparison is between
two ways of refusing mere convention while also refusing the single word as a
self-sufficient possession of truth.

## Current Result

The chapter III test now supports the displacement. Cassirer's use of Humboldt
and Kawi extends the movement from isolated linguistic products toward the
formative operations by which language organizes intuition, relation, person,
and conceptual expression.

Held limit: this is still a source-near working synthesis, not a claim that
Plato anticipates Humboldt or that Cassirer derives PSF I from the *Cratylus*.
The counter-pressure remains built into the comparison: the *Cratylus* itself
undermines any simple natural-name doctrine, and that weakening is what makes
the later Cassirer/Humboldt conversion sharper.
