# Experiment 02: `Umwelt` — anti-psychologism and reconstruction

Purpose: test whether a second replay packet on the same live term produces a distinct usable shift, or merely redescribes the pressure already recorded in Experiment 01.

Status: living-layer pilot. This is an experiment note, not a canonical synthesis.

## Baseline To Beat

The cheap result would be:

> packet 02 just restates that `Umwelt` is organism-centered and difficult to translate.

This run only counts if it produces:

- a distinct shift from Experiment 01
- one sharper warning about at least one current English candidate
- a plausible judgment on whether `Umwelt` carries more than one live pressure

## Question

What does the 02b anti-psychologism passage do to the current pressure on `Umwelt`?

## Packet

See `packet-02-umwelt-anti-psychologism-and-reconstruction.md`.

## Method

1. Hold fixed the glossary state and the anti-privacy finding.
2. Carry forward the result of Experiment 01.
3. Read only for what the anti-psychologism passage changes in how `Umwelt` is available to us.
4. Force the result back onto the existing English alternatives.

## Result

This experiment does not overturn the prior `Umwelt` findings. It **adds a methodological constraint** to them.

Experiment 01 made `Umwelt` harder on the side of structure:

- not passive surroundings
- not neutral container
- the outer segment of the circle is itself planfully answering

Experiment 02 makes `Umwelt` harder on the side of access:

- the animal's world is not something we may simply *switch into*
- `Umwelt` is available only as a **constrained reconstruction within our own apperceptive unity**
- the term therefore resists not only neutralization (`environment`) but also empathic immediacy

So the term's pressure shifts again:

- from `not environment because centered`
- to `not environment because answering`
- now also to `not own-world because the world is not directly occupiable from the animal's side`

The result is that `Umwelt` now appears as a double refusal:

- refusal of passive outer-world language
- refusal of naive perspective-switching

That is a more useful pressure statement than either experiment produces on its own.

## What survived unchanged

- The anti-privacy finding still holds. Nothing here turns `Umwelt` into a sealed private interior.
- The planful answering structure from Experiment 01 survives. This passage does not weaken the world-side fit.
- The centeredness of `Umwelt` still matters; the subject-cut world remains real in Uexküll's argument.
- Keeping `Umwelt` in German in running drafts remains the right decision.

## What shifted

- Experiment 01 sharpened the structure of the world-side.
- Experiment 02 sharpens the *epistemic stance* from which that world may be treated.
- The key shift is that `Umwelt` can no longer be described simply as "the animal's own world" without a warning. That phrase is directionally right, but it hides the methodological prohibition against sudden viewpoint-switching.

So the glossary pressure on `Umwelt` now has at least two non-identical components:

1. **answering structure**
   the world-side is fitted into the functional circle rather than merely surrounding the organism

2. **reconstruction constraint**
   the foreign Umwelt must be reconstructed from our own qualities and apperception, not inhabited by empathic transfer

This looks less like one difficulty deepening and more like a junction of multiple pressures.

## New residue made visible by the replay

The English failures now split differently than in Experiment 01:

- `environment` still fails by passivizing the world-side
- `surrounding world` still stays too merely adjacent
- `own-world` now fails more sharply than before, because it invites exactly the wrong move: as if one could simply occupy the animal's side and report its world from within

So Experiment 02 does not generate a better rendering. It makes one candidate's hidden danger more explicit.

The cleaner sentence this experiment earns is:

> `Umwelt` is not only an organism-centered, planfully answering world; for us it is available only as a constrained reconstruction, not as an adoptable inner viewpoint.

That sentence is again not a replacement rendering. It is a pressure summary.

## Provisional Judgment

Experiment 02 succeeded.

Why:

- it produced a distinct shift from Experiment 01 rather than repeating it
- it made one English failure (`own-world`) more precisely diagnosable
- it suggests that `Umwelt` is a junction-term carrying at least two live pressures

That is enough to justify one more test before saying the replay form has really earned a standing place.

## What this does not license

- not the claim that `Umwelt` is now adequately captured by "constrained reconstruction"
- not promotion of a new glossary note without rereading the surrounding campaign
- not a general thesis that Uexküll is simply anti-psychological
- not the conclusion that all open terms split into multiple pressure-components
- not a standing method rule built from two packets alone
