# Experiment 03: `thread:time-crisis` x `thread:orthogonal-time` — temporal occlusion?

Purpose: test whether a bounded collision between Jünger's `thread:time-crisis` and Dick's `thread:orthogonal-time` produces a sharper shared structure than the cheap sentence "both oppose ordinary linear time."

Status: living-layer pilot. This is an experiment note, not a canonical synthesis.

## Baseline To Beat

The cheap result would be:

> Jünger and Dick both think measured or ordinary time is false and that a truer time lies behind it.

This run only counts if it produces:

- a sharper bridge sentence than the baseline
- one useful negative where direct equivalence fails
- a clearer judgment on whether the bridge belongs between terms or between relations

## Question

When `thread:time-crisis` meets `thread:orthogonal-time`, is the real shared structure simply "hidden time," or something sharper like **temporal occlusion**?

## Packet

See `packet-03-time-crisis-orthogonal-time-temporal-occlusion.md`.

## Method

1. Use `thread:time-crisis` as the Jünger-side traversal surface, with `§4` as the local pressure-point.
2. Use `thread:orthogonal-time` as the Dick-side traversal surface, with `black-iron-prison` only where fake time and occlusion need to be specified.
3. Force the comparison at the level of relation between substitute and real time, not at the level of total worldview.
4. Require one negative result where direct term-pairing breaks.

## Result

The packet does **not** justify the sentence `Schicksalszeit = orthogonal time`.

It does justify a narrower shared structure:

> Both dossiers track situations in which a substitute temporality becomes the operative reality by covering over a more real time-order.

That is sharper than the baseline because the shared issue is not simply "another time exists." The sharper issue is:

- a false or flattened time can become livable, practical, and even persuasive
- the real time-order is then not merely absent, but **occluded** by the substitute

## What carries the bridge on the Jünger side

On the Jünger side, the bridge is carried by the relation between measurable time and `Schicksalszeit`, not by `Schicksalszeit` alone.

The decisive local pressure is `§4`:

- man values `Da-Sein` more than `So-Sein`
- duration, survival, luck, and continuation are preferred to substance, character, and significance

That pressure helps explain why measurable time wins. It is not simply imposed from nowhere. It answers a built human bias toward what can be counted, extended, insured, and planned.

So the Jünger-side result is:

- measurable time is not just one chronology among others
- it becomes a practical regime because it fits the organismic and civilizational demand for `Da-Sein`
- `Schicksalszeit` is therefore not destroyed but covered over by a time-order better suited to planning, accident-accounting, and uniformity

This is why `thread:time-crisis` is the right surface: the dossier is already showing how the problem widens from astrology into planning, record, catastrophe, right angle, and mass-form.

## What carries the bridge on the Dick side

On the Dick side, the bridge is carried by the relation between fake time and de-occlusion, not by orthogonal time as a settled register.

The decisive local pressures are:

- Folder 21's fourfold prison doctrine: fake world, perceptual occlusion, deterministic enslavement, and amnesia
- the orthogonal-time dossier's repeated claims about spurious interpolation, counterfeit duration, and no real time having passed since `Acts`
- Folder 90's late correction: the occlusion is lifted from us, not from the world

So the Dick-side result is:

- fake time is the temporal form of imprisonment
- orthogonal time is often the name for the axis along which counterfeit duration is interrupted, corrected, or seen through
- the real issue is not just a second register of time, but a world in which apparent elapsed history can function as camouflage over a truer order

This makes Dick's side structurally closer to prison and de-occlusion than to a simple alternate calendar.

## Useful negative

`Schicksalszeit` does **not** pair cleanly with `orthogonal-time`.

That pairing fails because:

- `Schicksalszeit` is Jünger's name for the more real qualitative time being crowded out by measurable time
- `orthogonal-time` is often Dick's name for the axis or mechanism by which counterfeit duration is pierced, corrected, or bypassed

So the tighter bridge is not:

- `Schicksalszeit` <-> `orthogonal-time`

but:

- measurable time <-> fake / counterfeit duration
- `Schicksalszeit` <-> the more real time-order that fake duration hides

That is still only a problem-structure match, not a term-equivalence.

## Provisional Judgment

Experiment 03 succeeded.

Why:

- it produced a sharper bridge sentence than "both oppose linear time"
- it preserved a useful negative rather than smoothing the terms into one another
- it clarified that the real cross-project contact is at the level of **occlusion relation**, not shared vocabulary

The packet also explains why the earlier `Umwelt` pressure matters here without forcing a separate Uexküll bridge note:

- on the Jünger side, measurable time succeeds because it answers a real bias in the subject or organism
- on the Dick side, counterfeit duration succeeds because damaged awareness can inhabit it as if it were reality

That is enough to make `temporal occlusion` a live packet target.

## What this does not license

- not a new cross-project handle yet
- not the claim that Jünger and Dick have the same temporal metaphysics
- not the claim that Dick's "no real time has passed since Acts" and Jünger's `Schicksalszeit` are interchangeable
- not import of sheaf/topos vocabulary
- not a biosemiotics or Peirce turn
