# Experiment 03A: Time Crisis Passage-Scale Rerun

1. Result statement: The contact survives at passage scale, but only as a relation between substitute time and a more-real order, not as a shared doctrine of time itself.

2. Bridge sentence: Jünger’s measurable duration versus Schicksalszeit and Dick’s counterfeit elapsed time both mark ordinary time as secondary to a deeper order, but they place that deeper order in different registers.

3. Refusal sentence: The passages do not justify treating `Schicksalszeit` and orthogonal time as the same thing, because Jünger keeps fate tied to the world-clock and visible historical pressure while Dick makes false time a perceptual artifact that collapses from the subject side.

4. What on the Jünger side carries the bridge: `§4` gives the key pressure point in the contrast between `Da-Sein` and `So-Sein`, where measurable duration, luck, and continuation outrank substance and significance; `§102` adds the time-wall scene in which monotony robs within historical space while something future knocks from beyond it.

5. What on the Dick side carries the bridge: Folder 21 gives counterfeit duration through look-differents and spurious elapsed time, and Folder 90 sharpens the correction by saying the blockage is lifted from us rather than from the world.

6. Provisional judgment: The packet worked. It produces a real passage-scale contact, but the contact sits at the level of relation and pressure, not at the level of a common ontology or shared terminology.

7. What the result does not license: It does not license a direct equivalence between Jünger’s fate-time and Dick’s orthogonal time, nor a wider synthesis of their cosmologies, nor the claim that the world itself is equally transformed in both cases.
