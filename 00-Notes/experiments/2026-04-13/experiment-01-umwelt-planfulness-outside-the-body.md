# Experiment 01: `Umwelt` — planfulness outside the body

Purpose: test whether a small replay packet can produce a usable note about how pressure on one live open term changes, rather than just restating what the passage says.

Status: living-layer pilot. This is an experiment note, not a canonical synthesis.

## Baseline To Beat

The cheap result would be:

> `Umwelt` is difficult because it means something like the organism's own environment.

This run only counts if it produces:

- a sharper pressure statement than the existing glossary note
- one explicit continuity with the earlier anti-privacy finding
- one explicit shift produced by the new passage
- a more precise account of why the English candidates fail differently

## Question

What does the 02b `Planmäßigkeit`-outside-the-body passage do to the current pressure on `Umwelt`?

## Packet

See `packet-01-umwelt-planfulness-outside-the-body.md`.

## Method

1. Hold the term state fixed from the existing glossary entry.
2. Carry forward the strongest prior finding from encounter 05.
3. Read only for what the new passage changes in the pressure profile.
4. Force the result back onto the current English alternatives rather than drifting into theory language.

## Result

The passage does not cool the term at all. It **densifies** the pressure and shifts its center.

Before replay, the strongest pressure on `Umwelt` was:

- not neutral container
- not merely private bubble
- part of a weave of overlapping worlds

After replay, a sharper pressure becomes visible:

- `Umwelt` is not only centered and organism-bound
- it is also the **world-side segment of a planful circuit**
- the outside part of the circle must be read under `Planmäßigkeit`, not only the organismic part

This matters because it makes the failure of `environment` more precise. The problem is not only that `environment` sounds too neutral or homogeneous. The deeper problem is that it leaves the outer world passive. In this passage the outer segment is not passive. It is built, cut, and functioning as part of one biological whole.

The compact foot/path, mouth/food, weapon/enemy sequence intensifies this further. It gives `Umwelt` a reciprocal or answering structure:

- the organism does not merely face surroundings
- the world-side counterpart is already fitted to the kind of act the organism can perform

So the pressure on `Umwelt` shifts from:

- `not environment because centered`

to:

- `not environment because the world-side is functionally answering, not merely surrounding`

## What survived unchanged

- The earlier anti-privacy finding survives fully. Nothing here reverts `Umwelt` to a sealed inner bubble.
- The centeredness of `Umwelt` still matters. The world remains organism-bound rather than neutrally given.
- The decision to keep `Umwelt` in German in running drafts remains correct.

## What shifted

- Encounter 05 made the strongest pressure point plurality and overlap.
- This replay passage shifts the pressure back toward **reciprocal fit** and **world-side planfulness**.
- The term is now harder in a different way: not because it names many incompatible worlds, but because it names a world that answers as part of one circuit.

That means the current glossary note is right but incomplete in emphasis. It names centeredness and non-privacy well. It understates the degree to which the *outside segment* of the circle is itself under `Planmäßigkeit`.

## New residue made visible by the replay

The English alternatives now split along different losses:

- `environment` preserves outerness but makes the world-side passive
- `surrounding world` preserves worldness better, but still sounds too merely adjacent
- `own-world` preserves centeredness, but slides back toward privacy and misses the answering structure

So the replay does not produce a better English candidate. It produces a better account of **why all three fail differently**.

The cleanest short sentence the replay earns is:

> `Umwelt` is not just the organism's centered world; it is the planfully answering outer segment of the functional circle.

That sentence is not a glossary replacement. It is a pressure summary.

## Provisional Judgment

Experiment 01 succeeded.

It did not produce a new settled rendering, but it did produce a more usable pressure statement than the prior note alone:

- the term got hotter, not cooler
- the shift was specific
- the note is more useful for the next reading session than a generic summary would have been

## What this does not license

- not promotion of any English equivalent for `Umwelt`
- not a new glossary status
- not a claim that `Umwelt` is now solved by the phrase `answering world`
- not a general theory of replay packets
- not a framework-level claim that all open terms should now be tracked this way
