# Experiment 03: `Umwelt` — `Merkzeichen -> Merkmal` family pressure

1. **Result statement**

   The packet works, but it does not leave `Umwelt` isolated. The explicit `Merkzeichen -> Merkmal` transfer deepens the already-visible answering-structure pressure and shows that `Umwelt` is only stable inside a larger `Merk* / Funktionskreis / Planmäßigkeit` family.

2. **What survived unchanged**

   `Umwelt` is still not a neutral external container and not a private bubble. The centered, organism-bound world pressure from Experiments 01 and 02 survives intact, and the late anti-privacy finding from Encounter 05 still holds.

3. **What shifted**

   The new passage does not add a wholly different problem; it sharpens the same one by making the inner sign and outer feature share one law-bound structure. `Merkzeichen` is no longer just a local sign term, and `Merkmal` is no longer just a loose world-feature: the passage shows them as one transfer under `Planmäßigkeit`.

4. **Is `Umwelt` still isolable?**

   Less isolable. The passage only reads cleanly when `Umwelt` is kept inside the broader `Merk*` family, because the lawfulness binding subject and world now runs through the sign-to-feature transfer itself rather than around it.

5. **New residue in the English alternatives**

   `environment` still passivizes the world-side. `surrounding world` keeps worldness but loses the family transfer. `own-world` preserves centeredness but wrongly suggests privacy. None of them carries the pressure where attention-marks become world-features under one law.

6. **Provisional judgment**

   The packet worked. It produces a sharper diagnosis than the earlier two packets: the real unit is not `Umwelt` alone but the `Merk* / Funktionskreis / Planmäßigkeit` cluster that makes `Umwelt` readable at all.

7. **What this does not license**

   It does not license a settled English rendering, a general theory of Uexküll biology, or a claim that `Merkzeichen`, `Merkmal`, `Merkwelt`, and `Umwelt` can be split back apart without loss.
