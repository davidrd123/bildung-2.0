# Experiment 03: `Umwelt` — `Merkzeichen -> Merkmal` family pressure

Purpose: test whether a third replay packet on the same open term still
produces a usable isolated pressure note, or whether Encounter 03's explicit
`Merkzeichen -> Merkmal` transfer shows that `Umwelt` can only be read inside a
larger `Merk* / Funktionskreis / Planmaessigkeit` family.

Status: living-layer pilot. This is an experiment note, not a canonical
synthesis.

## Baseline To Beat

The cheap result would be:

> Encounter 03 just confirms that `Umwelt` is part of a larger chain and
> therefore remains difficult.

This run only counts if it produces:

- a sharper account of what the `Merk*` transfer changes in the pressure on
  `Umwelt`
- a real judgment about whether `Umwelt` is still the right isolated target
- one more precise diagnosis of what the current English candidates now fail to
  carry

## Question

What does the explicit `Merkzeichen -> Merkmal` sequence do to the current
pressure on `Umwelt`?

## Packet

See `packet-03-umwelt-merkzeichen-merkmal-family-pressure.md`.

## Method

1. Hold fixed the current glossary state for `Umwelt`.
2. Carry forward the two strongest replay findings already earned:
   answering structure and reconstruction constraint.
3. Read only the quoted `Merkzeichen -> Merkmal` sequence and the packet's
   cited support line on `Planmaessigkeit`.
4. Force the result back onto the existing English alternatives rather than
   solving the wider family.

## Result

This packet works, but not by giving `Umwelt` a cleaner isolated profile. It
shows that the term's pressure can no longer be stated adequately at the level
of `Umwelt` alone.

Experiment 01 made one thing harder on the side of structure:

- the world-side of the circle is not passive surroundings
- it is a planfully answering outer segment

Experiment 02 made one thing harder on the side of access:

- the foreign `Umwelt` is available only as constrained reconstruction
- we do not step directly into the animal's own viewpoint

Encounter 03 does not simply add a third unrelated difficulty. It shows how the
first two pressures belong to one transfer:

- inner `Merkzeichen` are defined as just-noticeable changes in attention
- these `Merkzeichen` become the world's `Merkmale`
- the same laws bind both sides

That changes the pressure on `Umwelt` in a specific way. The world-side is no
longer only \"answering\" in the looser sense earned by Experiment 01. It is now
under pressure as the outer articulation of the same lawful structure that first
appears inwardly as `Merkzeichen`. And the reconstruction warning from
Experiment 02 no longer looks like a separate methodological caution laid on top
of that structure. It now appears as a consequence of it: the observer cannot
know the foreign subject's own `Merkzeichen`, so the foreign `Umwelt` must be
reconstructed through our own objectified `Merkmale`.

So the packet's real result is not:

- `Umwelt` has acquired one more pressure component

but rather:

- the previously visible pressures now look internally linked
- `Umwelt` appears less as a standalone difficult noun and more as the
  world-level node in a larger `Merk* / Funktionskreis / Planmaessigkeit`
  complex

## What survived unchanged

- `Umwelt` is still not a neutral external container.
- `Umwelt` is still not a merely private bubble; Encounter 05's overlapping and
  partly contradictory worlds remain the strongest check against that.
- Experiment 01's answering-structure finding survives. The outer side is still
  not passive surroundings.
- Experiment 02's reconstruction constraint survives. We still do not gain the
  foreign subject's own viewpoint by simple perspective-switching.
- Keeping `Umwelt` in German in running drafts remains the right decision.

## What shifted

- Before this packet, `Umwelt` looked like a junction carrying at least two live
  pressures: answering structure and reconstruction constraint.
- After this packet, those pressures look less parallel and more structurally
  connected.
- The key shift is that the world-side is now pressured not just as fitted
  outer counterpart, but as the lawful continuation of `Merkzeichen` into
  `Merkmale`.
- That means the observer problem is no longer merely \"we cannot get inside the
  animal's experience.\" It is also that any foreign `Umwelt` reaches us only
  through the same family-transfer that already binds inner sign and outer
  feature.

So the packet does not mainly deepen `Umwelt` as an isolated term. It shifts the
operative unit from the isolated node toward the family that makes the node
legible.

## Is `Umwelt` still the right isolated pressure target?

Less and less.

As an entry point, `Umwelt` still works. As the main isolated replay target, it
now looks too narrow. Encounter 03 makes the family look like the real unit,
because `Umwelt` only comes into view here through the transfer from
`Merkzeichen` to `Merkmale` under one `Planmaessigkeit`, and because the older
`Funktionskreis` result from Experiment 01 is what gives that world-side its
circulatory place.

So the packet's direct judgment is:

> `Umwelt` is still usable as a node, but no longer looks like the right
> isolated pressure target. The `Merk* / Funktionskreis / Planmaessigkeit`
> family has become the operative unit.

## New residue made visible by the replay

The English alternatives now fail more specifically than before:

- `environment` preserves outerness but treats the world as a ready-made field
  around the organism. It cannot carry the pressure that worldly `Merkmale` are
  the outer continuation of inward `Merkzeichen` under one law.
- `surrounding world` keeps more worldness, but it still sounds adjacent rather
  than transferred and law-bound. It misses the family pressure almost as badly
  as `environment`.
- `own-world` preserves centeredness, but it now fails twice: it invites privacy
  and it suggests that centeredness is the whole issue, when the passage is
  actually tightening the relation between sign, feature, and world.

The new residue is therefore not only that no English candidate settles
`Umwelt`. It is that any isolated English candidate for `Umwelt` now drops the
very family-structure that made the packet useful. The smoother the English
gets, the more completely the `Merk*` transfer disappears.

## Provisional Judgment

Experiment 03 succeeded.

Why:

- it did not merely restate that `Umwelt` belongs to a larger chain
- it showed that the earlier two pressures are now better read as one linked
  pressure-complex
- it produced a real judgment about unit of analysis: the family has become more
  decisive than the isolated term

The packet therefore worked, but it also revealed a limit of the replay form:
another `Umwelt`-only pass would likely hide the very thing this packet brought
into view.

## What this does not license

- not a settled English rendering for `Umwelt`
- not a settled English rendering for `Merkzeichen` or `Merkmal`
- not a full theory of Uexkuell's biology
- not a promoted doctrine of supra-subjective lawfulness
- not the claim that `Umwelt` should be removed as a glossary node
- not a new glossary architecture on the basis of this packet alone

## Next Honest Move

If there is a next bounded note here, it should not be another replay of
`Umwelt` in isolation. It should either:

- stop with the judgment already earned here
- or track the `Merk* / Funktionskreis / Planmaessigkeit` cluster explicitly,
  because that is now where the pressure is visibly gathering
