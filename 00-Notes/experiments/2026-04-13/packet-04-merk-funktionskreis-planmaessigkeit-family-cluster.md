# Packet 04: `Merk* / Funktionskreis / Planmaessigkeit` family cluster

Purpose: test the smallest family-level claim now earned by the Uexkuell
source campaign after the first three `Umwelt` replay packets. Packet 03
showed a limit: `Umwelt` still works as a node, but no longer looks like the
right isolated pressure target. This packet asks what the source itself now
forces when the surrounding family is treated as the operative unit.

Status: successor packet only. This is a packet note, not a result note.

## Task

Using the bounded surfaces below, answer this question:

What is the minimum real family claim the source now earns across
`Merkzeichen`, `Merkmal`, `Merkwelt`, `Umwelt`, `Funktionskreis`, and
`Planmaessigkeit`?

More narrowly:

- what makes these terms one real local cluster rather than a loose glossary
  neighborhood
- what still keeps them from collapsing into one generic doctrine
- whether the cluster is strong enough to deserve explicit tracking as a
  reading unit

## Constraints

- Do not write a full theory of Uexkuell's biology.
- Do not treat lexical family alone as proof of conceptual unity.
- Do not simply restate that the terms are "related."
- Do not dissolve the distinct work done by each term.
- Keep prior packet results in view, but do not let replay language outrun the
  passages themselves.
- Require one explicit statement of what binds the family and one explicit
  statement of what still differentiates its members.
- Do not settle any English rendering.
- Do not turn this into a glossary-architecture proposal.
