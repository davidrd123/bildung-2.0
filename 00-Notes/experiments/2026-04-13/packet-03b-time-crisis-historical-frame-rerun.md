# Packet 03B: `thread:time-crisis` — historical-frame rerun against `orthogonal-time.md`

Purpose: preserve a second passage-scale successor to Packet 03. This packet tests whether the cross-project contact survives when the Jünger side is taken not from `Da-Sein / So-Sein` or substance-accounting, but from the failure of the historical frame and the danger of naming what appears beyond it.

Status: successor packet only. This is a packet note, not a result note.

## Task

Using the bounded surfaces below, answer this question:

Does the contact between Jünger's `thread:time-crisis` and Dick's `orthogonal-time.md` still hold when the Jünger side is framed through historical breakdown and threshold-naming rather than through `Schicksalszeit` in its earlier explicit contrast?

More narrowly:

- is there a shared relation between an inherited time-frame that no longer fits and a more-real order pressing through it
- if so, what exactly carries that relation on each side
- what direct pairing still fails and should be refused

## Constraints

- Do not assume a shared structure in advance.
- Do not use the phrase `temporal occlusion` unless the passages themselves force it.
- Do not equate `Zeitmauer`, `Schicksalszeit`, and orthogonal time.
- Keep the comparison at the level of temporal relation and frame-failure, not whole cosmology.
- Require one bridge sentence and one refusal sentence.
- Treat the Exegesis side as a dossier surface, not as a declared handle-equivalent.
- Do not import modern vocabulary (`sheaf`, `topos`, Peirce, Hoffmeyer) into the result.

## Baseline To Beat

The cheap result would be:

> Jünger and Dick both think the present order hides something more real that our usual language cannot grasp.

This run only counts if it produces:

- a sharper bridge than that baseline
- one explicit refusal where direct pairing breaks
- a clearer judgment on whether the contact lives at the level of terms, relations, or only threshold-atmosphere
