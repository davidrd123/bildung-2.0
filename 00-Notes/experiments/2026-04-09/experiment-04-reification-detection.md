# Experiment 04: Reification Detection

Purpose: test whether Cassirer's reification pressure can actually discriminate among Junger's ordering terms, rather than merely casting all late depth-language as suspect.

Status: living-layer pilot. This is an experiment note, not a canonical synthesis.

## Baseline To Beat

The cheap result would be:

> every use of `Weltplan`, `Schöpfungsplan`, or `Urgrund` is already reified.

This run only counts if it produces:

- one clear hardened or object-like case
- one clear open or productive case
- one clear non-case
- a judgment on whether the Cassirer packet actually discriminated, rather than just generalized suspicion

## Question

When does an ordering term remain regulative, opening, or diagnostic, and when does it harden into a self-standing content, power, or legitimating agent?

## Method

1. Distill a Cassirer-side discriminator:
   - productive order remains a condition of valid inquiry
   - reification begins when that condition is made to depend on a self-standing object, force, or guarantor
2. Apply that discriminator to each Junger occurrence separately.
3. Force local classifications instead of one global verdict.
4. Keep one lexical decoy in view:
   - `Weltplan = Gesetz`
   - this should fail if the packet is honest

## Result

The packet discriminated. It did not simply generalize suspicion.

The stronger sentence is:

> Cassirer marks reification where a methodological or validity-conferring relation is forced to take support in a self-standing object, force, or guarantor; applied to Junger, this does not condemn depth-language wholesale but separates uses of `Weltplan` and `Urgrund` that remain bounded orientation-problems from uses that begin to legitimate events through a hidden superior agency.

More concretely:

- `§146` is the clearest hardened case
- `§110` is mixed and at risk
- `§182` is pressured but still productive
- `§175` is a clear non-case

## What the packet clarified

### 1. Cassirer's discriminator is narrower than “anti-metaphysics”

The Cassirer packet does not say that every larger ordering term is suspect.

It says something more precise:

- inquiry becomes valid when it holds to relations, functions, and measurable conditions
- trouble begins when those conditions are no longer enough on their own and are re-secured through a hidden support
- the decisive slide is not from science to largeness, but from methodological validity to existence-bearing power

That is why `115` is needed as baseline and `141/144` are needed as warning signs.

### 2. `§146` is a genuine hardened case

This is the clearest case in the Junger packet.

Why:

- the successful experiment is said to testify `auf sichtbare und unwiderlegliche Weise` to `Fortschritt im Weltplan`
- research works inside a plan superior to state-plans
- moral and theological objections fail before the experiment

Under the Cassirer discriminator, the problem is not that experiment is valued. Cassirer himself treats lawful construction and exact science positively.

The problem is that `Weltplan` here begins to function as a legitimating higher agency:

- it authorizes research beyond ordinary political or moral challenge
- it gives experiment a superior warrant rather than leaving it within bounded method

This is the nearest Junger comes in the packet to turning an ordering claim into an oversecured guarantee.

### 3. `§110` is mixed and at risk, not yet simply hardened

`§110` repeatedly pushes toward reification:

- human plan is enclosed by `Schöpfungsplan`
- `Weltplan` is `grenzenlos`
- every plan contains a hidden regulative
- `Weltvernunft` and the great chess-player image lean toward hidden agency

But the passage does not simply use `Weltplan` as a ready-made explanatory substance.

It still does real diagnostic work:

- plans are corrected when they overrun the historical field
- purpose and outcome diverge
- planning contains an element that escapes planning

So this is best classified as at-risk / mixed:

- still partly restatable as a claim about the limits and redirection of planning
- already leaning toward a quasi-agentive world-order

### 4. `§182` is pressured but still productive

This is the hardest case in the packet, and the most useful one.

`Urgrund` here does real explanatory work:

- the ground begins to stir
- historical experience and its methods are no longer enough
- questioning itself must rise from another quality

That could have collapsed into pure depth-intensifier language. It does not quite do so.

Why it remains productive:

- the passage does not use `Urgrund` to guarantee the truth of a settled doctrine
- it is bound to earth-historical experiment, dangerous acquisition of experience, and the inadequacy of existing method
- its force is to unsettle methodological self-sufficiency, not to close inquiry

This makes `§182` a productive/open case under pressure, not a clean hardened case.

It also justifies `opus`'s preference for the `v2` packet: if the discriminator can handle `§182`, it is doing real work.

### 5. `§175` is the clear non-case

This is the cleanest negative in the packet.

`Weltplan` here does not secure, explain, or authorize. It names lack:

- `das Unergänzte`
- `die Lücke`
- what no thought and no `Staatsplan` can fill

That is the opposite of Cassirer's reification pattern.

Instead of turning validity into a thing-like support, the passage marks an internal incompletion that remains unfilled.

So `§175` is not merely “less reified.” It is a real non-case.

## Negative result

`Weltplan = Gesetz` still fails.

The lexical temptation remains strong:

- Cassirer's `Gesetz`
- Junger's `Weltplan`

But under return to the passages, the pairing breaks again.

Why it fails:

- Cassirer's `Gesetz` belongs to the constructive logic of exact phenomenal knowledge
- Junger's `Weltplan` crosses scientific, political, sacred-time, and emptiness registers
- the Cassirer packet works here only as a discriminator of use, not as a term-equivalence machine

This is a useful failure because it prevents the experiment from collapsing into “law in one vocabulary, plan in another.”

## What this does not license

- not “all Junger depth-vocabulary is reified”
- not “Cassirer simply solves `Zeitmauer`”
- not “every supra-human ordering term is metaphysical failure”
- not “`Urgrund` is just a free intensifier”
- not “`Weltplan` is a neo-Kantian law concept in mythic language”

## Provisional Judgment

Experiment 04 succeeded.

The Cassirer packet did what it needed to do:

- it found one hardened case
- it found one mixed case
- it preserved one productive case
- it found one clear non-case

So the result is not a generalized suspicion of Junger's late vocabulary.

It is a usable operational distinction:

- beware passages where `Weltplan` or `Urgrund` begin to underwrite legitimacy or efficacy from above
- do not flatten passages where they instead mark limit, incompletion, or the inadequacy of current method

## Method Lesson

`Opus`'s composite recommendation was right here.

For reification-detection, the `v2` packet was the better default because:

- it kept the Cassirer side small and sharp
- it included `§182`, which is a genuine hard case rather than an easy dismissal
- it forced the result to discriminate rather than moralize

The extra `v1` loci were not needed for a first successful run.

## Next Honest Move

The next experiment should not widen this into a general thesis about Junger or about metaphysical language.

The strongest next use of this result is local:

- carry the discriminator forward into later `Zeitmauer` comparisons and the inverse-Bayle test
- keep `§146` and `§175` as the clearest contrast pair
- keep `§182` as the hard case that prevents the anti-reification rule from becoming too cheap
