# Experiment 06: Translation-Residue Clustering

Purpose: test whether the recorded losses now visible in the experiment chain and the two completed source runs cluster around a small number of German reasoning-pressures, or whether they are mainly isolated lexical problems.

Status: living-layer pilot. This is an experiment note, not a canonical synthesis.

## Baseline To Beat

The cheap result would be:

> German philosophical prose is just hard, and some words do not translate neatly.

This run only counts if it produces:

- at least two recurring residue types
- several examples for each
- one real non-cluster case
- a judgment on whether the clusters cut by reasoning-pressure rather than by project

## Question

Do recorded translation losses cluster around specific kinds of German reasoning-pressure, or are they mainly local quirks?

More narrowly:

- is there a recurring relation / operation residue family
- is there a recurring housing / footing / enclosure residue family
- do the compound-heavy examples form a real cluster or mainly a translation-handling pattern

## Method

1. Start with the pre-sorted families from the sharpened packet rather than inventing categories afterward.
2. Test whether each family hangs together by reasoning-pressure rather than by mere lexical similarity.
3. Force one non-cluster case.
4. Keep the pilot modest: identify recurring types, not a full translation theory.

## Result

The residue does cluster. Not into one grand pattern, but into two strong families and one weaker secondary family.

The strongest sentence is:

> The recorded losses cluster chiefly around German terms that bind cognition to active relation and effecting, and around German terms that cast epistemic insufficiency as housing, enclosure, and footing; the compound-heavy examples form a weaker secondary family that is less a single conceptual pressure than a recurring translation-management problem where English would close the field too quickly.

That is better than “German is hard,” because the packet did not merely collect awkward words. It found repeatable kinds of pressure.

## What the packet clarified

### 1. Strong family A: relation / operation residue

This family is real.

Its common feature is:

- German keeps truth and cognition tied to relation-as-operation, validity-as-function, or conceptual support-as-activity
- English tends to nominalize or flatten these into static abstractions

The clearest examples are:

- `Gesetzlichkeit des Wirkens`
  - not just “laws” and not just neutral `operation`, but the law-governed character of active effecting
- `reale Beziehung`
  - truth as a real relation rather than a freestanding proposition
- `reine Funktion der Geltung`
  - validity as active function rather than a detachable property
- `gedankliches Substrat`
  - not a hidden substance, but a conceptual support laid beneath appearances to make them measurable

What ties them together is not vocabulary class. It is the repeated pressure to resist turning active relation into static conceptual objecthood.

### 2. Strong family B: housing / footing / enclosure residue

This family is also real.

Its common feature is:

- German casts epistemic pressure in spatial and jurisdictional terms
- English can say “cannot be accommodated” or “standing-ground,” but often loses the stronger sense of housing, enclosure, or footing

The clearest examples are:

- `im Geschichtlichen nicht unterzubringen`
  - the issue is not just mismatch but non-housability within the historical frame
- `Standfläche`
  - not merely a chosen viewpoint, but the surface on which inquiry stands
- `umgrenzt`, `umfriedet`
  - plan is not just limited but bounded and enclosed
- `die Lücke`
  - not generic absence, but a locatable internal gap

This is a genuine reasoning-pressure family because it governs how inadequacy is pictured:

- not as abstract error
- but as failure of frame, shelter, or jurisdiction

### 3. Family C is weaker: compounds and retained German form a secondary cluster

This family partly holds, but less strongly than the first two.

The examples do recur:

- `Vorweiser / Vorweisung`
- `Leitfähigkeit`
- `Nomosschwund`
- `Ur-Kunde`
- `Abbreviaturen`
- `Leihgabe`
- and at the overlap, `Unergänzte`

But they do not all point to one conceptual pressure.

What they share more reliably is a translation-handling problem:

- German compresses a family, relation, or image into a term that English would either unpack too thinly or naturalize too quickly
- the translation response therefore keeps the German visible, or uses an intentionally awkward English, to avoid false closure

So this is a real secondary family, but it is best understood as a repeated translation-management pressure rather than a single reasoning-pressure on the level of Families A and B.

### 4. `Unergänzte` is the hinge example

`Unergänzte` sits between Families B and C.

It is:

- a compound-like case that resists smooth English
- but also a housing / gap case, because it names an internal incompletion within `Weltplan`

That overlap is useful. It shows the clusters are not rigid taxonomies.

## Non-cluster result

`literaliter` is the clearest non-cluster case.

Why it stays local:

- Junger himself glosses it in context with `historisch`
- it belongs to a specific triadic schema: `carnaliter / literaliter / spiritualiter`
- the difficulty is real, but it does not open out into a broader recurring residue family

So this is not a useful cluster-builder. It is a local rhetorical and historical pressure.

## Do the clusters cut by reasoning-pressure rather than by project?

Yes, but unevenly.

The first two clusters are best understood by pressure type:

- relation / operation
- housing / footing / enclosure

The evidence is unevenly distributed:

- Family A is Cassirer-heavy
- Family B is Junger-heavy

But that is because the two completed runs pressure different domains, not because the clustering is arbitrary.

So the pilot result is:

- the clusters do cut by reasoning-pressure more than by project
- but the current evidence is not broad enough for a stronger repo-wide law

## What this does not license

- not “all difficult German terms belong to a cluster”
- not “left in German” automatically means philosophically deeper
- not “the repo now has a full theory of translation residue”
- not “Cassirer and Junger share the same residue profile”

## Provisional Judgment

Experiment 06 succeeded as a pilot.

It found:

- two strong recurring residue types
- one weaker secondary family
- one real non-cluster

That is enough to justify carrying the distinction forward in later packet design and translation review.

It is not yet enough to justify a strong repo-wide claim without a more deliberate ledger-mining pass.

## Method Lesson

`v2`'s pre-sorted packet was the right way to run this.

If the cluster categories had been invented after the fact, this would have been much less trustworthy.

What made the pilot work:

- pre-sorted candidate families
- explicit non-cluster control
- willingness to say one family is weaker and partly a handling strategy rather than a deep conceptual unity

## Next Honest Move

The next experiment should be the inverse-Bayle / self-critique test.

The translation-residue pilot improved the control surface, but it did not itself demand a wider theory.

Its most useful immediate afterlife is practical:

- use Family A when a translation risks turning active relation into static abstraction
- use Family B when a translation risks smoothing non-housability or shifted footing into generic context
- use Family C as a warning that some compounds need visible awkwardness or retained German rather than premature naturalization
