# Experiment 02: Limit of the Orderable

Purpose: rerun the cross-text collision with a tighter packet and a sharper question, using the failure-analysis from `experiment-01-glossary-collision.md`.

Status: living-layer pilot. This is an experiment note, not a canonical synthesis.

## Baseline To Beat

Experiment 01 yielded:

> Cassirer shows how lawful relation positively constitutes objectivity; Junger shows how a later historical situation exposes the insufficiency of inherited lawful and historical partitions to contain what is becoming visible.

This second run should only count as a gain if it sharpens that sentence rather than merely restating it.

## Question

Where does each text place the limit of the historically or scientifically orderable?

More narrowly:

- when does ordered cognition still count as positive constitution?
- when does it become insufficient, overextended, or provincial?

## Method

1. Keep the packet at the level of concept/title pressure, not figures or thread bundles.
2. Ask one question only: where is order positive, and where does its limit appear?
3. Require one sentence that is more precise than the baseline from Experiment 01.
4. Record one negative pair where direct term-equivalence fails.

## Result

This packet collides more cleanly than Experiment 01.

The stronger sentence is:

> Cassirer places the limit of the orderable where lawful cognition is either not yet secured or is reified back into substance; Junger places it where historical, scientific, and state-plan forms of order remain real but become provincial before a threshold field they cannot fully name, master, or contain.

This is better than the first experiment's sentence because it distinguishes two different kinds of limit:

- Cassirer's limit is internal to the constitution of exact knowledge
- Junger's limit is the boundedness of an order-regime within a larger threshold situation

## What the collision clarified

### 1. Cassirer's limit is not “beyond science,” but the failure to secure lawful relation without reification

The key Cassirer line remains:

- exact science becomes possible only when relation and law are treated as constructive conditions of objectivity
- the recurrent failure comes when those conditions are turned back into substances, powers, or contents

So the limit of the orderable in Volume I is not a mystical outside. It is the point where lawful cognition is either:

- not yet methodically secured
- or deformed by being metaphysically re-secured

### 2. Junger's limit is not the destruction of order, but the failure of exclusive orderability

`Schicksalszeit`, `Weltplan`, and `An der Zeitmauer` together show that Junger does not simply oppose chaos to order.

Instead:

- measurable and historical forms of order are real
- state-plan and research-plan remain operative
- but those forms no longer exhaust the field in which naming, action, experiment, and historical self-understanding occur

The time wall is therefore not a place where order ceases. It is a place where inherited order-regimes become insufficient.

### 3. `An der Zeitmauer` sharpens the packet by naming the limit as threshold rather than as concept-failure alone

This is the biggest gain over Experiment 01.

`An der Zeitmauer` adds something neither `Schicksalszeit` nor `Weltplan` alone could carry:

- the limit is not only qualitative time
- not only a larger plan
- but a threshold where naming becomes dangerous, answers differ on this side and beyond, and even experiment moves in an intermediate field where the invisible outweighs the visible

That gives the Junger side a more precise shape than “later pressure on lawful order.”

### 4. The best bridge is now “positive order under bounded jurisdiction”

The useful common structure is:

- both texts preserve a positive role for order
- neither text allows one order-regime to count as the whole of reality

But the way they limit that regime differs.

- Cassirer limits it from within by the conditions of valid cognition
- Junger limits it from beyond by a threshold field that inherited categories cannot contain

## Negative result

`Gesetz` does **not** pair cleanly with `Weltplan`.

It is tempting to read them as the same kind of thing:

- Cassirer's law
- Junger's larger plan

But that pairing breaks under return to the sources.

Why it fails:

- Cassirer's `Gesetz` belongs to the constructive logic of exact phenomenal knowledge
- Junger's `Weltplan` crosses scientific, political, sacred-time, and emptiness registers
- `Weltplan` repeatedly requires an anti-reification test, whereas Cassirer's whole point is to keep law from hardening into substantial contents

So the collision works at the level of order-regime and its limit, not at the level of direct term-equivalence.

This is a useful failure.

## What this experiment does not license

- not “Cassirer already anticipates the time wall”
- not “`Weltplan` is just a neo-Kantian law concept in mythic language”
- not “Junger abandons positive order for pure threshold mysticism”
- not “the lawful and the fateful are now one cross-project distinction”

## Provisional Judgment

This second packet is better curated than the first and produces a stronger result.

What it now earns is:

- Cassirer and Junger both preserve a positive role for order
- but they place the limit of that order in fundamentally different places

That is a real cross-project clarification and is more precise than the output of Experiment 01.

It is still only a problem-structure result, not a framework claim.

## Method Lesson

The packet-design correction from Experiment 01 was right.

This run worked better because it:

- stayed closer to one scale
- sharpened the question
- dropped a term (`der Hinzutretende`) that had already produced a clean negative
- used direct local pressure surfaces instead of broad thematic inventory
