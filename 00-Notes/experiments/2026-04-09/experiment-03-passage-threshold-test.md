# Experiment 03: Passage Threshold Test

Purpose: test whether the result of `experiment-02-limit-of-orderable.md` survives contact with actual sentences rather than only glossary and term-pressure surfaces.

Status: living-layer pilot. This is an experiment note, not a canonical synthesis.

## Baseline To Beat

Experiment 02 yielded:

> Cassirer places the limit of the orderable where lawful cognition is either not yet secured or is reified back into substance; Junger places it where historical, scientific, and state-plan forms of order remain real but become provincial before a threshold field they cannot fully name, master, or contain.

This run should count as a gain only if passage-level contact sharpens that sentence rather than merely restating it.

## Question

At passage level, does the limit of order appear as:

- a methodological boundary
- a historical threshold
- or both

And more narrowly:

- does Cassirer's positive account of relation and law still hold up when set beside Junger's actual time-wall sentences
- or does the collision dissolve into atmosphere once whole sentences are brought in

## Packet

### Cassirer side

One compact local packet from `texts/erkenntnisproblem-vol1/parts/101-galilei-from-substance-to-function-and-phenomenal-knowledge.md`:

- the turn from `operari sequitur esse` to a general lawfulness of operation
- the shift from things to relations, from substance to function
- the Galileo restriction of knowledge to empirical marks rather than inner essence

Reason for choosing it:

- it keeps `Relation`, `Gesetz`, and exact science close together
- it states the positive methodological gain in one local stretch
- it makes Cassirer's limit internal to cognition rather than merely thematic

### Junger side

Two short threshold passages:

1. `texts/zeitmauer/parts/16-humane-einteilungen.md`, `§48`
   - future things outline themselves beyond the time wall
   - they are not yet historically or anthropologically fixed
   - in every naming danger slumbers

2. `texts/zeitmauer/parts/38-siderische-einteilungen.md`, `§114`
   - the same question is answered differently on this side and beyond the time wall
   - the issue concerns the standpoint or standing-ground of inquiry
   - geology, earth-history, and human plan interlock rather than staying in sealed domains

Reason for choosing them:

- `§48` gives the threshold in its historical and naming form
- `§114` gives the same threshold in methodological form
- together they test whether Junger's limit is only atmospheric or actually reorganizes inquiry

## Method

1. Keep the packet micro: one Cassirer local unit, two short Junger passages.
2. Ask only whether Experiment 02 survives sentence-level contact.
3. Require one constrained sentence and one informative negative.
4. Treat loss of clarity as an honest failure if the passages generate more mood than structure.

## Result

The Experiment 02 result survives passage-level contact and becomes slightly sharper.

The stronger sentence is:

> Cassirer places the limit of order where inquiry mistakes things or inner essence for its starting point instead of beginning from lawful relation and empirical marks; Junger places it where the same questions can no longer be contained by historical and disciplinary partitions and must be approached from another standing-ground at the time wall.

This is better than Experiment 02's sentence in one specific way:

- it shows Cassirer's limit as methodological from within the act of inquiry
- and Junger's limit as both historical and methodological, because the threshold changes not only what appears but from where and how it must be asked

## What the packet clarified

### 1. Cassirer's limit is enacted in the method itself

The selected Cassirer passage does not say merely that science values law and relation.

It says more precisely:

- the old thing-centered order starts from substances and classes
- Galileo begins instead from a general lawfulness of operation
- the deeper issue is whether inquiry starts with things or with relations
- knowledge becomes stricter when it gives up the claim to penetrate inner essence and remains with empirical marks that can ground further science

So the boundary here is methodological before it is thematic. Cassirer is not locating an outside to science. He is locating the internal discipline by which science becomes valid at all.

### 2. Junger's threshold is not only a future-image but a changed standpoint

`§48` gives the historical pressure:

- something is coming into being that cannot be accommodated within the historical
- the future outlines itself beyond the time wall
- naming that zone is dangerous

`§114` then prevents this from remaining pure mood:

- the same question is answered differently on this side and beyond the time wall
- inquiry itself has a standpoint or standing-ground
- geology, earth-history, and human plan overlap rather than remaining cleanly partitioned

So Junger's limit is not simply that order fails. It is that inherited jurisdictions of order no longer suffice, and the pressure shows up both in what becomes visible and in the footing of inquiry itself.

### 3. The contact remains structural rather than lexical

At passage level the bridge does not become “law meets fate.”

What survives is narrower:

- both texts refuse naive immediacy
- both texts insist that valid orientation depends on the right ordering stance
- both texts reject the idea that the first available categories exhaust the real

But they do this differently:

- Cassirer by tightening lawful cognition against substance and essence
- Junger by exposing a threshold where historical and disciplinary categories become insufficient

### 4. The methodological and the historical are not separable on the Junger side

This is the main gain over Experiment 02.

Experiment 02 already said Junger's limit was jurisdictional. The passages show that the jurisdictional limit is not merely social or historical. It also reorganizes inquiry itself. `§48` and `§114` belong together because the danger of naming and the shift in standpoint are two faces of the same threshold.

## Negative result

Cassirer's anti-scholastic movement from words to things does **not** pair cleanly with Junger's line that in every naming danger slumbers.

The lexical temptation is obvious:

- both seem dissatisfied with inherited language
- both seem to want a relation to the real that is not blocked by stale words

But the pairing breaks under return to the passages.

Why it fails:

- Cassirer does not warn that naming as such is dangerous; he argues that the anti-scholastic appeal to experience culminates in a reform of concept and logic
- Junger does not simply ask for better concepts; he says there are emerging phenomena whose premature naming is itself risky because they are not yet historically containable

So the collision is not “both critique language.” That would be a false simplification.

This is a useful failure.

## What this experiment does not license

- not “Cassirer already contains the time wall as a hidden methodological concept”
- not “Junger is just extending scientific method into a larger domain”
- not “both texts reject concepts in favor of direct experience”
- not “the anti-scholastic return to things and the danger of naming are the same move”

## Provisional Judgment

Experiment 03 is a success.

The passage-level test did not dissolve the earlier result into atmosphere. It confirmed that the collision is real, while also sharpening its limit:

- Cassirer's limit is internal to valid inquiry
- Junger's limit is the point where historical and disciplinary partitions cease to suffice and inquiry must proceed from another standing-ground

That is a genuine gain over the term-level packet.

It still remains a bounded problem-structure result.

## Method Lesson

The hand-curated checklist still holds.

This run worked because it:

- stayed very small
- used direct local passages rather than whole chapter summaries
- preserved the strongest negative from the lexical temptation
- tested the prior sentence instead of inventing a new thematic frame

So the higher-resolution packet index still has **not** earned its entrance.
