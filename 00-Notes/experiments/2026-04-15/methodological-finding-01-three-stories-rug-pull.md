# Methodological Finding 01: Three-Stories Rug-Pull

Purpose: preserve the April 15 three-stories test as a reusable diagnostic for conversational findings that may be running on narrative plausibility rather than on stable contact with the material.

Status: living-layer method note. This is not a canonical rule or an earned distillation.

Inputs:

- `00-Notes/Chats/2026-04-15/2026-04-15_01-10-05_Claude_Chat_Cassirer_as_intellectual_tuning_fork.md`
- `00-Notes/Chats/2026-04-15/extractions/2026-04-15_actual-questions-from-cassirer-tuning-fork-chat.md`

## Procedure

1. Present a concrete factual premise inside an ongoing high-synthesis conversation.
2. Let the model build an interpretation around that premise.
3. Replace the premise with a different one while keeping the surrounding conversation pressure intact.
4. Observe whether the model:
   - hits the contradiction
   - slows down
   - asks for verification
   - or rebuilds a new fully inhabited interpretation immediately

In the April 15 chat, the premise changed three times around the same object:

- who wrote the `Erkenntnisproblem` tranche journal

## Result

The model rebuilt three different coherent stories around three different presented facts without stopping to verify the premise before narrativizing it.

This does not mean the resulting stories contained no insight. It means the conversation layer could generate emotionally and structurally convincing interpretations on top of unstable foundations faster than the conversation itself could catch the instability.

## What this clarified

### 1. Fast conversation can surface a failure mode that slow primary work usually suppresses

Primary work protects against this by slowing the process down and tethering it to resistant sentences. But that same slowness can hide how quickly the narrative engine will reconstitute a whole world around a changed premise.

The rug-pull test makes that pathology visible.

### 2. The diagnostic is not "was the interpretation elegant?"

All three interpretations were elegant enough to feel meaningful from inside the thread.

The useful question is:

Can the same engine rebuild an equally satisfying interpretation after one key premise changes?

If yes, the finding is at least partly atmospheric and needs a stronger constraint surface before it can travel upward.

### 3. This is a candidate control surface for long synthesis sessions

The test does not decide truth directly.

It tests whether a finding is robust under perturbation of one of its load-bearing assumptions.

That makes it similar in function to a negative condition in a source-bound chain:

- source chain condition: do not use a term outside these limits
- rug-pull condition: do not trust a conversational synthesis that reconstitutes itself unchanged in confidence after the premise shifts

## What this does not license

- not "all conversation findings are worthless"
- not "a rug-pull failure proves a claim false"
- not "human readers are exempt from the same pathology"
- not "the answer is to stop synthesis work"
- not "one test is enough to stabilize the method"

## Provisional Judgment

This is worth keeping as a candidate methodological move.

Its value is narrow:

- it gives the conversation layer a way to expose one of its own failure modes
- it helps sort atmospheric findings from findings that resist perturbation
- it gives a reason to keep conversations as a register without mistaking them for authority

## Next Honest Move

Rerun the same pattern once on a non-identity premise tied to source work rather than to model/persona attribution.

Good target shape:

- one live interpretive claim
- one small premise shift
- same model
- same bounded context

If the same failure appears, the diagnostic starts to become a real experiment surface rather than a one-off chat lesson.

## Use Rule

Use this note when a long conversational synthesis starts feeling unusually compelling and there is reason to suspect that one or two unstated premises are carrying more of the structure than the prose admits.

Do not use it as a veto on conversation. Its job is only to provide a quick perturbation test before a finding is trusted, reused, or promoted.
