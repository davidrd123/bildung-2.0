# Experiment 02: Grothendieck patron-failure / Bogdanov contact

Purpose: test whether the bounded Grothendieck patron-failure /
non-transmission surface earns a real second Bogdanov contact, and if so,
which term actually clarifies the local difficulty without turning Bogdanov
into a worldview layer.

Status: living-layer packet result. This is a bounded result note, not a
source-opening decision or a cross-project framework claim.

## Question

Does Grothendieck's patron-failure / non-transmission diagnosis earn a bounded
Bogdanov contact, and if so, which contact actually fits?

## Packet

See `packet-02-grothendieck-patron-failure-bogdanov-contact.md`.

## Method

1. Read only the packet's cited Grothendieck and Bogdanov surfaces plus the
   minimum carry-forward notes they already named.
2. Ask for the most exact organizational difficulty the Grothendieck passages
   themselves generate, rather than importing a generic story about vision.
3. Force a discriminating choice among `deingression`, `law of leasts`, weaker
   analogy, or refusal.
4. Keep the comparison reversible by preserving what remains specifically
   Grothendieckian even if one Bogdanov term helps locally.

## Result

This packet yields a narrow second contact, but only at the level of the
post-departure breakup. The strongest bounded clarification is not that
Grothendieck's school \"lost the vision\" in some generic sense. It is that the
work had been organized around a center that posed the problems, supplied the
notions, and gave partial tasks their unity; once that center was gone, the
remaining work separated into detachable pieces.

**Fit sentence:** `deingression` fits Grothendieck's masonry-collapse image
because once the untransmitted organizing role disappears, the prior whole
breaks into detached tasks, carried-off tools, and practical separateness.

**Refusal sentence:** `law of leasts` does not fit this passage, because
Grothendieck is not diagnosing one weakest limiting link inside a still-held
organization; he is diagnosing the non-transmission of the very activity that
posed problems and gave the partial tasks their unity.

## What on the Grothendieck side generates the organizational difficulty

The source difficulty is more exact than \"loss of viewpoint.\"

In the self-critical `L'eleve posthume` note, Grothendieck says his former
students \"do not really know what mathematical creation is,\" and he locates
part of the responsibility in the fact that \"the problems were all posed\" by
him. He then sharpens the point: he had sought collaborators to develop
intuitions and ideas already formed in him, rather than requiring them to find
the problems and notions themselves.

The later patron-collapse passage shows the organizational consequence of that
arrangement. After his departure, the \"workers\" leave with the small things
they can use, while \"the essential had disappeared\": the unity that gave sense
to partial tasks, and even a certain warmth. So the difficulty is:

- partial tasks and tools were transmissible
- the generative center that posed and unified them was not
- the organization therefore reproduced usage of results better than the
  capacity for creation

That is the local problem Bogdanov is being asked to clarify.

## Which Bogdanov term, if any, actually clarifies it

`Deingression` is the best fit, but only narrowly.

The glossary note makes `deingression` the disorganizing counter-mechanism to
`ingression`, the mechanism that yields separateness and crisis when a prior
complex no longer holds. That matches the Grothendieck passage where the
previous whole is no longer operative and the remaining actors keep only local
bricoles and detached tasks.

What it clarifies is the shape of the breakup:

- one prior whole
- loss of the relation that held its parts together
- resulting separateness of tasks that continue only in scattered form

`Law of leasts` is weaker here because the passage does not describe a whole
bounded by its most lagging branch. It describes a whole whose organizing role
was never successfully transmitted beyond the patron-centered arrangement.

`Chain connection` is not needed for the result. The source pressure is not
mainly about reciprocal branching conditions; it is about dependence on a
non-reproduced center.

## What remains unclarified on the Grothendieck side

Bogdanov does not capture the specifically Grothendieckian pressure around
creation, pedagogy, and responsibility.

- He does not explain what it means to \"know what mathematical creation is.\"
- He does not explain how problem-posing and notion-forging are or are not
  taught.
- He does not capture the difference between using tools from a program and
  inhabiting the generative `point de vue` that made the program possible.
- He does not capture the note's ethical reflexivity: Grothendieck's claim that
  he himself may have helped produce the failure.
- He does not capture the source's extra register of \"warmth,\" which is not
  merely organizational glue.

So Bogdanov clarifies the disaggregation that follows the failure. He does not
exhaust the failure itself.

## Provisional Judgment

This is a real second contact, but a narrow one.

It holds if the claim is kept this small:

> Grothendieck's patron-failure passage earns `deingression` as a name for the
> post-departure disaggregation of a work-whole whose generative center was not
> successfully transmitted.

It does **not** hold as a full explanation of Grothendieck's diagnosis. If the
packet is asked to show that Bogdanov explains the failure of creative
apprenticeship itself, rather than the organizational breakup that followed,
the packet fails and should be treated as a refusal rather than a second
contact.

## What this does not license

- not promotion of Grothendieck out of `sources/modern/incoming/`
- not a generalized burial thesis for twentieth-century mathematics
- not rereading Grothendieck primarily through Bogdanov
- not treating the patron simply as a \"weak link\" or the school simply as a
  bottlenecked chain
- not importing `deingression` into `point de vue / vision` as though the
  creative problem were now solved
- not using this result as authorization for a broader Bogdanov-first
  framework
