# Collision 03: competing bands — time crisis vs threshold and naming

Purpose: first direct comparison between `collision-bundle-01-time-crisis-orthogonal-time.md` and `collision-bundle-02-threshold-and-naming.md`.

Status: working-layer comparison note. Not a replay packet. Not authority for retiring either band. Not yet a fresh-reader competition run.

## Question

If both bundles are treated as candidates for the same Dick / Jünger contact zone, which one actually explains the pressure better?

More narrowly:

- which band is better for the first break in the shared material
- where does each band stop helping
- do they truly compete, or do they take over at different moments

## Materials compared

### Bundle 01

- Jünger `§47-48`
- Dick `Folder 21 counterfeit time / receptive pattern`
- Dick `Folder 90 Spinner's blindness / de-occlusion`

Current center:

- `boundary under pressure`
- `access under pressure`
- `reality persists, but the inherited way of meeting it fails`

### Bundle 02

- Jünger `§51 + §54`
- Dick `Folder 83 exegesis as memory-work`
- Dick `Folder 1 splintered truth`
- support from `Folder 88` in the bundle itself

Current center:

- typology under dangerous naming
- memory-work under splintered truth
- holding operations under lagging names

## Result

The bands do not finally compete for one slot.

They separate by **phase of pressure**.

If the question is:

- what first breaks
- how the old frame stops fitting
- how reality can remain while access to it fails

then bundle 01 is the stronger band.

If the question is:

- what to do once inherited naming has started to misfire
- how not to lose the event while no final name is trustworthy
- how a record survives by reranking, fragment-preservation, or typology

then bundle 02 is the stronger band.

So the comparison yields a sequence:

1. **Bundle 01 for first-order failure**
   - frame-failure
   - counterfeit duration
   - de-occlusion
   - damaged access

2. **Bundle 02 for second-order holding**
   - dangerous naming
   - symptom before meaning
   - retention without closure
   - reranking without deletion

## What this clarifies on the Jünger side

`§47-48` belong more naturally to bundle 01 when the issue is the break in historical fit itself:

- more and more events do not fit the historical frame
- inherited political-historical words grow deceptive
- the future outlines itself beyond the time wall

Those are first-order crisis markers.

`§51-54` belong more naturally to bundle 02 when the issue becomes what thought can honestly do under that pressure:

- future things are visible before adequate naming
- names retain conjuring force and therefore danger
- facts indicate change rather than cause it
- old compartments fail, but the pressure points toward eventual system-formation

So bundle 02 is not the better bundle for Jünger in general. It is the better bundle once the crisis has already been entered.

## What this clarifies on the Dick side

Folder 21 and Folder 90 belong more naturally to bundle 01:

- fake elapsed time
- look-differents
- perceptual occlusion
- the blockage lifted from us rather than from the world

These are first-order access failures.

Folder 83, Folder 1, and Folder 88 belong more naturally to bundle 02:

- repetition as preservation rather than failed construction
- truth as splintered and recollectable piecemeal
- hierarchy and reranking instead of simple recantation

These are second-order archival responses to a disclosure that outstrips the available names.

So here too the bands separate by phase:

- bundle 01 asks how access failed
- bundle 02 asks how the archive behaves once that failure cannot be honestly closed

## Surprise

The useful surprise is that the competition does not produce one preferred band.

It produces a **handoff point**.

Bundle 01 wins when the material is still best read as crisis in meeting reality.

Bundle 02 wins when the material is best read as discipline under naming lag.

That is more useful for the locator than a single winner would have been.

It means later agents should not ask only:

- which band fits this material

but also:

- at what phase is this material being entered

## What resists flattening

1. Bundle 01 cannot do bundle 02's work.

It can diagnose frame-failure and de-occlusion, but it does not explain repetition as retention or reranking as an honest archival move.

2. Bundle 02 cannot do bundle 01's work.

It can describe naming pressure and delay, but it loses the sharper account of counterfeit duration, damaged access, and perceptual correction.

3. The bands are not just two vocabularies for the same contact.

They are keyed to different layers of the same broader problem.

## Provisional judgment

This is the first comparison run that feels closer to the actual middle-zone task.

Not because it widened the theory.

Because it tested the locating infrastructure itself.

What it found is:

> bundle 01 is the better entry band; bundle 02 is the better follow-on band once naming and reranking become the live pressure.

That is a real operational result.

## What this does not license

- not deletion or demotion of either bundle
- not a single master bundle for Dick / Jünger
- not a fixed sequence for every future run
- not the claim that all cross-project work should now be staged this way

## Next honest move

Run the same competition shape with a fresh reader and no preferred sequence.

Ask only:

- which bundle better explains the material first
- where the other bundle begins to take over
- whether the handoff point survives independent reading

If a fresh reader reaches the same staged result, then the locator has started doing real work beyond note-writing.
