# Passage-encounter bench as candidate method move

Status: candidate method note. Not yet pressure-tested beyond one demonstration encounter (`atelier/encounters/encounter-2026-04-19-baratynsky-patmos-ruins-as-signs.md`). Not authority for literary-philological practice in general.

Purpose: name a bounded move for text-first philological work that the 2026-04-19 chat articulated clearly enough to be worth testing, while keeping it below default-method status until two or three real repo trials earn it.

## Problem it sharpens

The repo's existing apparatus is very good at theoretical-passage pressure (Uexküll, Cassirer, Jünger, Grothendieck) — glossary discipline, term-evidence chains, stereoscopic translation strain-reports. It is less well-exercised on **literary primary texts** — poems, narrative passages, dramatic speeches — where the text's work is often *to resist literalization* rather than to articulate claims.

Theoretical passages are pressured by asking whether their claims hold. Literary passages are doing something structurally different: holding open a compression, a rhythm, an address-form that any paraphrase closes. Applying theoretical-passage tools to literary passages tends to produce displacement — the apparatus generates readings that the text was specifically refusing.

The question is whether a dedicated low-apparatus move can work directly on literary passages while keeping openness as the operative value.

## Candidate move

Place two or three short primary passages side-by-side in original language, each with literal rendering, under a minimal field note that describes what is alive in the adjacency **without forcing synthesis**. The note stays open. Recruitment (whether other passages join the family) is the signal of whether the adjacency is doing real work.

Output is not an argument. Output is the **adjacency-with-field-note**. Over time, the bench accumulates encounters; named-passage handles get earned by recruitment; the bench becomes an exemplar library in Kuhn's sense for a philological paradigm the repo is trying to cultivate.

Key structural commitments:

- **Text-first.** Original-language first, literal-rendering second, translation-for-reading third (optional). Not paraphrase-first.
- **Minimal connective prose.** One or two paragraphs of field note, describing what is alive without closing it.
- **No synthesis section.** The encounter can stay unresolved indefinitely.
- **Tentative relations only.** Echoes to existing repo handles allowed but not required; no forced classification.
- **Recruitment is the signal.** If a third or fourth passage joins, the bench is working. If the pair stays alone for months, that is also fine and worth noticing.

## What it is for

At best, the move can:

- Preserve the figurative-apprehension phase of philological thinking (per Langer, PNK preface — fantastic inventions as the necessary first mark of any theoretical structure)
- Train the I–I autocommunicative channel for recruitment without trying to force the tertiary associative layer (per Lotman, *Внутри мыслящих миров* I.2)
- Provide daily confrontation with formal resistance in the Bessis sense — cultivating the underlying associative capacity through sustained, small, repeated engagement with real primary material
- Build a Kuhnian exemplar set that could transmit the practice to future readers (including future repo sessions) without requiring explicit rule-statement
- Preserve adjacencies that may not yet be justifiable as arguments but are real enough to deposit

## What it is not for

- Not a replacement for close reading
- Not a substitute for source-language competence
- Not a place to deposit every passage that catches the eye — only passages that have earned at least one note's worth of adjacent-thought
- Not argumentative apparatus; if the encounter is trying to prove something, it is doing pairing-work, not encounter-work, and should move to `pairings/`
- Not authorized to replace glossary evidence-chains, translation notes, or term-evidence handles
- Not a default move on every difficult passage

## Current constraints

The move is only honest if all of the following are kept explicit:

1. Passages are in original language with literal rendering. A translation-only encounter is not an encounter; it is a literary chat.
2. At least one of the passages must be meaningfully bounded — located in a known work, with attribution sufficient to return to it.
3. The field note must be brief (one or two short paragraphs), and must not perform synthesis. If synthesis is demanded by the material, the material is asking for pairing-work instead.
4. Tentative repo relations (`echoes`, `translates`, `pressures`) are allowed; `grounds` or `crystallizes` are not appropriate at encounter-level.
5. Passage citation must be sufficient for later source scaffolding. Inline passage text is a placeholder; the eventual goal is transclude-from-scaffolded-source.

## Good first test

The first test is the Baratynsky / Hölderlin encounter already on disk: `00-Notes/atelier/encounters/encounter-2026-04-19-baratynsky-patmos-ruins-as-signs.md`.

It is a good first test because:

- Both passages are short and self-contained
- The adjacency was produced under real concept-pressure (the gottferne / symbolic-regime-transition thread) rather than manufactured for the test
- The passages are in two different languages with neither being the reader's native
- The "inverse readings of ruin" relation is legible enough to be a real observation but loose enough not to be a thesis
- Both passages could plausibly recruit other family-members (Jünger `schachtgaenge`, Celan's post-catastrophic address-work, Mandelstam on Petersburg), giving the bench a real recruitment-test over subsequent months

The second test would be a pair chosen in a different key — ideally where one of the passages already has repo-side source material (so that transclude can be tested rather than inline citation).

## Failure modes

- **Essay-drift.** If field notes grow beyond two short paragraphs, the bench is silting up into essay-work. Cut or migrate to pairing.
- **Forced pairs.** If every passage the reader encounters gets benched with something, the bench is performing coverage rather than recruitment. Only bench when the adjacency is actually pressing.
- **Translation-creep.** If encounters are increasingly in English-only, the original-language discipline is eroding and the bench is becoming a commonplace-book. Hold the discipline.
- **Apparatus-import.** If field notes start citing theoretical frameworks to explain the adjacency, the bench is regressing to argument-first work. The theory goes in pairings or method notes; the bench stays text-first.
- **Infinite openness.** If encounters accumulate indefinitely without any earning named-passage handles or being recruited by second encounters, the bench may be generating atmosphere rather than tracking real philological pressure. After six months, review.

## What would make it earn more

- Two further trials, each in a different key (say: three-passage cluster with one shared author; cross-language encounter where transclude is possible because a source exists; explicit failure-case encounter that does not recruit and is honestly documented as such)
- Evidence that at least one encounter has contributed to source-near working material elsewhere in the repo (a close reading, a glossary entry, a term-evidence handle)
- A negative condition for future use: a case where encounter-work was *not* appropriate and a different move served better

## Relation to existing practice

This move does not import foreign method. It names and structures something the atelier already authorizes as a form of curator-energy:

- `sparks/` already permits live-before-justifiable notes
- `pairings/` already works on structural resonance between works or thinkers
- The handle schema's named-passage mechanism already anticipates passage-level recruitment signals
- Stereoscopic/parallax translation (see `stereoscopic-parallax-translation-candidate.md`) is a within-encounter move that can be deployed on a passage inside an encounter

What is new is the *unit* — a bounded passage-adjacency with its own file, its own discipline, its own recruitment-based promotion criterion — and the explicit license for **text-first, minimal-apparatus, openness-preserving work** as distinct from the theoretical-apparatus work the repo is otherwise very good at.

## Relation to the caesura-technology concept

See `atelier/sparks/spark-2026-04-19-caesura-technologies.md`. The passage-encounter bench is a candidate caesura technology in that spark's terms — it is structurally designed to install small bifurcation-moments into philological practice (per Lotman + Hölderlin) at a sustainable daily rate. If the caesura-technology concept earns further exercise, this method-candidate is one of its worked instances.

## Current judgment

This is strong enough to keep in `method/` as a candidate because:

1. One demonstration encounter exists and is honest about what it is testing
2. The bucket exists (`atelier/encounters/`) at seed status with a README explaining its place
3. The theoretical scaffolding (Langer, Bessis, Peirce, Lotman, Hölderlin) is independently documented in the Langer-Bessis-Peirce pairing and the caesura-technology spark

It is **not** strong enough yet to count as a standing repo method.

The next honest step is:

- Run a second encounter when a real question and a real adjacency present themselves (not manufactured)
- Attempt a third encounter only if the first two are doing real work
- After three encounters, decide whether the move promotes to `tentative` method status, stays `candidate`, or is retired as not-earning

## Open questions

- How does the bench interact with generated-from-chat sessions? (This is the first session to deposit encounter-material; the discipline for future chat-generated encounters is not yet clear.)
- What is the right relation between encounter-notes and conversation source-chats they may originate in? (Current practice: link conversation-chat in frontmatter, but chat is not source-of-authority; the source is the primary text itself.)
- When a handled literary source exists, does the encounter get re-written with transclude references, or does the inline version stay as a time-stamped snapshot? (Current leaning: rewrite with transclude, because the source of authority is the scaffolded text; the encounter is a pointer-assembly.)

None of these block first use. They should be tracked and revisited after the second encounter is on disk.
