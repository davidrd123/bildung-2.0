# Encounter: Blackiston et al. on memory through metamorphosis as documented middle term between Lotman's structural bifurcation and Mooney's karmic-disposition hypothesis

A third passage enters the Lotman-Mooney family: a 2008 experimental paper showing that associative memories trained in caterpillars survive metamorphosis into adult moths. This is a documented empirical instance of the structural operator the prior two passages describe at semiospheric and metaphysical scales. Its arrival as a third family-member is the first real recruitment-signal the bench has produced.

**Mooney, *Reincarnation for Everyone* (forthcoming, *Mind*), p. 11** (already on the bench; see sister encounter):

> The karmic disposition in question is a disposition to preserve whichever first-person perspective is already supported by the body in which it resides.

**Blackiston, Silva Casey, and Weiss, *PLoS ONE* 2008 (from abstract and results):**

> Fifth instar *Manduca sexta* caterpillars received an electrical shock associatively paired with a specific odor in order to create a conditioned odor aversion, and were assayed for learning in a Y choice apparatus as larvae and again as adult moths. We show that larvae learned to avoid the training odor, and that this aversion was still present in the adults. The adult aversion did not result from carryover of chemicals from the larval environment. [...] The present study, the first to demonstrate conclusively that associative memory survives metamorphosis in Lepidoptera, provokes intriguing new questions about the organization and persistence of the central nervous system during metamorphosis.

And the developmental-window finding:

> Larvae trained at third instar still showed odor aversion after two molts, as fifth instars, but did not avoid the odor as adults, consistent with the idea that post-metamorphic recall involves regions of the brain that are not produced until later in larval development.

Full citation: Blackiston DJ, Silva Casey E, Weiss MR (2008) Retention of Memory through Metamorphosis: Can a Moth Remember What It Learned As a Caterpillar? PLoS ONE 3(3): e1736. https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0001736

Relevant follow-up: Blackiston, Shomrat, Levin (2015) "The stability of memories during brain remodeling: a perspective," *Communicative & Integrative Biology* 8(5): e1073424. This is the Michael Levin lab, which has continued the theoretical work on memory-preservation through substrate reorganization.

## Field note (2026-04-19)

The moth paper supplies what the first two encounters pointed at structurally but could not demonstrate empirically. Lotman gives the semiospheric-scale bifurcation mechanism; Mooney gives the hypothesized karmic-disposition-as-preserver at the ontogenetic scale of biological death; Blackiston et al. document the preserver operating at the ontogenetic scale of metamorphosis, where histolysis physically dissolves much of the caterpillar body and rebuilds it as the moth.

The mechanism in the moth case is not karma and not formal bifurcation; it is concrete neural substrate. Surviving mushroom body neurons and their synaptic connections carry the conditioned odor-aversion across the pupal reorganization. The biology is specific and observable. But the *structural form* of what is happening matches what Lotman and Mooney describe: a radical substrate-reorganization across which certain prior determinations are preserved by a mechanism built into the substrate's design.

The developmental-window finding is the philologically richest part. Third-instar learning does not survive metamorphosis; fifth-instar learning does. The difference is that by fifth instar, the neural substrate that will persist through pupation has been built. Memories laid down in substrate that will not persist do not carry forward; memories laid down in substrate that will persist do. **There is a developmentally-gated window within which the substrate capable of carrying information through bifurcation becomes available.** This is not a metaphor for Hui's *generatio aequivoca* — it is a biological instance of what *generatio aequivoca* names at its most literal. The passage from heteronomy to autonomy is mediated by the specific emergence of substrate-types capable of bearing retrospective-determination forward.

Three recognitions converge on the bench:

The operator the prior encounter named as structurally homologous between Lotman and Mooney is, at minimum, **biologically precedented**. Nature has evolved substrate-preservation mechanisms that carry cognitive content through radical bodily reorganization. The Abhidharma hypothesis that analogous mechanisms operate across biological death is a speculative extension of a real class of phenomena, not a free-standing metaphysical conjecture.

Mooney's argument-from-conceivability for karmic dispositions (p. 6 of his paper) can be considerably strengthened by adding an argument-from-biological-precedent. The moth is an existence-proof that substrate-preserving mechanisms are not metaphysically strange — they are observed, documented, species-specific. The question becomes whether the moth-mechanism has larger-scale analogues, which is an empirical question rather than a purely metaphysical one.

Hui's page on generatio aequivoca, re-read through this encounter, turns out to be asking a question developmental biology is actually positioned to answer in specific cases. The organic is not one thing; it is the class of substrates that builds substrate-preservation mechanisms of varying scope, within developmentally-gated windows. Lotman's semiospheric explosion is the scaled-up version of the moth's mushroom-body persistence through pupation: both are retrospective-determination operating across substrate-reorganization, at different scales and via different mechanisms.
