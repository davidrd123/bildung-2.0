# Encounters

Status: proposed new atelier bucket, seed status. The existing starter buckets (`sparks/`, `ghost-books/`, `pairings/`) are canonical. This one is additional and should stay at seed until it either earns its keep through repeated use or gets folded back into `pairings/`.

## Purpose

A place for **passage-to-passage textual encounters** that are text-first rather than argument-first. An encounter note holds two or more short literary/primary passages next to each other with minimal connective prose, and permits the adjacency itself to do the thinking.

Different from `pairings/`: pairings argue for structural resonance between whole works or thinkers. Encounters hold specific passages side-by-side without requiring that argument, and may stay productive even when no synthesis emerges.

## Relation to handle schema and transclusion

Long-term: encounter notes should be constituted by transclude-style references to handled source passages, not by copying text. When literary source scaffolds exist under `sources/{lang}/`, named passages become the unit of recruitment (per `patterns/handle-schema.md`), and encounters assemble by pointer.

Near-term (before literary scaffolds exist): passages may be inlined with full citation. The inline version is a placeholder that should be swapped for transclude references once the source is scaffolded.

## Discipline

- One question or charge per encounter. More than one and it dissolves into thematic essay.
- Passages in original language first. Literal rendering second. Translation-for-reading third (optional).
- Brief field note. The note describes what is alive in the adjacency without forcing synthesis.
- Tentative relations to existing repo handles if any echo, but no forcing.
- No synthesis section. An encounter can stay open indefinitely.

## Promotion

An encounter graduates out of atelier only if:

- The same passage-pair is recruited by three or more independent encounters (evidence the pair is doing real work across contexts), OR
- The encounter directly informs source-near working material (a close reading, a glossary entry, a method note)

Otherwise it stays here.

## Why this bucket rather than `pairings/`

Pairings stand on structural-theoretical claims about whole thinkers or works. Encounters are lower-resolution and less argumentative. A pairing can grow *from* an encounter if the adjacency keeps pressing, but not every encounter should be pressured into pairing-shape. The additional bucket exists to protect the lower-resolution form.

## Naming

- `encounter-YYYY-MM-DD-short-name.md`
