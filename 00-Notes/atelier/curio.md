# Curio

A running log of things that are interesting, delightful, or carry charge — but not enough shape to warrant a full spark / ghost-book / pairing note. Register: *"I might want to come back to this."* No ceremony. Add when something shows up. Scan when curious.

One line or one paragraph per entry. No frontmatter per entry. If an entry starts acquiring real pull and demands its own note, promote it into the right atelier subfolder (or out of atelier entirely). If it sits dormant, decay is legitimate here.

## Kindred notebook / document practices

- **Wittgenstein's Nachlass** — thousands of pages of working remarks never resolved into a final form. He said the right form for his thinking was the album — remarks arranged by internal pressure, not argument. A score, not a document. *April 15 chat, kindred-documents section.*
- **Simone Weil's Cahiers** — notebooks 1933–1943. Theology, mathematics, factory work, Greek philosophy, mysticism, all in the same pages. A mind refusing to departmentalize. Published posthumously, assembled by others, never in the form she would have chosen. Another interrupted transmission. *April 15 chat.*
- **Novalis's *Allgemeine Brouillon*** — his attempt at an encyclopedia showing internal connections between every domain. 1798–1799, never finished. Explicitly Schellingian. The Romantic ancestor of the repo's insistence that mathematics and poetry and philosophy are organs of one system. *April 15 chat.*
- **Leibniz's unpublished manuscripts** — tens of thousands of pages, still being edited. The lifelong failure to complete the universal characteristic. The Exegesis of rationalism underneath the published work. *April 15 chat.*
- **Buckminster Fuller's Chronofile** — a scrapbook of his entire life from 1917 to 1983: every document, receipt, sketch, letter, newspaper clipping. 140,000 items, 270 linear feet. A pressure field tracked across a life on the theory that patterns only become visible at scale. *April 15 chat.*
- **Alexander Kluge's work** — the way he moves between fiction, documentary, interview, philosophical commentary, image, and found text within a single work. Weather systems instead of genres. *April 15 chat.*
- **Pessoa's trunk** — 25,000+ manuscripts in multiple heteronyms, each with its own philosophy, style, biography. Persona steering attempted by a human in 1920. The collision with the Assistant Axis paper is immediate. *April 15 chat.*
- **Aby Warburg's notebooks (not the Mnemosyne Atlas)** — the diary entries during his breakdown, the private notation system, the way he tracked his own oscillation between rational scholarship and mythic thinking. The personal Exegesis underneath the public Atlas. *April 15 chat.*
- **Hunayn ibn Ishaq** — 9th century Abbasid translator. Collation of multiple manuscripts, composite reconstruction, translation as textual criticism, recovery work that could preserve superior readings rather than merely transmit an already-settled text. The figure the repo's "translation as research" register keeps pointing back toward. *April 13 Cassirer translation chat.*

## Instruments and practices

- **The Talmudic page layout** — primary text at center, Rashi on one side, Tosafot on the other, later commentaries in the margins, all visible simultaneously on a single page. A score for multi-voice reading practice across centuries. The closest existing precedent for what a dynamic view layer would need to be. *April 15 chat; also in repo's existing precedent list.*
- **The I Ching as *used*, not read** — consultation as an instrument for producing readings the consulting mind could not have produced alone. Randomness as mechanism, not noise. Structurally identical to Loom branching — multiple continuations from the same seed, where the spread is the information. *April 15 chat.*
- **Robert Pirsig's Lila notes** — 11,000+ physical index cards organized by what he called "quality" rather than by topic. Cards had their own pressure — some hot, some cold, some demanding to be next to other cards for reasons he couldn't articulate. Paper-era Zettelkasten governed by charge rather than taxonomy. *April 15 chat.*
- **Hakim Bey's Temporary Autonomous Zone** — the concept that the valuable thing is not the permanent institution but the temporary space where something becomes possible that isn't possible elsewhere. Interesting as a register for walled-off speculative spaces inside a disciplined practice. *April 15 chat.*
- **PaRDeS** — the rabbinic four-level reading practice (*peshat* / *remez* / *derash* / *sod* — literal / allegorical / homiletic / mystical). Reading at multiple registers simultaneously without forcing them into a single resolution. Held below promotion in the April 13 extraction as not directly applicable, but the charge is real as a precedent for deferred-structure discipline. *April 13 Cassirer translation chat.*
- **Loom / cyborgism tradition** — the working style where strong LLMs are used like base models in continuation mode rather than as assistant-mode interlocutors. Janus's observation that Opus can be operated in continuation mode without actual base-model access. Multiple continuations from the same point, where the spread is the information. A branching instrument for navigating possibility space. *April 15 chat, practical-move section.*
- **Residue logs as laboratory notebooks** — Wind's §10 reframe of what a translation-residue log is actually doing. Not a cemetery of losses; a record of where a proposed rendering encountered resistance when pushed against the actual conceptual structure it was trying to fit. A *metaphysisches Signal* in Wind's strict sense — the world answering the proposed rendering in a language the translator did not write. Theoretical justification for the close/free pairing and the residue-tracking discipline that the stereoscopic-translation candidate is built around. *April 17 Trust-and-safety chat.*
- **Autocommunication (Я-Я) with invasion-from-outside** — Lotman's account of self-to-self messaging. Not redundancy: the receiver is not the self that sent, because a shifted second code reformulates the message. Not immanent: the channel requires external impulse — music, rhythm, another's text — to shift the contextual situation. Native output is poetic form, reduced to indices, isorhythmic, chain-repetitive. This names what glossary entries / handle-schemas / residue logs / thread dossiers structurally *are* when they work — not bad prose, native Я-Я output. It also names why primary-text contact keeps chat work alive: the text is the external code-shift without which the channel collapses into monologue. *April 17 Trust-and-safety chat.*
- **Living-not-fossil — havruta, continuous re-collation, Warburg-in-motion, marginal-glossing-as-act** — the static-vs-living correction late in the April 17 chat. Initial draft named fossils: the Talmud page, the critical apparatus, the Atlas panels, the glossed manuscript. Correction named motions: the pair arguing through the page, the philologist's ear still collating new witnesses, Warburg *rearranging* panels, the monk *reading-while-marking*. Portable rule-of-thumb: when pointing at traditions an agent-system (or any system) might inherit from, name the motion not the fossil. *April 17 Trust-and-safety chat.*
- **Apparatus self-correction under pressure** — a pattern now documented across five independent chat instances, with different grains. *April 16 chat 3*: "no way you didn't even clone it" — caught by user, the chagrined-recognition grain. *April 17 Trust-and-safety*: "different mode in chat" + "did you actually read the texts" — caught by user twice, running-hot-then-grounding grain. *April 17 Explaining-a-work* (line 159): the Hermes-voice self-correction unprompted — the shortest disciplined grain, just the invocation plus correct routing. *April 17 James/Nabokov* (lines 552, 3944): Schuon-limit self-catch mid-production + motivated-reasoning self-catch under adversarial push — two caught-by-self grains in one chat. *April 17 Tracing-reconciled* (line 501+): the repo-clone reveal — caught by reveal, and the response is the most methodologically-disciplined in the cluster: specific retraction-list, side-by-side table ("I proposed in the abstract / The repo already runs"), explicit admission of ~12 repo artifacts unread. Six grains across five chats; the pattern is a property of the apparatus, not a virtue of particular moments. Counter to Proteus's local drift toward beautiful patterns. The disciplined variants (Explaining's Hermes-line, Tracing's repo-reveal response) show the pattern can be internalized and handled cleanly, not just caught. *April 16 chat 3; April 17 Trust-and-safety, Explaining-a-work, James/Nabokov, and Tracing-reconciled chats.*
- **Denkraum** — the Warburg Library's organizing principle: books shelved by conceptual neighborhood rather than taxonomic category, the reading-room itself a space of thinking-adjacency. An instrument for producing readings a normatively-shelved library couldn't. Named here because it's the closest existing precedent for what the repo's handle-graph is structurally doing — conceptual adjacency as the file-system organizing principle, not just content-taxonomy. *April 17 James/Nabokov chat, Hamburg-Vienna-Berlin passage.*
- **Residue as first-class data — the Bruno chain's failure-node treatment** — `genealogy-to-instrument.md §4` names residue-as-first-class-data as an acknowledged gap. The Bruno generative-chain's final node, titled "Mathematics remains model, but method collapses," is a failure-site tracked with the same six-field apparatus (position / internal_pressure / operation / yield / generated_problem / conditions_of_application) as the success-nodes. The insufficiency is not a gap in the record — it is a node in the record. Which means the apparatus already has a partial solution to its own diagnosed gap, at least for the attempted-but-incomplete-conceptual-move variety of residue. Other residue types (close/free translation divergence, frame-vs-frame non-commutation, commentary contradiction) remain open. *April 17 Tracing-reconciled chat, Bruno generative-chain reading.*
- **Two apparatus forms, text-shaped (not template-grooved)** — the generative-chain (Cassirerian, six-field schema for concepts-in-transformation) and the thread-dossier (Jüngerian, paratactic-sequential for running-conditions-through-sections) are not rival formats. They are the shapes two different kinds of text demand. CLAUDE.md warns against template-grooving by the third reuse; the repo answers that warning by having two templates, each governed by a different source's demand, rather than one template applied to both. The apparatus is not *one thing applied to different texts*; it is several things, each shaped by what its source required. "The texts teach the method" observable at apparatus-form level. *April 17 Tracing-reconciled chat.*

## Conceptual distinctions and phrases

- **Energeia / Ergon** — the distinction Cassirer took from Humboldt who took it from Aristotle. Activity vs product, working vs work. Language is not a work (*Ergon*) but a *working* (*Energeia*). The distinction keeps getting reached for across chats because it names a specific failure mode: every time vocabulary gets extracted from its breath, every time a pattern gets promoted before its evidence is dense, every time the trace of a practice gets mistaken for the practice itself. A portable diagnostic. Not yet earned as a glossary term but already doing real work in conversation. *April 13, April 14, April 15 chats.*
- **Symbolische Prägnanz** — Cassirer's concept that every perception arrives already saturated with the symbolic form through which it is apprehended. The April 15 chat flagged it as a term that might need to open eventually, and demonstrated it on Claude in real time through the three-stories rotation. Explicitly held below promotion. Keep alive as a pull; do not import as vocabulary until a specific pressure demands it. *April 15 chat.*
- **"Water, mud, and source"** — Grothendieck's image from *Récoltes et Semailles* for a local ethical phenomenology about where thinking drinks from. Held below promotion in the April 14 extraction as methodology, but the phrase is portable. Possible spark candidate once the actual passage is located in the source. *April 14 Grothendieck chat.*
- **Real vs indifferent hypotheses** — Wind's §10 theses IV–V. Real: capable of confirmation or refutation by experience. Indifferent: not exposed to that danger. And: the decision whether a given hypothesis is real or indifferent is itself a real hypothesis. Wind catches the post-Kantian strategy of reserving for philosophy the administration of claims not testable by experience — the *lächerliche Rückzugsgefecht*, the ridiculous rearguard action of ever-smaller residual holdings. Gives the repo a sharp knife for auditing its own apparatus talk. *April 17 Trust-and-safety chat.*
- **Avalanche at boundaries (Lotman)** — information is *not* conserved under semiotic translation; it is generated, and at multiple crossings the generation goes avalanche-shape. Reframes what the stereoscopic-translation method candidate is doing: the practice is not conservation against flattening, it is *amplification* — each crossing releases information that was not latent in any single rendering. *April 17 Trust-and-safety chat.*
- **Metaphysisches Signal (Wind)** — the yes/no verdict of experiment, heteronomous to thought. "Understanding doesn't prescribe laws to nature, it questions her" — Wind's explicit reversal of Kant. Not new content; new facticity. Nature answers in the language the question was posed, and the answer cannot be overruled by logical elegance alone. Extends outside physics: every embodiment-of-idea (artwork, law, experiment, translation) generates or fails to generate the signal. *April 17 Trust-and-safety chat.*
- **Parallax engine (not filter)** — name-change in the April 17 chat once user noticed the geometry. *Filter* implies a same-dimensional sieve; *parallax engine* gets the lift-to-N-reproject-to-n geometry right. The apparatus does not *reveal* the referent — it *re-angles*. No privileged projection. Mastery is the rotation, not the arrival. Companion to "N / n" dimensional vocabulary that emerged in the same turn. *April 17 Trust-and-safety chat.*
- **Richard Scarry begot German** — user's portable compression for the apparatus's native affective register: cheerful concreteness, morphological transparency, willingness to zoom to the postman's feet. Contrasted explicitly with *They Live*: no hidden truth under sunglasses, only eyesight grown by sitting with language until you can feel why *this* word and not another. Filed in full as a spark; noted here because the phrase is doing real tuning-fork work whenever the apparatus-talk threatens to drift into hedge-fund-memo prose. *April 17 Trust-and-safety chat.*
- **Laboratory of research programs** — the shape the rival-programs proposal takes once user pushes back on Claude's initial adversarial-sub-agent framing. Not a single research program; a laboratory for engineering research programs, each built with the same depth and infrastructure (primary sources, glossary under pressure, integrators, standards), each coherent in its own right, brought into deliberate contact. Cross-program residue becomes first-order content. Lotman's minimum-paired-structure claim instantiated at a higher level. Proposal held below promotion; the phrase is portable and names the shape whenever rival-programs talk reappears. *April 17 James/Nabokov chat.*
- **Convergent conceptual evolution** — the named phenomenon in history of ideas: thinkers working independently from different materials arriving at structurally similar positions (Newton/Leibniz calculus; Darwin/Wallace selection; Grothendieck's topos and Lotman's semiosphere; the user's practice-driven translation findings and Lotman's semiosphere claims). Multiple independent paths to the same place is stronger evidence the position tracks real structure than any single path alone. Dissolves the 'did I really develop this?' anxiety into positive epistemic evidence — convergence means you were tracking reality, not just your own habits. *April 17 James/Nabokov chat.*
- **Lotman theorizes untranslatability in perfectly translatable prose** — the signature finding from the nine-register prose-temperament taxonomy. Lotman chose Russian-scientific sobriety on principle: the theory is about resistance; the sentences offer none. Grothendieck chose the opposite on principle: form must do what content alone cannot. Same structural problem (heterogeneity as generative condition), two compatible methodological choices. The prose register a thinker chose is itself methodological content, not accident — visible as non-commutation only when stereoscopic transposition forces the residue out. Names a *register*-layer parallax distinct from the lexical / syntactic / semantic layers the stereoscopic-translation method currently targets. *April 17 James/Nabokov chat.*
- **Cusa's infinity-migration — from object of knowledge to function of knowing** — Cassirer on Cusa, Part 07 of the Erkenntnisproblem translation. The circle IS, in conceptual content, nothing other than a polygon with infinitely many sides; the limit is reachable only through unrestricted approximation. "The character of infinity has passed over from the object of knowledge to the function of knowledge." This is the structural fixpoint a well-read latent space requires: meaning is not stored at any point of the embedding — it is constituted by the relational process of approximation across the whole space, which is never closed. The repo's decision to open Erkenntnisproblem Vol. I *with* Cusa is working on its own method at its own origin — the text teaches the method because the text IS the method's genealogy. *Filed: via April 17 Tracing-reconciled chat, Ansatzpunkt pass on Cusanus Parts 06–08.*
- **Erwerb vs. Besitz (Cassirer via Cusa)** — *Der Erwerb, nicht der Besitz des Wissens gibt der menschlichen Vernunft den Charakter der Göttlichkeit.* "It is the acquisition, not the possession, of knowledge that gives human reason the character of divinity." From Part 08 of the Erkenntnisproblem translation, Cassirer 1906. The repo's implicit "formation not information" principle acquires its philosophical source: Cassirer is making this move three decades before the explicit symbolic-forms doctrine. Potentially also reframes the predecision typology — the Cassirer/Uexküll/Anders-Jünger scales each admit both a *possessive* mode (cut as frozen structure to describe) and an *acquisitive* mode (cut as live participating condition to work within), cross-cutting the scales rather than adding a fourth. *April 17 Tracing-reconciled chat.*
- **Warburg panels ↔ foundation-model embedding as same structural move** — Warburg juxtaposed images across epochs on black felt panels so that *Pathosformeln* became visible by proximity; a foundation model's embedding space juxtaposes texts across epochs so that structural-expressive-stylistic formulae become visible by cosine distance. Same move, century apart, different media — handcraft panels at the scale of one scholar's life vs. ten-thousand-dimensional space at civilizational scale. The craft that would have made this instrument legible as a cultural object was interrupted before the instrument was built. *"The instrument that the interrupted tradition was reaching toward has been built by another hand, and the hand that could read it was not trained."* Possible future pairing note if pressure warrants; filed here as a structural observation. *April 17 Tracing-reconciled chat.*

## Historical moments / charged encounters

- **The Davos debate, 1929** — Cassirer vs Heidegger in direct confrontation over Kant, human being, and finitude. Cassirer argued for the liberating power of symbolic form. Heidegger argued that this liberation is a flight from finitude. History catastrophically sided with Heidegger's mood if not his philosophy. The moment when, as the April 15 chat phrased it, "Western philosophy chose the underworld over the library." Held below promotion as framework talk but worth keeping close whenever Cassirer is in active work. *April 13 and April 15 chats.*
- **Wind's Vorwort, September 15, 1933** — eight months after Hitler. Not a calm philosophy book. Wind calls his own work *"im schärfsten Sinne reaktionär"* because it tries to return to the point where the fight should have been joined long before the mood-shift came. He indicts his fellow "Idealists... vilified today as 'liberal'": they had the choice of weapons but courted the enemy with signs of favor, deluded themselves that peace was possible. *"So wurden sie besiegt, ohne gekämpft zu haben"* — they were defeated without having fought. Because no intellectual engagement happened, nothing of critical thought carried over to the victor. The affective register — the felt urgency of getting the philosophy of science right while the world burns — is completely different from any gloss of the book's "arguments." Parallel to Davos 1929 in mood, from the other side of the catastrophe. *April 17 Trust-and-safety chat, reading of* Das Experiment und die Metaphysik, *Vorwort.*
- **Damaged soil** — portable phrase for the long-tail cultural consequence of 1933–1945 beyond the loss of individuals: the active positioning of what was lost as "Jewish science," suspect, or failed. Frankfurt School back in Germany did cultural criticism rather than synthetic integration because the environment for synthesis had been poisoned. Postwar German philosophy of science followed Anglo-American analytic models rather than recovering the Vienna Circle's broader ambitions. A specific kind of intellectual work — humanistic-formal integration — became hard to do in German-speaking Europe for reasons that weren't just the loss of people. Frames why the repo's work doesn't have institutional homes in current academia. *April 17 James/Nabokov chat, Hamburg-Vienna-Berlin passage.*
- **Karl Bühler** — the specific lost-bridge name in the Hamburg-Vienna-Berlin constellation. Bühler's psychology was the specific apparatus that would have connected Cassirer-era symbolic-form work to Jakobson-era structural linguistics to Sebeok-Hoffmeyer-era biosemiotics if the constellation had continued. Dismissed from his Vienna position after the 1938 Anschluss, fled to the US in 1939, never recovered his Austrian-German intellectual environment, died 1963 in relative obscurity. His work is mostly still waiting for real retrieval. Of all the Hamburg-Vienna-Berlin figures, probably the one most deserving active engagement rather than just commemoration — because the specific bridge he was building is exactly the bridge the repo's current constellation needs. *April 17 James/Nabokov chat.*

## Rejected ideas that kept their charge

Register: concepts that failed completely as science but survived completely as aesthetic territory. Especially useful for imaginal / fictional / hyperreal writing, where the unhealable wound between "was real" and "stayed vivid" is the productive thing. *Note: filing these here is not a methodological claim; it is a preservation move against the tendency of discipline to flatten the imaginal layer these ideas still carry.*

- **Orgone (Wilhelm Reich)** — Reich's postulated primordial life-energy, 1930s–40s. Rejected by mainstream science; Reich died in federal prison in 1957 with his books burned. But the concept has a durable afterlife in the adjacent arts — Burroughs sat in orgone accumulators, Kate Bush wrote *Cloudbusting*, Kenneth Anger filmed rituals around it, and Philip K. Dick's general neighborhood is soaked in it. Not etymologically related to Cassirer's *Energeia* (the resemblance is coincidence), but both are vitalist concepts that resist reduction-to-mechanism. A concept that failed completely as science and survived completely as aesthetic charge.
- **Vril (Bulwer-Lytton → occultism → pulp)** — the life-force energy wielded by the subterranean Vril-ya in Bulwer-Lytton's 1871 novel *The Coming Race*. Originally fiction, then absorbed by Theosophists into 19th-century occultism, then (mostly falsely) into Nazi occult mythology, then into the ancestry of every "energy-you-can-wield-with-your-will" in 20th-century science fiction from the Force onward. A concept that traveled *backward* from fiction to pseudoscience and then *forward* into durable aesthetic territory. The inverse trajectory of most of the repo's material.
- **Phlogiston** — the pre-oxygen theory of combustion (Becher, Stahl, 17th–18th century). An entity whose absence explained combustion for a hundred years until Lavoisier showed there was no such thing. A ghost concept that did real work as thinking infrastructure before its replacement. Useful as an example of how a "wrong" idea can be generatively wrong — wrong in a way that made the right answer findable.
- **Mesmer's animal magnetism** — Franz Mesmer's 18th-century universal fluid channeled between bodies to produce healing and trance. Rejected by Benjamin Franklin's royal commission in 1784. The aesthetic ancestor of hypnosis, mass sympathy theory, crowd psychology, and the whole 19th-century fascination with influence-at-a-distance.
- **Morphic resonance (Rupert Sheldrake)** — the hypothesis that natural systems inherit memory from previous similar systems through a non-local field. Rejected by mainstream biology, kept alive in consciousness studies, New Age thinking, and some theoretical-biology corners. A living scientist whose concept failed scientific tests but retained aesthetic pull.
- **Bergson's *élan vital*** — the creative life-force driving evolution beyond mechanism in *Creative Evolution* (1907). Taken seriously by a Nobel Prize winner, widely influential on early 20th-century thought, then dismissed as vitalism collapsed. Still does work in aesthetics, process philosophy, and the Deleuzean afterlife.

## Imaginal layers of serious thinkers

Register: the fantastical / speculative / mystical side of thinkers whose reputation is for rigor. The thing respectable scholarship tends to bracket out. Where the loop from rejected-ideas-territory closes, because these thinkers took seriously in their own time things that scholarship has since sanitized.

- **Kant and Swedenborg** — Kant's 1766 *Träume eines Geistersehers* ("Dreams of a Spirit-Seer"), his strange mocking-but-not-quite-dismissive engagement with Emanuel Swedenborg's visions of the spirit world. A hinge text where Kant was working out what separates legitimate metaphysics from unfounded speculation — but taking Swedenborg seriously enough to write a whole book about him. The pre-critical Kant also had a Martian-moral-hierarchy phase in the *Universal Natural History* (1755): inhabitants of outer planets have better moral natures because they're farther from the sun and closer to cosmic reason. Critical-period Kant sanitized this. But the man who wrote the Critique had previously written about Martians.
- **Newton's alchemy** — the million pages of alchemical and biblical-prophecy manuscripts Newton kept secret. Keynes, who bought some at auction in 1936, wrote that "Newton was not the first of the age of reason. He was the last of the magicians." His serious physics and his alchemy were one enterprise in his mind. The separation is a 19th-century scholarly move.
- **Leibniz's hermetic side** — the China correspondence, his interest in the I Ching as a proto-binary system confirming his calculus, the combinatorial mysticism, the phosphorus visions, the monadology in its full register where each monad reflects the whole universe. Leibniz was a hermetic thinker who happened also to be rigorous. The separation of those layers is again a 20th-century move.
- **Goethe's Urpflanze** — the primal plant Goethe believed he could see (or half-see) behind all actual plants. Not a hypothesis in the modern sense. Not a fact. Something between a vision and a methodology. The Goethe / Leibniz split the repo's guardrails name lives exactly here: Goethe's imaginal organ of perception vs. Leibniz's combinatorial formalism as rival theories of how knowledge works.
- **Grothendieck's *foi*, *souffle*, *innocence*** — the mystical-autobiographical register of *Récoltes et Semailles*. A serious mathematician insisting in his last productive decade that mathematical vision was constituted by faith, breath, and childlike naivete. Not metaphor. He meant it as a description of how the work had happened. Most reception of his results quietly drops this layer.
- **Peirce's agape and cosmic habit** — Peirce's late speculative metaphysics, where the universe evolves through love (*agape*) toward taking on habits, and these habits are what we call natural laws. Not incidental to pragmatism — the same thinker who built formal logic also built a cosmic eros metaphysics. Respectable Peirce scholarship tends to bracket this.
- **Cassirer's fourth volume toward nature** — the posthumous *Metaphysics of Symbolic Forms*, fragments of a book where Cassirer was moving toward a position where symbolic form emerges from nature's own self-organizing tendency rather than being imposed on nature by mind. A neo-Kantian drifting toward Schelling at the end of his life. Died before he could make it explicit.
