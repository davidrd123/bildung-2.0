# Curio

A running log of things that are interesting, delightful, or carry charge — but not enough shape to warrant a full spark / ghost-book / pairing note. Register: *"I might want to come back to this."* No ceremony. Add when something shows up. Scan when curious.

One line or one paragraph per entry. No frontmatter per entry. If an entry starts acquiring real pull and demands its own note, promote it into the right atelier subfolder (or out of atelier entirely). If it sits dormant, decay is legitimate here.

## Kindred notebook / document practices

- **Wittgenstein's Nachlass** — thousands of pages of working remarks never resolved into a final form. He said the right form for his thinking was the album — remarks arranged by internal pressure, not argument. A score, not a document. *April 15 chat, kindred-documents section.*
- **Simone Weil's Cahiers** — notebooks 1933–1943. Theology, mathematics, factory work, Greek philosophy, mysticism, all in the same pages. A mind refusing to departmentalize. Published posthumously, assembled by others, never in the form she would have chosen. Another interrupted transmission. *April 15 chat.*
- **Novalis's *Allgemeine Brouillon*** — his attempt at an encyclopedia showing internal connections between every domain. 1798–1799, never finished. Explicitly Schellingian. The Romantic ancestor of the repo's insistence that mathematics and poetry and philosophy are organs of one system. *April 15 chat.*
- **Leibniz's unpublished manuscripts** — tens of thousands of pages, still being edited. The lifelong failure to complete the universal characteristic. The Exegesis of rationalism underneath the published work. *April 15 chat.*
- **Buckminster Fuller's Chronofile** — a scrapbook of his entire life from 1917 to 1983: every document, receipt, sketch, letter, newspaper clipping. 140,000 items, 270 linear feet. A pressure field tracked across a life on the theory that patterns only become visible at scale. *April 15 chat.*
- **Alexander Kluge's work** — the way he moves between fiction, documentary, interview, philosophical commentary, image, and found text within a single work. Weather systems instead of genres. *April 15 chat.*
- **Pessoa's trunk** — 25,000+ manuscripts in multiple heteronyms, each with its own philosophy, style, biography. Persona steering attempted by a human in 1920. The collision with the Assistant Axis paper is immediate. *April 15 chat.*
- **Aby Warburg's notebooks (not the Mnemosyne Atlas)** — the diary entries during his breakdown, the private notation system, the way he tracked his own oscillation between rational scholarship and mythic thinking. The personal Exegesis underneath the public Atlas. *April 15 chat.*
- **Hunayn ibn Ishaq** — 9th century Abbasid translator. Collation of multiple manuscripts, composite reconstruction, translation as textual criticism, recovery work that could preserve superior readings rather than merely transmit an already-settled text. The figure the repo's "translation as research" register keeps pointing back toward. *April 13 Cassirer translation chat.*

## Instruments and practices

- **The Talmudic page layout** — primary text at center, Rashi on one side, Tosafot on the other, later commentaries in the margins, all visible simultaneously on a single page. A score for multi-voice reading practice across centuries. The closest existing precedent for what a dynamic view layer would need to be. *April 15 chat; also in repo's existing precedent list.*
- **The I Ching as *used*, not read** — consultation as an instrument for producing readings the consulting mind could not have produced alone. Randomness as mechanism, not noise. Structurally identical to Loom branching — multiple continuations from the same seed, where the spread is the information. *April 15 chat.*
- **Robert Pirsig's Lila notes** — 11,000+ physical index cards organized by what he called "quality" rather than by topic. Cards had their own pressure — some hot, some cold, some demanding to be next to other cards for reasons he couldn't articulate. Paper-era Zettelkasten governed by charge rather than taxonomy. *April 15 chat.*
- **Hakim Bey's Temporary Autonomous Zone** — the concept that the valuable thing is not the permanent institution but the temporary space where something becomes possible that isn't possible elsewhere. Interesting as a register for walled-off speculative spaces inside a disciplined practice. *April 15 chat.*
- **PaRDeS** — the rabbinic four-level reading practice (*peshat* / *remez* / *derash* / *sod* — literal / allegorical / homiletic / mystical). Reading at multiple registers simultaneously without forcing them into a single resolution. Held below promotion in the April 13 extraction as not directly applicable, but the charge is real as a precedent for deferred-structure discipline. *April 13 Cassirer translation chat.*
- **Loom / cyborgism tradition** — the working style where strong LLMs are used like base models in continuation mode rather than as assistant-mode interlocutors. Janus's observation that Opus can be operated in continuation mode without actual base-model access. Multiple continuations from the same point, where the spread is the information. A branching instrument for navigating possibility space. *April 15 chat, practical-move section.*

## Conceptual distinctions and phrases

- **Energeia / Ergon** — the distinction Cassirer took from Humboldt who took it from Aristotle. Activity vs product, working vs work. Language is not a work (*Ergon*) but a *working* (*Energeia*). The distinction keeps getting reached for across chats because it names a specific failure mode: every time vocabulary gets extracted from its breath, every time a pattern gets promoted before its evidence is dense, every time the trace of a practice gets mistaken for the practice itself. A portable diagnostic. Not yet earned as a glossary term but already doing real work in conversation. *April 13, April 14, April 15 chats.*
- **Symbolische Prägnanz** — Cassirer's concept that every perception arrives already saturated with the symbolic form through which it is apprehended. The April 15 chat flagged it as a term that might need to open eventually, and demonstrated it on Claude in real time through the three-stories rotation. Explicitly held below promotion. Keep alive as a pull; do not import as vocabulary until a specific pressure demands it. *April 15 chat.*
- **"Water, mud, and source"** — Grothendieck's image from *Récoltes et Semailles* for a local ethical phenomenology about where thinking drinks from. Held below promotion in the April 14 extraction as methodology, but the phrase is portable. Possible spark candidate once the actual passage is located in the source. *April 14 Grothendieck chat.*

## Historical moments / charged encounters

- **The Davos debate, 1929** — Cassirer vs Heidegger in direct confrontation over Kant, human being, and finitude. Cassirer argued for the liberating power of symbolic form. Heidegger argued that this liberation is a flight from finitude. History catastrophically sided with Heidegger's mood if not his philosophy. The moment when, as the April 15 chat phrased it, "Western philosophy chose the underworld over the library." Held below promotion as framework talk but worth keeping close whenever Cassirer is in active work. *April 13 and April 15 chats.*

## Rejected ideas that kept their charge

Register: concepts that failed completely as science but survived completely as aesthetic territory. Especially useful for imaginal / fictional / hyperreal writing, where the unhealable wound between "was real" and "stayed vivid" is the productive thing. *Note: filing these here is not a methodological claim; it is a preservation move against the tendency of discipline to flatten the imaginal layer these ideas still carry.*

- **Orgone (Wilhelm Reich)** — Reich's postulated primordial life-energy, 1930s–40s. Rejected by mainstream science; Reich died in federal prison in 1957 with his books burned. But the concept has a durable afterlife in the adjacent arts — Burroughs sat in orgone accumulators, Kate Bush wrote *Cloudbusting*, Kenneth Anger filmed rituals around it, and Philip K. Dick's general neighborhood is soaked in it. Not etymologically related to Cassirer's *Energeia* (the resemblance is coincidence), but both are vitalist concepts that resist reduction-to-mechanism. A concept that failed completely as science and survived completely as aesthetic charge.
- **Vril (Bulwer-Lytton → occultism → pulp)** — the life-force energy wielded by the subterranean Vril-ya in Bulwer-Lytton's 1871 novel *The Coming Race*. Originally fiction, then absorbed by Theosophists into 19th-century occultism, then (mostly falsely) into Nazi occult mythology, then into the ancestry of every "energy-you-can-wield-with-your-will" in 20th-century science fiction from the Force onward. A concept that traveled *backward* from fiction to pseudoscience and then *forward* into durable aesthetic territory. The inverse trajectory of most of the repo's material.
- **Phlogiston** — the pre-oxygen theory of combustion (Becher, Stahl, 17th–18th century). An entity whose absence explained combustion for a hundred years until Lavoisier showed there was no such thing. A ghost concept that did real work as thinking infrastructure before its replacement. Useful as an example of how a "wrong" idea can be generatively wrong — wrong in a way that made the right answer findable.
- **Mesmer's animal magnetism** — Franz Mesmer's 18th-century universal fluid channeled between bodies to produce healing and trance. Rejected by Benjamin Franklin's royal commission in 1784. The aesthetic ancestor of hypnosis, mass sympathy theory, crowd psychology, and the whole 19th-century fascination with influence-at-a-distance.
- **Morphic resonance (Rupert Sheldrake)** — the hypothesis that natural systems inherit memory from previous similar systems through a non-local field. Rejected by mainstream biology, kept alive in consciousness studies, New Age thinking, and some theoretical-biology corners. A living scientist whose concept failed scientific tests but retained aesthetic pull.
- **Bergson's *élan vital*** — the creative life-force driving evolution beyond mechanism in *Creative Evolution* (1907). Taken seriously by a Nobel Prize winner, widely influential on early 20th-century thought, then dismissed as vitalism collapsed. Still does work in aesthetics, process philosophy, and the Deleuzean afterlife.

## Imaginal layers of serious thinkers

Register: the fantastical / speculative / mystical side of thinkers whose reputation is for rigor. The thing respectable scholarship tends to bracket out. Where the loop from rejected-ideas-territory closes, because these thinkers took seriously in their own time things that scholarship has since sanitized.

- **Kant and Swedenborg** — Kant's 1766 *Träume eines Geistersehers* ("Dreams of a Spirit-Seer"), his strange mocking-but-not-quite-dismissive engagement with Emanuel Swedenborg's visions of the spirit world. A hinge text where Kant was working out what separates legitimate metaphysics from unfounded speculation — but taking Swedenborg seriously enough to write a whole book about him. The pre-critical Kant also had a Martian-moral-hierarchy phase in the *Universal Natural History* (1755): inhabitants of outer planets have better moral natures because they're farther from the sun and closer to cosmic reason. Critical-period Kant sanitized this. But the man who wrote the Critique had previously written about Martians.
- **Newton's alchemy** — the million pages of alchemical and biblical-prophecy manuscripts Newton kept secret. Keynes, who bought some at auction in 1936, wrote that "Newton was not the first of the age of reason. He was the last of the magicians." His serious physics and his alchemy were one enterprise in his mind. The separation is a 19th-century scholarly move.
- **Leibniz's hermetic side** — the China correspondence, his interest in the I Ching as a proto-binary system confirming his calculus, the combinatorial mysticism, the phosphorus visions, the monadology in its full register where each monad reflects the whole universe. Leibniz was a hermetic thinker who happened also to be rigorous. The separation of those layers is again a 20th-century move.
- **Goethe's Urpflanze** — the primal plant Goethe believed he could see (or half-see) behind all actual plants. Not a hypothesis in the modern sense. Not a fact. Something between a vision and a methodology. The Goethe / Leibniz split the repo's guardrails name lives exactly here: Goethe's imaginal organ of perception vs. Leibniz's combinatorial formalism as rival theories of how knowledge works.
- **Grothendieck's *foi*, *souffle*, *innocence*** — the mystical-autobiographical register of *Récoltes et Semailles*. A serious mathematician insisting in his last productive decade that mathematical vision was constituted by faith, breath, and childlike naivete. Not metaphor. He meant it as a description of how the work had happened. Most reception of his results quietly drops this layer.
- **Peirce's agape and cosmic habit** — Peirce's late speculative metaphysics, where the universe evolves through love (*agape*) toward taking on habits, and these habits are what we call natural laws. Not incidental to pragmatism — the same thinker who built formal logic also built a cosmic eros metaphysics. Respectable Peirce scholarship tends to bracket this.
- **Cassirer's fourth volume toward nature** — the posthumous *Metaphysics of Symbolic Forms*, fragments of a book where Cassirer was moving toward a position where symbolic form emerges from nature's own self-organizing tendency rather than being imposed on nature by mind. A neo-Kantian drifting toward Schelling at the end of his life. Died before he could make it explicit.
