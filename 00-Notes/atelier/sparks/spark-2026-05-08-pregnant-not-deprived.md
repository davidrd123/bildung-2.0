# Spark: pregnant, not deprived

From Bruno, *De la causa, principio et uno*, Dialogo quarto:

> And we say that for this reason it has none, so that it may have all.

And then, a few lines later:

> I say that it is deprived of forms and without them, not as ice is without
> heat, as the deep is deprived of light, but as the pregnant one is without
> her offspring, which she sends and draws forth from herself.

## What pulls

The reversal is total. Peripatetic matter is bare substrate that receives form
from outside. Bruno's matter is the pregnant source that brings forms forth from
its own womb. The absence of determinate form is not lack — it is the condition
for containing all forms.

The pregnancy image is what stays. It is not a metaphor decorating an argument.
It is the argument. What the tradition reads as empty and passive, Bruno reads
as full and generative. The woman is not the deprived partner waiting to receive
from the male form-giver. She is the one who already contains what she will
bring forth.

This is also the moment where Bruno's feminized matter stops being a polemic
against Polihimnio's misogyny and becomes a positive doctrine. The reversal is
not just rhetorical. It changes what matter is.

## Use as image

Not as philosophical claim about matter outside Bruno's text. The image belongs
to the register where a single sentence can anchor a whole reading of a
dialogue.

Useful as:

- anchor for the positive Bruno matter-doctrine against any reading that treats
  his matter as merely Aristotelian substrate
- image for the project's own methodological principle: terms held open are not
  empty, they are carrying more than they can yet express
- contrast point if the Weyl campaign encounters a passage where mathematical
  structure is treated as imposed from outside rather than unfolded from within

The pregnancy reversal is probably the single most portable image the Bruno
campaign produced.
