# Spark: the bone and the club

From Grothendieck, *Récoltes et Semailles*:

> *Tu peux utiliser un os (s'il est gros) pour fracturer un crâne... Mais ce n'est pas là sa vraie fonction.*

> You can use a bone (if it's large enough) to fracture a skull... But that's not its real function.

## What pulls

Grothendieck is describing what his students did with his mathematical tools after the *décès du patron* — they ripped the bones from the living body of his work and used them as clubs. The tools survived because tools are *Ergon*: extractable, portable, storable. The body — the vision, the *souffle*, the organizing wholeness — was buried because it is *Energeia*: only alive in activity.

The image is the sharpest available figure for what the repo's guardrails exist to catch. Every time a framework gets imported before its primary material has been encountered, every time a pattern gets promoted before its evidence is dense, every time vocabulary enters atmospheric drift rather than source-near pressure — that is the bone being used as a club.

## Use as image

Not as method claim. The image belongs to the register where a phrase can anchor a paragraph without having to prove itself. Useful as:

- an epigraph for any piece of writing about the repo's discipline
- a closing image in an essay on Energeia / Ergon
- a moment in a fictional piece about a scholar rediscovering an interrupted project

The bone-and-club is probably the single most portable image the April 15 chat produced. Filing small so it stays findable.
