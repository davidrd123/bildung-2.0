# Play: the pedant's voice

A reading diagnostic that uses Bruno's Polihimnio as a named stance to test
whether you are encountering a text or consuming it.

## The move

1. **Read a difficult passage in your own voice.** Whatever you normally do:
   close reading, translation, annotation, paraphrase. Produce your best
   understanding.

2. **Now read it as Polihimnio would.** Polihimnio reads for surface, for
   lexicon, for citeable authority. He wants the passage to confirm what he
   already knows. He quotes Latin tags. He reaches for the established term. He
   understands but does not grasp. His own formulation: "I understand, but do
   not grasp" (*intendo, ma non capio*).

3. **Compare the two readings.** If they are the same, you were reading as
   Polihimnio in step 1 and didn't notice. The passage has not yet taught you
   anything you didn't bring to it.

4. **Read again as Teofilo would.** Teofilo reads through the words to the
   thing. He follows the argument's own pressure rather than matching it to
   received categories. He is willing to say "you judge" and leave the
   interlocutor holding the conclusion. He lets the material resist his
   framing.

5. **Notice the gap between steps 2 and 4.** That gap is where encounter
   happens. If there is no gap, the passage may not be ready to teach you yet,
   or you may not be ready to hear it. Either way, the diagnostic has done its
   job.

## What this does

It gives you a named character for the default reading mode that the project's
guardrails exist to catch. When the CLAUDE.md says "listen to the material
before structuring it" and "deferred structure is a discipline, not a
deficiency," it is saying: don't be Polihimnio. But "don't be Polihimnio" is
abstract. Actually performing the Polihimnio reading — hearing yourself cite,
categorize, file, and move on — makes the default audible.

Bruno built this diagnostic into his own text. Polihimnio is not incidental
comic relief. He is the interlocutor who represents every reader's first
instinct: to hear the words and miss the thing. His presence in every dialogue
is Bruno's way of showing you the failure mode in real time.

The secondary thing it does: it makes the Teofilo voice available by contrast.
Teofilo is not oracular. He is patient, concrete, willing to work through
examples, willing to say "it is so" and then let the next question do the
advancing. That patience — advancing by the interlocutor's own recognition
rather than by assertion — is the register the project calls encounter.

## What this is not

- **Not a method claim** about how the repo reads primary sources. Plays do not
  promote. If this technique earns method status, it does so through normal
  channels elsewhere; this file stays in atelier as the imaginal ancestor.
- **Not a personality test.** You are not Polihimnio or Teofilo. The point is
  recognizing which voice you are using in a specific reading moment, not
  deciding which character you are.
- **Not a license to dismiss Polihimnio entirely.** He is sometimes right. He
  knows Latin. He notices things. His final line in the fifth dialogue — the
  widowed coin, the "Woe to the solitary!" — produces the occasion for
  Teofilo's most important distinction. The pedant's voice is a diagnostic, not
  a discard pile.

## Seed example

One small test, to be tried on any passage currently under encounter:

Take the opening of the second dialogue's universal intellect passage. Read it
as Polihimnio: "Ah, this is the world-soul doctrine, Neoplatonist in origin,
standard Renaissance syncretism, cf. Ficino." Then read it as Teofilo: what is
the intellect doing? It fills, illuminates, directs, impregnates, explicates
from within the seed. Is that Ficino? Or is the vegetal image — root to trunk
to branch to bud to fruit and back — doing something Ficino's version doesn't?

If the two readings produce the same result, try again with a harder passage.

## Why it lives here

This is a general reading technique — not a specific image (spark), not a
particular unwritten book (ghost-book), not a side-by-side textual adjacency
(pairing). It names a move that can be applied across the project. Plays is the
right home.
