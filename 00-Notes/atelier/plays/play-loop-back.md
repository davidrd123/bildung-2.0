# Play: the loop-back

A reading gambit that uses fiction-world permission to re-enter the imaginal layer of serious thinkers — the part respectable scholarship tends to sanitize out.

## The move

1. **Enter a rejected-ideas world as if it were real.** Orgone. Vril. Mesmerism. The hollow earth. Reich's accumulator. Not ironically. Not as a student of crank history. As an inhabitant of the world in which the thing is the case. Read the original text (or its best advocate) in the register it was written, not in the register of its dismissal.
2. **Operate inside the world long enough that the logic loosens your grip on the respectable version of knowledge.** This is the step that matters. If you cannot feel the loosening, you have not actually entered the world — you were reading crank history at a distance and calling it immersion.
3. **Return to a serious thinker with the loosening still active.** Kant. Newton. Leibniz. Peirce. Grothendieck. Any of the figures in the repo's genealogy.
4. **Notice what the sanitized version had bracketed out.** Kant's *Träume eines Geistersehers*. Newton's alchemical manuscripts. Leibniz's I Ching correspondence. Peirce's cosmic love. Grothendieck's *foi* and *souffle*. These stop looking like embarrassments to be hurried past and start looking like the same thinker continuing to think.
5. **Write the thing the loosening made possible** — a fictional paragraph, an imagined letter, a scene, a parable. Not a theory claim. An atelier-grade artifact that lives in the loop and nowhere else.

## What this does

It refuses the sanitization step that twentieth-century respectable scholarship applies to pre-twentieth-century thinkers. Working assumption: these thinkers did not separate their imaginal layer from their rigorous layer, and the separation is later scholarship's convenience, not the thinker's. The loop-back lets you read the thinker as they actually wrote, rather than through the filter that trimmed their fantastical side.

A secondary thing it does: it keeps the imaginal register useful without collapsing the distinction between what the repo treats as source and what it treats as play. The loop is **explicitly bounded** — you go out into Orgone-world, you come back to Kant, and the return path runs through the actual Kant text, not through whatever Orgone-world suggested Kant might have been. The source has the last word; the loosening only determines which parts of the source you can now read.

## What this is not

- **Not a method claim** about how the repo reads primary sources. Plays do not promote.
- **Not a license to import woo into serious work.** The loosening is for entering the thinker's *actual* imaginal layer as documented in their own texts, not for adding material the thinker never wrote.
- **Not a substitute for source encounter.** The return step requires real primary contact. If the return gets skipped, you have been writing pseudoscience fiction. Which is also fine but belongs in a different part of atelier entirely.
- **Not the same as psychoanalyzing a thinker's subconscious.** The imaginal layer in question is what the thinker consciously wrote and published. Newton's alchemy was not a hidden neurosis — it was his primary intellectual activity for decades, recorded in manuscripts he chose to keep.

## Seed examples

Three small writing prompts the play could generate, filed elsewhere in atelier if any of them is actually written:

- **Kant reads Mesmer in 1784 and doesn't quite dismiss him.** One paragraph, fictional, at the register of the real *Träume eines Geistersehers*. The challenge: write it with the same tone Kant actually used — half-mocking, half-attentive, the philosophical eye working in the presence of something it can neither accept nor fully dismiss.
- **Newton and Reich exchange letters about the secret substance of life.** Cross-century correspondence. Absurd in its setup, straight-faced in its voice. The productive tension: both men wrote seriously about life-substances, and both were professionally destroyed (Newton socially, Reich literally) for failing to keep that writing private.
- **Leibniz receives a Vril accumulator from a Chinese correspondent and writes about it as confirmation of the monadology.** A pastiche that takes its own premise seriously enough to produce a real sentence. The test: does the pastiche voice sound like Leibniz or like 20th-century Leibniz-reception?

Each would be atelier-grade. None would claim method status. Each would be a test of whether the play actually produces something live or just sounds good when described.

## Why it lives here

This is a general technique — not a particular unwritten book (ghost-book territory), not a specific phrase or image (spark), not a specific side-by-side object (pairing), not a reference to something to come back to (curio). The existing atelier registers don't have a home for general moves. Plays is that home.
