# Ghost book: *Récoltes et Semailles, Tome III: Le Souffle et la Forme*

The unwritten third volume of Grothendieck's *Récoltes et Semailles*. The volume where — having diagnosed the *décès du patron* and the failed transmission in the real volumes — he would have returned to constructive mode and built *the notation for how a vision actually breathes*. Not more autobiography. Not more grievance. A practical manual of *souffle*.

## Proposed table of contents

- **I. *Ce que la maison enseigne*** — what the house teaches. Housing pressure as a formative constraint for mathematical thought. The *lit étriqué* and the *maison trop petite* from the real text, extended.
- **II. *Le patron revenant*** — the returning patron. What happens when the departed organizing intelligence comes back through the *chantiers* that outlived him. Not reconciliation. Return as diagnosis of what was lost.
- **III. *L'ingression et la chambre nuptiale*** — ingression and the nuptial chamber. Bogdanov's organizational vocabulary entering Grothendieck's language through the metaphor of the room that binds parts into wholes.
- **IV. *La forme qui respire*** — the form that breathes. The constructive finale. How to build a notation that preserves *Energeia*, not just *Ergon*. The thing Grothendieck did not live to write.

## What the pull is

The fictional TOC emerged mid-conversation while colliding Grothendieck, Bogdanov, and the Cassirer Energeia / Ergon distinction. It has specific enough chapter shapes to anchor a small writing test — a single draft letter, a single fictional chapter, or an essay-per-chapter — without committing to a full book project.

The pull is that the transmission problem Grothendieck names in the real *Récoltes* is structurally unresolved *inside* the real *Récoltes*: the existing volumes diagnose the failure but cannot articulate what the successful alternative would have looked like. Tome III is the book that would fill that gap from the fictional side — not as solution, but as *what Grothendieck would have reached for if he'd had another decade and had encountered Bogdanov*.

The four chapter titles each name a specific pull:
- I names a housing pressure the real *Récoltes* keeps circling
- II names the one emotional state the real *Récoltes* cannot inhabit (the patron returned rather than departed)
- III names a collision with another thinker Grothendieck never met on the page
- IV names the constructive register that the real autobiography keeps deferring

## Use

If a live source campaign on any of the four chapter titles generates real pressure — housing, returning patron, ingression, breath — that is the signal the ghost book has something to say and the right next step is a normal source-near note elsewhere, with a link back here.

Otherwise it stays here. A pull without a promotion path.

As a writing test: one draft chapter, one sitting, to see whether the form actually carries anything or is atmosphere once the keyboard is engaged. The atelier-equivalent of a replay packet.
