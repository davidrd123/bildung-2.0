# Atelier

Purpose: give the repo a legitimate home for things that feel alive, curious, playful, or aesthetically charged for the curator without forcing them through canonical gates too early.

This space is for:

- sparks that feel live but may never earn promotion
- ghost books, unwritten continuations, or impossible volumes
- strange pairings or collisions that are interesting before they are justifiable
- playful or fantastical continuations that sustain the practice

This space is **not**:

- authority
- a backlog of things that "should probably be promoted later"
- a substitute for `sources/modern/incoming/`
- a place to sneak framework imports past the normal guardrails

## Use rule

- Nothing here overrules canonical files, `patterns/`, or source-bound work.
- Nothing here is owed promotion.
- Things may stay here indefinitely.
- If something here becomes live because primary work generates specific demand, the next step is a new source-near note elsewhere, with a link back here if useful.
- Felt aliveness is a legitimate organizing principle here and only here.

## Why this exists

The repo already has strong homes for:

- source-bound translation and commentary
- experiments
- extractions
- distillations
- working syntheses

What it has lacked is a clean home for curator-energy itself: the things that are fun, odd, delightful, aesthetically charged, or simply too early to justify but still worth preserving.

The point of this space is not to lower the bar for canonical work.

The point is to keep discipline from pretending it contains the whole practice.

## Starter buckets

- `sparks/`
  - small notes for things that feel alive right now
  - examples: a phrase, image, possible source, strange adjacency, or one-page curiosity

- `ghost-books/`
  - unwritten volumes, impossible sequels, continued voices, or books that never existed but exert real pull

- `pairings/`
  - collisions, cousins, dark twins, or surprising side-by-side objects that are interesting before they are methodologically earned

## Naming suggestions

- `spark-YYYY-MM-DD-short-name.md`
- `ghost-book-short-name.md`
- `pairing-short-name.md`

## Minimal discipline

- Keep notes concrete enough that future-you can tell what was interesting.
- Prefer one live thing per note.
- Do not argue for promotion from inside the note.
- If a note starts becoming source-bound and necessary, stop expanding it here and open the next artifact in the proper layer.
