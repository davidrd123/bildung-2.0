# On / From / With Text

Status: living method note. Source-bound to Lotman, but not repo
architecture.

## Problem

The May 6 Lotman extraction produced a useful distinction among three ways of
working with a text:

- `on text`: treating the text as an object of description.
- `from text`: treating the text as a source of terms, passages, evidence, and
  translated material.
- `with text`: treating the text as an active partner in a restructuring
  encounter.

This note preserves the distinction only as a practice guardrail. It does not
license claims about the ontology of texts, authors, readers, or LLMs.

## Source Pressure

Lotman first blocks the simple packaging model. In `Vnutri`, Part I, section
1, the text-as-package model assumes that the receiver decodes a text and gets
back the sender's original message. Lotman accepts adequate transmission as a
real function, but says that if it becomes the only or primary function, the
model collapses into paradox: natural languages would look defective because
real senders and receivers never have identical codes, memories, norms,
references, pragmatics, or cultural baggage
(`sources/russian/lotman-vnutri-mislyashih-mirov/source/raw/005-part-i-01-tri-funktsii-teksta.txt:61`,
`:65`, `:75`, `:91`, `:109`). He then names the creative function: a full
semiotic system does not only transmit ready messages, but generates new ones
(`sources/russian/lotman-vnutri-mislyashih-mirov/source/raw/005-part-i-01-tri-funktsii-teksta.txt:116`).

Section 2 gives the strongest binding for `with text`. Lotman says that a
verbal text can function as message, code, or both at once, depending partly on
the receiver's stance
(`sources/russian/lotman-vnutri-mislyashih-mirov/source/raw/006-part-i-02-avtokommunikatsiya.txt:432`,
`:436`, `:439`). A text is used as code when it does not just add new facts,
but transforms the self-understanding of the text-generating personality and
translates existing messages into a new value system
(`sources/russian/lotman-vnutri-mislyashih-mirov/source/raw/006-part-i-02-avtokommunikatsiya.txt:454`).
His examples are Tatyana and Lensky reading novels as models for self and
world
(`sources/russian/lotman-vnutri-mislyashih-mirov/source/raw/006-part-i-02-avtokommunikatsiya.txt:461`,
`:485`, `:511`). Art then oscillates between the two communication systems:
code becomes message, and message becomes code
(`sources/russian/lotman-vnutri-mislyashih-mirov/source/raw/006-part-i-02-avtokommunikatsiya.txt:531`,
`:561`).

`Kultura i vzryv` adds the repeat-reading test. The same recording or literary
text can still produce nonzero information because the receiver changes; in
such cases, even unchanged preservation is transformation, and the creative
initiative belongs to the listener or reader
(`sources/russian/lotman-kultura-i-vzryv/source/cleaned/017-fenomen-iskusstva.txt:35`,
`:39`, `:45`).

## Working Distinctions

`On text` is the stance of description, review, criticism, and synthesis. It is
needed when the repo says what a text is doing. Its risk is fluent external
commentary that loses contact with the passage.

`From text` is the stance of extraction, translation, diplomatic cleanup,
glossary pressure, and evidence gathering. It is needed whenever claims must
be answerable to source wording. Its risk is quarrying the text for usable
fragments while missing the transformation of the whole.

`With text` is the stance of slow encounter. It allows the text to function as
a code that reorganizes existing notes, questions, reading order, and
self-understanding. Its risk is projection: treating the reader's changed
state as if it automatically belonged to the source.

## Operating Rule

Use all three stances, but keep them distinct:

- Claims `on text` must cite what they are describing.
- Claims `from text` must preserve enough wording, page, file, and context to
  be rechecked.
- Claims `with text` must be written as encounter effects unless source
  passages also warrant promotion.

The repo's strongest slow-reading practice is usually `with text` disciplined
by `from text` and made communicable as `on text`.
