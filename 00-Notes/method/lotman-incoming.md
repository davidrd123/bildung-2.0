## What seemed to sharpen

Five contact points surfaced against live repo problems:

1. **Translation non-adequacy as meaning-generation.** One language suffices for transmitting ready-made information; generating new information requires at least two mutually non-adequate languages. The productive gap is the mechanism, not the noise. Contact: open-term practice, translation residue as data.

2. **Collision across partial non-translatability.** Culture is filled with systems of differing translatability. "Another structure, another world" is what produces dynamism. Contact: bounded collision notes, refusal of easy term-equivalence.

3. **Explosion vs. retrospective necessity.** Lived history contains a bundle of real possibilities; retrospective narration falsifies that plurality by pretending the realized path was necessary. Contact: caution against premature closure, against backfilling inevitability into a text or thread.

4. **Translation of the untranslatable as semantic explosion.** Culture continuously turns such explosions into transmissible text. Commentary, translation, and art are meaning-producing operations, not secondary packaging. Contact: charter's equal dignity of translation, commentary, and decryption.

5. **Text boundaries as contextual.** A chapter, book, cycle, or whole oeuvre can function as "the text" depending on the reading situation. Contact: handle/thread/dossier logic, archive-boundary questions in Exegesis and Grothendieck.

## What this does not license

- Lotman is not a project authority. He does not govern method; he sharpens already-live problems.

- Do not use Lotman vocabulary (semiosphere, explosion, etc.) as explanatory framework for the repo. Use only when a specific local problem benefits from the sharpening.
