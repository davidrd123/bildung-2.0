## What this sharpens (repo method)

The repo has a lot of infrastructure for residues:

- distillations, working syntheses, dossiers, glossaries, handles, patterns

All of these are *products* that preserve trace and constraint. They are necessary.

But the practice also needs protected space for ongoing activity that is not trying to become authority. Cassirer gives an old, clean articulation of the distinction in his Humboldt section:

- language is not a finished *work* (`Ergon`)
- it is an ongoing *activity* (`Energeia`)
- its "definition" is therefore genetic rather than object-like
- even where we speak of the "substantiality" of language, the "thing" is the function and its lawful exercise rather than a static deposit

Read against repo structure:

- canonical artifacts are the *Ergon* side (necessary residues, stabilized enough to cite)
- the living practice is the *Energeia* side (translation under resistance, repair passes, prompt-design, bounded probes, and also the "atelier" register)

The method point is not "prefer Energeia." It is "don’t confuse the residue with the working."

## What this does *not* license

- Not a license to treat Cassirer / symbolic forms as the repo’s master vocabulary.
- Not a license to reclassify everything as Energeia/Ergon and pretend the distinction solves promotion discipline.
- Not a license to treat conversations as inherently higher-value than source-bound work.
- Not a license to smuggle “energeia/ergon” into `patterns/` as a governing ontology.

## Practical use (if needed)

Use this distinction only when it clarifies a *local* method problem, e.g.:

- why a protected play/curio space (atelier) is legitimate without becoming promotion pressure
- why “a better phrase” is not the same thing as “a settled term”
- why a viewer/index is an instrument (residue), not the practice itself

If this starts to feel like a general-purpose explanatory frame, it’s probably being overused.
