# Spine Author Dossier Discipline

Status: living method note. This is not repo architecture and does not govern
`patterns/` unless later crystallized there.

## Purpose

Use this method when a major thinker is strong enough to become a spine author
but loose enough that the project could accidentally inherit their vocabulary,
metaphysics, or institutional self-narrative before the claims are earned.

The aim is to extract power without inheriting looseness.

## Epistemic Role

Treat a spine author first as a generator of testable hypotheses, not as a
source of settled doctrine.

Concepts earn standing use by surviving contact with passages, examples,
scope limits, overreach tests, and comparison with other spine authors. They do
not earn standing use because the author's vocabulary is fluent, attractive, or
already useful in conversation.

## Concept Shelves

### Mechanisms

A mechanism names a repeatable operation. It should answer: what happens, by
what relation, under what conditions?

Examples:

- boundary as translation membrane
- self-description as boundary formation
- memory reorganizing past states from a later position
- text-within-text as code switching inside an object

### Diagnostic Lenses

A diagnostic lens helps notice a pattern but is too broad, cultural, or
historically loaded to act as machinery by itself.

Examples:

- binary/ternary culture
- law/mercy
- East/West
- sex or death as a universal cultural language

Lenses are useful, but they stay non-foundational unless converted into a
source-bounded mechanism.

### Orientation Reading

Orientation gives background, institutional self-description, or intellectual
weather. It can help locate a thinker, but it does not seed claims by itself.

If a text has no source passage, no worked example, no scope limit, and no
testable operation, it remains orientation.

## Dossier Template

```text
# <Concept Name>

Status: tentative | passing | retired
Author:
Source passage:
Source status:

## Worked Examples

- example 1
- example 2

## Mechanism

What operation is actually being claimed?

## Layer

Mechanism | historical diagnosis | literary reading | mixed

If mixed, preserve the slide as evidence but do not collapse the layers.

## Scope Of Applicability

Where does this work?

## Where It Does Not Apply

What cases would require modification or refusal?

## Risk / Overreach

What false doctrine would this become if overpromoted?

## Comparator

Which other spine author constrains or triangulates this?

## Counter-Check

What rival reading, historical case, or source passage would weaken it?

## Repo Links

Local passages, journals, encounters, terms, threads, or source files.
```

## Passing Standard

A concept remains tentative until it has all of the following:

1. At least two distinct worked examples, preferably from different domains.
2. A clear mechanism or a clear admission that it is only a lens.
3. An explicit scope limit.
4. A relation to at least one other spine author that constrains its use.
5. A named overreach risk.

If any of these is missing, keep the concept tentative. If the concept cannot
meet the standard after repeated encounters, retire it or demote it to an
orientation note.

## Three Analytical Layers

When reading a spine author, separate:

- semiotic or conceptual mechanism: how meaning, relation, memory, sign, or
  form operates
- historical diagnosis: how a culture, period, institution, or social form is
  described
- literary or source reading: how a specific text or artifact stages the issue

Some authors move productively by sliding among these layers. Preserve the
slide as evidence about the author's thinking, but do not treat a successful
literary reading as automatic proof of a historical diagnosis or general
mechanism.

## Comparative Checks By Layer

Use comparators precisely.

- Mechanism layer: compare with authors who constrain sign, relation, form, or
  organism-level semiosis.
- Historical diagnosis layer: compare with historians, rival cultural cases,
  and historically grounded theorists.
- Literary reading layer: compare with close readers of the same or adjacent
  textual traditions.

The point is not to surround a claim with names. The point is to put pressure
on the layer where the claim is actually operating.

## Promotion Path

Dossiers live first near the source or in `00-Notes/` method space. They are
not glossary entries, thread dossiers, or `patterns/` objects by default.

Promote only when repeated source-facing encounters show that the concept is
needed outside its local dossier. Confirmation alone is not promotion.
