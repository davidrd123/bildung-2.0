## Scope

Question:

What does the recent grounding distinction add to the Vault's
`formal-knowledge-design` frame?

## Starting point in the Vault frame

The frame is already asking the right first-order questions:

- purpose drives design
- principled vs. conventional structural choices
- explicit ontological commitments
- boundary clarity
- rationale documentation
- simplest structure that does the required work

This is strong as far as it goes.

## Main pressure

The frame currently emphasizes `ontological commitments`.

The added pressure is that formal knowledge design also embeds
`grounding commitments`.

Not every knowledge object is carried by the same kind of contact with what it
claims to know.

So the design question is not only:

- what does this structure assume exists?

It is also:

- what kind of grounding carries this object?
- how stable or graded is that grounding?
- how is failure repaired?

That pressure deepens the frame's own warning against the appearance of rigor
without purpose.

## Three useful grounding types

For design purposes, the smallest useful distinction is:

### 1. Referential / action grounding

The object is anchored by practical contact, correction, and consequences.

Examples:

- measurements
- procedures
- operational instructions
- empirical observations with clear test or correction paths

### 2. Social / symbolic grounding

The object is anchored by institutions, genres, discourse communities, and
chains of uptake.

Examples:

- legal terms
- academic categories
- organizational labels
- historically transmitted conceptual distinctions

### 3. Embodied / discernment grounding

The object depends on the thinker's or practitioner's quality of contact,
clarity, regulation, or situated judgment.

Examples:

- reflective judgments
- aesthetic or relational discernment
- "this distinction feels real but is not yet formalized"
- practical wisdom that degrades under stress, haste, or poor attunement

## What this changes in the frame

### 1. Purpose does not only choose structure

Purpose also determines which kind of grounding the structure must preserve.

A structure for reproducible procedures can flatten ambiguity differently than a
structure for aesthetic discernment or collaborative sense-making.

### 2. Ontological clarity is not enough

A schema can be ontologically neat and still epistemically wrong if it forces
heterogeneous grounding types into one representation style.

Typical failure:

- a design treats procedures, institutional categories, and reflective
  judgments as if they were the same kind of knowledge object because they all
  "belong in the vault"

### 3. Repair paths matter

Good formal design should preserve not only the object but the way it gets
corrected.

Examples:

- action-grounded items repair through testing and consequence
- social-symbolic items repair through reinterpretation, use, and contested
  uptake
- embodied-discernment items repair through renewed attention, comparison,
  conversation, and changed state

If those repair modes are different, the structure should not pretend they are
the same.

## Practical design questions this suggests

If the Vault wants to develop this frame, the next useful additions are not
more ontology words. They are questions like:

1. What kind of grounding does this object primarily rely on?
2. Is that grounding binary, or graded?
3. What weakens confidence in this object?
4. How is the object repaired when it drifts or fails?
5. Which parts are principled because of the domain, and which parts are
   principled because of the grounding mode?

## Minimal possible revision to the frame

If John wanted to deepen the frame without changing its identity, the cleanest
addition would be a few concern bullets like:

- what kind of grounding a structure assumes for different knowledge objects
- whether a design preserves graded vs. binary forms of confidence
- how a structure encodes repair paths when different kinds of knowledge fail

And a short sentence in the perspective:

`Every taxonomy embeds not only ontological commitments but assumptions about
what counts as sufficient grounding for a claim.`

## What this does not claim

- that the Vault should stop caring about formal knowledge design
- that embodied discernment must be formalized as a field everywhere
- that the Vault should import `bildung-2.0`'s ontology or handle system
- that formal structure is the wrong lens for the Vault

The narrower claim is:

`formal-knowledge-design` gets stronger if it can distinguish different modes
of grounding rather than treating "knowledge" as one epistemic substance.
