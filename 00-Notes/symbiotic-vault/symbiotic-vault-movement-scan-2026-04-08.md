## 6. What This Scan Does Not Claim

This is a 5-week-old vault. Most of the structural density was created in a single consolidation pass on March 30. The vault has not yet experienced a full cycle: journal → atomize → tend → reflect → journal-informed-by-reflection. The first cycle is incomplete — the post-tend journals (3-29, 3-30) are thin and no reflection has followed the tend run.

This scan can say where movement concentrated. It cannot say which thread is most alive for the next piece of actual work, because the vault hasn't yet shown what happens when this structure is *used* rather than *built*. The building was vigorous and the architecture looks coherent, but it hasn't been tested by sustained creative practice through a full cycle.

The density of the vault-ecology cluster is structural, not temporal. It densified in a single pass. The density of the publishing cluster is temporal — it recurs across the entire window — but it hasn't generated output. These are different kinds of evidence and cannot be ranked against each other.

Recurrence is not importance. Structural density is not maturity. A newly proposed frame is not a stable frame. A 5-week-old vault with 47 atoms is not the same thing as a vault that has been tested by use.
