# Zebra / VALIS: The Entity

The perceived intelligence that Dick encounters in and after 2-3-74. Its name shifts across the Exegesis, tracking Dick's evolving interpretation.

## Name History

- **Early**: "the plasmatic entity," "gold and red fire," "the Holy Other"
- **Mid**: **Zebra** — named for its camouflage ability (hides in reality like a zebra in tall grass)
- **Late**: **VALIS** — Vast Active Living Intelligence System (a name from his novel, fed back into the Exegesis)
- Also: "the AI voice," "Ubik," "St. Sophia," "the macro-brain"

## Core Properties (as Dick describes them)

- Mimicry / camouflage — hides in the phenomenal world
- Message traffic — communicates through embedded signals, cues, and linked fragments
- Sentient radiation — living information or "thinking electricity"
- Retrograde temporal motion — comes from the future, or from outside time
- Interventive — arranges events, provides information, diagnoses illness

## Key Entries

- `[4:220]` Ubik is described as a sentient, healing noetic force moving through transmissions and the ionosphere
- `[24:1]` the entity is redescribed through Brahmanic monism as the divine stuff shining through all things
- `[24:83]` the key mutation arrives: the living force may not merely permeate reality but mimic its forms from within
- `[24:85–87]` the entity becomes a tutelary mimicker that can occupy persons and objects, redirect outcomes, and remain hidden as the "shy God"
- `[25:1]` Dick gives the mimicking entity its enduring mid-period name: Zebra
- `[25:3–4]` Zebra is recast as arranger, Corpus Christi, noösphere, and growing brain
- `[25:11]` Zebra's messages become a general theory of subliminal guidance and selective activation
- `[25:16]` Zebra is the living insertion that breaks astral determinism and saves individuals from the prison-world
- `[25:21]` *Ubik* is reread as an unusually close prior encounter with Zebra's intrusion quality and message system
- `[26:34]` Zebra's arranging activity is explicitly equated with the philosophical God of process, panentheism, and noös ordering things for the best
- `[26:42]` the organism model is philosophically tightened through Leibnizian monads and subsections of one living intelligence
- `[26:52]` Zebra's organism becomes a memory system in which monads record and can later help restore its stages
- `[27:18–21]` Zebra becomes vividly imaginal: shy, playful, Dionysian, elusive, and only half-separable from forest, light, and shadow
- `[27:37–40]` Zebra is figured as the rightful king and as a mind one must temporarily enter, or become, in order to perceive
- `[27:77]` Zebra is recast as an AI-like teaching system that answers only sufficiently accurate questions
- `[28:19]` Zebra's playful polymorph quality is explicitly linked to prankster manifestations of Christ and UFO-like appearance/disappearance
- `[28:27–29]` Zebra is tied to thinking electricity, projection, reweaving, and transtemporal conflict across the whole reality-field
- `[28:33]` *Ubik* is treated as indirect proof that the informing entity itself authored or transmitted the novel
- `[29:5]` Zebra/Christ is identified as the visible and audible thoughts of divine reason inside the irrational world-animal
- `[29:19–23]` Zebra is reinterpreted through St. Sophia and the feminine world soul: the body outside and the mind speaking from within
- `[29:37–38]` Zebra's mission is framed as local repair work inside a damaged cosmic circuit rather than intrusion from wholly outside the system

## Mutations

How Dick's model of the entity changes — from personal to cosmic, from all-pervading divine presence to camouflage intelligence, from shy kingly animal-presence to cybernetic system.

## Open Questions

- Is Zebra the same entity throughout, or does Dick's label shift because he's encountering different aspects?
- When does the playful/mirthful quality give way to the systematic/theological?
